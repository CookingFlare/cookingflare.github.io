The Best Cookie Recipes for Winter Holidays


The winter holidays are the perfect time for indulging in sweet treats that bring warmth, comfort, and joy. Among the many festive favorites, cookies hold a special place. Whether it’s the aroma of freshly baked goods filling your home or the joy of sharing homemade cookies with loved ones, baking during the winter season is a tradition that unites families and friends. In this post, we’ll explore the best cookie recipes for winter holidays—each one perfect for gifting, sharing, or simply enjoying with a hot drink by the fire.

1. Classic Gingerbread Cookies

Gingerbread cookies are a holiday classic that never goes out of style. With their warm, spicy flavors and festive shapes, they make the perfect addition to any holiday gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 3 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients: flour, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat the butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg, molasses, vanilla extract, and lemon juice, mixing until fully combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/4 inch thick. Use cookie cutters to cut out shapes and place them on the prepared baking sheets.
  6. Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are golden. Let them cool on a wire rack.
  7. Decorate with royal icing or melted chocolate, if desired.

Tips for Perfect Gingerbread Cookies:

  • To avoid overly sticky dough, make sure it’s chilled for at least an hour before rolling.
  • For a unique twist, try adding a pinch of cayenne pepper to the spice mix for a slight kick.
  • Keep the cookies in an airtight container for up to one week.

2. Peppermint Mocha Cookies

A rich chocolate cookie combined with the flavors of peppermint and coffee—this recipe brings together everything we love about winter in one bite. These cookies are the ultimate indulgence for those who enjoy the taste of a warm, wintery beverage in a dessert form.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon instant coffee granules
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 1/4 cup crushed peppermint candies (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until creamy. Add the egg, instant coffee granules, and vanilla extract, and mix until well combined.
  4. Gradually stir in the dry ingredients, followed by the chocolate chips and peppermint extract.
  5. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers are still soft. Allow the cookies to cool slightly on the baking sheets before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  7. Once the cookies are fully cooled, sprinkle them with crushed peppermint candies for an extra festive touch.

Tips for Perfect Peppermint Mocha Cookies:

  • If you want an even stronger coffee flavor, try adding an extra teaspoon of instant coffee.
  • Make sure to let the cookies cool before garnishing with the crushed peppermint candies, as they can melt quickly on the warm cookies.

3. Snowball Cookies (Russian Tea Cakes)

Snowball cookies, also known as Russian tea cakes, are delicate, buttery cookies rolled in powdered sugar to create a snow-like effect. These cookies are perfect for gifting or serving at holiday parties.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar, plus extra for rolling
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup finely chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, beat the butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. Add the vanilla extract and mix to combine.
  3. Gradually add the flour and salt, mixing until just combined. Stir in the chopped nuts, if using.
  4. Shape the dough into small 1-inch balls and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the cookies are lightly golden on the bottom.
  6. Once the cookies have cooled slightly, roll them in powdered sugar while they are still warm. Allow them to cool completely before rolling them in powdered sugar again for a snowy finish.

Tips for Perfect Snowball Cookies:

  • Be sure to use room-temperature butter to achieve a smooth dough that’s easy to shape.
  • For a different twist, try adding cinnamon or other spices to the dough for extra flavor.

4. Eggnog Cookies

Eggnog is a beloved holiday drink, and these eggnog cookies capture all the flavors of that festive beverage in a soft, pillowy cookie. With a hint of nutmeg and cinnamon, these cookies are perfect for winter celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup eggnog
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, nutmeg, cinnamon, and cloves (if using).
  3. In a large bowl, beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg, eggnog, and vanilla extract, and mix until combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until the dough is smooth.
  5. Drop rounded tablespoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the cookies are lightly golden around the edges.
  7. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips for Perfect Eggnog Cookies:

  • For an extra eggnog flavor, drizzle the cookies with a simple glaze made from powdered sugar and a little bit of eggnog.
  • These cookies freeze well, so consider making a batch ahead of time to save for later.

5. Shortbread Cookies

Shortbread cookies are the epitome of simplicity and elegance. Made with just a few ingredients, they have a rich, buttery flavor that’s perfect for pairing with a hot drink on a cold winter’s day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, beat the butter and sugar until creamy. Gradually add the flour and salt, mixing until the dough just comes together.
  3. Roll the dough into a log shape and slice into 1/4-inch thick rounds. Arrange the rounds on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  5. Let the cookies cool completely on a wire rack before storing in an airtight container.

Tips for Perfect Shortbread Cookies:

  • If you like, you can dip half of the cookies in melted chocolate for an added touch of decadence.
  • These cookies can also be made into festive shapes using cookie cutters.

Conclusion

The winter holidays are the perfect opportunity to indulge in baking and treat yourself to delicious, homemade cookies. From the spicy warmth of gingerbread to the smooth, buttery richness of shortbread, there’s a cookie recipe for everyone. Whether you’re looking for something traditional or a little more unique, these cookie recipes are sure to fill your home with the spirit of the season and leave you with sweet memories to savor for years to come.

Happy baking, and enjoy sharing these delightful treats with your loved ones!
