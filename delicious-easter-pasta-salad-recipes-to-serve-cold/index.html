Delicious Easter Pasta Salad Recipes to Serve Cold


Spring has fully blossomed, and with it comes the joyous spirit of Easter—family gatherings, colorful decorations, and, of course, delightful feasting. Among the myriad dishes that grace the Easter table, cold pasta salads have emerged as a favorite. They offer a vibrant combination of textures and flavors, are easy to prepare ahead of time, and complement a variety of main courses. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, picnic, or a festive dinner, these Easter pasta salad recipes will brighten your table and please your guests with every bite.

In this post, we’ll explore an array of pasta salad recipes tailored to suit the Easter mood. Each salad is designed to serve cold, making them perfect for relaxing springtime meals or potlucks. We’ll dive into fresh ingredients, creative flavor pairings, and friendly preparation tips that even the busiest cooks can appreciate. So, grab your favorite pasta shapes, and let’s dive into these stunning Easter pasta salads!

1. Spring Garden Pasta Salad with Lemon-Herb Dressing

This vibrant pasta salad captures the freshness of spring produce with crunchy peas, tender asparagus, and sun-ripened cherry tomatoes. Tossed in a bright lemon-herb dressing, it’s a refreshing way to celebrate the season.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces tri-color rotini pasta
  • 1 cup fresh asparagus tips, blanched and chilled
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas, halved
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup diced cucumber (seeds removed)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 3 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)

Lemon-Herb Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon honey or agave
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rotini pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and rinse in cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the dressing by whisking olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, honey, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper until emulsified.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cold pasta, asparagus tips, sugar snap peas, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, parsley, basil, and green onions.
  4. Drizzle dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  5. Garnish with crumbled feta cheese before serving.
  6. Refrigerate for at least an hour to allow flavors to meld beautifully.

Why It Works:

This salad bursts with fresh, crisp vegetables that scream spring. The lemon-herb dressing cuts through the richness of the cheese and pasta with zesty brightness, making it ideal for warm days.

2. Creamy Deviled Egg Pasta Salad

Inspired by the classic deviled eggs often found at Easter brunch, this creamy pasta salad puts a playful twist on tradition. It combines the flavors of mustard, paprika, and tangy mayonnaise with pasta and crunchy bacon for satisfying texture.

Ingredients:

  • 10 ounces medium shell pasta
  • 6 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon yellow mustard
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika, plus extra for garnish
  • 4 slices cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup diced celery
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh chives
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente; drain and cool with cold water.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk mayonnaise, mustard, apple cider vinegar, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the pasta, chopped eggs, celery, bacon, and chives to the dressing, folding gently to combine.
  4. Sprinkle additional smoked paprika over the top and chill for at least an hour before serving.

Why It Works:

This recipe turns a beloved Easter appetizer into a robust salad that pairs perfectly with ham or roast chicken. The creamy dressing provides rich comfort, while bacon offers a smoky snap.

3. Mediterranean Easter Orzo Salad with Roasted Red Peppers and Olives

For those favoring Mediterranean flavors to complement their Easter menu, this orzo pasta salad delivers a harmony of salty, sweet, and tangy notes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups orzo pasta
  • 1 cup roasted red peppers, diced
  • 1/2 cup kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled goat cheese or feta
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Dressing:

  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the orzo according to package instructions; drain and rinse to cool.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Combine orzo, roasted red peppers, olives, goat cheese, pine nuts, mint, and parsley in a large bowl.
  4. Pour dressing over salad and toss well.
  5. Refrigerate for one hour or overnight for intensified flavor.

Why It Works:

The nutty orzo shines when paired with the vibrant roasted red pepper and the briny kick of olives. Fresh herbs add that unmistakable Mediterranean freshness, ideal for an Easter celebration that aims to impress.

4. Colorful Rainbow Veggie Pasta Salad

Celebrate Easter’s palette with this colorful pasta salad loaded with chopped bell peppers, carrots, and radishes, all tossed in a honey-mustard vinaigrette. It’s healthy, eye-catching, and perfect for kids and adults alike.

Ingredients:

  • 14 ounces farfalle (bow-tie) pasta
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced yellow bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/3 cup sliced radishes
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels (fresh or thawed frozen)
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions

Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare pasta as directed. Drain and rinse under cold water.
  2. Whisk together the dressing ingredients until thoroughly combined.
  3. Mix pasta and chopped vegetables in a large serving bowl.
  4. Pour dressing over salad and toss to evenly coat.
  5. Chill for 30 minutes before serving.

Why It Works:

The combination of crunchy veggies and sweet, tangy dressing makes this pasta salad light yet satisfying. The burst of color also makes it stand out on any buffet table.

5. Sweet Pea and Prosciutto Pasta Salad with Parmesan

If you’re craving a sophisticated yet simple pasta salad, this recipe’s subtle flavors combined with the saltiness of prosciutto and sweetness of peas are delightful. The Parmesan adds a lovely sharp finish.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces penne pasta
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas, cooked and chilled
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced prosciutto, torn into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup toasted pine nuts
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste

Dressing:

  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon finely minced shallot
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook penne pasta until al dente, then rinse under cold water.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, honey, shallot, salt, and pepper.
  3. Combine pasta, peas, prosciutto, and Parmesan in a large mixing bowl.
  4. Drizzle dressing over the salad and toss until combined.
  5. Sprinkle toasted pine nuts on top and add extra cracked pepper before serving chilled.

Why It Works:

The saltiness from prosciutto and nuttiness from pine nuts contrasts beautifully with sweet peas and sharp Parmesan, crafting an elegant pasta salad perfect for an Easter feast.

Final Tips for Crafting Perfect Easter Pasta Salads:

  • Choose the Right Pasta: Smaller shapes like rotini, fusilli, or bow tie are ideal as they hold dressing well and blend smoothly with other ingredients.
  • Cook Pasta Properly: Al dente pasta is essential to avoid mushiness when served cold. Rinse pasta under cold water immediately after cooking to stop the cooking process and cool it down.
  • Flavor Balance: A good pasta salad balances acidity, sweetness, saltiness, and freshness. Tasting as you dress the salad helps keep it from being bland or overpowering.
  • Prep Ahead: Pasta salads often taste better after resting in the refrigerator for at least an hour, allowing flavors to meld.
  • Add Crunch: Incorporating nuts, celery, or bell peppers adds an enjoyable crunch, making texture just as interesting as taste.

Whether your Easter plans include a formal sit-down dinner or a casual outdoor gathering, these pasta salads bring colorful variety to the table and celebrate the season’s freshest flavors. Try one—or mix and match for a vibrant spread that highlights the abundance of spring.

Happy Easter and happy cooking!
