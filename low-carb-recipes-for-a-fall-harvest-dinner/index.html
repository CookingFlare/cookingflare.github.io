Low Carb Recipes for a Fall Harvest Dinner


With the crisp air and vibrant hues of autumn settling in, there’s no better time to celebrate the season with a fall harvest dinner. Whether you’re hosting friends and family or simply indulging in a cozy meal at home, crafting a menu that embraces seasonal produce while staying mindful of lower-carb options can feel both satisfying and rewarding. In this blog post, we’ll explore a collection of delicious, wholesome, and nutrient-packed dishes perfect for a fall harvest dinner. These recipes bring autumn’s bounty to the table with rich, earthy flavors while supporting your health goals.

Appetizer: Roasted Spaghetti Squash and Sage Dip

Start your meal with a warm, comforting dip that celebrates the quintessential flavors of fall. Spaghetti squash offers the perfect low-carb base, and the addition of fresh sage creates a rich aroma that pairs beautifully with autumn spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash (about 3 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh sage, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup full-fat Greek yogurt
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C). Slice the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise, remove the seeds, and place the halves cut-side down on a baking sheet. Roast for 35–40 minutes, or until the flesh becomes tender.
  2. Using a fork, scrape out the spaghetti squash strands and set them aside to cool slightly.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sage, cooking for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Blend the spaghetti squash strands, garlic and sage mixture, and Greek yogurt in a food processor until smooth.
  5. Season the dip with salt and pepper. Serve warm with low-carb crackers, cucumber slices, or bell pepper sticks for dipping.

Main Course: Herb Crusted Pork Loin with Roasted Root Vegetables

Pork is a versatile protein that pairs beautifully with the earthy, sweet notes of roasted autumn vegetables. This herb crusted pork loin dish is packed with flavor and serves as a delightful centerpiece for your harvest-inspired dinner.

Ingredients:

For the Pork Loin:

  • 1 (2-pound) pork loin
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Root Vegetables:

  • 2 cups turnips, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup radishes, trimmed and halved
  • 1 medium rutabaga, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Prepare a roasting pan lined with parchment paper.
  2. Rub the pork loin with Dijon mustard and olive oil. Sprinkle it evenly with rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. Place the pork loin on the roasting pan.
  3. Toss the chopped root vegetables in a large mixing bowl with olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Spread them around the pork loin on the roasting pan.
  4. Roast everything for 35–40 minutes, turning the vegetables halfway through and ensuring the pork loin reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C).
  5. Let the pork rest for 5–10 minutes before slicing. Serve slices alongside the roasted vegetables for a hearty fall meal.

Side Dish: Creamy Cauliflower Mash with Parmesan

Say goodbye to carb-heavy mashed potatoes and hello to creamy cauliflower mash that’s just as indulgent. This dish is packed with nutrients and makes the perfect low-carb complement to your fall feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 3 tablespoons heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Steam the cauliflower florets in a large pot until very tender, about 10–12 minutes.
  2. Drain the cauliflower and transfer it to a food processor. Add heavy cream, butter, Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
  4. Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with fresh parsley if desired. Serve warm alongside your main course.

Salad: Harvest Greens with Spiced Pumpkin Dressing

Salads can be as vibrant and flavorful as the season itself. This harvest-inspired salad blends fresh greens with toasted nuts, creamy goat cheese, and a homemade spiced pumpkin dressing for a unique autumn twist.

Ingredients:

For the Salad:

  • 4 cups mixed greens (such as arugula, spinach, and kale)
  • 1/2 cup toasted pecans
  • 1/3 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds

For the Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons canned pumpkin puree
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Assemble the mixed greens, pecans, goat cheese, and pomegranate seeds in a large salad bowl.
  2. Whisk together all dressing ingredients until well combined. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad before serving. Toss gently to combine and enjoy the refreshing mix of flavors.

Dessert: Pumpkin Spice Almond Flour Cookies

End your fall harvest dinner on a sweet note with these delightful pumpkin spice almond flour cookies. Grain-free and naturally low-carb, these cookies are soft, chewy, and bursting with autumn spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup sweetener of choice (such as erythritol or monk fruit)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the canned pumpkin puree, egg, sweetener, and vanilla extract.
  3. Add almond flour, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, baking soda, and salt to the wet mixture, stirring to combine. The dough should be soft but hold together well.
  4. Scoop the dough into tablespoon-sized balls and flatten slightly on the baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 12–15 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. Allow cookies to cool completely before serving.

Final Thoughts

A fall harvest dinner doesn’t have to be laden with carbs to be satisfying and full of flavor. By focusing on seasonal ingredients, warm spices, and creative cooking techniques, you can craft a meal that feels indulgent without sacrificing your health goals. Whether you’re enjoying roasted root vegetables, creamy cauliflower mash, or pumpkin spice cookies for dessert, these recipes embody the essence of autumn while keeping your carb intake in check. Gather your loved ones and let the flavors of the season bring warmth and joy to your table.
