10 Beef Recipes for the Grill: Perfectly Cooked Every Time


Grilling is an age-old tradition that brings out the best in meat, especially beef. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue, enjoying a family dinner, or just craving something juicy and flavorful, grilling beef is always a great option. From succulent steaks to delicious burgers, the possibilities are endless. However, perfecting the art of grilling beef requires knowing the right cuts, techniques, and flavors that complement the meat.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through ten mouthwatering beef recipes that will elevate your grilling game. Each recipe is carefully crafted to ensure your beef is cooked to perfection every time, with tips on seasoning, grilling times, and best practices.

1. Classic Grilled Ribeye Steak

Ribeye steaks are known for their marbling, which gives them that melt-in-your-mouth tenderness. When grilled, the fat renders perfectly, creating a juicy and flavorful bite. Here’s how to get the perfect ribeye steak every time.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ribeye steaks (1.5 inches thick)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh rosemary (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to high heat (around 450°F).
  2. Brush both sides of the ribeye steaks with olive oil. Season generously with salt and pepper. Optionally, you can also rub with crushed garlic and fresh rosemary for added flavor.
  3. Place the steaks on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes on each side for medium-rare. Adjust the time depending on your preferred level of doneness:
    • Rare: 3-4 minutes per side
    • Medium: 5-6 minutes per side
    • Well-done: 7-8 minutes per side
  4. Remove the steaks from the grill and let them rest for 5 minutes before serving. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a tender, flavorful bite.

Pro Tip: For an added layer of flavor, top the steaks with compound butter after grilling, made from butter, garlic, and herbs.

2. Grilled Beef Tenderloin

Beef tenderloin is one of the most tender cuts of beef, and grilling it properly can create a delicious, restaurant-quality meal. The key to grilling tenderloin is not overcooking it, as it can dry out.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole beef tenderloin (about 2 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (around 400°F).
  2. Coat the beef tenderloin with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, thyme, salt, and pepper. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Grill the tenderloin for 5-7 minutes on each side, depending on the thickness. Use a meat thermometer to check for doneness:
    • Medium-rare: 130-135°F
    • Medium: 135-145°F
    • Well-done: 150°F and above
  4. Remove the tenderloin from the grill and let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing.

Pro Tip: Serve the tenderloin with a red wine reduction sauce or a creamy horseradish sauce to complement its rich flavor.

3. Grilled Skirt Steak Fajitas

Skirt steak is known for its intense beef flavor and chewy texture, making it perfect for grilling and slicing thinly for fajitas. This recipe brings together smoky grilled beef, fresh vegetables, and tortillas for an unforgettable meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds skirt steak
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • Flour tortillas

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Mix the chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, paprika, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Rub the mixture onto the skirt steak.
  3. Grill the steak for 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, or adjust based on your preferred level of doneness.
  4. While the steak is grilling, place the sliced onion and bell pepper on the grill. Cook them for 4-5 minutes until softened and slightly charred.
  5. Slice the skirt steak against the grain into thin strips. Serve with the grilled vegetables and warm tortillas.

Pro Tip: Add a dollop of sour cream, guacamole, or salsa for extra flavor.

4. Grilled Beef Kebabs

Beef kebabs are a fun and easy way to grill beef, offering versatility with vegetables and marinades. You can use various cuts like sirloin or flank steak for kebabs, each bringing its own unique flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound sirloin or flank steak, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, cubed
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 onion, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, soy sauce, honey, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Marinate the beef cubes in this mixture for 30 minutes.
  3. Thread the beef, bell pepper, zucchini, and onion onto skewers, alternating between meat and vegetables.
  4. Grill the kebabs for 4-5 minutes on each side until the beef is cooked to your desired doneness.
  5. Serve with a side of rice or a fresh salad.

Pro Tip: For an even more vibrant flavor, marinate the vegetables in the same mixture as the beef.

5. Grilled Beef Burger Patties

No grilling list is complete without a classic beef burger. Grilling the perfect burger requires the right balance of seasoning, juiciness, and grill technique.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef (80% lean)
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Lettuce, tomato, cheese, and condiments (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Shape the ground beef into 4 patties. Season each patty with salt, pepper, and a dash of Worcestershire sauce.
  3. Grill the patties for 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare. Add a slice of cheese during the last minute of grilling for a melty topping.
  4. Toast the buns on the grill for a minute or two.
  5. Assemble the burgers with your desired toppings and serve.

Pro Tip: To prevent the burgers from shrinking while grilling, make a small indentation in the center of each patty with your thumb before grilling.

6. Grilled Flank Steak with Chimichurri Sauce

Flank steak is lean, flavorful, and perfect for grilling. Pair it with a zesty chimichurri sauce for an Argentine-inspired dish that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 flank steak (about 1.5 pounds)
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For Chimichurri:

  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to high heat.
  2. Season the flank steak with salt and pepper, then brush it with olive oil.
  3. Grill the steak for 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare. Slice it against the grain to ensure tenderness.
  4. While the steak is grilling, mix the chimichurri ingredients in a bowl.
  5. Serve the grilled flank steak with chimichurri sauce drizzled on top.

Pro Tip: Chimichurri can be made ahead of time and stored in the fridge for up to a week. It also works wonderfully as a marinade.

7. Grilled Beef Brisket

Beef brisket is a cut of meat that benefits from slow, low-heat cooking. However, with the right technique, you can make a grilled version that’s both flavorful and tender.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole beef brisket (3-4 pounds)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 cups beef broth (for basting)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to a low heat (around 250°F).
  2. Coat the brisket with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the brisket on the grill, fat side up. Close the lid and cook for 3-4 hours, basting with beef broth every 30 minutes.
  4. Once the brisket reaches an internal temperature of 190°F, remove it from the grill and let it rest for 15 minutes before slicing.

Pro Tip: For an extra smoky flavor, add wood chips to the grill during cooking.

8. Grilled Beef Tri-Tip Roast

Tri-tip is a flavorful cut of beef that’s perfect for grilling. It’s best when seasoned simply to allow the meat’s natural flavors to shine.

Ingredients:

  • 2-pound beef tri-tip roast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Rub the tri-tip roast with olive oil and season generously with garlic powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the tri-tip for 5-6 minutes on each side, then reduce the heat and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally.
  4. Let the roast rest for 10 minutes before slicing against the grain.

Pro Tip: Tri-tip is great for sandwiches or served with a side of grilled vegetables.

9. Grilled Beef Short Ribs

Beef short ribs are a fantastic option for grilling, as the meat becomes tender and flavorful when cooked properly.

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Mix olive oil, soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Coat the short ribs with the marinade and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  3. Grill the short ribs for 4-5 minutes on each side.
  4. Let them rest for a few minutes before serving.

Pro Tip: Brush with extra marinade during grilling for added flavor.

10. Grilled Beef Tacos

Beef tacos are an easy, fun way to enjoy grilled beef. Whether you use ground beef, steak, or another cut, these tacos are guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon taco seasoning
  • 8 small tortillas
  • Lettuce, tomato, cheese, sour cream, salsa (optional toppings)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the ground beef in a grill-safe pan or skillet until cooked through, breaking it up as it cooks.
  3. Season with taco seasoning and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Warm the tortillas on the grill for 30 seconds per side.
  5. Serve the beef in tortillas and top with your favorite toppings.

Pro Tip: Add some grilled onions or peppers for a smoky twist on classic beef tacos.

Grilling beef can be both an art and a science, but with the right techniques and recipes, you’ll be able to cook perfect beef every time. Whether you’re cooking a juicy steak or making kebabs, these recipes will help you create meals that are sure to impress your guests and satisfy your cravings. Happy grilling!
