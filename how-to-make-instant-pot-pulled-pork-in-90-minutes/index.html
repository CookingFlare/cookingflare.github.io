How to Make Instant Pot Pulled Pork in 90 Minutes


Pulled pork is a classic dish that can be served in a variety of ways: on sandwiches, over rice, in tacos, or simply as a main dish with your favorite sides. Traditionally, pulled pork takes hours to cook, often requiring slow roasting or smoking for a tender, fall-apart texture. However, using the Instant Pot, you can achieve that same rich flavor and melt-in-your-mouth tenderness in just 90 minutes. This recipe is perfect for those craving a quick, flavorful pulled pork meal without sacrificing quality. In this guide, we will walk you through every step of making Instant Pot pulled pork that will impress your family, friends, or guests.

Ingredients

To make the best Instant Pot pulled pork, you’ll need a few basic ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to gather:

  • Pork shoulder (or pork butt): 3 to 4 pounds (bone-in or boneless)
  • Yellow onion: 1 large, sliced
  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced
  • Apple cider vinegar: 2 tablespoons
  • Chicken broth: 1 cup (for moisture and depth of flavor)
  • Brown sugar: 2 tablespoons (for sweetness and caramelization)
  • Paprika: 1 tablespoon
  • Ground cumin: 1 teaspoon
  • Chili powder: 1 tablespoon
  • Salt: 1 tablespoon
  • Black pepper: 1 teaspoon
  • Olive oil: 2 tablespoons (for searing the pork)
  • BBQ sauce: 1 cup (optional for finishing)

Optional Garnishes:

  • Pickles
  • Coleslaw
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Jalapeño slices

Preparing the Pork

Step 1: Seasoning the Pork

Begin by preparing the pork shoulder. If you’re using a bone-in cut, the bone adds extra flavor, but you can also use boneless pork shoulder, depending on your preference. Pat the pork dry with paper towels to remove any excess moisture. This will help the seasoning stick and allow for better searing.

In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Rub this spice mixture all over the pork shoulder. Don’t be afraid to coat the meat generously with the seasoning to ensure a flavorful, crusty exterior once it’s cooked.

Step 2: Searing the Pork

Before cooking the pork in the Instant Pot, it’s essential to sear the meat to lock in its juices and enhance the flavor. Set your Instant Pot to the ‘Sauté’ mode and add 2 tablespoons of olive oil. Allow the oil to heat up for about 2 minutes, then carefully add the pork to the pot. Sear the pork on all sides, about 3 to 4 minutes per side, until the surface is golden brown and crispy. This step will add depth to the final flavor and create a lovely crust on the pork.

Step 3: Adding Aromatics

Once the pork is seared, remove it from the Instant Pot and set it aside on a plate. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic to the pot, scraping the bottom to release any brown bits that have stuck to the surface. These bits contain a lot of flavor and will help create a rich base for your pulled pork.

Sauté the onions and garlic for 2 to 3 minutes until softened and fragrant. This will enhance the overall aroma of the dish.

Step 4: Adding Liquids

Now it’s time to add the liquids that will help tenderize the pork and create the perfect braising environment. Pour in the chicken broth and apple cider vinegar, scraping the bottom of the Instant Pot once more to release any remaining bits of seasoning or browned pork. The vinegar will balance out the richness of the pork and give the dish a slight tang.

Return the seared pork shoulder to the Instant Pot, ensuring that it’s sitting in the liquid with the onions and garlic. If the pork doesn’t fit comfortably in the pot, you can cut it into smaller pieces to ensure even cooking.

Cooking the Pork

Step 5: Setting the Instant Pot

Once the pork is in the Instant Pot with the aromatics and liquid, close the lid and set the valve to “sealing.” Select the ‘Pressure Cook’ or ‘Manual’ setting and cook on high pressure for 60 minutes. Yes, you read that right – just 60 minutes of cooking time!

During this time, the Instant Pot will build up steam, and the pressure will ensure the pork cooks quickly, breaking down the connective tissue and rendering the fat. The result will be perfectly tender, juicy pulled pork that falls apart with ease.

Step 6: Natural Release

After the cooking time is complete, allow the Instant Pot to naturally release pressure for about 15 minutes. This will help the pork maintain its moisture and tenderness. Once the 15 minutes have passed, carefully release the remaining pressure manually by turning the valve to “venting.”

Step 7: Shredding the Pork

Once the pressure has fully released, open the Instant Pot and carefully remove the pork shoulder. It will be incredibly tender at this point, so using two forks, gently shred the meat. If the pork is too tough to shred, it likely needs a bit more time in the pot, so you can return it and cook it for an additional 10-15 minutes under high pressure.

While shredding the pork, you may notice a lot of delicious liquid left in the pot. You can discard some of the excess liquid or use it to moisten the pork further. Some people prefer to mix the shredded pork back into the liquid for extra flavor.

Step 8: Adding BBQ Sauce (Optional)

If you prefer to serve your pulled pork with BBQ sauce, now is the time to mix it in. Add about 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce to the shredded pork and stir it well to coat. This is optional but recommended if you want that tangy-sweet barbecue flavor.

If you’re serving the pork as-is, you can skip this step or serve the sauce on the side for guests to add to their liking.

Serving the Pulled Pork

Now that you have your pulled pork ready, it’s time to serve it up! There are plenty of ways to enjoy this flavorful dish:

Pulled Pork Sandwiches

Pile the shredded pulled pork onto soft buns, and top with your favorite condiments. Add a scoop of coleslaw, pickles, and extra BBQ sauce for a classic, mouthwatering pulled pork sandwich.

Pulled Pork Tacos

For a Tex-Mex twist, serve the pulled pork in soft corn or flour tortillas. Top with fresh cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and maybe some pickled onions or jalapeños for an extra kick.

Pulled Pork Over Rice

Serve the pulled pork over a bed of steamed rice for a comforting and hearty meal. You can even drizzle some of the cooking liquid or BBQ sauce over the top to enhance the flavor.

Pulled Pork with Sides

Pulled pork pairs beautifully with a variety of side dishes. Try it with mashed potatoes, corn on the cob, mac and cheese, or a simple green salad.

Tips for Success

  • Resting the Pork: Let the pork rest for a few minutes before shredding to retain its juices and ensure maximum tenderness.
  • Cutting the Pork: If you prefer smaller pieces of pork, feel free to cut the pork shoulder into smaller chunks before cooking. This can speed up the shredding process.
  • Extra Flavor: If you like a smoky flavor, try adding a teaspoon of smoked paprika or a few drops of liquid smoke before cooking the pork.
  • Adjusting the Cooking Time: Depending on the size of the pork shoulder, you may need to adjust the cooking time. For a 4-pound pork shoulder, 60 minutes on high pressure should be perfect, but for larger cuts, you might need to add an extra 10-15 minutes.

Conclusion

Making pulled pork in the Instant Pot is a game changer. In just 90 minutes, you can have a delicious, tender, and flavorful pulled pork that’s perfect for sandwiches, tacos, or any meal you desire. The pressure cooker ensures that the pork cooks quickly while still maintaining that melt-in-your-mouth texture. Whether you’re feeding a crowd or just looking for a quick weeknight dinner, this recipe will become a staple in your cooking routine.

Enjoy your Instant Pot pulled pork, and don’t forget to experiment with different seasonings, sauces, and toppings to make it your own!
