Bacalao al Pil Pil: A Basque Delight with Garlic and Chili


If there’s one dish that captures the essence of Basque cuisine, it’s Bacalao al Pil Pil. This iconic dish is a true delight for the senses, combining the rich flavors of salted cod with the warmth of garlic and the subtle heat of chili. Originating from the Basque region in northern Spain, Bacalao al Pil Pil has become a beloved classic that is cherished for its simplicity and depth of flavor.

The Origins of Bacalao al Pil Pil

Bacalao, or salted cod, has been a staple in Basque cuisine for centuries. The tradition of preserving cod through salting dates back to the time when fresh fish was scarce in the mountainous Basque region. Over time, this preservation method gave rise to a variety of cod-based dishes, with Bacalao al Pil Pil emerging as a standout favorite.

The name “Pil Pil” refers to the emulsified sauce that forms during the cooking process. This sauce is created by the natural gelatin in the cod, combined with olive oil, garlic, and chili, resulting in a luxurious and flavorful accompaniment to the tender cod.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To recreate this Basque delight in your own kitchen, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound (450g) salted cod
  • 1 cup (240ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • 6 cloves of garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 dried red chili, thinly sliced
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish
  • Crusty bread, for serving

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Salted Cod

Begin by soaking the salted cod in cold water for 24-48 hours, changing the water several times to remove the excess salt. Once the cod has been desalted, pat it dry with paper towels and cut it into serving-sized pieces.

Step 2: Infuse the Olive Oil

In a large, heavy-bottomed skillet, heat the olive oil over low heat. Add the sliced garlic and dried chili to the oil, allowing them to gently infuse the oil with their flavors. Be careful not to let the garlic brown; you want it to soften and release its aroma without becoming too dark.

Step 3: Cook the Cod

Once the oil is infused, add the pieces of cod to the skillet, making sure they are submerged in the flavored oil. Cook the cod over low heat, gently shaking the skillet from time to time to encourage the emulsification of the oil and the natural gelatin from the cod. This process will create the signature “pil pil” sauce – a silky, garlicky emulsion that envelops the cod in richness.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Once the cod is cooked through and the pil pil sauce has formed, remove the skillet from the heat. Sprinkle the dish with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of freshness and color. Serve the Bacalao al Pil Pil hot, accompanied by crusty bread to soak up every last bit of the flavorful sauce.

A Taste of Basque Tradition

Bacalao al Pil Pil is more than just a dish – it’s a testament to the culinary heritage of the Basque people. With its simple yet exquisite flavors and the art of emulsifying the sauce, this recipe captures the essence of traditional Basque cooking. Whether enjoyed in a bustling pintxos bar in San Sebastian or prepared in your own kitchen, Bacalao al Pil Pil offers a taste of Basque tradition that is truly unforgettable.
