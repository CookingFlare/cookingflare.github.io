Vegan Blueberry Muffins: Bursting with Flavor


If you’re a fan of blueberries and crave a delicious, plant-based treat, then these Vegan Blueberry Muffins are perfect for you! Made with wholesome ingredients and bursting with natural flavors, these muffins are a delightful addition to any breakfast or snack time. Plus, they’re incredibly easy to make! So, let’s dive right into the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup almond milk (unsweetened)
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the almond milk, coconut sugar, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.

  4. Gently fold in the fresh blueberries, being careful not to burst them.

  5. Spoon the batter evenly into the prepared muffin tin, filling each liner about three-quarters full.

  6. Bake the muffins for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  7. Remove the muffins from the oven and allow them to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Once cooled, serve and enjoy these moist, flavorful Vegan Blueberry Muffins!

Tips and Variations

  • You can swap out the fresh blueberries for frozen ones if you don’t have access to fresh berries. Just make sure to thaw and drain them before adding them to the batter.

  • Feel free to add a sprinkle of cinnamon or a handful of chopped nuts to the batter for an extra flavor boost.

  • These muffins are best enjoyed fresh, but you can store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. To keep them moist, place a slice of bread in the container with the muffins.

  • If you want to make these muffins gluten-free, you can use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend instead of regular flour. Just make sure it contains xanthan gum or add 1/2 teaspoon of xanthan gum to the recipe.

Final Thoughts

These Vegan Blueberry Muffins are a delightful treat that will satisfy your sweet tooth without compromising your dietary choices. They are moist, fluffy, and bursting with the natural flavors of juicy blueberries. Enjoy them as a quick breakfast on the go, a snack, or even as a dessert. With just a few simple ingredients and minimal effort, you can create these delicious muffins in no time. So why wait? Get baking and indulge in the goodness of Vegan Blueberry Muffins today!

Remember, you can always experiment with different flavors and ingredients to make these muffins your own. Let your creativity shine and enjoy the process of creating delicious vegan treats that will leave you craving more. Happy baking!
