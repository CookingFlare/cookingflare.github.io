A Classic French Breakfast: Croissant and Jam


Welcome to my cooking blog, where I’ll be sharing a delightful and classic French breakfast recipe: Croissant and Jam. This iconic combination is a staple in French culture and is sure to transport you to the charming streets of Paris. In this blog post, I will guide you through the process of making flaky, buttery croissants from scratch, and pairing them with a delicious homemade jam. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the croissants, you’ll need:

  • 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small cubes
  • ¾ cup whole milk, cold
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

For the jam, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups fresh berries (strawberries, raspberries, or a mix)
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

Croissants:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Mix well.

  2. Add the chilled butter cubes to the flour mixture. Using your fingertips or a pastry cutter, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Pour in the cold milk and mix until a sticky dough forms. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes, until smooth and elastic.

  4. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover with a clean kitchen towel and let it rise in a warm place for 1 to 1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.

  5. Punch down the risen dough and roll it out into a rectangle, about ¼ inch thick. Fold the rectangle into thirds, like folding a letter. Repeat this process two more times, chilling the dough in the refrigerator for 10-15 minutes between each fold.

  6. After the final fold, roll out the dough into a large rectangle, about ¼ inch thick. Cut the dough into triangles, then roll each triangle tightly, starting from the wide end, to form croissants.

  7. Place the croissants on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving some space between them. Brush the croissants with the beaten egg wash.

  8. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the croissants for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and flaky.

  9. Remove the croissants from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack before serving.

Jam:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the fresh berries, sugar, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture comes to a boil.

  2. Reduce the heat to low and let the jam simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until thickened. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. Remove the jam from heat and let it cool completely. Transfer to a sterilized jar and store in the refrigerator.

Serving Suggestions

Serve your freshly baked croissants warm, alongside a jar of homemade jam. Spread the jam generously on the croissants, allowing the sweet flavors to melt into the buttery layers. Pair it with a cup of strong coffee or a glass of freshly squeezed orange juice for a truly authentic French breakfast experience.

Conclusion

There you have it – a classic French breakfast of Croissant and Jam. By following these simple steps, you can recreate the magic of a Parisian cafe right in your own kitchen. The flaky croissants and sweet homemade jam are the perfect way to start your day with a touch of elegance. So why not treat yourself and your loved ones to this delightful breakfast? Bon appétit!
