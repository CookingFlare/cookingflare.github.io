Spicy Basil Beef: A Bold and Flavorful Thai Stir-Fry


When it comes to cooking, few cuisines are as bold and daring as Thai. With its emphasis on spicy and sour flavors, Thai food is a perfect choice for those who crave excitement and adventure in their meals. One dish that embodies the spirit of Thai cuisine is Spicy Basil Beef, a stir-fry that combines the tender sweetness of beef with the pungency of fresh basil and the fiery heat of chilies. In this recipe, we’ll explore the secrets of making this dish at home, and show you how to add a touch of Thai flair to your cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef strips (sirloin or ribeye work well)
  • 2 cups mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, and shiitake)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
  • 2 cups fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Beef

Cut the beef strips into thin slices and season with salt and pepper. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes to allow the meat to absorb the flavors.

Step 2: Cook the Beef

Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add the beef and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. Remove the beef from the wok and set it aside.

Step 3: Cook the Mushrooms and Aromatics

In the same wok, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the mixed mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Step 4: Add the Curry Paste and Chili

Add the Thai red curry paste to the wok and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the paste is fragrant and slightly darkened. Add the sliced Thai bird’s eye chilies and cook for an additional minute.

Step 5: Add the Basil and Beef

Add the fresh Thai basil leaves to the wok and cook until they start to wilt, about 1-2 minutes. Add the cooked beef back into the wok and stir to combine with the basil and curry paste.

Step 6: Add the Soy Sauce, Fish Sauce, and Brown Sugar

Add the soy sauce, fish sauce, and brown sugar to the wok and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly.

Step 7: Season and Serve

Season the dish with salt and pepper to taste. Add the chopped cilantro if desired. Serve the Spicy Basil Beef hot, garnished with additional fresh basil leaves and chilies if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the dish more substantial, add some cooked rice or noodles to the stir-fry.
  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the beef with extra-firm tofu or tempeh.
  • Experiment with different types of chilies or add some diced bell peppers to the stir-fry for added flavor and texture.
  • Make the dish more authentic by using Thai ingredients such as fish balls or shrimp paste.

Conclusion

Spicy Basil Beef is a bold and flavorful Thai stir-fry that is sure to become a staple in your cooking repertoire. With its combination of tender beef, pungent basil, and spicy chilies, this dish is a perfect choice for those who crave excitement and adventure in their meals. By following these simple steps and using fresh, high-quality ingredients, you can create a dish that is sure to impress your friends and family. So why not give it a try and add a touch of Thai flair to your cooking?
