Vegan and Vegetarian BBQ Recipes for Summer


As the weather warms up and the days get longer, there’s nothing quite like enjoying a BBQ with friends and family. However, traditional BBQ fare often leans heavily on meat, leaving vegans and vegetarians scrambling for alternatives. But fear not! The world of plant-based BBQ options is vast, vibrant, and full of flavor. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or simply looking for delicious, meatless BBQ alternatives, this guide will introduce you to a range of vegan and vegetarian BBQ recipes perfect for your summer cookouts.

1. Vegan BBQ Skewers: A Colorful and Flavorful Delight

BBQ skewers are a quintessential part of any summer BBQ, and they can be easily made vegan. These skewers feature a variety of vegetables and plant-based protein options, making them perfect for grilling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 block of firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 1 cup of pineapple chunks
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, soy sauce, maple syrup, lemon juice, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper to make the marinade.
  2. Thread the vegetables, tofu, and pineapple onto skewers, alternating the ingredients for a colorful presentation.
  3. Brush the skewers with the marinade and let them sit for at least 30 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and grill the skewers for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  5. Serve with a drizzle of extra marinade or a side of tangy BBQ sauce.

2. Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burgers: The Ultimate Meat-Free Burger

Portobello mushrooms are hearty, meaty, and perfect for grilling. When marinated and grilled, they become a delicious substitute for a classic burger patty, satisfying both vegetarians and vegans alike.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large Portobello mushroom caps
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 4 whole-grain burger buns
  • Lettuce, tomato slices, red onion rings, and pickles (for topping)
  • Vegan mayo or mustard (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, olive oil, soy sauce, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, and black pepper to create the marinade.
  2. Place the Portobello mushrooms in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, ensuring the mushrooms are well coated. Let them marinate for 20-30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the mushrooms on the grill, gill-side down, and cook for 4-5 minutes per side until tender and nicely charred.
  4. Toast the burger buns on the grill until lightly browned.
  5. Assemble the burgers by placing a grilled mushroom cap on the bottom of each bun. Add your desired toppings like lettuce, tomato, onion, and pickles, and finish with a dollop of vegan mayo or mustard.
  6. Serve these Portobello mushroom burgers with a side of crispy fries or a fresh salad.

3. Grilled Veggie Tacos: A Flavor Explosion in Every Bite

Tacos are a perfect way to enjoy the vibrant flavors of summer grilling. These vegan and vegetarian grilled veggie tacos are bursting with smoky, savory flavors that will transport your taste buds straight to taco heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ears of corn, husked
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 small red onion, sliced into rings
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salsa, guacamole, or vegan sour cream (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the corn, bell peppers, zucchini, and onion with olive oil, lime juice, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper until well coated.
  3. Place the vegetables on the grill and cook for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are tender and lightly charred.
  4. Remove the vegetables from the grill and allow them to cool slightly before chopping them into smaller pieces.
  5. Warm the corn tortillas on the grill for 1-2 minutes on each side.
  6. Fill each tortilla with the grilled vegetables, and top with fresh cilantro, salsa, guacamole, or vegan sour cream for extra flavor.
  7. Serve your grilled veggie tacos with a side of Mexican rice or a refreshing summer salad.

4. Vegan BBQ Pulled “Pork” Sandwiches

For a BBQ classic with a vegan twist, try these BBQ pulled “pork” sandwiches made with jackfruit. When cooked, jackfruit has a shredded texture that resembles pulled pork, making it an ideal meat substitute for this recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of young green jackfruit in brine, drained and shredded
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (make sure it’s vegan)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 soft hamburger buns
  • Coleslaw (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the shredded jackfruit to the skillet along with the smoked paprika, cumin, chili flakes, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and cook for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Pour in the BBQ sauce and stir until the jackfruit is fully coated. Let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the jackfruit absorbs the sauce and becomes tender.
  4. While the jackfruit is simmering, toast the hamburger buns on the grill or in a toaster.
  5. Once the jackfruit is cooked, pile it onto the bottom half of the buns and top with a generous portion of coleslaw.
  6. Place the top of the bun on and serve with a side of baked beans or a cucumber salad.

5. Grilled Cauliflower Steaks with Chimichurri Sauce

Cauliflower has become a star ingredient in many plant-based recipes, and when grilled, it takes on a beautiful smoky flavor. This dish features cauliflower steaks topped with a fresh, zesty chimichurri sauce that adds a burst of flavor with every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the chimichurri sauce:
    • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
    • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1/2 cup olive oil
    • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Slice the cauliflower into 1-inch thick steaks, being careful not to break them apart.
  3. Drizzle the cauliflower steaks with olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper, making sure they’re evenly coated.
  4. Place the cauliflower steaks on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes per side, until they are tender and nicely charred.
  5. While the cauliflower is grilling, prepare the chimichurri sauce by combining all of the ingredients in a small bowl and stirring until well mixed.
  6. Once the cauliflower steaks are grilled, drizzle them generously with the chimichurri sauce and serve immediately.
  7. These grilled cauliflower steaks pair beautifully with a side of quinoa salad or roasted sweet potatoes.

6. Vegan BBQ Ribs (Made with Seitan)

For a truly decadent BBQ experience, try making vegan BBQ ribs using seitan, which has a chewy texture that mimics the consistency of traditional ribs. With the right seasoning and BBQ sauce, these seitan ribs are a hit at any summer BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups vital wheat gluten (for the seitan)
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup BBQ sauce (vegan)
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp liquid smoke (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together vital wheat gluten, nutritional yeast, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and a pinch of salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together vegetable broth, soy sauce, tomato paste, olive oil, apple cider vinegar, and liquid smoke.
  4. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, mixing until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 3-4 minutes until it becomes elastic.
  5. Shape the dough into a rib-like structure, wrapping it in parchment paper and foil.
  6. Bake the seitan for 25-30 minutes, then unwrap it and coat it with BBQ sauce. Return the seitan to the oven and bake for another 10-15 minutes.
  7. Once the seitan is cooked, slice it into “ribs” and serve with a side of grilled corn and a tangy slaw.

Conclusion

With these vegan and vegetarian BBQ recipes, you’re ready to enjoy a summer cookout that caters to everyone. From vibrant veggie skewers to smoky grilled cauliflower steaks and hearty BBQ pulled jackfruit sandwiches, these plant-based dishes offer the perfect balance of flavors, textures, and satisfaction. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and enjoy the flavors of summer with these delicious and cruelty-free BBQ recipes!
