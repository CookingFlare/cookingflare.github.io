Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells: A Cheesy and Satisfying Pasta Bake


If you’re in the mood for a comforting and delicious pasta dish, look no further than these spinach and ricotta stuffed shells. This recipe is a perfect combination of cheesy goodness and wholesome spinach, all nestled in tender pasta shells and baked to perfection. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, this dish is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the details of creating this mouthwatering spinach and ricotta stuffed shells pasta bake.

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Shells:

  • 1 box jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Tomato Sauce:

  • 2 cans (28 ounces each) crushed tomatoes
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Garnish:

  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Additional grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven and Cook the Pasta Shells:

    • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the jumbo pasta shells according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside to cool.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, egg, chopped spinach, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.

  3. Stuff the Pasta Shells:

    • Using a spoon, carefully fill each cooked pasta shell with the spinach and ricotta mixture and place them in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.

  4. Make the Tomato Sauce:

    • In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
    • Pour in the crushed tomatoes and add the dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 10 minutes.

  5. Assemble and Bake:

    • Pour the tomato sauce over the stuffed pasta shells in the baking dish, making sure to cover them evenly.
    • Sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup of mozzarella cheese over the top.
    • Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Garnish the spinach and ricotta stuffed shells with fresh basil leaves and additional grated Parmesan cheese before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For a meaty twist, add cooked ground sausage or shredded rotisserie chicken to the spinach and ricotta mixture.
  • Experiment with different cheeses such as fontina or provolone for a unique flavor profile.
  • If you prefer a bit of heat, sprinkle red pepper flakes over the pasta shells before baking.

Conclusion

This spinach and ricotta stuffed shells pasta bake is a delightful combination of creamy ricotta, earthy spinach, and savory tomato sauce, all coming together in a satisfying and indulgent dish. Whether you’re a fan of Italian cuisine or simply crave a hearty and comforting meal, this recipe is a winner. Serve it alongside a crisp green salad and a glass of your favorite wine for a memorable dining experience. So why wait? Gather your ingredients and get ready to savor every cheesy, delicious bite of this delightful pasta bake. Your taste buds will thank you!
