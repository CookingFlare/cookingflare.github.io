Vegan Desserts for Kids: Fun Recipes They'll Love


Are you looking for delightful vegan desserts that your kids will not only enjoy but also find fun to make? Look no further! This guide is packed with a variety of delicious, plant-based recipes that are perfect for kids of all ages. These desserts are not just tasty; they are also easy to prepare, healthy, and made from wholesome ingredients. Let’s dive in!

1. Rainbow Fruit Popsicles

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of fresh fruit (strawberries, kiwi, blueberries, mango)
  • 1 cup of coconut water or fruit juice
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon of maple syrup or agave nectar for sweetness

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Fruit: Wash and chop your chosen fruits into small pieces. Aim for a variety of colors to create a visually appealing pop.
  2. Blend the Base: In a blender, combine the coconut water or fruit juice with a small amount of maple syrup if desired. Blend until smooth.
  3. Layer the Popsicles: In popsicle molds, layer the chopped fruits. Pour the blended juice mixture over the fruit layers, filling the molds.
  4. Freeze: Insert sticks into the molds and freeze for at least 4-6 hours or until solid.
  5. Serve: To remove the popsicles, run warm water over the outside of the molds for a few seconds. Enjoy these refreshing treats on a hot day!

2. Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/2 cup of cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup of maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup of almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Blend Ingredients: In a high-speed blender, combine the avocados, cocoa powder, maple syrup, almond milk, vanilla extract, and salt.
  2. Blend Until Smooth: Blend until the mixture is creamy and free of lumps. Taste and adjust sweetness if necessary.
  3. Chill: Transfer the mousse into individual serving bowls and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to let it set.
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh berries or a sprinkle of cocoa powder before serving. This rich dessert will have your kids asking for seconds!

3. No-Bake Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup of smooth peanut butter (or any nut butter)
  • 1/4 cup of maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup of cocoa powder (optional)
  • 1/4 cup of shredded coconut (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix together the rolled oats, peanut butter, maple syrup, cocoa powder, shredded coconut, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  2. Form Cookies: Using your hands, form small balls of the mixture and flatten them into cookie shapes. Place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Chill: Refrigerate the cookies for about 30 minutes or until firm.
  4. Enjoy: These cookies are not only easy to make but also pack a nutritious punch!

4. Banana Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Muffins

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1 cup of rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup of almond milk
  • 1/4 cup of maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup of dairy-free chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a bowl, combine the mashed bananas, almond milk, and maple syrup.
  3. Combine Dry Ingredients: In another bowl, mix the rolled oats, baking powder, and cinnamon.
  4. Combine Mixtures: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Fill Muffin Tins: Spoon the batter into the muffin liners, filling each about 3/4 full.
  6. Bake: Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool and Serve: Let them cool for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack. These muffins are perfect for breakfast or a midday snack!

5. Coconut Chia Seed Pudding

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of chia seeds
  • 2 cups of coconut milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup of maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruit for topping (mango, berries, or banana)

Instructions

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the chia seeds, coconut milk, maple syrup, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  2. Let it Sit: Cover the bowl and let it sit for about 10 minutes. Stir the mixture again to prevent clumping.
  3. Refrigerate: Transfer the pudding to individual serving containers and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Serve: Top with fresh fruit before serving. This refreshing dessert is rich in omega-3 fatty acids and fiber!

6. Apple Nachos with Caramel Dip

Ingredients

  • 2 large apples, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of almond butter or peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons of maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup of granola
  • Optional toppings: raisins, shredded coconut, or mini dairy-free chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Prepare Apples: Slice the apples into thin wedges and arrange them on a large plate.
  2. Make Caramel Dip: In a small bowl, mix together the almond butter and maple syrup until smooth.
  3. Drizzle and Top: Drizzle the caramel dip over the apple slices. Sprinkle granola and any additional toppings of your choice.
  4. Serve: Enjoy this fun and healthy snack that’s perfect for sharing!

Conclusion

These vegan dessert recipes are not only simple and fun to make, but they also introduce kids to healthy eating habits without sacrificing flavor. Whether for a birthday party, a family gathering, or just a weekend treat, these desserts are sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. Encourage your children to get involved in the kitchen and let their creativity shine as they whip up these delightful vegan treats. Happy cooking!
