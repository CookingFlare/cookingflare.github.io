Savory Herb Fougasse Bread: A French Classic


The mouth-watering, fragrant aroma of freshly baked bread has a unique way of capturing our senses. Fougasse bread, a French classic, is an exquisite addition to every food lover’s palate. With its crispy exterior, soft interior, and delectable blend of herbs, this savory bread will leave you craving more. In this post, we’ll explore the art of crafting the perfect fougasse bread, packed with aromatic herbs, and accentuating the flavor of each component.

Ingredients: • 3 cups all-purpose flour • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast • Milk and water (_ratio depends on the dough’s consistency, about 1 cup) • 2 tablespoons olive oil • Salt (to taste) • Herbs of your choice (rosemary, thyme, oregano, etc.) • A pinch of black pepper • A handful of grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. First, let’s prepare the dough. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour and active dry yeast. Gradually add milk and water, mixing constantly until you achieve a smooth and elastic consistency. Follow up with olive oil, salt, and herbs of choice. Let the mixture rest for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld, and the dough to start fermenting.
  2. With a dusted work surface, turn out the dough. Knead for a few minutes more, ensuring a well-formed ball.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rise for about an hour, or until doubled in size.
  4. Once your dough has risen, care is taken to roll out the dough carefully. Uniform thickness is essential for that perfect French fougasse.
  5. With the olive oil, lightly brush over the dough and add the herbs of choice, along with a pinch or two of black pepper.
  6. If desired, sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese lightly over the herb-specked surface.
  7. Let your bread rest for 20-30 minutes, lightly covered. This step is critical as it allows the cheese and herbs to mingle harmoniously, without the detrimental effect of baking.
  8. Preheat the oven to 425°F (218°C) with a baking stone or baking sheet inside to help replicate the authentic French bakery style. With the dough on a piece of parchment paper, carefully transfer it to the hot stone or sheet.
  9. Bake for approximately 15-20 minutes or until the fougasse is a rich golden brown. Baking time may vary depending on your oven.
  10. Once cooked, remove your wonderful creation from the oven, and allow it to cool slightly. While still warm, score the bread with a zigzag pattern or any design of your choice but don’t cut entirely through the bread. This allows steam to escape during baking, resulting in an evenly baked fougasse.
  11. Allow the fougasse to cool to room temperature before serving. For the best experience, serve your freshly baked savory herb fougasse bread with a glass of crisp white wine.

Experiencing and appreciating the art of making French fougasse bread is an adventure in itself. The scents of herbs, combined with the golden, crispy exterior, and a warm, soft interior, takes one on a delicious journey - a testament to the culinary heritage of France. Bon appétit!
