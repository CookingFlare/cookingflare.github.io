Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Butter and Parmesan Cheese: A Halloween Dinner Recipe


As the leaves turn vibrant shades of red and gold and the air grows crisp, the season of Halloween ushers in a delightful opportunity to indulge in comforting, festive meals. One dish that perfectly encapsulates the essence of autumn is Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Butter and Parmesan Cheese. This recipe combines the earthy sweetness of pumpkin with the nutty richness of Parmesan and the aromatic warmth of sage, creating a comforting dish that’s ideal for a Halloween dinner.

Why Pumpkin Ravioli?

Pumpkin ravioli is not just a feast for the palate but also a visual treat. Its rich orange hue is reminiscent of fall, making it a perfect centerpiece for your Halloween table. The dish is versatile, allowing you to serve it as a main course or as a starter. The combination of flavors is both sophisticated and comforting, making it a crowd-pleaser for family gatherings or intimate dinners.

Ingredients

For the ravioli dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Water (as needed)

For the pumpkin filling:

  • 1 cup pureed pumpkin (canned or freshly roasted)
  • 1/4 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the sage butter sauce:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 10-12 fresh sage leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly grated Parmesan for serving

Equipment Needed

  • Rolling pin or pasta machine
  • Mixing bowls
  • Fork
  • Baking sheet
  • Pot for boiling ravioli
  • Skillet for making the sauce

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Make the Ravioli Dough

  1. Prepare the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the eggs and olive oil. Using a fork, gradually mix the flour into the egg mixture until a rough dough forms.

  2. Knead the dough: Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour; if it’s too dry, add a few drops of water.

  3. Rest the dough: Shape the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and let it rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature. This resting period allows the gluten to relax, making it easier to roll out.

Step 2: Prepare the Pumpkin Filling

  1. Combine ingredients: In a medium-sized bowl, mix the pureed pumpkin, ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan, nutmeg, cinnamon, salt, and pepper until well blended. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

  2. Chill the filling: Cover the filling and refrigerate it while you roll out the dough. Chilling the filling helps it firm up, making it easier to handle.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

  1. Divide the dough: After resting, cut the dough into four equal portions. Keep the portions you’re not currently using covered to prevent them from drying out.

  2. Roll the dough: On a floured surface, roll out one portion of the dough into a thin sheet (about 1/16 inch thick). If using a pasta machine, start with the widest setting and gradually work your way to the thinnest.

  3. Cut the dough: Using a sharp knife or a pasta cutter, cut the sheet into squares or circles, approximately 3 inches in size.

Step 4: Assemble the Ravioli

  1. Fill the ravioli: Place about a teaspoon of the pumpkin filling in the center of each dough square or circle.

  2. Seal the ravioli: Moisten the edges of the dough with a little water. Fold the dough over the filling to create a pocket, pressing down to seal. Ensure there are no air pockets, as this can cause the ravioli to burst during cooking.

  3. Prepare for cooking: Place the sealed ravioli on a floured baking sheet and cover with a clean kitchen towel while you assemble the remaining ravioli.

Step 5: Cook the Ravioli

  1. Boil water: Fill a large pot with water, add a generous pinch of salt, and bring it to a rolling boil.

  2. Cook the ravioli: Carefully drop the ravioli into the boiling water, working in batches if necessary. Cook for about 3-4 minutes or until they float to the surface and are tender.

  3. Drain the ravioli: Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked ravioli to a plate, tossing them lightly with a little olive oil to prevent sticking.

Step 6: Prepare the Sage Butter Sauce

  1. Melt the butter: In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the unsalted butter until it begins to foam.

  2. Add the sage: Add the fresh sage leaves to the melted butter. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, allowing the butter to brown slightly and the sage to become crispy. Be careful not to burn the butter; you want a nutty aroma.

  3. Combine the ravioli and sauce: Gently add the cooked ravioli to the skillet and toss to coat them in the sage butter sauce. Season with salt to taste.

Step 7: Serve

  1. Plate the ravioli: Divide the ravioli among serving plates.

  2. Garnish: Finish with a generous sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a few extra crispy sage leaves, if desired.

  3. Enjoy: Serve immediately and savor the rich, comforting flavors of this seasonal dish.

Tips for Success

  • Fresh vs. Canned Pumpkin: If you opt for fresh pumpkin, roast it until tender, then puree it for the filling. Canned pumpkin is a convenient and flavorful alternative.

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the dough and filling a day in advance. Store the dough wrapped in plastic in the refrigerator and the filling in an airtight container. Just roll out and assemble the ravioli when you’re ready.

  • Freezing Ravioli: If you want to make a large batch, freeze the uncooked ravioli on a baking sheet until firm, then transfer them to a freezer bag. Cook from frozen, adding a minute or two to the boiling time.

  • Pairing Suggestions: This dish pairs beautifully with a crisp white wine, such as Pinot Grigio or Chardonnay, enhancing the flavors of the pumpkin and sage.

Conclusion

Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Butter and Parmesan Cheese is a delightful dish that captures the essence of Halloween and autumn. Its combination of flavors and textures makes it a perfect choice for a cozy dinner at home or a festive gathering with friends and family. With its beautiful presentation and comforting taste, this recipe is sure to become a seasonal favorite. So, roll up your sleeves, embrace the spirit of the season, and treat yourself and your loved ones to this delicious Halloween dinner recipe. Happy cooking!
