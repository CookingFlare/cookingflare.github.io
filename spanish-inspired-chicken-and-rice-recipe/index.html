Spanish Inspired Chicken and Rice Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the warmth and heartiness of a well-prepared chicken and rice meal. The Spanish-inspired chicken and rice recipe takes this classic combination and elevates it with vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and a colorful presentation that would make any dinner table shine. Whether you are hosting a family gathering, preparing a cozy weeknight dinner, or just craving a taste of Spain, this dish is sure to impress.

Ingredients

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s gather all the ingredients that will bring this Spanish-inspired dish to life. The following list will serve approximately four to six servings.

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 4 chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on for added flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Juice of 1 lemon

For the Rice and Vegetables:

  • 1 1/2 cups long-grain rice (such as Basmati or Jasmine)
  • 4 cups chicken broth (homemade or low-sodium)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes (with juices)
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads (optional but recommended for authenticity)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Optional Add-ins:

  • 1/2 cup green olives, sliced
  • 1/2 cup cooked chorizo, diced (for an extra kick)

Cooking Instructions

Now that we have our ingredients ready, it’s time to embark on this flavorful culinary journey. Follow these steps to create a delightful Spanish-inspired chicken and rice dish that will transport your taste buds straight to the sun-soaked streets of Spain.

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and lemon juice.
  2. Add the chicken thighs to the bowl, ensuring they are well-coated with the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for maximum flavor infusion.

Step 2: Sear the Chicken

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or paella pan, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Once the oil is shimmering, add the marinated chicken thighs skin-side down. Sear them for about 5-7 minutes until the skin is golden brown and crispy.
  3. Flip the chicken thighs and sear the other side for an additional 5 minutes. Once seared, remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.

Step 3: Sauté the Vegetables

  1. In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium and add the chopped onion, red bell pepper, and green bell pepper. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables become soft and translucent.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, stirring frequently to prevent burning.

Step 4: Add the Rice and Spices

  1. Stir in the rice, ensuring it gets coated with the vegetable mixture. Toast the rice for about 2-3 minutes, allowing it to absorb the flavors.
  2. Add the can of diced tomatoes (with juices), chicken broth, saffron threads, ground cumin, dried oregano, and bay leaf. Stir to combine all ingredients, bringing the mixture to a gentle boil.

Step 5: Return the Chicken to the Skillet

  1. Nestle the seared chicken thighs back into the skillet, skin-side up. Ensure they are partially submerged in the rice and broth mixture.
  2. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet with a lid, and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the rice is tender and has absorbed most of the liquid. Avoid removing the lid during this time to retain steam.

Step 6: Add the Peas and Optional Ingredients

  1. In the last 5 minutes of cooking, sprinkle the frozen peas over the dish. If using, also add the sliced olives and cooked chorizo at this stage.
  2. Cover again and allow the heat to finish cooking the peas and warming the olives and chorizo.

Step 7: Fluff and Serve

  1. Once the rice is cooked, remove the skillet from the heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 5 minutes. This resting period helps the flavors meld together.
  2. Remove the bay leaf and fluff the rice with a fork. Adjust the seasoning to taste, adding more salt or pepper if needed.
  3. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley before serving.

Tips for Success

  • Quality Ingredients Matter: Use good quality chicken and fresh vegetables for the best flavor. If possible, opt for organic or free-range chicken.
  • Saffron for Authenticity: While saffron is optional, it adds a distinctive flavor and beautiful color to the dish. If saffron is unavailable, consider using turmeric as a substitute.
  • Customize to Your Taste: Feel free to add your favorite vegetables or proteins. Shrimp or fish can be excellent additions for a seafood twist.
  • Leftovers and Storage: This dish stores well in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove with a splash of broth to prevent it from drying out.

Pairing Suggestions

To complement your Spanish-inspired chicken and rice, consider serving it with a side of:

  • Spanish Salad: A refreshing mix of tomatoes, cucumbers, olives, and onions dressed with olive oil and vinegar.
  • Crusty Bread: A loaf of crusty bread is perfect for soaking up the delicious juices.
  • Wine Selection: Pair the meal with a glass of chilled Spanish white wine, such as Albariño, or a light red like Garnacha.

Conclusion

This Spanish-inspired chicken and rice recipe is more than just a meal; it’s an experience that brings people together around the dinner table. With its rich flavors, vibrant colors, and comforting texture, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether enjoyed on a special occasion or as a comforting weeknight dinner, the warmth of this dish echoes the spirit of Spanish cuisine. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the aromas of this delicious dish fill your kitchen. ¡Buen provecho!
