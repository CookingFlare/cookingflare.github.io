The Art of Making Sourdough Bagels: A Guide


Have you ever craved a chewy, flavorful bagel that boasts a hint of tangy sourdough? Look no further, because today, we’re delving into the captivating world of sourdough bagels. These delightful treats are a perfect fusion of the classic bagel and the distinct sourdough flavor, offering a unique and satisfying experience for your taste buds. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the art of making sourdough bagels from scratch, providing step-by-step instructions, tips, and tricks to ensure your bagels turn out perfectly every time.

Ingredients

For the Sourdough Starter

  • 1/2 cup (60g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) water
  • 2 tablespoons (30g) active sourdough starter

For the Bagel Dough

  • 3 1/2 cups (420g) bread flour
  • 1 cup (240ml) water
  • 1/4 cup (60g) sugar
  • 2 teaspoons (10g) salt
  • 1 cup (240g) active sourdough starter
  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) olive oil

For Boiling and Topping

  • 2 quarts (2 liters) water
  • 1/4 cup (60g) honey
  • Everything bagel seasoning, sesame seeds, or poppy seeds for topping

Instructions

Day 1: Prepare the Sourdough Starter

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, water, and active sourdough starter. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  2. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let it sit at room temperature for 8-12 hours, or until the mixture becomes bubbly and doubles in size.

Day 2: Make the Bagel Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, water, sugar, salt, active sourdough starter, and olive oil. Mix until a rough dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 10-15 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  3. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a kitchen towel, and let it rise at room temperature for 3-4 hours, or until it doubles in size.

Shaping and Boiling

  1. Once the dough has doubled in size, punch it down and divide it into 8 equal portions.
  2. Roll each portion into a ball, then use your finger to poke a hole through the center and stretch it into a bagel shape.
  3. Place the shaped bagels on a parchment-lined baking sheet, cover them with a kitchen towel, and let them rise for 1 hour.
  4. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the honey to the boiling water.
  5. Boil the bagels in the water for 1-2 minutes on each side, then return them to the baking sheet.

Baking

  1. Sprinkle your desired toppings onto the boiled bagels.
  2. Bake the bagels in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until they turn golden brown.

Tips for Success

  • Use a ripe and active sourdough starter for the best flavor and rise.
  • Don’t skip the boiling step, as it gives the bagels their signature chewy texture and shiny crust.
  • Experiment with different toppings and flavor combinations to customize your sourdough bagels.

Now that you’ve mastered the art of making sourdough bagels, it’s time to savor the fruits of your labor. Whether you enjoy them toasted with cream cheese, as a sandwich base, or simply on their own, these sourdough bagels are sure to elevate your culinary repertoire. Embrace the process, get creative with flavors, and delight in the joy of baking your own delicious sourdough bagels. Happy baking!
