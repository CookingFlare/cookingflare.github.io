Veggie Delights: 4 Marinades for Grilled Portobello Mushrooms


Grilled portobello mushrooms are a vegetarian delight that can satisfy even the most carnivorous cravings. These meaty mushrooms are the perfect canvas for a variety of flavors, making them a versatile option for any meal. In this blog post, we will explore four delicious marinades that will elevate your grilled portobello mushrooms to new heights. So fire up the grill and let’s get cooking!

1. Balsamic Glazed Portobello

The sweet and tangy flavors of balsamic vinegar create a delightful glaze for your grilled portobello mushrooms. To prepare this marinade, whisk together the following ingredients:

  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Place the portobello mushrooms in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Allow the mushrooms to marinate for at least 30 minutes, turning them occasionally to ensure even coating. Grill the mushrooms over medium heat for about 5-7 minutes per side, until they are tender and nicely charred. Serve with a drizzle of the remaining marinade.

2. Asian-inspired Teriyaki Portobello

For an Asian twist on your grilled portobello mushrooms, try this mouthwatering teriyaki marinade. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced

Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Place the portobello mushrooms in a ziplock bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal the bag and let the mushrooms marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour. Grill the mushrooms over medium-high heat for about 4-5 minutes per side, until they are tender and caramelized. Sprinkle with sliced green onions before serving.

3. Mediterranean Herb Portobello

Transport your taste buds to the Mediterranean with this aromatic herb marinade. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Place the portobello mushrooms in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Let the mushrooms marinate for at least 20 minutes, turning them occasionally. Grill the mushrooms over medium heat for about 5 minutes per side, until they are tender and infused with the Mediterranean flavors. Serve with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.

4. Smoky Chipotle Portobello

If you crave a bold and smoky flavor, this chipotle marinade is perfect for your grilled portobello mushrooms. Gather the following ingredients:

  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, minced
  • 2 tablespoons adobo sauce (from the chipotle peppers can)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a small bowl, combine the minced chipotle peppers, adobo sauce, olive oil, lime juice, smoked paprika, ground cumin, salt, and pepper. Place the portobello mushrooms in a ziplock bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal the bag and let the mushrooms marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours. Grill the mushrooms over medium-high heat for about 4-5 minutes per side, until they are tender and have a delicious smoky char. Serve with a sprinkle of fresh lime juice.

Final Thoughts

Grilled portobello mushrooms are a true veggie delight, and these four marinades will take them to the next level. Whether you prefer the tangy sweetness of balsamic glaze, the Asian-inspired flavors of teriyaki, the aromatic herbs of the Mediterranean, or the bold smokiness of chipotle, there’s a marinade here for everyone. So next time you’re firing up the grill, don’t forget to try one of these marinades for a truly unforgettable grilled portobello experience. Happy cooking!
