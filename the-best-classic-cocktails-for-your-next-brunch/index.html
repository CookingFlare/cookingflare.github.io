The Best Classic Cocktails for Your Next Brunch


Brunch is a special occasion that requires a special drink to match its unique blend of breakfast and lunch. While there are countless cocktails to choose from, some classic drinks stand out as the perfect accompaniment to your eggs, pancakes, and waffles. In this article, we’ll explore the best classic cocktails to serve at your next brunch.

1. Bloody Mary

The Bloody Mary is a classic brunch cocktail that’s both savory and tangy. Made with vodka, tomato juice, and various mixers, this drink is a staple at many brunch gatherings. To make a Bloody Mary, combine 1 1/2 ounces of vodka with 4 ounces of tomato juice, 1/2 ounce of freshly squeezed lemon juice, 1/2 ounce of grenadine, and a dash of hot sauce in a shaker filled with ice. Strain the mixture into a chilled glass filled with ice and garnish with a celery stalk, a slice of lemon, and a cherry tomato.

2. Mimosa

The Mimosa is another classic brunch cocktail that’s easy to make and always a crowd-pleaser. Made with champagne and orange juice, this drink is a simple yet elegant addition to any brunch spread. To make a Mimosa, combine 1 1/2 ounces of orange juice with 4 ounces of champagne in a chilled glass. Garnish with a slice of orange and a sprig of mint.

3. Bellini

The Bellini is a classic Italian cocktail made with peach puree and Prosecco. This sweet and fruity drink is perfect for spring and summer brunches. To make a Bellini, combine 1 1/2 ounces of peach puree with 4 ounces of Prosecco in a chilled glass. Garnish with a slice of peach.

4. French 75

The French 75 is a classic cocktail made with gin, lemon juice, sugar, and champagne. This refreshing drink is perfect for hot summer days and pairs well with a variety of brunch dishes. To make a French 75, combine 1 1/2 ounces of gin with 1/2 ounce of freshly squeezed lemon juice, 1/2 ounce of simple syrup, and 4 ounces of champagne in a shaker filled with ice. Strain the mixture into a chilled glass and garnish with a lemon twist.

5. Mary Pickford

The Mary Pickford is a classic cocktail made with gin, orange juice, and grenadine. This sweet and tangy drink is perfect for brunch gatherings and pairs well with a variety of dishes. To make a Mary Pickford, combine 1 1/2 ounces of gin with 2 ounces of orange juice and 1/2 ounce of grenadine in a shaker filled with ice. Strain the mixture into a chilled glass and garnish with a slice of orange and a cherry.

6. Screwdriver

The Screwdriver is a classic cocktail made with vodka and orange juice. This simple yet refreshing drink is perfect for brunch gatherings and pairs well with a variety of dishes. To make a Screwdriver, combine 1 1/2 ounces of vodka with 4 ounces of orange juice in a chilled glass. Garnish with a slice of orange.

7. Sidecar

The Sidecar is a classic cocktail made with cognac, Cointreau, and lemon juice. This strong and tangy drink is perfect for brunch gatherings and pairs well with a variety of dishes. To make a Sidecar, combine 1 1/2 ounces of cognac with 1/2 ounce of Cointreau and 1/2 ounce of freshly squeezed lemon juice in a shaker filled with ice. Strain the mixture into a chilled glass and garnish with a lemon twist.

8. Harvey Wallbanger

The Harvey Wallbanger is a classic cocktail made with vodka, orange juice, and Galliano. This sweet and tangy drink is perfect for brunch gatherings and pairs well with a variety of dishes. To make a Harvey Wallbanger, combine 1 1/2 ounces of vodka with 2 ounces of orange juice and 1/2 ounce of Galliano in a shaker filled with ice. Strain the mixture into a chilled glass and garnish with a slice of orange.

In conclusion, these classic cocktails are sure to be a hit at your next brunch gathering. Whether you’re looking for something sweet and fruity or strong and tangy, there’s a cocktail on this list that’s sure to please. So go ahead and get mixing, and don’t forget to have fun and be creative with your cocktail-making!
