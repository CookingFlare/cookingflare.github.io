How to Make Spanish Stuffed Peppers: A Flavorful Recipe


Spanish stuffed peppers, or “pimientos rellenos,” are a delightful dish that combines vibrant flavors with a satisfying texture. This recipe will guide you step-by-step to create these colorful delights that are perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re looking to impress guests at a dinner party or simply want a comforting meal at home, these stuffed peppers are sure to please.

Ingredients

For the Peppers:

  • 4 large bell peppers (red, yellow, or green)
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup cooked rice (white or brown)
  • 1 pound ground beef or turkey (or a vegetarian substitute)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a blend)
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped, for garnish

For the Sauce:

  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation

Step 1: Prepare the Peppers

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Wash the bell peppers. Cut the tops off and remove the seeds and membranes. Keep the tops for later use if you desire.
  3. Drizzle the peppers with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place them upright in a baking dish.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the ground meat to the skillet, breaking it apart with a spatula. Cook until the meat is browned and cooked through, about 7-10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, cooked rice, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, and half of the shredded cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well and let it simmer for 5 minutes until heated through.

Step 3: Stuff the Peppers

  1. Carefully spoon the filling into each bell pepper, packing it tightly but leaving a little space at the top.
  2. Place the tops back on the peppers if desired, or leave them open for a cheesier finish.
  3. Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top of each stuffed pepper.

Step 4: Prepare the Sauce

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the tomato sauce over medium heat. Stir in olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes.

Step 5: Bake the Stuffed Peppers

  1. Pour the tomato sauce evenly over the stuffed peppers in the baking dish.
  2. Cover the dish with aluminum foil to keep the moisture in during baking.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is bubbly and golden.

Step 6: Serve

  1. Remove the stuffed peppers from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.
  2. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro before serving. These peppers can be served as a main dish or as a side alongside a salad or crusty bread.

Tips for Perfect Stuffed Peppers

  • Choose the Right Peppers: While bell peppers are traditional, feel free to experiment with other varieties like poblano or Anaheim for a different flavor profile.
  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the filling ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator. Stuff the peppers just before baking for a quick meal.
  • Add Some Heat: If you like a little spice, add some diced jalapeños or a pinch of cayenne to the filling.
  • Vegetarian Option: Substitute the meat with lentils, quinoa, or a mix of your favorite vegetables for a delicious vegetarian version.

Nutritional Benefits

Spanish stuffed peppers are not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Bell peppers are high in vitamins A and C, antioxidants, and dietary fiber. By adding lean protein and whole grains like rice, this dish becomes a balanced meal that satisfies hunger while providing essential nutrients.

Pairing Suggestions

To complement your Spanish stuffed peppers, consider serving them with:

  • A crisp green salad drizzled with a light vinaigrette.
  • A side of Spanish rice or quinoa for added texture.
  • Fresh crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce.

Conclusion

Spanish stuffed peppers are a versatile and flavorful dish that can be adapted to suit various tastes and dietary needs. With their vibrant colors and rich flavors, they are sure to become a favorite in your home. Follow this recipe, and you’ll be on your way to creating a mouthwatering meal that’s as pleasing to the eye as it is to the palate. Enjoy your culinary adventure, and don’t forget to share your stuffed pepper creations with friends and family!
