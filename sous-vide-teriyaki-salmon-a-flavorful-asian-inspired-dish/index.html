Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon: A Flavorful Asian-Inspired Dish

3 min · 616 words

If you’re looking for a dish that combines the delicate flavors of salmon with the bold, umami-rich taste of teriyaki sauce, then look no further. Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon is the perfect fusion of Asian-inspired flavors and modern cooking techniques. With the help of sous vide, you can achieve perfectly cooked, tender salmon every time, while the teriyaki sauce adds a burst of flavor that will leave your taste buds wanting more.

The Magic of Sous Vide Cooking

Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking method that involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a temperature-controlled water bath. This technique ensures even cooking throughout the entire piece of salmon, resulting in a moist and tender texture that is hard to achieve through other cooking methods.

The Teriyaki Sauce

Teriyaki sauce is a staple in Japanese cuisine, known for its combination of salty, sweet, and savory flavors. The sauce is made from a mixture of soy sauce, mirin (a sweet rice wine), sake, sugar, and ginger. It’s important to marinate the salmon in the teriyaki sauce before cooking to allow the flavors to penetrate the fish.

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, and grated ginger to make the teriyaki sauce.
  2. Place the salmon fillets in a zip-top bag and pour the teriyaki sauce over them. Seal the bag, removing as much air as possible.
  3. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 130°F (54°C).
  4. Submerge the sealed bag of salmon in the water bath and cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour for medium-rare salmon or 1 to 2 hours for well-done salmon.
  5. Once the salmon is cooked, carefully remove it from the bag and pat it dry with a paper towel.
  6. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and add a small amount of oil.
  7. Sear the salmon fillets, skin-side down, for about 1 minute or until the skin is crispy.
  8. Serve the Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon hot with steamed rice and your favorite vegetables.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, garnish the salmon with sesame seeds and chopped green onions before serving.
  • If you prefer a sweeter teriyaki sauce, you can add a tablespoon of honey or maple syrup to the marinade.
  • You can also experiment with different marinades or glazes to customize the flavors of your salmon. Try adding some garlic, chili flakes, or even a splash of citrus juice for a unique twist.

Conclusion

Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon is a dish that brings together the best of both worlds—tender, perfectly cooked salmon and a flavorful, Asian-inspired teriyaki sauce. The sous vide technique ensures that the salmon is cooked to perfection, while the teriyaki sauce adds a delightful burst of flavor. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can create a restaurant-quality dish in the comfort of your own kitchen. So why not give it a try and impress your family and friends with this mouthwatering dish?
