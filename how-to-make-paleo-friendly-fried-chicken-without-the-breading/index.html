How to Make Paleo-Friendly Fried Chicken Without the Breading


Fried chicken is a beloved comfort food, often associated with crispy, golden breading and juicy meat inside. However, for those following the paleo diet, traditional fried chicken might not be the best option. This is because the breading commonly used in fried chicken typically contains grains and processed ingredients, which are not part of the paleo lifestyle.

But fear not! You can still enjoy the crispy, savory goodness of fried chicken while adhering to paleo principles. In this post, we’ll guide you through the steps to make delicious, paleo-friendly fried chicken without the breading. This recipe is easy to prepare, uses natural ingredients, and results in a crispy, flavorful chicken that will satisfy your cravings.

Why Go Paleo-Friendly?

Before diving into the recipe, let’s briefly touch on the importance of sticking to a paleo-friendly approach. The paleo diet focuses on whole, nutrient-dense foods that our ancestors would have eaten. This includes high-quality meats, seafood, fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds while avoiding grains, legumes, and dairy.

Traditional fried chicken often relies on flour or breadcrumbs for the crispy coating, but these ingredients are not paleo-approved. To create a more nutritious and grain-free alternative, we’ll make use of ingredients like almond flour, coconut flour, or even crushed nuts that mimic the crispy texture without compromising the essence of the dish.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this paleo-friendly fried chicken, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Chicken:

  • Bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces – This could include thighs, drumsticks, or breasts. Bone-in, skin-on chicken helps to retain moisture and flavor during frying. The skin also adds a crispy texture that enhances the overall experience.

Marinade:

  • Buttermilk or coconut milk – For a paleo-friendly version, coconut milk is an excellent substitute for dairy-based buttermilk. It helps tenderize the chicken and infuses a subtle coconut flavor.
  • Apple cider vinegar – This helps mimic the acidity of buttermilk and adds flavor to the chicken.
  • Garlic powder – For an aromatic kick.
  • Onion powder – Adds a mild onion flavor to the marinade.
  • Paprika – Adds color and a slight smokiness to the chicken.
  • Cayenne pepper (optional) – For a touch of heat.

Dry Coating:

  • Almond flour – This is a fantastic grain-free option for creating a crisp exterior. It’s low in carbs, rich in healthy fats, and adds a slight nutty flavor.
  • Coconut flour – This flour has a light texture and is great for binding the coating.
  • Ground flaxseeds or chia seeds – These can add extra crunch to the coating while also contributing fiber and omega-3 fatty acids.
  • Salt and pepper – To taste, for seasoning.

Frying:

  • Coconut oil or avocado oil – These oils are stable at high temperatures, making them ideal for frying.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Chicken for Marinating

The first step in making paleo-friendly fried chicken is to marinate your chicken. Marinating helps tenderize the meat and infuse flavor throughout the chicken. Start by patting your chicken pieces dry with paper towels to remove any excess moisture. This ensures the marinade adheres well to the chicken.

In a large bowl or zip-top bag, combine your coconut milk (or dairy-free milk of your choice), apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and cayenne pepper if you’re using it. Stir to combine all the ingredients.

Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, making sure each piece is fully coated. Seal the bag or cover the bowl and place it in the refrigerator for at least 1-2 hours, or preferably overnight. The longer the chicken marinates, the more tender and flavorful it will become.

2. Prepare the Dry Coating

While the chicken is marinating, you can prepare the dry coating mixture. In a large shallow dish or bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut flour, ground flaxseeds (or chia seeds), salt, and pepper. You can adjust the amount of salt and pepper to suit your taste. If you like your fried chicken extra crispy, you can even add a bit of extra almond flour or coconut flour to create more coating.

The flaxseeds or chia seeds will add extra crunch and nutrition to the coating, while the almond and coconut flours provide a crispy texture that’s close to what you would expect from traditional fried chicken.

3. Coat the Chicken

Once your chicken has finished marinating, remove it from the refrigerator and allow it to come to room temperature for about 10-15 minutes. This step helps the chicken cook more evenly.

Take each piece of chicken and dredge it in the dry coating mixture, pressing the coating onto the chicken to ensure it sticks. The coconut milk marinade will help the dry mixture adhere without the need for egg or breadcrumbs. Make sure the chicken is well-coated on all sides.

4. Heat the Oil

Next, it’s time to fry the chicken. In a large skillet, heat about 1-2 inches of coconut oil or avocado oil over medium-high heat. The oil should reach a temperature of about 350°F (175°C). To test if the oil is hot enough, drop a small amount of the dry coating mixture into the oil – if it sizzles, the oil is ready.

If you don’t have a thermometer, you can also test the oil by dropping a small piece of chicken into it. If it starts to bubble and sizzle immediately, the oil is ready for frying.

5. Fry the Chicken

Carefully place the coated chicken pieces into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the chicken in batches if necessary. Let the chicken cook undisturbed for about 6-8 minutes on one side until the coating turns golden brown and crispy. Flip the chicken over and cook for another 6-8 minutes on the other side, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

If you’re using larger pieces of chicken like thighs or drumsticks, it may take a bit longer for them to cook through. If needed, lower the heat slightly to ensure the chicken cooks evenly without burning the coating.

6. Drain and Rest

Once the chicken is fully cooked, carefully remove the pieces from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving to allow the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and flavorful bite.

7. Serve and Enjoy

Now that your paleo-friendly fried chicken is ready, it’s time to enjoy! Serve it with your favorite side dishes, such as roasted vegetables, a fresh salad, or cauliflower rice for a complete meal. You can also dip the chicken in a homemade paleo-friendly sauce, such as a creamy avocado dip or a tangy mustard sauce, for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Paleo Fried Chicken

  • Don’t rush the frying process. Frying chicken at the right temperature is key to achieving that golden, crispy coating. Make sure the oil stays at a consistent temperature throughout the frying process. If the oil is too hot, the chicken may burn on the outside while remaining raw on the inside. If the oil is too cool, the coating might become soggy.

  • Use high-quality oil. Coconut oil and avocado oil are both excellent choices for frying because they have high smoke points and are rich in healthy fats. Avoid using vegetable or canola oil, as they are highly processed and not ideal for paleo cooking.

  • Let the chicken rest. After frying, it’s important to let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving. This helps to lock in moisture and prevents the chicken from becoming dry.

  • Experiment with flavors. Feel free to get creative with the seasoning in the marinade or the dry coating. Add herbs like thyme, rosemary, or oregano for an extra layer of flavor. You can also swap the paprika for smoked paprika or try adding a pinch of cumin or turmeric to the dry coating for a unique twist.

  • Bake for a healthier version. If you want to skip the frying process entirely, you can bake the coated chicken in the oven. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and bake the chicken on a wire rack over a baking sheet for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the coating is crispy.

Final Thoughts

This paleo-friendly fried chicken recipe is the perfect solution for anyone looking to enjoy the crispy, savory goodness of fried chicken without compromising on health or sticking to paleo principles. The lack of breading doesn’t mean you have to miss out on flavor or texture. With the right ingredients and techniques, you can create a dish that is both satisfying and nutritious.

By following these simple steps, you can easily make fried chicken that fits your paleo lifestyle. Whether you’re preparing a family dinner, meal prepping for the week, or satisfying a craving for crispy comfort food, this recipe will surely become a staple in your kitchen. Enjoy!
