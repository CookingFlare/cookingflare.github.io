Sous Vide Broccoli with Lemon Garlic Butter


Broccoli, a vegetable beloved for its versatility and nutritional benefits, often gets relegated to the side of the plate, serving as a simple and bland accompaniment to more exciting dishes. However, when prepared with the sous vide method, broccoli reaches a new level of flavor, texture, and vibrancy. In this recipe, we elevate the humble vegetable with a decadent lemon garlic butter sauce that will turn it into the star of the meal. The combination of sous vide cooking and a flavorful finishing sauce makes this dish an unforgettable experience for your taste buds.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, which translates from French as “under vacuum,” is a cooking technique that involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise, consistent temperature. This method has become increasingly popular because it allows for incredibly tender, perfectly cooked food that retains its natural flavors and nutrients.

By cooking food slowly and gently at lower temperatures, sous vide ensures that the texture of delicate vegetables like broccoli remains tender yet firm, while locking in the flavors. This cooking process also eliminates the risk of overcooking, which can often turn broccoli into a mushy or unpleasant dish. When done correctly, sous vide cooking can result in perfectly cooked broccoli every time, with a bright color and a crisp-tender bite that traditional methods like steaming or boiling can’t match.

Why Choose Sous Vide for Cooking Broccoli?

Precision Cooking

Sous vide is all about precision. When you cook broccoli using the sous vide method, you can ensure that every piece is cooked evenly. Unlike other methods, where the heat fluctuates and can lead to uneven cooking, sous vide cooking allows the broccoli to reach the exact doneness that you want without any guesswork.

Retains Nutrients

Boiling vegetables can cause a loss of valuable nutrients, especially water-soluble vitamins like vitamin C. Since sous vide cooking takes place in a sealed bag, all of the natural nutrients stay locked in, leading to a more nutritious dish.

Consistent Texture

If you’ve ever had broccoli that was too soft or too tough, sous vide will solve this issue. The gentle cooking process gives the broccoli a perfectly tender, crisp texture that is difficult to achieve with other cooking methods.

Easy Prep

Preparing vegetables with sous vide requires minimal hands-on time. Simply season your broccoli, seal it in a bag, and let it cook in the water bath. No need to monitor the cooking process constantly. While the broccoli cooks, you can prepare the lemon garlic butter sauce or focus on other parts of your meal.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Broccoli with Lemon Garlic Butter

For the Broccoli:

  • 1 lb broccoli florets (fresh, not frozen)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)

For the Lemon Garlic Butter Sauce:

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Equipment Needed

  • Sous vide precision cooker
  • Vacuum-seal bags or resealable freezer bags (water displacement method)
  • Large pot or container for the water bath
  • Skillet for finishing the sauce
  • Tongs or slotted spoon for removing the broccoli from the bag

Instructions for Sous Vide Broccoli with Lemon Garlic Butter

Step 1: Prepare the Broccoli for Sous Vide Cooking

The first step in creating the perfect sous vide broccoli is to prepare the vegetable itself. Start by cutting your broccoli into florets. If the florets are too large, consider cutting them into smaller, more uniform pieces to ensure they cook evenly.

Once the broccoli is prepared, place the florets in a vacuum-seal bag. Add the olive oil, salt, and pepper to the bag, and if desired, toss in some minced garlic for added flavor. The olive oil will help to infuse the broccoli with richness during the cooking process, while the seasoning will enhance the natural flavor of the vegetable.

Seal the bag using a vacuum-sealer or use the water displacement method with a resealable freezer bag. To do this, carefully lower the bag into a pot of water, ensuring that the water pushes the air out of the bag, and then seal it tightly.

Step 2: Set Up the Sous Vide Water Bath

Fill a large pot or container with water and set your sous vide precision cooker to 185°F (85°C). This temperature will cook the broccoli to a perfect tender-crisp texture. If you prefer softer broccoli, you can cook it at a slightly higher temperature, but 185°F is ideal for maintaining the vegetable’s structure while enhancing its flavor.

Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, carefully lower the sealed bag of broccoli into the water. Ensure that the bag is fully submerged. You may want to use a clip or weight to keep the bag from floating to the surface.

Cook the broccoli for 45 minutes. The great thing about sous vide is that you don’t have to worry about overcooking; the broccoli will be perfect no matter when you remove it, as long as it’s been cooked for the full duration.

Step 3: Make the Lemon Garlic Butter Sauce

While the broccoli is cooking, you can prepare the lemon garlic butter sauce. This sauce adds a rich, tangy flavor that complements the broccoli perfectly.

In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute, or until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter if overcooked.

Next, add the butter to the skillet and let it melt. Once the butter has melted, add the lemon zest and juice. Stir the sauce to combine, then season with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the sauce to simmer for another 2–3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Once the sauce is ready, remove it from the heat and stir in the chopped parsley for a fresh, herby finish.

Step 4: Finish the Broccoli

After 45 minutes, carefully remove the bag of broccoli from the water bath. Use tongs or a slotted spoon to remove the broccoli from the bag, and transfer it to a plate.

At this point, the broccoli is perfectly cooked, but you can take it a step further by briefly searing it in a hot pan to give it a little texture and color. To do this, heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the cooked broccoli to the pan and sauté for 1–2 minutes until the florets are lightly browned.

Step 5: Serve

Place the broccoli on a serving platter or individual plates. Drizzle the warm lemon garlic butter sauce generously over the top. You can also garnish with additional fresh parsley or lemon slices for extra visual appeal.

Step 6: Enjoy!

Your sous vide broccoli with lemon garlic butter is now ready to be enjoyed. The broccoli is tender yet crisp, with a bright, tangy kick from the lemon and a richness from the garlic butter. This dish can be served as a side for a variety of main courses or enjoyed on its own for a light, healthy meal.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Broccoli

  • Temperature Control: If you’re new to sous vide cooking, remember that temperature is crucial. For tender-crisp broccoli, 185°F is ideal, but you can experiment with slightly higher or lower temperatures to adjust to your preference.

  • Customize Your Flavors: While lemon garlic butter is a classic combination, you can easily customize the flavors to suit your taste. Try adding red pepper flakes for a spicy kick or a touch of Parmesan cheese for an extra layer of richness.

  • Add a Protein: This recipe pairs beautifully with grilled chicken, fish, or steak. You can even add some cooked quinoa or rice to make the dish a more substantial main course.

  • Make Ahead: Sous vide cooking is a great make-ahead option. You can cook the broccoli in advance and store it in the fridge for up to 2 days. Reheat it in the water bath at 185°F for 10 minutes before serving, and then drizzle with the lemon garlic butter.

Why This Recipe Works

Sous vide broccoli with lemon garlic butter is a standout recipe because it combines the precision of sous vide cooking with the bold flavors of lemon, garlic, and butter. The sous vide method ensures that the broccoli is perfectly cooked, maintaining its bright green color and tender, yet firm, texture. The lemon garlic butter sauce adds an irresistible richness, while the freshness of the lemon cuts through the richness and balances the dish.

This recipe is perfect for those who want a delicious, healthy side dish that’s both easy to prepare and impressive enough for any dinner party. Whether you’re a sous vide expert or just getting started, this recipe is a great way to explore the benefits of this cooking technique and elevate your vegetable dishes to new heights.

Final Thoughts

Sous vide cooking has revolutionized the way we prepare vegetables, and this recipe for sous vide broccoli with lemon garlic butter demonstrates just how versatile and flavorful this method can be. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of time, you can create a dish that is both healthy and indulgent. So, give this recipe a try, and let the magic of sous vide transform your everyday broccoli into something extraordinary!
