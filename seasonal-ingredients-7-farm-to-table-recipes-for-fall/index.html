Seasonal Ingredients: 7 Farm to Table Recipes for Fall


Fall brings with it a magical change in the landscape, cooler temperatures, and a bounty of seasonal ingredients. It’s the perfect season to embrace farm-to-table cooking, letting the rich, earthy flavors of autumn inspire your kitchen creations. Savoring fresh, locally grown produce not only supports your local farmers, but it also ensures your meals are packed with optimal flavor and nutrition. Below are seven delicious recipes featuring seasonal ingredients that fall has to offer, from hearty root vegetables to crisp apples and nutty squash.

1. Honey-Roasted Butternut Squash with Sage

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • A handful of fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, honey, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the squash evenly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Scatter the sage leaves on top of the squash.
  5. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the squash is tender and caramelized.
  6. Serve as a side dish or add to salads for an extra burst of autumn flavor.

This recipe highlights the nutty sweetness of butternut squash, pairing it with the aromatic profile of sage—a quintessential herb for fall.

2. Creamy Pumpkin and Lentil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 small pumpkin or 2 cups pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 4 cups vegetable stock
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. If using fresh pumpkin, peel, seed, and cube it. Steam or boil until tender.
  2. Heat a large pot over medium heat and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add lentils, curry powder, pumpkin (cubed or puree), and stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  4. Cook until the lentils are tender, about 25 minutes.
  5. Blend the soup using an immersion blender or transfer it in batches to a blender.
  6. Stir in the coconut milk, adjust the seasoning, and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  7. Serve warm with a drizzle of coconut milk or a sprinkle of toasted pumpkin seeds.

This dish showcases pumpkin—a star of autumn—with its velvety texture and ability to pair beautifully with hearty lentils and spices.

3. Warm Kale and Apple Salad with Walnut Vinaigrette

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of curly kale, stems removed and leaves torn
  • 2 crisp apples (e.g., Honeycrisp or Fuji), thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted and chopped
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Massage the kale leaves with a pinch of salt to soften them, then set aside.
  2. Prepare the vinaigrette by whisking together apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the kale, apple slices, toasted walnuts, and cranberries.
  4. Pour the vinaigrette over the salad and toss to coat.
  5. Serve immediately or let it rest for 10-15 minutes to enhance the flavors.

Fall’s bounty of crisp apples pairs wonderfully with kale’s heartiness in this simple, nutrient-packed salad.

4. Herb-Crusted Pork Loin with Pears

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork loin (about 2 pounds)
  • 2 ripe Bosc pears, quartered and cored
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Rub the pork loin with Dijon mustard and honey, then season with rosemary, thyme, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet and sear the pork loin on all sides.
  4. Transfer the seared pork to a roasting pan, surround it with pear quarters, and roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).
  5. Let the pork rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with the roasted pears.

This dish brings together a savory herb crust and the natural sweetness of pears, making it ideal for a cozy autumn dinner.

5. Beet and Goat Cheese Tart with Thyme

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed
  • 3 medium beets, roasted and thinly sliced
  • 4 ounces goat cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry on a baking sheet and prick with a fork.
  3. Spread crumbled goat cheese evenly on the pastry, leaving a 1-inch border.
  4. Layer the roasted beet slices over the cheese and sprinkle with thyme, salt, and pepper.
  5. Drizzle honey on top and fold in the edges of the pastry.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown.

Earthy beets and tangy goat cheese are an elegant pairing that celebrates fall’s most vibrant produce.

6. Wild Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable stock, kept warm
  • 1 cup wild mushrooms (e.g., chanterelles, shiitake), sliced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan and sauté the onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook until golden. Remove and set aside.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice and toast for 1-2 minutes before deglazing with white wine.
  4. Add the stock one ladle at a time, stirring constantly, until the liquid is absorbed. Repeat this process until the rice is tender and creamy.
  5. Stir in the mushrooms, Parmesan, and butter. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

This creamy risotto highlights fall’s earthy wild mushrooms, creating a comforting and refined dish.

7. Spiced Pear and Ginger Crumble

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe pears, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss the pears with ginger, brown sugar, and cinnamon, then transfer to a greased baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix oats, flour, pecans, and melted butter until crumbly.
  4. Sprinkle the crumble topping over the pear mixture.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the topping is golden and the filling is bubbly. Serve warm, optionally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

This dessert balances sweet and spice, making it the perfect ending to any fall feast.

Savoring the seasonal ingredients of fall allows you to embrace the flavors, textures, and soul-warming qualities of the season. Each of these recipes is designed to inspire your appreciation for local produce and introduce a bit of autumn magic to your table. Whether you’re hosting guests or enjoying a quiet dinner, let these creations bring the best of fall into your home.
