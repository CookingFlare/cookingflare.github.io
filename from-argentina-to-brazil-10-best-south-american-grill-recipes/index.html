From Argentina to Brazil: 10 Best South American Grill Recipes


South American cuisine has an unparalleled depth of flavor, history, and culture that is intimately tied to the grilling traditions of the region. The diverse landscapes, from Argentina’s sprawling plains to Brazil’s lush rainforests, have given birth to a wide variety of grilling techniques, each influenced by local ingredients, customs, and regional specialties. Whether it’s the aromatic woods used to smoke meats in Brazil or the Argentine method of cooking over an open flame, South American grilling is a celebration of both the land and its people.

In this post, we’ll take a culinary journey from Argentina to Brazil, exploring 10 of the best South American grill recipes that showcase the vibrant flavors of the region. From tender steaks to smoky sausages and inventive side dishes, these recipes will inspire you to fire up your grill and bring a taste of South America to your backyard.

1. Argentine Asado: The National Grill Feast

What is Asado?

Argentine Asado is more than just a meal—it’s a tradition that has been passed down through generations. Often referred to as Argentina’s national dish, Asado is a social event centered around grilling beef, lamb, pork, and sometimes even chicken over an open flame or on a parrilla (grill). The meat is usually seasoned with a simple rub of salt and occasionally complemented with chimichurri, a tangy sauce made from parsley, garlic, vinegar, and oil.

Ingredients:

  • 2kg beef ribs or short ribs
  • 1 tablespoon sea salt
  • Chimichurri sauce (optional)

Method:

  1. Prepare your grill with medium heat, ensuring you have an open flame or hot coals.
  2. Season the meat generously with sea salt and allow it to rest at room temperature for 20 minutes.
  3. Place the ribs on the grill, bone side down, and cook slowly, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking. The key is to cook the meat low and slow to achieve tender, juicy results.
  4. Once the meat reaches your desired level of doneness, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes.
  5. Serve the Asado with chimichurri sauce on the side.

2. Brazilian Churrasco: A Taste of Brazil’s BBQ Culture

The Spirit of Churrasco

Churrasco is a Brazilian style of barbecue that features a wide variety of meats, including beef, chicken, sausage, and pork, often cooked on large skewers over an open flame. It’s a staple at gatherings known as “churrascarias,” where various meats are cooked and served on skewers directly to the table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sirloin steaks (about 1 kg)
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • Olive oil for brushing

Method:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Season the steaks with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the steaks lightly with olive oil.
  4. Skewer the steaks and grill them, turning occasionally to achieve a perfect sear.
  5. Once cooked to your desired level of doneness, remove the meat from the skewers and slice thinly to serve.

Serve with Brazilian farofa (toasted manioc flour) and a refreshing caipirinha to complete the experience.

3. Colombian Arepas: The Perfect Grilled Side

What Are Arepas?

Arepas are cornmeal-based cakes that are commonly eaten in Colombia and Venezuela. They’re versatile and can be grilled, fried, or baked. When grilled, they develop a delightful crisp exterior while maintaining a soft, fluffy interior. Arepas are often enjoyed as a side dish to grilled meats or topped with cheese, avocado, or meat fillings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cornmeal (preferably precooked)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for grilling)

Method:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the cornmeal, salt, melted butter, and warm water until a dough forms. Let it rest for 5 minutes.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls and then flatten them into round patties about 1/2 inch thick.
  3. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium heat. Brush the grill with olive oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Grill the arepas for about 4 minutes per side, or until they develop grill marks and a golden brown crust.
  5. Serve hot with cheese, avocado, or any topping of your choice.

4. Peruvian Anticuchos: A Skewer of Flavor

What are Anticuchos?

Anticuchos are marinated skewers of meat, often beef heart, that are grilled to perfection. This dish has its roots in Peruvian street food and is a must-try for any grilling enthusiast looking for something with a bit of history and bold flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef heart (or any other tender cut of beef)
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers

Method:

  1. Cut the beef heart into bite-sized cubes.
  2. In a bowl, combine the cumin, paprika, garlic powder, red wine vinegar, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Add the meat to the marinade and let it sit for at least 2 hours (or overnight).
  3. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  5. Grill the skewers for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until the meat is cooked to your preference.
  6. Serve the anticuchos with a side of potatoes or a light salad.

5. Chilean Completo: A Grilled Hot Dog with a Twist

What Makes a Completo Special?

In Chile, the completo is a beloved hot dog that’s taken to the next level with an array of toppings, such as avocado, mayonnaise, and sauerkraut. It’s often served with grilled sausages, making it a perfect recipe for any barbecue.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large hot dog buns
  • 4 beef sausages or hot dogs
  • 1 avocado, mashed
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sauerkraut
  • 1 tablespoon mustard

Method:

  1. Grill the sausages or hot dogs on medium heat until golden brown and slightly charred.
  2. Slice the buns and toast them lightly on the grill.
  3. Assemble the completos by spreading a generous amount of mashed avocado on the bun, followed by a sausage or hot dog.
  4. Add a drizzle of mayonnaise, mustard, and a spoonful of sauerkraut.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy the flavorful combination of creamy, tangy, and savory elements.

6. Uruguayan Chivito: A Grilled Sandwich

What is Chivito?

Chivito is a hearty Uruguayan sandwich traditionally made with grilled beef, cheese, lettuce, tomato, olives, and mayo. The grilled meat can vary from steak to chicken, but the key is the tender, juicy beef paired with the flavorful toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 beef steaks (or chicken breasts)
  • 2 sandwich rolls
  • 2 slices of mozzarella cheese
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • Lettuce
  • Mayonnaise
  • Olives, pitted and sliced

Method:

  1. Grill the beef steaks on medium heat, cooking them to your desired doneness.
  2. Slice the rolls and lightly toast them on the grill.
  3. Assemble the sandwiches by placing a grilled steak on each roll, topping it with cheese, tomato slices, lettuce, mayo, and olives.
  4. Serve with French fries or a light salad on the side.

7. Venezuelan Parrilla de Pollo: Grilled Chicken with a Citrus Twist

Why Parrilla de Pollo?

Grilled chicken in Venezuela is often marinated in a citrus-based sauce that infuses the meat with a tangy, flavorful kick. The citrus helps tenderize the chicken, creating a juicy and aromatic result.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs or breasts
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method:

  1. In a bowl, combine the orange juice, lime juice, garlic, cumin, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken and marinate for at least 2 hours.
  2. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  3. Grill the chicken for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  4. Serve with grilled vegetables or a side of rice.

8. Ecuadorian Hornado: Slow-Roasted Pork

The Essence of Hornado

Hornado is a slow-cooked Ecuadorian dish that features pork roasted to perfection over an open fire. It’s often marinated with a blend of spices, then slowly cooked until the skin is crispy and the meat is melt-in-your-mouth tender.

Ingredients:

  • 2kg pork shoulder
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon achiote powder
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Rub the pork shoulder with garlic paste, cumin, achiote, orange juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Let the pork marinate for at least 4 hours (overnight for best results).
  3. Roast the pork on the grill, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking, for about 3-4 hours, or until the skin is crispy and the meat is tender.
  4. Slice and serve with boiled potatoes or a side of rice.

9. Bolivian Salteñas: Grilled Empanadas

What Makes Salteñas Unique?

Salteñas are savory pastries filled with a rich mixture of beef, pork, chicken, or vegetables. They’re typically baked, but when grilled, they acquire a delightful smoky flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup beef or chicken filling (pre-cooked)
  • 1 egg, for brushing

Method:

  1. Prepare the dough by combining the flour and butter and adding just enough water to form a dough.
  2. Roll out the dough and cut into rounds.
  3. Fill the rounds with pre-cooked beef or chicken filling.
  4. Seal the edges tightly and brush the tops with egg wash.
  5. Grill over medium heat, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy.

10. Paraguayan Mbeju: Grilled Corn Flour Cakes

The Charm of Mbeju

Mbeju is a traditional Paraguayan dish made from corn flour, cheese, and starch. When grilled, it becomes a crispy, cheesy delight, perfect for pairing with grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup corn flour
  • 1/2 cup cheese, grated
  • 1/4 cup starch
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon butter

Method:

  1. Mix all the ingredients in a bowl until you form a dough.
  2. Shape the dough into small patties.
  3. Grill the patties for about 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden and crispy.
  4. Serve as a side with grilled meats or as a snack.

Conclusion

South American grilling is a rich and diverse tradition that offers something for every palate. Whether you prefer the smoky flavor of Brazilian churrasco, the juicy tenderness of Argentine Asado, or the bold seasoning of Colombian anticuchos, these recipes are guaranteed to bring the vibrant flavors of South America to your grill. So, gather your friends and family, fire up the grill, and indulge in a feast that celebrates the best of South American cuisine.
