Homemade Cherry Garcia Ice Cream: A Classic Ben & Jerry's Flavor


If there’s one thing that can transport you back to your childhood summers, it’s the nostalgic taste of Ben & Jerry’s Cherry Garcia ice cream. This classic flavor, with its rich cherry ice cream, chunks of dark chocolate, and sweet cherries, is a favorite among ice cream lovers everywhere. But did you know that you can recreate this beloved treat right in the comfort of your own kitchen? With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can whip up a batch of homemade Cherry Garcia ice cream that rivals the original.

Ingredients

To make your homemade Cherry Garcia ice cream, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 cup of pitted and halved cherries
  • 1/2 cup of chopped dark chocolate

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a simmer. Remove from heat.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks. Slowly pour about 1/2 cup of the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly. This step is important to temper the eggs and prevent them from curdling.

  3. Pour the tempered egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream mixture. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 8-10 minutes. Be careful not to let the mixture boil.

  4. Once the mixture has thickened, remove it from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and cover it with plastic wrap, making sure the wrap touches the surface of the custard. This will prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate the custard until it’s completely chilled, at least 4 hours or overnight.

  5. Once the custard is chilled, pour it into the bowl of an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.

  6. While the ice cream is churning, prepare the cherries and chocolate. Pit and halve the cherries, and chop the dark chocolate into small chunks.

  7. When the ice cream is almost done churning, add the cherries and chocolate to the machine and let it mix for another minute or two, just until the chunks are evenly distributed.

  8. Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for at least 2 hours, or until firm.

Serving Suggestions

Homemade Cherry Garcia ice cream is delicious on its own, but you can take it to the next level by serving it in a warm waffle cone or alongside a slice of warm cherry pie. The combination of the creamy ice cream, tart cherries, and rich chocolate is a match made in dessert heaven.

Conclusion

Making your own Homemade Cherry Garcia ice cream is not only a fun and rewarding experience, but it also allows you to customize the flavors and ingredients to your liking. Whether you’re a fan of extra cherries or prefer a darker chocolate, this recipe gives you the freedom to create your perfect scoop. So why wait? Grab your ice cream maker and get ready to savor the sweet nostalgia of Ben & Jerry’s Cherry Garcia right in your own kitchen.
