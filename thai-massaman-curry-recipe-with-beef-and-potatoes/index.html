Thai Massaman Curry Recipe with Beef and Potatoes


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’ve probably come across the rich and flavorful Massaman curry. This dish has its roots in Thailand but carries influences from Persian and Indian cuisine, resulting in a unique and aromatic flavor profile. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create an authentic Thai Massaman curry with beef and potatoes that will tantalize your taste buds and transport you to the bustling streets of Bangkok.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds of beef, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup of Massaman curry paste
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cardamom pods
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon of tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted peanuts
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Beef: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant. Add the beef pieces and cook until browned on all sides.

  2. Add the Aromatics: Once the beef is browned, add the Massaman curry paste to the pot, stirring to coat the beef evenly. Allow the curry paste to cook with the beef for a few minutes to release its flavors.

  3. Create the Curry Base: Pour in the coconut milk and stir well to combine with the beef and curry paste. Add the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and cloves to infuse the curry with their aromatic flavors.

  4. Simmer the Curry: Bring the curry to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cover the pot and let the curry simmer for about 1 hour or until the beef is tender.

  5. Add Potatoes and Peanuts: Once the beef is tender, add the cubed potatoes to the pot, along with the unsalted peanuts. Stir gently to incorporate the potatoes and peanuts into the curry.

  6. Season the Curry: Add the tamarind paste, fish sauce, and brown sugar to the curry, adjusting the seasoning to your taste. Allow the curry to simmer for an additional 15-20 minutes until the potatoes are cooked through.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the curry is ready, remove the cinnamon stick and any whole spices. Serve the Massaman curry hot over a bed of jasmine rice, garnished with fresh cilantro.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For a richer flavor, you can sear the beef in batches before adding the onion and garlic.
  • If you prefer a thicker curry, you can add a small amount of peanut butter to the curry base.
  • Massaman curry pairs well with a refreshing cucumber salad or pickled vegetables on the side.

Now that you have the know-how to create an authentic Thai Massaman curry with beef and potatoes, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and embark on a culinary adventure. The combination of tender beef, creamy coconut milk, and aromatic spices will undoubtedly make this dish a staple in your recipe repertoire. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your stove, and get ready to savor the tantalizing flavors of Thailand right in your own kitchen.
