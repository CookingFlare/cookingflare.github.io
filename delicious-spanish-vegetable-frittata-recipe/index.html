Delicious Spanish Vegetable Frittata Recipe


Frittatas are a delightful way to enjoy a variety of ingredients in one dish, and the Spanish vegetable frittata is no exception. This dish is not only delicious but also versatile, allowing you to use whatever vegetables you have on hand. In this post, we’ll explore a step-by-step guide to creating a mouthwatering Spanish vegetable frittata that’s perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even a light dinner. Packed with flavor, nutrition, and a vibrant display of colors, this recipe will quickly become a favorite in your household.

What is a Frittata?

A frittata is an Italian-style omelet that is cooked slowly over low heat and often finished in the oven. Unlike a traditional omelet, which is folded and served immediately, a frittata is cooked flat and can be served in wedges or slices. This makes it an excellent option for feeding a crowd or for meal prep. The beauty of a frittata lies in its adaptability; you can easily incorporate different ingredients based on your preferences or seasonal availability.

Ingredients for the Spanish Vegetable Frittata

To create a delicious Spanish vegetable frittata, you will need the following ingredients:

Base Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs: The foundation of the frittata, providing protein and structure.
  • 1/4 cup milk: To make the frittata creamy and rich.
  • Salt and pepper: For seasoning.

Vegetables

  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped: Adds sweetness and depth of flavor.
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced: Offers a pop of color and sweetness.
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced: Adds crunch and freshness.
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced: Contributes moisture and a mild flavor.
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped: Provides vibrant color and nutrition.
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved: Adds a juicy burst of flavor and color.

Additional Flavorings

  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika: A key ingredient that gives the frittata its distinct Spanish flair.
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder: Enhances the overall flavor.
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese: For a savory, cheesy finish.
  • Olive oil: For sautéing the vegetables.

Cooking Equipment

To prepare the Spanish vegetable frittata, you will need:

  • A large mixing bowl
  • A whisk or fork
  • A skillet (preferably oven-safe, such as cast iron)
  • A spatula
  • An oven

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s dive into the preparation of the Spanish vegetable frittata.

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This step is essential as it prepares the oven for the final cooking phase of the frittata.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

  1. Heat the skillet: In an oven-safe skillet, heat approximately 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the onions: Once the oil is shimmering, add the chopped onion. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes, or until the onion becomes translucent.
  3. Incorporate the peppers: Add the diced red and green bell peppers to the skillet. Cook for another 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally until they soften.
  4. Add zucchini and spinach: Mix in the diced zucchini and chopped spinach. Continue cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the spinach to wilt and the zucchini to soften slightly.
  5. Season the mixture: Sprinkle in the smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir well to ensure the vegetables are evenly coated with the spices.

Step 3: Prepare the Egg Mixture

  1. Whisk the eggs: In a large mixing bowl, crack open the six eggs and add the milk. Whisk until well combined and frothy.
  2. Add the cheese: Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese for added flavor and creaminess.
  3. Combine with the vegetables: Pour the egg mixture over the sautéed vegetables in the skillet. Gently stir to ensure the vegetables are evenly distributed within the egg mixture.

Step 4: Cook the Frittata on the Stovetop

  1. Cook on low heat: Allow the frittata to cook on the stovetop over low heat for about 5-7 minutes. During this time, the edges will begin to set while the center remains slightly runny.
  2. Check for doneness: Run a spatula around the edges to ensure it doesn’t stick. If the bottom is browning too quickly, reduce the heat further.

Step 5: Transfer to the Oven

  1. Bake the frittata: Once the edges are set, transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the center is fully set and the top is lightly golden.
  2. Check for doneness: A toothpick inserted into the center should come out clean. If it’s still wet, return it to the oven for a few more minutes.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

  1. Let it cool: Once baked, remove the skillet from the oven and allow the frittata to cool for a few minutes.
  2. Slice and serve: Using a sharp knife, cut the frittata into wedges. Serve warm or at room temperature. It pairs wonderfully with a simple side salad or crusty bread.

Variations and Tips

The Spanish vegetable frittata is incredibly versatile. Here are some variations and tips to customize your frittata experience:

  • Add Proteins: For a heartier dish, consider adding cooked chorizo, ham, or even sautéed mushrooms. This will introduce additional flavors and textures.
  • Cheese Choices: Try different cheeses like feta, goat cheese, or even cheddar to give your frittata a unique twist.
  • Herbs and Spices: Fresh herbs like parsley, chives, or basil can elevate the flavor profile. Add them to the egg mixture before baking.
  • Use Seasonal Vegetables: Feel free to swap out the vegetables based on what’s in season or what you have in your fridge. Asparagus, broccoli, or even artichokes work beautifully in a frittata.
  • Meal Prep: This frittata is perfect for meal prep as it can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. Just reheat slices in the microwave or oven.

Nutritional Benefits

The Spanish vegetable frittata is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Here are some of the health benefits of the ingredients used:

  • Eggs: A great source of protein, vitamins, and healthy fats. They also contain essential amino acids necessary for muscle repair.
  • Vegetables: Loaded with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, the vegetables in this frittata contribute to overall health and wellness.
  • Spinach: Rich in iron and vitamins A and C, spinach is a powerhouse of nutrition that supports immune function and healthy skin.
  • Olive Oil: A healthy fat that can promote heart health and provide anti-inflammatory benefits.

Conclusion

The delicious Spanish vegetable frittata is a fantastic dish that brings together simple ingredients into a flavorful and satisfying meal. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, looking for a quick weeknight dinner, or meal prepping for the week ahead, this frittata is sure to impress. With its rich flavors and stunning presentation, it’s not just food; it’s an experience. So gather your ingredients, follow the steps above, and enjoy a delightful slice of Spain in your kitchen!
