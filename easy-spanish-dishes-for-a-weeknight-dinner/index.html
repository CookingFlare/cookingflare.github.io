Easy Spanish Dishes for a Weeknight Dinner


Spanish cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors, colors, and textures, making it a fantastic choice for weeknight dinners. Whether it’s the rich and comforting taste of a classic paella or the savory allure of a simple tapas dish, Spanish food offers something for everyone. In this post, we’ll explore a week’s worth of easy Spanish dishes that you can whip up in no time, transforming your weeknight dinners into a delightful culinary adventure.

Day 1: Spanish Tortilla

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Potatoes and Onions: Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced potatoes and onions, seasoning with salt and pepper. Cook until the potatoes are tender and slightly golden, about 15-20 minutes.

  2. Beat the Eggs: In a large bowl, beat the eggs and add a pinch of salt. Once the potatoes and onions are ready, drain any excess oil and add the mixture to the eggs. Stir gently to combine.

  3. Cook the Tortilla: In the same skillet, add a bit more olive oil if needed and pour in the egg mixture. Cook over low heat for about 10 minutes, or until the edges are set. Carefully flip the tortilla (using a plate to assist) and cook the other side for another 5-7 minutes until fully cooked.

  4. Serve: Slide the tortilla onto a serving plate and let it cool slightly before slicing into wedges. Enjoy warm or at room temperature, perfect for dinner or a snack!

Day 2: Chickpea and Spinach Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the Spice and Tomatoes: Stir in the smoked paprika and cook for an additional minute. Then, add the diced tomatoes with their juices and bring to a simmer.

  3. Add Chickpeas and Spinach: Stir in the chickpeas and spinach, cooking until the spinach is wilted and everything is heated through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Serve: Ladle the stew into bowls and serve it with crusty bread for a hearty meal that’s both filling and nutritious.

Day 3: Pisto Manchego

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green), diced
  • 1 medium eggplant, diced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fried eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Vegetables: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant. Stir in the zucchini, bell pepper, and eggplant, cooking until softened.

  2. Add Tomatoes: Pour in the diced tomatoes and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Serve: Serve hot, topped with a fried egg if desired. This dish is a fantastic way to enjoy a variety of vegetables in one meal.

Day 4: Shrimp and Chorizo Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz chorizo sausage, sliced
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Skewers: Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Thread the shrimp and chorizo onto skewers, alternating between the two.

  2. Grill the Skewers: Brush the skewers with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Grill for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the shrimp are opaque and cooked through.

  3. Serve: Remove from the grill, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and serve with lemon wedges. These skewers make for a fantastic and quick dinner with a burst of flavor.

Day 5: Garlic Shrimp (Gambas al Ajillo)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Garlic: In a large skillet, heat a generous amount of olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes, sautéing until the garlic is fragrant but not browned.

  2. Cook the Shrimp: Add the shrimp to the skillet, cooking until they turn pink and opaque, about 3-4 minutes.

  3. Finish and Serve: Remove from heat, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and serve immediately with lemon wedges. This dish is excellent served with crusty bread to soak up the flavorful oil.

Day 6: Spanish Rice with Peas and Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper, cooking until softened.

  2. Add Rice: Stir in the rice, allowing it to toast slightly for about 2 minutes. Then, add the vegetable broth and bring to a boil.

  3. Cook the Rice: Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes. Stir in the frozen peas and cook for an additional 5 minutes until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.

  4. Serve: Fluff the rice with a fork and serve as a side dish or a main course, enjoying the colorful and nutritious blend of ingredients.

Day 7: Flan (Spanish Caramel Custard)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Make the Caramel: In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the sugar until it turns a golden brown. Quickly pour the caramel into a round baking dish, tilting to coat the bottom evenly.

  2. Prepare the Custard: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. Pour the mixture over the caramel in the baking dish.

  3. Bake: Place the baking dish in a larger baking pan filled with hot water (a water bath). Bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 50-60 minutes, or until the custard is set.

  4. Cool and Serve: Allow to cool before refrigerating for a couple of hours. To serve, invert the flan onto a plate, allowing the caramel sauce to drizzle over the top. A sweet and indulgent finish to your week of Spanish dinners!

Conclusion

With these easy Spanish dishes, your weeknight dinners can be both delicious and effortless. Each recipe brings a taste of Spain to your table, allowing you to explore a variety of flavors and ingredients. From the comforting Spanish tortilla to the indulgent flan, these meals are sure to satisfy your cravings and make your evenings more enjoyable. Happy cooking!
