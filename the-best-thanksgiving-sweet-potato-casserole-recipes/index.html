The Best Thanksgiving Sweet Potato Casserole Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering, giving thanks, and indulging in irresistible dishes that celebrate the season’s flavors. Among the cherished side dishes that grace the Thanksgiving table, sweet potato casserole holds a special place in many hearts. Whether you’re a fan of the traditional marshmallow-topped variety or prefer a nutty pecan crumble, sweet potato casserole offers the perfect balance of sweetness, warmth, and comforting goodness. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best sweet potato casserole recipes that’ll make your holiday table unforgettable.

What Makes Sweet Potato Casserole a Must-Have?

Sweet potato casserole bridges the gap between side dishes and dessert, offering a cozy sweetness without being overpowering. The smooth texture of mashed sweet potatoes pairs harmoniously with the crunch and sweetness of its toppings. The natural sweetness of sweet potatoes also allows this dish to shine without added refined ingredients, while the spices often associated with autumn—like cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves—enhance its festive appeal.

What truly sets sweet potato casserole apart is its versatility. You can opt for simple, wholesome recipes or elevate it with bold, indulgent flourishes. Now, let’s dive into the best recipes that deserve a spot at your Thanksgiving feast.

Classic Sweet Potato Casserole

Let’s start with the traditional take on this Thanksgiving favorite. The classic sweet potato casserole combines creamy mashed sweet potatoes with a topping of golden, gooey marshmallows. It’s ideal for those seeking a nostalgic flavor reminiscent of holidays past.

Ingredients:

For the sweet potato filling:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg

For the topping:

  • 2 cups mini marshmallows

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the sweet potato filling: Boil the sweet potato cubes until soft (typically around 20 minutes). Drain and mash thoroughly in a large bowl.
  2. Mix in melted butter, brown sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, milk, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Stir until smooth and well combined.
  3. Assemble the casserole: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread the mashed sweet potato mixture evenly in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  4. Layer marshmallows across the top, ensuring even coverage.
  5. Bake: Bake for 15 minutes, or until the marshmallows are golden brown and slightly melted.
  6. Remove from the oven, let it cool slightly, and serve warm for that traditional Thanksgiving experience.

Pecan Streusel Sweet Potato Casserole

Looking for a more decadent twist on the classic? The pecan streusel topping transforms this simple casserole into an indulgent crowd-pleaser. The crumbly topping is a perfect combination of buttery richness and nutty texture.

Ingredients:

For the sweet potato filling:

  • 3 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup milk
  • ¾ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

For the pecan topping:

  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, diced and chilled
  • 1 cup chopped pecans

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the sweet potatoes: Boil the sweet potato cubes until fork-tender, about 20 minutes. Drain and mash until smooth.
  2. Stir in melted butter, maple syrup, brown sugar, eggs, milk, cinnamon, and salt. Blend thoroughly until creamy and smooth.
  3. Make the streusel topping: In a bowl, combine flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon. Cut in the chilled butter using a fork or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in pecans.
  4. Assemble and bake: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the sweet potato mixture into a greased baking dish. Sprinkle the pecan streusel evenly on top.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden and crisp.
  6. Serve warm and watch your guests dive into this irresistible dish!

Vegan Sweet Potato Casserole

For those adhering to a plant-based diet or simply looking to incorporate a healthier option into their Thanksgiving spread, this vegan sweet potato casserole recipe is a winner. It’s dairy-free and egg-free, yet still bursting with flavor.

Ingredients:

For the sweet potato filling:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ¼ cup coconut oil, melted
  • ⅓ cup maple syrup
  • ⅓ cup almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ⅛ teaspoon nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt

For the oat-pecan topping:

  • ½ cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup chopped pecans
  • ¼ cup whole wheat flour
  • ¼ cup coconut oil, melted
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • ¼ teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sweet potatoes in boiling water until softened, about 20 minutes. Drain and mash in a large bowl.
  2. Mix in coconut oil, maple syrup, almond milk, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt until creamy.
  3. In another bowl, combine oats, chopped pecans, flour, coconut oil, maple syrup, and cinnamon to form the topping.
  4. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a baking dish. Spread the sweet potato mixture evenly in the dish. Sprinkle the oat-pecan topping generously over the mashed sweet potatoes.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes until the topping is crisp and fragrant.
  6. Serve your vegan sweet potato casserole warm and enjoy a guilt-free, flavorful treat.

Bourbon Sweet Potato Casserole with Spiced Crumble

Take your Thanksgiving casserole game up a notch with the addition of bourbon. This recipe introduces warming spiced flavors and a lightly boozy undertone that elevates the dish into culinary decadence.

Ingredients:

For the sweet potato filling:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • ¼ cup bourbon
  • ⅓ cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of cloves

For the spiced crumble topping:

  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup flour
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon cardamom
  • ¼ cup butter, chilled and diced
  • ¾ cup chopped walnuts

Instructions:

  1. Boil the sweet potatoes until tender, then mash them well. Add melted butter, bourbon, brown sugar, eggs, cream, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and cloves. Combine until smooth.
  2. For the crumble, blend together brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cardamom in a bowl. Cut in chilled butter until crumbly, then stir in walnuts.
  3. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and spread the sweet potato mixture evenly in a greased casserole dish. Scatter the crumble topping generously across the surface.
  4. Bake for 35-40 minutes until the topping is golden and crisp.
  5. Serve warm and savor the luxurious depth of flavors!

Tips for Perfect Sweet Potato Casserole Every Time

  • Choose fresh sweet potatoes: Opt for firm, vibrant sweet potatoes to enhance the flavor and texture of your dish.
  • Don’t over-mash: Overworking the sweet potatoes can result in a sticky texture. Aim for smooth, fluffy mashed potatoes.
  • Balance sweetness: Taste the mixture before baking to ensure the sweetness complements the other dishes on your Thanksgiving table.
  • Experiment with toppings: Combine marshmallows, pecans, oats, or even crushed cookies to create your own signature casserole.

Thanksgiving is the perfect occasion to share a delectable sweet potato casserole with loved ones, showcasing seasonal ingredients in a memorable way. Whether you stick to tradition or experiment with new flavors, one thing’s for certain—sweet potato casserole will add a touch of warmth and sweetness to your holiday table!
