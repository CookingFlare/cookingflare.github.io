Sous Vide Roasted Brussels Sprouts: Crispy and Caramelized


Brussels sprouts have gained popularity in recent years as a versatile and nutritious vegetable. While traditionally roasted in the oven, today we’re going to explore a different cooking technique that will elevate these humble sprouts to new heights: sous vide roasting. This method ensures that the sprouts are perfectly cooked, crispy on the outside, and caramelized to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of Brussels sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of honey or maple syrup (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide machine to 185°F (85°C).

  2. Prepare the Brussels sprouts by trimming off the tough ends and removing any loose outer leaves. Cut larger sprouts in half to ensure even cooking.

  3. In a large bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, pepper, and minced garlic. Make sure the sprouts are well coated with the seasoning.

  4. Transfer the seasoned Brussels sprouts to a vacuum-sealable bag or a ziplock bag. If using a ziplock bag, remove as much air as possible by using the water displacement method.

  5. Once the sous vide machine has reached the desired temperature, carefully lower the bag into the water bath. Make sure the bag is fully submerged.

  6. Cook the Brussels sprouts for 45 minutes to 1 hour. This longer cooking time allows the sprouts to become tender while retaining their vibrant green color.

  7. While the Brussels sprouts are cooking, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  8. After the sous vide cooking time is complete, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully transfer the Brussels sprouts to the prepared baking sheet, making sure to spread them out in a single layer.

  9. Drizzle the sprouts with balsamic vinegar and, if desired, honey or maple syrup for a touch of sweetness.

  10. Roast the Brussels sprouts in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until they are golden brown and crispy on the edges.

  11. Once roasted, remove the sprouts from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

  12. Serve the sous vide roasted Brussels sprouts as a delicious side dish or even as a healthy snack. They pair well with a variety of main courses and are sure to impress your family and friends with their crispy texture and caramelized flavor.

Conclusion

Sous vide roasting is a game-changer when it comes to preparing Brussels sprouts. By cooking them in a water bath at a precise temperature, you ensure that each sprout is cooked evenly and retains its natural flavors and nutrients. The final step of roasting in the oven adds an extra layer of texture and caramelization that takes these sprouts to a whole new level of deliciousness.

So, next time you’re looking for a creative and flavorful way to enjoy Brussels sprouts, give this sous vide roasting method a try. You won’t be disappointed with the crispy, caramelized results that are sure to please even the most skeptical eaters. Enjoy!
