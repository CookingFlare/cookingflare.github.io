Paleo Pork Chops with Apples Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


As the weather starts to cool down, there’s nothing quite like a hearty and comforting meal to warm the soul. And what better way to achieve that than with a classic combination of pork chops and apples? In this post, we’ll explore five different Paleo pork chops with apples recipes that are not only delicious but also grain-free, making them perfect for those following a Paleo diet.

Recipe 1: Balsamic Glazed Pork Chops with Apples and Onions

This recipe is a twist on the classic pork chops with apples, adding a tangy and sweet glaze made with balsamic vinegar. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 4 pork chops
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 apples, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish

Preheat your oven to 400째F (200째C). In a large skillet, cook the pork chops over medium-high heat until browned on both sides, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the pork chops from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the sliced onions and cook until caramelized, about 5-6 minutes. Add the sliced apples to the skillet and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until they start to soften. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, honey, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Pour the glaze over the onions and apples, and stir to combine. Return the pork chops to the skillet and spoon some of the glaze over the top of each chop. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the pork chops are cooked through. Garnish with fresh thyme leaves and serve hot.

Recipe 2: Apple Cider Braised Pork Chops with Carrots and Parsnips

This recipe is perfect for a chilly fall or winter evening, as it’s slow-cooked in apple cider and flavored with aromatic spices. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 4 pork chops
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 large parsnips, peeled and chopped

Preheat your oven to 300째F (150째C). In a large Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the pork chops and cook until browned on both sides, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the pork chops from the Dutch oven and set aside. Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant. Add the apple cider, cinnamon, nutmeg, and allspice to the pot, and stir to combine. Return the pork chops to the pot and add the chopped carrots and parsnips. Cover the pot with a lid and transfer it to the preheated oven. Braise the pork chops for 2-3 hours, or until they’re tender and falling apart. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

Recipe 3: Pan-Seared Pork Chops with Apples and Mustard

This recipe is a quick and easy option that’s perfect for a weeknight dinner. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 4 pork chops
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large apple, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley leaves for garnish

Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the pork chops and cook until browned on both sides, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the pork chops from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the sliced apples and cook until caramelized, about 5-6 minutes. In a small bowl, whisk together the Dijon mustard and honey. Pour the glaze over the apples and stir to combine. Return the pork chops to the skillet and spoon some of the glaze over the top of each chop. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the pork chops are cooked through. Garnish with fresh parsley leaves and serve hot.

Recipe 4: Grilled Pork Chops with Apples and Rosemary

This recipe is perfect for a summer evening, as it’s grilled to perfection and flavored with aromatic rosemary. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 4 pork chops
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 large apples, sliced
  • 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, sliced apples, and sprigs of rosemary. Brush the mixture onto both sides of the pork chops. Grill the pork chops for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they’re cooked through. Serve hot, garnished with additional rosemary if desired.

Recipe 5: Pork Chops with Apples and Onions in a Cast-Iron Skillet

This recipe is a hearty and comforting option that’s perfect for a family dinner. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 4 pork chops
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 large apples, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Heat the olive oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Add the pork chops and cook until browned on both sides, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the pork chops from the skillet and set aside. Add the sliced onions to the skillet and cook until caramelized, about 5-6 minutes. Add the sliced apples to the skillet and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until they start to soften. In a small bowl, mix together the ground cinnamon and ground nutmeg. Sprinkle the spice mixture over the onions and apples. Return the pork chops to the skillet and spoon some of the onion and apple mixture over the top of each chop. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the pork chops are cooked through. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

These five recipes offer a variety of delicious and grain-free options for pairing pork chops with apples. Whether you’re in the mood for a hearty and comforting meal or a quick and easy weeknight dinner, there’s something on this list for everyone. So go ahead, get cooking, and enjoy the perfect combination of pork chops and apples!
