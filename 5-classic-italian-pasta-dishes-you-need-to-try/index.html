5 Classic Italian Pasta Dishes You Need to Try


Italian cuisine is famous for its rich history, diverse regional flavors, and the sheer simplicity of its dishes. Among the treasures of Italian cooking, pasta stands as one of the most iconic and beloved elements. With its endless varieties and infinite combinations of ingredients, pasta can be transformed into something truly spectacular with just a few simple touches. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, there are some classic Italian pasta dishes that are essential to know and try.

If you’ve ever had the pleasure of indulging in an authentic Italian meal, you’ll know that pasta can range from fresh, hand-rolled varieties to dried forms, with sauces that range from creamy and rich to light and flavorful. In this post, we’ll dive into five of the most cherished pasta dishes from Italy that will bring a taste of la dolce vita straight to your plate.

1. Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is one of Italy’s most iconic pasta dishes, and for good reason. Originating from Rome, this comforting dish is as simple as it is delicious. The key to its greatness lies in the quality of its ingredients and the delicate technique required to create a silky, creamy sauce without the use of cream.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g guanciale (cured pork cheek)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente, about 9 minutes (or according to package instructions).

  2. Cook the Guanciale: While the pasta is cooking, cut the guanciale into small cubes or strips. In a large pan, heat a small amount of olive oil over medium heat. Add the guanciale and cook until crispy and golden brown. Set aside.

  3. Prepare the Egg Mixture: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. This will form the creamy sauce once it combines with the pasta.

  4. Combine and Toss: When the pasta is done, reserve a cup of pasta cooking water and drain the pasta. Add the hot pasta directly to the pan with the crispy guanciale and toss to combine. Remove the pan from the heat and quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture, tossing continuously to create a creamy, smooth sauce. If necessary, add a little reserved pasta water to achieve the desired consistency.

  5. Serve: Plate the pasta and finish with additional black pepper and Pecorino Romano cheese. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Spaghetti Carbonara is a dish that exemplifies the Italian philosophy of cooking—using a few high-quality ingredients and simple techniques to create a meal that is nothing short of spectacular.

2. Lasagna alla Bolognese

No list of classic Italian pasta dishes would be complete without mentioning Lasagna. This layered pasta bake hails from the Emilia-Romagna region and is the epitome of comfort food. Lasagna alla Bolognese is made with layers of wide pasta sheets, rich Bolognese sauce, béchamel, and grated Parmigiano-Reggiano.

Ingredients:

  • 400g fresh lasagna sheets
  • 500g ground beef
  • 200g ground pork
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 400g canned tomatoes
  • 250ml beef broth
  • 100ml red wine
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 100g Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, grated
  • 500ml milk
  • 50g butter
  • 50g flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bolognese Sauce: Heat a splash of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until softened. Add the ground beef and pork, browning the meat until it’s fully cooked. Stir in the tomato paste, then add the red wine, allowing it to reduce by half.

  2. Add the Tomatoes: Once the wine has reduced, add the canned tomatoes and beef broth. Season with salt and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for about 1 hour, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and deepens in flavor.

  3. Make the Béchamel Sauce: In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for a minute to form a roux. Gradually whisk in the milk, ensuring there are no lumps. Continue to cook and stir until the sauce thickens, then season with salt and pepper.

  4. Assemble the Lasagna: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). In a large baking dish, spread a thin layer of Bolognese sauce at the bottom. Add a layer of lasagna sheets on top, followed by more Bolognese sauce and a generous spoonful of béchamel sauce. Sprinkle with grated Parmigiano-Reggiano. Repeat the layers until the dish is full, finishing with a top layer of béchamel and cheese.

  5. Bake: Cover the lasagna with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. After that, remove the foil and bake for another 10-15 minutes to allow the top to become golden and bubbly.

  6. Serve: Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before serving to allow the layers to set. Slice and enjoy!

This dish is perfect for feeding a crowd, and it’s even better the next day when the flavors have had time to meld together. Lasagna alla Bolognese is a true celebration of Italian flavors, combining rich, savory meat sauce with creamy béchamel and indulgent layers of pasta.

3. Pesto Genovese

Pesto Genovese is a fragrant, herbaceous pasta dish from the coastal region of Liguria. It’s the perfect representation of Italy’s love for fresh, seasonal ingredients. The combination of basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and olive oil creates a lush, flavorful sauce that pairs beautifully with any pasta shape.

Ingredients:

  • 200g fresh basil leaves
  • 50g pine nuts
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100g Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 50g Pecorino cheese
  • 200ml extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 400g pasta (trofie or trenette work best)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Pesto: In a mortar and pestle (or food processor), combine the basil, pine nuts, and garlic. Pound or pulse until the ingredients form a coarse paste. Gradually add the cheeses and olive oil, continuing to blend until the pesto is smooth and creamy. Season with salt to taste.

  2. Cook the Pasta: Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta until al dente. Save a cup of pasta water before draining.

  3. Combine: Return the pasta to the pot, add the pesto, and toss well, adding a little pasta water to help the sauce coat the noodles evenly.

  4. Serve: Plate the pasta and drizzle with a little more olive oil, if desired. Garnish with extra pine nuts and Parmigiano-Reggiano for an added touch of flavor.

Pesto Genovese is a bright and refreshing dish, perfect for summer months when basil is at its peak. It’s a versatile sauce that can be used with a variety of pastas, making it a dish you’ll want to return to again and again.

4. Orecchiette con Cime di Rapa

Orecchiette con Cime di Rapa is a traditional pasta dish from the Puglia region in southern Italy. The name “orecchiette” translates to “little ears,” referring to the pasta’s distinctive shape. This dish pairs the pasta with cime di rapa, a type of bitter broccoli, and features the flavors of garlic, anchovies, and red pepper flakes.

Ingredients:

  • 400g orecchiette pasta
  • 300g cime di rapa (or broccoli rabe)
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 2 anchovy fillets
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • Olive oil for cooking
  • Salt for pasta water

Instructions:

  1. Blanch the Greens: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and add the cime di rapa. Cook for 3-4 minutes, then remove and set aside. In the same water, cook the orecchiette until al dente.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: While the pasta is cooking, heat olive oil in a large pan. Add the sliced garlic, anchovies, and red pepper flakes. Sauté over low heat until the garlic is golden and the anchovies have dissolved into the oil.

  3. Combine: Add the cooked cime di rapa to the pan and toss together with the garlic and anchovy mixture. When the pasta is ready, add it to the pan, tossing everything together to combine.

  4. Serve: Plate the pasta, drizzling with a little extra olive oil and a sprinkling of Parmigiano-Reggiano if desired.

Orecchiette con Cime di Rapa is a dish that highlights the balance of bitter and savory flavors. The richness of the anchovies and garlic pairs perfectly with the slight bitterness of the greens, making it a truly unique and satisfying meal.

5. Fettuccine Alfredo

Fettuccine Alfredo may be more famous in the United States than in Italy, but it still holds a special place in Italian kitchens, especially in Rome. This dish is known for its creamy, buttery sauce that coats each strand of pasta in richness.

Ingredients:

  • 400g fettuccine pasta
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 150g heavy cream
  • 100g Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, grated
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the fettuccine until al dente.

  2. Make the Alfredo Sauce: In a large pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the heavy cream and cook for a couple of minutes until warmed through. Stir in the grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese and season with salt and pepper.

  3. Combine: Once the pasta is cooked, reserve a bit of pasta water and then drain the fettuccine. Add the pasta directly to the pan with the sauce and toss to coat. Add a little pasta water if needed to adjust the sauce’s consistency.

  4. Serve: Serve the fettuccine with additional cheese and freshly ground black pepper.

Fettuccine Alfredo is a decadently rich dish that’s beloved for its simplicity and indulgence. Its creamy, buttery sauce is the ultimate comfort food and perfect for a special occasion or a treat.

Conclusion

These five classic Italian pasta dishes—Spaghetti Carbonara, Lasagna alla Bolognese, Pesto Genovese, Orecchiette con Cime di Rapa, and Fettuccine Alfredo—represent the heart and soul of Italy’s culinary traditions. Each dish tells a story of the region it comes from, highlighting local ingredients, flavors, and techniques that have been passed down through generations.

No matter which one you decide to try first, you’re guaranteed to experience the magic of Italian cooking in your own kitchen. So gather your ingredients, get your hands on some good-quality pasta, and let the flavors of Italy transform your next meal.
