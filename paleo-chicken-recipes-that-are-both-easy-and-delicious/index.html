Paleo Chicken Recipes That Are Both Easy and Delicious


Chicken is a staple for many who follow the Paleo diet, and for good reason—it’s versatile, protein-packed, and pairs beautifully with the nutrient-rich whole foods at the core of Paleo living. Whether you’re new to the Paleo lifestyle or simply looking to spice up your weekly meal plan, these Paleo chicken recipes are designed to be easy to prepare while delivering satisfying flavors. From simple skillet dishes to hearty soups and vibrant salads, these recipes will make you fall in love with chicken all over again.

The Foundations of Paleo Cooking

Before diving into the recipes, it’s important to share a few insights into Paleo cooking. Paleo meals emphasize whole, unprocessed foods with a focus on ingredients like lean proteins, healthy fats, non-starchy vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds. Grains, legumes, dairy products, processed sugars, and most modern processed foods are off-limits. By working with fresh, wholesome ingredients, you’ll not only create delicious meals but also nourish your body in a way that promotes optimal health and energy levels.

Now that the basics are covered, let’s explore five Paleo chicken recipes that are both incredibly easy and packed with flavor.

1. Lemon Garlic Roasted Chicken Thighs

This quick-and-easy roasted chicken thigh recipe bursts with citrusy freshness and savory garlicky notes. Perfect for weeknight dinners or meal prep, these chicken thighs require minimal preparation while delivering a bold punch of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 6 chicken thighs (skin-on, bone-in)
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil (or olive oil)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix the avocado oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, minced garlic, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the mixture evenly over the chicken thighs, ensuring the chicken is well-coated.
  4. Place the chicken thighs onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or into a greased roasting pan.
  5. Roast in the oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C).
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving. Pair with roasted Brussels sprouts or cauliflower rice for a complete Paleo-friendly meal.

2. Coconut Chicken Curry

This creamy, dairy-free chicken curry showcases the rich flavors of coconut milk and aromatic spices. It’s a vibrant, comforting dish that’s perfect for busy nights when you need something quick yet satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 3 cups spinach
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and ginger to the skillet, cooking for an additional minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in curry powder, turmeric, and cumin, allowing the spices to toast for 30 seconds.
  4. Add the chicken pieces to the skillet, season with salt and pepper, and cook until no longer pink, around 5-6 minutes.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth. Simmer for 10 minutes until the sauce thickens and comes together.
  6. Add the spinach and stir until wilted, about 2 minutes.
  7. Serve warm, garnished with fresh cilantro, alongside cauliflower rice or zucchini noodles.

3. Paleo-Friendly Chicken Salad with Avocado Dressing

This refreshing chicken salad uses a creamy avocado-based dressing instead of traditional mayonnaise, making it fully Paleo-compliant and loaded with healthy fats.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked chicken breast, shredded or diced
  • 1 avocado, peeled and pitted
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley or cilantro

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, blend avocado, lime juice, olive oil, garlic, Dijon mustard (if using), salt, and pepper until smooth. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine shredded chicken, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and fresh parsley or cilantro.
  3. Pour the avocado dressing over the salad and toss until everything is thoroughly coated.
  4. Serve immediately or chill in the refrigerator until ready to eat.

4. Garlic Herb Grilled Chicken Breasts

This simple yet flavorful grilled chicken recipe is perfect for outdoor cookouts or quick meals. Customize the herbs to suit your taste and enjoy the delicious smokiness brought out by grilling.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a shallow dish or resealable bag. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 8 hours in the refrigerator.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove chicken from the marinade and discard leftover marinade.
  4. Grill the chicken for 5-7 minutes per side, or until an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C) is reached.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite Paleo-friendly sides like grilled zucchini or a fresh green salad.

5. Chicken Stir-Fry with Vegetables

Packed with vibrant veggies, this chicken stir-fry is a quick and easy one-pan meal that’s perfect for those busy nights when you need dinner on the table fast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup coconut aminos (a Paleo-friendly soy sauce alternative)
  • 1 tsp arrowroot powder (optional, for thickening)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat avocado oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and season with salt and pepper. Cook for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is fully cooked.
  2. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Add the red bell pepper, broccoli, carrot, and garlic to the same skillet. Stir-fry for 4-5 minutes, until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. Return the chicken to the skillet and pour in the coconut aminos. If you prefer a thicker stir-fry sauce, mix the coconut aminos with arrowroot powder before adding. Toss everything together and cook for 1-2 minutes until the sauce coats the chicken and vegetables.
  5. Serve warm on its own or over cauliflower rice for a complete Paleo meal.

Final Thoughts

These Paleo chicken recipes prove that simplicity doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor. By prioritizing fresh, whole ingredients, you can create meals that are nourishing, delicious, and perfect for anyone following a Paleo lifestyle. Don’t hesitate to experiment with herbs, spices, and vegetables to tailor these recipes to your personal preferences. Whether you’re entertaining guests or whipping up a quick weekday meal, these recipes are sure to become staples in your Paleo kitchen.

Start exploring these recipes today and rediscover the joys of cooking with clean, flavorful ingredients. Healthy eating has never been this satisfying!
