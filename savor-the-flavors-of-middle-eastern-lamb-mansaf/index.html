Savor the Flavors of Middle Eastern Lamb Mansaf


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’re in for a treat with this incredible recipe for Lamb Mansaf. This traditional Jordanian dish is a true celebration of flavors, combining tender lamb, fragrant rice, and a rich, tangy sauce. Mansaf is often enjoyed during special occasions and gatherings, making it the perfect dish to impress your guests with its unique and delicious taste.

Ingredients

For the Lamb:

  • 2 lbs of lamb shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil

For the Sauce:

  • 2 cups of plain yogurt
  • 3 cups of chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup of pine nuts
  • 2 tablespoons of ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups of long-grain rice
  • 1/4 cup of ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • Salt to taste

For Garnish:

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Almonds, toasted

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Lamb: In a large bowl, combine the lamb chunks, chopped onion, minced garlic, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the lamb is evenly coated with the spices. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a large pot, heat the ghee over medium heat. Add the pine nuts and toast them until golden brown. Remove the pine nuts from the pot and set them aside. In the same pot, add the marinated lamb and brown it on all sides. Once the lamb is browned, pour in the chicken broth and bring it to a gentle simmer. Allow the lamb to cook in the broth for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until it becomes tender.

  3. Prepare the Rice: In a separate pot, heat the ghee over medium heat. Add the rice and toast it for a few minutes until it becomes fragrant. Pour in enough water to cover the rice by about an inch. Add the ground turmeric and salt. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Allow the rice to cook for 15-20 minutes, or until it is tender and the water is absorbed.

  4. Assemble the Mansaf: Once the lamb is tender, remove it from the pot and set it aside. Whisk the plain yogurt into the sauce remaining in the pot, creating a creamy and tangy mixture. Adjust the seasoning with salt if needed. To serve, spread the cooked rice on a large platter, then arrange the tender lamb on top. Pour the creamy sauce over the lamb and rice, then sprinkle the toasted pine nuts, chopped parsley, and almonds on top as a garnish.

Enjoy the Feast

Lamb Mansaf is best enjoyed with loved ones, as it embodies the warmth and hospitality of Middle Eastern culture. The rich, savory flavors of the lamb, combined with the fragrant rice and tangy yogurt sauce, create a truly memorable dining experience. Whether you’re hosting a special gathering or simply want to indulge in a delicious and exotic meal, Lamb Mansaf is sure to delight your taste buds and leave you craving more. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the flavors of the Middle East, and savor every bite of this remarkable dish.
