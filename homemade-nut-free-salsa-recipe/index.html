Homemade Nut-Free Salsa Recipe


Are you tired of buying store-bought salsa that may contain nuts or other allergens? Look no further! In this recipe, we will guide you through making a delicious and nut-free salsa right in the comfort of your own kitchen. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a flavorful and fresh salsa that will be a hit at any gathering or as a tasty topping for your favorite Mexican dishes.

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Begin by preparing the tomatoes. Rinse them under cold water and pat them dry. Remove the core and chop them into small, bite-sized pieces. Place them in a large mixing bowl.

  2. Finely chop the red onion and add it to the bowl with the tomatoes.

  3. Next, it’s time to prepare the jalapeno pepper. Carefully remove the seeds and membranes to reduce the heat, if desired. Mince the pepper and add it to the bowl.

  4. Chop the fresh cilantro and mince the garlic cloves. Add them to the bowl along with the lime juice, cumin, salt, and pepper.

  5. Now, it’s time to mix everything together. Gently toss the ingredients in the bowl until they are well combined. Make sure the flavors are evenly distributed throughout the salsa.

  6. Taste the salsa and adjust the seasonings according to your preference. If you like it spicier, you can add more jalapeno pepper. If you prefer it milder, reduce the amount of jalapeno or remove the seeds completely.

  7. Once you are satisfied with the taste, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the salsa to develop its full potential.

  8. After chilling, give the salsa a final stir and serve it with your favorite tortilla chips or as a topping for tacos, burritos, or grilled chicken. The possibilities are endless!

Tips and Variations

  • For a smoky flavor, you can roast the tomatoes, red onion, and jalapeno pepper in the oven before combining them with the other ingredients.
  • If you prefer a chunkier salsa, simply dice the tomatoes and other ingredients larger.
  • Experiment with different types of peppers to add more heat or different flavors to your salsa. Try serrano peppers or even habaneros if you’re feeling adventurous.
  • If you like a hint of sweetness in your salsa, you can add a teaspoon of honey or agave syrup.
  • Feel free to add other ingredients to customize your salsa to your liking. Some popular additions include corn, black beans, mango, or pineapple.

Now that you have this easy and nut-free salsa recipe in your arsenal, you can enjoy the fresh flavors of homemade salsa whenever you please. Say goodbye to store-bought options that may not meet your dietary needs or taste preferences. Whip up a batch of this delicious salsa and impress your friends and family with your culinary skills. Enjoy!
