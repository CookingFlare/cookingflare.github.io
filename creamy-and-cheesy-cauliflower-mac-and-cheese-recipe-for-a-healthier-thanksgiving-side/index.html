Creamy and Cheesy Cauliflower Mac and Cheese Recipe for a Healthier Thanksgiving Side


Thanksgiving is a time for indulgence and enjoying delicious comfort foods. However, if you’re looking for a healthier twist on a classic dish, look no further than this Creamy and Cheesy Cauliflower Mac and Cheese recipe. By replacing traditional macaroni with cauliflower, you can still enjoy all the creamy, cheesy goodness while adding a nutritious element to your Thanksgiving spread.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head of cauliflower
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups milk (you can use any type of milk, including plant-based alternatives)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: chopped fresh parsley or breadcrumbs

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a baking dish and set aside.

  2. Wash the cauliflower thoroughly and remove the leaves and stem. Cut the cauliflower into small florets, resembling the size of macaroni.

  3. Boil a large pot of water and add the cauliflower florets. Cook for about 5 minutes until the cauliflower is tender but still firm. Drain the cauliflower and set aside.

  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and whisk continuously for about 1 minute to create a roux. This will help thicken the sauce.

  5. Slowly add the milk to the saucepan, whisking constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Continue whisking until the mixture thickens and comes to a gentle simmer.

  6. Reduce the heat to low and add the garlic powder, onion powder, nutmeg, salt, and black pepper to the sauce. Stir until well combined.

  7. Gradually add the shredded cheddar, mozzarella, and grated Parmesan cheese to the sauce, stirring until melted and smooth.

  8. Add the cooked cauliflower florets to the cheese sauce, gently folding them in until well coated.

  9. Transfer the cauliflower mac and cheese mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it evenly.

  10. If desired, sprinkle some chopped fresh parsley or breadcrumbs on top for added flavor and texture.

  11. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the dish is bubbling.

  12. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Creamy and Cheesy Cauliflower Mac and Cheese makes for a delicious Thanksgiving side dish that everyone will love. Serve it alongside roasted turkey, cranberry sauce, and a fresh green salad for a well-rounded holiday meal. You can also enjoy this dish on its own as a comforting and satisfying vegetarian main course.

Conclusion

With this Creamy and Cheesy Cauliflower Mac and Cheese recipe, you can enjoy a healthier twist on a classic Thanksgiving side dish. By substituting cauliflower for traditional macaroni, you’re adding a nutritious element to your meal without sacrificing flavor or creaminess. This dish is sure to be a hit at your Thanksgiving gathering, and it’s so delicious that even the pickiest eaters won’t be able to resist. Give it a try and enjoy the best of both worlds – indulgence and health – this holiday season!
