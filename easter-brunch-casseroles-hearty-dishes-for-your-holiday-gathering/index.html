Easter Brunch Casseroles: Hearty Dishes for Your Holiday Gathering


As the Easter holiday approaches, many of us are already thinking about the delicious meals we’ll be enjoying with our loved ones. While traditional Easter Sunday dinner often focuses on ham or lamb, Easter brunch is a wonderful opportunity to create a heartier, more satisfying meal that’s perfect for a springtime celebration.

One of the most convenient and crowd-pleasing ways to serve Easter brunch is with a casserole. These one-dish wonders can be made ahead of time, are easy to transport, and offer a satisfying combination of flavors and textures that will leave your guests feeling full and content.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the most popular Easter brunch casserole recipes, each with its own unique twist and flavor profile. From classic breakfast-inspired dishes to more adventurous options that incorporate seasonal ingredients, we’re confident you’ll find the perfect casserole to serve at your Easter gathering.

Casserole Recipe 1: Spinach and Feta Strata

A strata is a type of Italian-inspired casserole that’s perfect for brunch. This recipe combines fresh spinach, crumbled feta cheese, and crusty bread to create a rich and satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 loaf Italian bread, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 6 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the chopped spinach and garlic in olive oil until wilted.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, Parmesan cheese, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, create a layer of bread cubes.
  5. Top the bread with the spinach mixture, followed by a sprinkle of feta cheese.
  6. Repeat the layers two more times, ending with a layer of bread on top.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the bread, making sure to cover the entire surface.
  8. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the top is golden brown.

Casserole Recipe 2: Asparagus and Prosciutto Quiche

Quiche is a classic brunch dish that’s easy to make and can be customized with a variety of ingredients. This recipe combines the flavors of asparagus and prosciutto for a delicious and elegant Easter brunch option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust
  • 1 1/2 cups grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh asparagus
  • 6 slices prosciutto, chopped
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and place in a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, heavy cream, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Arrange the chopped asparagus and prosciutto in the pie crust, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.
  5. Sprinkle the grated cheese over the asparagus and prosciutto.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the cheese, making sure to cover the entire surface.
  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the crust is golden brown.

Casserole Recipe 3: Sweet Potato and Sage Hash Brown Casserole

This casserole is a twist on the classic hash brown casserole, with the added sweetness of sweet potatoes and the earthy flavor of sage.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and grated
  • 1 large onion, grated
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh sage
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the grated sweet potatoes, onion, and butter.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together heavy cream, sage, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Add the cream mixture to the sweet potato mixture and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a 9x13-inch baking dish and top with shredded cheese.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and the top is golden brown.

Casserole Recipe 4: Broccoli and Cheddar Frittata

Frittatas are a great option for Easter brunch, as they’re easy to make and can be customized with a variety of ingredients. This recipe combines the flavors of broccoli and cheddar for a delicious and satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 6 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and place in a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Arrange the broccoli florets in the pie crust, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.
  5. Sprinkle the grated cheese over the broccoli.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the cheese, making sure to cover the entire surface.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the crust is golden brown.

These are just a few ideas to get you started on your Easter brunch casserole journey. Whether you’re looking for a classic breakfast-inspired dish or something more adventurous, we’re confident you’ll find the perfect recipe to serve at your holiday gathering. Happy cooking!
