Step-by-Step Guide to Crafting a Neapolitan Pizza


Few culinary experiences match the joy of biting into a perfectly crafted Neapolitan pizza. With its iconic thin, airy crust, slightly charred edges, tangy tomato base, and creamy mozzarella cheese, the Neapolitan pizza has established itself as a centerpiece of global cuisine. Though rooted in Naples, Italy, this pizza style can be recreated in kitchens worldwide with some dedication and attention to detail. In this comprehensive guide, I’ll walk you through how to create an authentic Neapolitan pizza step by step, from preparing the dough to perfecting the bake.

The Hallmarks of a Neapolitan Pizza

Before diving into the process, let’s explore what makes a Neapolitan pizza so special. Unlike other pizzas, this style emphasizes simplicity and quality. A traditional Neapolitan pizza features:

  • Authentic Dough: Made with Italian-style flour, water, salt, and yeast. No oil, sugar, or additives—just a basic recipe that allows the subtle flavor of the dough to shine.
  • San Marzano Tomatoes: For the sauce, these sweet and tangy tomatoes are a key ingredient.
  • Mozzarella di Bufala or Fior di Latte: Creamy, fresh mozzarella cheese pulls the dish together. Quality matters here.
  • High-Temperature Baking: Neapolitan pizza is traditionally cooked in a wood-fired oven that reaches temperatures of 800°F (426°C) or higher, creating its iconic charred edges and soft center.

Now that we know the fundamentals, let’s roll up our sleeves and get to work.

Step 1: Preparing Authentic Dough

The dough is the foundation of any great pizza, and with a Neapolitan style, simplicity rules. This recipe yields two medium pizzas.

Ingredients:

  • 500g 00 flour (Italian-style finely ground flour)
  • 325ml warm water
  • 10g sea salt
  • 3g fresh yeast (or 1g active dry yeast)

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, dissolve the fresh yeast in warm water. Add the salt and mix well. Gradually incorporate the flour, stirring with your hands or a wooden spoon until a sticky dough forms.

  2. Knead the Dough: Transfer the mixture onto a lightly floured surface. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth, elastic, and no longer sticks to the counter. The goal is to develop the gluten for a light, airy crust.

  3. Rest and Rise: Place the dough in a clean bowl, cover it with a damp kitchen towel, and let it rise at room temperature for 8-12 hours. Alternatively, you can let it rise overnight in the refrigerator for slow fermentation, which enhances the flavor.

  4. Divide and Shape: After rising, divide the dough into two equal portions. Shape each portion into a smooth ball by folding the edges inward. Place the dough balls on a floured surface, cover them with a cloth, and let them rest for an additional hour.

Step 2: Crafting the Perfect Tomato Sauce

The sauce for Neapolitan pizza is raw and minimal, allowing the natural flavor of the tomatoes to shine.

Ingredients:

  • 250g San Marzano tomatoes
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • Optional: A small drizzle of extra virgin olive oil or torn fresh basil leaves for added flavor

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tomatoes: If using canned San Marzano tomatoes, drain slightly and crush them by hand or with a fork. Authenticity comes from their rustic texture.

  2. Season: Stir in a pinch of sea salt. If desired, add a small drizzle of olive oil or a few torn basil leaves. Avoid overloading the sauce with spices—the natural brightness of the tomatoes should stand out.

Step 3: Selecting and Preparing the Cheese

For a true Neapolitan pizza, mozzarella takes center stage. Mozzarella di Bufala is often preferred for its rich creaminess, but Fior di Latte is an excellent alternative.

Instructions:

  1. Drain the Cheese: If using fresh mozzarella, drain excess liquid to avoid a soggy pizza. Pat the cheese dry with a paper towel.
  2. Slice or Tear: Cut the mozzarella into small chunks or tear it into bite-sized pieces. Keep it evenly distributed to ensure every bite has a creamy component.

Step 4: Preheating the Oven

Achieving the blistered crust of a Neapolitan pizza requires intense heat. If you don’t have a wood-fired oven, a home oven combined with a pizza stone or steel can produce stellar results.

Instructions:

  1. Position the Pizza Stone: Place the stone or steel on the middle rack of your oven.
  2. Preheat to Maximum Heat: Set your oven to its highest setting, typically around 500°F (260°C), and let it preheat for at least an hour. This provides the stone sufficient time to reach optimal temperature.

Step 5: Assembling Your Neapolitan Pizza

Instructions:

  1. Stretch the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, gently stretch one dough ball into a 10-12 inch circle. Avoid using a rolling pin—stretch the dough by hand to preserve air bubbles.
  2. Spread the Sauce: Using the back of a spoon, spread a thin layer of tomato sauce onto the dough, leaving about an inch of space around the edge for the crust.
  3. Add Cheese: Scatter the mozzarella chunks or pieces evenly over the sauce.
  4. Optional Toppings: Traditional Neapolitan pizza is minimalist, but feel free to add a few fresh basil leaves or a drizzle of olive oil.

Step 6: Baking the Pizza

Instructions:

  1. Transfer to the Oven: Carefully transfer the assembled pizza onto the preheated stone or steel.
  2. Cook Until Perfect: Bake for 6-8 minutes, or until the crust is slightly charred and bubbly, and the cheese is melted and golden. Neapolitan pizza cooks quickly due to the high temperature.
  3. Cool Briefly: Once removed from the oven, let the pizza cool for two minutes to avoid burning your tongue on piping-hot cheese.

Step 7: Enjoying Your Creation

Slice the pizza into wedges and savor every bite. The balance of airy crust, rich tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella, and slight char marks is everything you’d expect from an authentic Neapolitan experience. Pair it with a light salad or a glass of Italian red wine to elevate your meal.

Tips for Perfecting Your Neapolitan Pizza

  • Do Not Overload Toppings: The essence of a great Neapolitan pizza lies in its simplicity. Keep the focus on the crust, sauce, and cheese combo.
  • Invest in Good Flour: Italian 00 flour has a fine texture and high gluten content, creating the light and airy crust associated with Neapolitan pizzas.
  • Experiment with Oven Techniques: If you don’t have a pizza stone, an upside-down baking sheet can serve as a decent alternative.
  • Master the Stretching: Hand-stretching your dough will preserve the delicate air bubbles, resulting in an airy, soft texture.

Conclusion

Crafting a Neapolitan pizza at home may seem daunting, but with patience and practice, you’ll master the process and delight in the results. From the hand-kneaded dough to the high-temperature bake, each step honors the centuries-old tradition of pizza-making while allowing room for your personal touch. Whether it’s your first time attempting this iconic dish or a delightful way to hone your culinary skills, creating a Neapolitan pizza is a unique journey that rewards you with every bite. Try it today, and bring the flavors of Naples into your kitchen!
