Grilled Pizza: A New Spin on BBQ Classics


When summer arrives, the call of the grill becomes irresistible. The smoky aroma wafting through the air, the sizzle of meat hitting hot grates, and the anticipation of a delicious meal shared with family and friends are all part of the magic. But what if you could elevate your outdoor cooking game by adding a twist to a classic favorite? Enter grilled pizza—an innovative way to enjoy this beloved dish right on your barbecue.

Why Grilled Pizza?

Grilled pizza combines the best of both worlds: the crispy, charred crust of wood-fired pizza and the convenience of cooking on a grill. The high heat of the grill creates a beautiful crust, while the toppings melt and caramelize to perfection. It’s not only a fun and interactive cooking method but also a fantastic way to cater to diverse tastes. Whether you prefer classic pepperoni or gourmet toppings, grilling pizza allows for endless customization.

The Perfect Pizza Dough

Before diving into the toppings and grilling techniques, let’s start with the foundation: the pizza dough. Making your own dough is simple and rewarding. Here’s a basic recipe to get you started.

Ingredients for Pizza Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3/4 cup warm water (around 110°F)

Instructions

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the flour, yeast, and salt. Stir until well mixed.

  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Create a well in the center and add the olive oil and warm water. Mix until a rough dough forms.

  3. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a floured surface. Knead for about 5-7 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  4. Let it Rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

  5. Shape the Dough: Once risen, punch down the dough and divide it into two equal portions. Shape each portion into a ball and let it rest for another 15 minutes before rolling out.

Grilling Techniques

Grilling pizza requires a different approach than baking in an oven. Here are some essential tips to ensure perfect results:

Preheat the Grill

Before grilling, preheat your grill to a high temperature, around 500°F. This creates a hot surface that mimics a pizza oven, allowing for a crispy crust while keeping the toppings fresh.

Use a Pizza Stone or Direct Grilling

You can grill pizza directly on the grates or use a pizza stone. A stone helps distribute heat evenly and absorbs moisture, resulting in a perfectly crisp crust. If you prefer direct grilling, ensure your grates are clean and well-oiled to prevent sticking.

Prepare Your Toppings

While your grill is heating up, prepare your toppings. Choose a balance of flavors and textures. Here are some classic and creative combinations to consider:

  • Classic Margherita: Fresh mozzarella, sliced tomatoes, and basil leaves with a drizzle of olive oil.
  • BBQ Chicken: Grilled chicken, barbecue sauce, red onions, and cilantro.
  • Mediterranean Delight: Feta cheese, Kalamata olives, artichoke hearts, and spinach.
  • Breakfast Pizza: Scrambled eggs, bacon, cheddar cheese, and a sprinkle of chives.

Grilling Your Pizza

  1. Shape the Dough: Roll out your rested dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.

  2. Oil the Grate: Use a paper towel dipped in oil to grease the grill grates.

  3. Pre-cook the Crust: Place the rolled-out dough directly on the grill grates. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the bottom is golden brown. Use tongs to check.

  4. Flip and Add Toppings: Once the bottom is cooked, flip the dough using tongs. Quickly add your sauce and toppings to the cooked side.

  5. Close the Lid: Lower the grill lid to retain heat and help melt the cheese and cook the toppings. Grill for an additional 3-5 minutes.

  6. Check for Doneness: The pizza is ready when the cheese is bubbly and the crust is crispy. Remove it from the grill using a pizza peel or spatula.

Grilled Pizza Recipes

1. Spicy Pepperoni and Jalapeño

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (prepared as above)
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup sliced pepperoni
  • 1/2 cup sliced jalapeños (fresh or pickled)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions

  1. Roll out the pizza dough and pre-cook it on the grill.
  2. Spread pizza sauce over the cooked side of the dough.
  3. Sprinkle mozzarella cheese and layer with pepperoni and jalapeños.
  4. Grill as described above until cheese is melted and bubbly.

2. Grilled Veggie Delight

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (prepared as above)
  • 1/2 cup pesto sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup assorted grilled vegetables (zucchini, bell peppers, onions)
  • Balsamic glaze for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Roll out the dough and pre-cook it on the grill.
  2. Spread pesto sauce over the cooked side.
  3. Add mozzarella cheese and top with grilled vegetables.
  4. Grill until cheese is melted. Drizzle with balsamic glaze before serving.

3. Fig and Prosciutto Gourmet Pizza

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (prepared as above)
  • 1/2 cup fig jam
  • 1 cup arugula
  • 1 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 6 slices prosciutto
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Roll out the pizza dough and pre-cook it on the grill.
  2. Spread fig jam over the cooked side.
  3. Crumble goat cheese and layer with prosciutto.
  4. Grill until heated through, then top with fresh arugula and a drizzle of olive oil.

4. Thai Chicken Pizza

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (prepared as above)
  • 1/2 cup peanut sauce
  • 1 cup cooked, shredded chicken
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Roll out the dough and pre-cook it on the grill.
  2. Spread peanut sauce over the cooked side.
  3. Add shredded chicken, carrots, and mozzarella cheese.
  4. Grill until cheese is melted. Garnish with green onions and cilantro before serving.

Pairing Suggestions

Grilled pizza pairs wonderfully with a variety of sides and beverages. Consider serving it with a fresh salad, grilled vegetables, or even some homemade garlic knots. For drinks, craft beers, fruity cocktails, or a light-bodied red wine complement the flavors beautifully.

Conclusion

Grilled pizza is a fun, versatile, and delicious way to enjoy a classic favorite during your summer barbecues. By experimenting with different dough recipes, sauces, and toppings, you can create a unique pizza that reflects your personal taste. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and savor the delightful experience that grilled pizza brings to outdoor dining. Your guests will be raving about this new spin on BBQ classics for years to come!
