How to Make the Perfect French Coq au Vin


If you’re looking for a classic French dish that’s rich, flavorful, and perfect for a special occasion, then Coq au Vin is the answer. This traditional French stew features tender chicken cooked in red wine with aromatic vegetables and herbs, creating a dish that’s both comforting and elegant. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the steps to create the perfect Coq au Vin that will impress your guests and transport them to the heart of France.

Ingredients

Before you start cooking, gather the following ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 200g of bacon, diced
  • 10-12 small pearl onions
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 bottle of good quality red wine
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3-4 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

Start by marinating the chicken pieces in a bowl with a generous amount of red wine, chopped onions, garlic, thyme, and bay leaves. Let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or preferably overnight. This step will infuse the chicken with the rich flavors of the wine and aromatics.

Step 2: Sear the Chicken and Bacon

Once the chicken has marinated, remove it from the bowl and pat it dry with paper towels. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, heat some olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the chicken pieces until they are golden brown on all sides. Remove the chicken and set it aside. In the same pot, add the diced bacon and cook until it’s crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.

Step 3: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pot, add the pearl onions, carrots, and celery. Sauté the vegetables until they start to caramelize and develop a rich flavor. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat evenly. This will help thicken the sauce later on.

Step 4: Deglaze with Wine and Stock

Return the chicken and bacon to the pot with the sautéed vegetables. Pour in the remaining red wine from the marinade, chicken stock, and tomato paste. Stir everything together and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Cover the pot and let it cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the chicken is tender and the flavors have melded beautifully.

Step 5: Final Touches

Once the Coq au Vin is ready, taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. Remove the bay leaves and thyme sprigs. Serve the Coq au Vin in deep plates, garnished with fresh parsley. This dish is traditionally enjoyed with crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or buttered noodles.

Conclusion

Making the perfect French Coq au Vin is a labor of love, but the results are well worth the effort. This soul-warming dish is a timeless classic that embodies the essence of French culinary tradition. With its deep, complex flavors and tender chicken, Coq au Vin is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a special meal, this recipe will elevate any occasion with its rustic charm and exquisite taste. Bon appétit!
