Greek Olive and Herb Focaccia: A Fragrant and Savory Bread


If there’s one bread that can transport you to the sun-soaked shores of Greece with just one bite, it’s the Greek Olive and Herb Focaccia. This fragrant and savory bread is a delightful mix of chewy, airy dough infused with the richness of olive oil, the brininess of olives, and the aromatic flavors of Mediterranean herbs. Whether you’re a seasoned home baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through the process of creating a truly irresistible loaf that’s perfect for sharing with loved ones or savoring all to yourself.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups of warm water
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/3 cup of extra virgin olive oil

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup of mixed Greek olives, pitted and halved
  • 2-3 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons of fresh thyme leaves
  • Coarse sea salt
  • Additional olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy.

  2. Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.

  3. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 10-15 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  4. First Rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1-1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Shape the Focaccia: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and transfer it to a parchment-lined baking sheet. Gently stretch and press the dough to fit the baking sheet. Cover the dough with the same kitchen towel and let it rise for an additional 30-45 minutes.

  6. Preheat the Oven: While the dough is rising, preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).

  7. Add Toppings: After the second rise, use your fingers to create dimples all over the dough. Drizzle the top with olive oil, then sprinkle the olives, rosemary, thyme, and a pinch of coarse sea salt evenly over the surface.

  8. Bake: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

  9. Enjoy: Once baked, transfer the focaccia to a wire rack to cool slightly. Serve it warm or at room temperature, and savor the delightful blend of flavors with each and every bite.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more intense olive flavor, you can use Kalamata olives in the topping.
  • Experiment with different herb combinations such as oregano, basil, or marjoram to suit your taste preferences.
  • This focaccia pairs wonderfully with a variety of dishes, from vibrant Greek salads to hearty stews.

There you have it – a recipe that celebrates the vibrant flavors of Greece in every mouthwatering bite. Whether you’re seeking a delightful accompaniment to a meal or a satisfying snack on its own, this Greek Olive and Herb Focaccia is sure to leave a lasting impression. Embrace the art of bread making and let the aroma of freshly baked focaccia fill your kitchen, inviting everyone to gather around and indulge in a slice of Mediterranean delight.
