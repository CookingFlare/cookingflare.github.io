10 Easy and Affordable Stir-Fry Recipes


Stir-fry dishes are a go-to meal for many home cooks because they are quick, customizable, and full of vibrant flavors. Whether you’re cooking for one or feeding a crowd, stir-fries are perfect for busy weeknights or casual weekend meals. These meals are not only packed with healthy vegetables, proteins, and flavor, but they also offer endless possibilities for substitutions based on what you have in your kitchen. In this post, we’ll walk you through 10 easy and affordable stir-fry recipes that will make dinner a breeze.

1. Classic Chicken and Vegetable Stir-Fry

If you’re looking for a simple stir-fry recipe, a classic chicken and vegetable stir-fry is an excellent place to start. The savory chicken breast pairs perfectly with crunchy vegetables, creating a balanced and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chicken slices and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add garlic and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the broccoli, bell pepper, and carrots. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until tender but still crisp.
  6. Return the chicken to the skillet. Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Stir to coat the chicken and vegetables evenly.
  7. Cook for another 2–3 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice or noodles.

This chicken and vegetable stir-fry is not only healthy but incredibly versatile. You can swap out the vegetables for whatever you have on hand, like zucchini, mushrooms, or snap peas.

2. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

This classic beef and broccoli stir-fry is a favorite for its tender beef and savory sauce that brings out the best in the vegetables. It’s a dish that’s simple to make and can be ready in less than 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced beef and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the garlic and ginger. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the broccoli and cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Return the beef to the skillet. Add soy sauce, hoisin sauce, and oyster sauce. Stir to combine.
  7. Cook for another 2–3 minutes, ensuring the sauce coats the beef and broccoli evenly.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.

This beef and broccoli stir-fry is a great option for a quick weeknight dinner. The rich sauce enhances the flavors of the beef and vegetables, making it a hit with everyone at the table.

3. Tofu and Vegetable Stir-Fry

For those who prefer a vegetarian option, this tofu and vegetable stir-fry is a flavorful and protein-packed meal that’s easy to prepare. The tofu adds a satisfying texture, while the vegetables provide crunch and freshness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu to remove excess water. Cut it into cubes.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the tofu cubes and cook until golden and crispy, about 5–7 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add garlic and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the zucchini, bell pepper, and snow peas. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until tender.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and honey.
  7. Return the tofu to the skillet and pour the sauce over everything. Stir to coat.
  8. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the sauce to thicken.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice.

This tofu stir-fry is a great way to use plant-based protein in your meals. You can easily customize it with different vegetables based on what’s in season or your personal preferences.

4. Shrimp Stir-Fry with Garlic and Ginger

Shrimp stir-fry is a quick and delicious meal that’s perfect for seafood lovers. The garlic and ginger add aromatic flavor, while the shrimp cooks up in just minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the shrimp and cook for 2–3 minutes on each side, or until pink and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add garlic and ginger, cooking for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the bell pepper and snap peas, stir-frying for 3–4 minutes.
  5. Return the shrimp to the skillet and pour in the soy sauce and oyster sauce. Stir to combine.
  6. Cook for an additional 2 minutes, ensuring the shrimp and vegetables are coated in the sauce.
  7. Serve hot with rice or noodles.

This shrimp stir-fry is light but flavorful and makes for a delicious dinner that doesn’t require a lot of effort.

5. Pork and Green Bean Stir-Fry

Pork is an affordable and versatile protein that can be used in stir-fry dishes. This pork and green bean stir-fry is a tasty, savory meal that comes together in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork tenderloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced pork and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Remove the pork from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add garlic and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the green beans and stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until tender.
  6. Return the pork to the skillet. Add soy sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar. Stir to combine.
  7. Cook for another 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  8. Serve hot with rice or noodles.

This simple stir-fry is a fantastic way to incorporate lean pork and fresh vegetables into your meal plan.

6. Sweet and Sour Chicken Stir-Fry

This sweet and sour chicken stir-fry is a flavorful and tangy option that’s perfect for anyone craving bold flavors. It combines the sweetness of pineapple with the savory taste of chicken.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chicken slices and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the onion and bell pepper. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes until tender.
  5. Add the pineapple chunks and cook for another minute.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, ketchup, and honey.
  7. Return the chicken to the skillet and pour the sauce over everything. Stir to combine.
  8. Cook for an additional 2 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  9. Serve with steamed rice or noodles.

This sweet and sour stir-fry is a great balance of flavors and makes for a fun and colorful dinner option.

7. Veggie Stir-Fry with Cashews

If you’re in the mood for a meatless stir-fry that’s still packed with flavor, this veggie stir-fry with cashews is a perfect choice. The crunchy cashews add a nutty element to the dish, while the mix of vegetables ensures every bite is satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1/4 cup cashews, roasted
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the broccoli, bell pepper, carrot, and snap peas. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes.
  4. Add the soy sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar. Stir to coat the vegetables.
  5. Toss in the roasted cashews and stir-fry for an additional 2 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with rice or noodles.

This veggie stir-fry is light but satisfying, making it a perfect dish for a meatless dinner that doesn’t compromise on flavor.

8. Spicy Chicken Stir-Fry

For those who love a little heat in their meals, this spicy chicken stir-fry delivers bold flavors with a spicy kick. Packed with fresh vegetables and a flavorful sauce, it’s sure to spice up your dinner rotation.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chicken slices and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the bell pepper, onion, and snow peas, stir-frying for 3–4 minutes until tender.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, chili paste, sesame oil, and rice vinegar.
  7. Return the chicken to the skillet and pour the sauce over everything.
  8. Stir to coat, and cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the sauce to thicken.
  9. Serve hot with rice or noodles.

This spicy chicken stir-fry is perfect for anyone who loves a little heat with their dinner. Adjust the level of spice to suit your taste!

9. Garlic and Soy Sauce Stir-Fried Rice

Stir-fried rice is a versatile and delicious way to use up leftover rice, and this simple garlic and soy sauce version makes for a quick and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Push the garlic to one side of the skillet and pour the beaten egg into the other side. Scramble the egg until cooked through.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps.
  5. Pour the soy sauce over the rice and stir to combine.
  6. Cook for another 2–3 minutes, allowing the rice to fry slightly.
  7. Sprinkle with chopped green onions and serve.

This stir-fried rice is a perfect side dish, but it can also be eaten on its own for a light meal.

10. Spicy Beef and Cabbage Stir-Fry

This beef and cabbage stir-fry is a great option for those who enjoy a savory dish with a little bit of spice. The tender beef and cabbage come together to create a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 2 cups shredded cabbage
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili garlic sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Remove any excess fat and add garlic. Cook for an additional 30 seconds.
  4. Add the cabbage and stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until tender.
  5. Add the soy sauce, chili garlic sauce, and sesame oil, stirring to combine.
  6. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.

This beef and cabbage stir-fry is an easy and budget-friendly meal that’s packed with flavor and heat.

These 10 stir-fry recipes are perfect for anyone looking for quick, affordable, and flavorful meals. Whether you’re cooking for a busy family or preparing a solo dinner, you can customize these recipes to suit your preferences and make them your own. Enjoy!
