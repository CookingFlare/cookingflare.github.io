How to Cook with Coconut: 5 Indian Recipes Featuring Coconut


Coconuts are a staple ingredient in Indian cuisine, adding flavor, richness, and texture to a wide variety of dishes. From creamy curries to crunchy flatbreads, coconut plays a starring role in many Indian recipes. In this post, we'll explore five mouth-watering Indian recipes that feature coconut as the main attraction. Recipe 1: Coconut Chicken Biryani Biryani is a popular Indian dish made with aromatic rice, spices, and marinated meat or vegetables. In this Coconut Chicken Biryani recipe, we'll use shredded coconut to add moisture and flavor to the biryani. ** Servings:** 4-6 Ingredients:

1 cup basmati rice 2 cups water 1 cup shredded coconut 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs 1 onion, thinly sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon coconut oil 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1 teaspoon turmeric powder Salt, to taste Chopped cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

Rinse the rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain the water and set the rice aside. In a large pan, heat the coconut oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds. Add the sliced onion and cook until it's lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Add the chicken and cook until it's browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Add the shredded coconut, cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt to the pan. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes. In a large saucepan, combine the soaked rice, 2 cups of water, and 1 cup of coconut milk. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low and cover the saucepan with a tight-fitting lid. Simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the water is absorbed and the rice is cooked. To assemble the biryani, place a layer of cooked rice in a large serving dish or individual bowls. Top with the chicken and coconut mixture, followed by another layer of rice. Repeat the layers one more time, ending with a layer of rice on top. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve hot.

Recipe 2: Coconut Shrimp Curry Shrimp are a popular ingredient in Indian cuisine, and when paired with coconut, they create a dish that's both delicious and indulgent. In this Coconut Shrimp Curry recipe, we'll use shredded coconut to add richness and flavor to the curry. Servings: 4-6 Ingredients:

1 cup heavy coconut milk 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 1 onion, thinly sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined 1 teaspoon curry powder 1 teaspoon garam masala 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder Salt, to taste Chopped cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and cook until it's lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Add the shrimp and cook until they're browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. In a blender or food processor, combine the coconut milk, curry powder, garam masala, and turmeric powder. Blend until smooth. Add the blended coconut milk mixture to the pan and stir to combine. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 2-3 minutes. Season the curry with salt to taste. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve hot with steamed basmati rice or roti.

Recipe 3: Coconut Chapati Chapati is a popular Indian flatbread that's often served with a variety of curries or vegetables. In this Coconut Chapati recipe, we'll use shredded coconut to add flavor and texture to the dough. Servings: 4-6 Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour 1/4 cup shredded coconut 1/4 teaspoon salt 1 tablespoon ghee or vegetable oil 1/2 cup lukewarm water

Instructions:

In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, shredded coconut, and salt. Add the ghee or vegetable oil to the dry ingredients and mix until the dough comes together. Gradually add the lukewarm water to the dough and mix until it's smooth and elastic. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it's soft and pliable. Divide the dough into 4-6 equal portions, depending on how large you want your chapatis to be. Roll out each portion into a thin circle, about 6-7 inches in diameter. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Cook the chapati for 1-2 minutes on each side, until it's lightly browned and puffed. Brush the chapati with melted butter or ghee, if desired. Serve hot with a variety of curries, vegetables, or dal.

Recipe 4: Coconut Tandoori Chicken Tandoori chicken is a popular Indian dish made by marinating chicken in a mixture of spices and yogurt before grilling it in a tandoor oven. In this Coconut Tandoori Chicken recipe, we'll use shredded coconut to add moisture and flavor to the marinade. Servings: 4-6 Ingredients:

2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs 1 cup plain yogurt 1/4 cup shredded coconut 2 tablespoons lemon juice 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 teaspoons cumin powder 1 teaspoon garam masala 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt, to taste

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, shredded coconut, lemon juice, ghee or vegetable oil, cumin powder, garam masala, cayenne pepper, and salt. Add the chicken to the marinade and mix well to coat. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and cook for 5-7 minutes on each side, until it's cooked through. Serve hot with a side of cucumber raita or salad.

Recipe 5: Coconut Rice Pudding Coconut rice pudding is a creamy and comforting dessert that's perfect for hot summer days. In this Coconut Rice Pudding recipe, we'll use shredded coconut to add flavor and texture to the pudding. Servings: 4-6 Ingredients:

1 cup uncooked white rice 3 cups coconut milk 1 cup shredded coconut 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 teaspoon salt 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

Rinse the rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain the water and set the rice aside. In a medium saucepan, combine the coconut milk, shredded coconut, granulated sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring constantly. Add the cooked rice to the saucepan and stir to combine. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pudding has thickened and the coconut has dissolved. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with toasted coconut flakes or chopped nuts.

Conclusion Coconut is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of Indian recipes, from creamy curries to crunchy flatbreads. In this post, we've explored five delicious Indian recipes that feature coconut as the main attraction. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a beginner, these recipes are sure to inspire you to create something new and exciting in the kitchen. So go ahead, experiment with coconut in your cooking, and discover the many wonders of this amazing ingredient!
