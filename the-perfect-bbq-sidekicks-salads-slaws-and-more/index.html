The Perfect BBQ Sidekicks: Salads, Slaws, and More


When it comes to a perfect BBQ, the main dishes often steal the spotlight. But let’s not forget about the sidekicks—the salads, slaws, and more—that elevate the entire meal and make it truly memorable. These refreshing and vibrant accompaniments not only provide a burst of flavor but also balance out the richness of the grilled meats. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delicious and easy-to-make BBQ sidekicks that will take your outdoor gathering to the next level.

1. Classic Coleslaw

No BBQ is complete without a classic coleslaw. This tangy and crunchy salad is a crowd-pleaser that pairs perfectly with any grilled meat. To make it, start by shredding a head of cabbage and a carrot. In a bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, vinegar, sugar, salt, and pepper to create the dressing. Toss the dressing with the shredded cabbage and carrot until well-coated. Let the coleslaw sit in the refrigerator for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld. Serve chilled and enjoy the refreshing crunch of this timeless sidekick.

2. Watermelon Feta Salad

On a hot summer day, a watermelon feta salad is a refreshing and unexpected addition to your BBQ spread. Start by cubing a juicy watermelon and crumble some feta cheese on top. Add a handful of fresh mint leaves and a drizzle of olive oil. Toss gently to combine and sprinkle with a pinch of salt. This vibrant and sweet-savory salad will surprise your guests with its burst of flavors and textures.

3. Grilled Vegetable Skewers

Take advantage of the grill by preparing some colorful and flavorful grilled vegetable skewers. Start by cutting your favorite vegetables, such as bell peppers, zucchini, onions, and cherry tomatoes, into bite-sized pieces. Thread them onto skewers and brush with a mixture of olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Grill the skewers over medium heat until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred. The smoky flavors will complement the grilled meats and add a healthy touch to your BBQ feast.

4. Quinoa Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

For a lighter option, try a quinoa salad with a zesty lemon vinaigrette. Cook the quinoa according to the package instructions and let it cool. In a separate bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper to make the vinaigrette. Toss the cooked quinoa with diced cucumbers, cherry tomatoes, red onions, and chopped fresh herbs like parsley and mint. Drizzle the lemon vinaigrette over the salad and mix well. The combination of flavors and textures in this salad will surely impress your guests.

5. Caprese Skewers

Simple yet elegant, caprese skewers are a delightful addition to any BBQ spread. Thread cherry tomatoes, fresh basil leaves, and mini mozzarella balls onto skewers. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and sprinkle with salt and pepper. These bite-sized skewers are not only visually appealing but also bursting with the flavors of summer. Your guests won’t be able to resist these tasty treats.

Conclusion

When it comes to hosting a BBQ, don’t overlook the importance of sidekicks like salads, slaws, and more. These dishes add depth, freshness, and balance to your outdoor feast. Whether it’s the classic coleslaw, the refreshing watermelon feta salad, the grilled vegetable skewers, the quinoa salad with lemon vinaigrette, or the elegant caprese skewers, these BBQ sidekicks will elevate your meal and impress your guests. So fire up the grill, prepare these delicious sidekicks, and get ready to enjoy a memorable and flavorful BBQ experience.
