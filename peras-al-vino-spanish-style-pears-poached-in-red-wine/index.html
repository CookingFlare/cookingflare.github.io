Peras al Vino: Spanish-Style Pears Poached in Red Wine


If you’re looking for a unique and elegant dessert to impress your guests, look no further than Peras al Vino. This traditional Spanish dessert combines the sweetness of pears with the richness of red wine, resulting in a flavor combination that is truly divine. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a show-stopping dessert that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients

To make Peras al Vino, you will need:

  • 4 ripe pears
  • 1 bottle of red wine (preferably a full-bodied variety such as Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot)
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 vanilla bean, split lengthwise
  • Zest of 1 orange

Instructions

  1. Start by peeling the pears, leaving the stem intact. This will not only add visual appeal to your dish but will also make it easier to handle the pears while poaching.

  2. In a large saucepan, combine the red wine, sugar, cinnamon stick, vanilla bean, and orange zest. Stir gently to dissolve the sugar and bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat.

  3. Once the wine mixture is simmering, carefully add the pears to the pan. Make sure they are fully submerged in the liquid. If necessary, you can add more wine or water to ensure that the pears are covered.

  4. Reduce the heat to low and let the pears simmer gently for about 30-40 minutes, or until they are tender. You can test the doneness of the pears by inserting a sharp knife into the flesh - it should glide through easily.

  5. Once the pears are cooked, remove them from the liquid using a slotted spoon and transfer them to a serving dish. Set aside to cool slightly.

  6. Meanwhile, increase the heat to medium-high and bring the liquid to a boil. Let it boil for about 15-20 minutes, or until the liquid has reduced and thickened slightly.

  7. Once the liquid has reduced, remove the cinnamon stick and vanilla bean from the saucepan. Continue simmering the liquid until it reaches a syrup-like consistency.

  8. Pour the syrup over the pears, making sure to coat them evenly. You can also drizzle some of the syrup around the pears for added presentation.

  9. Allow the pears to cool completely in the syrup before serving. This will help the flavors meld together and intensify the taste.

  10. To serve, place a pear on a dessert plate and spoon some of the syrup over the top. You can also garnish with a sprig of fresh mint or a dusting of powdered sugar for an extra touch of elegance.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra flavor boost, you can add a splash of brandy or orange liqueur to the wine mixture before simmering.
  • If you prefer a sweeter dessert, you can increase the amount of sugar in the recipe or use a sweeter variety of red wine.
  • Peras al Vino can be served warm or cold, depending on your preference. If serving warm, you can reheat the pears in the syrup just before serving.
  • This dessert pairs well with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for added indulgence.

Final Thoughts

Peras al Vino is a classic Spanish dessert that showcases the beauty of simplicity. With just a handful of ingredients and a little bit of time, you can create a dessert that is both visually stunning and incredibly delicious. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to treat yourself to something special, this recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try, and savor the flavors of Spain in every bite. Buen provecho!
