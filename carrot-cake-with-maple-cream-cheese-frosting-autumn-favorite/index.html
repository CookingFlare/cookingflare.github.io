Carrot Cake with Maple Cream Cheese Frosting: Autumn Favorite


Autumn is just around the corner, and with it comes a fresh harvest of juicy, glistening carrots. There’s nothing quite like the warm colors of the fall season, those stunning shades of orange, yellow, and red simply beckoning to be incorporated into our meals. One of our favorite ways to savor this autumnal bounty is by indulging in a scrumptious Carrot Cake with Maple Cream Cheese Frosting. This deceptively simple, yet moist and flavorful treat will make your taste buds dance with every bite. Here’s how to create this ultimate autumn dessert that’s sure to be a hit at your next gathering, even with the most discerning of sweet teeth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 pound grated carrots (about 4 medium-sized carrots)
  • 1 cup chopped pecans or walnuts (optional)
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

1. Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and generously grease two 9-inch round cake pans. You can also line the bottoms with parchment rounds for easier removal.

2. Mix Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and nutmeg until evenly combined. Set this mixture aside for now.

3. Whisk Wet Ingredients

In a separate large bowl, beat the eggs, then incorporate the sugar, oil, and vanilla extract, mixing until smooth.

4. Combine Dry and Wet Mixtures

Slowly fold the dry ingredients into the wet mix, stirring gently until just incorporated.

5. Prepare the Carrots

Using a box grater or a food processor fitted with a grating attachment, grate the carrots. Don’t forget to give them a good rinse before grating them.

6. Fold in the Finishing Touches

Stir in the carrots and chopped pecans or walnuts if you’re using them. Make sure to distribute the ingredients evenly throughout the batter.

7. Bake the Cakes

Once your batter is prepared, divide it evenly between the two prepared cake pans. Smooth the tops with a spatula and place the pans in the preheated oven for about 35 to 40 minutes, or until a toothpick or skewer inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.

8. Cool the Cakes

Once done, remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the pans for about 10 minutes. Then, gently run a knife around the edges to loosen the cakes and transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

9. Prepare the Frosting

While the cakes are cooling, you can start making the frosting. Using an electric mixer, beat the softened cream cheese and butter until creamy. Gradually beat in the confectioners’ sugar, followed by the maple syrup and a pinch of salt. Mix everything together until the frosting reaches a smooth and spreadable consistency.

10. Assemble the Cake

Once your cakes are fully cooled, spread a generous layer of maple cream cheese frosting on one of them. Place the second cake on top and frost the entire cake with the remaining frosting, smoothing it out evenly.

11. Enjoy!

Finally, if you wish, garnish your Carrot Cake with Maple Cream Cheese Frosting with a sprinkle of chopped pecans or a few edible flowers, and behold your masterpiece for all to enjoy.

This Carrot Cake with Maple Cream Cheese Frosting should be stored in the refrigerator in an airtight container or wrapped well in plastic wrap. It will keep for up to four days, but believe us, it won’t last that long!

So, why not take advantage of the autumnal harvest while it lasts and treat yourself and your loved ones to this delightful dessert? Your taste buds and your guests will thank you. Happy baking, and may your kitchen be filled with the warm scents of autumn’s bounty, always.
