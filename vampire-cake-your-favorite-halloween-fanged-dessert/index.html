Vampire Cake: Your Favorite Halloween Fanged Dessert


Halloween is a spooky time of year when we are not only haunted by ghosts and goblins but also indulged in some of the best sweet treats. And what better way to celebrate Halloween than with a fanged treat that will make your taste buds go wild? Introducing the Vampire Cake - an impressive dessert that’s both whimsical and delicious. Ideal for your Halloween party, this chilling cake will leave your guests craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box devil’s food cake mix
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon instant coffee
  • 2 tablespoons red food coloring
  • 1/4 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 1/2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 cups icing sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup of raspberry jam

Decoration:

  • Dye some heavy whipping cream red to mimic blood.
  • Black fondant for teeth.
  • Black gel food coloring for drawing fangs.
  • Plastic vampire teeth, optional.

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the cake

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees F. Grease an 8-inch round cake pan or 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or non-stick spray.
  2. In a large bowl or stand mixer combine the cake mix, water, vegetable oil, and eggs. Blend with a mixer until the ingredients are well combined.
  3. Stir in the instant coffee and food coloring to intensify the chocolate flavor reminiscent of vampires’ dark nature.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Remove from the oven and let it cool for at least 20 minutes in the pan. Then turn the cake out onto a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 2: Make the vanilla buttercream frosting

  1. In a large mixing bowl with an electric mixer, combine the softened butter, powdered sugar, cocoa powder and beat until smooth.
  2. Add in the heavy cream, and whisk until the frosting is fluffy and spreadable.

Step 3: Assemble the cake

  1. When the cake has fully cooled, spread a layer of raspberry jam over the entire surface.
  2. Then spread the chocolate buttercream frosting evenly over the cake to cover the jam.
  3. Place the cake in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to allow the frosting to set.

Step 4: Decorate your vampire cake

  1. Whip the heavy cream with a few drops of red food coloring until it reaches stiff peaks. Then pipe blood-like swirls on top of the cake.
  2. Optionally, add some vampire teeth by using the black fondant to form teeth for each side of the cake, or place plastic vampire teeth on top.
  3. If desired, you can use some black gel food coloring to draw additional fangs on the cake.

Conclusion:

Vampire Cake is a delicious and fun dessert for any Halloween gathering. This chilling cake will make everyone’s blood run cold when it comes to dessert time. You can play around with the blood color to make it darker or more vivid, and have fun decorating -just don’t get bitten! So put those fangs away, and let the Halloween feast begin with this tantalizing Vampire Cake!

(Happy Hauntings!)
