Apple Cinnamon French Toast Casserole: A Brunch-Inspired Dessert


When it comes to brunch, few dishes can match the comfort and warmth of a good French toast casserole, especially when it’s infused with the delightful flavors of apples and cinnamon. This Apple Cinnamon French Toast Casserole is a sweet twist on the classic dish, making it perfect for cozy gatherings or leisurely weekend mornings. Imagine waking up to the smell of apples lightly caramelized with cinnamon and sugar, mingling with the rich aroma of baked French toast. This recipe is not just a feast for the senses; it’s also a delicious way to start your day.

Why Choose a Casserole?

The beauty of a casserole is that it allows for easy preparation and serving. Instead of standing at the stove flipping slices of French toast, you can prepare this casserole ahead of time and pop it in the oven when your guests arrive. This not only saves you time but also keeps you in the mix, allowing you to enjoy your brunch without being tethered to the kitchen. Plus, when served warm from the oven, this casserole transforms into a delightful dessert that can be enjoyed anytime.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create the perfect Apple Cinnamon French Toast Casserole, gather the following ingredients:

For the Casserole:

  • 1 loaf of French bread (preferably a day or two old)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 medium-sized apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)

For the Topping:

  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, melted

For Serving:

  • Maple syrup
  • Powdered sugar (optional)
  • Whipped cream (optional)

Preparation: Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Bread

Start by cutting the French bread into 1-inch cubes. If your bread is fresh, consider letting it sit out for a few hours or even overnight to dry out slightly. This will help it absorb the egg mixture better without becoming too soggy.

Step 2: Make the Egg Mixture

In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, whole milk, heavy cream, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, and salt until well combined. This mixture will be the custard base for your casserole.

Step 3: Combine Apples and Bread

In a large mixing bowl, combine the diced apples and the cubed bread. Pour the egg mixture over the bread and apples, gently folding to ensure everything is evenly coated. If you’re using nuts, fold them in at this stage as well.

Step 4: Let It Soak

Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it sit in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or ideally overnight. This allows the bread to soak up the custard, leading to a richer and creamier casserole.

Step 5: Preheat the Oven

When you’re ready to bake, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

Step 6: Transfer to Baking Dish

After the soaking period, pour the bread mixture into the prepared baking dish. Spread it out evenly, ensuring that the apples are distributed throughout.

Step 7: Prepare the Topping

In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, ground cinnamon, and melted butter. Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the top of the casserole. This will create a sweet and crunchy topping once baked.

Step 8: Bake

Place the casserole in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the center is set. You can test for doneness by inserting a knife into the center; it should come out clean.

Step 9: Cool and Serve

Once baked, remove the casserole from the oven and let it cool for about 10 minutes before serving. This will help it set a little more and make it easier to cut into squares.

Serving Suggestions

This Apple Cinnamon French Toast Casserole is delicious on its own, but you can elevate it even further with a few serving suggestions:

  • Maple Syrup: Drizzle warm maple syrup over each serving for a touch of sweetness that complements the apples and cinnamon.
  • Powdered Sugar: A light dusting of powdered sugar can add a beautiful touch and a bit of extra sweetness.
  • Whipped Cream: For an indulgent treat, serve with a dollop of whipped cream on top.

Variations to Try

While this casserole is delightful as is, there are numerous variations you can explore to make it your own:

  • Add Raisins or Dried Cranberries: For a chewy texture, throw in some raisins or dried cranberries along with the apples.
  • Use Different Fruits: Experiment with pears or berries for a different fruit flavor. Just remember to adjust the sugar accordingly based on the sweetness of the fruit.
  • Make It Nut-Free: If you have nut allergies, simply omit the nuts or substitute them with sunflower seeds or pumpkin seeds for added crunch.
  • Try Different Breads: While French bread is traditional, you could use challah, brioche, or even whole grain bread for a healthier option.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (which is often the case because it’s so delicious!), you can store the casserole in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply cover it with foil and warm it in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 15-20 minutes, or until heated through. Alternatively, individual servings can be microwaved for a quick breakfast option.

Conclusion

The Apple Cinnamon French Toast Casserole is more than just a brunch item; it’s a warm embrace on a plate, perfect for lazy weekends or holiday gatherings. With its easy preparation and mouthwatering flavors, it’s sure to become a staple in your recipe collection. So next time you’re looking to impress guests or simply indulge yourself, whip up this delightful casserole. Each bite is a reminder of the comfort of home, the joy of good company, and the sweetness of life. Enjoy!
