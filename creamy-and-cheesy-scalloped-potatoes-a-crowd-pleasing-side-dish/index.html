Creamy and Cheesy Scalloped Potatoes: A Crowd-pleasing Side Dish


Creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes are a classic and beloved side dish that never fails to impress. With their velvety texture and rich flavor, they are the perfect accompaniment to any meal, from holiday feasts to weeknight dinners. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to create a crowd-pleasing dish that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet)
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • ½ teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 cups of shredded cheese (cheddar, Gruyere, or a combination)
  • 2 tablespoons of butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. Peel the potatoes and slice them into thin rounds, about ⅛-inch thick. You can use a mandoline slicer for even and consistent slices.

  3. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper. Heat over medium heat until it just starts to simmer. Remove from heat and set aside.

  4. Arrange a layer of potato slices in the greased baking dish, slightly overlapping each other. Pour a portion of the cream mixture over the potatoes, ensuring they are evenly coated. Sprinkle a generous amount of shredded cheese over the layer.

  5. Repeat the process, layering the potatoes, cream mixture, and cheese until you have used up all the ingredients. Make sure to reserve some cheese for the top layer.

  6. Dot the top of the potatoes with small pieces of butter, which will help create a golden and crispy crust.

  7. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 45 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden and the potatoes are tender when pierced with a knife.

  8. Once cooked, remove the dish from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld together and the dish to set.

  9. Serve the creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes as a side dish alongside roasted chicken, grilled steak, or any protein of your choice. They are also delicious on their own as a vegetarian option.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, you can add a layer of caramelized onions or sautéed mushrooms between the potato layers. This will add an extra depth of flavor to the dish.

  • Experiment with different types of cheese to find your favorite combination. Sharp cheddar, Gruyere, and Parmesan all work well in this recipe.

  • If you prefer a crispy top, broil the dish for a few minutes at the end of the baking time. Keep a close eye on it to prevent burning.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes are a timeless and comforting dish that will elevate any meal. With their simple ingredients and easy preparation, they are a go-to side dish for both casual and special occasions. Give this recipe a try and watch as it becomes a family favorite that will be requested time and time again. Enjoy!
