One Pot Beef and Potato Stew


There’s something incredibly comforting about a hearty stew, especially when it’s made with tender beef and wholesome potatoes. This one-pot beef and potato stew recipe is a warm, filling, and delicious meal that’s perfect for a cozy night in or a family gathering. The best part? It’s easy to make and requires minimal cleanup, thanks to the one-pot cooking method.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of stewing beef, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 carrots, sliced
  • 4 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • 1 cup of red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

Instructions

  1. Brown the Beef: Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned on all sides. This step adds depth of flavor to the stew.

  2. Add Aromatics: Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the pot and sauté until the onion becomes translucent. This will release the aromatic flavors that will infuse the stew.

  3. Deglaze the Pot: If using red wine, pour it into the pot and stir, scraping any browned bits from the bottom. Let the wine cook for a few minutes to reduce slightly.

  4. Add Veggies and Seasonings: Add the diced potatoes, sliced carrots, chopped celery, bay leaves, dried thyme, tomato paste, and beef broth to the pot. Stir everything together, ensuring that the ingredients are well combined.

  5. Simmer: Bring the stew to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together. Remember to stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  6. Season to Perfection: Once the stew is cooked, taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. The longer cooking time allows the flavors to develop, and the stew becomes more delicious with time.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Ladle the hot beef and potato stew into bowls and serve with a crusty bread or a side salad for a wholesome and satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra flavor boost, you can add a splash of Worcestershire sauce or a sprinkle of smoked paprika to the stew.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can toss the diced potatoes in a tablespoon of flour before adding them to the pot. The flour will help thicken the stew as it simmers.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables in the stew based on what you have on hand or your personal preferences. Mushrooms, parsnips, or turnips would all be wonderful additions.

Final Thoughts

This one-pot beef and potato stew is a classic comfort food that brings warmth and satisfaction with every spoonful. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply craving a cozy meal, this recipe is a timeless winner. The rich flavors, tender beef, and hearty potatoes make it a dish that will be requested time and time again.

So, next time you’re in the mood for a comforting and nourishing meal, give this recipe a try. It’s a simple yet satisfying dish that embodies the essence of home-cooked goodness. Enjoy!
