How to Make Perfectly Crispy Chinese Salt and Pepper Tofu


Tofu is a versatile ingredient that can be prepared in many different ways, and one of the most delicious and popular ways to enjoy it is by making Chinese Salt and Pepper Tofu. This dish is known for its perfectly crispy exterior and flavorful seasoning. In this recipe, we will guide you through the steps to achieve that irresistible texture and taste.

Ingredients

  • 1 block of firm tofu
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the tofu. Drain the tofu and pat it dry with paper towels. Cut it into bite-sized cubes, ensuring they are all roughly the same size. Place the tofu cubes on a clean kitchen towel or paper towels to absorb any excess moisture.

  2. In a shallow dish, combine the cornstarch, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly combined.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium-high heat. You want enough oil to cover the tofu cubes when frying.

  4. While the oil is heating, carefully toss the tofu cubes in the cornstarch mixture, making sure each cube is evenly coated. Shake off any excess coating before placing the tofu in the hot oil.

  5. Gently add the coated tofu cubes into the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the tofu in batches if needed. Cook for about 3-4 minutes or until the tofu turns golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon or tongs to remove the tofu from the oil and transfer it to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

  6. Repeat the frying process until all the tofu cubes are cooked.

  7. Once all the tofu is fried, sprinkle it with a pinch of salt and pepper while it’s still hot. This will enhance the flavor of the dish.

  8. Serve the crispy tofu immediately, garnished with fresh cilantro if desired. It can be enjoyed as an appetizer, a side dish, or even as a main course with steamed rice and stir-fried vegetables.

Tips for Success

  • Make sure to use firm tofu for this recipe as it holds its shape better during frying.
  • Patting the tofu dry before coating it with cornstarch will help achieve a crispier texture.
  • Adjust the amount of salt and pepper according to your taste preferences. Feel free to add more or less depending on your preference for spiciness.
  • Be cautious when adding the tofu to the hot oil to avoid any oil splatters. It’s always a good idea to use a long-handled utensil or tongs for safety.

Enjoy the deliciously crispy Chinese Salt and Pepper Tofu that you’ve made from scratch. This dish is sure to impress your family and friends with its flavorful crunchiness. Experiment with different seasoning variations and add your own personal touch to make it truly unique. Happy cooking!

Note: This recipe serves as a basic guide. Feel free to adapt and modify it to suit your taste and dietary preferences.
