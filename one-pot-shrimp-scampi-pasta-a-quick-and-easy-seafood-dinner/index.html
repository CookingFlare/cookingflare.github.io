One-Pot Shrimp Scampi Pasta: A Quick and Easy Seafood Dinner


Are you a seafood lover looking for a quick and easy dinner option that’s bursting with flavor? Look no further than this delicious One-Pot Shrimp Scampi Pasta recipe! With just a handful of ingredients and a single pot, you can whip up a mouthwatering seafood dish that will impress even the most discerning palates.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 ounces of linguine pasta
  • 4 tablespoons of butter
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup of white wine (optional)
  • 1/4 cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for some heat)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Cook the linguine pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about a minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.

  3. Add the shrimp to the pot and cook for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the pot and set aside.

  4. Pour the chicken broth, white wine (if using), and lemon juice into the pot. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for a couple of minutes to reduce slightly.

  5. Return the cooked linguine pasta to the pot and toss it in the flavorful broth. Make sure the pasta is well-coated.

  6. Add the cooked shrimp back to the pot and gently toss them with the pasta. Season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if desired) to taste.

  7. Sprinkle the chopped fresh parsley over the top and give everything a final toss to combine.

  8. Serve the One-Pot Shrimp Scampi Pasta immediately while it’s still hot. You can garnish it with a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese and a wedge of lemon for an extra touch of freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For a healthier twist, you can use whole wheat linguine or substitute the pasta entirely with zucchini noodles or spaghetti squash.
  • Experiment with different seafood options like scallops or mussels to create your own seafood pasta masterpiece.
  • If you’re not a fan of white wine, you can simply omit it from the recipe or replace it with additional chicken broth.
  • Feel free to add some vegetables to the dish for extra color and nutrients. Sautéed spinach, cherry tomatoes, or roasted asparagus would be great choices.

Final Thoughts

One-Pot Shrimp Scampi Pasta is a perfect weeknight dinner option when you’re craving something flavorful and satisfying. The combination of succulent shrimp, garlic-infused butter, and tangy lemon juice creates a symphony of flavors that will leave you wanting more. Plus, the convenience of cooking everything in a single pot means less time spent on cleaning up after your delicious meal.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for a seafood dinner that’s both quick and easy, give this One-Pot Shrimp Scampi Pasta recipe a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
