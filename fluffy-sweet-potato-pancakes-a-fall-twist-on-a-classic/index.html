Fluffy Sweet Potato Pancakes: A Fall Twist on a Classic


Fall is the perfect time to indulge in comforting and seasonal dishes, and what better way to start your day than with a stack of fluffy sweet potato pancakes? This delightful twist on a classic breakfast favorite will leave you craving for more. Made with the goodness of sweet potatoes, these pancakes are not only delicious but also packed with nutrients.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cooked and mashed sweet potatoes
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Set aside.

  2. In another bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, milk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.

  3. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense pancakes.

  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease the surface with butter or cooking spray.

  5. Pour 1/4 cup of the batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.

  6. Repeat with the remaining batter, adding more butter or cooking spray as needed to prevent sticking.

  7. Serve the sweet potato pancakes warm with your favorite toppings. Maple syrup, whipped cream, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar are all excellent choices. You can also add a dollop of Greek yogurt and some fresh berries for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • To cook the sweet potatoes, you can either boil or bake them until soft. Once cooked, remove the skin and mash until smooth.

  • If you prefer a thicker batter, you can add a little more flour to achieve your desired consistency.

  • Feel free to customize your pancakes by adding chopped nuts, raisins, or chocolate chips to the batter for some added texture and flavor.

  • For a healthier twist, you can substitute some or all of the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour. You can also use a non-dairy milk alternative and replace the butter with a plant-based spread.

  • Leftover pancakes can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in a toaster or microwave before serving.

Conclusion

These fluffy sweet potato pancakes are a delightful fall-inspired treat that will satisfy your cravings and add a touch of warmth to your breakfast table. With their soft texture and subtle sweetness, they are sure to become a favorite in your household. So, grab some sweet potatoes and give this twist on a classic pancake recipe a try. You won’t be disappointed! Enjoy the flavors of the season and indulge in the cozy goodness of these delicious fall pancakes.
