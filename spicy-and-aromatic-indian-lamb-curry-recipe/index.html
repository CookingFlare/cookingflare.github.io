Spicy and Aromatic: Indian Lamb Curry Recipe


If you’re a fan of bold flavors and exotic spices, then this Indian Lamb Curry recipe is a must-try! Bursting with aromatic herbs and spices, this dish will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of India. The tender lamb pieces simmered in a rich and spicy curry sauce will leave you wanting more. So, let’s dive right in and learn how to make this tantalizing dish!

Ingredients

  • 500g lamb, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large, deep pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds until fragrant.

  2. Add the chopped onions to the pan and sauté until golden brown. This will take around 5-7 minutes.

  3. Once the onions are caramelized, add the minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.

  4. Now, it’s time to add the pureed tomatoes and green chilies. Cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes are soft and well incorporated into the mixture.

  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, paprika, and salt. Add this spice mixture to the pan and stir well, ensuring all the ingredients are combined.

  6. Add the lamb pieces to the pan and coat them thoroughly with the spice mixture. Cook for 5 minutes on medium heat, stirring occasionally.

  7. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. Allow the lamb to simmer in its own juices for about 45 minutes or until tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  8. Once the lamb is cooked, remove the lid and increase the heat to medium-high. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes to reduce the sauce and intensify the flavors.

  9. Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning if needed. If you prefer a spicier curry, you can add more red chili powder or green chilies at this stage.

  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves for a burst of freshness and aroma.

  11. Serve the Indian Lamb Curry hot with steamed rice or naan bread. The combination of tender lamb and spicy sauce is sure to impress your taste buds.

Final Thoughts

This Spicy and Aromatic Indian Lamb Curry is a true delight for anyone who loves the vibrant flavors of Indian cuisine. The combination of aromatic spices, tender lamb, and rich curry sauce creates a dish that is both comforting and exciting. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a flavorful meal, this recipe is a winner.

So, why wait? Gather your ingredients, follow the simple steps, and transport yourself to the bustling streets of India with every mouthful. Happy cooking!
