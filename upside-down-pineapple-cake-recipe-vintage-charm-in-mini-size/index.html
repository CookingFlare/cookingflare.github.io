Upside-Down Pineapple Cake Recipe: Vintage Charm in Mini Size


Ah, pineapple upside-down cake! That classic, moist, and mouthwatering dessert that has been delighting our taste buds for generations. It’s the embodiment of comfort and nostalgia, bringing up warm memories of Grandma’s kitchen and sunny afternoons shared with family. This delightful cake is traditionally quite rich, but what happens when we take that nostalgia-inducing flavor and transform it into bite-sized goodness? Behold, the upside-down pineapple cake recipe in mini form – a delightful fusion of vintage charm and contemporary convenience.

Now imagine, warm, sweet, and succulent pineapple slices toying with your taste buds, basking in caramelized glory nestled right underneath a pillowy cake layer. Oh, and let’s not forget the glistening cherries, playfully reminding us of sun-kissed summer days. Is there anything more adorable and delectable than this combination in perfectly proportioned mini-sized portions? Our mini upside-down pineapple cakes are not only a feast for the eyes but also a scrumptious treat for your palate.

Let’s embark on this lovely culinary adventure together. Roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and prepare to be amazed by this vintage charm reimagined. Trust me, once you delve into these mini pineapple wonders, you’ll never look at cake the same way again.

Ingredients:

For the Pineapple Layer:

  • 1 can of pineapple slices (about 20 slices)
  • 20 maraschino cherries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract

For the Cake Layer:

  • 1 and 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup milk
  • 1/3 cup dark rum (optional)

Instructions:

Prep work:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and adjust your oven rack to the center position.
  2. Generously grease your mini bundt pans with butter, ensuring each crevice is coated evenly.

Pineapple Layer:

  1. Drain the pineapple slices and cherries, reserve the juice for another use or store it in the fridge for later.
  2. Melt the unsalted butter in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add the brown sugar, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract, stirring gently until the sugar has dissolved and formed a smooth caramel-like mixture.
  3. Arrange the pineapple slices in the bundt pans, ensuring each little cavity has one slice. Place a cherry on top of each pineapple slice and pour the caramel mixture over the fruit.
  4. Set aside the skillet and prepare the cake batter.

Cake Layer:

  1. Start by whisking the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together in a small bowl. Set this aside for now.
  2. In a separate large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs, one by one, then stir in the vanilla extract and dark rum for that warm, tropical finish.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients and milk to the butter mixture, alternating between the two and starting with the dry mixture.
  5. Mix until all the ingredients are well incorporated and you’re left with a smooth, velvety cake batter.

Now comes the fun part - assembling our mini pineapple cakes. Carefully spoon a dollop of the cake batter over the pineapple layer in each bundt pan. Fill almost all the way to the brim, as the batter will slightly rise in the oven.

Baking:

  1. Place the Bundt pans onto a baking sheet and put it in the preheated oven.
  2. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the cake portion comes out clean.
  3. Remove from the oven and let the mini cakes cool in the pan for about 5-10 minutes.
  4. After that, take a careful but gentle plunge with a knife or butter knife around each mini cake to loosen it from the pan. Then invert the pan onto a wire rack before they completely cool down.

And voilà! Your upside-down pineapple cakes are ready to be crowned as the kings and queens of your next dessert spread. Serve these adorable mini cakes warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, for a taste of exquisite nostalgia and divine flavor.

Remember, don’t forget to bookmark this charming recipe for those delightful moments when you wish to bring together vintage allure with contemporary style. Happy cooking, and may your kitchen be filled with the delectable scent of pineapple, cherries, and warm cake!
