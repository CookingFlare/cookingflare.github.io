Christmas Recipes for the Grill: Festive Outdoor Cooking


As the holiday season approaches, many of us envision cozy gatherings around the fireplace, sipping hot cocoa and enjoying comfort foods. However, grilling can also bring a unique twist to your Christmas celebrations, allowing you to enjoy festive flavors while basking in the crisp winter air. Whether you’re hosting a family gathering or a casual get-together with friends, these Christmas recipes for the grill will add a delightful spark to your outdoor cooking experience.

Grilled Honey-Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup honey
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium heat, around 325°F (160°C). If using a charcoal grill, create a two-zone fire, placing coals on one side for indirect cooking.

  2. Prepare the Ham: Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern. This will help the glaze penetrate and enhance the flavor.

  3. Make the Glaze: In a saucepan, combine honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, cinnamon, cloves, and ginger. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is well combined. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

  4. Grill the Ham: Place the ham on the grill over the indirect heat side. Brush the glaze generously over the ham, reserving some for later. Close the grill lid and cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours, basting with the glaze every 30 minutes.

  5. Finish: When the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C), remove the ham from the grill. Let it rest for 15 minutes before slicing. Garnish with fresh rosemary and serve warm.

Grilled Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  2. Cook the Bacon: In a cast-iron skillet or grill-safe pan, cook the chopped bacon on the grill until crispy. Remove and set aside, leaving the bacon drippings in the pan.

  3. Grill the Brussels Sprouts: In a bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place them cut side down on the grill. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until they develop grill marks.

  4. Combine: Add the cooked bacon back to the pan with the Brussels sprouts. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar and toss to combine.

  5. Finish: Remove from the grill and serve warm. This dish brings a smoky, savory flavor that’s perfect for your holiday feast.

Grilled Garlic and Herb Turkey

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (10-12 pounds)
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, quartered

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill for indirect cooking, aiming for a temperature of around 325°F (160°C).

  2. Prepare the Turkey: Pat the turkey dry with paper towels. In a bowl, mix olive oil, garlic, thyme, rosemary, sage, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the turkey, including under the skin.

  3. Stuff the Turkey: Place the quartered lemon inside the cavity of the turkey for added flavor.

  4. Grill the Turkey: Place the turkey breast-side up on the grill grates over the indirect heat. Close the lid and cook for approximately 13-15 minutes per pound, basting occasionally with the drippings. Use a meat thermometer to check for doneness; the internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C).

  5. Rest and Serve: Once cooked, remove the turkey from the grill and let it rest for at least 20 minutes before carving. This will ensure juicy, flavorful meat.

Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon Sugar

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium heat.

  2. Make the Cinnamon Sugar: In a small bowl, mix brown sugar and cinnamon.

  3. Grill the Pineapple: Brush the pineapple rings with melted butter and sprinkle the cinnamon sugar over them.

  4. Cook: Place the pineapple on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side until grill marks appear and the fruit is caramelized.

  5. Serve: Enjoy warm as a delightful dessert or a sweet side dish. You can also serve it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a festive treat.

Grilled Stuffed Mushrooms

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large portobello or cremini mushrooms, stems removed
  • 1 cup cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium heat.

  2. Make the Filling: In a mixing bowl, combine cream cheese, Parmesan cheese, parsley, garlic, salt, pepper, and breadcrumbs until well mixed.

  3. Stuff the Mushrooms: Generously fill each mushroom cap with the cheese mixture.

  4. Grill the Mushrooms: Place the stuffed mushrooms on the grill, close the lid, and cook for about 10-12 minutes, until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is golden.

  5. Serve: These savory bites are perfect as appetizers for your Christmas gathering.

Tips for Grilling during the Holidays

  1. Plan Ahead: Make a timeline for your grilling schedule, especially if you’re preparing multiple dishes. This will help you manage cooking times and ensure everything is ready to serve together.

  2. Stay Warm: If you’re grilling in colder weather, consider using a portable heater or wearing layered clothing to stay comfortable while cooking.

  3. Use Quality Ingredients: Fresh, high-quality ingredients will enhance the flavors of your dishes. Visit local markets to find the best seasonal produce and meats.

  4. Experiment with Flavors: Don’t be afraid to try new flavor combinations. Adding seasonal spices and herbs can elevate your dishes and impress your guests.

  5. Have Fun: Grilling during the holidays should be an enjoyable experience. Invite friends or family to join you, share stories, and create lasting memories around the grill.

Conclusion

Grilling during the Christmas season offers a wonderful way to celebrate with delicious flavors and fresh air. From honey-glazed ham to sweet grilled pineapple, these recipes will not only tantalize your taste buds but also create a warm, festive atmosphere for your gatherings. So fire up the grill, embrace the holiday spirit, and enjoy the unique flavors of outdoor cooking this Christmas!
