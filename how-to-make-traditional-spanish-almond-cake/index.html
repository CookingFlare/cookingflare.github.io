How to Make Traditional Spanish Almond Cake


If you’re looking to impress your friends and family with a delightful dessert, look no further than the traditional Spanish almond cake, also known as Tarta de Santiago. This rich and flavorful cake is not only a classic in Spanish cuisine but also embodies the essence of Mediterranean flavors. Made from simple ingredients, it boasts a moist and tender crumb that will leave everyone craving more. In this blog post, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of making this exquisite cake, along with tips and tricks to ensure your baking adventure is a success.

The History of Tarta de Santiago

Originating from the region of Galicia in Spain, Tarta de Santiago has deep historical roots. Traditionally prepared to honor Saint James, the patron saint of Spain, this cake is often served during religious festivals and special occasions. The cake is adorned with the iconic cross of Saint James, typically dusted with powdered sugar, which adds a beautiful touch to its presentation. The main ingredient, almonds, is a staple in Spanish desserts, bringing both flavor and texture to this beloved treat.

Ingredients You Will Need

Before diving into the preparation, gather the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups of ground almonds (almond flour)
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • The zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • A pinch of salt
  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Optional: a splash of Amaretto or orange liqueur for added flavor

Notes on Ingredients

  • Almonds: For the best texture, use blanched almonds and grind them yourself, or purchase high-quality almond flour.
  • Eggs: Room temperature eggs will incorporate more easily into the batter.
  • Flavorings: The lemon zest adds a refreshing touch, while liqueurs can enhance the cake’s flavor profile.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat Your Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures that your cake will bake evenly and rise beautifully.

Step 2: Prepare Your Baking Pan

Choose a round cake pan, ideally 9 inches in diameter. Grease the pan with butter and line the bottom with parchment paper to prevent sticking. For an authentic touch, you can also use a removable bottom pan.

Step 3: Mix the Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground almonds, granulated sugar, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Mix these dry ingredients until well incorporated.

Step 4: Add Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, crack the eggs and whisk them until frothy. Add the lemon zest and any optional liqueurs you wish to use. Pour this mixture into the bowl with the dry ingredients and mix until you have a smooth batter. Be careful not to overmix; just combine until everything is well integrated.

Step 5: Pour the Batter into the Pan

Transfer the batter to your prepared cake pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles.

Step 6: Bake the Cake

Place the cake in the preheated oven and bake for about 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The top should be lightly golden brown.

Step 7: Cool and Remove

Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, carefully invert it onto a wire rack to cool completely. Remove the parchment paper.

Step 8: Dust with Powdered Sugar

Once the cake is completely cool, it’s time to give it that classic finish. Place a stencil of the cross of Saint James on top of the cake and dust generously with powdered sugar. Carefully remove the stencil to reveal the design.

Serving Suggestions

Tarta de Santiago is best enjoyed at room temperature. Serve it plain or accompany it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful contrast. It pairs beautifully with a cup of coffee or a glass of sweet dessert wine.

Tips for Success

  • Storage: The cake can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. For longer storage, wrap it tightly and freeze for up to 3 months.
  • Flavor Variations: Feel free to experiment with flavors. Add a teaspoon of cinnamon for warmth, or substitute some of the almond flour with coconut flour for a tropical twist.
  • Presentation: To elevate the presentation, consider garnishing with sliced almonds or fresh berries around the base of the cake.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make this cake gluten-free?

Yes, Tarta de Santiago is naturally gluten-free since it uses almond flour instead of wheat flour. Just ensure that all your ingredients are certified gluten-free if you are serving someone with a gluten intolerance.

What if I don’t have almond flour?

If almond flour is not available, you can make your own by blending blanched almonds in a food processor until finely ground. Just be careful not to over-process, as this can turn the almonds into almond butter.

Can I use other nuts?

While traditional recipes call for almonds, you can experiment with other nuts like hazelnuts or walnuts. Keep in mind that this will alter the flavor and texture of the cake.

Conclusion

Making a traditional Spanish almond cake is a rewarding experience that brings a taste of Spain right into your kitchen. With its fragrant almond flavor and tender texture, Tarta de Santiago is sure to be a hit at any gathering. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply indulging in a sweet treat, this cake is a delightful choice that showcases the beauty of Spanish cuisine. So gather your ingredients, follow these steps, and enjoy the fruits of your labor with friends and family. Happy baking!
