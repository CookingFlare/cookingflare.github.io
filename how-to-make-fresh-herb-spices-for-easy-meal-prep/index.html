How to Make Fresh Herb Spices for Easy Meal Prep


Meal prepping is one of the most effective ways to stay on track with healthy eating while saving time during the busy week. One of the simplest, yet most impactful, additions to your meal prep routine is fresh herb spices. Whether you’re creating a savory dish, a zesty sauce, or a vibrant marinade, fresh herbs have the power to elevate your meals. In this guide, we’ll show you how to make fresh herb spices that will bring flavor, color, and nutritional benefits to your meal prep without the hassle.

Why Use Fresh Herb Spices in Meal Prep?

Fresh herbs not only enhance the flavor of your meals but also come with a variety of health benefits. Herbs like basil, cilantro, rosemary, and thyme are packed with antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals that support overall health. By incorporating these fresh herbs into your cooking, you’re not just improving the taste of your meals but also nourishing your body.

Additionally, making your own fresh herb spices allows you to control the ingredients and customize them to your taste preferences. You can create blends that suit your dietary needs, such as low-sodium, sugar-free, or gluten-free. Homemade herb spices also cut down on the number of preservatives and additives found in store-bought seasoning mixes.

Benefits of Fresh Herb Spices:

  1. Flavor Variety: Fresh herbs bring depth and complexity to your dishes.
  2. Nutritional Boost: Fresh herbs are rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and minerals.
  3. Customization: You can tailor your spice mixes to your dietary preferences and avoid unwanted additives.
  4. Time-Saving: Having pre-made fresh herb spices ready to go makes meal prep quicker and easier.
  5. Cost-Effective: Growing herbs at home or purchasing them in bulk can save you money compared to buying pre-made seasonings.

What Herbs Work Best for Homemade Spice Mixes?

When it comes to fresh herb spices, there are countless options. Some herbs shine in specific dishes, while others are more versatile and can be used across a wide range of cuisines. Here are some of the best herbs to incorporate into your homemade spice blends:

1. Basil

Basil is an essential herb in Mediterranean and Italian cooking. Its sweet and peppery flavor pairs perfectly with tomatoes, pasta, and pesto. When making your own basil spice, it’s best to use fresh basil leaves as they contain the most flavor and nutrients.

2. Cilantro

Cilantro has a vibrant, citrusy flavor that adds a fresh kick to many dishes. It’s commonly used in Mexican, Indian, and Middle Eastern cuisines. Cilantro works well in salsas, curries, and salads.

3. Rosemary

This woody herb has a pungent aroma and pairs wonderfully with meats like lamb, chicken, and beef. It’s perfect for infusing oils and making marinades for grilling or roasting. Rosemary also works well in roasted vegetables and potato dishes.

4. Thyme

Thyme is a versatile herb with earthy, slightly minty notes. It can be used in everything from soups and stews to roasted chicken and pasta dishes. Thyme also pairs well with vegetables, particularly root vegetables like carrots and potatoes.

5. Oregano

Oregano is known for its bold, slightly bitter flavor and is a staple in Italian and Mediterranean cuisine. It’s perfect for pizza, pasta, and marinades. Fresh oregano is more aromatic and flavorful than dried, so it makes an excellent addition to homemade herb mixes.

6. Parsley

Fresh parsley is a mild herb with a slightly peppery taste, and it works well as a garnish or ingredient in a variety of dishes. It’s particularly common in Middle Eastern, European, and American cooking. Parsley is excellent in salads, tabbouleh, and as a topping for soups and stews.

7. Mint

Mint brings a refreshing coolness to your meals. It’s often used in sweet dishes, beverages, and salads, but also adds a unique flavor to savory dishes like lamb or yogurt-based sauces. Fresh mint can give your spice mixes a burst of freshness and a vibrant green color.

How to Make Fresh Herb Spices for Meal Prep

Creating fresh herb spices is simple and can be done in several ways. You can blend them into an oil or vinegar-based marinade, combine them with other seasonings for a dry rub, or infuse them into salt for a flavorful finishing touch. Below, we’ll outline three popular methods to make fresh herb spices and incorporate them into your meal prep routine.

1. Fresh Herb Oil Blends

Herb-infused oils are a great way to make your own spice blend while also creating a versatile cooking ingredient. You can use fresh herbs to create fragrant oils that can be drizzled on vegetables, meats, or pasta dishes. The process is simple, and the results are delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup olive oil (or any oil of your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Wash and chop your herbs into small pieces.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the olive oil over low heat. Be careful not to burn the oil.
  3. Add the chopped herbs and garlic to the oil. Let the herbs infuse into the oil for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Once the oil has absorbed the flavors of the herbs, remove the saucepan from heat and let the oil cool.
  5. Strain out the herbs and store the infused oil in a glass bottle or jar. You can store the oil in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

Use this herb-infused oil to cook meats, drizzle over roasted vegetables, or mix with balsamic vinegar to create a flavorful salad dressing.

2. Fresh Herb Salt

Herb-infused salt is an easy and effective way to add flavor to almost any dish. This simple combination of sea salt and fresh herbs enhances the natural taste of your food and is an excellent addition to your meal prep arsenal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sea salt
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the sea salt with the chopped herbs and lemon zest.
  2. Stir the mixture together, ensuring the herbs are evenly distributed throughout the salt.
  3. Spread the herb salt onto a baking sheet and allow it to dry for about 1-2 hours.
  4. Once the salt has dried, store it in an airtight jar or container.

This herb salt can be used to season meats, sprinkle over roasted vegetables, or add to soups and salads. The possibilities are endless!

3. Herb Dry Rubs

Dry rubs are an excellent way to create flavorful, well-seasoned meats for grilling or roasting. They are simple to make and can be customized to suit your taste preferences. By using fresh herbs as a base, you’ll elevate your rubs to a whole new level.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a small bowl and mix well.
  2. Apply the rub to your choice of meat, ensuring it’s evenly coated.
  3. Allow the meat to marinate for at least 30 minutes (or longer for more intense flavor) before cooking.

This fresh herb dry rub works wonderfully with chicken, beef, lamb, or pork. It’s a great way to prepare meals ahead of time for grilling or roasting.

Storing Fresh Herb Spices

Once you’ve made your fresh herb spices, it’s important to store them properly to maintain their flavor and freshness. Here are some tips for storing your homemade herb blends:

1. Infused Oils

Store your infused oils in a glass bottle or jar with a tight-fitting lid. Keep the oils in the refrigerator, as they can go rancid quickly when exposed to heat. Be sure to use them within 2 weeks.

2. Herb Salt

Keep your herb-infused salt in an airtight container or glass jar. Store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Herb salt will stay fresh for several weeks.

3. Dry Rubs

Store your dry rubs in an airtight container or spice jar. Keep the rubs in a cool, dark place, and they will stay fresh for several weeks to months.

Tips for Making the Most of Your Fresh Herb Spices

  • Batch Prep: Prepare your herb spice mixes in larger batches and store them for easy use throughout the week.
  • Experiment with Combinations: Don’t be afraid to experiment with different herb combinations to suit your taste preferences.
  • Grow Your Own Herbs: Growing herbs at home ensures a constant supply of fresh ingredients for your spice blends.
  • Use in Sauces and Dressings: Fresh herb spices can be easily incorporated into sauces, dressings, and marinades for added flavor.

Conclusion

Making fresh herb spices for your meal prep is a simple yet impactful way to boost the flavor of your dishes while also improving your health. Whether you’re creating herb-infused oils, herb salts, or dry rubs, these homemade spice blends will make meal prep faster, healthier, and more delicious. By incorporating fresh herbs into your cooking, you’ll have access to vibrant flavors and nutritional benefits that will take your meals to the next level.
