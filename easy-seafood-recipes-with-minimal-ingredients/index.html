Easy Seafood Recipes with Minimal Ingredients


Seafood is often thought of as a luxurious ingredient, reserved for special occasions or intricate recipes that require hours in the kitchen. However, cooking seafood at home can be incredibly simple, quick, and delicious, even with minimal ingredients. Whether you’re a novice in the kitchen or a seasoned chef looking to streamline your meals, these easy seafood recipes are the perfect way to bring the flavors of the ocean to your table with minimal fuss.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of seafood dishes that require just a handful of ingredients, most of which you probably already have in your pantry. With just a few simple steps, you can create satisfying meals that are full of flavor. From shrimp and fish to scallops and crab, there’s something for every seafood lover.

1. Garlic Butter Shrimp

Garlic butter shrimp is a classic dish that combines the rich flavors of butter and garlic with the delicate sweetness of shrimp. This recipe is so simple, you’ll wonder why you didn’t make it sooner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the shrimp: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the shrimp and season with salt and pepper. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque.
  2. Add the garlic: Push the shrimp to one side of the skillet and add the minced garlic. Let it cook for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
  3. Combine: Stir the garlic and shrimp together, coating them in the buttery goodness. Remove from heat.
  4. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle with fresh parsley, if desired, and serve with a side of rice or a salad for a complete meal.

This garlic butter shrimp is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner and can be enjoyed with pasta, rice, or even a fresh loaf of bread to soak up the sauce.

2. Lemon Herb Grilled Salmon

Salmon is a fantastic fish that’s both flavorful and easy to cook. This lemon herb grilled salmon is full of zesty citrus and fresh herbs, making it a perfect light meal for warm weather.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the salmon: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Rub the salmon fillets with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and fresh dill.
  2. Grill the salmon: Place the salmon fillets on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the fillets. The salmon should easily flake with a fork when done.
  3. Add lemon: While the salmon is grilling, place lemon slices on the grill to lightly char them. This will bring out the citrus flavor.
  4. Serve: Once the salmon is cooked, serve it with the grilled lemon slices for an added burst of flavor. Pair with a side of roasted vegetables or a simple green salad for a fresh, healthy meal.

3. Crispy Baked Fish Tacos

Fish tacos are a fun and flavorful way to enjoy seafood, and you don’t need a long list of ingredients to make them. The crispy fish combined with fresh toppings is a meal the whole family will love.

Ingredients:

  • 4 white fish fillets (such as cod or tilapia)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Corn tortillas
  • Optional toppings: shredded cabbage, avocado, salsa, cilantro, sour cream

Instructions:

  1. Prep the fish: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Dip each fish fillet into the beaten egg, then coat it with breadcrumbs, pressing down to ensure they stick.
  2. Bake the fish: Place the breaded fillets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Drizzle with olive oil and bake for 12-15 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the fish is golden and crispy.
  3. Assemble the tacos: Warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave. Once the fish is done, break it into pieces and place them in the tortillas.
  4. Add toppings: Top the fish with your favorite taco toppings such as shredded cabbage, avocado, salsa, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
  5. Serve: Serve the tacos immediately, with extra lime wedges on the side.

These crispy baked fish tacos are a fun and satisfying meal that doesn’t require a lot of ingredients, but still delivers big flavor.

4. Simple Scallops in Lemon Butter Sauce

Scallops are an elegant seafood option that can be cooked quickly and easily. This lemon butter sauce is the perfect complement to the sweet, delicate flavor of the scallops.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb scallops, patted dry
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the scallops: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Pat the scallops dry with a paper towel and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Sear the scallops: Add 2 tablespoons of butter to the skillet and sear the scallops for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Make the sauce: In the same skillet, add the remaining butter and lemon juice, stirring to combine. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the sauce is slightly thickened.
  4. Serve: Return the scallops to the skillet, tossing them in the lemon butter sauce. Garnish with fresh parsley, if desired, and serve with a side of steamed vegetables or a light pasta.

These scallops in lemon butter sauce are perfect for a special dinner or a quick, gourmet weeknight meal. With just a few ingredients, you can elevate your seafood experience.

5. Shrimp and Avocado Salad

This refreshing shrimp and avocado salad is a perfect light lunch or dinner. The creamy avocado pairs beautifully with the succulent shrimp, while the simple dressing ties everything together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the cooked shrimp, diced avocados, and cucumber.
  2. Make the dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Drizzle the dressing over the shrimp and avocado mixture.
  3. Toss and serve: Gently toss the salad to combine all ingredients. Serve immediately or chill for 30 minutes before serving.

This shrimp and avocado salad is light, fresh, and full of healthy fats, making it a great choice for a quick and nutritious meal.

6. One-Pan Garlic Shrimp and Asparagus

For a meal that’s both easy and healthy, this one-pan garlic shrimp and asparagus is a great option. The shrimp is perfectly cooked with tender asparagus, all seasoned with garlic and olive oil for maximum flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare the ingredients: In a large bowl, toss the shrimp and asparagus with olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Bake the dish: Spread the shrimp and asparagus on a baking sheet in an even layer. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through, and the asparagus is tender.
  4. Serve: Serve immediately, perhaps with a side of quinoa or rice for a well-rounded meal.

This one-pan dish is quick to prepare, making cleanup a breeze while still delivering plenty of flavors.

7. Crab Cakes with Lemon Aioli

Crab cakes are a delicious way to enjoy seafood, and they’re surprisingly easy to make. With minimal ingredients, these crab cakes are packed with flavor and perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb crab meat, drained
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Make the crab cake mixture: In a bowl, combine the crab meat, egg, breadcrumbs, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix gently until well combined.
  2. Form the cakes: Shape the mixture into 6-8 small patties.
  3. Fry the crab cakes: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry the crab cakes for 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Make the aioli: In a small bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of mayonnaise, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, and a pinch of salt. Stir to combine.
  5. Serve: Serve the crab cakes with the lemon aioli on the side for dipping.

These crab cakes are flavorful, crispy on the outside, and tender on the inside. Serve them with a side salad or steamed vegetables for a complete meal.

Conclusion

As you can see, cooking seafood doesn’t have to be complicated. Whether you’re making shrimp, fish, scallops, or crab, the key is using fresh ingredients and keeping the recipe simple. These easy seafood dishes are perfect for busy weeknights, lazy weekends, or even when you want to impress your guests with minimal effort. With just a few simple ingredients, you can enjoy the rich and flavorful taste of the ocean in your own kitchen. Happy cooking!
