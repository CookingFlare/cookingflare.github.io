Savory Vegan Portobello Mushroom Burgers for BBQs


If you’re tired of the same old veggie burgers at your BBQs, it’s time to try something new and exciting. These savory vegan Portobello mushroom burgers are a game-changer. Packed with flavor and texture, they are sure to impress even the most dedicated meat-eaters at your gathering.

Ingredients

  • 4 large Portobello mushroom caps
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns
  • Burger toppings of your choice (lettuce, tomato, onion, etc.)

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, minced garlic, soy sauce, smoked paprika, dried thyme, salt, and pepper.

  2. Place the Portobello mushroom caps in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Make sure the mushrooms are well coated. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, turning them occasionally to ensure even distribution of flavors.

  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you don’t have a grill, you can also cook the mushrooms on a grill pan or in a skillet.

  4. Once the grill is hot, place the marinated Portobello mushrooms on the grates, cap side down. Cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until the mushrooms are tender and slightly charred.

  5. While the mushrooms are grilling, prepare your burger buns and toppings. Toast the buns if desired and gather your favorite burger accompaniments.

  6. Once the mushrooms are done, transfer them to a plate and let them rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute and the flavors to meld together.

  7. To assemble the burgers, place a Portobello mushroom cap on the bottom half of each bun. Add your desired toppings, such as lettuce, tomato, onion, or any other condiments you prefer.

  8. Finally, top each burger with the remaining bun half and serve immediately. These savory vegan Portobello mushroom burgers are best enjoyed fresh off the grill.

Tips and Variations

  • If you want to add an extra layer of flavor to your burgers, feel free to experiment with different marinades. Try adding a splash of Worcestershire sauce, a squeeze of lemon juice, or even some hot sauce for a spicy kick.

  • For a cheesy twist, melt some vegan cheese on top of the grilled mushrooms during the last minute of cooking. This will add a creamy and indulgent element to your burgers.

  • If you’re not a fan of mushrooms or want to offer more variety, you can also grill some sliced eggplant or zucchini and use them as burger patties instead.

  • Don’t forget about the sides! Serve your Portobello mushroom burgers with some grilled vegetables, a refreshing salad, or even some homemade sweet potato fries for a complete and satisfying meal.

So, the next time you’re planning a BBQ, think beyond the traditional veggie burgers and give these savory vegan Portobello mushroom burgers a try. They’re easy to make, bursting with flavor, and will surely impress your guests. Enjoy the smoky goodness and the satisfaction of creating a delicious plant-based option that everyone can enjoy. Happy grilling!
