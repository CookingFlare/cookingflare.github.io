Culinary Tour of Vietnam: 5 Must-Try Pho Recipes


As the world becomes increasingly smaller and our cultures intertwine, it's no surprise that Vietnamese cuisine has gained immense popularity globally. The art of cooking in Vietnam revolves around the delicate balance of flavors, textures, and aromas, which is perhaps most evident in their iconic and revered dish: Pho. This article takes you on a culinary tour of Vietnam, focusing on 5 must-try Pho recipes that will leave you craving for more. Pho Origins Pho's journey began in the 9th century in rural Vietnam, where Buddhist temples served noodle soups as a humble dish for monks. The name "Pho" is believed to be derived from the Chinese word 'Fàn" or 'phàn', meaning 'Rice' or 'Rule'. Over time, beef was introduced to the region, and the gradual refinement of Pho's rich flavor profile led to its widespread popularity in the Hoa people's – an ethnic minority group in China - epicenter, Hanoi, today.
