Sesame Chicken: A Popular Chinese Takeout Dish Made at Home


Sesame chicken is a beloved dish in Chinese cuisine, known for its crispy texture, rich flavors, and aromatic sesame seeds. While many people enjoy ordering it from their favorite Chinese takeout restaurants, making sesame chicken at home can be just as delicious and rewarding. In this recipe, we will guide you through the steps to recreate this classic dish in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the chicken. Cut the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and set aside.

  2. In a shallow bowl, combine the cornstarch, flour, salt, and black pepper. Dredge each piece of chicken in the mixture, ensuring it is thoroughly coated.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the coated chicken pieces, a few at a time, and fry until golden brown and crispy. Remove the cooked chicken from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, hoisin sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, and grated ginger until well combined.

  5. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch and water to create a slurry. Set aside.

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove any excess oil from the skillet or wok, leaving about 1 tablespoon. Return the skillet to medium heat and add the sauce mixture. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce begins to thicken.

  7. Gradually add the cornstarch slurry to the skillet, stirring constantly, until the sauce reaches your desired consistency. This will help thicken the sauce and give it a glossy appearance.

  8. Add the cooked chicken to the skillet and toss gently to coat each piece with the sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the chicken to absorb the flavors.

  9. Remove the skillet from the heat and sprinkle the sliced green onions and sesame seeds over the sesame chicken. Toss lightly to distribute the garnishes evenly.

  10. Serve the sesame chicken hot, accompanied by steamed rice or noodles. The nutty flavor of the sesame seeds and the savory-sweet sauce will surely delight your taste buds.

Conclusion

Making sesame chicken at home is a fantastic way to enjoy this popular Chinese takeout dish without leaving the house. By following this recipe, you can savor the crispy chicken, fragrant sesame seeds, and the delightful balance of flavors in the homemade sauce. So why not give it a try? Impress your family and friends with your culinary skills and enjoy the satisfaction of creating a delicious meal from scratch. Happy cooking!
