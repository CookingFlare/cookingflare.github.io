Quick Canning Recipes for Busy People


Canning is an excellent way to preserve your favorite fruits, vegetables, and even meats for later use. It allows you to enjoy seasonal produce year-round and stock up on homemade condiments and meals. However, the traditional canning process often involves long hours of preparation and complicated steps, making it seem daunting to busy people. If you’re constantly on the go but still want to preserve fresh foods, quick canning recipes are the solution.

In this post, we will share some of the best quick canning recipes that are perfect for those with a busy lifestyle. These easy-to-make recipes won’t require a lot of prep time or special equipment. So, if you’re ready to start canning in a snap, keep reading!

Why Quick Canning?

Traditional canning methods, while effective, can take hours from start to finish. Between prepping the produce, sterilizing jars, processing them in a water bath or pressure canner, and then waiting for them to seal, the process can be quite time-consuming. That’s where quick canning comes in—these methods focus on simplicity, shorter processing times, and less hands-on involvement, making it easy to preserve food even on your busiest days.

Additionally, quick canning allows you to preserve produce while it’s still fresh, locking in its flavors and nutrients. Many of these recipes use ingredients that you likely already have at home, reducing the need for extensive shopping trips.

Basic Tools for Quick Canning

Before diving into the recipes, let’s go over the essential tools you’ll need. For quick canning, you won’t need any complex equipment, just the basics:

  • Mason Jars: These are the most common containers for canning. Ensure they are clean and free of cracks or chips.
  • Lids and Bands: Lids need to be brand-new for a proper seal, while the bands can be reused as long as they are in good condition.
  • Large Pot or Stockpot: This is used for boiling your jars or making your canning recipe.
  • Jar Lifter: This tool helps safely remove hot jars from the boiling water.
  • Funnel: A canning funnel helps pour hot liquids or chunky mixtures into jars without spills.
  • Tongs: For handling hot lids and bands.
  • Timer: Quick canning still requires accurate timing for best results.

With these basic tools, you’re ready to start preserving your favorite foods. Let’s dive into some quick canning recipes that will save you time while giving you delicious results.

1. Quick Pickled Cucumbers

Pickles are a classic choice for quick canning because they’re easy to prepare and store. Quick pickled cucumbers add a tangy, refreshing flavor to any meal and are perfect for snacking or as a topping for burgers and sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 medium cucumbers, sliced thin
  • 2 cups white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Begin by sterilizing your mason jars. You can do this by placing them in a hot water bath or running them through the dishwasher.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the vinegar, water, sugar, garlic, mustard seeds, peppercorns, red pepper flakes, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for about 5-7 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  4. While the brine simmers, pack the sliced cucumbers tightly into the sterilized mason jars.
  5. Pour the hot brine over the cucumbers, ensuring they are completely covered. Leave about 1/2 inch of headspace at the top of the jar.
  6. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean towel and screw the lids on tightly.
  7. Process the jars in a water bath for 5 minutes.
  8. Allow the jars to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate. They’ll be ready to eat within 24 hours, but the flavor improves over time.

Quick pickled cucumbers are perfect for busy people because they’re made in less than 30 minutes and provide a flavorful side dish or snack.

2. Simple Jam and Jelly Recipes

Making jam or jelly is another quick canning method that’s perfect for busy schedules. Fresh fruit jams and jellies are a great way to preserve the sweetness of the season while adding a homemade touch to your breakfast, desserts, or snacks.

Strawberry Jam

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and mashed
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 box pectin (liquid or powder)

Instructions:

  1. Sterilize your jars and lids before you begin.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the mashed strawberries, sugar, and lemon juice. Stir the mixture over medium heat until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil and add the pectin according to the instructions on the package.
  4. Let the jam boil for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. You can test the consistency by placing a small spoonful on a cold plate and running your finger through it. If it wrinkles, it’s done.
  5. Carefully pour the hot jam into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace.
  6. Wipe the rims, seal the jars, and process in a boiling water bath for 5-10 minutes.
  7. Let the jars cool before storing. Homemade strawberry jam can be enjoyed for months, making it an easy recipe to make during berry season and enjoy later.

Grape Jelly

Grape jelly is another simple canning recipe that can be made in less than an hour. The sweet, fruity flavor pairs well with toast, pastries, or even as a glaze for meats.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups grape juice (100% pure)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 box pectin (liquid or powder)
  • 4 cups sugar

Instructions:

  1. Sterilize your jars.
  2. Combine the grape juice and lemon juice in a large saucepan and bring to a boil.
  3. Add the pectin and stir until it dissolves.
  4. Once the mixture comes to a full boil, stir in the sugar. Let the jelly boil for 1-2 minutes, constantly stirring to prevent burning.
  5. Test the jelly’s consistency using the cold plate method. Once it’s ready, pour the jelly into jars, leaving 1/4 inch of headspace.
  6. Seal the jars and process them in a boiling water bath for 5-10 minutes.
  7. Let the jars cool completely and store.

Both strawberry jam and grape jelly are quick to make, and you can enjoy the delicious results in a matter of hours, without spending an entire day in the kitchen.

3. Quick Relish Recipes

Relishes are an excellent way to preserve garden vegetables like peppers, cucumbers, and zucchini. They add a zesty crunch to sandwiches, hot dogs, and burgers. Here’s a quick relish recipe for busy cooks.

Sweet Pepper Relish

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers, finely chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sterilize the jars and lids.
  2. In a large pot, combine the chopped peppers and onion with vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, turmeric, and salt.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat and cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Once the mixture thickens, pour it into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace.
  5. Seal the jars and process in a water bath for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Let the jars cool to room temperature before storing in a cool, dark place. This sweet pepper relish is perfect for pairing with grilled meats and sandwiches.

4. Quick Tomato Sauce

Tomatoes are often in abundance during the summer months, and quick canning can help you preserve them for use throughout the year. Homemade tomato sauce is versatile and can be used in a variety of dishes, from pasta to casseroles and pizzas.

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 ripe tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Begin by sterilizing your mason jars.
  2. Blanch the tomatoes by placing them in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then transferring them to ice water. Peel the skins off and chop the tomatoes.
  3. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Pour the sauce into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace.
  6. Wipe the rims of the jars, seal them, and process them in a water bath for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Let the jars cool before storing them in a cool, dark place.

This quick tomato sauce is a fantastic base for various dishes and can be made in just under an hour.

Conclusion

Canning doesn’t have to be time-consuming or overwhelming. With these quick canning recipes, you can easily preserve your favorite fruits, vegetables, and condiments in no time at all. From pickles to jam, relish, and tomato sauce, these recipes are designed for busy people who want to make the most of their time and enjoy homemade preserves throughout the year.

Whether you have a bountiful harvest from your garden or you simply want to preserve produce on sale at the local market, quick canning offers a simple solution. With a little preparation and a few basic ingredients, you can enjoy the fruits of your labor in just a few hours. So, grab your mason jars and get started with these quick canning recipes today!
