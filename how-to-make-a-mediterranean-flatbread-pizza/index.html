How to Make a Mediterranean Flatbread Pizza


The Mediterranean diet is celebrated for its vibrant flavors, healthy ingredients, and its emphasis on fresh vegetables, whole grains, and healthy fats. One dish that embodies the essence of Mediterranean cuisine is the Mediterranean flatbread pizza. A delightful fusion of crispy flatbread topped with fresh ingredients like olives, feta cheese, tomatoes, and herbs, this pizza offers a refreshing, healthier alternative to traditional pizza. Whether you’re preparing a quick weeknight dinner or hosting a casual gathering, this Mediterranean flatbread pizza recipe is sure to impress.

What Makes Mediterranean Flatbread Pizza So Special?

Mediterranean flatbread pizza is a wonderful blend of flavors and textures. Unlike traditional pizza dough, which can be dense and doughy, flatbread pizza uses thin, crisp flatbread as the base, which creates a lighter and crispier texture. The toppings are fresh, vibrant, and packed with nutrients, offering a balance of savory, tangy, and earthy flavors. This pizza typically includes ingredients such as tangy feta cheese, juicy tomatoes, crunchy olives, and fragrant herbs like oregano and basil. Whether you make it with a homemade flatbread or use store-bought versions, this pizza is incredibly versatile and customizable to suit your taste preferences.

Ingredients for Mediterranean Flatbread Pizza

The beauty of Mediterranean flatbread pizza lies in its simplicity. You can easily find these ingredients at your local grocery store, and many of them are pantry staples. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Flatbread Base:

  • 2 store-bought or homemade flatbreads (you can use pita bread, naan, or any other flatbread that you prefer)
  • Olive oil (for brushing)
  • Salt and pepper (for seasoning)

For the Toppings:

  • 1 cup of crumbled feta cheese (you can substitute with goat cheese or ricotta if desired)
  • 1/2 cup of Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced (green olives are also a great option)
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved (or any variety of ripe tomatoes, sliced)
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh oregano leaves (or 1 teaspoon dried oregano)
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of extra virgin olive oil (for drizzling)
  • 1 tablespoon of balsamic glaze (optional but recommended for a tangy finish)
  • Freshly ground black pepper (for seasoning)
  • Fresh arugula (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Mediterranean Flatbread Pizza

Step 1: Prepare the Flatbread Base

Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). This temperature is perfect for achieving a crispy, golden-brown flatbread base.

If you’re using store-bought flatbreads, simply place them on a baking sheet. If you’re using homemade flatbreads, make sure they are evenly shaped and the right size to fit your baking sheet.

Brush each flatbread lightly with olive oil to enhance its crispiness and flavor. Season with a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper.

Step 2: Assemble the Pizza

Once the flatbreads are prepared, it’s time to add the toppings. Here’s where the Mediterranean magic happens.

Start by evenly spreading the crumbled feta cheese over the flatbread base. Feta is the star ingredient here, lending its creamy, briny flavor that pairs beautifully with the other ingredients.

Next, scatter the sliced Kalamata olives over the cheese. The salty, savory flavor of the olives will complement the richness of the feta.

Add the halved cherry tomatoes or sliced tomatoes next. Their juicy sweetness will contrast with the savory elements and provide a pop of color.

Now, add the thinly sliced red onion. The mild sweetness of the onion adds a subtle crunch and a sharp contrast to the other ingredients.

Sprinkle the fresh oregano leaves (or dried oregano) over the pizza. Oregano is a quintessential Mediterranean herb, offering earthy, aromatic flavors that tie all the ingredients together. If you love garlic, you can also sprinkle a bit of garlic powder over the pizza for an extra flavor boost.

Step 3: Bake the Pizza

Place the assembled flatbread pizzas in the preheated oven and bake for 8-12 minutes, or until the flatbread is golden brown and crispy around the edges. The cheese should be slightly melted, and the toppings should be warmed through.

Since flatbreads are thinner than traditional pizza dough, they cook quickly, so keep a close eye on them to prevent burning.

Step 4: Garnish and Serve

Once the Mediterranean flatbread pizza has baked to perfection, remove it from the oven and drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the top for an added layer of richness. If you’re feeling adventurous, a drizzle of balsamic glaze will provide a tangy contrast that balances the richness of the feta and olives.

For a refreshing touch, add a handful of fresh arugula on top of the pizza. The peppery bite of the arugula adds a delightful crunch and a burst of freshness.

Finally, tear some fresh basil leaves and scatter them over the pizza for an aromatic finish. Season with freshly ground black pepper to taste.

Slice your Mediterranean flatbread pizza into wedges and serve immediately. The crispy flatbread base, combined with the fresh toppings, creates a pizza that is both satisfying and light at the same time.

Tips for Customizing Your Mediterranean Flatbread Pizza

One of the best things about Mediterranean flatbread pizza is its versatility. Here are a few ideas for customizing the recipe to suit your preferences or dietary needs:

1. Add Protein

If you’re craving more protein, consider adding grilled chicken, shrimp, or lamb. These proteins pair perfectly with the Mediterranean flavors, and grilled meats will add a delicious smoky depth to the pizza. Alternatively, you can add chickpeas for a plant-based option.

2. Swap the Flatbread

If you want a heartier base, you can swap the flatbread for pita bread, naan, or even gluten-free flatbread if you have dietary restrictions. The key is to ensure that whatever base you choose is crisp and sturdy enough to support the toppings.

3. Experiment with Other Cheeses

While feta is the traditional choice for Mediterranean pizzas, you can experiment with other cheeses like goat cheese, ricotta, or even mozzarella if you want a creamier or more melty texture.

4. Vary the Vegetables

Feel free to mix up the vegetables based on what’s in season or what you have on hand. Thinly sliced cucumbers, roasted red peppers, artichokes, or zucchini can all make excellent additions to your Mediterranean flatbread pizza.

5. Use Different Olives

Kalamata olives are a classic Mediterranean choice, but you can also use green olives, black olives, or a mix of both. The flavor of the olives will influence the overall taste of the pizza, so feel free to experiment.

6. Try Different Herbs and Spices

While oregano is the go-to herb for Mediterranean dishes, you can also try fresh thyme, rosemary, or marjoram for different flavor profiles. A pinch of crushed red pepper flakes will add a touch of heat if you like a bit of spice.

Health Benefits of Mediterranean Flatbread Pizza

Mediterranean flatbread pizza is not just delicious; it’s also packed with healthy ingredients that offer numerous benefits:

  • Olives: Rich in heart-healthy monounsaturated fats, antioxidants, and anti-inflammatory compounds, olives can help reduce the risk of cardiovascular diseases.
  • Feta Cheese: Feta is lower in fat and calories compared to many other cheeses, and it contains beneficial probiotics that support gut health.
  • Tomatoes: Loaded with vitamins A and C, tomatoes are rich in lycopene, an antioxidant that has been shown to promote heart health and protect against certain cancers.
  • Olive Oil: Extra virgin olive oil is high in monounsaturated fats and antioxidants, making it a great choice for reducing inflammation and promoting heart health.
  • Herbs: Fresh herbs like oregano and basil are not only flavorful but also provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

By using wholesome ingredients, Mediterranean flatbread pizza offers a healthier twist on traditional pizza, making it an excellent choice for anyone looking to enjoy a nutritious meal without compromising on taste.

Conclusion

Making a Mediterranean flatbread pizza is a fun and easy way to bring the flavors of the Mediterranean into your kitchen. With its crispy base, fresh toppings, and rich, savory flavors, this pizza is perfect for a quick weeknight meal or a casual gathering with friends and family. Plus, it’s highly customizable to suit your dietary needs and preferences.

Whether you’re looking for a lighter alternative to traditional pizza or simply want to try something new, this Mediterranean flatbread pizza recipe is a winner. With its simple ingredients and easy preparation, you can enjoy a fresh, flavorful meal that’s both satisfying and healthy. So go ahead, get creative with your toppings, and enjoy the delicious taste of the Mediterranean!
