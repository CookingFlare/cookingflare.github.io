Yummy Low Carb Teriyaki Chicken Stir Fry Recipe


If you’re on a low-carb diet but still crave the savory, sweet flavors of traditional teriyaki chicken, this Yummy Low Carb Teriyaki Chicken Stir Fry recipe is the perfect solution. Bursting with fresh vegetables and tender chicken, this dish is not only healthy but also incredibly satisfying. Let’s dive into the details of making this delicious stir fry that will keep your taste buds dancing and your carb count in check!

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon erythritol or your preferred low-carb sweetener (optional)

For the Stir Fry

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil (or any high-heat oil of your choice)
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup bell peppers, thinly sliced (red, yellow, or a mix)
  • 1 cup snap peas or green beans, trimmed
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, minced ginger, minced garlic, and erythritol.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. For best results, marinate for 2 hours or overnight.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables

While the chicken is marinating, prepare your vegetables:

  • Wash and chop the broccoli into bite-sized florets.
  • Slice the bell peppers into thin strips.
  • Trim the ends of the snap peas or green beans.
  • Cut the zucchini into half-moons.
  • Slice the mushrooms.

Step 3: Stir Fry the Chicken

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Remove the chicken from the marinade, allowing excess liquid to drip off. Reserve the marinade for later.
  3. Add the chicken to the skillet in a single layer. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is browned and cooked through.
  4. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside on a plate.

Step 4: Stir Fry the Vegetables

  1. In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of coconut oil.
  2. Add the broccoli, bell peppers, snap peas, zucchini, and mushrooms. Stir fry for about 5-6 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  3. Pour in the reserved marinade and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.

Step 5: Combine and Serve

  1. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet, tossing it with the vegetables and sauce until everything is well-coated.
  2. Cook for another minute to heat everything through.
  3. Remove from heat and serve immediately, garnished with chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds.

Tips for the Best Low Carb Teriyaki Chicken Stir Fry

  • Cut Uniformly: Ensure that chicken and vegetables are cut into uniform pieces to promote even cooking.
  • Fresh Ingredients: Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor. Organic vegetables and fresh chicken can elevate the dish.
  • Meal Prep: This stir fry makes for excellent meal prep. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days.
  • Low Carb Vegetables: Feel free to swap in your favorite low-carb vegetables based on what you have on hand. Cauliflower, bok choy, or asparagus are great alternatives.
  • Adjust Sweetness: If you prefer a sweeter teriyaki sauce, adjust the amount of erythritol or opt for a low-carb teriyaki sauce.

Nutritional Benefits of Low Carb Teriyaki Chicken Stir Fry

This Yummy Low Carb Teriyaki Chicken Stir Fry is not only delicious but also comes with several nutritional benefits:

  • High Protein: Chicken is an excellent source of lean protein, essential for muscle repair and growth.
  • Low in Carbs: With a careful selection of ingredients, this dish is low in carbohydrates, making it suitable for those watching their carb intake.
  • Packed with Vegetables: The variety of vegetables adds vitamins, minerals, and fiber, contributing to a well-rounded meal.
  • Healthy Fats: The use of coconut oil and sesame oil provides healthy fats that support heart health.

Conclusion

Incorporating a Yummy Low Carb Teriyaki Chicken Stir Fry into your weekly meal rotation is a fantastic way to enjoy a flavorful dish without compromising your dietary goals. This recipe not only satisfies your cravings but also aligns with a low-carb lifestyle, making it a win-win for any health-conscious individual. Give this recipe a try, and enjoy a delectable, homemade teriyaki experience that you can feel good about!

FAQs

Can I use another protein instead of chicken?
Absolutely! This recipe works well with shrimp, beef, or tofu, allowing you to customize it based on your preferences or dietary needs.

Is this recipe suitable for meal prepping?
Yes! This stir fry can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to three days, making it a convenient option for meal prep.

How can I make this dish spicier?
If you enjoy heat, consider adding red pepper flakes or a dash of sriracha to the marinade or stir fry for an extra kick.

Can I freeze leftovers?
While it’s best enjoyed fresh, you can freeze leftovers in an airtight container. However, the texture of the vegetables may change upon reheating.
