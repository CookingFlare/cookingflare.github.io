10 Thai Recipes for a Festive Celebration


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and colorful presentations. When it comes to festive celebrations, Thai dishes can elevate any gathering, bringing a sense of joy and excitement to the table. Whether you’re hosting a family reunion, a holiday party, or a special occasion, these ten Thai recipes will impress your guests and create a memorable dining experience. Let’s dive into the world of Thai cooking and explore these delightful recipes!

1. Tom Yum Goong (Spicy Shrimp Soup)

Ingredients:

  • 500g large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
  • 4-5 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 3-4 slices galangal (or ginger if unavailable)
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 3-4 Thai bird’s eye chilies, smashed (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring the broth to a boil. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  2. Add the mushrooms and chilies, cooking for another 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add the shrimp and cook until they turn pink, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce and lime juice. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

Tom Yum Goong is a quintessential Thai dish that combines spicy, sour, and savory flavors, making it a perfect starter for any festive celebration.

2. Pad Thai (Stir-Fried Noodles)

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200g chicken breast or tofu, sliced
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 100g bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Crushed peanuts and lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes until softened. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan or wok, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken or tofu and cook until browned. Push to one side of the pan and pour in the beaten eggs, scrambling until cooked.
  4. Add the soaked noodles, fish sauce, tamarind paste, and sugar. Toss everything together until well combined.
  5. Stir in the bean sprouts and green onions, cooking for another minute.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with crushed peanuts and lime wedges.

Pad Thai is a beloved street food dish that brings a delightful mix of textures and flavors, making it a must-have for any festive gathering.

3. Green Curry Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup eggplant, diced
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and cook until they are no longer pink.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth, bringing it to a simmer.
  4. Add the eggplant and bell peppers, cooking until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Stir in the fish sauce and sugar, adjusting to taste.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

Green Curry Chicken is a rich and creamy dish that embodies the essence of Thai cuisine, perfect for sharing with loved ones during festive occasions.

4. Som Tum (Green Papaya Salad)

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium green papaya, peeled and shredded
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, smashed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • 100g cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 50g green beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Crushed peanuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a mortar and pestle, pound the chilies and garlic until they form a paste.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Add the shredded papaya, cherry tomatoes, and green beans to the bowl.
