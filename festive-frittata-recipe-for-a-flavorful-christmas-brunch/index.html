Festive Frittata Recipe for a Flavorful Christmas Brunch


As the festive season approaches, there’s no better way to celebrate than with a delicious and hearty Christmas brunch. A festive frittata is a perfect addition to your holiday menu, offering a flavorful and satisfying dish that is sure to impress your guests. This versatile and easy-to-make recipe allows you to incorporate a variety of seasonal ingredients, making it a festive and colorful addition to your Christmas spread.

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup diced cooked ham or smoked salmon
  • 1 cup chopped bell peppers (red and green for a festive touch)
  • 1 cup chopped spinach
  • 1/2 cup diced cherry tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup chopped red onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 10-inch oven-safe skillet.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and Parmesan cheese until well combined. Season the mixture with salt, pepper, oregano, and thyme.

  3. Heat the olive oil in the skillet over medium heat. Add the red onions and sauté until they are soft and translucent. Then, add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  4. Add the bell peppers, mushrooms, and cherry tomatoes to the skillet, and cook until the vegetables are tender.

  5. Stir in the diced ham or smoked salmon, along with the chopped spinach, and cook for a few minutes until the spinach wilts.

  6. Pour the egg mixture evenly over the ingredients in the skillet. Allow the frittata to cook undisturbed for 5 minutes, or until the edges start to set.

  7. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the frittata is set in the center and the top is lightly golden.

  8. Remove the frittata from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing it into wedges.

Serving Suggestions

This festive frittata is best served warm, making it the perfect centerpiece for your Christmas brunch. You can complement the frittata with a fresh fruit salad, crispy bacon, and toasted bread or English muffins. For a touch of elegance, consider serving it with a dollop of sour cream or a drizzle of hollandaise sauce.

Conclusion

The festive frittata is a delightful addition to any Christmas brunch, offering a harmonious blend of flavors and colors that capture the spirit of the season. Its versatility allows you to personalize the recipe with your favorite seasonal ingredients, making it a crowd-pleaser for guests with varying tastes. Whether you’re hosting a small gathering or a large family feast, this festive frittata is sure to bring joy to your holiday table. Embrace the warmth of the season and treat your loved ones to a memorable Christmas brunch with this delightful and flavorful dish.
