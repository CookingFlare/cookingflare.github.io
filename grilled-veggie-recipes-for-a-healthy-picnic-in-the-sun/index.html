Grilled Veggie Recipes for a Healthy Picnic in the Sun


As the sun shines brightly and the days grow longer, there’s no better time to gather your friends and family for a delightful picnic. What better way to celebrate the season than with a spread of vibrant, grilled vegetables? Grilling not only enhances the natural flavors of veggies but also adds a smoky depth that makes them irresistible. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of grilled veggie recipes that are perfect for a healthy picnic in the sun. From colorful skewers to hearty salads, these dishes are sure to impress and nourish.

The Benefits of Grilling Vegetables

Grilling vegetables is not just about flavor; it also offers numerous health benefits. When you grill veggies, you retain more nutrients compared to boiling or frying. The high heat of the grill caramelizes the natural sugars in vegetables, creating a deliciously sweet and smoky flavor. Additionally, grilling requires little to no added fats, making it a heart-healthy cooking method.

Choosing the Right Vegetables

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s talk about which vegetables are best for grilling. Some of the most popular choices include:

  • Bell Peppers: Their sweetness intensifies when grilled, making them a favorite for skewers and salads.
  • Zucchini: This versatile vegetable becomes tender and flavorful on the grill.
  • Eggplant: With its meaty texture, eggplant holds up well to grilling and absorbs marinades beautifully.
  • Mushrooms: Their umami flavor is enhanced by grilling, making them a great addition to any dish.
  • Asparagus: This green vegetable becomes tender and slightly crispy when grilled, adding a delightful crunch.
  • Corn on the Cob: Grilling corn brings out its natural sweetness and adds a smoky flavor.

Now that we’ve covered the basics, let’s get into some delicious grilled veggie recipes that will elevate your picnic experience.

Grilled Vegetable Skewers

Ingredients

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 8 oz. mushrooms, cleaned and stems removed
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Skewers: If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning on the grill.

  2. Make the Marinade: In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.

  3. Marinate the Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine the bell peppers, zucchini, red onion, mushrooms, and cherry tomatoes. Pour the marinade over the vegetables and toss to coat. Let them marinate for at least 15 minutes.

  4. Assemble the Skewers: Thread the marinated vegetables onto the skewers, alternating between different types for a colorful presentation.

  5. Grill the Skewers: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.

  6. Serve: Remove from the grill and serve warm. These skewers are perfect as a side dish or a main course for your picnic.

Grilled Vegetable Salad

Ingredients

  • 1 eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, or your choice)
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Grill: Heat your grill to medium-high.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: Brush the eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and asparagus with olive oil. Season with salt, pepper, and dried oregano.

  3. Grill the Vegetables: Place the vegetables on the grill and cook for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they are tender and have grill marks.

  4. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

  5. Assemble the Salad: In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens and grilled vegetables. Drizzle with the dressing and toss gently to combine. Top with crumbled feta cheese if desired.
