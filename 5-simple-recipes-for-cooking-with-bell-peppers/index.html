5 Simple Recipes for Cooking with Bell Peppers


Bell peppers are a vibrant and versatile ingredient that can add color, flavor, and nutrition to a wide array of dishes. Whether you prefer them raw, roasted, stir-fried, or stuffed, bell peppers can elevate your meals and make them more enjoyable. In this blog post, we will explore five simple yet delicious recipes that highlight the unique taste and texture of bell peppers. Each recipe is easy to follow and perfect for families, busy weeknights, or impressing your guests at a dinner party. Let’s dive into the world of bell peppers!

1. Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup cooked rice (white, brown, or quinoa)
  • 1 pound ground beef or turkey (or a plant-based substitute)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Set aside.
  3. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the ground meat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the cooked rice, black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir until well combined and heated through.
  5. Spoon the mixture into the hollowed-out bell peppers and place them in a baking dish. Top each filled pepper with shredded cheese.
  6. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  7. Remove from the oven and let cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Tips

  • Feel free to customize the filling by adding your favorite vegetables or spices.
  • These stuffed peppers can be made ahead of time and frozen for later use.

2. Bell Pepper Stir-Fry

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 bell peppers (red, yellow, and green), sliced into strips
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup snap peas or broccoli florets
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving
  • Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions and garlic, and stir-fry for about 2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the bell peppers and snap peas (or broccoli) to the skillet. Stir-fry for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. Drizzle the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and sesame oil over the vegetables. Toss to coat evenly and cook for another minute.
  5. Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds, if desired.

Tips

  • Add protein such as chicken, shrimp, or tofu for a more filling dish.
  • You can also include other vegetables like carrots or mushrooms in your stir-fry.

3. Roasted Bell Pepper Soup

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color), halved and seeded
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the halved bell peppers and chopped onion on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and slightly charred.
  3. Once roasted, transfer the peppers and onions to a large pot. Add the minced garlic, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, basil, and oregano. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer the soup in batches to a blender and blend until smooth.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil if desired.

Tips

  • Serve the soup with crusty bread for a comforting meal.
  • You can add a splash of cream or coconut milk for a creamier texture.

4. Bell Pepper Fajitas

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound chicken breast, sliced (or use beef or a vegetarian alternative)
  • 3 bell peppers (red, yellow, and green), sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Flour or corn tortillas for serving
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, guacamole, salsa, shredded cheese

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced chicken (or beef/vegetarian alternative) to the skillet. Season with chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook until the meat is cooked through and browned, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the sliced bell peppers and onion to the skillet. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Warm the tortillas in a separate skillet or microwave.
  5. Serve the fajita mixture on warm tortillas, topped with your favorite toppings.

Tips

  • Marinate the meat in lime juice and spices for at least 30 minutes before cooking for extra flavor.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables like zucchini or mushrooms for variety.

5. Bell Pepper Salad

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed greens (spinach, arugula, or romaine)
  • 1 large bell pepper (any color), diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled (optional)
  • 1/4 cup olives (black or green)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, diced bell pepper, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and red onion.
  2. If using, sprinkle the salad with feta cheese and olives.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Serve immediately or refrigerate for up to an hour before serving for the flavors to meld.

Tips

  • Add grilled chicken or chickpeas for extra protein.
  • Experiment with different dressings to match your taste preference.

Conclusion

Bell peppers are not only delicious but also packed with essential nutrients and vitamins. These five simple recipes showcase the versatility of bell peppers and can easily be adapted to suit your tastes. From hearty stuffed peppers to refreshing salads, there’s something for everyone. So next time you’re at the grocery store, grab a few bell peppers and try out these recipes to bring some vibrant flavors and colors to your table. Happy cooking!
