Tiramisu Gelato: A Coffee-Infused Delight


Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert loved by many for its rich flavors and indulgent texture. But have you ever thought about turning this delectable treat into a refreshing gelato? Well, today I’m going to share with you a recipe for Tiramisu Gelato that will take your taste buds on a delightful journey.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of instant coffee powder
  • 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup of mascarpone cheese
  • 1/4 cup of Kahlua or coffee liqueur
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Ladyfingers, crushed (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, heat the milk and heavy cream over medium heat until it reaches a gentle simmer. Remove from heat and set aside.

  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until creamy and pale yellow in color.

  3. Gradually pour the warmed milk and cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent curdling.

  4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Do not let it boil.

  5. In a small bowl, dissolve the instant coffee powder in 2 tablespoons of hot water. Add the coffee mixture, cocoa powder, mascarpone cheese, Kahlua (or coffee liqueur), and vanilla extract to the saucepan. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  6. Remove the mixture from heat and let it cool to room temperature. Once cooled, cover the mixture with plastic wrap, ensuring the plastic wrap touches the surface of the mixture to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Once the mixture is chilled, transfer it to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions until it reaches a soft, creamy consistency.

  8. Transfer the gelato to a lidded container, cover, and freeze for at least 2 hours or until firm.

  9. Serve the Tiramisu Gelato in bowls or cones, garnished with crushed ladyfingers for an extra touch of texture and flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of coffee flavor, you can add a shot of espresso to the mixture before churning it in the ice cream maker.
  • If you prefer a more pronounced cocoa flavor, increase the amount of cocoa powder to your liking.
  • Feel free to experiment with different liqueurs to add a unique twist to your Tiramisu Gelato. Amaretto, hazelnut liqueur, or even rum can all be delicious alternatives to Kahlua.
  • For a vegan-friendly version of this gelato, substitute the milk and cream with plant-based alternatives such as almond milk and coconut cream. Use a vegan-friendly egg substitute or omit the eggs altogether.
  • If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can still make this gelato by placing the mixture in a shallow dish in the freezer. Every 30 minutes, stir the mixture vigorously to break up any ice crystals until it reaches the desired consistency.

Final Thoughts

Tiramisu Gelato is the perfect dessert for coffee lovers who want to indulge in a cool and creamy treat. With its combination of coffee, cocoa, and mascarpone cheese, this gelato captures the essence of the beloved Tiramisu in a refreshing form. Whether you enjoy it on a hot summer day or as a delightful after-dinner treat, this Coffee-Infused Delight is sure to satisfy your cravings. So, grab your ingredients and get ready to embark on a delicious journey with Tiramisu Gelato!
