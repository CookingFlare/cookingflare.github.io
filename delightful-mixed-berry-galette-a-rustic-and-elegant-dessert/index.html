Delightful Mixed Berry Galette: A Rustic and Elegant Dessert


As the summer sun begins to wane, the air is filled with the sweet aroma of ripening berries. There’s something special about biting into a juicy, plump berry that’s been picked at the peak of freshness. This delightful mixed berry galette is a celebration of that sweet summer magic, combining the flavors and textures of multiple berries in a rustic, elegant dessert that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Confectioners’ sugar (for dusting)

Instructions

Begin by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the mixed berries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Let the mixture sit for about 15 minutes, allowing the berries to release their juices and the cornstarch to absorb excess moisture.

In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a shaggy mass.

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it a few times until it becomes smooth and pliable. Form the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom, gently pressing it into the corners and up the sides of the pan. Trim any excess dough from the edges.

Pour the berry mixture into the center of the dough, leaving a 1 1/2-inch border around the edges. Fold the edges of the dough up over the berries, forming a rustic galette shape. Brush the edges of the dough with the beaten egg for a golden glaze.

Bake the galette for 40-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the berries are tender and bubbly. Remove the galette from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Dust with confectioners’ sugar and serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more intense berry flavor, use a combination of sweet and tart berries, such as strawberries and raspberries.
  • Add a sprinkle of sugar to the berry mixture before baking for an extra burst of sweetness.
  • Substitute other types of berries, such as peaches or apricots, for a unique flavor combination.
  • Serve the galette with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or whipped cream for a decadent dessert.

This delightful mixed berry galette is a perfect dessert for summer gatherings, potlucks, or family dinners. Its rustic elegance and sweet-tart flavors are sure to impress even the most discerning palates. So go ahead, indulge in the sweet magic of summer berries, and treat your loved ones to a truly delightful dessert.
