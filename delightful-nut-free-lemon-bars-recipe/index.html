Delightful Nut-Free Lemon Bars Recipe


Lemon bars are a classic dessert that balances the tartness of lemons with a sweet, buttery crust. Perfect for any occasion, these delightful treats cater to those with nut allergies, ensuring that everyone can enjoy their zesty goodness. In this blog post, we will guide you through an easy-to-follow recipe to create nut-free lemon bars that are sure to impress family and friends alike.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (about 2-3 lemons)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest (about 1 lemon)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Kitchen Tools Needed

  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • 8x8 inch baking dish
  • Parchment paper (optional)
  • Zester or grater
  • Sifter (for powdered sugar)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat the Oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures the crust bakes evenly and achieves a golden-brown color.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a medium mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/4 cup of granulated sugar, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Whisk these ingredients together until well blended.

  3. Add the Butter: Next, add 1/2 cup of softened unsalted butter to the dry mixture. Using a fork or pastry cutter, blend the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs. This step is crucial for creating a tender crust.

  4. Form the Crust: Press the crumb mixture evenly into the bottom of an 8x8 inch baking dish. If you prefer, line the dish with parchment paper for easier removal later. Make sure the crust is packed down firmly to prevent it from crumbling during baking.

  5. Bake the Crust: Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. Keep an eye on it to prevent over-baking.

Making the Lemon Filling

  1. Whisk the Eggs: In a separate mixing bowl, crack 2 large eggs and whisk them until they are well beaten. This will help create a light and fluffy filling.

  2. Add Sugar and Lemon: Gradually add 1 cup of granulated sugar to the beaten eggs, whisking continuously. Then, add 1/4 cup of fresh lemon juice and 1 tablespoon of lemon zest. The zest will enhance the lemon flavor, making your bars even more delightful.

  3. Incorporate Flour: Finally, add 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour to the lemon mixture, whisking until it is fully incorporated and smooth. This flour helps to set the filling as it bakes.

Baking the Lemon Bars

  1. Combine the Mixtures: Once the crust has finished baking, remove it from the oven and pour the lemon filling over the hot crust. Spread it evenly to ensure every bite is filled with lemony goodness.

  2. Bake Again: Return the baking dish to the oven and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are lightly browned. The center may still have a slight jiggle, but it will firm up as it cools.

  3. Cool Down: Once baked, remove the lemon bars from the oven and allow them to cool in the pan at room temperature. For the best results, refrigerate them for at least 2 hours before slicing. This helps the bars to set completely and makes cutting easier.

Serving Your Lemon Bars

  1. Slice and Dust: Once chilled, use a sharp knife to cut the lemon bars into squares or rectangles. Dust the tops with powdered sugar using a sifter for a beautiful presentation.

  2. Garnish (Optional): For an added touch, you can garnish each bar with a small slice of lemon or a sprig of fresh mint. This not only enhances the look but also adds an aromatic experience.

  3. Enjoy: Serve these delightful nut-free lemon bars as a refreshing dessert at your next gathering, or enjoy them as a sweet treat with your afternoon tea or coffee.

Storage Tips

  • Room Temperature: If you plan to consume the lemon bars within a day or two, they can be stored at room temperature in an airtight container.
  • Refrigerate: For longer storage, keep the lemon bars in the refrigerator, where they will last for about a week.
  • Freezing: You can also freeze the lemon bars for up to three months. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and then place them in an airtight container. Thaw in the fridge before serving.

Conclusion

These nut-free lemon bars are a delightful addition to any dessert table, offering a zesty burst of flavor that everyone can enjoy. With their buttery crust and tangy lemon filling, they strike the perfect balance between sweet and tart. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply indulging in a personal treat, this recipe is sure to please.

So gather your ingredients and whip up a batch of these delightful nut-free lemon bars today. Your taste buds will thank you!
