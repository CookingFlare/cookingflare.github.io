Easter Recipes for a Crowd: Feeding Family and Friends


Easter is one of those special holidays that brings families and friends together around the table. Whether it’s to celebrate with a grand feast or to enjoy more casual gatherings, Easter meals are often elaborate, comforting, and full of tradition. But when you’re feeding a crowd, the challenge becomes ensuring that the food is plentiful, delicious, and offers something for everyone. From savory dishes to sweet treats, there are countless ways to make your Easter gathering a memorable culinary experience. Below are some fantastic Easter recipes for a crowd that will keep everyone satisfied, from appetizers to desserts.

1. Classic Easter Ham

A traditional Easter ham is the centerpiece of many Easter tables, and it’s a dish that can easily be made in large quantities to feed a crowd. A good ham is not only flavorful but also versatile when it comes to the choice of glaze and accompanying sides. Here’s a simple but elegant recipe that will please guests of all ages.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in ham (about 10-12 pounds)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C).
  2. Score the ham in a diamond pattern and rub it with a little salt and pepper.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, honey, cinnamon, cloves, and ginger. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves and the glaze thickens slightly.
  4. Place the ham on a roasting rack in a large pan, then brush half of the glaze over the ham.
  5. Roast the ham for 2 to 2.5 hours, basting every 30 minutes with the remaining glaze.
  6. Once the ham reaches an internal temperature of 140°F (60°C), remove it from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing.

This ham is delicious served with mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a light spring salad. The sweet and tangy glaze perfectly complements the rich, savory flavor of the ham.

2. Scalloped Potatoes for a Crowd

No Easter meal is complete without a hearty potato dish. Scalloped potatoes are a perfect choice, as they’re creamy, cheesy, and comforting. This recipe can easily be scaled up to serve a large crowd, and it’s a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds Yukon gold potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (177°C). Butter a large 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, butter, garlic, salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the butter is melted and the mixture is heated through.
  3. Arrange a layer of sliced potatoes in the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Pour a portion of the cream mixture over the potatoes, then sprinkle with some cheddar cheese.
  4. Repeat this layering process until all potatoes, cream, and cheese are used, finishing with a layer of cheese on top.
  5. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 1 hour. After 1 hour, remove the foil and continue baking for another 30 minutes or until the potatoes are tender and the top is golden and bubbly.
  6. Let the potatoes rest for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Scalloped potatoes are a perfect complement to the Easter ham and are ideal for serving a large group. The creamy texture and cheesy flavor are sure to have everyone coming back for seconds.

3. Spring Vegetable Medley

As Easter falls in the heart of spring, showcasing fresh vegetables in your meal is a great way to celebrate the season. A vibrant vegetable medley is simple to make and can be easily prepared in large batches. You can use any combination of vegetables you prefer, but the following recipe is a wonderful option for a crowd.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups baby carrots, peeled
  • 2 cups asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 cups green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the carrots, asparagus, and green beans with olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the vegetables out in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
  4. Remove from the oven and drizzle with lemon juice. Sprinkle with fresh thyme and serve.

This colorful vegetable medley is a great side dish to add brightness and freshness to your Easter meal. It’s quick to prepare and packed with flavor, making it perfect for large gatherings.

4. Deviled Eggs with a Twist

Deviled eggs are a quintessential Easter appetizer, and they’re always a hit when serving a crowd. While traditional deviled eggs are fantastic on their own, giving them a creative twist adds an extra layer of flavor that guests will love.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 cup chopped chives
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Hard boil the eggs by placing them in a saucepan and covering with water. Bring the water to a boil, then cover and reduce heat. Let the eggs sit for 10-12 minutes, then transfer them to an ice bath to cool.
  2. Once cooled, peel the eggs and slice them in half lengthwise. Remove the yolks and place them in a bowl.
  3. Mash the yolks and mix with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, vinegar, paprika, cayenne pepper (if using), and salt and pepper.
  4. Spoon or pipe the yolk mixture back into the egg whites. Sprinkle with smoked paprika, chopped chives, and a little extra cayenne pepper if desired.
  5. Arrange the deviled eggs on a platter and serve chilled.

These deviled eggs with a twist are an excellent appetizer to kick off your Easter meal, and their zesty flavor will have everyone reaching for more.

5. Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cake is an Easter classic that is both delicious and fitting for the occasion. With its rich flavor, moist crumb, and creamy frosting, it’s the perfect dessert to end your Easter meal. This recipe is large enough to serve a crowd, and it’s sure to be the star of the dessert table.

Ingredients:

For the Cake:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 cups finely grated carrots
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

For the Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs, sugar, oil, and vanilla extract together until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Stir in the grated carrots and chopped walnuts, if using.
  6. Pour the batter evenly into the prepared cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cakes to cool completely in the pans before transferring to a wire rack.
  8. For the frosting, beat the cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt, and beat until light and fluffy.
  9. Once the cakes are cool, frost them with the cream cheese frosting. Garnish with additional chopped walnuts or shredded coconut if desired.

Carrot cake is an iconic Easter dessert, and this recipe, with its decadent cream cheese frosting, will satisfy the sweet tooth of every guest at your gathering.

Conclusion

Easter meals are a wonderful opportunity to gather friends and family around the table and create lasting memories. With the right combination of savory and sweet dishes, you can easily prepare a festive spread that will feed a crowd while showcasing the flavors of the season. From a succulent Easter ham to decadent carrot cake, these recipes will ensure that your Easter celebration is both delicious and memorable. Enjoy the process of cooking and sharing, and make sure to savor every bite with the loved ones who make this holiday so special.
