How to Make an Authentic New York-Style Pizza


New York-style pizza, with its large, thin, and foldable slices, is iconic. Known for its crispy yet chewy crust and deliciously tangy tomato sauce, it has become a favorite among pizza lovers worldwide. Making this type of pizza at home can seem daunting, but with the right techniques and ingredients, it’s absolutely achievable. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to make an authentic New York-style pizza right in your kitchen.

Understanding New York-Style Pizza

New York-style pizza has a few defining features. It is traditionally made with a thin, crispy crust that’s slightly chewy on the inside. The crust is typically hand-tossed to achieve its signature texture, and the pizza is baked in a high-temperature gas or coal oven, which imparts a crispy texture without burning the toppings. The pie is usually cut into large slices, often folded in half for easy eating on the go.

While many modern variations exist, true New York-style pizza remains faithful to a few key components: a perfectly thin crust, a simple yet flavorful tomato sauce, and mozzarella cheese. The toppings are where you can get creative, but the base must be just right to capture the essence of this classic style.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make an authentic New York-style pizza, you’ll need quality ingredients that capture the flavor and texture of the pizza you’d get in the city. Here’s a breakdown of what you’ll need for the dough, sauce, and toppings.

For the Dough

  • 500 grams (4 cups) all-purpose flour: High-gluten flour is ideal for New York-style pizza dough because it helps achieve that chewy, airy texture, but all-purpose flour works just fine if that’s what you have.
  • 325 ml (1 1/3 cups) warm water: The warm water will activate the yeast and help the dough rise.
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast: This is the leavening agent that will help your dough rise and become light and airy.
  • 1 teaspoon sugar: Sugar feeds the yeast and helps the dough rise faster.
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil: Olive oil adds richness to the dough and helps it become slightly crispy when baked.
  • 2 teaspoons salt: Salt enhances the flavor of the dough and helps balance the yeast’s taste.

For the Tomato Sauce

  • 1 can (28 ounces) whole peeled tomatoes: Choose a good-quality, preferably San Marzano brand, for a rich, authentic flavor.
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil: This helps sauté the garlic and gives the sauce a rich flavor.
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced: Fresh garlic gives the sauce a zesty, aromatic kick.
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano: A classic pizza seasoning that adds depth to the sauce.
  • 1 teaspoon sugar: A small amount of sugar balances the acidity of the tomatoes.
  • Salt and pepper, to taste: Adjust for seasoning.

For the Toppings

  • 400 grams (14 oz) mozzarella cheese: Fresh mozzarella is preferred, as it has a higher moisture content, which gives the pizza its deliciously stretchy texture. Some people mix it with low-moisture mozzarella for a better melt and texture.
  • Fresh basil (optional): If you like, you can top the pizza with fresh basil leaves after baking.
  • Toppings of your choice: From pepperoni to mushrooms to sausage, New York-style pizza can be topped with anything you desire. But remember, less is more; this pizza is about the crust, sauce, and cheese.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making New York-Style Pizza

Step 1: Prepare the Pizza Dough

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, mix the warm water with the sugar and active dry yeast. Stir gently, and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. Make the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a small well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Use a wooden spoon or your hands to mix the ingredients until a dough begins to form.
  3. Knead the dough: Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour; if it’s too dry, add a splash of water.
  4. Let the dough rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, covering it with a damp cloth. Allow it to rise in a warm spot for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size. This rise helps the dough develop flavor and texture.

Step 2: Make the Tomato Sauce

  1. Sauté the garlic: In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  2. Cook the tomatoes: Add the whole peeled tomatoes (with juice) to the saucepan, breaking them up with a spoon or potato masher. Let the mixture simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Season the sauce: Add the dried oregano, sugar, salt, and pepper to taste. Stir everything together, and let the sauce simmer for another 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool before spreading it on the dough.

Step 3: Shape the Dough

  1. Preheat the oven: Place a pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven and preheat to 500°F (260°C). A pizza stone will give you the best results for a crispy crust, but if you don’t have one, a baking sheet will work just fine.
  2. Divide the dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two equal portions if you want to make two smaller pizzas or keep it whole for one large pizza.
  3. Stretch the dough: On a lightly floured surface, stretch the dough by hand into a large, thin circle. The goal is to get the dough as thin as possible without tearing it, while still leaving a slightly thicker border around the edges for the crust.

Step 4: Assemble the Pizza

  1. Prepare the crust: If you’re using a pizza stone, sprinkle a little cornmeal or flour on your pizza peel or the back of a baking sheet to prevent the dough from sticking. Transfer the stretched dough onto the pizza peel or baking sheet.
  2. Add the sauce: Spread a thin layer of the cooled tomato sauce onto the dough, leaving about an inch around the edges for the crust.
  3. Top with cheese: Tear the fresh mozzarella into pieces and scatter it evenly over the sauce. Don’t overload the pizza with cheese—New York-style pizza is known for its balance between crust, sauce, and cheese.
  4. Add toppings: If you want additional toppings, now is the time to add them. Keep it simple with a few slices of pepperoni or add mushrooms, bell peppers, or olives. Remember, the crust is the star, so don’t overdo it on the toppings.

Step 5: Bake the Pizza

  1. Bake the pizza: Carefully slide the pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or place the baking sheet in the oven. Bake for 10-15 minutes or until the crust is golden and crispy, and the cheese is melted and bubbly. Keep an eye on it to ensure the crust doesn’t burn.
  2. Finish with fresh basil (optional): If you like, sprinkle fresh basil leaves over the pizza after it’s baked for a burst of color and flavor.

Step 6: Slice and Serve

  1. Slice the pizza: Once the pizza is baked to perfection, remove it from the oven. Let it rest for a couple of minutes to firm up. Then, use a pizza cutter or a sharp knife to slice it into large, triangular slices.
  2. Serve and enjoy: Serve your homemade New York-style pizza hot and fresh, ideally with a cold drink. You’ll get that perfect foldable slice just like you would from a pizzeria in the heart of New York.

Tips for Perfect New York-Style Pizza

  • Use a pizza stone: A pizza stone simulates the high-heat environment of a professional pizza oven, ensuring a crispy crust. If you don’t have one, a baking sheet will work in a pinch.
  • High oven temperature: New York-style pizza needs to bake at a high temperature (around 500°F/260°C). This high heat ensures the pizza cooks quickly and the crust becomes crispy without overcooking the toppings.
  • Don’t overload the sauce: One of the keys to a true New York-style pizza is a thin layer of sauce. Too much sauce can make the pizza soggy, so less is more.
  • Rest the dough: Letting your dough rise for a good 1-2 hours allows the yeast to fully develop, which gives the crust that perfect texture. Don’t rush this step!
  • Preheat the oven: Always preheat your oven to the highest setting. A hot oven is crucial for achieving that perfect crispy yet tender crust.

Conclusion

Making an authentic New York-style pizza at home requires a bit of practice, but once you nail the dough, sauce, and baking techniques, you’ll be enjoying a slice that rivals the best pizzerias in the city. The key is in the details—from using the right flour for the dough to baking at the highest temperature possible. With this guide, you can bring a taste of New York right to your kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a pizza night or just craving a classic slice, your homemade New York-style pizza will surely impress.

So, fire up that oven, roll out the dough, and enjoy the delicious, iconic flavors of New York pizza at home.
