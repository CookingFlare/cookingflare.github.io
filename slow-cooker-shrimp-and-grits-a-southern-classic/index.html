Slow Cooker Shrimp and Grits: A Southern Classic


When it comes to Southern comfort food, few dishes can compete with the rich, savory, and utterly satisfying taste of shrimp and grits. This iconic dish, with its roots deeply embedded in the culinary traditions of the South, combines the sweetness of shrimp with the creamy texture of grits, creating a harmonious blend that speaks to the soul. In this post, we’ll explore the art of making Slow Cooker Shrimp and Grits, ensuring each ingredient shines while allowing your slow cooker to do the heavy lifting.

The Essence of Shrimp and Grits

Shrimp and grits are more than just a meal; they represent a rich cultural tapestry woven through generations. Originating from the Lowcountry region of the American South, particularly South Carolina and Georgia, this dish was initially a breakfast for fishermen. Today, it’s enjoyed at any meal and has evolved into a gourmet standard, often featuring in fine dining establishments.

What Makes This Recipe Special?

The beauty of slow cooking shrimp and grits lies in the convenience and depth of flavor it brings. By letting the ingredients meld over several hours, the dish becomes infused with a savory richness that’s hard to beat. This method also ensures the shrimp remain tender and juicy, while the grits take on a luxurious creaminess.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Grits

  • 1 cup stone-ground grits: Opt for high-quality stone-ground grits for the best texture.
  • 4 cups chicken broth: This adds depth of flavor compared to water.
  • 1 cup heavy cream: For that indulgent creaminess.
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese: Grated for a tangy kick.
  • Salt and pepper to taste: Essential for seasoning.

For the Shrimp

  • 1 pound large shrimp: Peeled and deveined. Fresh shrimp is ideal, but frozen works well too—just thaw before cooking.
  • 4 slices bacon: Diced. Bacon adds a smoky flavor that complements the shrimp beautifully.
  • 1 medium onion: Diced, for sweetness.
  • 3 cloves garlic: Minced, to enhance the savoriness.
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green): Diced, for color and crunch.
  • 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning: For that signature Southern spice—feel free to adjust to your heat preference.
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce: Adds depth and umami.
  • Juice of half a lemon: Brightens the dish.
  • Fresh parsley for garnish: Adds a pop of color and freshness.

Preparing Your Slow Cooker Shrimp and Grits

Step 1: Set the Foundation with Grits

  1. Combine Grits and Broth: In the slow cooker, add the stone-ground grits and chicken broth. Stir well to combine.

  2. Add Cream and Cheese: Pour in the heavy cream and add the grated cheddar cheese. Stir until everything is well mixed.

  3. Season: Add salt and pepper according to your taste. Remember, the cheese and chicken broth will already contribute some saltiness, so taste as you go.

Step 2: Prepare the Shrimp Mixture

  1. Cook the Bacon: In a skillet over medium heat, cook the diced bacon until crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pan.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same skillet, add the diced onion and bell pepper to the bacon fat. Sauté until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute, stirring frequently to avoid burning.

  3. Season the Shrimp: In a bowl, toss the shrimp with Cajun seasoning, Worcestershire sauce, lemon juice, and the cooked bacon. Let this mixture marinate briefly while you prepare the slow cooker.

Step 3: Combine and Cook

  1. Add Shrimp to Slow Cooker: Carefully layer the shrimp mixture over the grits in the slow cooker.

  2. Cook on Low: Cover and cook on low for 4-6 hours. The low temperature allows the flavors to meld beautifully, while the shrimp will be perfectly tender.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

  1. Final Stir and Serve: Once the time is up, give everything a gentle stir to combine. The shrimp will have released their juices into the dish, enhancing the flavor of the grits.

  2. Garnish: Serve the shrimp and grits hot, garnished with fresh parsley for a touch of color and freshness.

Tips for Perfecting Your Dish

  • Quality Ingredients: The success of this dish lies in the quality of ingredients. Fresh shrimp and stone-ground grits make a noticeable difference.
  • Adjust the Spice: For a milder version, reduce the Cajun seasoning or choose a blend that suits your palate.
  • Make Ahead: This dish is perfect for meal prep. You can prepare the shrimp mixture a day in advance and combine everything in the slow cooker the next day.

Pairing Suggestions

To complement your Slow Cooker Shrimp and Grits, consider serving it with:

  • A Side Salad: A simple arugula salad with lemon vinaigrette balances the richness of the dish.
  • Cornbread: A Southern staple that adds a delightful sweetness.
  • Fried Green Tomatoes: For an extra crunch and flavor contrast.

Conclusion: A Taste of the South

Slow Cooker Shrimp and Grits encapsulates the heart of Southern cooking—unpretentious yet deeply flavorful and satisfying. It’s a dish that tells a story, of tradition and innovation, of family gatherings and Sunday dinners. Whether you’re enjoying it as a comforting meal on a chilly evening or serving it at a festive gathering, this recipe is sure to impress.

So, gather your ingredients, set your slow cooker, and prepare to indulge in a true Southern classic that will warm your heart and please your palate. Enjoy every bite of this hearty dish, and let the flavors transport you to the sun-kissed shores of the South, where every meal is a celebration of life and love.
