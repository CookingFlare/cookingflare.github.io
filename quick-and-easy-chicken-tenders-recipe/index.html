Quick and Easy Chicken Tenders Recipe


Chicken tenders are a beloved comfort food that combines crispy breading with tender, juicy chicken, making them an irresistible treat for people of all ages. Whether you’re preparing a quick weeknight dinner or serving up a crowd-pleasing snack, chicken tenders are always a great choice. This recipe provides a simple and delicious way to make your own homemade chicken tenders in no time at all. Get ready to enjoy golden, crispy chicken tenders that are sure to satisfy your hunger.

Ingredients for the Perfect Chicken Tenders

Before diving into the cooking process, gather all the ingredients you’ll need for your homemade chicken tenders. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Chicken Breasts or Chicken Tenderloins: The base of the recipe, either boneless skinless chicken breasts cut into strips or pre-cut chicken tenderloins.
  • All-Purpose Flour: For dredging the chicken, providing a light and crispy coating.
  • Breadcrumbs: A mix of panko breadcrumbs or regular breadcrumbs. Panko gives an extra crunchy texture.
  • Eggs: Used for dipping the chicken into to help the breadcrumbs stick.
  • Milk: A small amount of milk mixed with the eggs to create a smooth dipping mixture.
  • Salt and Pepper: To season the chicken before and after cooking.
  • Garlic Powder: Adds a delicious savory flavor to the coating.
  • Onion Powder: Enhances the flavor of the breading.
  • Paprika: A dash of paprika adds a mild smokiness and color to the chicken.
  • Cayenne Pepper (optional): For a little heat. If you prefer spicier tenders, you can adjust the amount of cayenne pepper to taste.
  • Vegetable Oil: For frying, as it helps achieve that perfect crispy exterior.

These simple ingredients are easy to find in your kitchen or at your local grocery store. With these basics, you’ll be on your way to making the crispiest, juiciest chicken tenders ever!

Preparation Steps for the Chicken Tenders

1. Prep the Chicken

Start by preparing the chicken. If you’re using chicken breasts, slice them into even strips about 1 to 1.5 inches wide. This helps ensure that each tender cooks evenly. If you’re using chicken tenderloins, they are already cut to the perfect size, so you can skip this step.

Once your chicken is cut into strips or if you’re using tenderloins, season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper on both sides. This initial seasoning enhances the overall flavor and ensures that the tenders aren’t bland.

2. Set Up a Breading Station

Next, set up your breading station. You’ll need three shallow bowls or plates:

  • In the first bowl, place the all-purpose flour. You can season this flour with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  • In the second bowl, beat the eggs and add a couple of tablespoons of milk. Whisk them together until smooth.
  • In the third bowl, mix your breadcrumbs with the garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using). This seasoned breadcrumb mixture will give the tenders their flavor-packed, crispy coating.

3. Dredge the Chicken

Now it’s time to coat the chicken tenders. Start by dipping each chicken strip into the flour, ensuring it’s coated evenly. Shake off any excess flour. Then, dip the floured chicken into the egg mixture, making sure it’s completely covered in the liquid. Finally, coat the chicken in the breadcrumb mixture, pressing lightly to make sure the breadcrumbs stick well.

Repeat this process for all the chicken tenders, and set them aside on a plate. You can also refrigerate the breaded tenders for 10-15 minutes before cooking to help the coating set and stay on better while frying.

4. Heat the Oil

To cook the chicken tenders, you’ll need to heat a generous amount of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. The oil should be about 1/2 inch deep in the pan to allow the tenders to fry properly. To test if the oil is hot enough, drop a small breadcrumb into the pan. If it sizzles right away, the oil is ready for frying.

5. Fry the Chicken Tenders

Carefully place the breaded chicken tenders into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. You want to give each tender enough space to fry evenly. Let the chicken cook for 3-4 minutes on each side or until golden brown and crispy. You’ll want the chicken to reach an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) for safe consumption.

Once the tenders are cooked through, use tongs or a slotted spoon to remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

6. Optional: Keep the Tenders Warm

If you’re cooking a large batch of tenders, you can keep them warm in a preheated oven at 200°F (93°C) while you finish frying the rest of the batch. This way, every tender will stay crispy and hot when it’s time to serve.

Serving the Chicken Tenders

Now that your chicken tenders are perfectly crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, it’s time to serve them! Here are a few ideas for serving:

  • Dipping Sauces: Chicken tenders are fantastic when paired with a variety of dipping sauces. Try classic options like ketchup, honey mustard, barbecue sauce, ranch dressing, or even buffalo sauce for an extra kick.
  • Side Dishes: Serve your chicken tenders alongside some classic sides like French fries, mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or a simple salad. You can also serve them with roasted vegetables or even a fresh fruit salad for a healthier twist.
  • Sandwiches: If you’re in the mood for a chicken tender sandwich, place a few tenders between a soft bun with lettuce, tomato, and your favorite condiments. Add a slice of cheese if you like, and you’ve got a delicious meal on the go.

Chicken tenders are versatile and pair well with almost anything, so get creative and make it your own!

Tips for the Best Chicken Tenders

  • Use Fresh Chicken: For the best texture and flavor, opt for fresh chicken tenders or chicken breasts. Frozen tenders can be convenient, but fresh chicken provides a superior taste and texture.
  • Don’t Skip the Resting Time: After breading the chicken, give it a few minutes to rest before frying. This helps the breading adhere better and reduces the chance of it falling off during cooking.
  • Adjust the Spice Level: If you prefer your chicken tenders with a little heat, increase the amount of cayenne pepper or add hot sauce to the egg mixture for an extra zing.
  • Double Dredging: For an extra crunchy coating, you can double dredge the chicken tenders. After the first coating of flour, egg, and breadcrumbs, dip the tenders back into the egg and breadcrumbs for a thicker crust.
  • Air Fryer Option: If you’re looking for a healthier version of chicken tenders, you can cook them in an air fryer instead of frying them in oil. Preheat the air fryer to 400°F (200°C) and cook the tenders for 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway through.

Storing Leftover Chicken Tenders

If you happen to have any leftover chicken tenders (which is rare), you can store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat them, place the tenders on a baking sheet in a preheated 375°F (190°C) oven for about 10-12 minutes, or until they are heated through and crispy once again.

You can also freeze leftover tenders for longer storage. Simply place them on a baking sheet in a single layer and freeze them for 1-2 hours. Once frozen, transfer them to a zip-top bag or airtight container. To reheat, bake the frozen tenders in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 20 minutes.

Conclusion

This quick and easy chicken tenders recipe is a must-try for anyone looking for a satisfying and flavorful meal that can be made in a flash. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner, hosting a party, or simply craving a crispy snack, homemade chicken tenders never disappoint. With a few simple ingredients and some easy-to-follow steps, you can have perfectly cooked chicken tenders in less than 30 minutes.

So, next time you’re in the mood for comfort food, give this recipe a try. Serve them with your favorite sides and dipping sauces, and enjoy a meal that will have everyone coming back for seconds!
