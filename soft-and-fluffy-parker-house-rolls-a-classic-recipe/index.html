Soft and Fluffy Parker House Rolls: A Classic Recipe


There’s something truly special about biting into a warm, soft, and fluffy Parker House roll. These rolls have a rich history and have been beloved by many for generations. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to recreate this classic bread at home, filling your kitchen with the enticing aroma of freshly baked rolls.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 ¼ cups warm milk (110°F/43°C)
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Additional melted butter for brushing

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes, or until the mixture becomes frothy.

  2. Add the melted butter and salt to the yeast mixture, and stir well to combine.

  3. Gradually add the flour, one cup at a time, while continuously stirring. Continue until the dough becomes soft and slightly sticky.

  4. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm area for approximately 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

  6. Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer it back to the floured surface and divide it into 16 equal portions.

  7. Roll each portion of dough into a ball, and then flatten it slightly with your hands. Fold the flattened dough in half, creating a crescent shape, and place it on a greased baking sheet. Repeat this process with the remaining portions of dough.

  8. Cover the baking sheet with the rolls and let them rise for an additional 30-45 minutes, or until they have puffed up nicely.

  9. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  10. Bake the rolls for 15-18 minutes, or until they turn golden brown on the top.

  11. Remove the rolls from the oven and immediately brush them with melted butter for a beautiful glossy finish.

  12. Allow the rolls to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy them warm, with a pat of butter or your favorite spread.

Tips and Variations

  • For a richer flavor, you can substitute half of the all-purpose flour with bread flour.
  • Add a sprinkle of sesame seeds or poppy seeds on top of the rolls before baking for an extra touch of flavor and visual appeal.
  • Experiment with different herbs and seasonings to create unique flavor profiles. Dried rosemary, garlic powder, or even a touch of cinnamon can elevate these rolls to new heights.
  • These Parker House rolls freeze well. Simply let them cool completely, then store them in an airtight container or freezer bag. They can be reheated in the oven or toaster for a quick and delicious treat.

Now that you have the recipe for these soft and fluffy Parker House rolls, it’s time to gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get baking! The process may take a little time and effort, but the end result will be well worth it. Enjoy the satisfaction of creating a timeless classic in your own kitchen, and share the joy of these delectable rolls with your loved ones. Happy baking!
