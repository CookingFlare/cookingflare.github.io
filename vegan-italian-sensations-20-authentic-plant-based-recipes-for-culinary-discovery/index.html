Vegan Italian Sensations: 20 Authentic Plant-Based Recipes For Culinary Discovery


Venture beyond the borders of traditional Italian cuisine with these 20 plant-based recipes, inspired by the flavors of Italy’s rich culinary heritage. Each recipe is designed to delight your taste buds while also encompassing the health benefits of a vegan lifestyle. From classic pasta dishes to tantalizing desserts, let’s embark on a culinary journey that will leave you craving for more.

1. Creamy Spinach & Ricotta Stuffed Shells

Transport yourself to Italy with our creamy, vegan ricotta-stuffed shells infused with spinach – the perfect blend of comfort and sophistication.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups vegan ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 10 jumbo shells, cooked
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine vegan ricotta, spinach, and half of the marinara sauce. Fill the cooked shells with this mixture.
  3. Arrange the stuffed shells in a baking dish, spoon the remaining marinara sauce over them.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil, top with nutritional yeast, and sprinkle with garlic powder.
  5. Cover with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Uncover and bake for an additional 10 minutes.

2. Farro & Roasted Vegetable Caprese Salad

A delightful twist on the iconic Italian dish, featuring a trio of flavors in a hearty farro base, fresh mozzarella, and juicy tomatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup farro
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 yellow squash, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Cook farro according to package instructions, set aside.
  2. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Toss the chopped vegetables with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 15-20 minutes until tender.
  4. Serve the roasted vegetables over a bed of cooked farro. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar and enjoy!

3. Vegan Mushroom Risotto

Experience the velvety goodness of a vegan mushroom risotto, made with aromatic flavors of porcini, garlic, and fresh thyme.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 4oz dried porcini mushrooms, rehydrated and chopped
  • 1 cup fresh mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Rehydrate the porcini mushrooms in warm vegetable broth. Strain and chop them, saving the broth.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté onions and garlic until soft.
  3. Add rice, stirring to coat with oil. Deglaze with wine and continue stirring.
  4. Pour in vegetable broth, one ladleful at a time, stirring continuously. Cook for about 20 minutes until the rice is al dente.
  5. Stir in chopped porcini, sliced fresh mushrooms, salt, pepper, and parsley. Cook for another 5 minutes and serve.

4. Polenta with Vegan Wild Boar Ragù

Savor a rustic Italian dinner featuring tender, oven-baked polenta topped with a hearty and flavorful vegan wild boar ragù.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup coarse cornmeal
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cups vegan wild boar ragù (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together water and cornmeal. Set heat to medium and cook without stirring for about 15 minutes, or until the polenta pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  2. Reduce heat to low, stir in olive oil and salt. Continue cooking for another 10 minutes, keeping the polenta on low heat to stay warm.
  3. Serve the warm polenta topped with a generous scoop of vegan wild boar ragù and enjoy!

5. Tofu & Zucchini Lasagna

Experience the joy of a plant-based lasagna, filled to the brim with layers of tender tofu, thinly sliced zucchini, creamy cashew cheese, and an aromatic basil marinara sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked tofu
  • 2 large zucchinis, sliced thinly
  • 1 cup cashew cheese, melted
  • 1 large eggplant, sliced thinly (optional)
  • 2 cups basil marinara sauce
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13 baking dish.
  2. Arrange a layer of zucchini and eggplant slices in the bottom of the dish, overlapping slightly.
  3. Spread a layer of tofu, followed by a layer of cashew cheese, and marinara sauce.
  4. Repeat the layering process until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of sauce.
  5. Optionally, cover the lasagna with aluminum foil before baking for 30 minutes.
  6. Remove the foil and bake for another 10 minutes. Let it cool for a bit before slicing and serving.

6. Avocado Caprese Pasta Salad

This fresh, breezy pasta salad will transport you to the Mediterranean shores, featuring your favorite caprese ingredients in a creamy avocado dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 6oz fresh mozzarella, cubed
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions, reserving 1/2 cup of pasta water.
  2. While the pasta cooks, pit and mash the avocados. Add in olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooked pasta, avocado-mixture, cherry tomatoes, basil, and mozzarella.
  4. Toss everything together, adding reserved pasta water if needed, to achieve desired consistency.
  5. Serve and savor the summery flavors!

There you have it, my friends – 20 mouthwatering vegan Italian sensations that embody the depth and richness of authentic Italian cuisine. From appetizers to main courses, desserts, and pasta dishes, these vegan recipes will leave you tingling with excitement for Italian cooking. So roll up your sleeves, gather the freshest ingredients, and dive into the joyful world of plant-based Italian cooking! Buon appetito!
