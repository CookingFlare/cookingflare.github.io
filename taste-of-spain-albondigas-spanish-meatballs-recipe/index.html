Taste of Spain: Albondigas (Spanish Meatballs) Recipe


If you’re a fan of Spanish cuisine or simply looking to spice up your dinner routine, then this Albondigas (Spanish Meatballs) recipe is a must-try! Albondigas are traditional Spanish meatballs that are bursting with flavor and are perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to treat yourself to a delicious homemade meal, these Albondigas will not disappoint.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, bread crumbs, milk, finely chopped onion, minced garlic, chopped parsley, ground cumin, paprika, dried oregano, salt, black pepper, and beaten egg. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  2. Shape the meat mixture into small meatballs, about 1 inch in diameter. You should be able to make around 20 to 24 meatballs.

  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic becomes fragrant.

  4. Add the red bell pepper to the skillet and cook for about 2 minutes, until it begins to soften.

  5. Push the onion, garlic, and bell pepper to one side of the skillet and add the meatballs to the other side. Cook the meatballs for about 2 minutes on each side, until they are browned and cooked through.

  6. Once the meatballs are cooked, add the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, dried thyme, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir everything together gently, making sure the meatballs are coated in the sauce.

  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the Albondigas simmer for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  8. Serve the Albondigas hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley. They can be enjoyed on their own as an appetizer or served with rice or crusty bread for a complete meal.

Why You’ll Love Albondigas

Albondigas are not your average meatballs. The combination of ground beef and pork gives them a rich and juicy texture, while the blend of spices and herbs adds a burst of flavor that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Spain. These meatballs are versatile and can be enjoyed as a tapas dish, appetizer, or as the main course. The savory tomato-based sauce brings everything together and adds a comforting touch to every bite.

Final Thoughts

If you’re craving a taste of Spain, then give this Albondigas recipe a try. With its simple yet satisfying flavors, it’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re cooking for friends, family, or just for yourself, these Spanish meatballs are a delicious way to elevate your culinary skills and bring a touch of Spain to your dinner table. So, go ahead and indulge in the flavors of Spain with Albondigas - your taste buds will thank you!
