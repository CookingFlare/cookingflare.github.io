Savory and Allergy-Friendly Stuffed Peppers for a Wholesome Meal


If you’re looking for a wholesome, allergy-friendly meal that’s packed with flavor and nutrition, then you’ve come to the right place. Stuffed peppers are a versatile dish that can be customized to accommodate various dietary needs and preferences. In this recipe, we’ll explore a savory and allergy-friendly version of stuffed peppers that will satisfy your taste buds and nourish your body.

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Peppers:

  • 6 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 ½ cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound ground turkey (or plant-based ground meat for a vegan option)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, canned, or frozen)
  • 1 ½ cups tomato sauce
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (dairy or dairy-free)

For the Topping:

  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Avocado, sliced
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Prepare a baking dish by lightly greasing it with olive oil or cooking spray.

  2. Prepare the Quinoa: In a medium saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

  3. Prepare the Peppers: Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. If the peppers don’t stand upright, slice a thin layer off the bottom to create a flat surface. Place the peppers in the prepared baking dish and set aside.

  4. Cook the Filling: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened. Add the ground turkey (or plant-based ground meat) and cook until browned. Season with cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir in the black beans, corn, cooked quinoa, and 1 cup of the tomato sauce. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Stuff the Peppers: Spoon the filling into the prepared bell peppers, pressing down gently to pack the filling in. Pour the remaining ½ cup of tomato sauce over the stuffed peppers.

  6. Bake and Serve: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender. Remove the foil, sprinkle the shredded cheese on top of each pepper, and return to the oven for an additional 5 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Garnish and Enjoy: Once the stuffed peppers are out of the oven, garnish with chopped cilantro, sliced avocado, and a squeeze of lime juice. Serve hot and enjoy a wholesome, allergy-friendly meal!

Allergy-Friendly Options

This recipe can easily be adapted to accommodate various food allergies and dietary preferences. For a dairy-free or vegan option, simply use plant-based cheese instead of dairy cheese. Ensure that the ground meat used is suitable for your dietary needs, and check for gluten-free labels on the vegetable broth and other packaged ingredients if needed. By making these simple swaps, you can create a delicious and allergy-friendly meal that everyone can enjoy.

Stuffed peppers are not only a delicious and satisfying meal, but they also offer a beautiful presentation that’s perfect for special occasions or everyday dinners. The combination of tender bell peppers, flavorful filling, and vibrant toppings creates a dish that’s as visually appealing as it is delicious. Plus, the versatility of this recipe allows you to get creative with different fillings and toppings, making it a go-to meal for any occasion.

Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or guests with dietary restrictions, these savory and allergy-friendly stuffed peppers are sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor every bite of this wholesome meal. Happy cooking!
