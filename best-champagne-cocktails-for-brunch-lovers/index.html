Best Champagne Cocktails for Brunch Lovers


There’s something undeniably delightful about pairing brunch with an effervescent cocktail. The relaxed pace of a late morning meal and the unmistakable sparkle of champagne create a match made in culinary heaven. Whether you’re hosting friends, celebrating a special occasion, or simply treating yourself, champagne cocktails elevate any brunch from ordinary to extraordinary. Here, we dive into a collection of the best champagne cocktails that are perfect for brunch lovers. Each recipe is carefully curated to enhance your brunch experience and impress your guests.

The Classic Mimosa: A Timeless Favorite

No champagne cocktail list is complete without the Mimosa. This brunch essential combines bright, tangy orange juice with the crisp, bubbly taste of champagne, creating a drink that is refreshingly simple yet sophisticated.

Ingredients:

  • 3 oz champagne
  • 3 oz freshly squeezed orange juice

Instructions:

  1. Chill your champagne and orange juice in advance for the ultimate refreshing experience.
  2. Pour the orange juice into a champagne flute, filling it halfway.
  3. Gently top with champagne, filling the glass the rest of the way.
  4. Stir lightly, if needed, and garnish with a small orange wedge or twist of orange peel.

The Mimosa is endlessly customizable, and you can swap orange juice for grapefruit, pineapple, or even pomegranate juice to create a unique twist on this classic drink.

French 75: A Sophisticated Sparkler

For brunch-goers looking for something a bit more refined, the French 75 provides a perfect balance of sweetness, citrus, and botanical notes. Named after the French 75mm field gun due to its “kick,” this cocktail is equal parts elegant and spirited.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz champagne
  • 1 oz gin
  • 0.5 oz freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 0.5 oz simple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Start by chilling your champagne.
  2. In a shaker filled with ice, combine the gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup.
  3. Shake well and strain into a chilled champagne flute.
  4. Top with champagne and garnish with a thin lemon twist.

This festive drink is perfect for brunch gatherings that call for something a little more refined and celebratory.

Bellini: The Sweet and Fruity Delight

Originating in Venice, the Bellini is a classic that’s built on the natural sweetness of fresh peaches. Its peachy flavor is light and fruity, making it the perfect pairing for laid-back brunch dishes like croissants and fruit platters.

Ingredients:

  • 4 oz champagne
  • 2 oz fresh peach puree

Instructions:

  1. Peel and puree ripe peaches until smooth. For an extra smooth consistency, strain the puree through a fine mesh sieve.
  2. Add two ounces of peach puree to a champagne flute.
  3. Slowly top with champagne, stirring gently to combine.
  4. Garnish with a fresh peach slice, if desired.

If peaches aren’t in season, frozen peaches can be thawed and pureed, or you can experiment with other fruits like mango or apricot.

Aperol Spritz: A Bright Italian Classic

The Aperol Spritz has grown in popularity in recent years, and it’s easy to see why. Its vibrant orange hue, bittersweet flavor, and sparkling nature make it an ideal brunch cocktail. Better yet, it’s incredibly easy to make and pairs wonderfully with savory dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 3 oz prosecco or champagne
  • 2 oz Aperol
  • 1 oz soda water
  • Ice
  • Orange slice for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Fill a large wine glass with ice.
  2. Add Aperol, followed by prosecco or champagne, and top with soda water.
  3. Stir gently to combine and garnish with a slice of fresh orange.

This drink not only tastes great but also adds an eye-catching splash of color to your brunch table.

Kir Royale: A Luxurious, Minimalist Option

For a cocktail that’s both simple and stunning, the Kir Royale is an excellent choice. With only two ingredients, it delights the palate with a subtle sweetness and a touch of elegance.

Ingredients:

  • 5 oz champagne
  • 0.5 oz crème de cassis (blackcurrant liqueur)

Instructions:

  1. Pour crème de cassis into a champagne flute.
  2. Gently top with champagne, pouring slowly to preserve bubbles.
  3. Garnish with a single fresh blackberry or a twist of lemon for added flair.

This decadent drink is perfect for brunches where presentation matters.

Pomegranate Royale: A Seasonal Showstopper

For brunches taking place in cooler months or during festive occasions, the Pomegranate Royale puts a seasonal spin on champagne cocktails. The rich, ruby red hue makes it as visually stunning as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 4 oz champagne
  • 1 oz pomegranate juice
  • Pomegranate seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Pour the pomegranate juice into a chilled champagne flute.
  2. Slowly add the champagne, allowing it to mix naturally.
  3. Garnish with a handful of pomegranate seeds for a burst of flavor and color.

This cocktail brings a touch of sophistication to any brunch, whether it’s for the holidays or just a Sunday morning treat.

Raspberry Rosé Spritz: A Fresh and Floral Option

If you’re drawn to rosé champagne, this cocktail combines the fruity notes of raspberries with the floral and crisp profile of sparkling rosé wine, offering a light and refreshing take on the classic spritz.

Ingredients:

  • 4 oz sparkling rosé
  • 1 oz elderflower liqueur
  • Handful of fresh raspberries
  • Splash of soda water
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. Add ice to a large wine glass or stemmed glass.
  2. Pour in the elderflower liqueur and add the fresh raspberries.
  3. Top with sparkling rosé and a splash of soda water.
  4. Stir gently and enjoy!

This vibrant cocktail is ideal for spring or summer brunches and pairs well with lighter, herb-infused dishes.

Blood Orange Champagne Mule: A Refreshing Twist

For a creative spin on brunch cocktails, the Blood Orange Champagne Mule combines the zesty flavors of blood oranges with the crispness of champagne alongside the spiciness of ginger beer.

Ingredients:

  • 3 oz champagne
  • 1 oz blood orange juice
  • 2 oz ginger beer
  • Ice
  • Thin blood orange slice for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a copper mug or highball glass filled with ice, pour the blood orange juice and ginger beer.
  2. Top with champagne and stir gently to combine.
  3. Add a thin slice of blood orange for garnish.

This zesty cocktail provides a tangy and effervescent alternative to traditional brunch drinks.

Tips for Perfect Champagne Cocktails

  • Chill Everything: Ensure your champagne, juices, and spirits are well-chilled. Champagne cocktails are at their best when served cold.
  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Freshly squeezed juices and ripe fruits make a world of difference in flavor and quality.
  • Select the Right Champagne: While you don’t need the most expensive bottle, opt for a dry or semi-dry champagne or sparkling wine to balance the sweetness of the other ingredients.
  • Garnish Thoughtfully: Garnishes don’t only add visual appeal; they also contribute to the overall flavor and aroma of your cocktail.

Final Sip

Brunch is more than just a meal; it’s an experience—a celebration of good food, great flavors, and even better company. Adding any of these champagne cocktails to your brunch elevates the occasion, ensuring guests leave with sparkling spirits and fond memories. Whichever drink you choose, you’ll be bringing bubbly magic to the table that will leave everyone toasting to brunch again and again.
