Easter Side Dishes: Complement Your Main Course with These Recipes


Easter is a time for family gatherings, celebration, and, most importantly, delicious food to brighten up the festivities. While the main course often takes center stage, the side dishes play an equally important role in creating a memorable holiday feast. Whether you’re hosting a formal Easter dinner or a casual buffet-style brunch, the right selection of Easter side dishes can truly elevate the table, bringing balance, variety, and loads of flavor to every bite.

From vibrant spring vegetables to creamy, comforting classics, we’ve put together a collection of Easter side dish ideas that will delight your guests and perfectly complement your main course. Read on for recipes and inspiration to enhance your Easter menu with options that are as beautiful as they are tasty.

Creamy Scalloped Potatoes: A Comforting Classic

No Easter meal is complete without a decadent potato dish, and scalloped potatoes are a timeless choice that never fails to impress. Thinly sliced potatoes are layered with a velvety cream sauce and baked to golden, bubbly perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère or Cheddar cheese (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a baking dish with butter.
  2. Combine the heavy cream, milk, minced garlic, salt, and pepper in a saucepan. Heat gently until warm, stirring occasionally.
  3. Layer half the potato slices in the prepared baking dish, making sure they overlap slightly. Pour half of the warm cream mixture over the potatoes and, if desired, sprinkle with Gruyère or Cheddar cheese.
  4. Repeat with the remaining potatoes and cream mixture, ending with an optional cheese layer.
  5. Bake uncovered for 45–50 minutes or until the potatoes are tender and the top is browned. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

The result is a creamy, comforting dish that pairs beautifully with roast lamb, baked ham, or any other hearty Easter main course.

Roasted Asparagus with Lemon and Parmesan

Easter celebrates the arrival of spring, and what better way to highlight the season than with fresh, roasted asparagus? This simple yet sophisticated dish is bursting with flavor and is quick to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb asparagus, tough ends trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Toss the asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper until evenly coated. Spread the spears in a single layer on the baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 12–15 minutes, turning once, until the asparagus is tender and lightly charred.
  4. Remove from the oven and drizzle with fresh lemon juice. Sprinkle with lemon zest and Parmesan cheese before serving.

This vibrant side dish adds a burst of freshness to the table and is the perfect complement to rich, savory mains like glazed ham.

Honey-Glazed Carrots with Thyme

Sweet, tender carrots glazed with honey and a hint of thyme bring warmth and natural sweetness to your Easter spread. They’re simple to prepare and are sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb baby carrots or large carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • ½ teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, stir in the honey.
  2. Add the carrots to the skillet and cook for 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they begin to soften.
  3. Sprinkle in the thyme, salt, and pepper, then continue cooking for another 6–8 minutes until the carrots are tender and caramelized.
  4. Transfer to a serving dish and garnish with additional thyme if desired.

These honey-glazed carrots are a delightful combination of sweet and savory and pair harmoniously with roasted poultry or pork.

Herb-Infused Deviled Eggs

Deviled eggs are a staple at Easter gatherings and offer a versatile way to incorporate fresh flavors into your menu. Adding fresh herbs like dill, chives, or parsley gives this classic appetizer a refreshing spin.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh herbs (dill, chives, or parsley)
  • Paprika for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Place the eggs in a saucepan and cover with water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Drain the eggs and transfer them to a bowl of ice water to cool. Once cooled, peel the eggs and slice them in half lengthwise.
  3. Scoop out the yolks and place them in a mixing bowl. Mash the yolks with a fork, then stir in mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, vinegar, salt, pepper, and fresh herbs until smooth.
  4. Spoon or pipe the mixture back into the egg whites and sprinkle lightly with paprika.

Deviled eggs are easy to make ahead and offer a creamy, flavorful addition to the Easter table.

Cheesy Broccoli Casserole

If you’re looking for an indulgent vegetable side dish, consider a cheesy broccoli casserole. It’s a heartwarming crowd-pleaser that both kids and adults will love.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups broccoli florets, steamed
  • 1 can cream of mushroom soup (10.5 oz)
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 cups shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup crushed Ritz crackers or breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cream of mushroom soup, sour cream, 1½ cups Cheddar cheese, salt, and pepper. Fold in the steamed broccoli until well coated.
  3. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it evenly. Top with the remaining cheese, followed by crushed crackers, and drizzle with melted butter.
  4. Bake for 25–30 minutes or until bubbly and golden brown.

This casserole is a rich and satisfying addition to a holiday meal, and it pairs wonderfully with roasted meats.

Fresh Spring Pea Salad with Mint and Feta

Add a burst of color and crunch to your Easter table with a refreshing spring pea salad. Combined with mint and feta, this dish embraces the essence of the season.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas (thawed)
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh mint
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the peas and red onion.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to create a simple vinaigrette.
  3. Pour the vinaigrette over the peas and toss gently to coat.
  4. Stir in the fresh mint and feta cheese just before serving.

This light, zesty salad pairs perfectly with heavier main dishes, adding a refreshing balance to the menu.

These Easter side dishes will not only complement your main course but also add variety and depth to your holiday feast. With a mix of comforting classics and fresh spring-inspired options, your guests are sure to savor every bite. Whether you’re drawn to creamy potatoes, bright green vegetables, or sweet-glazed carrots, these recipes are crafted to fit seamlessly into your Easter celebration. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to create a spread that looks as good as it tastes!
