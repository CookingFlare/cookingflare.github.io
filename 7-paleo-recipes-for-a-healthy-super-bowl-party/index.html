7 Paleo Recipes for a Healthy Super Bowl Party


The Super Bowl is one of the biggest sporting events of the year, and if you’re hosting or attending a Super Bowl party, you’re probably planning a spread of mouthwatering snacks and appetizers to keep everyone energized and satisfied. However, for those following a Paleo lifestyle, it can be a challenge to find party foods that fit the bill without sacrificing taste or enjoyment.

But don’t worry, you can still enjoy the big game with healthy, satisfying, and delicious snacks that are entirely Paleo-approved. In this post, we’ll share seven easy and flavorful Paleo recipes that will be the hit of your Super Bowl party while keeping you on track with your health goals.

1. Paleo Chicken Wings

Chicken wings are a classic Super Bowl snack, and luckily, they fit perfectly into the Paleo diet when prepared without processed sauces or breading. These wings are crispy, juicy, and full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 10-12 chicken wings
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together olive oil, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, cumin, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toss the chicken wings in the spice mixture until evenly coated.
  4. Arrange the wings in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the wings are golden brown and crispy.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

These wings are flavorful and crispy without any flour or breadcrumbs, making them an ideal Paleo-friendly choice for your Super Bowl spread.

2. Guacamole with Veggie Chips

No Super Bowl party is complete without guacamole, and this creamy, flavorful dip is the perfect addition to your Paleo menu. Serve it with homemade veggie chips for a satisfying and healthy crunch.

Ingredients for Guacamole:

  • 3 ripe avocados, mashed
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeds removed and finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Ingredients for Veggie Chips:

  • 1 medium zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 sweet potato, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions for Guacamole:

  1. In a bowl, mash the avocados until smooth.
  2. Add the chopped onion, tomato, jalapeño, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Mix everything together and adjust seasoning as needed.

Instructions for Veggie Chips:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Toss the vegetable slices with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Lay the slices in a single layer on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until crispy and golden brown.

Serve your creamy guacamole alongside the crispy veggie chips for a satisfying and nutritious Paleo snack.

3. Paleo Meatballs with Spicy Dipping Sauce

These savory meatballs are made from grass-fed beef, ground turkey, or chicken, and they’re seasoned with fresh herbs and spices. They make a great finger food for a Super Bowl party and pair perfectly with a zesty dipping sauce.

Ingredients for Meatballs:

  • 1 lb ground beef, turkey, or chicken
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Ingredients for Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup Paleo-friendly ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions for Meatballs:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, almond flour, egg, garlic, parsley, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix until everything is well incorporated.
  3. Shape the mixture into small meatballs (about 1-1.5 inches in diameter) and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until the meatballs are golden brown and cooked through.

Instructions for Dipping Sauce:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the ketchup, mustard, hot sauce, and apple cider vinegar.
  2. Adjust the spice level to your liking by adding more hot sauce if desired.

Serve the meatballs with the spicy dipping sauce for a delicious and satisfying Paleo snack.

4. Paleo Bacon-Wrapped Dates

Bacon and dates are a match made in heaven. This sweet and savory appetizer is simple to make and will be a crowd favorite at your Super Bowl party.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large Medjool dates, pitted
  • 12 slices of nitrate-free bacon
  • 12 whole almonds (optional)
  • Toothpicks

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. If using, stuff each date with a whole almond.
  3. Wrap each date with a slice of bacon and secure with a toothpick.
  4. Place the wrapped dates on the baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the bacon is crispy and golden.
  5. Serve warm.

These bacon-wrapped dates offer the perfect balance of sweetness and savory goodness, making them a hit at your Super Bowl gathering.

5. Paleo Veggie Platter with Avocado Ranch Dip

A fresh vegetable platter is a healthy, colorful addition to any party spread, and it’s easy to make Paleo-friendly with the addition of a creamy avocado ranch dip. This dip is made with whole-food ingredients and will be a great pairing for all your fresh veggies.

Ingredients for Veggie Platter:

  • Carrot sticks
  • Celery sticks
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Cucumber slices
  • Bell pepper strips

Ingredients for Avocado Ranch Dip:

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1/2 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. To make the dip, combine the avocado, coconut milk, lemon juice, dill, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a food processor. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Arrange the fresh veggies on a platter.
  3. Serve the avocado ranch dip alongside the veggie platter for a refreshing and satisfying snack.

This Paleo-friendly dip is a great way to get in your veggies and enjoy a healthier alternative to traditional creamy dips.

6. Paleo Stuffed Mushrooms

These stuffed mushrooms are full of flavor and make an excellent finger food for your Super Bowl party. They are filled with a savory mixture of sausage, spinach, and herbs, all baked to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large mushrooms, stems removed
  • 1/2 lb ground sausage (preferably pasture-raised)
  • 1/2 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned and fully cooked.
  3. Add the onion and spinach to the skillet and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the spinach is wilted.
  4. Stir in the almond flour, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat.
  5. Spoon the sausage mixture into the mushroom caps and arrange them on the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is golden.

These stuffed mushrooms are an irresistible addition to your Paleo-friendly Super Bowl spread.

7. Paleo Sweet Potato Fries

No Super Bowl party is complete without fries, but traditional fries are off-limits on the Paleo diet. Luckily, sweet potatoes are a perfect substitute, and these crispy, flavorful fries are just as satisfying as their deep-fried counterparts.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into fries
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Toss the sweet potato fries with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the fries in a single layer on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until crispy and golden.

These sweet potato fries are a great alternative to regular fries and will definitely be a crowd-pleaser.

Conclusion

With these seven Paleo recipes, you can enjoy a healthy and delicious Super Bowl party without compromising your dietary goals. From crispy chicken wings to creamy guacamole and zesty meatballs, these snacks are sure to impress your guests while keeping you on track with your Paleo lifestyle. So go ahead, enjoy the game, and indulge in these tasty and nutritious treats!
