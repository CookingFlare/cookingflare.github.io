Brilliant Brunch: Spring Veggie and Goat Cheese Frittata


There’s nothing quite like a leisurely brunch on a beautiful spring morning, and what better way to celebrate the season than with a delightful Spring Veggie and Goat Cheese Frittata! This brunch favorite is not only easy to make but also a versatile dish that can be customized with your favorite seasonal vegetables. Whether you’re hosting a brunch gathering or simply treating yourself to a special weekend meal, this frittata is sure to impress with its vibrant colors and delicious flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • 4 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and position the rack in the middle.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.

  3. Sauté the Vegetables: Heat the olive oil in a 10-inch oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the asparagus and cook for another 3-4 minutes until slightly tender. Toss in the cherry tomatoes and spinach, and cook until the spinach is wilted, about 2 minutes.

  4. Add the Egg Mixture: Pour the egg and milk mixture over the sautéed vegetables in the skillet. Gently stir to distribute the vegetables evenly.

  5. Top with Goat Cheese: Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese over the top of the frittata.

  6. Bake the Frittata: Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the frittata is set in the middle and the edges are golden brown.

  7. Let it Rest: Once done, remove the skillet from the oven and let the frittata rest for a few minutes before slicing.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Garnish with fresh herbs if desired, then slice the frittata into wedges and serve warm.

Tips for Customization:

  • Feel free to swap in your favorite seasonal vegetables such as bell peppers, zucchini, or mushrooms.
  • Consider adding a sprinkle of feta cheese or grated Parmesan for an extra layer of flavor.
  • For a heartier option, add cooked bacon, ham, or sausage to the frittata.

Final Thoughts:

This Spring Veggie and Goat Cheese Frittata is a celebration of fresh, seasonal produce and simple, wholesome ingredients. Its vibrant colors and rich flavors make it a standout dish for any brunch occasion. The beauty of this recipe lies in its adaptability, allowing you to personalize it with your favorite ingredients. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary journey, this frittata is a brilliant addition to your brunch repertoire. So, gather your loved ones, savor the flavors of the season, and enjoy a delightful brunch with this irresistible frittata at the heart of the table. Cheers to good food and great company!

So, there you have it – a delightful recipe for a Spring Veggie and Goat Cheese Frittata that’s bound to elevate your brunch experience. We hope you enjoy creating and savoring this delicious dish as much as we do. Cheers to good food, great company, and the joys of spring!
