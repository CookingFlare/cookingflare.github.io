How to Prepare Thanksgiving Brussels Sprouts with Parmesan


Thanksgiving is a time for family, tradition, and, of course, an abundance of delicious food. While turkey, mashed potatoes, and pumpkin pie often take center stage, it’s the side dishes that truly make the meal complete. One such dish that will surely elevate your Thanksgiving spread is Brussels sprouts with Parmesan. This simple, yet flavorful dish combines the earthy bitterness of Brussels sprouts with the rich, savory taste of Parmesan cheese, creating a perfect balance of textures and flavors.

In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to prepare Brussels sprouts with Parmesan, offering helpful tips and suggestions for achieving the ideal taste and texture. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is approachable and sure to impress your guests.

The Best Brussels Sprouts for Your Dish

When it comes to Brussels sprouts, freshness is key. Look for sprouts that are small to medium-sized, as they tend to be more tender and flavorful. Avoid those that are yellowing, shriveled, or have loose leaves. Fresh Brussels sprouts are typically bright green and firm to the touch.

If possible, buy Brussels sprouts on the stalk. This not only guarantees that they’re fresh but also allows you to trim them yourself, which can help ensure that each sprout is of uniform size.

Cleaning and Preparing Brussels Sprouts

Before you cook your Brussels sprouts, it’s important to clean and prepare them properly:

  1. Trim the stems: Use a sharp knife to trim off the tough, woody stems at the base of each Brussels sprout.
  2. Peel away any loose or yellowing outer leaves: The outer leaves are often the most bitter and may not cook evenly. Removing these will ensure your dish has a better texture and flavor.
  3. Halve or quarter the sprouts: Depending on the size of the Brussels sprouts, you can halve them for a tender bite or quarter them for a more crispy texture. This step helps the Brussels sprouts cook evenly and allows the seasonings to penetrate better.

Prepping Your Ingredients

Aside from the Brussels sprouts, you’ll need a few basic ingredients to make this dish sing. The key to achieving the perfect balance of flavors lies in the seasoning and Parmesan cheese:

  • Parmesan cheese: Opt for freshly grated Parmesan, as it will melt better and provide a more robust flavor compared to pre-shredded varieties. The nutty and slightly salty taste of Parmesan complements the Brussels sprouts beautifully.
  • Olive oil: You’ll need a good-quality olive oil to roast the Brussels sprouts. Olive oil helps to crisp up the edges of the sprouts while also providing a rich flavor.
  • Garlic: Garlic adds a layer of savory depth and aroma. You can mince it finely or use whole cloves, depending on your preference.
  • Salt and pepper: Simple seasoning of salt and freshly cracked black pepper is all that’s needed to bring out the natural sweetness of the Brussels sprouts.
  • Optional additions: You can add a touch of lemon zest for a hint of brightness or a sprinkle of balsamic vinegar to add depth and tang. For those who prefer a bit of heat, a pinch of red pepper flakes can also be a great addition.

Roasting Brussels Sprouts to Perfection

The key to achieving crispy, tender Brussels sprouts is roasting them at a high temperature. Roasting brings out the natural sugars in the Brussels sprouts, caramelizing them and creating a sweet, nutty flavor.

  1. Preheat your oven: Set your oven to 400°F (200°C). A high temperature ensures that the Brussels sprouts roast evenly and become crispy on the outside while remaining tender on the inside.
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil and seasoning: Place the cleaned and halved Brussels sprouts in a large mixing bowl. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and minced garlic (or garlic powder if you prefer). Toss everything together to ensure the Brussels sprouts are evenly coated.
  3. Spread the sprouts on a baking sheet: Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or aluminum foil for easy cleanup. Arrange the Brussels sprouts in a single layer, making sure they are not overcrowded. This ensures they roast evenly and don’t steam.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes: Roast the Brussels sprouts for 20-25 minutes, stirring once halfway through to ensure even cooking. You’ll know they’re done when the outer leaves are crispy and caramelized, and the sprouts are fork-tender on the inside. If you like extra crispiness, you can let them roast for a few extra minutes.

Adding the Parmesan

The magic of this dish comes when you add the Parmesan cheese. As the Brussels sprouts are roasting, the edges get crispy, creating the perfect base for melting cheese.

  1. Sprinkle Parmesan: In the last 5 minutes of roasting, remove the Brussels sprouts from the oven and sprinkle a generous amount of freshly grated Parmesan cheese on top. Place the baking sheet back in the oven to allow the cheese to melt and form a delicious crust on the Brussels sprouts.
  2. Return to the oven for melting: Roast for an additional 5 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. The cheese should have a slightly golden color at the edges, adding an irresistible crunch to every bite.

Serving Your Thanksgiving Brussels Sprouts with Parmesan

Once your Brussels sprouts are perfectly roasted and coated in melted Parmesan, it’s time to serve! You can either present them in a large bowl for family-style serving or place them on individual plates for a more elegant presentation. Garnish with freshly cracked black pepper or a squeeze of lemon juice for an extra burst of flavor.

For a fun twist, you can serve them alongside a creamy dipping sauce, such as garlic aioli or a tangy ranch dressing. This adds an extra layer of flavor and a touch of decadence that will make your Thanksgiving Brussels sprouts even more irresistible.

Tips for the Perfect Thanksgiving Brussels Sprouts with Parmesan

While the recipe itself is simple, there are a few tips and tricks you can use to ensure that your Brussels sprouts come out perfectly every time:

  • Avoid overcrowding the pan: If you place too many Brussels sprouts on the baking sheet, they will steam rather than roast. This will prevent them from getting crispy. Use two baking sheets if necessary.
  • Flip the sprouts halfway through: Roasting Brussels sprouts requires even heat distribution. Make sure to flip the sprouts halfway through the roasting time to ensure they cook evenly and become crispy all over.
  • Adjust the cooking time based on size: If your Brussels sprouts are particularly large, you may need to roast them a little longer. Conversely, smaller Brussels sprouts will cook faster, so keep an eye on them.
  • Enhance with extras: For added flavor, consider adding crushed red pepper flakes, balsamic vinegar, or even a sprinkle of chopped fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary before roasting.
  • Make ahead: You can prep the Brussels sprouts the day before Thanksgiving. Trim and halve them, then store them in an airtight container in the fridge. On the day of your meal, simply roast and finish with Parmesan for a stress-free side dish.

Variations to Try

While Brussels sprouts with Parmesan is delicious as is, don’t be afraid to experiment with different flavor combinations. Here are a few variations you can try to make this dish your own:

  1. Bacon Brussels Sprouts with Parmesan: Add crumbled crispy bacon for a smoky, savory twist. The combination of bacon, Parmesan, and Brussels sprouts is a crowd-pleaser.
  2. Garlic Butter Brussels Sprouts: Instead of olive oil, toss the Brussels sprouts in melted garlic butter for an extra indulgent flavor. Finish with Parmesan cheese for a rich and savory side dish.
  3. Cranberry and Almond Brussels Sprouts: For a festive touch, add dried cranberries and sliced almonds after roasting. The sweetness of the cranberries pairs beautifully with the earthy Brussels sprouts and salty Parmesan.
  4. Spicy Brussels Sprouts: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or drizzle with hot sauce to give your Brussels sprouts a spicy kick.

Conclusion

Brussels sprouts with Parmesan make a delicious and flavorful addition to your Thanksgiving spread. Whether you’re looking for a simple, classic side dish or something with a twist, this recipe is versatile, easy to prepare, and sure to be a hit with your guests. Roasting Brussels sprouts brings out their natural sweetness, and when paired with the savory richness of Parmesan cheese, it creates a perfect balance of flavors and textures. With just a few simple ingredients and a little time, you can transform humble Brussels sprouts into a mouthwatering dish that everyone will love.

So this Thanksgiving, don’t forget to include Brussels sprouts with Parmesan on your menu—it’s the side dish that’s sure to steal the show!
