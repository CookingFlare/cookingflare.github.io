Korean Seafood Pancakes (Haemul Pajeon) Recipe


If you’re craving something savory, crispy, and packed with flavors, Korean seafood pancakes, also known as Haemul Pajeon, should be at the top of your list. These delicious pancakes are a beloved dish in Korean cuisine, often served as a snack, appetizer, or even as a main course. Made with a medley of fresh seafood, green onions, and a simple yet flavorful batter, Haemul Pajeon offers a perfect balance of crunch and tenderness, with the seafood giving it an added depth of flavor.

In this recipe, we will walk you through how to make the perfect Haemul Pajeon at home. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just beginning to explore Korean cuisine, this dish is easy to prepare and sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients for Haemul Pajeon

To prepare a traditional Korean seafood pancake, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Pancake Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rice flour (optional, but it gives a crispier texture)
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup cold water (add more if necessary)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (for extra flavor)

For the Seafood and Vegetables:

  • 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined, chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup squid, cleaned and cut into rings or small pieces
  • 1/2 cup mussels or clams (optional), scrubbed and cleaned
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 4-6 stalks green onions (scallions), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1/2 bell pepper, thinly sliced (optional)
  • A handful of fresh herbs like cilantro (optional)

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Korean chili flakes (gochugaru) or chili paste (gochujang), optional for spice
  • A few sesame seeds for garnish

How to Make Korean Seafood Pancakes (Haemul Pajeon)

Step 1: Prepare the Seafood

Start by prepping your seafood. If you’re using shrimp, squid, and mussels, make sure they are cleaned, peeled, and chopped into bite-sized pieces. For a fresh seafood taste, it’s best to use a combination of seafood that’s available to you—shrimp, squid, and even clams or mussels work perfectly in this dish.

Once your seafood is ready, place it in a bowl and set it aside. You can mix all the seafood together, or you can keep the different types separate, depending on your preference.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables

Slice the onion and bell pepper thinly. Cut the green onions into 2-inch pieces. You can also add a handful of fresh herbs, such as cilantro, for a bit of extra flavor and freshness. The vegetables add a nice crunch and contrast to the soft seafood in the pancake.

Step 3: Make the Batter

In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, rice flour, cornstarch, salt, and black pepper. Stir until evenly mixed. Add the egg, and slowly incorporate the cold water, mixing as you go. The batter should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon but still pourable. If it’s too thick, add a little more water to achieve the right consistency. The sesame oil is added to enhance the flavor of the batter and give it a subtle depth.

Whisk the batter until smooth and no lumps remain. A well-mixed batter is crucial to ensuring a crispy and light pancake.

Step 4: Combine Seafood and Vegetables with the Batter

Once your batter is ready, fold in the seafood and vegetables. Gently stir until everything is evenly coated in the batter. The seafood and vegetables should be well-distributed so that each bite is filled with flavors and textures.

Step 5: Cook the Haemul Pajeon

Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Add a generous amount of vegetable oil to the pan, ensuring it covers the bottom evenly. The oil helps achieve that golden, crispy texture on the outside of the pancake.

Once the oil is hot, pour the batter into the skillet, spreading it out evenly. You want the pancake to be about 1/2 inch thick so that it cooks properly and crisps up nicely on the edges. Press down gently to flatten it out.

Cook the pancake for about 4-5 minutes on the first side until it turns golden brown and crispy. Flip the pancake carefully using a spatula, and cook for another 3-4 minutes on the other side until it’s golden and cooked through. Be patient and avoid turning the pancake too early, as this can cause it to fall apart.

Step 6: Prepare the Dipping Sauce

While the pancake is cooking, prepare the dipping sauce. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, garlic, and optional chili flakes or gochujang for a spicy kick. Stir until the sugar dissolves and everything is well-combined. Sprinkle a few sesame seeds on top for an extra touch of flavor.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once the Haemul Pajeon is cooked to a crisp, remove it from the skillet and place it on a plate. You can cut it into wedges like a pizza or serve it whole, depending on your preference. Serve it with the dipping sauce on the side for guests to dip their pancakes into.

Haemul Pajeon is best enjoyed fresh and hot, but if you have leftovers, you can store them in the fridge and reheat them in a skillet for that crispy texture. It makes for an excellent snack, appetizer, or light meal.

Tips for Perfect Haemul Pajeon

  • Use cold water for the batter: This is crucial for making a crispy and light pancake. Cold water helps prevent the batter from becoming too runny and ensures that the pancake has a nice, crispy texture on the outside.

  • Don’t overcrowd the pan: For the best results, cook the pancakes one at a time. If you add too much batter to the pan, it can become difficult to flip and may not cook evenly.

  • Customize your seafood: Haemul Pajeon is a versatile dish, and you can adjust the type of seafood based on what you like. You can even use just one type of seafood, such as shrimp or squid, or add more variety for a richer taste.

  • Make it spicier: If you enjoy a bit of heat, add more chili flakes or gochujang to the dipping sauce, or even directly to the batter.

  • Ensure a crispy edge: Adding sesame oil to the batter and using enough oil in the pan will help achieve that signature crispy edge that makes Haemul Pajeon so irresistible.

Why You’ll Love Haemul Pajeon

Korean seafood pancakes are not only delicious but also quite simple to make. The contrast of the crispy outside and tender, juicy seafood inside makes each bite an experience. Whether you’re serving them at a family dinner or enjoying them with friends, Haemul Pajeon is a crowd-pleaser that brings the vibrant flavors of Korea right to your kitchen.

Additionally, the recipe is highly adaptable, allowing you to customize the ingredients to fit your taste preferences. From seafood lovers to those who prefer vegetarian options, Haemul Pajeon can be tailored to suit everyone. It’s an exciting, flavorful dish that introduces you to the essence of Korean home-cooked meals and is perfect for anyone looking to dive into Korean cuisine.

Whether you’ve had Haemul Pajeon at a Korean restaurant or you’re trying it for the first time, this recipe will help you recreate that authentic, crispy seafood pancake experience in your own home. Serve it with a cold beer, a glass of soju, or a warm cup of green tea, and you’ll have a truly delightful meal to enjoy with family and friends.

Conclusion

Korean seafood pancakes (Haemul Pajeon) are a must-try for anyone interested in exploring Korean cuisine. With a delightful mix of seafood, green onions, and a crispy, golden exterior, it’s no wonder this dish is a favorite in Korean homes and restaurants alike. Simple to make yet bursting with flavor, Haemul Pajeon can be adapted to suit various tastes and is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re looking to impress your guests or enjoy a comforting meal at home, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire.
