Garlic Herb Dinner Rolls: A Flavorful Side Dish


Nothing adds more flavor to a meal than warm, buttery dinner rolls. With the perfect blend of savory herbs and spices, garlic herb dinner rolls make any dinner more satisfying. They can be a delightful addition to holiday feasts, barbecue parties, or simply as a comforting home meal.

The Story Behind the Recipe

Garlic herb dinner rolls originated from the need for a more flavorful alternative to the traditional dinner rolls. Experimenting with different herbs and spices, the creator discovered that garlic added a unique depth of flavor to the rolls. Over time, this simple yet inviting dish grew in popularity and became a go-to side dish for many families.

What You’ll Need

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 1/2 cups milk, at room temperature
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper

Equipment:

  • Stand mixer
  • Mixing bowl
  • Cutting board
  • Rolling pin
  • Parchment paper
  • Aluminum foil
  • Bread knife

How to Make Garlic Herb Dinner Rolls

To begin, combine 2 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour, 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast, and 1 tablespoon sugar in a large mixing bowl. In a separate bowl, combine 1/2 cup of warm water, 2 tablespoons of melted and cooled unsalted butter, and 1 large egg. Add 1 1/2 cups of milk that is at room temperature, 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 teaspoon of dried oregano, 1 teaspoon of dried parsley, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper.

Next, add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix using a stand mixer on medium speed until a smooth and slightly sticky dough forms. If needed, add extra flour one tablespoon at a time until the dough is manageable.

Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide into 18 even portions. Roll each portion into a smooth ball and place on the prepared baking sheet about 2 inches apart. Cover loosely with aluminum foil and let rise for an additional 20 minutes.

Bake the rolls for 15-18 minutes or until golden brown. Once finished baking, transfer the rolls to a wire rack and let cool for about 10 minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Serve warm garlic herb dinner rolls with your favorite main dishes like pasta, roasted meats, barbecue, or as a simple yet delightful appetizer. You can also get creative and add different herbs or spices to the recipe to cater to personal preferences or complement the main dish.

Enjoy your homemade garlic herb dinner rolls and elevate your dining experiences with family and friends.
