Korean Spicy Pork Belly Adventure: A Recipe for Dwaeji Samgyeopsal Perfection


Dwaeji Samgyeopsal is a popular Korean dish that combines succulent pork belly with a spicy marinade, resulting in a mouthwatering explosion of flavors. If you’re ready for a culinary adventure, then join me on this journey to create the perfect Dwaeji Samgyeopsal at home!

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds of pork belly, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of rice wine or mirin
  • 1 teaspoon of grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, rice wine or mirin, grated ginger, and black pepper. Mix well to form a smooth paste.

  2. Add the thinly sliced pork belly to the marinade and toss until each piece is coated evenly. Allow the pork to marinate for at least 30 minutes, but for the best results, let it marinate in the refrigerator overnight.

  3. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium heat. If using a grill, make sure it’s well-oiled to prevent the meat from sticking.

  4. Place the marinated pork belly slices on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are nicely charred and cooked through. Be careful not to overcook them, as pork belly can become tough if cooked for too long.

  5. Once cooked, transfer the pork belly slices to a serving platter and garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds. The vibrant colors will add a pop of freshness to the dish.

  6. Serve the Dwaeji Samgyeopsal hot with steamed rice, kimchi, and other Korean side dishes for a complete and authentic Korean dining experience. The combination of the spicy pork belly with the tangy kimchi is simply irresistible!

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a milder spice level, reduce the amount of gochujang and gochugaru according to your taste preferences. You can also add a bit more honey for a touch of sweetness.

  • For an extra punch of flavor, try adding sliced onions or mushrooms to the marinade. These additional ingredients will absorb the savory marinade and complement the pork belly beautifully.

  • If you don’t have access to a grill or grill pan, you can also cook the pork belly in a skillet over medium-high heat. Make sure to cook it in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan.

  • Leftover Dwaeji Samgyeopsal can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It makes for a delicious filling in sandwiches or wraps the next day.

  • Don’t forget to prepare some refreshing drinks to balance out the spiciness of the pork belly. Traditional Korean barley tea or a cold glass of Korean rice wine (makgeolli) are excellent choices.

Now that you have the recipe for Dwaeji Samgyeopsal perfection, it’s time to embark on your own Korean spicy pork belly adventure. Get ready to tantalize your taste buds and impress your family and friends with this delightful dish. Happy cooking!
