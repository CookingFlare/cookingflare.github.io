Classic Chiles Rellenos: Stuffed Peppers Recipe


Chiles Rellenos is a classic Mexican dish that brings together the vibrant flavors of roasted peppers and a delicious stuffing. This recipe is a beloved favorite, featuring a perfect balance of heat and savory goodness. In this blog post, we will guide you through the steps to create your own mouthwatering Classic Chiles Rellenos. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 6 large poblano peppers
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (such as Monterey Jack or Oaxaca)
  • 1 cup cooked and seasoned ground beef or shredded chicken
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Preheat your broiler to high. Place the poblano peppers on a baking sheet and broil, turning occasionally, until the skins are charred and blistered. This should take about 10 minutes. Remove the peppers from the oven and place them in a plastic bag. Seal the bag and allow the peppers to steam for 10 minutes. This will make it easier to remove the skins.

  2. After 10 minutes, remove the peppers from the bag and carefully peel off the charred skins. Make a small incision near the stem of each pepper and carefully remove the seeds and membranes. Rinse the peppers under cold water to remove any remaining seeds.

  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the shredded cheese and cooked ground beef or shredded chicken. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Gently stuff each pepper with the cheese and meat mixture, being careful not to overstuff.

  4. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form. In another bowl, beat the egg yolks until creamy and pale yellow. Gently fold the beaten egg yolks into the egg whites.

  5. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Dredge each stuffed pepper in flour, shaking off any excess. Dip the peppers into the beaten egg mixture, coating them evenly.

  6. Carefully place the coated peppers in the hot oil and fry until golden brown on all sides. This should take about 5 minutes. Transfer the fried peppers to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  7. In a small saucepan, combine the tomato sauce and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat. Stir in the chopped cilantro and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  8. To serve, place a fried stuffed pepper on a plate and drizzle the tomato sauce mixture over the top. Garnish with additional cilantro if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • You can customize the filling by using different types of cheese or adding vegetables like corn or diced onions.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian option, you can replace the meat with black beans or mushrooms.
  • For an extra kick, you can add diced jalapenos or a sprinkle of cayenne pepper to the filling.
  • Serve the Classic Chiles Rellenos with Mexican rice and refried beans for a complete and satisfying meal.

Now that you have mastered the art of making Classic Chiles Rellenos, it’s time to impress your friends and family with this delicious dish. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a flavorful Mexican meal, these stuffed peppers are sure to be a hit. Enjoy!
