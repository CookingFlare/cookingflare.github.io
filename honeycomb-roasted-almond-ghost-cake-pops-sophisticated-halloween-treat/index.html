Honeycomb & Roasted Almond Ghost Cake Pops: Sophisticated Halloween Treat


Ah, Halloween. A time for spooky decorations, eerie tunes, and, of course, delicious sweets! This year, surprise your guests with an unforgettable dessert that combines the richness of honeycomb and the nutty crunch of roasted almonds into a jaw-dropping ghost-shaped cake pop.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups crushed honeycomb cereal (approximately 3 large store-bought honeycomb bars or 2 homemade honeycomb slices)
  • 1/2 cup roasted almonds, finely chopped
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1.5 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups cake batter (use your favorite recipe or store-bought mix)
  • 1 cup white chocolate or candy melts
  • 1 cup black candy melts
  • Ghost-shaped cookie cutters or aluminum foil
  • Lollipop sticks or skewers

Instructions

  1. Begin with the cake. Bake your cake in a 9-inch round cake pan according to the package instructions or your preferred recipe. Once baked and completely cooled, crumble the cake into a bowl, leaving behind any crumbs that stick to the sides of the pan.

  2. In a separate bowl, combine the crumbled cake, cream cheese, powdered sugar, and melted butter. Stir until well-mixed and smooth.

  3. Fold in the crushed honeycomb cereal and chopped almonds, mixing thoroughly to create the base for your cake pops.

  4. Using a cookie scoop or spoon, form cake pop-sized balls from the mixture and place them on a baking sheet lined with wax or parchment paper. Refrigerate the pops for at least 30 minutes to solidify their shape.

  5. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare a small, shallow baking pan by lining it with parchment paper. Spread the remaining chopped almonds out on the pan and roast for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden brown. Remove from the oven and allow the almonds to cool.

  6. To make your ghost shapes, you can use any desired method. One simple way is to wrap a piece of aluminum foil around an empty toilet paper roll, and then cut the foil to the desired length for your ghost. Press this against the top of your cake pop to create the round “head.”

  7. Alternatively, use ghost-shaped cookie cutters - press the cutter around the cake pop, ensuring that the top is slightly wider to resemble a ghost head. Secure the cookie cutter with a toothpick or skewer for stability if needed.

  8. To give your ghost cake pops that classic white look, microwave the white chocolate or candy melts in 30-second increments until fully melted. Dip the top of your cake pop into the melted chocolate, letting any excess drip off before attaching the cookie cutter or foil ghost head.

  9. For the ghost eyes, melt the black candy melts in a small, microwave-safe bowl. Using a piping bag or a plastic bag with the corner snipped off, carefully apply two small circles for each ghost eye. Allow the chocolate to set before serving your spooky treats.

  10. To remove the ghost shapes from your pops, gently push upwards from the bottom of the cutter or carefully peel away the foil, taking care not to disturbs the coated cake pop.

  11. Finally, insert lollipop sticks into the bottom of each cake pop and serve at room temperature on a Halloween-inspired display.

Enjoy these elegant and petite ghost cake pops as delicious Halloween treats that will leave a lasting impression on all who dare to dig in!
