BBQ Bison Chili: Hearty and Flavorful


BBQ Bison Chili is a delectable and robust dish that exudes a mouth-watering flavor. Made with bison meat, it offers a unique dining experience combining the cribbitness of the wild with the delights of backyard barbecuing. This chili is nutritious, filling, and a perfect option for game day, a chilly evening at home, or anytime you crave a hearty meal. In this post, we’ll explore the ingredients, process, and taste that will make your BBQ Bison Chili stand out.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Bison Meat (ground)
  • 1 large Onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves Garlic (minced)
  • 1 red Bell Pepper (diced)
  • 1 green Bell Pepper (diced)
  • 3-4 Jalapeno Peppers (diced)
  • 2 tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 2 tablespoons Paprika
  • 1 tablespoon Ground Cumin
  • 1 teaspoon Ground Coriander
  • 1 teaspoon Dried Oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon Cayenne Pepper
  • 2 tablespoons Chili Powder
  • 1 large Egg (beaten)
  • 2 cups Bean Blend (drained and rinsed)
  • 2 cups Crushed Tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon Tomato Paste
  • 2-3 cups Beef Broth
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Pepper (to taste)
  • Optional Toppings: Chopped Green Onions, Cilantro, Shredded Cheese, Avocado Slices, Lime Wedges

Process

Step 1: Preparing the Meat

Choose a high-quality ground bison meat for the best experience. If you can get your hands on venison or elk meat, that is an excellent option as well.

Step 2: Cooking the Ground Meat

Cook the ground meat in a skillet over medium heat. Stir regularly to prevent sticking and ensure even browning. Cook for roughly 10-12 minutes or until it reaches an internal temperature of 160°F.

Step 3: Preparing the Veggies

Dice the onions, bell peppers, and jalapeno peppers into fine pieces. Mince the garlic and set aside. This step is subjective, as you can choose the level of spiciness that suits your tongue’s capacity.

Step 4: Cooking the Vegetables

In a separate pan, sauté the diced onions, garlic, and bell peppers over medium heat. Cook for about 6-8 minutes or until the onions start to turn translucent. Add the jalapenos at this stage and cook for another 2 minutes.

Step 5: Assembling the Chili

In a large pot or Dutch oven, combine cooked meat, sautéed vegetables, olive oil, paprika, ground cumin, ground coriander, dried oregano, cayenne pepper, chili powder, and beaten egg. Mix well.

Step 6: Adding the Beans and Tomatoes

Stir in the bean blend, crushed tomatoes, and tomato paste. Pour in 2-3 cups of beef broth to create a thick yet soupy consistency.

Step 7: Seasoning

Season the BBQ Bison Chili to taste with salt and pepper.

Step 8: Simmering the Chili

Bring the mixture to a boil, and then reduce the heat to low. Simmer for about 2 hours, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the more flavorsome it will be.

Step 9: Serving and Toppings

Serve hot with your choice of toppings—chopped green onions, cilantro, shredded cheese, avocado slices, and lime wedges are excellent options.

Conclusion

BBQ Bison Chili is a scrumptious, pack-filled meal option that you can enjoy as a hearty dinner or a finger-licking game day snack. With this recipe in your arsenal, you’ll never run out of ideas for crowd-pleasing chili. Bon appétit!
