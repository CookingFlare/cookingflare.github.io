Authentic Carne Asada Tacos Recipe


Tacos are a staple in Mexican cuisine, and among the many variations, carne asada tacos stand out as a favorite. Whether you’re having a casual get-together, hosting a fiesta, or just looking for a comforting meal to enjoy, authentic carne asada tacos are always a crowd-pleaser. The combination of tender, flavorful grilled beef, warm tortillas, and vibrant toppings make this dish a true Mexican classic. In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of making authentic carne asada tacos that will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of Mexico.

What Is Carne Asada?

Carne asada, meaning “grilled meat” in Spanish, is typically made with beef, specifically skirt steak or flank steak. The meat is marinated in a mixture of citrus, garlic, and other spices, then grilled to perfection, imparting a smoky and charred flavor that is unmatched. The thinly sliced beef is then placed inside soft corn tortillas and topped with fresh, crunchy garnishes like onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.

The beauty of carne asada lies in its simplicity. The marinade tenderizes the meat and infuses it with bold flavors, while the grilling process gives the beef a satisfying smoky edge. When combined with the freshness of toppings, it becomes a mouthwatering taco that’s easy to prepare and perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients for Carne Asada Tacos

For the Carne Asada Marinade:

  • 2 lbs flank steak or skirt steak
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
  • 1/4 cup orange juice (about 1 orange)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Tacos:

  • 12 small corn tortillas
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped white onion
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • Salsa (optional)
  • Avocado slices (optional)
  • Hot sauce (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Marinade

The first step in making authentic carne asada tacos is preparing the marinade for the steak. In a large bowl, combine lime juice, orange juice, minced garlic, cilantro, olive oil, soy sauce, apple cider vinegar, ground cumin, paprika, and chili powder. Whisk everything together until well combined. Taste the marinade and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

Step 2: Marinate the Steak

Place the flank steak or skirt steak into a resealable plastic bag or shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the steak, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, but for best results, marinate for 4-6 hours or overnight. The longer the steak marinates, the more flavorful it will become.

Step 3: Prepare the Grill

While the steak is marinating, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you’re using a charcoal grill, allow the coals to burn down to a steady, hot temperature. For gas grills, make sure the grill grates are clean and oiled to prevent sticking. If you don’t have a grill, a grill pan or cast-iron skillet will work perfectly as well.

Step 4: Grill the Steak

Once your grill is hot and ready, remove the steak from the marinade and allow any excess liquid to drip off. Place the steak on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes per side, or until the steak reaches your desired level of doneness. Skirt steak is best served medium-rare to medium, which will give you a nice balance between tenderness and flavor.

As the steak cooks, it will develop a beautiful sear on the outside while staying juicy on the inside. If you prefer a smoky flavor, don’t be afraid to let the steak get a little charred on the edges. Once done, remove the steak from the grill and allow it to rest for 5 minutes before slicing.

Step 5: Slice the Carne Asada

To ensure the best texture, slice the carne asada against the grain. This means cutting the steak perpendicular to the direction of the muscle fibers, which helps make the meat more tender. Cut the steak into thin strips that are perfect for stuffing into your tortillas.

Step 6: Warm the Tortillas

While the steak is resting, warm your corn tortillas. You can do this directly on the grill for about 20 seconds per side or heat them in a dry skillet over medium heat until they become soft and pliable. Be careful not to overheat the tortillas, as they can become too crispy and break when you try to fold them.

Step 7: Assemble the Tacos

Now that everything is ready, it’s time to assemble your tacos. Lay a warm tortilla flat on a plate, and add a generous amount of sliced carne asada in the center. Top with chopped onions and fresh cilantro. For an extra burst of flavor, squeeze a wedge of lime over the tacos. You can also add salsa, sliced avocado, and a drizzle of hot sauce if desired.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Serve your carne asada tacos with additional lime wedges and salsa on the side. These tacos are perfect for any occasion—whether it’s a family dinner or a backyard barbecue. The balance of smoky grilled meat, fresh toppings, and soft tortillas makes for an irresistible combination that will have everyone coming back for more.

Tips for the Best Carne Asada Tacos

Choose the Right Cut of Meat

The best cuts of beef for carne asada tacos are skirt steak and flank steak. Both are flavorful and tender when grilled, making them ideal for this dish. If you can’t find these cuts, other options like sirloin or rib-eye can also work, but they may not have the same depth of flavor.

Let the Meat Marinate

Marinating the carne asada is crucial for infusing the meat with flavor and ensuring it’s tender. Don’t rush this step—allow the steak to marinate for at least a couple of hours, or even better, overnight. The citrus juice in the marinade helps tenderize the meat, while the spices give it a rich and zesty flavor.

Don’t Overcook the Meat

Carne asada is best cooked medium-rare to medium. Overcooking it can result in a tough and chewy texture, so be sure to watch the cooking time carefully. Use a meat thermometer if you’re unsure about the doneness of your steak. A medium-rare steak should have an internal temperature of about 130°F, while medium will be around 140°F.

Garnish to Taste

While the traditional carne asada taco includes just onions, cilantro, and lime, don’t be afraid to get creative with your toppings. Add a spoonful of salsa, a few slices of avocado, or even a sprinkle of crumbled queso fresco. The fresh garnishes bring brightness and crunch that perfectly complement the smoky, grilled meat.

Keep the Tortillas Soft

One of the keys to making great tacos is using soft, warm tortillas. Corn tortillas are the most traditional choice for carne asada tacos, but you can also use flour tortillas if you prefer. Be sure to warm the tortillas just before assembling the tacos to ensure they stay pliable and easy to fold.

Variations of Carne Asada Tacos

While the classic carne asada taco is delicious as is, there are several variations you can try to customize the dish to your liking:

  • Carne Asada Burrito: If you’re looking for a heartier meal, use the carne asada as a filling for a burrito. Add rice, beans, and your favorite toppings to make a filling and satisfying meal.
  • Street Tacos: Serve the carne asada on smaller, soft corn tortillas with minimal toppings, just like you would find at a taco stand in Mexico.
  • Carne Asada Fries: For a fun twist, try carne asada fries by layering crispy French fries with carne asada, cheese, guacamole, and sour cream.

The Best Drinks to Pair with Carne Asada Tacos

Carne asada tacos are often enjoyed with a refreshing beverage. Here are some of the best drinks to pair with this delicious dish:

  • Mexican Beer: A cold, crisp Mexican beer like Corona, Modelo, or Pacifico is a perfect match for the smoky flavors of carne asada tacos. You can also try a michelada, which is a beer cocktail made with lime, hot sauce, and spices.
  • Agua Fresca: A sweet and fruity agua fresca made from hibiscus, watermelon, or tamarind is a refreshing non-alcoholic drink that pairs beautifully with tacos.
  • Margarita: For a festive touch, serve a classic margarita with your carne asada tacos. The tangy lime and smooth tequila complement the rich flavors of the beef perfectly.

Conclusion

Authentic carne asada tacos are a celebration of bold flavors, fresh ingredients, and simple cooking techniques. Whether you’re grilling up a batch for a special occasion or simply craving something satisfying for dinner, this recipe will help you create tacos that are as close to the real deal as you can get without hopping on a plane to Mexico. With juicy carne asada, soft tortillas, and a mix of traditional toppings, your taste buds are in for a treat!
