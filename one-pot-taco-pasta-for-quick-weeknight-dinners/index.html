One-Pot Taco Pasta for Quick Weeknight Dinners


Weeknight dinners can be challenging, especially when you’re juggling a busy schedule. Between work, school, or other commitments, it can be tough to find the time or energy to cook a wholesome, flavorful meal. That’s where the one-pot taco pasta recipe comes in. This dish is the perfect solution for a delicious, easy-to-make dinner that the whole family will love.

With bold flavors inspired by classic tacos, this recipe combines all the ingredients you love about taco night with the comfort of pasta. Plus, it’s made in just one pot, so you can enjoy a quick and easy cleanup. This recipe is not only delicious but also customizable, making it an ideal option for busy weeknights when you’re craving something hearty and satisfying.

Ingredients

To make the one-pot taco pasta, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or chicken (ground turkey also works well)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (optional, but adds great color and texture)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 packet taco seasoning (or homemade taco seasoning)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz), undrained
  • 1 can kidney beans (15 oz), drained and rinsed (optional for added protein and fiber)
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 4 cups chicken or beef broth (or water if you’re in a pinch)
  • 2 cups elbow macaroni (or any pasta shape you prefer)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend works best)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for cooking)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, salsa, sliced jalapeños, avocado

Optional Variations and Add-ins

  • Ground Protein: While ground beef is traditional, you can swap it out for chicken, turkey, or even plant-based alternatives like tofu or textured vegetable protein (TVP) for a vegetarian version.
  • Vegetables: You can also add more veggies to the mix, such as zucchini, spinach, or mushrooms for extra nutrients.
  • Spicy Kick: If you like your dishes on the spicier side, throw in a diced jalapeño pepper or a dash of hot sauce while cooking.
  • Toppings: Consider adding crushed tortilla chips for a crunchy texture or a sprinkle of lime juice to give it a fresh twist.

Instructions

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics

Start by heating a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the olive oil and allow it to warm up. Once hot, toss in the chopped onion and bell pepper (if using). Sauté the vegetables for about 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they soften and become fragrant. Add the garlic and cook for another minute, allowing it to bloom and release its flavor.

Step 2: Cook the Ground Meat

Push the sautéed vegetables to the side of the pan and add your ground beef (or protein of choice) to the center. Break it up with a wooden spoon or spatula and cook until browned and fully cooked, about 6-8 minutes. Be sure to season the meat with a pinch of salt and pepper as it cooks to enhance its flavor.

Step 3: Add the Taco Seasoning and Tomatoes

Once the meat is cooked through, sprinkle in the taco seasoning and stir it into the meat mixture. This is where the magic happens, as the seasoning adds layers of bold, aromatic flavors to the dish. Pour in the canned diced tomatoes with their juices, and stir everything to combine. The acidity from the tomatoes balances out the richness of the meat, creating a well-rounded base for the pasta.

Step 4: Add the Pasta and Broth

Now, it’s time to add the pasta and liquid. Pour in the uncooked pasta and chicken or beef broth (or water), making sure the pasta is submerged in the liquid. Bring everything to a simmer, stirring occasionally to prevent the pasta from sticking to the bottom of the pan. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the skillet, and let it simmer for about 12-15 minutes or until the pasta is al dente and the liquid has mostly been absorbed.

Step 5: Stir in the Beans and Corn

Once the pasta is cooked, stir in the drained and rinsed beans and the corn. These ingredients not only add more flavor but also provide additional texture and nutritional benefits. Stir everything well to ensure the beans and corn are evenly distributed throughout the dish.

Step 6: Melt the Cheese

Turn off the heat and sprinkle the shredded cheese over the top of the pasta. Cover the skillet again and let the cheese melt for about 3-5 minutes. The result should be a wonderfully cheesy, gooey topping that binds everything together.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

Once the cheese has melted, give the dish a final stir to mix in the gooey cheese. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro for a burst of freshness. If desired, serve with optional toppings like sour cream, salsa, avocado, or a squeeze of lime juice for an extra layer of flavor.

Why One-Pot Taco Pasta Is Perfect for Busy Weeknights

Quick and Easy

The best part about this recipe is its simplicity. With minimal prep time and only one pot to clean, you can have a flavorful, comforting dinner ready in just 30 minutes. This makes it the perfect choice for hectic weeknights when you don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen.

Customizable for All Diets

This recipe is incredibly versatile and can be tailored to suit a wide variety of diets. Whether you’re looking for a low-carb option, a vegetarian meal, or a dish with added vegetables, this taco pasta can be modified to meet your preferences. You can swap the meat for a plant-based protein, add extra veggies, or even make it dairy-free by omitting the cheese.

Minimal Cleanup

Since this dish is made in one pot, you won’t have a sink full of dirty dishes waiting for you after dinner. Simply sauté the ingredients, add the pasta and broth, and let everything cook together in one pan. After dinner, cleanup is quick and easy, which is a huge bonus on busy nights.

Kid-Friendly and Family-Approved

Taco pasta is a hit with kids, and it’s a great way to sneak in some extra vegetables and protein without anyone noticing. The mild flavors of the taco seasoning and cheese make it appealing to even picky eaters. Plus, the customizable toppings give everyone the freedom to build their own perfect plate.

Great for Meal Prep

If you’re planning ahead for the week, this one-pot taco pasta is a fantastic option for meal prep. You can make a larger batch and store leftovers in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. The flavors even improve after a day or two, making it an excellent choice for preparing meals in advance. It’s also a great dish to pack for lunches!

Tips for Perfect One-Pot Taco Pasta

  • Stir the pasta regularly to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pot. You can also add a splash of water or broth if the mixture starts to look too dry during cooking.
  • Taste as you go – The taco seasoning can vary in saltiness and spice, so adjust the seasoning to your taste as you cook. If you like things spicier, add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a few dashes of hot sauce.
  • Use a sturdy pot or skillet with a tight-fitting lid to prevent the liquid from evaporating too quickly, which could result in undercooked pasta.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. You can reheat it on the stovetop over low heat, adding a little broth to loosen the pasta if needed.

Conclusion

This one-pot taco pasta is the ultimate weeknight dinner solution for busy families, offering a quick, easy, and flavorful meal with minimal cleanup. Whether you’re a taco fan or simply looking for a new way to enjoy pasta, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. Its versatility allows you to customize it based on your preferences, making it a go-to recipe that will never get old. So next time you’re searching for a dinner that’s both comforting and simple, give this taco pasta a try—you won’t be disappointed!

Enjoy your dinner!
