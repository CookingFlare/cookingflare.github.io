Pudin de Pan: Spanish-Style Bread Pudding


¡Bienvenidos a mi cocina! Today, I want to share with you a delightful and comforting dessert that will transport you straight to the sunny streets of Spain. Pudin de Pan, or Spanish-Style Bread Pudding, is a traditional dessert that has been enjoyed by generations. Made with simple ingredients and a touch of Spanish flair, this dessert is sure to become a favorite in your home.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups of stale bread, torn into small pieces
  • 4 cups of milk
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Pinch of salt
  • Caramel sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the torn bread pieces and milk. Let it sit for about 15 minutes, allowing the bread to soak up the milk and become soft.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, lemon zest, and a pinch of salt until well combined.

  4. Pour the egg mixture over the bread and milk mixture, gently stirring until everything is evenly coated. Let it sit for another 10 minutes, allowing the bread to absorb the flavors.

  5. Carefully transfer the mixture into the greased baking dish, making sure to spread it out evenly.

  6. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 40-45 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the pudding is set. To check if it’s done, insert a toothpick into the center of the pudding – if it comes out clean, it’s ready!

  7. Remove the pudding from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving. If desired, drizzle caramel sauce over the top for an extra touch of sweetness.

  8. Serve warm or at room temperature. Pudin de Pan is delicious on its own, but you can also enjoy it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an indulgent treat.

Tips and Variations

  • Stale bread works best for this recipe as it absorbs the milk more easily. If you don’t have stale bread, you can cut fresh bread into small cubes and let them dry out for a few hours.

  • Experiment with different types of bread to add unique flavors and textures to your pudding. Traditional Spanish bread, like brioche or baguette, works wonderfully.

  • To enhance the flavors even more, you can add a handful of raisins or chopped nuts to the pudding mixture before baking.

  • If you’re feeling adventurous, try adding a splash of Spanish sherry or rum to the egg mixture for an adult twist.

  • Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to three days. Simply reheat in the microwave or enjoy chilled.

Conclusion

Pudin de Pan is a classic Spanish dessert that is both simple and satisfying. With its rich flavors and comforting texture, this bread pudding will surely become a staple in your dessert repertoire. Whether you enjoy it on a cozy winter evening or as a sweet treat during summertime, Pudin de Pan is a delightful way to indulge in the flavors of Spain.

So gather your ingredients, embrace the warmth of the oven, and savor the taste of this traditional Spanish treat. ¡Buen provecho!
