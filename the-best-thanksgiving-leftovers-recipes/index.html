The Best Thanksgiving Leftovers Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering around the table with loved ones, reveling in delicious dishes, and creating cherished memories. But once the feast is over, there’s usually an abundance of leftovers waiting to be tackled—and that’s where the magic happens. Thanksgiving leftovers are the culinary gift that keeps on giving, and with some creativity, those scraps can transform into meals just as memorable as the main event itself. Whether you’re staring at mountains of turkey, cranberry sauce, stuffing, or mashed potatoes, there’s a world of options to explore beyond reheating the same plate. Below, you’ll find innovative recipes to make the most out of your Thanksgiving leftovers.

Glorious Turkey Soup

Turkey tends to take center stage on Thanksgiving, and its leftovers can be reinvented in a hearty soup that warms both the heart and the belly. Start by simmering the leftover turkey bones in water with a few aromatic vegetables like onions, celery, and carrots for the perfect homemade turkey broth. Strain the liquid, add shredded turkey meat, and toss in your favorite veggies or even leftover green beans. For added texture, mix in cooked rice, egg noodles, or barley to complete the dish. Season with thyme, rosemary, and a touch of garlic for an aromatic delight that’ll make you forget you’re eating leftovers.

Stuffing-Stuffed Mushrooms

Stuffing often ends up as the sole survivor of a Thanksgiving meal, barely touched and overlooked. Why not turn it into an irresistible appetizer or snack? Repurpose your stuffing as the filling for mushroom caps. Simply scoop out the stems from large button or portobello mushrooms, stuff each cap with your leftover stuffing, and bake until golden brown. For added flair, sprinkle some shredded cheese over the top and finish with a drizzle of balsamic glaze. These stuffing-stuffed mushrooms make for a bold, flavor-packed bite that’s perfect for entertaining guests.

Mashed Potato Pancakes

Mashed potatoes are a Thanksgiving staple that stick around long after the turkey is gone. Reinvent them into crispy, golden-brown pancakes that work as a breakfast or side dish. Combine your leftover mashed potatoes with flour, a beaten egg, and a dash of salt. Shape the mixture into small patties and fry until browned on both sides. For a savory twist, mix in shredded cheddar cheese, chopped green onions, or crumbled bacon before cooking. Serve them alongside scrambled eggs or a dollop of sour cream to elevate your brunch game.

Cranberry Sauce Oatmeal Bars

Turn your cranberry sauce into a dessert worth savoring. These sweet and tangy oatmeal bars are an ideal midday snack or post-dinner treat. Prepare a simple streusel crust using butter, brown sugar, rolled oats, and flour. Spread the leftover cranberry sauce over the crust, and top with additional streusel before baking until golden and firm. Cut into bars once cooled—these delicious treats are portable, easy to make, and showcase Thanksgiving flavors in a whole new way.

Turkey Pot Pie

Transform your leftover turkey and vegetables into an irresistible turkey pot pie. Start by creating a creamy filling using shredded turkey, leftover green beans, diced carrots, and gravy. Pour the mixture into a baking dish or individual ramekins and top with a flaky pie crust (store-bought or homemade). Bake until golden and bubbly for a dish that’s hearty, comforting, and perfect for cool autumn nights. This recipe is equally customizable—swap the pie crust for biscuit dough or puff pastry if you prefer a fluffier topping.

Stuffing Waffles

Your waffle maker can surprisingly come to the rescue in a Thanksgiving leftovers predicament. Repurpose stuffing into crispy, savory waffles that are ideal as a standalone snack or base for other toppings. Press your stuffing into the heated waffle iron and cook until crispy and golden. Top the stuffing waffle with a fried egg and drizzle with turkey gravy for a playful breakfast-inspired dish, or layer it with shredded turkey and cranberry sauce for a leftover sandwich upgrade.

Turkey and Cranberry Quesadilla

Take a Tex-Mex approach to your Thanksgiving leftovers with a turkey and cranberry quesadilla. Layer shredded turkey, leftover cranberry sauce, and shredded cheese (like Monterey Jack or cheddar) between two flour tortillas. Cook in a hot skillet until the cheese melts and the tortilla crisps up. Serve with a dollop of sour cream or guacamole for dipping. The sweet and savory combination will keep you coming back for more.

Sweet Potato and Turkey Hash

Leftover sweet potatoes can shine in a breakfast hash packed with flavor and texture. In a skillet, sauté diced sweet potatoes, shredded turkey, and onions with olive oil until everything caramelizes. Add in your favorite spices, such as smoked paprika or chili powder, for an extra kick. Toss in some chopped kale, spinach, or leftover green beans for added color and nutrients. Top the finished hash with a fried egg to complete the dish.

Turkey Fried Rice

Thanksgiving meets takeout night with this clever recipe. Repurpose your turkey, leftover vegetables, and even cranberry sauce into a quick turkey fried rice. Start by stir-frying your carb fix—rice works best, though leftover quinoa or farro is equally delightful—with chopped turkey, peas, carrots, and onions. Add soy sauce and sesame oil for a signature fried rice flavor and mix in the cranberry sauce for a sweet and tangy twist. It’s a speedy dish that’s perfect for lunch or dinner in a pinch.

Pumpkin Pie Milkshake

Pumpkin pie is undoubtedly one of the most iconic Thanksgiving desserts, but leftover slices don’t need to stay confined to the plate. Blend your pumpkin pie—crust and all—with vanilla ice cream and milk for an indulgent milkshake that feels as festive as the Thanksgiving feast itself. Sprinkle with crushed graham crackers or a dollop of whipped cream to fully embrace the holiday vibes in a glass. This simple yet decadent treat is the epitome of dessert bliss.

Green Bean Casserole Grilled Cheese

Leftover green bean casserole can morph into a gooey, irresistible grilled cheese sandwich. Butter two slices of bread, place a generous layer of casserole on one side, and add a few slices of mozzarella or cheddar. Cook in a skillet until the bread is toasted and the cheese melts. The combination of creamy casserole and cheese creates a perfect texture, while the toasted bread adds a satisfying crunch to every bite.

Thanksgiving Leftovers Shepherd’s Pie

Bring all your leftovers together for a cohesive dish by creating a Thanksgiving-inspired shepherd’s pie. Use mashed potatoes as the topping, turkey as the protein, and mix the remaining gravy and veggies for the base. Layer everything in a baking dish, top with a sprinkle of shredded cheese, and bake until bubbling. This recipe uniquely captures the essence of Thanksgiving in every spoonful.

Turkey and Sweet Potato Tacos

Leftovers make for remarkable taco fillings, especially when paired with roasted sweet potatoes. Start by warming up shredded turkey and diced sweet potatoes, then season them with cumin, paprika, and a squeeze of lime juice. Fill soft tortillas with the mixture, and garnish with avocado slices, crumbled feta, and a drizzle of cranberry sauce. These inventive tacos are easy to assemble and perfect for using up multiple leftovers at once.

Thanksgiving leftovers shouldn’t feel boring or uninspired. With these creative recipes, you’ll turn your fridge full of scraps into culinary masterpieces that keep the holiday spirit alive. From savory dishes like turkey pot pie and stuffing waffles to sweet treats like cranberry oatmeal bars, the possibilities are endless. It’s time to embrace the Thanksgiving leftovers journey and give your extra ingredients the spotlight they deserve.
