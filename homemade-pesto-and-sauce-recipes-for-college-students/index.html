Homemade Pesto and Sauce Recipes for College Students


As a college student, cooking can often feel like a daunting task, especially when you’re juggling classes, assignments, and a social life. However, with the right recipes, you can create delicious and satisfying meals right in your dorm room or apartment. One of the best ways to elevate a simple dish is by adding a flavorful homemade sauce, and today, we’re going to explore some easy and budget-friendly homemade pesto and sauce recipes that are perfect for busy college students.

Classic Basil Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the basil leaves, pine nuts, and garlic cloves. Pulse until coarsely chopped.
  2. Add the grated Parmesan cheese and pulse again.
  3. While the food processor is running, slowly pour in the olive oil until the mixture is smooth and well combined.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Tips:

  • Use this classic basil pesto as a pasta sauce, spread it on sandwiches, or drizzle it over grilled chicken or fish for a burst of flavor.

Creamy Avocado Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup unsalted roasted sunflower seeds
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Scoop the flesh of the avocados into a food processor.
  2. Add the basil leaves, sunflower seeds, garlic, and lemon juice.
  3. Pulse until the ingredients are combined.
  4. With the food processor running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the mixture is creamy.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Tips:

  • This creamy avocado pesto is a great alternative to traditional pesto and can be used as a dip for veggies, a spread for sandwiches, or a sauce for zucchini noodles.

Marinara Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (28 ounces) whole peeled tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Crush the whole peeled tomatoes with your hands and add them to the skillet, along with their juices.
  5. Stir in the dried oregano and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Simmer the sauce for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it has thickened.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

Tips:

  • This marinara sauce is perfect for topping spaghetti, making homemade pizza, or serving as a dipping sauce for mozzarella sticks.

Spicy Peanut Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup warm water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, sriracha, and minced garlic.
  2. Gradually add the warm water, whisking until the sauce reaches your desired consistency.

Tips:

  • Use this spicy peanut sauce as a dip for spring rolls, a dressing for cold noodle salads, or a marinade for grilled tofu or chicken.

Conclusion

With these homemade pesto and sauce recipes, college students can easily elevate their meals without breaking the bank or spending hours in the kitchen. Experiment with different ingredients and flavor combinations to create your own signature sauces that will impress your friends and make your dorm room or apartment feel like a five-star restaurant. Happy cooking!
