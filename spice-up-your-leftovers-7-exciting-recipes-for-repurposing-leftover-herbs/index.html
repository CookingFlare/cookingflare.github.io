Spice Up Your Leftovers: 7 Exciting Recipes for Repurposing Leftover Herbs


Have you ever found yourself with a surplus of herbs after cooking a delicious meal? Instead of letting them go to waste, why not repurpose them into exciting and flavorful dishes? Herbs are a versatile ingredient that can add a burst of freshness and aroma to your leftover meals. In this blog post, we’ll explore seven creative recipes that will help you spice up your leftovers using those leftover herbs. Let’s dive right in!

1. Herb-Infused Oil

One of the easiest ways to repurpose leftover herbs is by infusing them into oil. Simply gather your leftover herbs, such as rosemary, thyme, or basil, and combine them with olive oil in a jar. Let the herbs steep in the oil for a few days to allow the flavors to meld together. This herb-infused oil can be used as a dressing for salads, a marinade for meats, or a flavorful drizzle for roasted vegetables.

2. Herb Butter

If you have leftover butter and herbs, why not combine them to create a delicious herb butter? Chop up your herbs finely and mix them into softened butter. You can experiment with different herb combinations, such as parsley and chives or dill and lemon zest. Herb butter can be spread on bread, melted over grilled meats, or tossed with hot pasta for a quick and flavorful meal.

3. Herb Pesto

Pesto is a classic sauce that can be made with various herbs. Instead of using traditional basil, try using your leftover herbs to create a unique and vibrant pesto. Blend your herbs with garlic, nuts, Parmesan cheese, and olive oil until smooth. This herb pesto can be tossed with pasta, spread on sandwiches, or used as a flavorful dip for bread or vegetables.

4. Herb Salt

Herb salt is a fantastic way to preserve the flavors of your leftover herbs. Simply combine your herbs with coarse salt and let the mixture dry out for a few days. Once the herbs have dried, give them a quick blitz in a food processor or mortar and pestle to create a fragrant herb salt. This herb salt can be sprinkled over roasted vegetables, grilled meats, or even used as a seasoning for popcorn.

5. Herb-infused Vinegar

Similar to herb-infused oil, herb-infused vinegar is a fantastic way to add a burst of flavor to your dishes. Combine your leftover herbs with white wine vinegar or apple cider vinegar and let the mixture sit for a few days. The vinegar will take on the vibrant flavors of the herbs, making it a wonderful addition to dressings, marinades, or even homemade pickles.

6. Herb Frittata

Leftover herbs can be a fantastic addition to a frittata, a versatile and easy-to-make egg dish. Whisk together eggs, milk, cheese, and your chopped leftover herbs. Pour the mixture into a greased baking dish and bake until set. A herb frittata can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, and is a great way to use up any other leftover ingredients you may have.

7. Herb Ice Cubes

Last but not least, herb ice cubes are a fun and creative way to preserve your leftover herbs. Chop your herbs and place them into ice cube trays. Fill the trays with water or broth and freeze until solid. These herb ice cubes can be added to soups, stews, or sauces to enhance the flavors and add a pop of freshness.

With these seven exciting recipes, you can transform your leftover herbs into delicious and flavorful dishes. Don’t let those precious herbs go to waste – get creative and experiment with different combinations to discover new and exciting flavors. Whether you choose to infuse them into oils, create herb-infused vinegars, or make herb butter, there are endless possibilities for repurposing your leftover herbs. So next time you find yourself with a surplus of herbs, remember these recipes and spice up your leftovers with ease!
