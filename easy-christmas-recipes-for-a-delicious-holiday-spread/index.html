Easy Christmas Recipes for a Delicious Holiday Spread


The holiday season is the perfect time to gather with family and friends to celebrate, and what better way to create lasting memories than with a delicious spread of food? From savory dishes to sweet treats, Christmas meals can be as festive as they are flavorful. If you’re looking to make your holiday gatherings extra special without spending hours in the kitchen, we’ve got you covered. Here are some easy Christmas recipes that will help you create a delicious holiday spread everyone will love.

1. Classic Roast Turkey with Herb Butter

A Christmas dinner isn’t complete without a golden, juicy turkey at the center of the table. This simple roast turkey recipe, enhanced with a savory herb butter, will be the star of your spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (about 12-14 lbs)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 cups chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Pat the turkey dry with paper towels. Season the inside of the turkey with salt and pepper. Place the quartered onion inside the cavity.
  3. In a bowl, combine the softened butter, rosemary, thyme, parsley, garlic, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  4. Gently loosen the skin of the turkey and rub half of the herb butter mixture under the skin. Rub the remaining butter mixture on top of the skin.
  5. Tie the turkey legs together with kitchen twine and tuck the wings under the bird.
  6. Place the turkey on a roasting rack in a large roasting pan. Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the pan.
  7. Roast the turkey, basting occasionally with the pan juices, for about 3 to 3.5 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  8. Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving. Serve with the pan drippings for extra flavor.

This herb-infused turkey will be juicy, flavorful, and sure to impress your guests.

2. Cranberry Sauce with Orange and Ginger

No Christmas spread is complete without a tangy, refreshing cranberry sauce. This version adds a twist with the bright flavors of orange and a hint of ginger.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, peeled and grated
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine all the ingredients: cranberries, sugar, orange juice, orange zest, grated ginger, and water.
  2. Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Stir occasionally as the cranberries begin to burst and the sauce thickens, about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Once the sauce has reached a jam-like consistency, remove from heat and let it cool.
  4. Transfer to a serving dish and refrigerate until ready to serve.

This cranberry sauce has just the right balance of sweet, tart, and aromatic flavors, making it the perfect complement to your turkey or ham.

3. Garlic Mashed Potatoes with Parmesan

Creamy mashed potatoes are a holiday staple. This garlic and Parmesan variation takes the classic side dish to the next level, making it rich, comforting, and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Place the potatoes and garlic in a large pot. Cover with water and bring to a boil. Cook until the potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  2. While the potatoes cook, heat the heavy cream and butter in a small saucepan over low heat until the butter is melted.
  3. Drain the potatoes and garlic, then return them to the pot. Mash the potatoes until smooth and creamy.
  4. Gradually stir in the warm cream and butter mixture, then add the Parmesan cheese. Continue mashing until the potatoes are fluffy and smooth.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and serve warm.

These mashed potatoes are the perfect side to your turkey, ham, or any holiday main dish.

4. Honey Glazed Carrots

These honey-glazed carrots are sweet, savory, and super easy to make. The glaze gives them a beautiful shine and a rich, comforting flavor that pairs wonderfully with any holiday meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the carrots and sauté for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Drizzle in the honey, sprinkle with fresh thyme, and season with salt and pepper. Stir to coat the carrots evenly.
  4. Continue cooking for another 5-7 minutes, or until the carrots are tender and glazed.
  5. Serve warm, garnished with additional thyme if desired.

These honey-glazed carrots are the perfect side dish for a festive dinner, offering a beautiful burst of color and flavor to your spread.

5. Stuffing with Sausage and Sage

Stuffing is a classic holiday side dish, and this version with sausage and sage is packed with flavor. It’s savory, slightly spicy, and herbaceous, making it a perfect complement to turkey or roast beef.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of crusty bread, cut into cubes
  • 1 lb sausage (Italian or breakfast sausage)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned, breaking it up as it cooks.
  3. Add the onion, celery, and garlic to the skillet. Cook for 5 minutes until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Stir in the fresh sage and cook for another minute. Remove from heat.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the sausage mixture with the cubed bread. Pour in the chicken broth and melted butter, and mix until everything is evenly coated.
  6. Transfer the stuffing to a greased baking dish. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes to crisp the top.
  7. Serve warm.

This sausage and sage stuffing is aromatic, hearty, and the perfect side dish to round out your holiday meal.

6. Chocolate Yule Log Cake

For dessert, the Yule Log is a holiday classic that will impress your guests with its festive appearance and delicious chocolate flavor. This easy version is a light sponge cake rolled with rich chocolate ganache for a decadent treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of chocolate cake mix
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 oz semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line a jelly roll pan with parchment paper.
  2. Prepare the chocolate cake mix according to the package instructions, adding the oil, eggs, and water.
  3. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly.
  4. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. While the cake is baking, prepare the ganache. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan over medium heat until it starts to simmer. Pour the hot cream over the chopped chocolate and butter. Let sit for 5 minutes, then stir until smooth.
  6. Once the cake has cooled for a few minutes, place a clean kitchen towel on a flat surface and dust it with powdered sugar. Carefully invert the cake onto the towel and remove the parchment paper.
  7. While the cake is still warm, roll it up tightly from one short side, using the towel to help. Let it cool completely.
  8. Once cooled, unroll the cake, spread the ganache inside, and then reroll the cake. Spread the remaining ganache on top and dust with powdered sugar.

This rich, chocolatey Yule Log is sure to be a hit and will add a touch of elegance to your holiday spread.

Conclusion

With these easy Christmas recipes, you can create a memorable holiday feast that’s full of flavor and festive cheer. Whether you’re preparing a classic roast turkey, a decadent Yule Log cake, or simple but delicious sides like garlic mashed potatoes and honey-glazed carrots, these dishes are sure to impress your guests and make your holiday celebrations even more special. Enjoy the season, indulge in these flavorful dishes, and savor the time spent with loved ones around the table!
