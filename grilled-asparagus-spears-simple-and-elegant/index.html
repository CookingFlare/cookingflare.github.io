Grilled Asparagus Spears: Simple and Elegant


An elegant side dish that requires minimal effort, grilling asparagus emphasizes their natural nutty flavor and tender-crisp texture. There is no better way to delight your family and friends than with this simple, yet exquisite dish. Read on to discover the ease of creating a gourmet meal at home.

Preparation for Grilling Asparagus

To prepare your asparagus for grilling, follow these steps:

  1. Wash and Pat Dry: Rinse each asparagus spear under running water, then pat them dry to remove excess moisture. This prevents them from steaming rather than caramelizing on the grill.

  2. Trim the Ends: Although you can snap the ends off asparagus or use a knife to cut them, it can be helpful to utilize a vegetable peeler for a less messy approach. Hold the base of the asparagus with one hand, and slowly run the peeler against the stalk’s side, and the tip will fall off.

  3. Marination: Even if you are not using any marinade other than olive oil and seasoning, a brief soak in the liquid can help infuse the flavor deeper into your spears. I recommend the following simple marinade below:

    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Optional: garlic cloves, minced; Dijon mustard, thyme, or balsamic vinegar.

  4. Soak in Water: To ensure a consistent cook, soaking asparagus spears in water for a little while before grilling is a helpful step. This traps moisture in the spears that prevents charring when grilling.

Grilling Asparagus Spears

Now that your asparagus spears are prepped, grilling them is as effortless as:

  1. Heat the Grill: Preheat your grill (gas or charcoal) to medium-high heat. Remember, the optimal temperature is crucial to prevent the spears from burning or remaining raw inside.

  2. Lay the Asparagus: Laying the asparagus up on the grate will improve the chances of a uniform cook. You can also place a grill pan inside to protect your asparagus from burning and facilitate even cooking.

  3. No Need to-peel or devein: Unlike shrimp, asparagus spears don’t require a “vein” to remove. Peeling the ends of asparagus covers that notion.

  4. Turn and Baste: Every few minutes, flip your asparagus spears evenly and brush them with your marinade, ensuring the exteriors get a chance to caramelize and char while the interiors remain tender.

  5. Ready when Tender: Asparagus spears will take around 8-12 minutes to cook, depending on thickness, and they’ll be ready when they reach this tender but still slightly crunchy texture.

Tips for Presentation and Variations

  1. Serve Cold: If you fancy a refreshing side dish, grill your asparagus and cool them in ice water or a refrigerator for a simple cold serving.

  2. Easy upgrades: Don’t hesitate to pimp your asparagus dish by garnishing them with your favorite nut or cheese. Goat cheese, shaved parmesan, and candied almonds are delicious ways to add depth.

Grilled asparagus is undoubtedly a restaurant-quality dish that you can make in your own kitchen. Serve them alongside grilled chicken or fish for a perfect light meal, or as a simple side for a celebratory dinner. Bon appétit!
