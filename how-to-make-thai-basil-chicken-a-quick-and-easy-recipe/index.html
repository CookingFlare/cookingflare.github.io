How to Make Thai Basil Chicken: A Quick and Easy Recipe


If you’re craving a flavorful, savory, and aromatic dish that’s easy to prepare, Thai Basil Chicken (known as Gai Pad Krapow) is the perfect solution. This popular Thai street food is packed with bold flavors, thanks to the perfect balance of spicy, sweet, and savory ingredients. In this blog post, we’ll show you how to make this vibrant dish quickly and easily, ensuring that you can enjoy it at home even on the busiest days.

What is Thai Basil Chicken?

Thai Basil Chicken is a flavorful stir-fry dish that combines minced chicken with fresh Thai basil, garlic, chilies, and a savory sauce. It’s often served with a side of rice, and sometimes topped with a fried egg, making it a comforting yet aromatic meal. The unique blend of herbs and spices makes this dish a standout in Thai cuisine, bringing a warm and fragrant experience to your taste buds.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this dish at home, you’ll need to gather a few key ingredients. Don’t worry, most of them are easy to find at your local grocery store or an Asian market. Here’s what you need:

  • Chicken: Typically, boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs work best. You can mince the chicken by hand or use a food processor.
  • Thai Basil: This herb is essential to the dish, and it’s what gives it that distinct aroma and flavor. It’s different from regular sweet basil, so if you can find it, don’t substitute.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic cloves provide an incredible depth of flavor.
  • Chilies: Thai bird’s eye chilies are the traditional choice, giving the dish its signature spiciness. If you prefer a milder version, you can use regular red or green chilies.
  • Onion: A small amount of onion adds a bit of sweetness and texture to the dish.
  • Fish Sauce: This is a must for authentic Thai flavor. Fish sauce provides a salty, umami-rich element.
  • Soy Sauce: For added depth and color.
  • Sugar: A little bit of sugar balances out the savory and spicy flavors.
  • Oyster Sauce: Adds a rich, slightly sweet flavor to the sauce.
  • Vegetable Oil: For frying.
  • Rice: Jasmine rice is the preferred choice for serving.

How to Make Thai Basil Chicken

Making Thai Basil Chicken at home is surprisingly simple and quick. The key to a great result is to prep all your ingredients ahead of time so you can stir-fry the dish quickly and efficiently. Follow the step-by-step instructions below to make this mouth-watering dish:

Step 1: Prep the Ingredients

Start by preparing all your ingredients:

  • Mince the chicken into small pieces using a sharp knife or food processor.
  • Peel and finely chop the garlic.
  • Slice the chilies into small rounds.
  • Slice the onion thinly.
  • Pick the leaves off the Thai basil stems, making sure to use only the leaves for the dish.

Having everything prepped and ready to go is important, as stir-frying happens quickly!

Step 2: Make the Sauce

In a small bowl, combine the fish sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar. Stir the mixture until the sugar dissolves. Set the sauce aside for later.

Step 3: Cook the Chicken

Heat a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of vegetable oil to the pan and wait for it to get hot.

Once the oil is heated, add the minced garlic and chopped chilies. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until the garlic becomes fragrant. Be careful not to burn it!

Add the minced chicken to the pan. Use a spatula to break it apart and cook it evenly. Stir the chicken continuously to ensure that it cooks through and turns golden brown. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

Step 4: Add the Onion and Sauce

Once the chicken is cooked, add the sliced onions to the pan and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the onions become tender and slightly caramelized.

Pour the pre-made sauce mixture over the chicken and onions. Stir everything together so that the chicken is evenly coated in the sauce. Let the sauce simmer for about 1-2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 5: Add the Basil

Now it’s time to add the star ingredient: the Thai basil. Turn the heat off and stir the basil leaves into the dish. The residual heat will wilt the basil slightly, releasing its aromatic oils into the chicken.

Give everything a final mix to ensure the basil is distributed throughout.

Step 6: Serve

Serve the Thai Basil Chicken hot over a bed of freshly steamed jasmine rice. For an extra touch, you can top the dish with a fried egg, which is a common addition in Thailand.

The egg adds richness to the dish, balancing out the heat from the chilies and the saltiness from the sauce. If you’re not into eggs, don’t worry – the dish is perfect without it as well!

Optional Add-ins

While the traditional version of this dish is delicious on its own, you can also customize it to your liking by adding:

  • Vegetables: Add bell peppers, zucchini, or green beans for a more substantial meal.
  • Mushrooms: Sautéed mushrooms make a great addition and add extra umami flavor.
  • Other proteins: While chicken is the most common protein in this dish, you can easily swap it for ground pork, beef, or tofu for a vegetarian version.

Tips for the Best Thai Basil Chicken

  1. Use Fresh Thai Basil: While regular basil can be used in a pinch, using fresh Thai basil is what makes this dish special. It has a peppery, anise-like flavor that’s essential to achieving the authentic taste.
  2. Adjust the Spice Level: If you’re not a fan of spicy food, you can reduce the number of chilies or use a milder variety. On the other hand, if you love heat, feel free to add extra chilies to suit your taste.
  3. Don’t Overcook the Basil: Thai basil is delicate, so be sure to add it at the end and let the residual heat wilt it gently. Overcooking the basil can cause it to lose its aromatic flavor.
  4. Serve with Fried Egg: For an authentic touch, serve your Thai Basil Chicken with a fried egg on top. The richness of the egg pairs perfectly with the spicy and savory chicken.
  5. Make it a Meal: You can make this a complete meal by adding a side of stir-fried vegetables or a fresh cucumber salad to balance out the heat.

Why You’ll Love This Dish

Thai Basil Chicken is incredibly satisfying, combining tender chicken with bold flavors and the delightful aroma of basil. It’s a perfect balance of spicy, salty, and savory with a hint of sweetness. The quick cooking time makes it an ideal option for busy nights when you want something flavorful yet easy to prepare. Plus, it’s versatile – you can adjust the ingredients based on what you have at home or your dietary preferences.

The best part? It’s made with simple ingredients that are easy to find, and the cooking process doesn’t require much expertise. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or friends, this dish will impress everyone with its delicious flavors.

Conclusion

Now that you know how to make Thai Basil Chicken, it’s time to try your hand at this tasty and satisfying dish. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a flavorful meal that’s perfect for lunch or dinner. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire.

So next time you’re looking for a quick, easy, and delicious dinner, whip up this Thai Basil Chicken recipe, and enjoy the vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine right in the comfort of your own kitchen!
