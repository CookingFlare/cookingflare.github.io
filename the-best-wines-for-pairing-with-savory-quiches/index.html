The Best Wines for Pairing with Savory Quiches


Quiche, a classic French dish, has become a beloved staple in kitchens around the world. Its versatility allows for a myriad of fillings, from rich cheeses and savory meats to fresh vegetables and aromatic herbs. The creamy custard base, combined with a flaky crust, creates a delightful texture that pairs beautifully with a variety of wines. In this post, we will explore the best wines to complement savory quiches, enhancing your dining experience and elevating your culinary creations.

Understanding Quiche

Before diving into wine pairings, it’s essential to understand what makes quiche so special. Traditionally, quiche consists of a pastry crust filled with a mixture of eggs, cream, and various ingredients. The most famous version, Quiche Lorraine, features crispy bacon and Gruyère cheese. However, the beauty of quiche lies in its adaptability. You can create a quiche with almost any combination of ingredients, making it suitable for breakfast, brunch, lunch, or dinner.

Common Ingredients in Savory Quiches

  1. Cheeses: Cheddar, goat cheese, feta, and Gruyère are popular choices.
  2. Vegetables: Spinach, mushrooms, bell peppers, and leeks add flavor and texture.
  3. Meats: Bacon, ham, and smoked salmon are often included for a savory touch.
  4. Herbs and Spices: Fresh herbs like thyme, chives, and parsley enhance the overall flavor profile.

The Art of Wine Pairing

Wine pairing is an art that involves balancing flavors, aromas, and textures. The goal is to enhance both the wine and the dish, creating a harmonious experience. When it comes to quiche, the richness of the custard and the variety of fillings can influence your choice of wine. Here are some key factors to consider when selecting the perfect wine for your savory quiche:

  1. Acidity: Wines with higher acidity can cut through the richness of the custard, providing a refreshing contrast.
  2. Body: The weight of the wine should match the dish. Lighter quiches pair well with lighter wines, while heartier quiches can stand up to fuller-bodied options.
  3. Flavor Profile: Consider the dominant flavors in your quiche. A wine that complements these flavors will enhance the overall experience.

Best Wines for Pairing with Savory Quiches

1. Chardonnay

Chardonnay is a classic choice for pairing with quiche, particularly those with creamy fillings. The wine’s buttery texture and notes of apple, pear, and citrus complement the richness of the custard.

  • Best Pairings: Quiche Lorraine, spinach and feta quiche, or any quiche with a creamy cheese.
  • Serving Tip: Opt for a lightly oaked Chardonnay to avoid overwhelming the dish with too much woodiness.

2. Sauvignon Blanc

Sauvignon Blanc is known for its crisp acidity and vibrant flavors, making it an excellent match for vegetable-based quiches. The wine’s herbal notes can enhance the freshness of the ingredients.

  • Best Pairings: Asparagus and goat cheese quiche, tomato and basil quiche, or any quiche featuring fresh herbs.
  • Serving Tip: Serve chilled to accentuate its refreshing qualities.

3. Pinot Grigio

Pinot Grigio is a light and zesty white wine that pairs well with a variety of quiches. Its bright acidity and fruity notes make it a versatile option for both meat and vegetable fillings.

  • Best Pairings: Mushroom and Swiss quiche, ham and cheese quiche, or a quiche with mixed vegetables.
  • Serving Tip: Enjoy it well-chilled to enhance its crispness.

4. Riesling

Riesling, especially in its dry form, offers a unique balance of sweetness and acidity. This wine can complement the savory flavors of quiche while providing a refreshing contrast.

  • Best Pairings: Quiche with smoked salmon, spinach and ricotta quiche, or quiche with caramelized onions.
  • Serving Tip: Serve slightly chilled to bring out its aromatic qualities.

5. Sparkling Wine

Sparkling wine, such as Champagne or Prosecco, adds a celebratory touch to any meal. The effervescence cuts through the richness of quiche, making it a delightful pairing.

  • Best Pairings: Any quiche, particularly those served at brunch, such as a classic Quiche Lorraine or a vegetable quiche.
  • Serving Tip: Serve well-chilled to enhance the refreshing bubbles.

6. Light Red Wines

While white wines are often the go-to for quiche, certain light red wines can also work beautifully. Look for wines with low tannins and bright fruit flavors.

  • Best Pairings: Quiche with bacon or ham, or a quiche featuring roasted red peppers.
