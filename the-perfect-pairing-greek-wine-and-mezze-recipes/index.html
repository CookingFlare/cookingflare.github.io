The Perfect Pairing: Greek Wine and Mezze Recipes


When it comes to the culinary delights of Greece, few things can match the sheer joy of savoring a selection of mezze paired with a glass of exquisite Greek wine. This combination is not just a meal; it’s an experience steeped in tradition, culture, and the beautiful landscapes of Greece. In this blog post, we will explore the art of pairing Greek wines with delicious mezze recipes, delving into the rich flavors and vibrant aromas that make this pairing truly unforgettable.

Understanding Greek Wine

Greek wine has a history that dates back thousands of years, making it one of the oldest wine-producing regions in the world. The unique climate and diverse geography of Greece contribute to the wide array of grape varieties and wine styles available today. Common grape varieties include Assyrtiko, Xinomavro, and Agiorgitiko, each offering distinctive flavors that can enhance the taste of mezze.

Types of Greek Wine

  1. White Wine: Known for their crispness and refreshing qualities, Greek white wines, particularly those from the island of Santorini, are often characterized by citrus and mineral notes, making them ideal for pairing with lighter mezze.

  2. Red Wine: Rich and full-bodied, Greek red wines, especially those from the regions of Naoussa and Nemea, offer deep berry flavors and earthy undertones that complement heartier dishes.

  3. Rosé Wine: With a perfect balance between red and white, Greek rosé wines provide a versatile option that pairs beautifully with a wide range of mezze.

  4. Dessert Wine: Greek dessert wines, such as Vin Santo or Mavrodaphne, are sweet and aromatic, perfect for concluding your meal on a high note.

The Essence of Mezze

Mezze refers to a variety of small dishes served as appetizers, perfect for sharing and enjoying with family and friends. The beauty of mezze lies in its diversity, with options ranging from fresh salads to savory pastries and grilled meats. Here are some classic mezze recipes that pair wonderfully with Greek wines.

Classic Greek Tzatziki

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, grated and drained
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, minced garlic, olive oil, and red wine vinegar.
  2. Mix well until all ingredients are combined.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add chopped dill for an extra layer of flavor if desired.
  4. Refrigerate for at least one hour before serving for the best flavor.

Wine Pairing: A chilled glass of Assyrtiko complements the freshness of the tzatziki, enhancing the yogurt’s creaminess while balancing the garlic’s kick.

Stuffed Grape Leaves (Dolmadakia)

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar grape leaves in brine, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup rice, uncooked
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion until translucent.
  2. Add the rice and cook for a few minutes, stirring frequently.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, sautéed onion, pine nuts, parsley, dill, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Lay a grape leaf flat and place a spoonful of the filling at the stem end. Roll tightly, folding in the sides as you go.
  5. Place the stuffed grape leaves seam-side down in a pot. Add enough water to cover and simmer for about 45 minutes.

Wine Pairing: A crisp Greek rosé enhances the herbal flavors of the dolmadakia while providing a refreshing contrast to the rice and nuts.

Spanakopita (Spinach Pie)

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough, thawed
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil and sauté the onion until soft. Add the spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let cool.
  3. In a bowl, combine the cooked spinach mixture, feta cheese, beaten eggs, salt, and pepper.
  4. Layer several sheets of phyllo dough in a greased baking dish, brushing each layer with olive oil.
  5. Spoon the spinach filling into the center and fold the phyllo over the filling. Brush the top with olive oil.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown.

Wine Pairing: A glass of Xinomavro, with its rich tannins and berry flavors, complements the savory spinach and feta filling beautifully.

Grilled Octopus

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole octopus, cleaned
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the octopus in salted water for about 45 minutes until tender. Drain and let cool.
  2. In a bowl, mix olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Marinate the octopus in the mixture for at least an hour.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the octopus for 3-4 minutes on each side until charred.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Wine Pairing: A vibrant Assyrtiko enhances the smoky flavors of the grilled octopus and cuts through the richness of the olive oil.

Feta and Olives Platter

Ingredients:

  • 200g feta cheese, cubed
  • 1 cup mixed olives (Kalamata, green, etc.)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Fresh lemon slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Arrange the feta cubes and mixed olives on a serving platter.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with dried oregano.
  3. Garnish with fresh lemon slices.

Wine Pairing: A refreshing glass of Greek white wine, like Moschofilero, pairs excellently with the salty feta and olives, enhancing their flavors.

Greek Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh oregano for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, olives, and feta cheese.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently.
  4. Garnish with fresh oregano before serving.

Wine Pairing: The bright acidity of a Greek white like Sauvignon Blanc brings out the freshness of the salad ingredients, creating a delightful harmony.

Crafting the Perfect Mezze Platter

Creating a mezze platter is an art in itself. A well-balanced platter not only showcases a variety of flavors and textures but also allows guests to experience the essence of Greek cuisine. Here’s how to craft the perfect mezze platter that pairs beautifully with Greek wine:

Components of a Mezze Platter

  1. Dips: Incorporate tzatziki, hummus, and melitzanosalata (eggplant dip) for variety.
  2. Cheese: Add cubes of feta, kasseri, or graviera cheese for a rich touch.
  3. Vegetables: Fresh vegetables like cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, and bell peppers add crunch and color.
  4. Pickles: Include pickled vegetables or olives for a tangy bite.
  5. Bread: Offer pita bread or crusty bread for dipping.
  6. Meats: Consider cured meats like pastirma or grilled sausages for heartier options.

Presentation Tips

  • Use a large, flat platter to arrange the components in a visually appealing manner.
  • Group similar items together but create enough separation to highlight individual flavors.
  • Use garnishes like fresh herbs or lemon wedges to add color and freshness.

The Conclusion of Your Culinary Journey

Pairing Greek wine with mezze is an adventure that tantalizes the taste buds while celebrating the rich culinary traditions of Greece. Each dish tells a story, and every sip of wine enhances the flavors, creating a memorable dining experience. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or enjoying a quiet evening at home, the perfect pairing of Greek wine and mezze is sure to delight.

As you explore these recipes and pairings, remember that the essence of Greek dining lies in the joy of sharing and savoring good food with great company. So gather your loved ones, pour a glass of fine Greek wine, and indulge in the vibrant flavors of Greek mezze. Opa!
