Green Chicken Enchiladas: A Creamy and Tangy Delight


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Green Chicken Enchiladas. These enchiladas are not only incredibly delicious, but they also have a beautiful vibrant green color that is sure to impress your guests. With a perfect balance of creaminess and tanginess, this dish is a true delight for your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup green salsa
  • 1 cup Monterey Jack cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup diced green chilies
  • 1/4 cup diced onions
  • 1/4 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced green bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup diced jalapenos (optional for extra heat)
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked chicken, sour cream, green salsa, half of the shredded Monterey Jack cheese, cilantro, diced green chilies, onions, tomatoes, green bell peppers, and jalapenos (if using). Mix well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. Heat a small amount of olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Briefly fry each corn tortilla for about 10 seconds on each side, just until softened. Transfer the tortillas to a paper towel-lined plate to remove any excess oil.

  4. Spoon about 2-3 tablespoons of the chicken mixture onto each tortilla and roll it up tightly. Place the rolled tortillas, seam side down, in a greased baking dish.

  5. Once all the tortillas are rolled and placed in the baking dish, sprinkle the remaining shredded Monterey Jack cheese over the top.

  6. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Remove from the oven and let the enchiladas cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with additional chopped cilantro, diced tomatoes, and a dollop of sour cream if desired.

  8. Serve the Green Chicken Enchiladas hot and enjoy the creamy and tangy flavors!

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a spicier version, you can add more diced jalapenos or even a sprinkle of cayenne pepper to the chicken mixture.
  • For a vegetarian option, you can substitute the chicken with sautéed vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, and mushrooms.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought rotisserie chicken instead of cooking your own.
  • These enchiladas can be made ahead of time and refrigerated. Simply cover the baking dish with foil and store it in the refrigerator. When ready to serve, bake as directed, adding a few extra minutes to the baking time.

Now that you have the recipe for Green Chicken Enchiladas, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get cooking! Whether you’re preparing a cozy family dinner or entertaining guests, these enchiladas are sure to impress. The creamy and tangy flavors will leave everyone wanting more. So, put on your apron, turn up the music, and let’s get cooking!
