Springtime Spread: Herbed Ricotta and Pea Crostini


Spring is in the air, and what better way to celebrate the season than with a fresh and vibrant appetizer? These Herbed Ricotta and Pea Crostini are the perfect bite-sized treats to enjoy during the warmer months. With their creamy ricotta spread and burst of sweet peas, these crostini will impress your guests and awaken your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 1 baguette, sliced into 1/2-inch thick rounds
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Arrange the baguette slices on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Toast in the oven for about 10 minutes or until golden brown and crispy. Set aside to cool.

  2. In a small saucepan, bring water to a boil and add the peas. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until they are bright green and tender. Drain the peas and transfer them to a bowl of ice water to cool rapidly. This will help them retain their vibrant color. Once cooled, drain the peas again.

  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, chopped basil, chopped mint, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Stir well to incorporate all the flavors. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Take each toasted baguette slice and spread a generous amount of the herbed ricotta mixture on top. Use the back of a spoon to create an even layer.

  5. Take a few cooked peas and gently press them into the ricotta spread. The peas will add a burst of sweetness and a pop of color to the crostini.

  6. Repeat the process with the remaining baguette slices until all the crostini are assembled.

  7. Arrange the Herbed Ricotta and Pea Crostini on a serving platter and drizzle lightly with extra virgin olive oil.

  8. Serve immediately and enjoy the fresh flavors of spring!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can sprinkle some freshly grated Parmesan cheese over the crostini before baking them in the oven.
  • If you prefer a smoother texture for the ricotta spread, you can blend it in a food processor until creamy before adding the herbs and seasonings.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs such as parsley, chives, or dill to customize the flavor profile of the spread.
  • These crostini can be made ahead of time and stored in an airtight container for up to 2 days. However, it’s best to add the peas just before serving to maintain their freshness and vibrant color.

Conclusion

Springtime Spread: Herbed Ricotta and Pea Crostini are a delightful combination of creamy ricotta, fresh herbs, and sweet peas. The crispy baguette slices provide the perfect base for this appetizer that embodies the essence of spring. Whether you’re hosting a garden party or simply looking for a light and refreshing snack, these crostini are sure to impress. Enjoy the flavors of the season and let your taste buds dance with delight!
