The Perfect Comfort Food: Making Korean Army Stew


When it comes to comfort food, nothing quite hits the spot like a steaming bowl of Korean Army Stew, also known as “Budae Jjigae.” This hearty and flavorful dish has a fascinating history and a unique blend of ingredients that make it a perfect choice for a cozy night in or a gathering with friends. In this post, we’ll explore the origins of Korean Army Stew, delve into its key ingredients, and provide a step-by-step guide on how to make this delicious comfort food at home.

The History of Korean Army Stew

Korean Army Stew has its roots in the aftermath of the Korean War when food was scarce, and people had to make do with whatever ingredients were available. American military bases in South Korea provided surplus food items such as spam, canned beans, and processed cheese to the locals, which were then incorporated into traditional Korean stews. Over time, these humble ingredients evolved into the rich and flavorful dish known as Budae Jjigae, symbolizing resilience and creativity in the face of adversity.

Key Ingredients

The beauty of Korean Army Stew lies in its diverse and unexpected combination of ingredients. While the base of the stew typically includes kimchi, gochujang (Korean chili paste), and a variety of vegetables, the addition of processed meats like spam, hot dogs, and canned baked beans gives it a unique twist. The result is a harmonious blend of flavors that is both comforting and satisfying.

Here are the key ingredients you’ll need:

  • Kimchi: Fermented cabbage that forms the flavorful base of the stew.
  • Gochujang: A spicy and savory red chili paste that adds depth and heat to the dish.
  • Canned Spam: A nod to the stew’s origins, providing salty and savory notes.
  • Sausages or Hot Dogs: Adding a meaty element to the stew.
  • Instant Ramen Noodles: Contributing a comforting and familiar texture.
  • Tofu: Providing a soft and protein-rich addition to the stew.
  • Canned Baked Beans: Adding a touch of sweetness and substance.

Recipe: Making Korean Army Stew

Now, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making your own Korean Army Stew at home. This recipe is a wonderful way to experience the heartwarming flavors of this beloved comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of kimchi, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang
  • 4 cups of chicken or beef broth
  • 1 can of spam, sliced
  • 4 sausages or hot dogs, sliced
  • 2 packs of instant ramen noodles
  • 1 block of tofu, cubed
  • 1 can of baked beans
  • 1 cup of sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup of sliced scallions
  • 1 block of sliced American or cheddar cheese

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the chopped kimchi, gochujang, and chicken or beef broth. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced spam, sausages or hot dogs, and tofu to the pot. Allow the stew to simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  3. Gently add the instant ramen noodles, canned baked beans, mushrooms, and scallions to the pot. Stir gently to ensure all ingredients are well combined.
  4. Once the noodles are cooked and the ingredients are heated through, place the sliced cheese on top of the stew and let it melt, creating a rich and creamy layer.
  5. Serve the Korean Army Stew hot, straight from the pot, and enjoy with steamed rice or crusty bread for a truly satisfying meal.

In Conclusion

Korean Army Stew, with its intriguing history and comforting flavors, is a testament to the resilience and resourcefulness of the Korean people. This beloved dish has become a symbol of innovation and adaptability, showcasing how humble ingredients can come together to create something truly extraordinary. Whether you’re seeking a heartwarming meal for a chilly evening or a unique culinary experience, Korean Army Stew is sure to delight your taste buds and warm your soul. Embrace the spirit of Budae Jjigae by creating this comforting dish in your own kitchen and savoring its rich and complex flavors.
