Pad Thai Recipe with Shrimp and Tofu


Pad Thai is a beloved Thai street food that has gained popularity worldwide for its tantalizing flavors and unique combination of ingredients. While the traditional Pad Thai typically includes chicken or tofu, this recipe introduces a delightful twist by incorporating succulent shrimp and tofu, adding an extra layer of richness to this classic dish. Whether you’re a Pad Thai enthusiast or new to the world of Thai cuisine, this recipe is sure to tantalize your taste buds and become a favorite in your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of rice noodles
  • 1/2 pound of medium-sized shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 block of firm tofu, drained and cut into small cubes
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup of bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup of chopped unsalted peanuts
  • 3 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup of tamarind paste
  • 3 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon of chili flakes
  • Lime wedges for serving
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice Noodles:

    • Place the rice noodles in a large bowl and cover them with hot water. Let them soak for about 8-10 minutes until they are tender but still firm. Drain the noodles and set them aside.

  2. Cook the Shrimp and Tofu:

    • In a large skillet or wok, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and tofu cubes, and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the shrimp turns pink and the tofu is golden brown. Remove the shrimp and tofu from the skillet and set them aside.

  3. Prepare the Sauce:

    • In the same skillet, heat the remaining vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant. Crack the eggs into the skillet and scramble them until they are just set.

  4. Assemble the Pad Thai:

    • Add the drained rice noodles to the skillet with the eggs. Pour the tamarind paste, fish sauce, sugar, and soy sauce over the noodles. Toss everything together until the noodles are well coated with the sauce.

  5. Add the Final Touches:

    • Return the cooked shrimp and tofu to the skillet. Add the bean sprouts and half of the chopped green onions. Toss everything together for another 2-3 minutes until the bean sprouts are slightly wilted.

  6. Serve and Garnish:

    • Transfer the Pad Thai to a serving platter or individual plates. Sprinkle the chopped peanuts and remaining green onions over the top. Serve with lime wedges and garnish with fresh cilantro.

The Perfect Blend of Flavors

This Pad Thai recipe with shrimp and tofu offers a delightful combination of textures and flavors. The succulent shrimp adds a sweet and briny note, while the tofu brings a satisfying chewiness to each bite. The tender rice noodles soak up the tangy and slightly sweet tamarind-based sauce, creating a harmonious blend of savory and tangy flavors that will leave you craving more.

Tips for Success

  • Tamarind Paste: If you can’t find tamarind paste, you can substitute it with fresh lime juice mixed with a bit of brown sugar for a similar tangy flavor.
  • Customize Your Pad Thai: Feel free to add other ingredients such as shredded carrots, bell peppers, or even a sprinkle of crushed red pepper flakes for a spicier kick.
  • Preparation is Key: Have all your ingredients prepped and ready to go before you start cooking, as Pad Thai comes together quickly once you begin.

In Conclusion

This Pad Thai recipe with shrimp and tofu is a delightful fusion of traditional Thai flavors and modern twists. Whether you’re a seafood lover, a vegetarian, or simply craving a flavorful and satisfying meal, this recipe is a must-try. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your wok, and get ready to savor the tantalizing flavors of this unique Pad Thai creation. Enjoy!
