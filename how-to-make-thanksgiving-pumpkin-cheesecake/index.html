How to Make Thanksgiving Pumpkin Cheesecake


Thanksgiving is a time for family, friends, and, of course, incredible food. While turkey and stuffing often steal the show, a Thanksgiving dessert can easily be the highlight of the meal. One of the best ways to elevate your dessert table is with a delicious, creamy, and indulgent pumpkin cheesecake. Combining the rich, velvety texture of cheesecake with the warm, spiced flavors of pumpkin, this dessert is a perfect balance of sweetness and savory warmth. In this guide, you’ll learn how to make a pumpkin cheesecake from scratch that will leave your guests asking for seconds.

Ingredients for the Pumpkin Cheesecake Crust

Before we dive into the creamy, spiced filling, let’s focus on the crust. A good crust serves as the foundation of a great cheesecake. For this pumpkin cheesecake, we’ll use a simple graham cracker crust, but we’ll give it a twist with a hint of cinnamon, nutmeg, and brown sugar to complement the pumpkin flavor.

Ingredients for the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups of graham cracker crumbs
  • 3 tablespoons of brown sugar (packed)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground cloves (optional, for a bit of spice)
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • A pinch of salt

How to Make the Pumpkin Cheesecake Crust

  1. Prepare the Crust Mixture: In a large mixing bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. Stir to blend the dry ingredients thoroughly.
  2. Add the Butter: Pour in the melted butter and mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture resembles wet sand.
  3. Press into the Pan: Pour the graham cracker mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Press it firmly into the base and slightly up the sides to create an even, compact crust.
  4. Chill the Crust: To help the crust set and firm up, place the pan in the refrigerator while you prepare the cheesecake filling. This step ensures that the crust won’t fall apart when cutting the cheesecake.

Ingredients for the Pumpkin Cheesecake Filling

The filling is what makes this cheesecake truly special. It’s a creamy blend of cream cheese, pumpkin puree, and a mix of warming spices. The key to a successful pumpkin cheesecake is balancing the flavors without making the pumpkin too overpowering.

Ingredients for the filling:

  • 3 packages (8 oz each) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree (make sure to use 100% pure pumpkin, not pumpkin pie filling)
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs (at room temperature)
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

How to Make the Pumpkin Cheesecake Filling

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 325°F (163°C). The cheesecake will bake at a low temperature to ensure it cooks slowly and evenly.
  2. Mix the Cream Cheese and Pumpkin Puree: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and fluffy using a hand mixer or stand mixer. Add the pumpkin puree and continue beating until completely combined.
  3. Add the Sugars and Spices: Mix in the granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, cloves, and salt. Beat until the mixture is smooth, and the spices are well incorporated.
  4. Add Vanilla and Flour: Stir in the vanilla extract and sift the flour into the mixture. The flour helps to stabilize the filling and create a smooth texture.
  5. Add the Eggs: Add the eggs one at a time, beating on low speed after each addition to prevent overmixing. Overmixing the batter at this stage can cause cracks in your cheesecake.
  6. Finish with Sour Cream: Stir in the sour cream, which will add a rich, creamy texture to the filling and a slight tang that contrasts with the sweetness of the pumpkin.

Assembling the Pumpkin Cheesecake

Now that the crust and filling are ready, it’s time to assemble the cheesecake. Make sure you have everything prepped before you pour the filling into the pan.

  1. Prepare the Springform Pan: Remove the crust from the refrigerator. If you want to prevent any leakage during baking, you can line the outside of the springform pan with aluminum foil, ensuring the foil is tightly wrapped around the bottom and sides.
  2. Pour the Filling: Carefully pour the pumpkin cheesecake filling into the prepared crust. Gently tap the pan on the counter to remove any air bubbles trapped in the filling.
  3. Smooth the Top: Use a spatula to smooth the top of the filling into an even layer.

Baking the Pumpkin Cheesecake

  1. Bake at Low Temperature: Place the cheesecake on the center rack of the preheated oven and bake at 325°F (163°C) for about 55-65 minutes. The cheesecake is done when the edges are set and slightly golden, but the center still jiggles slightly when you gently shake the pan.
  2. Turn Off the Oven and Let it Cool: Once the cheesecake is baked, turn off the oven and leave the cheesecake inside with the door slightly ajar for about 1 hour. This gradual cooling process helps prevent cracks from forming.
  3. Chill in the Refrigerator: After an hour, remove the cheesecake from the oven. Let it cool to room temperature, then transfer it to the refrigerator and chill for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. The cheesecake needs time to firm up and develop its full flavor.

Topping Your Pumpkin Cheesecake

The beauty of a pumpkin cheesecake is that you can top it with various delicious garnishes. Some common toppings include whipped cream, caramel sauce, or even a sprinkle of crushed graham crackers for an extra crunch.

Whipped Cream Topping:

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

To make the whipped cream, beat the heavy whipping cream in a chilled bowl until soft peaks form. Add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue to beat until stiff peaks form. Spread the whipped cream on top of the chilled cheesecake just before serving.

Caramel Sauce Topping:

Drizzle with homemade or store-bought caramel sauce for an indulgent touch that pairs beautifully with the spiced pumpkin flavor.

Crushed Graham Crackers:

For a simple, crunchy topping, sprinkle some crushed graham crackers over the whipped cream to enhance the graham crust flavor.

Serving and Storing the Pumpkin Cheesecake

When serving your pumpkin cheesecake, use a sharp knife to carefully slice it. Clean the knife between slices to ensure clean, neat cuts. The cheesecake is rich and creamy, so small slices are often enough to satisfy your guests.

If you have leftovers, store the cheesecake in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. For longer storage, you can freeze slices of pumpkin cheesecake. Wrap each slice in plastic wrap, then place them in a freezer-safe container. Frozen cheesecake can last for up to 3 months. To thaw, place the cheesecake in the refrigerator overnight.

Tips for the Perfect Pumpkin Cheesecake

  1. Use Room Temperature Ingredients: For the smoothest, creamiest cheesecake, ensure that all your ingredients—especially the cream cheese and eggs—are at room temperature before mixing. This helps to prevent lumps and achieve a silky texture.
  2. Don’t Overmix the Filling: Overmixing can incorporate too much air into the filling, which may cause cracks in your cheesecake. Mix just until combined for the best results.
  3. Avoid Overbaking: The cheesecake should still have a slight jiggle in the center when you remove it from the oven. Overbaking can result in a dry, cracked cheesecake.
  4. Chill the Cheesecake for the Best Texture: Cheesecake needs time to set and develop its flavor. Be patient and allow the cheesecake to chill for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight.
  5. Try a Water Bath: To prevent cracks and ensure even baking, you can bake your cheesecake in a water bath. Wrap the springform pan tightly in aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in, and place the pan in a larger baking dish filled with hot water. This method ensures that the cheesecake bakes evenly and slowly.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving, make your dessert stand out with a rich and creamy pumpkin cheesecake. With a spiced graham cracker crust and a smooth, flavorful filling, this pumpkin cheesecake is sure to be a hit with your family and friends. By following these easy steps and tips, you can create a dessert that not only tastes incredible but also adds a beautiful touch to your holiday table. Whether served with a dollop of whipped cream, drizzled with caramel, or simply on its own, this pumpkin cheesecake will become a new Thanksgiving tradition in your home. Enjoy!
