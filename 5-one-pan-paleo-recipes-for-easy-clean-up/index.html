5 One-Pan Paleo Recipes for Easy Clean-Up


When it comes to cooking, nothing beats the convenience of a one-pan meal. Not only does it save you time in the kitchen, but it also minimizes the dreaded post-cooking clean-up. For those following a Paleo lifestyle, one-pan meals can be a game-changer, combining wholesome, nutrient-rich ingredients in a single dish that’s satisfying, simple, and colorful. Here are five delicious one-pan Paleo recipes that will add variety, flavor, and ease to your meal rotation.

1. Lemon Garlic Chicken with Veggies

This dish pairs tender chicken thighs with vibrant veggies for a meal bursting with zesty and savory flavors. It’s the perfect balance of protein, healthy fats, and nutrient-packed vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 3 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli florets, zucchini, and bell peppers)
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a large oven-proof skillet over medium heat. Season chicken thighs with sea salt, paprika, and black pepper, then sear them skin-side down until golden and crispy (about 5 minutes).
  3. Flip the chicken and push it to one side of the skillet. Add minced garlic, stirring until fragrant.
  4. Toss the mixed vegetables with lemon juice, lemon zest, dried oregano, and a sprinkle of salt and pepper. Place the veggies in the skillet alongside the chicken.
  5. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with additional lemon zest if desired.

Pro tip: Swap in seasonal vegetables for variety and to take advantage of fresh, local produce.

2. One-Pan Teriyaki Salmon with Stir-Fry Vegetables

This Paleo-friendly teriyaki salmon recipe is rich, flavorful, and protein-packed. Instead of traditional soy sauce, coconut aminos keep this recipe Paleo-approved while delivering the same umami punch.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 cups thinly sliced carrots and snap peas
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 2 tablespoons raw honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together coconut aminos, honey, sesame oil, grated ginger, and apple cider vinegar to create the teriyaki sauce.
  3. Place salmon fillets in a large baking dish or sheet pan. Arrange the carrots and snap peas around the salmon.
  4. Pour the teriyaki sauce over the salmon and vegetables, ensuring everything is evenly coated.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the salmon is flaky and the vegetables are tender.
  6. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions before serving.

This recipe is perfect for meal prep since it reheats beautifully for lunches or dinners later in the week.

3. Tex-Mex Cauliflower Rice Skillet with Ground Beef

Packed with bold, smoky flavors, this hearty Tex-Mex cauliflower rice skillet comes together in under 30 minutes. Low-carb and Paleo-friendly, it’s a perfect one-pan meal the whole family will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 4 cups cauliflower rice (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 1 small onion (diced)
  • 1 bell pepper (diced)
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon avocado or olive oil
  • Sea salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro and avocado slices for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat avocado oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, sautéing until softened.
  2. Add the ground beef, breaking it apart with a spoon, and cook until browned. Drain any excess fat if necessary.
  3. Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and diced tomatoes. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Add the cauliflower rice and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender and well-incorporated with the other ingredients.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and avocado slices for additional flavor and texture.

For an extra crunch, consider topping this skillet with crushed, Paleo-compliant plantain chips.

4. Sweet Potato Hash with Sausage and Eggs

Sweet potato hash is a versatile one-pan wonder that works equally well for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. This recipe features savory sausage and perfectly cooked eggs for a meal that’s as wholesome as it is satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes (diced into small cubes)
  • 1/2 pound ground sausage (check for Paleo-friendly ingredients)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 1 bell pepper (chopped)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Sea salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add sweet potato cubes and cook for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they begin to soften and brown.
  2. Push the sweet potato cubes to one side of the skillet and add the ground sausage. Cook until fully browned, breaking it up into small crumbles as it cooks.
  3. Add the onion, bell pepper, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together and cook until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Make four small wells in the skillet mixture and crack one egg into each well. Cover the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the eggs are cooked to your preference.
  5. Garnish with chopped parsley before serving.

This dish is easily customizable—add mushrooms, kale, or even diced apples to the hash for extra depth and flavor.

5. Greek-Inspired Shrimp and Vegetable Bake

Light, zesty, and brimming with Mediterranean flavors, this shrimp and vegetable bake is a quick and easy dinner that delivers big on taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup sliced zucchini
  • 1/2 red onion (sliced)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Sea salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley and lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, toss shrimp, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, and red onion with olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Arrange the mixture in an even layer on a baking tray or in an oven-safe skillet.
  4. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and fully cooked.
  5. Garnish with parsley and serve with fresh lemon wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

Serve this dish with a side of cauliflower rice or a simple arugula salad to round out your plate.

Final Thoughts

These five one-pan Paleo recipes prove that healthy eating doesn’t have to be complicated. They’re quick to prepare, easy to clean up, and loaded with fresh, nutrient-dense ingredients. Incorporating these recipes into your weekly meal plan is a surefire way to stay on track with your Paleo lifestyle while enjoying a variety of flavors and cuisines. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or entertaining guests, these one-pan wonders deliver simplicity without compromising taste.
