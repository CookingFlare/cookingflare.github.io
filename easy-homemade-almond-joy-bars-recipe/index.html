Easy Homemade Almond Joy Bars Recipe


There’s something magical about the combination of chocolate, coconut, and almonds. Whether you’re craving a sweet snack or a decadent treat to share, homemade Almond Joy bars are the perfect indulgence. These bite-sized morsels not only satisfy your sweet tooth but also give you the satisfaction of creating something delicious from scratch. If you’ve ever wondered how to make Almond Joy bars at home, you’re in for a treat. This recipe is easy to follow and requires minimal ingredients, most of which you may already have in your pantry.

In this post, we’ll guide you step by step on how to prepare these incredible homemade Almond Joy bars, packed with rich chocolate, creamy coconut, and a satisfying almond crunch. Plus, these bars make a great gift or party treat that will leave everyone asking for the recipe!

Ingredients for Homemade Almond Joy Bars

Before you get started, let’s gather all the ingredients you’ll need to make these delightful bars. The ingredients are simple and easy to find at your local grocery store.

For the Coconut Filling:

  • 2 cups sweetened shredded coconut
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil (melted)
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar (adjust sweetness to your liking)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the Chocolate Coating:

  • 2 cups milk chocolate chips (or semi-sweet, depending on preference)
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil (for smooth coating)

For the Topping:

  • 20-24 whole almonds (raw or roasted)

These are the basic ingredients you’ll need to make the bars. Of course, there are always opportunities to tweak the recipe, depending on your dietary preferences. You can opt for dark chocolate if you prefer a less sweet treat or use a sugar-free sweetener for a healthier alternative. But for this classic recipe, these ingredients will give you the signature Almond Joy flavor everyone loves.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Homemade Almond Joy Bars

Making homemade Almond Joy bars is straightforward, and you’ll be amazed at how easy it is to replicate this beloved candy bar at home. Let’s walk through the process:

Step 1: Prepare the Coconut Filling

  1. Combine the Coconut, Coconut Oil, and Sweeteners
    In a large bowl, add the shredded coconut, melted coconut oil, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Stir the mixture together until it forms a thick, sticky consistency. The coconut oil will help bind the coconut and sweeteners together, giving the filling a smooth texture that will hold up when shaped.

  2. Chill the Coconut Mixture
    Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for about 30 minutes. Chilling the mixture makes it easier to handle and shape into bars, ensuring that the coconut filling holds its form when pressed into the molds.

Step 2: Shape the Coconut Filling into Bars

  1. Form the Coconut Bars
    After the coconut mixture has chilled, take about 1 tablespoon of the mixture and roll it into a small log shape. Place it on a parchment-lined baking sheet or a tray. Repeat this process until all the coconut mixture is shaped into small bars. You should be able to make about 10-12 bars depending on how large you want them.

  2. Press a Whole Almond into the Center
    Take a whole almond and gently press it into the center of each coconut bar. The almond will be the signature topping of your Almond Joy bars, so make sure it is nestled into the coconut mixture securely.

  3. Refrigerate Again
    Once all the coconut bars are shaped and topped with almonds, return the tray to the refrigerator for another 15-20 minutes to firm up. This ensures that your bars hold together when you dip them in chocolate.

Step 3: Prepare the Chocolate Coating

  1. Melt the Chocolate and Coconut Oil
    While your coconut bars chill, prepare the chocolate coating. In a microwave-safe bowl, add the milk chocolate chips and coconut oil. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring in between, until the chocolate is smooth and fully melted. The coconut oil helps the chocolate coat the bars more easily and gives it a smooth texture.

  2. Dip the Coconut Bars in Chocolate
    Once the coconut bars are chilled and firm, itâ€™s time to coat them in chocolate. Using a fork or dipping tool, carefully dip each coconut bar into the melted chocolate. Make sure each bar is fully coated, allowing any excess chocolate to drip off.

  3. Place the Bars on a Parchment-Lined Tray
    After dipping the bars in chocolate, place them back on the parchment-lined baking sheet. You can repeat the dipping process for an extra thick layer of chocolate if you like your Almond Joy bars extra chocolaty.

Step 4: Allow the Bars to Set

  1. Chill the Bars to Set the Chocolate
    Once all the bars are coated, place the tray back in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes to an hour, or until the chocolate hardens and fully sets. This is the final step in making sure your bars have that satisfying snap when you bite into them.

Step 5: Enjoy Your Homemade Almond Joy Bars

Once the chocolate has set, your homemade Almond Joy bars are ready to enjoy! These bars are perfect for snacking, dessert, or even giving as gifts. The combination of creamy coconut, crunchy almonds, and smooth chocolate is sure to satisfy any sweet craving.

Storing Your Homemade Almond Joy Bars

To ensure that your homemade Almond Joy bars stay fresh, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week. If you want them to last longer, you can refrigerate them for up to 2 weeks or freeze them for up to 3 months. Just be sure to place parchment paper between layers to prevent them from sticking together.

Variations and Tips

While the classic Almond Joy flavor is hard to beat, there are a few ways you can customize this recipe to suit your taste.

1. Dark Chocolate Coating

If you’re a fan of dark chocolate, feel free to swap the milk chocolate chips for semi-sweet or dark chocolate. The rich bitterness of dark chocolate pairs wonderfully with the sweetness of the coconut filling.

2. Add More Nuts

In addition to the whole almond, you can experiment with adding other nuts. Try macadamia nuts, pecans, or even cashews for a unique twist.

3. Go for a Healthier Alternative

For those looking to make a healthier version, consider using unsweetened shredded coconut, stevia or monk fruit sweetener, and dark chocolate with a higher cocoa content. You can also use almond butter instead of coconut oil in the filling for an extra nutty flavor.

4. Make Mini Bars

If you prefer smaller portions, you can form the coconut mixture into mini bite-sized pieces instead of larger bars. This is a great option if you’re preparing them for a party or just want a quick snack.

5. Vegan Option

To make this recipe vegan, use dairy-free chocolate chips and replace the coconut oil with another plant-based oil, such as almond or avocado oil.
