Korean Snack Delight: How to Make Rabokki at Home


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine, you’ve probably heard of rabokki, a popular street food snack that combines the best of both worlds - delicious ramen noodles and spicy tteokbokki. This fusion dish is a favorite among locals and tourists alike, thanks to its bold flavors and satisfying texture. The good news is, you don’t have to travel all the way to Korea to enjoy rabokki. With a few simple ingredients and a little bit of kitchen magic, you can recreate this mouthwatering snack in the comfort of your own home.

Ingredients:

  • 200g tteokbokki rice cakes
  • 2 packs instant ramen noodles
  • 1 cup cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional)
  • Water for boiling

Instructions:

  1. Start by boiling a pot of water. Once the water reaches a rolling boil, add the tteokbokki rice cakes and cook for about 5 minutes, or until they become soft and chewy. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a separate pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onions, and sauté until the onions become translucent.

  3. Add the cabbage and carrot to the pot, and continue to sauté for another 2-3 minutes, or until the vegetables are slightly tender.

  4. In a small bowl, mix together the gochujang, soy sauce, and sugar until well combined. Add this mixture to the pot and stir to coat the vegetables evenly.

  5. Pour in 3 cups of water and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low and let it simmer for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. While the broth is simmering, prepare the instant ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain the noodles and set aside.

  7. Once the broth has simmered for 10 minutes, add the cooked tteokbokki rice cakes and the cooked ramen noodles to the pot. Stir gently to combine all the ingredients.

  8. Continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until everything is heated through.

  9. Remove the pot from the heat and drizzle the sesame oil over the rabokki. Give it a final stir to incorporate the sesame oil.

  10. Garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds, if desired.

And there you have it - a delicious bowl of homemade rabokki! The spicy and savory flavors of the tteokbokki, combined with the comforting noodles, make this dish an absolute delight. Whether you’re craving a quick snack or planning a Korean-themed dinner party, rabokki is sure to impress your taste buds.

Feel free to customize this recipe to suit your preferences. You can add different vegetables like mushrooms or bell peppers for extra flavor and texture. If you prefer a milder spice level, reduce the amount of gochujang or add a splash of milk to the broth. The possibilities are endless!

Now that you know how to make rabokki at home, there’s no need to wait for your next trip to Korea to enjoy this mouthwatering snack. Gather the ingredients, follow the steps, and get ready to indulge in a Korean culinary delight right in your own kitchen. Happy cooking!
