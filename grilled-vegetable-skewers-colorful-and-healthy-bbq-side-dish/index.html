Grilled Vegetable Skewers: Colorful and Healthy BBQ Side Dish


Grilled vegetable skewers are the perfect addition to any barbecue feast. Not only are they vibrant and visually appealing, but they also offer a healthy and delicious option for those looking to incorporate more vegetables into their diet. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply want to add some variety to your grill, these colorful skewers are sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients

To create these mouthwatering grilled vegetable skewers, you will need:

  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 yellow bell pepper
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 1 red onion
  • 1 zucchini
  • 1 eggplant
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 8-10 wooden skewers

Instructions

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Start by washing all the vegetables thoroughly. Then, slice the bell peppers, red onion, zucchini, and eggplant into bite-sized pieces. Leave the cherry tomatoes whole.

  2. Soak the skewers: To prevent the wooden skewers from burning on the grill, soak them in water for about 30 minutes. This will help keep them moist and make them less likely to catch fire.

  3. Assemble the skewers: Take a skewer and thread the vegetables onto it, alternating between the different vegetables to create a colorful pattern. Repeat this process until all the vegetables are used.

  4. Preheat the grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. This will ensure that the vegetables cook evenly and develop a nice char.

  5. Grill the skewers: Carefully place the vegetable skewers on the preheated grill. Cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning them occasionally to ensure even cooking. The vegetables should be tender and slightly charred when done.

  6. Serve and enjoy: Once the skewers are cooked to perfection, remove them from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve them as a side dish alongside your favorite barbecue fare, or enjoy them as a light and healthy meal on their own.

Tips and Variations

  • Marinate the vegetables: For an extra burst of flavor, you can marinate the vegetables before grilling them. Simply toss them in a mixture of olive oil, balsamic vinegar, garlic, and your favorite herbs and spices. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes before threading them onto the skewers.

  • Add protein: If you want to make these skewers more filling, you can add protein to the mix. Try threading pieces of tofu, tempeh, or even shrimp or chicken onto the skewers alongside the vegetables.

  • Experiment with seasonings: While grilled vegetables are delicious on their own, you can also experiment with different seasonings to enhance their flavor. Sprinkle some smoked paprika, cumin, or Italian seasoning over the vegetables before grilling them for an added kick.

  • Serve with dipping sauce: To take these skewers to the next level, serve them with a delicious dipping sauce. Whether it’s a tangy yogurt-based dip, a zesty chimichurri sauce, or a classic barbecue sauce, a dipping sauce can elevate the flavors and make the skewers even more enjoyable.

Grilled vegetable skewers are not only a colorful and healthy addition to any barbecue, but they also offer endless possibilities for customization. With a variety of vegetables and seasonings to choose from, you can create a dish that suits your taste preferences and dietary needs. So fire up your grill, grab some fresh vegetables, and get ready to enjoy a flavorful and nutritious BBQ side dish that will impress everyone at your next cookout.
