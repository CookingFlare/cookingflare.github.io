Haunted by the Sea: Supernatural-Inspired Seafood Recipes for Halloween


As the cool winds blow and leaves begin to fall, the nocturnal spirits awaken and Halloween creeps closer. The air turns heavy with the scent of pumpkins, apples, and perhaps a hint of brine from the nearby seas. This year, channel the eerie power of the deep and combine it with the mysterious and mystical touch of Halloween to create a collection of Supernatural-Inspired Seafood Recipes. Your guests will be enchanted by the blended flavors of the sea and hauntingly bewitching event. So, treasure yours as you venture into the world of cursed seafood dishes.

Captain’s Potion Soup

Begin your haunted dinner with a soup that is as mysterious as the ocean itself - Captain’s Potion Soup. This dish is perfect for warming the souls of your guests and setting the right atmosphere for your Halloween seafood feast. To create Captain’s Potion Soup, you will need:

  • 2 large cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 handful of fresh parsley, washed and chopped
  • ⅓ cup of white wine
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 quart of seafood stock
  • 1 cup of peeled and diced potatoes
  • 1 cup of hallowed-out pumpkin, the flesh reserved for another use
  • 1 ½ cups of seafood of your choice – shrimp, squid, or octopus - chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil or butter, for cooking

Begin by heating a large pot over medium heat. Add in olive oil or a knob of butter, then add the chopped onions and garlic. Sauté until the onions are soft and translucent. Add in the potatoes, pumpkin, and white wine. Cook, stirring the contents occasionally, until the wine has mostly evaporated. Pour in the seafood stock and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until the potatoes and pumpkin are cooked through – about 20 minutes. Add in the chopped seafood and continue to simmer until the seafood is cooked through. Season to taste with salt and pepper and sprinkle in the fresh chopped parsley. Serve piping hot with a flourish of hoop cheese.

Siren’s Gravestone Shrimp

The siren’s allure will have your guests enchanted with her Gravestone Shrimp. This seafood dish is perfect for a spooky feast and is sure to leave a lasting impression. To conjure up this tantalizing dish, you’ll need:

  • 2 lbs jumbo shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 4 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Begin by preheating your oven to 400°F. Season your shrimp with salt and pepper and then toss them in a mixture of almond flour and smoked paprika until evenly coated. Arrange the shrimp on a parchment-lined baking tray. Drizzle with olive oil and bake for 10-12 minutes until golden brown. During the last few minutes of cooking, toss in your minced garlic to give your shrimp a garlic, butter, and epic Halloween twist. After the shrimp are done, toss them with a squeeze of lemon juice and sprinkle of fresh parsley before serving.

Mermaid’s Enchanted Delicacy: A Tale of Two Tarts

No Halloween feast is complete without a touch of dessert. Introduce a mysterious, magical twist on a favorite classic: Mermaid’s Enchanted Delicacy - a tale of two tarts. To magically conjure such delightful treats, you will need:

  • 2 prepared pie crusts
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups fresh berries of your choice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Preheat your oven to 375°F. Beat the pumpkin puree, sugar, eggs, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and vanilla extract together in a bowl until well combined. Pour the mixture into the pie crusts, filling them to about 3/4 full. Place another crust on top and crimp around the edges, making sure to leave a few small openings for steam to escape. Place the tarts in the oven and bake until the crusts are golden brown and the filling is set – about 50-60 minutes. Let the tarts cool before transferring them to a serving dish. Just before serving, toss the fresh berries with a little bit of lemon juice and pile them atop your enchanted tarts.

As your guests venture into your haunted culinary adventure, the intertwining of otherworldly seafood flavors combined with Halloween flair will make for a hauntingly thrilling experience. So, let your imagination run wild and creativity roam free as you create your very own supernatural-inspired seafood recipes to bewitch your friends and family this year. So, raise a glass of apple cider and enjoy the feast fit for a phantom, Samhain celebration.

Happy Haunting!
