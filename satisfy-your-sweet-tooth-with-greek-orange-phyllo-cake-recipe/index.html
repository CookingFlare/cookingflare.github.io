Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth with Greek Orange Phyllo Cake Recipe


When it comes to desserts, few can rival the delightful experience of savoring a slice of Greek Orange Phyllo Cake. Known as “Portokalopita” in Greece, this cake combines the flaky goodness of phyllo pastry with the zesty brightness of fresh oranges, creating a dessert that is both refreshing and indulgent. Whether you are hosting a dinner party or simply looking to treat yourself, this recipe is sure to impress.

The Magic of Phyllo Pastry

Phyllo pastry is a staple in Greek cuisine, renowned for its light and crispy texture. Each layer is incredibly thin, and when baked, they create a delightful crunch that contrasts beautifully with moist fillings. The beauty of phyllo lies in its versatility; while it is often associated with savory dishes like spanakopita, it shines in sweet applications as well.

In this recipe, the phyllo is layered with a fragrant orange syrup, resulting in a cake that is not only visually stunning but also bursting with flavor. The combination of sweet and citrusy elements makes this cake an exceptional choice for any occasion.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this indulgent Greek Orange Phyllo Cake, gather the following ingredients:

For the Cake:

  • 1 package of phyllo pastry (about 16 oz), thawed
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter, melted (for brushing the phyllo)
  • 2 cups of Greek yogurt (or plain yogurt)
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • Zest of 2 large oranges
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • Pinch of salt

For the Orange Syrup:

  • 1 cup of freshly squeezed orange juice (about 2-3 oranges)
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1 teaspoon of lemon juice

Preparation Steps

Now that you have gathered all the necessary ingredients, it’s time to dive into the preparation process. Follow these steps to create your Greek Orange Phyllo Cake:

Step 1: Prepare the Orange Syrup

  1. In a saucepan, combine the orange juice, granulated sugar, orange zest, and water.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Once boiling, reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the lemon juice. Set aside to cool.

Step 2: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures that your cake bakes evenly and achieves that golden-brown color you desire.

Step 3: Prepare the Phyllo Pastry

  1. Carefully unroll the phyllo pastry sheets and cover them with a damp kitchen towel to prevent them from drying out.
  2. Brush the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish with melted butter.
  3. Lay one sheet of phyllo pastry in the dish, allowing the edges to hang over. Brush it lightly with melted butter.
  4. Repeat this process, layering about 8-10 sheets of phyllo, brushing each layer with butter.
  5. Reserve some phyllo sheets for the top layer.

Step 4: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, granulated sugar, eggs, orange zest, vanilla extract, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Mix until smooth and well combined, ensuring there are no lumps.
  3. Pour this mixture over the layered phyllo in the baking dish, spreading it evenly.

Step 5: Layer the Remaining Phyllo

  1. Take the remaining phyllo sheets and layer them over the filling, brushing each sheet with melted butter as you go.
  2. Crumple some of the phyllo sheets for added texture and visual appeal.
  3. Tuck any overhanging edges of the phyllo into the sides of the dish.

Step 6: Bake the Cake

  1. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  2. Keep an eye on it to prevent over-browning. If it browns too quickly, cover it loosely with aluminum foil.

Step 7: Soak the Cake

  1. Once the cake is baked, remove it from the oven and immediately pour the cooled orange syrup over the hot cake.
  2. Allow it to soak for at least 30 minutes before slicing. This step is crucial as it allows the syrup to infuse the cake with moisture and flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Your Greek Orange Phyllo Cake is now ready to be served! Here are a few ideas to elevate your presentation:

  • Garnish with Fresh Fruit: Top each slice with fresh orange slices or berries for a pop of color.
  • Serve with Whipped Cream: A dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream pairs beautifully with the citrusy flavors.
  • Add a Dusting of Powdered Sugar: For a touch of elegance, sprinkle powdered sugar over the top just before serving.

Storing Leftovers

If you manage to have any leftovers (which is unlikely, given how delicious this cake is), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. The flavors will continue to meld, and the cake will remain moist for up to three days.

Conclusion

The Greek Orange Phyllo Cake is a splendid dessert that brings the warmth of the Mediterranean to your table. Its unique combination of textures and flavors makes it a standout choice for any occasion. Whether you are celebrating a special event or simply craving something sweet, this cake is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth.

With its flaky phyllo layers and luscious orange filling, every bite is a reminder of the beauty of Greek cuisine. So why not gather your ingredients and embark on this culinary adventure? Your taste buds will thank you!
