Spicy Black-Eyed Pea Soup: A Southern Delight


If you’re looking for a hearty and flavorful dish that captures the essence of Southern cuisine, then look no further than Spicy Black-Eyed Pea Soup. This delicious soup is a perfect blend of bold spices, tender black-eyed peas, and a rich tomato base that will warm your soul and tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re a fan of Southern cooking or simply looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried black-eyed peas
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the black-eyed peas under cold water and remove any debris. Soak the peas overnight in a large bowl filled with water. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, diced green bell pepper, and chopped jalapeño pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the soaked black-eyed peas, canned diced tomatoes, vegetable or chicken broth, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  4. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 1 hour, or until the black-eyed peas are tender and cooked through. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  5. Taste the soup and season with salt and black pepper according to your preference. Adjust the spices if needed.

  6. Once the soup is ready, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, blend a portion of the soup until smooth. This step adds a creamy texture to the soup while still keeping some chunky bits of black-eyed peas.

  7. Serve the Spicy Black-Eyed Pea Soup hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley. It pairs perfectly with crusty bread or cornbread for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a meatier version, add diced ham or smoked sausage to the soup during step 3. This will add an extra layer of flavor and protein.
  • If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of cayenne pepper or omit it altogether. You can also remove the seeds from the jalapeño pepper for a milder heat.
  • Feel free to experiment with other spices and herbs to suit your taste. Some popular additions include bay leaves, thyme, or smoked paprika.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for future meals.

Conclusion

Spicy Black-Eyed Pea Soup is a true Southern delight that brings together the warmth and comfort of homemade cooking. This recipe is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients and fiber from the black-eyed peas. Whether you serve it as a main dish or a side, this soup is sure to impress your family and friends. So grab your apron and get ready to enjoy a bowl of Southern goodness that will have you coming back for seconds.
