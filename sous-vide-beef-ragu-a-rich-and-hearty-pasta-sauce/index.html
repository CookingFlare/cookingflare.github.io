Sous Vide Beef Ragu: A Rich and Hearty Pasta Sauce


If you’re looking for a pasta sauce that’s rich, flavorful, and deeply satisfying, then look no further than Sous Vide Beef Ragu. This classic Italian dish is a labor of love, requiring time and patience, but the results are well worth the effort. By using the sous vide cooking method, we can achieve tender, melt-in-your-mouth beef that’s infused with the flavors of aromatic herbs, tomatoes, and red wine. This slow-cooked beef ragu is the perfect companion to any pasta, delivering a truly indulgent dining experience.

The Magic of Sous Vide Cooking

Sous vide, which translates to “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique that involves sealing food in airtight bags and cooking it in a water bath at a precisely controlled temperature. This method allows the food to cook evenly and retain its natural juices, resulting in incredibly tender and flavorful dishes. When applied to beef ragu, sous vide ensures that the beef becomes succulent and tender, while also allowing the flavors to develop and intensify over time.

Ingredients You’ll Need

  • 2 lbs beef chuck roast, cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

The Sous Vide Process

  1. Preheat your water bath to 165°F (74°C).
  2. Season the beef chunks with salt and pepper, then vacuum-seal them in a bag with 1 tablespoon of olive oil.
  3. Submerge the bag in the water bath and cook for 24 hours.

Creating the Ragu

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, garlic, carrots, and celery, and sauté until softened.
  3. Pour in the red wine and simmer until it reduces by half.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, bay leaves, and thyme.
  5. Once the beef is done cooking, remove it from the bag and shred it using two forks.
  6. Add the shredded beef to the pan and let the ragu simmer for an additional 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Serving Suggestions

Sous Vide Beef Ragu is a versatile sauce that pairs beautifully with a variety of pasta shapes, such as pappardelle, tagliatelle, or rigatoni. The richness of the ragu also complements creamy polenta or mashed potatoes. For a finishing touch, garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of chopped parsley.

In Conclusion

Sous Vide Beef Ragu is a dish that embodies the essence of comfort food. Its deep, robust flavors and tender beef create a sauce that’s perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a cozy night in or a celebratory meal. With the sous vide method, the beef becomes the star of the show, elevating this classic pasta sauce to new heights of indulgence and satisfaction.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create a rich and hearty pasta sauce that will leave your taste buds singing. Enjoy the process, savor the aromas, and revel in the joy of creating a truly remarkable dish. Buon appetito!
