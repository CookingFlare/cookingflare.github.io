Carrillada de Cerdo: Spanish Braised Pork Cheeks


If you’re looking for a truly authentic and delicious Spanish dish, look no further than Carrillada de Cerdo, or Spanish Braised Pork Cheeks. This traditional dish is a favorite in Spain, known for its rich, tender, and flavorful pork cheeks braised to perfection. The slow cooking process allows the meat to become incredibly tender, making it a true delicacy that’s worth the time and effort. Whether you’re an experienced cook or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests.

Ingredients

To prepare Carrillada de Cerdo, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 pork cheeks
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig of rosemary
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • 2 cups of red wine
  • 2 cups of beef or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Pork Cheeks

Begin by thoroughly cleaning the pork cheeks and patting them dry with paper towels. Season the pork cheeks with salt and pepper, ensuring they are evenly coated on all sides.

Step 2: Searing the Pork Cheeks

In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the pork cheeks and sear them until they are browned on all sides. This process will help seal in the juices and enhance the flavor of the meat.

Step 3: Sauteing the Aromatics

Once the pork cheeks are nicely browned, remove them from the pot and set them aside. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, sliced carrots, and minced garlic. Saute the vegetables until they are soft and fragrant, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.

Step 4: Braising the Pork Cheeks

Return the seared pork cheeks to the pot, nestling them among the sauteed vegetables. Add the bay leaf, rosemary, and thyme, distributing them evenly around the pork cheeks. Pour in the red wine and broth, ensuring that the pork cheeks are partially submerged in the liquid.

Step 5: Slow Cooking

Bring the liquid to a gentle simmer, then cover the pot with a lid. Allow the pork cheeks to braise over low heat for approximately 2 to 2.5 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily falls apart with a fork.

Step 6: Serving

Once the pork cheeks are tender, remove them from the pot and transfer them to a serving platter. Strain the braising liquid and pour it over the pork cheeks, creating a luscious sauce. Serve the Carrillada de Cerdo with your choice of side dishes, such as creamy mashed potatoes, crusty bread, or a simple green salad.

Conclusion

Carrillada de Cerdo is a timeless Spanish dish that embodies the essence of slow cooking and traditional flavors. The succulent pork cheeks, infused with the aromatics and red wine, create a dish that is both comforting and elegant. Whether you’re preparing this dish for a special occasion or simply to indulge in a culinary adventure, Carrillada de Cerdo is a recipe that will undoubtedly leave a lasting impression. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a journey to savor the delightful flavors of Spanish Braised Pork Cheeks. Buena suerte!
