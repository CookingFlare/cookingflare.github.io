Spanish Orange and Almond Flan: A Citrusy Twist


If you’re looking for a dessert that perfectly captures the essence of Spanish cuisine, look no further than Spanish Orange and Almond Flan. This delightful dessert combines the zesty flavor of oranges with the nutty richness of almonds, creating a truly unique and indulgent treat. In this recipe, we’ll take a closer look at how to create this citrusy twist on a classic Spanish dessert.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 14-ounce can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 12-ounce can evaporated milk
  • Zest of 2 oranges
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1/2 cup ground almonds

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Caramel: In a small saucepan, combine the granulated sugar and water over medium heat. Stir gently until the sugar dissolves, then increase the heat to medium-high. Allow the mixture to boil without stirring, swirling the pan occasionally, until it turns a deep amber color. Immediately pour the caramel into the bottom of a 9-inch round cake pan, tilting the pan to evenly coat the bottom. Set aside to cool and harden.

  2. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  3. Prepare the Flan Mixture: In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, orange zest, orange juice, and almond extract until well combined. Gently fold in the ground almonds until evenly distributed.

  4. Assemble the Flan: Carefully pour the flan mixture over the hardened caramel in the cake pan.

  5. Bake the Flan: Place the cake pan in a larger roasting pan and fill the roasting pan with enough hot water to reach halfway up the sides of the cake pan. This water bath will help the flan cook evenly. Carefully transfer the roasting pan to the preheated oven and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the center of the flan is set but still slightly jiggly.

  6. Chill and Serve: Remove the flan from the water bath and let it cool to room temperature. Once cooled, cover the pan with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. When ready to serve, run a knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the flan. Place a serving plate on top of the pan, then quickly and carefully invert the flan onto the plate, allowing the caramel to drizzle over the top.

  7. Garnish and Enjoy: Serve the Spanish Orange and Almond Flan chilled, garnished with fresh orange slices or a sprinkle of toasted almond slices for an extra touch of elegance.

Conclusion

The Spanish Orange and Almond Flan is a delightful dessert that brings together the vibrant flavors of oranges and the nutty essence of almonds in every creamy bite. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of Spain, this citrusy twist on a traditional flan is sure to impress. With its silky texture and bright, refreshing flavor, it’s a perfect way to end any meal on a sweet note.

So, why not transport your taste buds to the sunny streets of Spain with this exquisite Spanish Orange and Almond Flan? Try this recipe and savor the delightful combination of citrus and nuttiness in every spoonful. It’s a dessert that’s as beautiful to look at as it is delicious to eat. Cheers to indulging in a taste of Spain with this delightful, citrus-infused flan!
