Homemade Tonkatsu: Crispy Breaded Pork Cutlets Done Right


Tonkatsu is a popular Japanese dish that features crispy breaded pork cutlets served with a tangy sauce and accompanied by rice and shredded cabbage. It’s a classic comfort food that is loved by many. While you may have enjoyed Tonkatsu at your favorite Japanese restaurant, have you ever considered making it at home? With this recipe, you can recreate the deliciousness of Homemade Tonkatsu right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless pork chops, about 1 inch thick
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This step is important as it will help keep the Tonkatsu warm while you finish frying the remaining cutlets.
  2. Start by seasoning the pork chops with salt and pepper on both sides. This will ensure that every bite is flavorful.
  3. Set up a breading station by placing the flour, beaten eggs, and panko breadcrumbs in three separate shallow dishes.
  4. Dredge each pork chop in the flour, making sure to coat it evenly. Shake off any excess flour.
  5. Dip the floured pork chops into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off.
  6. Finally, coat the pork chops with the panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere the breadcrumbs to the meat. Make sure to coat both sides thoroughly.
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. You’ll want the oil to be about 1/2 inch deep.
  8. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the breaded pork chops into the skillet, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  9. As you finish frying each cutlet, transfer it to a baking sheet lined with a wire rack. This will allow any excess oil to drain and keep the Tonkatsu crispy.
  10. Once all the pork cutlets are fried, place the baking sheet in the preheated oven for about 10 minutes. This step will ensure that the Tonkatsu is cooked through and remains warm.
  11. While the Tonkatsu is in the oven, you can prepare the accompanying sauce. You can find Tonkatsu sauce at most Asian grocery stores or make your own by combining Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, soy sauce, and sugar.
  12. Serve the Homemade Tonkatsu hot with steamed rice, shredded cabbage, and a drizzle of Tonkatsu sauce. The combination of the crispy pork cutlets, fluffy rice, and refreshing cabbage creates a delightful balance of flavors and textures.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra crisp and flavorful Tonkatsu, you can double coat the pork chops by repeating the breading process. This will create an even thicker and crunchier crust.
  • If you prefer a leaner option, you can use boneless, skinless chicken breasts instead of pork chops. The breading technique and cooking time will remain the same.
  • If you’re looking for a gluten-free alternative, you can replace the all-purpose flour with rice flour and use gluten-free panko breadcrumbs.
  • Tonkatsu is traditionally served with shredded cabbage, but you can also enjoy it with a side of steamed vegetables or a fresh salad.

Now that you have the recipe for Homemade Tonkatsu, it’s time to put on your apron and start cooking! With a few simple ingredients and a little bit of frying magic, you’ll be able to enjoy the crispy, golden goodness of Tonkatsu in the comfort of your own home. So go ahead, give it a try, and savor the flavors of this classic Japanese dish.
