Tasty Nut-Free Lemon Bars with Shortbread Crust

If you’re a fan of zesty, tangy desserts, then you’re in for a treat with this delicious recipe for nut-free lemon bars with a buttery shortbread crust. These delightful bars are perfect for any occasion, from picnics to potlucks, and they’re sure to impress your friends and family with their vibrant flavor and melt-in-your-mouth texture. Ingredients For the Shortbread Crust: 1 cup unsalted butter, softened 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 cups all-purpose flour 1/4 teaspoon salt For the Lemon Filling: 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 4 large eggs 2/3 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice Zest of 2 lemons Powdered sugar for dusting Instructions Step 1: Preparing the Shortbread Crust Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13 inch baking dish with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Gradually add the flour and salt to the butter mixture, mixing until a crumbly dough forms. Press the dough evenly into the prepared baking dish, using the back of a spoon to create a smooth, flat layer. Bake the crust for 20-25 minutes, or until it turns a light golden brown. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool while preparing the lemon filling. Step 2: Making the Lemon Filling In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and flour until well combined. Add the eggs, lemon juice, and lemon zest to the sugar mixture, whisking until smooth and creamy. Pour the lemon filling over the cooled shortbread crust, spreading it out evenly with a spatula. Return the baking dish to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are golden brown. Once done, remove the lemon bars from the oven and allow them to cool completely at room temperature. Step 3: Serving the Lemon Bars Once cooled, refrigerate the lemon bars for at least 2 hours to firm up before slicing. Use the parchment paper overhang to lift the bars out of the baking dish and transfer them to a cutting board. Dust the top of the bars with powdered sugar and slice into squares or rectangles. Serve the nut-free lemon bars chilled and enjoy the burst of citrusy goodness with every bite! Tips and Variations For a decorative touch, garnish the lemon bars with thin lemon slices or a sprig of fresh mint before serving. If you prefer a smoother texture, you can strain the lemon filling before pouring it onto the baked crust. Experiment with different citrus fruits such as limes or oranges to create unique variations of these delectable bars. Conclusion These nut-free lemon bars with a buttery shortbread crust are a delightful combination of sweet and tangy flavors, making them a delightful dessert for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a zesty treat, these lemon bars are sure to brighten up your day.
