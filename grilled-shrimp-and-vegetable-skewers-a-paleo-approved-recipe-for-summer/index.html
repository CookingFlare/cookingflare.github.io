Grilled Shrimp and Vegetable Skewers: A Paleo-Approved Recipe for Summer


As the sun shines bright and warm, our cravings for light, refreshing, and nutritious meals increase. What better way to satiate those cravings than with a delicious and easy-to-make recipe that’s perfect for the summer season? Introducing Grilled Shrimp and Vegetable Skewers, a paleo-approved recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your summer cooking repertoire.

The Magic of Skewers

Skewers are a summer cooking staple for many reasons. Not only do they allow for easy food preparation and grilling, but they also add a touch of elegance to any dish. And let’s be honest, who doesn’t love the visual appeal of colorful vegetables and succulent shrimp arranged neatly on a skewer?

The Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 red onion, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 zucchini, cut into 1-inch slices
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 10 bamboo skewers, soaked in water for at least 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the shrimp, bell peppers, onion, garlic, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes onto the bamboo skewers, leaving a small space between each piece.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Brush the skewers with the olive oil mixture, making sure each piece is coated evenly.
  5. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through.
  6. Remove the skewers from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, sprinkle some chopped fresh cilantro or parsley on top of the skewers before serving.
  • Swap out the smoked paprika for regular paprika if you prefer a milder flavor.
  • Add some heat to your skewers by sprinkling some red pepper flakes on top of the shrimp and vegetables.
  • Use different types of vegetables, such as mushrooms, asparagus, or bell squash, to change up the flavor and texture of the dish.

Paleo Perfection

This recipe is not only delicious, but it’s also paleo-approved! The shrimp and vegetables are free from grains, dairy, and refined sugars, making it a great option for those following a paleo diet.

Conclusion

Grilled Shrimp and Vegetable Skewers are the perfect recipe for a summer evening. Easy to make, delicious, and paleo-approved, this dish is sure to become a staple in your summer cooking repertoire. So go ahead, fire up your grill, and get ready to enjoy the taste of summer!
