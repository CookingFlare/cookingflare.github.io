Deliciously Nutty Pecan Tart: A Southern Classic


The sweet and nutty aroma of a freshly baked pecan tart is a staple of Southern cuisine, evoking memories of family gatherings, holidays, and warm summer days. This classic dessert is a staple in many Southern households, and for good reason - its rich, buttery crust and gooey, caramel-like filling are a match made in heaven. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of making a deliciously nutty pecan tart that’s sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup light corn syrup
  • 1/2 cup melted unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups pecan halves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

Making the Crust

To make the crust, combine the flour, salt, and cold butter in a large bowl. Use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Rolling Out the Crust

Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Trim the edges of the dough to fit the pan, and press the dough into the corners of the pan.

Filling the Tart

In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, light corn syrup, and melted butter until well combined. Stir in the vanilla extract. Add the pecan halves and stir until they’re evenly coated with the sugar mixture.

Assembling the Tart

Pour the pecan mixture into the prepared tart crust, spreading it evenly to the edges. Arrange the pecan halves in a decorative pattern, if desired.

Baking the Tart

Bake the tart for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly and caramelized. Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack.

Serving the Tart

Once the tart has cooled, remove it from the pan and transfer it to a serving plate. Slice the tart into wedges and serve warm or at room temperature. You can also garnish the tart with whipped cream or a sprinkle of sea salt, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To ensure that the crust doesn’t shrink too much during baking, make sure to chill it for at least 30 minutes before rolling it out.
  • If you find that your crust is too fragile, you can try adding a little more ice-cold water to the dough.
  • To add a little extra flavor to your tart, you can substitute the granulated sugar with brown sugar or add a teaspoon of bourbon or rum extract to the filling.
  • If you want to make individual pecan tarts, simply divide the dough and filling among 6-8 mini tart pans and bake for 20-25 minutes.

The deliciously nutty pecan tart is a true Southern classic, and with this recipe, you’ll be able to create a show-stopping dessert that’s sure to impress your friends and family. So why not give it a try? With its rich, buttery crust and gooey, caramel-like filling, this tart is sure to become a new favorite in your household.
