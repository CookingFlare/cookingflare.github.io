Sous Vide Fennel Salad with Citrus Dressing


Sous vide cooking has gained immense popularity in culinary circles for its precision and ability to elevate even the simplest ingredients. One dish that shines brighter through sous vide preparation is fennel salad. By gently cooking fennel with the sous vide method, you unlock its sweetness and tender texture while preserving its natural crunch. Complemented by a vibrant citrus dressing, this fennel salad is a celebration of fresh and zesty flavors that will brighten up your table.

Why Sous Vide Fennel?

Fennel, with its subtle licorice-like flavor, adds a unique dimension to dishes. While many enjoy it raw for its crisp texture, cooking fennel sous vide allows you to maintain its structure while unlocking deeper, caramelized notes. Unlike traditional stovetop cooking methods that can lead to uneven textures, sous vide ensures that the fennel is cooked consistently and evenly from edge to center.

Sous vide also gives you control over temperature and time, which is essential for maintaining the fennel’s natural sweetness. When paired with a citrus dressing, sous vide fennel becomes the star ingredient in a beautifully balanced salad.

Ingredients

Creating this Sous Vide Fennel Salad with Citrus Dressing requires simple, fresh ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the sous vide fennel:

  • 2 large fennel bulbs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

For the citrus dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey or agave syrup (optional for added sweetness)
  • ½ teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the salad assembly:

  • 1 cup mixed baby greens or arugula
  • 1 orange, segmented
  • ½ cup shaved Parmesan or crumbled goat cheese (optional)
  • ¼ cup toasted almond slivers
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (parsley or dill work beautifully)

Step-by-Step Recipe

1. Prepare the fennel for sous vide

Begin by washing the fennel bulbs thoroughly. Trim the stalks and fronds, and save them for garnish or future stock-making. Slice the bulbs into quarters or thick wedges, keeping the core intact to help the fennel stay together during cooking.

Place the sliced fennel in a vacuum-sealed bag or a resealable freezer bag suitable for sous vide cooking. Drizzle with olive oil, then season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Ensure the fennel wedges are coated evenly. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or by using the water displacement method.

2. Sous vide the fennel

Heat your sous vide water bath to 185°F (85°C). Submerge the bag of fennel into the water and cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour. This temperature and duration soften the fennel while retaining its slightly crisp texture.

Once cooked, carefully remove the fennel from the bag and set aside to cool slightly. The sous vide fennel can be prepared ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator until you’re ready to assemble the salad.

3. Make the citrus dressing

While the fennel is cooling, it’s time to create the bright, flavorful citrus dressing. In a small bowl or jar, combine freshly squeezed orange juice, lemon juice, orange zest, and lemon zest. Add the olive oil, honey (if using), Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to the mixture.

Whisk everything together until emulsified and smooth. Alternatively, you can shake the ingredients together in a lidded jar. Adjust the seasoning to taste, balancing the citrus tang with sweetness or saltiness as needed.

4. Assemble the salad

On a serving platter or individual plates, arrange the mixed greens or arugula as the base. Place the cooled fennel wedges on top, ensuring the slices are evenly spaced. Add the orange segments for a burst of color and sweetness.

Sprinkle shaved Parmesan or crumbled goat cheese over the salad for added richness and texture, if desired. Scatter toasted almond slivers to introduce crunch and nutty flavor. Finish with a drizzle of the citrus dressing over the entire salad.

For added freshness, garnish with chopped parsley, dill, or reserved fennel fronds.

Tips for Success

Choose the right equipment

Using a reliable sous vide machine and vacuum sealer will guarantee consistent results. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, the water displacement method works wonders.

Prep ahead of time

Sous vide fennel is a fantastic element to prep in advance. Cook it a day or two earlier, store it in the refrigerator in its sealed bag, and reheat gently before adding to your salad.

Enhance the flavor profile

While the base flavors of this dish are bright and citrus-forward, feel free to experiment with additional elements. Consider adding pomegranate arils, segments of grapefruit, or even a dash of chili flakes for a hint of spice.

Why You’ll Love This Salad

This Sous Vide Fennel Salad with Citrus Dressing is more than just a side dish; it’s a centerpiece for your table. Brimming with crisp, juicy fennel and bursting with citrusy brightness, it’s the perfect choice for any occasion—from a casual lunch to an elegant dinner party.

The sous vide method ensures unparalleled texture and flavor, while the citrus dressing ties the dish together with tang and sweetness. Every bite will transport you to a garden of vibrant greens, fresh fruits, and aromatic herbs.

Let this delicate yet flavorful recipe inspire your culinary creativity. Experiment with it, share it with friends and family, and make it a staple in your repertoire. From its precision sous vide preparation to the handmade dressing, this salad is a true testament to the magic of thoughtful cooking.
