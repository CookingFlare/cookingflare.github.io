Mediterranean Marvel: Grilled Greek Lamb Kebabs with Tzatziki


There’s something truly magical about the flavors of the Mediterranean, and these Grilled Greek Lamb Kebabs with Tzatziki are a perfect embodiment of that magic. Tender, succulent lamb marinated in a blend of herbs and spices, threaded onto skewers, and grilled to perfection, served with a cool and tangy tzatziki sauce – this dish is a celebration of bold flavors and simple, fresh ingredients.

Ingredients:

For the Lamb Kebabs:

  • 2 lbs. boneless lamb, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

For the Tzatziki Sauce:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, seeded and finely grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving:

  • Warm pita bread
  • Sliced red onions
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

Marinating the Lamb:

  1. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, oregano, thyme, lemon zest, and juice. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Add the lamb pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld.

Making the Tzatziki Sauce:

  1. Place the grated cucumber in a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth and squeeze out the excess moisture.
  2. In a bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, minced garlic, chopped dill, olive oil, and red wine vinegar. Season with salt and pepper. Stir well to combine. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

Assembling and Grilling the Kebabs:

  1. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning.
  2. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Thread the marinated lamb pieces onto the skewers, leaving a little space between each piece.
  4. Place the kebabs on the grill and cook for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the lamb is cooked to your desired level of doneness.

Serving:

  1. Warm the pita bread on the grill for a minute on each side.
  2. Serve the grilled lamb kebabs with warm pita bread, sliced red onions, tomatoes, a generous dollop of tzatziki sauce, and a sprinkle of fresh parsley.

Enjoy the Mediterranean Magic:

The aroma of the grilled lamb combined with the herby marinade is enough to transport you to a sun-soaked Greek island. The tender, juicy lamb, kissed by the flames of the grill, paired with the cool and refreshing tzatziki sauce, creates a flavor symphony that’s hard to resist. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, these Grilled Greek Lamb Kebabs with Tzatziki are sure to be a hit. So fire up the grill, gather your friends and family, and savor the magic of the Mediterranean with this delightful dish. Opa!
