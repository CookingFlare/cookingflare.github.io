Rustic Onion Tart: A French Twist on Pizza


If you’re a fan of pizza and looking to add a touch of French flair to your next meal, then you’re in for a treat. Today, we’ll be sharing a delicious recipe for Rustic Onion Tart, a mouthwatering dish that combines the familiar flavors of pizza with the rustic charm of French cuisine. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
  • 4-6 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh thyme sprigs for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt for the crust. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing with a fork until the dough comes together. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough is chilling, prepare the filling. In a large skillet, melt the butter and olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are caramelized and golden brown, about 20 minutes. Stir in the fresh thyme leaves, salt, and black pepper.

  5. Remove the dough from the refrigerator and roll it out on a lightly floured surface into a circle, about 12 inches in diameter. Transfer the rolled-out dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.

  6. Spread the caramelized onions evenly over the dough, leaving a border of about 1 inch around the edges. Sprinkle the shredded Gruyere cheese and grated Parmesan cheese over the onions.

  7. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling, creating a rustic crust. Brush the edges of the dough with a beaten egg for a golden finish.

  8. Bake the tart in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  9. Allow the tart to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Garnish with fresh thyme sprigs, if desired.

Serving Suggestions

This Rustic Onion Tart is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in various ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to inspire your culinary creativity:

  • Serve it as an appetizer: Cut the tart into small squares or triangles and serve it as a delightful appetizer at your next gathering. Your guests will love the combination of flavors and textures.

  • Make it a main course: Pair this tart with a crisp green salad for a satisfying vegetarian meal. The sweetness of the caramelized onions and the richness of the cheese create a perfect balance of flavors.

  • Enjoy it as a side dish: This tart is a fantastic accompaniment to roasted meats or grilled vegetables. The savory onion filling adds depth to any meal.

Final Thoughts

With its crispy crust, caramelized onions, and gooey cheese, this Rustic Onion Tart is a French twist on pizza that will transport your taste buds to the charming streets of Paris. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a unique and flavorful dish, this recipe is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to savor the deliciousness of this delightful tart. Bon appétit!
