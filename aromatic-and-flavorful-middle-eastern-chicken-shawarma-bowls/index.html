Aromatic and Flavorful: Middle Eastern Chicken Shawarma Bowls


If you’re looking for a dish that’s bursting with aromatic flavors and Middle Eastern flair, then you’ve come to the right place. In this recipe, we’re going to show you how to make delicious and satisfying Middle Eastern Chicken Shawarma Bowls. Packed with tender marinated chicken, fragrant spices, and a medley of fresh vegetables, this dish is a feast for the senses.

Ingredients

For the chicken marinade:

  • 2 pounds chicken breasts, boneless and skinless
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the shawarma bowls:

  • Cooked basmati rice or quinoa
  • Fresh lettuce or salad greens
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced red onions
  • Chopped fresh parsley
  • Tahini sauce or yogurt sauce for drizzling

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all the marinade ingredients and whisk them together until well combined.

  2. Slice the chicken breasts into thin strips and add them to the marinade mixture. Make sure every piece is well coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.

  3. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and discard any excess marinade.

  4. Grill the chicken for about 6-8 minutes per side, or until cooked through and slightly charred. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.

  5. While the chicken is resting, prepare the shawarma bowls. Start by layering a bed of cooked basmati rice or quinoa in each bowl.

  6. Top the rice or quinoa with a generous amount of fresh lettuce or salad greens.

  7. Arrange the sliced cucumbers, tomatoes, and red onions on top of the greens.

  8. Slice the grilled chicken into thin strips and place them on top of the vegetables.

  9. Sprinkle the bowls with freshly chopped parsley for added freshness and flavor.

  10. Finally, drizzle tahini sauce or yogurt sauce over the bowls to complete the Middle Eastern experience.

  11. Serve the Chicken Shawarma Bowls immediately and enjoy the explosion of flavors in every bite.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian twist, you can substitute the chicken with grilled tofu or roasted chickpeas.
  • Feel free to add other Middle Eastern-inspired toppings such as pickled turnips, olives, or crumbled feta cheese.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, sprinkle some chili flakes or drizzle hot sauce over the bowls.
  • These bowls can be meal-prepped in advance by storing the individual components separately and assembling them when ready to eat.

Conclusion

There you have it – a recipe for aromatic and flavorful Middle Eastern Chicken Shawarma Bowls that will transport you to the streets of the Middle East. With its tantalizing spices, juicy grilled chicken, and a colorful array of fresh vegetables, this dish is a true delight for the senses. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the grill, and get ready to indulge in a culinary adventure like no other. Bon appétit!
