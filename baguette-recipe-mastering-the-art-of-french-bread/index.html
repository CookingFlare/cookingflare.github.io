Baguette Recipe: Mastering the Art of French Bread


If there’s one iconic bread that represents the essence of French baking, it’s the beloved baguette. With its crisp crust, chewy interior, and unmistakable aroma, the baguette is a staple of French cuisine and a delight for bread enthusiasts around the world. In this recipe, we’ll delve into the art of crafting the perfect baguette right in your own kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s embark on a journey to master the art of French bread!

Ingredients

For the Poolish:

  • 1 cup (120g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup (240ml) water
  • 1/4 teaspoon (1g) instant yeast

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups (360g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons (9g) salt
  • 1 cup (240ml) water
  • 1/4 teaspoon (1g) instant yeast

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Poolish

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the flour, water, and instant yeast. Stir until well mixed.
  2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it sit at room temperature for 12-16 hours. The poolish should have a bubbly and fermented aroma when ready.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and instant yeast.
  2. Add the water and the prepared poolish to the dry ingredients.
  3. Mix the dough until it comes together and forms a slightly sticky, shaggy mass.

Step 3: Kneading

  1. Lightly flour a clean work surface and turn the dough out onto it.
  2. Knead the dough for about 10-12 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

Step 4: First Rise

  1. Place the kneaded dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rise for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 5: Shaping

  1. Once the dough has risen, gently deflate it and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface.
  2. Divide the dough into 3 equal portions.
  3. Shape each portion into a baguette by gently rolling and elongating the dough.

Step 6: Final Rise

  1. Place the shaped baguettes onto a well-floured couche or baguette pan, seam side up.
  2. Cover them with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise for 45-60 minutes, or until they have visibly puffed up.

Step 7: Preparing the Oven

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. Place a baking stone or an overturned baking sheet on the middle rack of the oven.

Step 8: Scoring and Baking

  1. Using a sharp knife or a razor blade, make 3-4 diagonal cuts on the surface of each baguette.
  2. Slide the baguettes onto the preheated baking stone or sheet.
  3. Quickly spray the inside of the oven with water to create steam and immediately close the oven door.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the baguettes are deeply golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Step 9: Cooling

  1. Once baked, transfer the baguettes to a wire rack and let them cool for at least 20 minutes before slicing.

Enjoy Your Masterpiece!

Congratulations, you’ve just mastered the art of French bread by creating your own batch of delectable baguettes! Whether you choose to enjoy them with a smear of creamy butter, as a sandwich vessel, or alongside a flavorful soup, these homemade baguettes are sure to elevate any meal. So, savor the fruits of your labor and relish in the satisfaction of baking your very own authentic French baguettes.

Remember, baking is an art, and mastering the baguette is a testament to your dedication to the craft. Embrace the delicate balance of simplicity and precision that defines this timeless bread, and let your kitchen be filled with the aroma of freshly baked baguettes. Bon appétit!
