Indulgent Chocolate Peanut Butter Cake: A Match Made in Heaven


Who can resist the combination of rich chocolate and creamy peanut butter? It’s a match made in heaven that never fails to satisfy even the most discerning sweet tooth. If you’re a fan of this indulgent pairing, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for a decadent Chocolate Peanut Butter Cake.

Ingredients

For the cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water

For the peanut butter frosting:

  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the chocolate ganache:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 ounces semisweet chocolate, chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.

  3. Add the milk, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Mix on medium speed for about 2 minutes, until well combined.

  4. Stir in the boiling water. The batter will be thin, but that’s normal.

  5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cake pans. Bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.

  6. Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the pans for 10 minutes. Then, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  7. While the cakes are cooling, prepare the peanut butter frosting. In a mixing bowl, beat the peanut butter and softened butter until smooth and creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract, and continue beating until the frosting is light and fluffy.

  8. Place one cake layer on a serving plate and spread a generous amount of peanut butter frosting on top. Repeat with the remaining cake layers.

  9. Once the cake layers are stacked and frosted, it’s time to make the chocolate ganache. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it begins to simmer. Remove from heat and add the chopped chocolate. Let it sit for a minute, then whisk until smooth and shiny.

  10. Pour the chocolate ganache over the top of the cake, allowing it to drip down the sides. Use a spatula to spread the ganache evenly over the cake, if needed.

  11. Place the cake in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes to allow the ganache to set.

  12. Remove the cake from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes before serving. Slice and enjoy the heavenly combination of chocolate and peanut butter!

Final Thoughts

Indulging in a slice of this Chocolate Peanut Butter Cake is like experiencing a little slice of heaven on your taste buds. The rich, moist chocolate cake layers perfectly complement the creamy, nutty peanut butter frosting, while the luscious chocolate ganache adds an extra layer of decadence. Whether it’s for a special occasion or simply a craving for something sweet, this cake is sure to impress. So go ahead, treat yourself and your loved ones to this irresistible dessert. You won’t regret it!
