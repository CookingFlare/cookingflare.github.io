Sensational Soups: Warm and Nourishing Recipes for Cooking with Kids


When it comes to cooking with kids, there’s something magical about making a pot of warm, nourishing soup together. Not only is it a great way to bond with your little ones, but it also provides an opportunity to introduce them to a variety of flavors and textures. Plus, the process of chopping, stirring, and tasting can be a fun and educational experience for children of all ages. In this blog post, we’ll explore some sensational soup recipes that are not only delicious but also perfect for cooking with kids. From classic favorites to creative twists, these recipes are sure to warm your hearts and tummies.

Creamy Tomato Basil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (28 ounces) whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the tomatoes and broth. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Using a wooden spoon, crush the tomatoes to release their juices.
  3. Add the fresh basil and continue to simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Using an immersion blender, puree the soup until smooth.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes, then serve hot with a sprinkle of fresh basil on top.

This classic creamy tomato basil soup is a hit with kids and adults alike. The vibrant color and rich, comforting flavor make it an ideal choice for a cozy family meal. Let your little ones help with crushing the tomatoes and stirring in the cream. It’s a simple yet rewarding recipe that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Hearty Chicken and Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 cup uncooked pasta (such as rotini or shells)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the whole chicken breasts and simmer for 15-20 minutes, until cooked through.
  3. Remove the chicken from the pot and shred it using two forks.
  4. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and add the chopped carrots, celery, and onion.
  5. Simmer for 10 minutes, then add the pasta and continue to cook until the pasta is tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

This hearty chicken and vegetable soup is a wholesome and satisfying meal that’s perfect for chilly days. It’s a great way to sneak in some extra veggies and lean protein into your kids’ diet. Let them help with shredding the chicken and adding the vegetables to the pot. The process of building the flavors and textures will keep them engaged and excited for the final dish.

Cheesy Broccoli Cheddar Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups broccoli florets
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the broth to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the broccoli florets and simmer for 8-10 minutes, until tender.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk and flour until smooth.
  4. Slowly pour the milk mixture into the pot, stirring constantly.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until melted and smooth.
  6. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

This cheesy broccoli cheddar soup is a kid-friendly way to get them excited about eating their greens. The creamy texture and cheesy goodness make it a winner in the flavor department. Let your little ones help with whisking the milk and flour together and stirring in the cheese. It’s a great opportunity to teach them about the science of melting cheese and thickening soups.

Conclusion

Cooking with kids can be a delightful and rewarding experience, especially when you’re making sensational soups together. These warm and nourishing recipes are not only delicious but also offer a fantastic opportunity to bond with your children in the kitchen. So, grab your aprons, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create some heartwarming memories with these delightful soup recipes. Happy cooking!
