Beginner's Guide to Perfectly Grilled BBQ Chicken


Summer is here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to fire up the grill and enjoy some delicious BBQ chicken. But, if you’re a beginner, the thought of grilling chicken can be intimidating. Don’t worry, with this beginner’s guide, you’ll be on your way to perfectly grilled BBQ chicken in no time.

Choosing the Right Chicken

Before we dive into the grilling process, let’s talk about choosing the right chicken. For BBQ chicken, you’ll want to use boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs. If you’re feeling adventurous, you can also use a combination of both. Make sure to choose chicken that is fresh and of high quality.

Preparing the Chicken

Once you have your chicken, it’s time to prepare it for grilling. Start by rinsing the chicken under cold water and patting it dry with paper towels. This will help the chicken cook more evenly and prevent it from sticking to the grill.

Next, season the chicken with your favorite spices and herbs. You can use a pre-mixed seasoning blend or create your own using paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Don’t be afraid to get creative and add your own unique flavors.

Grilling the Chicken

Now it’s time to fire up the grill. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat, or about 375°F (190°C). Once the grill is hot, place the chicken on the grill and close the lid. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is seared and has nice grill marks.

After 5-7 minutes, flip the chicken over and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). Use a meat thermometer to ensure the chicken is cooked to a safe internal temperature.

Glazing the Chicken

Once the chicken is cooked, it’s time to add the BBQ sauce. You can use a store-bought BBQ sauce or make your own using ketchup, brown sugar, vinegar, and spices. Brush the BBQ sauce onto the chicken and let it cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce is caramelized and sticky.

Serving the Chicken

Once the chicken is glazed, it’s ready to serve. You can serve it hot with your favorite sides, such as coleslaw, baked beans, or corn on the cob. You can also add some extra toppings, such as chopped onions, bell peppers, or jalapeños.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to keep in mind when grilling BBQ chicken:

  • Make sure to oil the grates before cooking to prevent the chicken from sticking.
  • Don’t overcrowd the grill, cook the chicken in batches if necessary.
  • You can also add some wood chips, such as hickory or apple, to the grill for added smoky flavor.
  • Experiment with different BBQ sauces and seasonings to find your favorite flavor combination.
  • You can also grill chicken breasts or thighs with the bone in for added flavor and moisture.

Conclusion

Grilling BBQ chicken is a simple and delicious way to enjoy a summer meal. With these beginner’s tips and tricks, you’ll be on your way to perfectly grilled chicken in no time. Don’t be afraid to experiment and try new things, and remember to always use a meat thermometer to ensure the chicken is cooked to a safe internal temperature. Happy grilling!
