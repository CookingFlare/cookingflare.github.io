Summer Vegetable and Cheese Frittata Recipe


Whether you’re looking for a delightful brunch dish, a light dinner option, or a flavorful addition to your summer menu, a Summer Vegetable and Cheese Frittata is the perfect solution. This versatile and easy-to-make dish combines the freshness of seasonal vegetables with the rich creaminess of cheese, all baked together in a fluffy egg base. Let’s dive into the steps to create this mouthwatering frittata that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C) to ensure it’s ready when your frittata is prepared.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced red onion and cook until translucent. Then, add the red bell pepper and zucchini, sautéing until they are slightly tender. Toss in the cherry tomatoes and spinach, cooking until the spinach wilts. Season the vegetables with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Whisk the Eggs: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. This mixture will serve as the base of your frittata.

  4. Combine Ingredients: Pour the egg mixture over the sautéed vegetables in the skillet. Stir gently to distribute the vegetables evenly in the egg mixture. Allow the frittata to cook undisturbed for a few minutes until the edges start to set.

  5. Add Cheese: Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese over the top of the frittata. This will add a creamy and tangy flavor to the dish.

  6. Bake the Frittata: Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for about 15-20 minutes or until the frittata is set in the middle and the top is lightly golden brown.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the frittata is cooked to perfection, remove it from the oven. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and added freshness. Slice the frittata into wedges and serve hot.

Tips and Variations:

  • Feel free to customize this frittata recipe with your favorite summer vegetables such as corn, asparagus, or mushrooms.
  • Experiment with different types of cheese like cheddar, mozzarella, or goat cheese for varied flavors.
  • For a heartier meal, add cooked bacon, ham, or sausage to the frittata mixture.
  • Leftover frittata can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days and enjoyed cold or reheated.

With its vibrant colors, fresh ingredients, and rich flavors, the Summer Vegetable and Cheese Frittata is a delightful dish that captures the essence of the season. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply treating yourself to a flavorful meal, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. Enjoy the process of creating this delicious frittata and savor every bite of this savory summer delight!
