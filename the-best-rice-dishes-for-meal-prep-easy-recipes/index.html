The Best Rice Dishes for Meal Prep: Easy Recipes


Meal prepping has become a popular and practical way to save time, reduce stress, and maintain a healthy lifestyle. Rice is one of the most versatile ingredients for meal prep, offering endless possibilities for creating satisfying and nutritious meals. Whether you’re preparing lunch for the week or need a base for a quick dinner, rice can be the foundation for a variety of flavorful dishes that are easy to make, store, and reheat. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best rice dishes for meal prep, featuring diverse flavors and ingredients to keep your meals exciting.

1. Chicken Fried Rice

Chicken fried rice is a classic dish that’s quick to prepare and perfect for meal prepping. It combines tender chicken with vegetables and rice, all sautéed together in a savory sauce. This recipe is not only flavorful but also offers a balanced meal with protein, fiber, and healthy fats.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine or brown rice (preferably day-old rice for the best texture)
  • 2 chicken breasts, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the rice: If you haven’t already, cook your rice and let it cool completely. Day-old rice works best because it’s less sticky and holds up better during stir-frying.
  2. Cook the chicken: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced chicken and cook until browned and cooked through. Season with salt and pepper. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.
  3. Sauté the vegetables: In the same skillet, add sesame oil and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant and soft. Add the frozen peas and carrots, and cook for a few minutes until tender.
  4. Scramble the eggs: Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet. Crack the eggs into the empty side and scramble them until cooked through.
  5. Combine the ingredients: Add the cooled rice and cooked chicken back into the skillet. Stir everything together and pour in the soy sauce. Continue cooking for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Store and serve: Divide the chicken fried rice into individual meal prep containers and garnish with green onions. Refrigerate for up to 4 days.

Meal Prep Tip:

This dish is perfect for storing in the fridge. It reheats well in the microwave and stays flavorful for several days, making it ideal for meal prepping.

2. Vegetable Stir-Fry Rice

For a meatless option that’s still packed with flavor, vegetable stir-fry rice is an excellent choice. This dish is full of colorful vegetables and stir-fried rice, making it not only delicious but visually appealing. You can use a variety of vegetables based on what you have on hand or what’s in season.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white or brown rice
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the rice: If you haven’t already, cook your rice and set it aside to cool.
  2. Cook the vegetables: Heat olive oil and sesame oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds. Add the carrots, bell peppers, and broccoli to the pan. Stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but still vibrant.
  3. Stir-fry the rice: Add the cooked rice to the skillet, tossing it with the vegetables. Pour in the soy sauce and hoisin sauce, and stir everything together. Cook for an additional 3-5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Garnish and serve: Top with sesame seeds and fresh cilantro for added flavor and texture. Divide into meal prep containers and refrigerate for up to 4 days.

Meal Prep Tip:

Vegetable stir-fry rice is great for meal prepping because it can easily be reheated and eaten with minimal fuss. The colorful vegetables make this dish not only nutritious but also appetizing after a long day.

3. Mexican Rice Bowls

Mexican rice bowls are a fun and customizable way to enjoy rice. This dish brings together rice, beans, veggies, and flavorful toppings to create a well-rounded meal. You can adjust the ingredients based on your preferences, making it a versatile option for meal prep.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked brown rice
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or your favorite cheese)
  • 1/4 cup salsa
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the rice: Cook your rice according to package directions, then fluff and set aside.
  2. Sauté the vegetables: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the corn, black beans, chili powder, cumin, and garlic powder. Stir for 3-5 minutes, until heated through and slightly caramelized.
  3. Assemble the bowls: In each meal prep container, layer a portion of rice, followed by the sautéed beans and corn mixture. Top with shredded cheese, diced avocado, and a spoonful of salsa.
  4. Garnish and serve: Add a lime wedge to each container for a burst of freshness before eating. Store in the fridge for up to 4 days.

Meal Prep Tip:

Mexican rice bowls can be customized with additional toppings such as sour cream, cilantro, or even grilled chicken or beef. The rice and bean mixture can be made in advance, and the toppings can be added when ready to serve for a fresh meal every time.

4. Curry Rice with Chickpeas

This dish is a comforting, plant-based option that’s packed with rich flavors and nutrition. The combination of curry spices and chickpeas makes this rice dish filling, hearty, and perfect for meal prep.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked basmati or jasmine rice
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the rice: Cook your rice and set it aside.
  2. Sauté the aromatics: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, garlic, and ginger. Cook until the vegetables soften, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chickpeas and spices: Stir in the chickpeas, curry powder, and turmeric. Cook for another 2 minutes, allowing the spices to toast and release their fragrance.
  4. Simmer the coconut milk: Pour in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper. Stir everything together, then let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine.
  5. Combine with rice: Add the cooked rice to the skillet and stir until everything is well mixed and heated through.
  6. Garnish and serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro, and store in meal prep containers. Refrigerate for up to 4 days.

Meal Prep Tip:

This curry rice with chickpeas is great for meal prepping because the flavors only improve after sitting for a day or two. Reheat in the microwave and enjoy a warm, comforting meal in no time.

5. Teriyaki Salmon Rice Bowls

For a light and healthy meal prep option, teriyaki salmon rice bowls are a perfect choice. The salmon is rich in omega-3s and pairs beautifully with the savory teriyaki sauce and fluffy rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets
  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • 1/4 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Cook the salmon: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and place the salmon fillets on it. Drizzle with olive oil and teriyaki sauce. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  2. Assemble the bowls: Divide the cooked rice between meal prep containers. Top with a portion of salmon, sliced cucumber, and avocado.
  3. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle with sesame seeds and chopped green onions. Refrigerate for up to 3 days.

Meal Prep Tip:

The teriyaki salmon rice bowls are a delicious and healthy option for meal prep. The salmon stays moist and flavorful in the fridge, and the bowls are easy to assemble, making them perfect for a quick, nourishing lunch or dinner.

Conclusion

Rice is a meal prep hero, offering endless possibilities for delicious, filling, and nutritious dishes. From classic fried rice to vibrant Mexican rice bowls and flavorful curry rice, there’s a rice dish for everyone. These recipes are easy to make, store well in the fridge, and reheat beautifully. By incorporating a variety of vegetables, proteins, and sauces, you can keep your meal prep exciting and satisfying week after week. So grab some rice, get cooking, and start preparing your meals in advance to save time and enjoy tasty, homemade dishes all week long!
