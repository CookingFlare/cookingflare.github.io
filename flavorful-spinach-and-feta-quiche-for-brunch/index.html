Flavorful Spinach and Feta Quiche for Brunch


Brunch is a cherished meal, a delightful amalgamation of breakfast and lunch where flavors collide and the atmosphere is often relaxed and inviting. Among the myriad of dishes that grace brunch tables, the Spinach and Feta Quiche stands out as a beacon of flavor and nourishment. This savory pie is not only pleasing to the palate but also visually appealing, making it perfect for gatherings, brunch parties, or a cozy family breakfast. In this blog post, we will explore the ins and outs of preparing a delectable Spinach and Feta Quiche that will impress your guests and leave them coming back for seconds.

The Allure of Quiche

Quiche is a versatile dish, originating from France, that has transcended borders and cultures. Its flaky crust envelops a rich custard filling, which can be customized with a variety of ingredients. Spinach and feta cheese create a flavorful filling that is both nutritious and satisfying. Spinach is packed with vitamins and minerals, while feta adds a tangy creaminess that elevates the overall taste.

Why Spinach and Feta?

Choosing spinach and feta for your quiche is a decision rooted in flavor and health. Spinach is a superfood, rich in iron, calcium, and vitamins A, C, and K. It brings a mild earthiness to the dish, balancing out the sharpness of feta cheese. Feta, with its crumbly texture and salty flavor, enhances the quiche, making each bite a delightful experience. Together, they create a harmonious blend that is not only delicious but also nourishing.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To embark on this culinary adventure, gather the following ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 to 5 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped (or 1/2 cup frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or whole milk for a lighter option)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese for added flavor
  • Optional: Fresh herbs (such as dill or basil) for garnish

Preparing the Crust

  1. Make the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled butter and mix using your fingertips or a pastry cutter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together. Avoid overworking the dough.

  2. Chill the Dough: Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This resting period allows the gluten to relax, ensuring a tender crust.

  3. Roll Out the Dough: Once chilled, roll the dough on a lightly floured surface into a circle about 1/8 inch thick. Carefully transfer it to a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it into the bottom and up the sides. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges.

  4. Pre-bake the Crust: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Poke the bottom of the crust with a fork to prevent bubbling. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment, and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes until lightly golden. Let it cool slightly.

Making the Filling

  1. Prepare the Spinach: If using fresh spinach, sauté it in a skillet over medium heat until wilted, about 2-3 minutes. For frozen spinach, ensure it is thoroughly thawed and drained to remove excess moisture, as this can make the quiche soggy.

  2. Mix the Egg Base: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. If you enjoy a bit of extra flavor, mix in the grated Parmesan cheese at this stage.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Gently fold in the sautéed spinach and crumbled feta cheese into the egg mixture until well incorporated.

Assembling the Quiche

  1. Pour the Filling: Carefully pour the spinach and feta mixture into the pre-baked crust. Use a spatula to ensure an even distribution of ingredients, ensuring the spinach and feta are spread throughout the filling.

  2. Bake the Quiche: Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is lightly golden. The center should be firm but still slightly jiggles when shaken gently.

  3. Cool and Serve: Once baked, remove the quiche from the oven and allow it to cool for about 10-15 minutes before slicing. This resting time helps the filling to set further, making it easier to cut clean slices.

Serving Suggestions

The Spinach and Feta Quiche is delightful on its own, but it can also be paired with a variety of accompaniments to elevate your brunch experience:

  • Fresh Salad: A light side salad with mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, and a tangy vinaigrette will complement the quiche beautifully.
  • Fruit Platter: Offer a refreshing fruit platter with seasonal fruits such as berries, melons, and citrus for a burst of sweetness.
  • Beverages: Serve with mimosas, freshly brewed coffee, or herbal teas to round out the meal.

Storing Leftovers

If you find yourself with leftovers (which is unlikely, considering how delicious this quiche is), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply place the slices in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10-15 minutes, or until warmed through. This quiche also freezes well; wrap individual slices in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer-safe bag for up to 2 months. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Variations to Try

While the classic Spinach and Feta Quiche is a crowd-pleaser, don’t hesitate to experiment with different ingredients to suit your taste or dietary preferences:

  • Add Protein: Incorporate cooked bacon, ham, or sautéed mushrooms for an extra layer of flavor.
  • Cheese Swap: Try goat cheese or shredded mozzarella in place of feta for a different taste profile.
  • Vegetable Medley: Add bell peppers, onions, or sun-dried tomatoes for an additional burst of flavor and color.

Conclusion

The Flavorful Spinach and Feta Quiche is a stunning centerpiece for any brunch table, combining rich flavors and wholesome ingredients that cater to a variety of tastes. Its ease of preparation and adaptability make it a go-to dish for both seasoned cooks and kitchen novices. Whether you’re hosting a brunch gathering or enjoying a quiet Sunday morning, this quiche promises to be a delightful addition to your meal. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to create a dish that will have everyone raving and requesting the recipe. Enjoy!
