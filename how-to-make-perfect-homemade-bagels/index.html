How to Make Perfect Homemade Bagels


Bagels are more than just a breakfast staple; they’re a comforting delight enjoyed any time of the day. With their crisp, golden-brown crust and chewy interior, homemade bagels surpass anything you’ll find pre-made in stores. The best part? Making your own bagels from scratch is easier than you think. With the right ingredients, a little patience, and a dash of love, you can create delicious, soft, and chewy bagels right in your own kitchen.

The Basics of Bagel Making

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a look at the essential ingredients and equipment you’ll need to make perfect homemade bagels.

Ingredients for Homemade Bagels

Making bagels involves basic pantry ingredients, but there are a few specifics you’ll want to focus on:

  • All-purpose flour: This is the base of your dough. You’ll also see bread flour used in some recipes, but all-purpose flour works wonderfully to create a chewy yet soft bagel.
  • Active dry yeast: Yeast is responsible for the rise and texture of your bagels. Always ensure your yeast is fresh.
  • Water: Use warm water to activate the yeast. Make sure it’s not too hot to avoid killing the yeast.
  • Sugar: A small amount of sugar helps activate the yeast and also gives a slight sweetness to your bagels.
  • Salt: This is crucial for flavor balance.
  • Barley malt syrup: This ingredient helps create that perfect bagel crust, giving it a distinctive sheen and taste. If you don’t have barley malt syrup, you can substitute with honey, but the taste will be slightly different.
  • Optional toppings: Sesame seeds, poppy seeds, everything seasoning, or coarse sea salt to sprinkle on top of your bagels.

Equipment Needed

  • Mixing bowl: For combining the ingredients.
  • Stand mixer (optional): If you have one, it can help knead the dough more efficiently, but you can also knead by hand.
  • Large pot: Used for boiling the bagels before baking, which gives them that distinct chewy texture.
  • Baking sheet: For placing the bagels on before baking.
  • Parchment paper: Helps prevent sticking and makes for easier cleanup.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Homemade Bagels

Making bagels might sound like a complex process, but it’s all about following the right steps. Once you’ve gathered your ingredients and equipment, here’s how to make perfect homemade bagels:

Step 1: Activating the Yeast

Begin by activating your yeast. This is a crucial step to ensure your bagels rise properly. In a small bowl, combine 1 ½ cups of warm water with 1 tablespoon of sugar. Sprinkle in 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast. Let the mixture sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy. If the mixture doesn’t froth, your yeast may be expired, and you’ll need to try again with fresh yeast.

Step 2: Making the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine 4 cups of all-purpose flour and 1 ½ teaspoons of salt. Once your yeast mixture is frothy, pour it into the flour and stir using a wooden spoon or spatula. Gradually incorporate the flour into the liquid to form a dough.

If you’re using a stand mixer, attach the dough hook and mix on low speed until the dough starts to come together. Then, increase the speed to medium and knead for about 8-10 minutes. The dough should be smooth and elastic, but still slightly sticky. If you’re kneading by hand, turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for the same amount of time.

Step 3: Let the Dough Rise

Once your dough is smooth and elastic, shape it into a ball and place it into a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place for 1 to 1 ½ hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 4: Shaping the Bagels

After the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air bubbles. Turn the dough out onto a clean surface and divide it into 8 to 10 equal pieces, depending on how large you want your bagels.

To shape the bagels, take each piece of dough and roll it into a ball. Then, poke your finger through the center of each ball to create a hole. Gently stretch the hole to form a ring shape. The hole should be about 1 ½ inches in diameter, as the bagels will shrink slightly during the boiling and baking process.

Step 5: Boiling the Bagels

Boiling is what gives bagels their signature chewy texture. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add 1-2 tablespoons of barley malt syrup or honey to the water for extra flavor and shine. Carefully drop each shaped bagel into the boiling water, a few at a time. Boil each bagel for about 1 minute per side, flipping them with a slotted spoon. After boiling, place the bagels on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

Step 6: Topping Your Bagels

This is the fun part where you can get creative! If you’d like to top your bagels, now is the time. Before baking, sprinkle each bagel with your choice of toppings. Popular options include sesame seeds, poppy seeds, everything seasoning, or coarse sea salt.

Step 7: Baking the Bagels

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place the baking sheet with the bagels into the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the bagels are golden brown and crisp. Be sure to check on them as they bake to ensure they don’t overcook. The bagels should have a beautiful golden crust and a firm, chewy interior.

Step 8: Cooling and Enjoying

Once your bagels are baked to perfection, remove them from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack for about 10 minutes before slicing. Cutting into a fresh, homemade bagel is a truly satisfying experience. The smell is irresistible, and the taste is like nothing you’ll get from store-bought bagels.

Optional Additions

If you want to go beyond classic plain bagels, there are plenty of options to add unique flavors or ingredients into the dough or as toppings. Here are a few ideas:

  • Cinnamon raisin bagels: Add 1 teaspoon of cinnamon and 1 cup of raisins to the dough before kneading.
  • Cheese bagels: Mix shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or Parmesan) into the dough or sprinkle on top before baking.
  • Herb bagels: Add fresh or dried herbs like rosemary, thyme, or garlic powder to the dough for a savory twist.
  • Whole wheat bagels: Replace half of the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour for a healthier alternative.

Troubleshooting Common Bagel Issues

While making bagels is relatively simple, some challenges can arise. Here are a few common issues you might encounter and how to solve them:

  • Bagels not rising: This could be due to inactive yeast. Always check the expiration date of your yeast and ensure the water temperature is warm, not hot.
  • Bagels too dense: If your dough is too heavy or dense, try kneading it a little longer to develop the gluten. This will help the bagels rise better and become chewier.
  • Bagels not crispy enough: If you prefer a crispier crust, you can bake the bagels on a pizza stone instead of a baking sheet. Also, increase the baking time slightly, checking the bagels every few minutes to avoid overbaking.

Tips for Storing and Freezing Homemade Bagels

Fresh bagels are always best enjoyed the day they’re made, but you can store them for later enjoyment as well. Let the bagels cool completely, then store them in an airtight container or a resealable plastic bag at room temperature for up to 3 days.

For longer storage, you can freeze bagels. Slice them before freezing so you can easily toast them directly from the freezer. Wrap each bagel individually in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer-safe bag. When you’re ready to enjoy, simply toast the bagel straight from the freezer for a warm, freshly baked taste.

Final Thoughts

Making homemade bagels is a fun and rewarding process. While it may take some time and effort, the results are well worth it. Freshly baked bagels have a flavor and texture that’s unbeatable, and they’re a great way to impress family and friends. With this step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to create perfect homemade bagels right in your own kitchen.

Happy baking, and enjoy your delicious homemade bagels!
