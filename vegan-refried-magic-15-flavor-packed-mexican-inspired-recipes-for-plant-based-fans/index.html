Vegan Refried Magic: 15 Flavor-Packed Mexican-Inspired Recipes for Plant-Based Fans


If you’re anything like me, there’s nothing quite as comforting as a piping-hot plate of Mexican food on a cold evening, while dreaming about margaritas and beaches. But what if you’re vegan or following a plant-based diet? Fear not, as we have collected 15 vegan Mexican-inspired recipes for you to embrace and explore. From traditional refried beans cooked on the stove with a variety of juice, oil, and spices, to heartwarming comfort meals, these combinations will leave your taste buds singing. After all, refried beans are not just a side dish, they are the star ingredient of so many delicious recipes, perfect for that after-work or weekend meal prep. So, put on your apron, grab your mixers and let the magic begin!

1. Cumin-Scented Stovetop Refried Beans

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dry pinto beans
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable broth
  • 2 teaspoons cumin

Directions: Soak pinto beans overnight, and then cook in enough water to cover them by 2 inches until tender. In a separate pan, sauté onion and garlic in oil until soft. Add cooked beans, vegetable broth, and spices, and mash until desired consistency is reached. Serve with warm corn tortillas.

2. Spicy Black Bean Tacos with Avocado Crema

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked black beans
  • 1 avocado
  • 1/2 cup non-dairy yogurt
  • 1/2 jalapeño pepper, seeded
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder

Directions: In a blender, combine avocado, yogurt, jalapeño, and garlic powder to make the crema. In a separate bowl, mix black beans with spices such as smoked paprika, and chili powder. Serve refried beans with warm corn tortillas, and top with avocado crema.

3. Nutty Mango Salsa with Spicy Refried Sweet Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 1 large sweet potato
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1/2 cup mango, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced

Directions: Boil sweet potato until tender, then mash with oil and spices such as cumin, chili powder, and paprika. In a separate bowl, combine mango, onion, cilantro, and jalapeño to create a delicious salsa. Serve sweet potato mash with mango salsa on top.

4. Zucchini and Refried Bean Quesadillas

Ingredients:

  • 4 large tortillas
  • 2 cups cooked pinto beans
  • 2 cups shredded zucchini
  • 1 cup shredded cheese
  • 2 teaspoons finely chopped garlic
  • 1/4 cup oil

Directions: In a pan, heat oil over medium heat, sauté zucchini and garlic for about 5 minutes. Stir in refried beans and cheese, and cook until heated through. Distribute the mixture on half of each tortilla, fold, and cook until gooey and golden brown. Serve with guacamole and fresh pico de gallo.

5. Vegetarian Pozole Rojo

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked pinto beans
  • 1 tablespoon dried guajillo chiles, seeded
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced

Directions: Rehydrate guajillo chiles in warm water, blend with onion, vegetable oil, and spices until smooth. Heat refried beans, and then stir in the chile paste. Serve pozole in bowls, topped with fresh cilantro, radishes, and a squeeze of lime.

6. Refried Black Bean Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers
  • 2 cups cooked black beans
  • 1/2 cup corn
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes

Directions: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut off the tops of bell peppers and remove seeds. Mix refried beans with corn, tomato, and desired spices. Fill each pepper with the mixture and bake for 30 minutes. Serve with a squeeze of lime and a side of avocado salsa.

7. Mexican Layered Nachos with Mango Black Bean Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 1 bag tortilla chips
  • 1 can refried black beans
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded cheese

Directions: Lay tortilla chips in an oven-safe dish and top with refried beans, cheese, and diced tomatoes. Bake in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for 10 minutes or until everything is melted and warm. Top with mango black bean salsa, and serve.

8. Sweet Potato and Refried Bean Tostadas

Ingredients:

  • 6 tostada shells
  • 1 baked sweet potato
  • 2 cups cooked pinto beans
  • 1/2 avocado
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice

Directions: Mash sweet potato with oil and spices for a delicious spread. Spread sweet potato mixture onto each tostada shell, top with refried beans, and avocado slices. Drizzle with lime juice and enjoy.

9. Spicy Refried Bean and Quinoa Stuffed Jalapeños

Ingredients:

  • 8 jalapeño peppers
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 2 cups cooked pinto beans
  • 1/3 cup shredded cheese

Directions: Cut the jalapeño peppers in half, and remove seeds. Combine quinoa, refried beans, and cheese in a bowl and stuff each jalapeño with the mixture. Place in a baking dish, and bake in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, until cheese is melted and bubbly.

10. Refried Bean and Corn Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked pinto beans
  • 1 cup corn
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 avocado, diced

Directions: Combine beans, corn, tomatoes, cilantro, and avocado in a large bowl. Toss well to mix and serve. For a zesty twist, sprinkle with chili powder or a squeeze of lime.

11. Black Bean and Mango Salsa Burritos

Ingredients:

  • 8 large tortilla wraps
  • 2 cups cooked black beans
  • 1 mango, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, diced
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1/4 cup low-fat sour cream

Directions: Mix black beans, mango, onion, and desired spices in a bowl. Lay out tortilla wraps and spread the bean mixture evenly. Top with shredded lettuce and a dollop of sour cream. Roll up the tortilla and serve.

12. Pinto Bean and Diced Tomato Enchiladas

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked pinto beans
  • 1 1/2 cups enchilada sauce
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage

Directions: Mix pinto beans, diced tomatoes, and a splash of enchilada sauce in a bowl. Lay out tortilla wraps, and spread the mixture onto each. Roll up, and place seam-side down in a baking dish. Pour the remaining sauce over the rolls, sprinkle shredded cabbage, and bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 20 minutes.

13. Creamy Vegan Refried Bean Dip

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked pinto beans
  • 1 avocado
  • 1/4 cup non-dairy sour cream
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salsa

Directions: Combine all ingredients except salsa in a blender, and blend until smooth. Pour into a serving dish, and top with salsa. Serve with toasted tortilla chips.

14. Refried Bean and Veggie Spaghetti

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 3 cups cooked pinto beans
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 cup shredded cheese

Directions: Cook spaghetti according to package instructions. In a food processor or blender, purée refried beans with vegetable broth. Heat a touch of olive oil and sauté your favorite vegetables. Stir in bean purée,-season with salt and pepper, and serve topped with shredded cheese.

15. Magical Refried Bean Chocolate Brownies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked pinto beans (rinsed and drained)
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs

Directions: Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Blend all the ingredients together in a food processor or blender until smooth. Pour into a greased cake pan, and bake for 30 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean. Let it cool before cutting into squares.

Conclusion

Vegan Mexican food does not have to be boring! These 15 Flavor-Packed Mexican-Inspired Recipes not only prove that but also show that you can easily enjoy a variety of flavors without ever compromising taste or texture. Plant-based food is all about getting creative with ingredients, and these dishes are perfect examples of how even traditional Mexican food can be made vegan. So, don’t hesitate to try these recipes, and let your taste buds be the judge of their deliciousness. Happy cooking!
