Pickled Okra: A Southern Favorite with a Tangy Twist


If you’re a fan of Southern cuisine, then you’re probably familiar with pickled okra. This tangy and flavorful treat is a staple in many Southern kitchens, and for good reason. The combination of the brine and the natural crunch of the okra makes for a delicious and addictive snack. But why settle for the traditional pickled okra when you can add a tangy twist to it? In this recipe, we’ll show you how to take this Southern favorite to the next level.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of fresh okra
  • 2 cups of white vinegar
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1/4 cup of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 2 cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of dill seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Start by washing the okra thoroughly and trim off the stem ends. You can leave the caps intact to add visual appeal to your pickled okra.

  2. In a large saucepan, combine the white vinegar, water, salt, and sugar. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring occasionally until the sugar and salt are fully dissolved.

  3. While the brine is heating, prepare the jars by sterilizing them. You can do this by placing the jars, lids, and bands in a pot of boiling water for about 10 minutes. Make sure to use tongs or a jar lifter to handle the hot jars.

  4. Once the brine is ready and the jars are sterilized, it’s time to pack the okra into the jars. Begin by placing a garlic clove, mustard seeds, dill seeds, and red pepper flakes in each jar. Then, tightly pack the okra into the jars, leaving a 1/2-inch headspace at the top.

  5. Pour the hot brine over the okra, making sure to cover the okra completely. Use a chopstick or a plastic spatula to remove any air bubbles and adjust the headspace if needed.

  6. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any brine residue. Place the lids on the jars and screw the bands on until they are fingertip tight.

  7. Now it’s time to process the jars in a boiling water bath canner. Place the jars in the canner, making sure they are fully submerged in water. Process the jars for 10 minutes, adjusting the processing time if you live at a high altitude.

  8. After the processing time is up, carefully remove the jars from the canner using tongs or a jar lifter. Place the jars on a clean towel and allow them to cool completely. As the jars cool, you may hear the lids “pop,” indicating a successful seal.

  9. Once the jars are completely cool, check the seals by pressing down on the center of each lid. If the lid doesn’t flex and is firmly sealed, your pickled okra is ready for storage. If any jars didn’t seal properly, refrigerate them and consume within a few weeks.

  10. Store your pickled okra in a cool, dark place for at least two weeks before enjoying. This will allow the flavors to meld and intensify. Once the two weeks are up, open a jar and savor the tangy, crunchy goodness of your homemade pickled okra.

Conclusion

With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of time, you can elevate the classic pickled okra to new heights. The tangy twist of this recipe adds a zing that will have your taste buds dancing with delight. So, why not give it a try? Whether you enjoy it as a snack, a side dish, or a topping for your favorite burger, pickled okra is sure to become a Southern favorite in your household. Happy pickling!
