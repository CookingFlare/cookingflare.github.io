Traditional Middle Eastern Falafel: Baked and Healthier


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’ve likely indulged in the savory, aromatic goodness of traditional falafel. These crispy, flavorful chickpea patties have been a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine for centuries. While the deep-fried version is undeniably delicious, today, we’re going to explore a healthier alternative – baked falafel. By baking instead of frying, we can cut down on the oil content while still preserving the delightful taste and texture of this beloved dish.

Ingredients

For the falafel:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

For the tahini sauce:

  • 1/2 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the chickpeas

Start by soaking the dried chickpeas in cold water for at least 8 hours or overnight. Once soaked, drain and rinse the chickpeas thoroughly.

Step 2: Blend the falafel mixture

In a food processor, combine the soaked chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, black pepper, and baking soda. Pulse the mixture until it becomes a coarse paste, ensuring not to over-process. Transfer the mixture to a large bowl and stir in the all-purpose flour until well combined. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 3: Preheat the oven

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it.

Step 4: Shape the falafel

Remove the falafel mixture from the refrigerator. Using your hands, shape the mixture into small patties, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter, and place them on the prepared baking sheet.

Step 5: Bake the falafel

Bake the falafel in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, flipping them halfway through, until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside.

Step 6: Make the tahini sauce

While the falafel are baking, prepare the tahini sauce. In a bowl, whisk together the tahini, water, lemon juice, minced garlic, and salt until smooth and creamy.

Step 7: Serve and enjoy

Once the falafel are ready, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool for a few minutes. Serve the baked falafel warm with the prepared tahini sauce and your choice of accompaniments, such as pita bread, hummus, or a fresh salad.

Healthier and Equally Delicious

By opting to bake instead of fry the falafel, you can enjoy this beloved Middle Eastern dish with a healthier twist. The baked falafel still boasts the enticing blend of herbs and spices, with a crispy exterior and a fluffy, flavorful interior. Plus, by making your own falafel at home, you have full control over the ingredients, ensuring a wholesome and nutritious meal.

Whether you’re a devoted fan of Middle Eastern cuisine or simply looking to expand your culinary repertoire, this baked falafel recipe is sure to become a household favorite. It’s a delightful, healthier alternative to the traditional deep-fried version, without compromising on taste or satisfaction.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure to savor the irresistible flavors of traditional Middle Eastern falafel – now baked and healthier!
