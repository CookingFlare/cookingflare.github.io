Grilled Watermelon Gazpacho: A Refreshing Soup


Welcome to a delightful culinary journey! Today, we’ll be exploring a unique and scrumptious twist on the classic Gazpacho, a refreshing soup originating from the warm areas of Andalusia, Spain. This unique spin is called Grilled Watermelon Gazpacho, a delicious way to enjoy a ripe, juicy watermelon in a whole new way.

To begin with, gather these ingredients:

Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 5 minutes Yield: 4 servings

For the Grilled Watermelon Gazpacho:

  • 1 large ripe watermelon, rind removed, cubed
  • 2 red bell peppers, seeded and char-grilled
  • 2 cucumbers, peeled and diced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tsp sherry vinegar
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish

For the Simplest Cucumber Salad:

  • 1 small cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Procedures involve simplicity and quickness. This Grilled Watermelon Gazpacho takes no more than 20 minutes from start to finish. Here’s how you do it:

For the Grilled Watermelon Gazpacho

  1. Preheat grill or cast iron skillet to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill or char the watermelon cubes and red bell peppers until nicely caramelized, about 5 minutes. You can also do this step on a stovetop griddle.
  3. In a food processor or blender, pulse together the grilled watermelon, red peppers, cucumbers, red onion, Kalamata olives, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, sherry vinegar, smoked paprika, and a pinch of salt and black pepper until smooth yet slightly chunky. You may have to do this in two or three small batches.
  4. Stir the mixture and taste, adding more salt or pepper as desired.
  5. Chill in the fridge for about 15 minutes to help meld flavors. Serve cold, garnished with fresh mint leaves.

For the Simplest Cucumber Salad

  1. In a bowl, combine sliced cucumbers and chopped dill.
  2. Shake up the olive oil and lemon juice in a jar or airtight container and season with salt and black pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the cucumber-dill mix, toss gently, and chill in the fridge.
  4. Serve alongside Grilled Watermelon Gazpacho for a perfect balance of flavors.

You might be wondering, “Why use a watermelon in gazpacho?” Watermelon is an excellent addition for an added refreshing contrast to the conventional Gazpacho ingredients. The natural sweetness from the watermelon ties in wonderfully with the smoky, tangy flavors, resulting in a lively, refreshing sensation.

Grilled Watermelon Gazpacho is a versatile dish. Enjoy it as a light lunch or as a delightful appetizer for dinner parties. This charming soup is perfect for warm days when you don’t want anything too heavy, but still crave a fulfilling meal.
