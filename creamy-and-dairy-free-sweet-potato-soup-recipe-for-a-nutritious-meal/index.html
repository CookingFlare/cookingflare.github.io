Creamy and Dairy-Free Sweet Potato Soup Recipe for a Nutritious Meal


Sweet potatoes are a staple ingredient in many cuisines around the world, and for good reason. They’re packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that can provide a wealth of health benefits when consumed. One of the most popular ways to enjoy sweet potatoes is in a warm and comforting soup. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a creamy and dairy-free sweet potato soup that’s perfect for a nutritious meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and chopped into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped sweet potatoes, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir to combine.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  5. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, you can transfer the soup to a blender and blend until smooth, then return it to the pot.
  6. In a small saucepan, heat the unsweetened almond milk over low heat until warm.
  7. Stir in the nutritional yeast until dissolved.
  8. Add the warm almond milk mixture to the pot with the sweet potato soup. Stir to combine.
  9. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  10. Serve the soup hot, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro leaves if desired.

Benefits of Sweet Potatoes

Sweet potatoes are a nutrient-dense food that can provide a range of health benefits when consumed. Here are just a few of the many benefits of sweet potatoes:

  • High in Vitamin A: Sweet potatoes are an excellent source of vitamin A, which is important for healthy vision, immune function, and skin health.
  • Rich in Fiber: Sweet potatoes are high in dietary fiber, which can help to support healthy digestion and bowel function.
  • Good Source of Minerals: Sweet potatoes are a good source of minerals such as potassium, magnesium, and iron, which are important for healthy blood pressure, bone health, and energy production.
  • Antioxidant-Rich: Sweet potatoes contain a range of antioxidants, including beta-carotene and lutein, which can help to protect against oxidative stress and inflammation.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the soup more substantial, you can add some cooked chickpeas, black beans, or diced chicken to the pot.
  • If you prefer a creamier soup, you can add some additional unsweetened almond milk or cashew cream.
  • You can also add some diced bell peppers, carrots, or celery to the pot for added flavor and nutrients.
  • For a spicy kick, you can add some diced jalapeños or red pepper flakes to the pot.

Conclusion

This creamy and dairy-free sweet potato soup is a delicious and nutritious meal option that’s perfect for a chilly fall or winter evening. With its rich and creamy texture, sweet potato flavor, and boost of vitamins and minerals, this soup is sure to become a favorite in your household. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the many health benefits of sweet potatoes!
