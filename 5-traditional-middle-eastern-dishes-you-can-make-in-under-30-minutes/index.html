5 Traditional Middle Eastern Dishes You Can Make in Under 30 Minutes


Middle Eastern cuisine is a vibrant, flavorful collection of dishes that have been perfected over centuries. Known for its aromatic spices, fresh vegetables, and healthy ingredients, Middle Eastern food offers a delightful combination of textures and tastes. While many people may think that creating these dishes requires hours of preparation, there are plenty of delicious Middle Eastern recipes that can be made in under 30 minutes. In this post, we’ll introduce you to five traditional Middle Eastern dishes that are easy, quick, and sure to impress your taste buds.

1. Hummus: A Creamy Delight

Hummus is arguably one of the most well-known Middle Eastern dishes around the globe. This smooth, creamy dip made from chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic is a perfect appetizer or snack. It’s rich in protein and healthy fats, making it a great addition to any meal. The best part is that you can make hummus from scratch in just 10 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (or 1 ½ cups cooked chickpeas)
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1-2 garlic cloves
  • Salt, to taste
  • ½ teaspoon cumin (optional)
  • Water (as needed for desired consistency)
  • Fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the chickpeas.
  2. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and salt.
  3. Blend until smooth. If the mixture is too thick, add a bit of water, a tablespoon at a time, until you reach your desired consistency.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning, adding more lemon juice, garlic, or salt as needed.
  5. Transfer to a serving dish and drizzle with olive oil. Garnish with cumin and fresh parsley if desired.
  6. Serve with pita bread or fresh vegetables.

Hummus is versatile and can be customized by adding roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, or spices like paprika for extra flair. It’s a fantastic addition to any Middle Eastern-inspired meal.

2. Fattoush Salad: A Fresh, Zesty Treat

Fattoush is a refreshing salad that combines the crunch of fresh vegetables with the delightful texture of toasted pita. This dish is bright and flavorful, with a tangy dressing made from olive oil, lemon juice, and sumac, a tangy Middle Eastern spice. It’s perfect as a light lunch or a side dish to accompany grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 pita breads
  • 2 cups Romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1-2 tomatoes, diced
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 small bunch of radishes, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • ½ cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon sumac (optional, but recommended)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the pita bread into triangles and place them on a baking sheet. Drizzle with a little olive oil and toast in the oven for 7-10 minutes or until they are crispy and golden brown.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, radishes, parsley, and mint.
  4. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, sumac, salt, and pepper.
  5. Add the toasted pita chips to the salad and pour the dressing over it. Toss gently to combine.
  6. Serve immediately for the freshest taste, or let the flavors marinate for a few minutes before serving.

Fattoush is an easy salad that can be made in under 20 minutes. Its zesty flavors and textures make it an ideal addition to any Middle Eastern-inspired meal. Plus, it’s easily customizable with other veggies or herbs based on your preferences.

3. Shakshuka: A Flavorful Egg Dish

Shakshuka is a popular breakfast dish in many Middle Eastern and North African countries. It consists of eggs poached in a spicy tomato sauce, often flavored with onions, bell peppers, and cumin. This dish is perfect for brunch, lunch, or even dinner. It’s nutritious, filling, and quick to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon chili powder (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 large eggs
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and bell pepper, sautéing for 5-7 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes, cumin, paprika, chili powder (if using), salt, and pepper. Simmer the mixture for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Make small wells in the sauce using a spoon and carefully crack the eggs into each well.
  6. Cover the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny.
  7. Remove from heat, garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley, and serve immediately with warm pita or crusty bread.

Shakshuka is incredibly versatile, and you can add ingredients like spinach, olives, or feta cheese to elevate the dish. It’s perfect for anyone looking for a quick and satisfying meal that packs a punch of flavor.

4. Chicken Shawarma Wrap: A Street Food Favorite

Shawarma is a beloved Middle Eastern street food, typically made with spiced meat that’s slow-cooked on a vertical rotisserie. However, you can easily replicate the delicious flavors of shawarma at home using chicken and a few spices. This chicken shawarma wrap is perfect for a quick dinner, and you can serve it with a variety of sauces, such as garlic sauce or tahini.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts or thighs, boneless and skinless
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4-6 large pita or flatbread wraps
  • Fresh vegetables (lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, onions)
  • Garlic sauce or tahini sauce for drizzling (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  2. Rub the chicken with olive oil and then coat it with the spice mixture. Let it marinate for at least 10 minutes.
  3. Heat a grill pan or skillet over medium heat and cook the chicken for 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked.
  4. While the chicken is cooking, prepare the fresh vegetables by slicing them thinly.
  5. Once the chicken is cooked, slice it thinly against the grain.
  6. To assemble the wraps, place a few slices of chicken on each pita or flatbread. Top with fresh vegetables and drizzle with garlic sauce or tahini sauce.
  7. Roll up the wrap and serve immediately.

This chicken shawarma wrap is an easy version of a classic Middle Eastern street food that doesn’t take long to prepare. You can also customize the fillings with additional ingredients like pickles or hummus for extra flavor.

5. Baba Ganoush: Smoky Eggplant Dip

Baba ganoush is a smoky, creamy dip made from roasted eggplant, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic. It’s a fantastic appetizer that pairs well with pita bread or fresh vegetables. The smoky flavor of the roasted eggplant gives this dish its signature taste, and it’s a great alternative to hummus for those who prefer a different flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pierce the eggplants a few times with a fork and place them on a baking sheet. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
  3. Remove the eggplants from the oven and let them cool slightly. Peel off the skin and scoop the flesh into a bowl.
  4. In a food processor, combine the roasted eggplant, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. Transfer the baba ganoush to a serving dish and drizzle with a little olive oil. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with pita or fresh vegetables.

Baba ganoush is another quick Middle Eastern dip that can be made in under 30 minutes. The smoky eggplant flavor pairs perfectly with the creaminess of tahini, creating a unique and delicious dip.

These five traditional Middle Eastern dishes are not only quick and easy to prepare, but they also pack a punch of flavor. Whether you’re making hummus for a snack, preparing a fresh fattoush salad, or enjoying a savory shakshuka, these recipes prove that you can experience the rich flavors of the Middle East in less than 30 minutes. Try these dishes at home and enjoy the tastes of the Middle East from the comfort of your kitchen!
