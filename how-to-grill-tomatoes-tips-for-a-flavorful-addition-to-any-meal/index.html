How to Grill Tomatoes: Tips for a Flavorful Addition to Any Meal


Grilling tomatoes is one of the simplest yet most delicious ways to enhance their natural flavor and texture. Whether you’re looking to elevate your BBQ, summer salad, or even a simple side dish, grilled tomatoes add a smoky, sweet richness that pairs perfectly with a wide range of dishes. In this post, we will explore various tips and techniques for grilling tomatoes, how to choose the best tomatoes for grilling, and different ways to incorporate them into your meals.

Why Grill Tomatoes?

Tomatoes are an incredibly versatile ingredient. They can be used in salads, sauces, soups, and more. However, when grilled, their flavor profile intensifies, creating a smoky, slightly caramelized taste that enhances their natural sweetness. Grilling tomatoes also softens their texture, making them more juicy and tender, which can be a fantastic addition to many dishes.

Grilling not only brings out the best in tomatoes, but it also adds a delightful contrast in texture. The exterior of the tomato gets charred and slightly crispy, while the interior remains juicy and tender. This makes grilled tomatoes a perfect companion for grilled meats, sandwiches, pasta dishes, or even as a topping for pizza.

Choosing the Best Tomatoes for Grilling

While most tomatoes can be grilled, some varieties are better suited for the grill than others. Here are a few tomato varieties to consider when preparing to grill:

1. Roma Tomatoes (Plum Tomatoes)

Roma tomatoes are known for their meaty texture and low moisture content, making them an excellent choice for grilling. Their firm structure ensures they won’t break apart on the grill, and their naturally tangy flavor intensifies when exposed to heat.

2. Beefsteak Tomatoes

Beefsteak tomatoes are large and juicy, and they are a favorite for grilling because they hold up well to heat. Their bold flavor and chunky texture make them perfect for slicing and placing directly on the grill.

3. Cherry or Grape Tomatoes

While smaller in size, cherry and grape tomatoes can also be grilled, especially when used in a skewer or grill basket. These small tomatoes caramelize quickly and provide a sweet burst of flavor.

4. Heirloom Tomatoes

Heirloom tomatoes come in various shapes, sizes, and colors, and many of them are perfect for grilling. Their unique flavors and dense, meaty texture make them ideal for grilling, especially when you want to add a touch of gourmet flair to your meal.

Preparing Tomatoes for Grilling

Before placing tomatoes on the grill, it’s essential to prepare them properly to ensure they cook evenly and develop their best flavors. Here’s how to prepare tomatoes for grilling:

1. Wash and Dry the Tomatoes

Start by washing your tomatoes thoroughly under cool water to remove any dirt or residue. Once cleaned, dry them gently with a clean kitchen towel. This step helps prevent the tomatoes from becoming soggy while grilling.

2. Slice the Tomatoes

For larger tomatoes like Roma or Beefsteak, slice them into thick rounds (about 1/2-inch thick). This will help them hold their shape during grilling while allowing the heat to penetrate and cook them through. For smaller tomatoes like cherry or grape tomatoes, it’s best to leave them whole or skewer them onto a skewer or grill basket.

3. Remove the Seeds (Optional)

If you prefer grilled tomatoes without excess moisture, you can choose to remove the seeds before grilling. To do this, simply cut the tomatoes in half and scoop out the seeds with a spoon. While this step is optional, it helps reduce the amount of water released during grilling and keeps the tomatoes from becoming too soggy.

4. Season the Tomatoes

Before grilling, season the tomatoes with a drizzle of olive oil, a pinch of salt, and a few cracks of black pepper. You can also add herbs and spices to enhance the flavor. Fresh or dried basil, oregano, thyme, or rosemary are great choices for grilled tomatoes. If you like a bit of heat, add a sprinkle of red pepper flakes or garlic powder.

5. Optional: Marinate the Tomatoes

For an extra layer of flavor, you can marinate the tomatoes before grilling. Create a simple marinade using olive oil, balsamic vinegar, garlic, and your favorite herbs. Allow the tomatoes to marinate for about 15-30 minutes, then proceed with grilling.

How to Grill Tomatoes

Grilling tomatoes is a relatively straightforward process, but a few tips can make the difference between perfectly grilled tomatoes and ones that are burnt or undercooked. Below are some essential steps to follow when grilling tomatoes.

1. Preheat the Grill

Start by preheating your grill to medium heat. If you are using a charcoal grill, allow the coals to burn down until they are covered in white ash. This will create a consistent, moderate heat ideal for grilling tomatoes. For gas grills, set the heat to medium and preheat for about 10 minutes.

2. Use Direct or Indirect Heat

Tomatoes are delicate, so it’s essential to avoid cooking them directly over high heat, as they can burn quickly. Instead, use indirect heat to cook the tomatoes slowly and evenly.

For larger tomatoes, place them on the grill away from the direct flame (indirect heat). This will allow the tomatoes to cook without burning while still getting that smoky flavor. For smaller tomatoes, use a grill basket or skewer them for easy handling over direct heat.

3. Place the Tomatoes on the Grill

Once the grill is preheated, place the tomatoes on the grill. If you’re grilling slices, lay them flat on the grill grates, cut-side up. For whole cherry or grape tomatoes, skewer them or place them in a grill basket.

4. Grill the Tomatoes

Grill the tomatoes for about 3-4 minutes per side for larger slices or 2-3 minutes per side for whole tomatoes. You want to achieve a nice char on the surface while keeping the interior juicy and tender. If you’re grilling slices, flip them carefully using tongs or a spatula.

Check for grill marks and slight charring to indicate the tomatoes are done. The exterior should be slightly crisp, while the interior remains soft and juicy.

5. Remove the Tomatoes from the Grill

Once the tomatoes are grilled to your liking, remove them from the grill. Let them rest for a few minutes before serving, as this helps redistribute the juices and allows them to cool slightly.

Different Ways to Serve Grilled Tomatoes

Grilled tomatoes are incredibly versatile and can be served in many different ways. Here are a few ideas for incorporating them into your meals:

1. Grilled Tomato Salad

Grilled tomatoes make a wonderful addition to fresh salads. Combine them with other grilled vegetables, such as zucchini, eggplant, and peppers, and toss with fresh greens like arugula or spinach. Top with crumbled feta cheese, a drizzle of balsamic glaze, and some fresh herbs like basil for a delicious and light salad.

2. Grilled Tomato Sandwiches

Slice the grilled tomatoes and layer them between slices of toasted bread. Add fresh mozzarella, basil leaves, and a drizzle of olive oil to create a simple yet satisfying sandwich. You can also add grilled chicken or bacon for extra protein.

3. Grilled Tomato Pizza

Grilled tomatoes are a perfect topping for homemade pizza. Slice the grilled tomatoes and scatter them over the pizza dough along with other toppings such as cheese, olives, and arugula. Bake until the cheese is melted and bubbly for a delicious and smoky pizza.

4. Grilled Tomatoes with Pasta

Add grilled tomatoes to your favorite pasta dish for an extra burst of flavor. Toss the grilled tomatoes with olive oil, garlic, and fresh herbs, and serve over spaghetti, penne, or any pasta you like. You can also add some grilled chicken or shrimp for a complete meal.

5. Grilled Tomato Salsa

Grilled tomatoes can be used to make a smoky, flavorful salsa. Grill the tomatoes, then chop them and mix with chopped onions, cilantro, lime juice, and a pinch of salt. This salsa pairs perfectly with chips, tacos, or grilled meats.

6. Grilled Tomato Bruschetta

Make a classic bruschetta with grilled tomatoes. Top slices of toasted baguette with diced grilled tomatoes, garlic, fresh basil, and a drizzle of olive oil. This simple appetizer is always a crowd-pleaser.

Tips for Perfect Grilled Tomatoes

  • Don’t Overcook: Grilled tomatoes cook quickly, so keep an eye on them. Overcooking can cause them to become mushy or dry out.
  • Experiment with Marinades: Feel free to experiment with different marinades to enhance the flavor of your tomatoes. Balsamic vinegar, garlic, and olive oil make a great base, but you can add different herbs, spices, and even a touch of honey for sweetness.
  • Grill with Other Vegetables: Tomatoes pair wonderfully with other grilled vegetables. Consider grilling zucchini, bell peppers, or onions alongside your tomatoes for a colorful and delicious vegetable medley.
  • Use a Grill Basket: If you’re grilling small tomatoes like cherry or grape, use a grill basket to keep them from falling through the grates.

Conclusion

Grilling tomatoes is a fantastic way to bring out their natural sweetness and smoky flavors, transforming them into a versatile and flavorful addition to any meal. With a few simple steps, you can turn ordinary tomatoes into something extraordinary. Whether you’re making a salad, sandwich, pasta dish, or even a side dish for a grilled steak, grilled tomatoes can enhance the flavor and texture of any meal. So fire up the grill, pick your favorite tomatoes, and enjoy the smoky goodness!
