Baking for a Picnic: Portable and Delicious Outdoor Eats


Looking to take your picnic experience to the next level? Why not add some freshly baked treats to your outdoor spread? Baking for a picnic can be a fun and rewarding experience, and the aroma of freshly baked goods will surely make your picnic a memorable one. In this blog post, we’ll explore some portable and delicious baking recipes that are perfect for your next picnic adventure.

Savory Hand Pies

Hand pies are the ultimate picnic food—they are compact, easy to eat, and bursting with flavor. These savory treats can be made with a variety of fillings, such as spinach and feta, chicken and mushroom, or even ham and cheese. The options are endless!

To make the crust, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter in a food processor. Pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Slowly add 1/4 cup of ice water and continue pulsing until the dough comes together. Divide the dough into two equal portions, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

For the filling, sauté your preferred combination of ingredients with some olive oil and seasonings until cooked. Let the filling cool completely before assembling the hand pies.

Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface until it’s about 1/8 inch thick. Cut the dough into circles using a round cutter or a glass. Spoon a small amount of the filling onto one half of each circle, leaving a border. Fold the other half over and press the edges to seal. Brush the pies with some beaten egg for a golden finish and bake at 375°F for about 20 minutes or until golden brown.

Mini Quiches

Quiches are a classic picnic dish that can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature. These individual-sized quiches are not only adorable but also incredibly tasty. You can customize the fillings to suit your preferences.

To make the crust, combine 1 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter in a bowl. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to blend the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add 2-3 tablespoons of ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

For the filling, whisk together 4 large eggs, 1 cup of milk, salt, pepper, and your choice of fillings such as diced vegetables, cooked bacon, or shredded cheese. Divide the chilled dough into 12 equal portions and press each portion into a greased muffin tin. Pour the egg mixture into the crusts, filling them about 3/4 full.

Bake the mini quiches at 375°F for 20-25 minutes or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown. Let them cool slightly before removing them from the muffin tin.

Blueberry Lemon Bars

No picnic is complete without a touch of sweetness. These blueberry lemon bars are not only delicious but also easy to transport. The tangy lemon filling combined with the burst of blueberries will leave you craving for more.

To make the crust, combine 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup of powdered sugar, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt in a bowl. Cut in 3/4 cup of cold unsalted butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Press the mixture into the bottom of a greased baking dish and bake at 350°F for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.

Meanwhile, whisk together 4 large eggs, 2 cups of granulated sugar, 1/3 cup of all-purpose flour, the zest of 2 lemons, and the juice of 4 lemons until well combined. Gently fold in 1 1/2 cups of fresh blueberries. Pour the mixture over the baked crust and return to the oven for an additional 25-30 minutes or until the filling is set.

Allow the blueberry lemon bars to cool completely before cutting them into squares. Pack them in an airtight container for your picnic and enjoy the refreshing flavors.

Conclusion

Baking for a picnic adds a special touch to your outdoor dining experience. With these portable and delicious recipes, you can impress your friends and family with homemade treats that are perfect for enjoying in the great outdoors. So pack your picnic basket, grab your baked goods, and head out for a delightful day of sunshine and deliciousness!
