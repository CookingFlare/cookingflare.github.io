How to Make Thai Grilled Chicken: A Simple Recipe


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and the perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy. One dish that embodies these characteristics beautifully is Thai Grilled Chicken, or “Gai Yang.” This dish is not only delicious but also easy to prepare, making it a perfect choice for a weeknight dinner or a weekend barbecue. In this post, we will guide you through a simple recipe for Thai Grilled Chicken that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

To make authentic Thai Grilled Chicken, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Marinade:

  • 4 chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on for more flavor)
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder (for color and flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

For Serving:

  • Lime wedges
  • Steamed jasmine rice
  • Fresh cucumber slices
  • Thai chili sauce (optional)

Equipment Needed

  • Mixing bowl
  • Whisk or fork
  • Grill (charcoal or gas)
  • Meat thermometer
  • Basting brush
  • Tongs

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Marinade

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the minced garlic, grated ginger, soy sauce, fish sauce, oyster sauce, brown sugar, lime juice, black pepper, turmeric powder, and vegetable oil. Whisk the ingredients together until well blended.

  2. Marinate the Chicken: Place the chicken thighs in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring each piece is well coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours, but for best results, marinate overnight. This allows the flavors to penetrate the chicken deeply.

Step 2: Preheat the Grill

  1. Prepare the Grill: If you’re using a charcoal grill, light the charcoal and let it burn until it’s covered with white ash. For a gas grill, preheat it to medium-high heat.

  2. Oil the Grate: To prevent the chicken from sticking, lightly oil the grill grate using a paper towel dipped in vegetable oil. Use tongs to hold the paper towel while wiping the grate.

Step 3: Grill the Chicken

  1. Remove Chicken from Marinade: Take the marinated chicken out of the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for about 15-20 minutes. This helps the chicken cook evenly.

  2. Place Chicken on the Grill: Using tongs, place the chicken thighs skin-side down on the grill. Close the lid and let them cook for about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Flip the Chicken: After 5-7 minutes, check the chicken for grill marks. If they have developed a nice char, flip them over. Cook for another 5-7 minutes on the other side.

  4. Check for Doneness: Use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature of the chicken. It should reach 165°F (75°C) for safe consumption. If it hasn’t reached this temperature, continue grilling, flipping occasionally, until done.

  5. Baste with Marinade: For extra flavor, you can baste the chicken with the leftover marinade during the last few minutes of grilling. This adds moisture and enhances the taste.

Step 4: Rest and Serve

  1. Rest the Chicken: Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the grill and let it rest for about 5 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and flavorful bite.

  2. Garnish and Serve: Slice the chicken into pieces and garnish with freshly chopped cilantro. Serve with lime wedges, steamed jasmine rice, fresh cucumber slices, and a side of Thai chili sauce for those who enjoy a bit of heat.

Tips for Perfect Thai Grilled Chicken

  • Choose the Right Cut: While chicken thighs are recommended for their flavor and juiciness, you can also use chicken breasts. Just be mindful that they may dry out more quickly, so adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  • Marination Time: The longer you marinate the chicken, the more flavorful it will be. If you have time, aim for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.

  • Grill Temperature: Maintaining a consistent medium-high heat is crucial for achieving a nice char without burning the chicken. If you notice flare-ups, move the chicken to a cooler part of the grill.
