The Best Dessert Recipes for a Summer BBQ


Summer BBQs are a staple gathering for friends and family—filled with smoky grills, fresh salads, and laughter under the sun. While the savory courses often steal the spotlight, the perfect dessert can elevate your outdoor feast from memorable to legendary. Whether you’re aiming for refreshing, indulgent, or fruity, this collection of dessert recipes will satisfy every sweet tooth and complement the bold flavors of your BBQ.

Why Dessert Matters at a Summer BBQ

After savoring grilled meats, charred veggies, and vibrant sides, a thoughtfully crafted dessert offers both balance and delight. Summer desserts often highlight seasonal fruits, light textures, and cool sensations that combat the heat. Plus, they can be designed for easy serving, simple prep, and crowd-pleasing appeal.

Let’s dive into the best dessert recipes for a summer BBQ that cater to all tastes, from creamy quick treats to fruity fire-roasted sweets.

1. Grilled Peaches with Honey and Mascarpone

This recipe marries the natural caramelization from the grill with the creamy richness of mascarpone and the floral sweetness of honey. It’s elegant, effortless, and designed for summer’s bounty.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large, ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 2 tablespoons honey, plus extra for drizzling
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Method:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the mascarpone cheese, honey, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy.
  3. Lightly brush the peach halves with a bit of oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Place peaches on the grill, cut side down, and cook for about 3-4 minutes, or until grill marks appear and the peaches are slightly softened.
  5. Flip and grill for another 2 minutes on the skin side.
  6. Remove from the grill and let cool for a minute or two.
  7. Spoon a dollop of the mascarpone mixture onto each peach half.
  8. Drizzle with honey and garnish with fresh mint leaves before serving.

Tip: Pair with a crisp Riesling or chilled sparkling water for a perfect combo.

2. No-Bake Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Cups

Accessible and individually portioned, these no-bake cheesecake cups are refreshing and portable – ideal for outdoor summer parties.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries

Method:

  1. Combine graham cracker crumbs and melted butter in a bowl. Press this mixture into the base of serving cups or jars.
  2. In another bowl, beat the cream cheese and powdered sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Whip the heavy cream to stiff peaks separately, then fold into the cream cheese mixture along with lemon zest and juice.
  4. Spoon the cheesecake filling over the graham cracker base evenly.
  5. Top with fresh blueberries and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

Bonus: For an adult twist, sprinkle a splash of blueberry liqueur over the top or within the filling.

3. Fire-Roasted Pineapple with Cinnamon and Lime

This tropical twist offers a vibrant, warm, and tangy dessert with a subtle smoky flavor, perfectly complementing traditional BBQ flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole fresh pineapple, peeled and cut into rings or chunks
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro or mint for garnish

Method:

  1. Mix brown sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.
  2. Sprinkle the sugar mixture evenly over pineapple slices.
  3. Grill the pineapple on medium heat for about 3-5 minutes per side until caramelized and slightly charred.
  4. Remove from the grill and squeeze lime juice on top.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro or mint.

Serve alongside vanilla ice cream or coconut yogurt for a luscious finish.

4. Classic Strawberry Shortcake with a Twist

Think fluffy biscuits complemented by macerated berries and fresh whipped cream — simple yet irresistibly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ½ cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • ¾ cup whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 4 cups strawberries, sliced
  • ¼ cup sugar (for berries)
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, salt, and ¼ cup sugar.
  3. Using a pastry cutter or fingers, cut in butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Add milk and vanilla, stirring just until combined.
  5. Drop spoonfuls of batter onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, about 8 large biscuits.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes, until golden brown.
  7. In the meantime, toss strawberries with sugar and let macerate for at least 30 minutes.
  8. Whip heavy cream until soft peaks form.
  9. To assemble, slice the shortcakes in half, layer with strawberries, and top with whipped cream.

Pro-tip: For added flair, drizzle warm balsamic reduction over the strawberries.

5. Mint Chocolate Chip Frozen Yogurt Bark

For those looking for a cool, healthier dessert, frozen yogurt bark is a fantastic choice. It’s customizable, quick, and such a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups plain or vanilla Greek yogurt
  • ½ cup honey or maple syrup
  • ½ teaspoon peppermint extract
  • ½ cup mini chocolate chips
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped (optional)

Method:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine Greek yogurt, honey, and peppermint extract until smooth.
  2. Fold in mini chocolate chips and chopped fresh mint if using.
  3. Spread the mixture evenly on a parchment-lined baking sheet to about ¼ inch thickness.
  4. Freeze for 3-4 hours or until completely solid.
  5. Break into pieces and serve immediately or store in an airtight container in the freezer.

Hint: Add crushed pretzels or nuts for a salty crunch that pairs beautifully with the sweet and minty flavors.

6. S’mores Dip: An Easy Crowd-Friendly Classic

Sometimes a nostalgic indulgence is the very thing a summer BBQ calls for. This s’mores dip is an easy way to share the joy of campfire treats without a fire pit.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup mini marshmallows
  • Graham crackers for dipping

Method:

  1. Preheat your oven’s broiler.
  2. In an oven-safe skillet or dish, spread chocolate chips evenly.
  3. Top the chocolate with marshmallows, covering the surface.
  4. Place under the broiler for 3-5 minutes, watching carefully, until marshmallows are golden and toasted.
  5. Remove from oven and serve immediately with graham crackers.

Warning: This dip disappears fast—make enough or plan seconds!

7. Watermelon Granita with Fresh Basil

Light, icy, and incredibly refreshing, this watermelon granita is the perfect palate cleanser or light dessert on a hot BBQ afternoon.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups seedless watermelon, cubed
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • ¼ cup sugar (adjust based on watermelon sweetness)
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Method:

  1. Puree the watermelon in a blender until smooth.
  2. Add lemon juice and sugar, blending to combine.
  3. Pour mixture into a shallow baking pan and place in the freezer.
  4. Every 30 minutes, scrape the mixture with a fork to create fluffy ice crystals.
  5. Repeat until fully frozen and flaky (usually 3-4 cycles).
  6. Serve in chilled glasses and garnish with basil leaves.

Pair with a light sparkling white wine for a festive touch.

Pro Tips for Perfect Summer BBQ Desserts

  • Prep ahead: Many of these desserts can be assembled or fully prepared before your guests arrive, giving you more time to enjoy the party.
  • Seasonal ingredients: Use peak-season fruits like berries, peaches, and melons to maximize flavor and freshness.
  • Portions matter: Smaller, individual servings like the cheesecake cups or yogurt bark make outdoor eating easier and minimize mess.
  • Balance flavors: After smoky, savory BBQ dishes, desserts with brightness (citrus), coolness (frozen treats), or natural sweetness (grilled fruit) feel especially satisfying.
  • Stay cool: Ice cream or frozen yogurt desserts need proper storage; keep them in coolers or near the shade to avoid melting.

This summer, treat your guests to these exhilarating dessert options that celebrate the season’s best flavors alongside your smoky BBQ spread. Whether it’s the simplicity of fire-roasted pineapple or the indulgence of strawberry shortcake, each dessert is designed to provide that sweet note of delight after a savory feast. So fire up your grill, bring on the laughter, and don’t forget to save room for dessert!
