The Best Indian Recipes for Your Slow Cooker


Indian cuisine is known for its rich, complex flavors and spices, but it's often thought to be challenging to make in a slow cooker. The good news is that many popular Indian dishes are easily adaptable to slow cooking, and with the right techniques and ingredients, you can create a variety of delicious and authentic Indian meals in the comfort of your home. Here, we'll explore some of the best Indian recipes for your slow cooker, perfect for busy cooks who want to experience the flavors of India without sacrificing tradition. Chicken Tikka Masala This classic Indian dish of marinated chicken in a creamy tomato sauce is a favorite around the world, and it's surprisingly easy to make in a slow cooker. Here's how: Ingredients:

1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs 1 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons lemon juice 2 teaspoons garam masala 1 teaspoon cumin 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon cardamom 2 cups chicken broth 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes 1/4 cup heavy cream Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

In a small bowl, whisk together yogurt, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin, cayenne pepper, salt, cinnamon, and cardamom. Add the chicken to a blender or food processor and blend until it's almost smooth. Add the marinade and blend until the chicken is well coated. Transfer the chicken to the slow cooker and add the chicken broth, diced tomatoes, and heavy cream. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours. Garnish with chopped cilantro, if desired.

Palak Paneer Palak paneer, a creamy spinach curry with cheese, is a staple of Indian cuisine, but it's often a time-consuming dish to prepare. Not with a slow cooker, however! Here's how to make it: Ingredients:

1 cup paneer (Indian cheese) 2 cups frozen spinach 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon garam masala 1 teaspoon cumin 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 cup heavy cream 2 cups chicken broth

Instructions:

In a blender or food processor, blend the spinach, onion, garlic, garam masala, cumin, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper until smooth. Add the paneer to the blender and blend until it's well coated with the spinach mixture. Transfer the paneer to the slow cooker and add the chicken broth and heavy cream. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours. Serve hot over basmati rice or with naan bread.

Saag Aloo Saag aloo, a spicy mustard green curry with potatoes, is a popular North Indian dish that's often made in a slow cooker. Here's how to make it: Ingredients:

2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and chopped 1 cup mustard greens 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon garam masala 1 teaspoon cumin 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 cups vegetable broth 1 tablespoon heavy cream

Instructions:

In a blender or food processor, blend the mustard greens, onion, garlic, garam masala, cumin, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper until smooth. Add the potatoes to the blender and blend until they're well coated with the mustard green mixture. Transfer the potatoes to the slow cooker and add the vegetable broth. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours. Stir in the heavy cream and serve hot.

Chana Masala Chana masala, a dried lentil stew with a rich, slightly sweet sauce, is a beloved Indian recipe. It's surprisingly easy to make in a slow cooker, and here's how: Ingredients:

1 cup dried chickpeas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon garam masala 1 teaspoon cumin 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 cups vegetable broth 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes 1/4 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

Rinse the chickpeas and soak them in water for at least 8 hours. Drain and add them to the slow cooker. Add the onion, garlic, garam masala, cumin, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper to the slow cooker and cook for 30 minutes. Add the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes to the slow cooker. Cook on low for 8-10 hours or high for 4-6 hours. Stir in the heavy cream and serve hot over basmati rice or with naan bread.

Biryani Biryani, a popular South Indian dish of rice, spices, and meat or vegetables, can be made in a slow cooker. Here's a simple recipe: Ingredients:

1 cup basmati rice 1 tablespoon ghee or oil 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon garam masala 1 teaspoon cumin 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 cups vegetable broth 1 cup basmati rice 1/4 cup raisins (optional)

Instructions:

Heat the ghee or oil in the slow cooker and add the onion and garlic. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add the garam masala, cumin, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Cook for 1 minute. Add the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Add the basmati rice, raisins (if using), and cover the slow cooker. Cook on low for 4-6 hours or high for 2-3 hours.

Chana Saag Chana saag, a mustard green curry with chickpeas, is a dish from Punjabi cuisine that's often served with naan bread. Here's how to make it in a slow cooker: Ingredients:

1 cup dried chickpeas 1 cup mustard greens 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon garam masala 1 teaspoon cumin 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 cups vegetable broth 1/4 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

Rinse the chickpeas and soak them in water for at least 8 hours. Drain and add them to the slow cooker. In a blender or food processor, blend the mustard greens, onion, garlic, garam masala, cumin, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper until smooth. Add the mustard green mixture to the slow cooker and cook for 30 minutes. Add the vegetable broth to the slow cooker and cook for another 30 minutes. Stir in the heavy cream and serve hot over basmati rice or with naan bread.

Conclusion Indian cuisine offers a vast array of delicious and authentic recipes that can be made in a slow cooker. From Chicken Tikka Masala to Chana Saag, these dishes are sure to satisfy your taste buds and impress your guests. Remember, the key to making great Indian food in a slow cooker is to cook on low and low and to avoid overcooking. Experiment with spices and ingredients to create your own unique flavors, and enjoy the convenience and versatility of your slow cooker. These slow-cooked recipes are perfect for busy home cooks who want to experience the rich flavors of India without sacrificing time. Whether you're looking for a hearty curry to serve with naan bread or a comforting vegetable dish to enjoy with a side of basmati rice, the options are endless. So why not try one of these recipes today and taste the magic of Indian slow cooking for yourself.
