Creamy Nut-Free Hummus Recipe for Allergy-Friendly Dipping


If you’re looking for a delicious and allergy-friendly dipping option, this creamy nut-free hummus recipe is the perfect choice. Hummus is a versatile and flavorful spread that can be enjoyed with veggies, pita bread, or as a sandwich filling. This recipe is not only nut-free, but it’s also packed with protein, making it a healthy and satisfying snack or appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 ounces each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/3 cup tahini
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish
  • Optional: additional olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, salt, and paprika. Blend until the mixture is smooth and creamy.

  2. While the food processor is running, gradually add the water until the hummus reaches your desired consistency. If it’s too thick, add more water, a tablespoon at a time.

  3. Once the hummus is smooth and creamy, transfer it to a serving bowl. If desired, drizzle a little extra olive oil on top and sprinkle with fresh parsley for a pop of color and flavor.

  4. Serve the nut-free hummus with an array of fresh, colorful vegetables such as carrot sticks, cucumber slices, bell pepper strips, and cherry tomatoes. It also pairs wonderfully with warm pita bread or as a spread in sandwiches and wraps.

Allergy-Friendly and Nut-Free

This nut-free hummus recipe is a great option for individuals with nut allergies or those looking for a nut-free alternative to traditional hummus. By omitting the use of nuts and focusing on wholesome ingredients like chickpeas, olive oil, and tahini, this recipe ensures that everyone can enjoy a creamy and flavorful dip without worry.

Health Benefits

Chickpeas, the main ingredient in hummus, are an excellent source of plant-based protein and fiber. They also contain essential vitamins and minerals such as folate, iron, and manganese. Tahini, made from sesame seeds, adds a nutty flavor and offers heart-healthy fats and additional protein. Combined with the antioxidant properties of garlic, the hummus becomes not only delicious but also a nutritious option for snacking.

Versatile and Delicious

This nut-free hummus is incredibly versatile. It can be used as a dip, spread, or even as a creamy sauce for bowls and salads. Its mild, nutty flavor makes it a crowd-pleaser, and the creamy texture is irresistible. Whether you’re hosting a party, packing a school lunch, or simply craving a healthy snack, this hummus is sure to be a hit.

Conclusion

With its creamy texture, rich flavor, and allergy-friendly nature, this nut-free hummus is a must-try recipe for anyone looking to enjoy a delicious, wholesome dip without the worry of nut allergies. It’s easy to make, packed with nutrients, and perfect for sharing with friends and family. Give this recipe a try and discover a new favorite for all your dipping and snacking needs!
