5 Easy Spring Desserts That Celebrate Seasonal Fruits


As the chill of winter melts away, spring bursts forth with a vibrant palette of fresh fruits that inspire us to create delightful desserts. The season brings an abundance of juicy strawberries, tangy rhubarb, fragrant cherries, and luscious citrus fruits. These ingredients not only taste amazing but also evoke the freshness and renewal that spring represents. In this blog post, we’ll explore five easy and delicious spring desserts that celebrate seasonal fruits, perfect for any occasion or simply to enjoy at home.

1. Strawberry Shortcake

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Strawberries: In a bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and granulated sugar. Toss gently and set aside for at least 30 minutes to allow the strawberries to macerate and release their juices.

  2. Make the Shortcake Biscuits: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to blend until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add Cream and Vanilla: Pour in the heavy cream and vanilla extract, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

  4. Shape and Bake: Turn the dough onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times. Pat into a 1-inch thick circle and cut out biscuits using a round cutter. Place on a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown.

  5. Assemble the Shortcakes: Once the biscuits have cooled slightly, slice them in half. Layer the bottom half with a generous spoonful of macerated strawberries, top with whipped cream, and place the other half of the biscuit on top. Finish with more strawberries and whipped cream as desired.

Enjoy!

This classic dessert is a celebration of spring’s star fruit, strawberries. The sweet and juicy berries combined with the light and fluffy shortcake create a delightful treat that’s perfect for any spring gathering.

2. Rhubarb Crisp

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rhubarb: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the chopped rhubarb, granulated sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Toss until the rhubarb is well coated. Pour this mixture into a greased baking dish.

  2. Make the Crisp Topping: In another bowl, mix together the rolled oats, flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon. Pour in the melted butter and stir until the mixture is crumbly.

  3. Assemble and Bake: Sprinkle the oat topping evenly over the rhubarb mixture. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the topping is golden and the rhubarb is bubbling.

  4. Serve Warm: Let the crisp cool slightly before serving. This dessert is delicious on its own or served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, making it a perfect way to enjoy the tartness of rhubarb.

Savor the Flavor

Rhubarb crisp is a comforting dessert that showcases the unique flavor of rhubarb. Its sweet and tart profile, combined with the crunchy oat topping, makes it a delightful end to any spring meal.

3. Lemon Blueberry Parfait

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup whipped cream
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Granola for layering

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Lemon Yogurt: In a mixing bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, honey, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Stir until well blended and creamy.

  2. Layer the Parfait: In serving glasses or bowls, start with a layer of the lemon yogurt mixture. Follow with a layer of fresh blueberries, then add a layer of granola. Repeat the layers until the glasses are filled, finishing with a dollop of whipped cream and a sprinkling of blueberries on top.

  3. Chill and Serve: Refrigerate the parfaits for about 30 minutes before serving to let the flavors meld together.

Refreshing Delight

This Lemon Blueberry Parfait is a bright and refreshing dessert that’s perfect for warmer days. The combination of tangy lemon yogurt, sweet blueberries, and crunchy granola creates a delightful texture and flavor that celebrates spring.

4. Cherry Clafoutis

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh cherries, pitted and halved
  • 1 cup milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Cherries: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a pie dish or baking pan with butter. Arrange the halved cherries evenly in the dish.

  2. Make the Batter: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, flour, and salt until smooth.

  3. Combine and Bake: Pour the batter over the cherries in the prepared dish. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the clafoutis is puffed and golden brown.

  4. Cool and Dust: Allow to cool slightly before dusting with powdered sugar. Serve warm or at room temperature.

A French Classic

Clafoutis is a traditional French dessert that is simple yet elegant. The creamy custard envelops the cherries, creating a delicious contrast of flavors and textures that perfectly captures the essence of spring.

5. Citrus Fruit Salad with Honey-Lime Dressing

Ingredients

  • 2 oranges, segmented
  • 2 grapefruits, segmented
  • 1 lemon, segmented
  • 1 lime, segmented
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Citrus: Using a sharp knife, carefully segment the oranges, grapefruits, lemon, and lime. Remove any seeds and pith, then place the segments in a large bowl.

  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the honey and lime juice until smooth.

  3. Combine and Serve: Drizzle the honey-lime dressing over the citrus segments and toss gently to combine. Garnish with fresh mint leaves before serving.

Bright and Zesty

This Citrus Fruit Salad is light, refreshing, and bursting with flavor. The combination of various citrus fruits, enhanced by the honey-lime dressing, makes it a vibrant dessert that celebrates the season’s bounty.

Conclusion

Spring is a time of renewal, and what better way to embrace this season than through its delicious fruits? These five easy spring desserts showcase the natural sweetness and vibrant flavors of seasonal produce. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply enjoying a quiet afternoon, these recipes are sure to bring joy and delight to your table. So grab your fresh fruits and get ready to indulge in the delights of spring!
