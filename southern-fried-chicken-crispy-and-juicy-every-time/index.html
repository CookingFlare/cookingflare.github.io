Southern Fried Chicken: Crispy and Juicy Every Time


Southern fried chicken is a dish that holds a special place in the hearts of food lovers around the world. Known for its perfectly crispy exterior and tender, juicy meat on the inside, this beloved dish has been a staple in Southern cuisine for generations. Whether served with mashed potatoes, collard greens, or cornbread, Southern fried chicken is a meal that never disappoints.

But what makes this fried chicken so irresistible? It’s all about the combination of a well-seasoned, crispy coating and the delicate balance of flavors in the chicken. The secret lies in the preparation, the choice of ingredients, and, of course, the frying technique. This blog post will walk you through how to make Southern fried chicken that is crispy and juicy every time.

Key Ingredients for Southern Fried Chicken

To make the perfect fried chicken, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken:

  • Bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces (legs, thighs, and wings are the most common, but breasts work too)
  • Buttermilk – This is essential for marinating the chicken, as it tenderizes the meat and helps the flour coating stick. It also adds a slight tanginess that enhances the flavor.
  • Hot sauce (optional) – For a bit of heat, you can add hot sauce to the buttermilk mixture.

For the Seasoned Flour Coating:

  • All-purpose flour – The base for the breading.
  • Cornstarch – This ingredient helps achieve that crispy texture everyone loves.
  • Paprika – Adds depth and a mild smokiness to the breading.
  • Garlic powder – A classic flavoring for that savory taste.
  • Onion powder – Complements the garlic powder and enhances the flavor profile.
  • Cayenne pepper – For a little kick, though you can adjust it to your heat tolerance.
  • Black pepper and salt – Essential seasonings that balance all the flavors.

For Frying:

  • Vegetable oil or peanut oil – These oils are ideal for frying because they have a high smoke point and won’t burn easily, which is key to getting the perfect crispy chicken.

Preparing the Chicken

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

To ensure the chicken remains juicy and tender, marinate it in buttermilk for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible. The longer it marinates, the more flavorful and moist the chicken will be. Add a few dashes of hot sauce to the buttermilk if you prefer a slightly spicy twist.

Step 2: Season the Chicken Coating

While your chicken is marinating, prepare the seasoned flour coating. In a large bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornstarch, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Stir the mixture thoroughly to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.

A tip: For extra crunch, some people add a pinch of baking powder to the flour mixture. This creates even more bubbles in the batter, resulting in a light and crispy coating.

Step 3: Dredge the Chicken

Once your chicken has marinated, remove it from the buttermilk, allowing any excess liquid to drip off. Next, dredge each piece of chicken in the seasoned flour mixture. Coat each piece thoroughly, pressing the flour into the skin to create a thick, even layer.

For extra crispiness, double-dip the chicken. This means dipping it back into the buttermilk and then dredging it again in the flour mixture. This technique helps to create a thicker, crunchier crust.

Frying the Chicken

Step 4: Heat the Oil

In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat the oil over medium-high heat. The oil should be deep enough to cover at least half of the chicken pieces. To test if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of flour into the oil. If it sizzles and bubbles immediately, it’s ready for frying.

The ideal frying temperature is around 350°F (175°C). If the oil is too hot, the coating will burn before the chicken cooks through; if it’s not hot enough, the chicken will become greasy and soggy.

Step 5: Fry the Chicken

Carefully add the chicken pieces to the hot oil, skin-side down. Don’t overcrowd the pan; you want to make sure each piece has enough space to cook evenly. Fry the chicken in batches if necessary.

Cook the chicken for 8-10 minutes per side, depending on the size of the pieces. You’ll know it’s done when the coating is golden brown and crispy, and the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165°F (74°C). You can use a meat thermometer to check for doneness. If you don’t have one, just make sure the juices run clear and the meat is no longer pink.

Step 6: Drain the Chicken

Once the chicken is golden brown and cooked through, use tongs to carefully remove it from the oil and place it on a wire rack or paper towels to drain any excess oil. This ensures that the chicken stays crispy and doesn’t become soggy from sitting in the oil.

Step 7: Rest the Chicken

Let the chicken rest for about 5-10 minutes before serving. This allows the juices to redistribute and ensures that each bite is tender and juicy.

Tips for Perfect Southern Fried Chicken

  1. Don’t skip the buttermilk marinade: The acid in the buttermilk tenderizes the chicken, while the fat helps create a flavorful coating. Plus, it gives the chicken a nice tangy flavor that complements the spices.

  2. Fry in batches: To ensure each piece of chicken cooks properly, fry in small batches. Overcrowding the pan can lower the oil temperature and result in greasy chicken.

  3. Use a thermometer: To avoid undercooking or overcooking your chicken, use a meat thermometer. The ideal internal temperature is 165°F (74°C).

  4. Rest the chicken: After frying, let the chicken rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to settle. This makes the chicken even juicier and ensures the crispy crust stays intact.

  5. Customize the seasoning: Feel free to adjust the seasoning to suit your taste. If you prefer a milder chicken, reduce the cayenne pepper. For extra flavor, add herbs like thyme or rosemary to the flour mixture.

Southern Fried Chicken Sides

Southern fried chicken is often served with a variety of delicious sides that complement its rich, savory flavor. Here are a few classic accompaniments:

  • Mashed Potatoes: Creamy mashed potatoes with gravy are a staple in Southern cooking and pair perfectly with fried chicken.

  • Collard Greens: The slightly bitter greens provide a nice contrast to the richness of the chicken.

  • Cornbread: A slightly sweet, crumbly cornbread is a great way to balance out the savory fried chicken.

  • Macaroni and Cheese: This comfort food classic is another Southern favorite that pairs perfectly with fried chicken.

  • Coleslaw: A tangy, crunchy coleslaw can provide a refreshing contrast to the crispy chicken.

Storing and Reheating Fried Chicken

If you have leftovers, you can store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, place the chicken in a 375°F (190°C) oven for about 10-15 minutes, or until it’s heated through and the coating becomes crispy again. Avoid reheating in the microwave, as it can make the crust soggy.

Conclusion

Making Southern fried chicken at home can seem intimidating, but with the right technique and ingredients, you can create a crispy, juicy masterpiece that rivals any restaurant version. The key is to take your time with the marinade, season the chicken well, and fry it at the right temperature. Whether you’re preparing a Sunday dinner for your family or hosting a backyard barbecue, Southern fried chicken is always a crowd-pleaser.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to make Southern fried chicken that is crispy, flavorful, and perfectly juicy every single time.
