The Perfect Pair: Duck Confit with Lentils


Duck confit is a classic French dish that never fails to impress. The tender and flavorful meat, combined with the earthy richness of lentils, creates the perfect balance of flavors. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making the perfect duck confit with lentils, step by step.

Ingredients

  • 4 duck legs
  • 4 cups of duck fat (or enough to completely submerge the duck legs)
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup of green lentils
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C).

  2. Season the duck legs with salt, pepper, dried thyme, and place them in a deep baking dish.

  3. In a small saucepan, heat the duck fat until melted. Pour the melted fat over the duck legs, ensuring they are fully submerged.

  4. Add the bay leaf to the baking dish and cover tightly with aluminum foil.

  5. Place the dish in the preheated oven and cook for about 3 to 4 hours, or until the duck meat is tender and easily falls off the bone.

  6. While the duck is cooking, prepare the lentils. Rinse the lentils under cold water and set aside.

  7. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, diced carrots, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and slightly caramelized.

  8. Add the rinsed lentils to the pot and stir well to coat them with the flavors from the vegetables.

  9. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.

  10. Once the duck is cooked, remove it from the oven and carefully transfer the duck legs to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess fat.

  11. In a separate pan, heat a little bit of the duck fat over medium-high heat. Add the duck legs, skin side down, and cook until the skin is crispy and golden brown, about 5 minutes.

  12. Serve the duck confit on a bed of lentils, garnished with fresh parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Tips and Variations

  • To save time, you can prepare the duck confit in advance and store it in the refrigerator for up to a week. When ready to serve, simply reheat the duck legs in a hot pan until warmed through.

  • If you don’t have duck fat on hand, you can substitute it with olive oil, although the flavor will be slightly different.

  • Feel free to add additional herbs and spices to the lentils according to your taste. Thyme, rosemary, or a pinch of cayenne pepper can add an extra layer of flavor.

  • For a complete meal, serve the duck confit with a side of roasted vegetables or a fresh green salad.

Enjoy this delightful dish that brings together the delicate flavors of duck confit and the hearty goodness of lentils. The tender, succulent meat paired with the earthy lentils is a match made in culinary heaven. So go ahead, impress your friends and family with this perfect pair!
