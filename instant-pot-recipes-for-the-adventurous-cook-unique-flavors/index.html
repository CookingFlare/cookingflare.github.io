Instant Pot Recipes for the Adventurous Cook: Unique Flavors


For those who enjoy pushing culinary boundaries and exploring unique flavors, the Instant Pot is more than just a convenient kitchen appliance—it’s your ticket to a world of vibrant and unconventional dishes. While this mighty device is often associated with hearty stews, soups, and classic staples, it’s also perfect for experimenting with global flavors, creative ingredient pairings, and daring techniques. In this blog post, we’ll dive into some adventurous Instant Pot recipes that are sure to excite your palate, challenge your cooking skills, and inspire your next creation.

Why the Instant Pot is Perfect for Adventurous Cooking

Before we jump into the recipes, let’s take a moment to appreciate the versatility of the Instant Pot. As a multifunctional wonder that works as a pressure cooker, slow cooker, sauté pan, steamer, and more, the Instant Pot can tackle a variety of cooking styles and techniques with ease. For cooks who love to experiment, this adaptability opens up almost endless possibilities.

Whether you’re trying to perfect the umami-packed depth of fermented foods, the delicate cooking balance required for sushi rice, or the tender yet crispy textures of fusion dishes, the Instant Pot has you covered. It also significantly reduces cooking times without compromising flavor, allowing you to explore complex dishes that might otherwise take hours to prepare.

Unique Instant Pot Recipes for Adventurous Cooks

1. Spicy Korean Braised Short Ribs (Galbi Jjim)

Korean cuisine is a hotspot for bold, spicy, and sweet flavors, and Galbi Jjim is a perfect reflection of this culinary identity. Traditionally a slow-cooked dish, this braised short rib recipe transforms beautifully in the Instant Pot, making it much faster to prepare while retaining the incredible depth of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef short ribs
  • 1 Asian pear, grated
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp gochujang (Korean fermented red chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 cup beef or chicken stock
  • 3 carrots, cut into chunks
  • 1 daikon radish, peeled and chopped
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Use the sauté function on the Instant Pot to sear the short ribs on all sides. Set aside.
  2. Mix pear, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, gochujang, sesame oil, and brown sugar into a marinade.
  3. Deglaze the Instant Pot with the beef stock, scraping up any browned bits.
  4. Return the short ribs to the pot, pour the marinade over them, and add the vegetables.
  5. Cook on high pressure for 45 minutes, then allow a natural release.
  6. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds, and serve with steamed rice or noodles.

2. Moroccan-Inspired Chickpea and Lamb Tagine

Transport yourself to the bustling souks of Marrakesh with this aromatic Moroccan-inspired dish. Traditionally cooked in a tagine, it adapts splendidly to the Instant Pot, preserving the intricate layers of spices and tenderizing the lamb to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb lamb shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/4 cup raisins or sultanas
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • Fresh cilantro and lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Use the Instant Pot’s sauté function to heat the olive oil, then brown the lamb. Set aside.
  2. Cook the onion and garlic in the pot until fragrant, then stir in all the spices.
  3. Return the lamb to the pot and add the chickpeas, apricots, raisins, stock, and tomatoes.
  4. Pressure cook on high for 35 minutes, then quick-release the pressure.
  5. Serve over couscous or rice, garnished with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lemon juice.

3. Indian-Spiced Mango Dal

For a vegetarian dish packed with vibrant, tangy, and savory flavors, look no further than this Indian-inspired mango dal. Lentils shine in this recipe, thanks to the Instant Pot’s ability to break them down into creamy perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 large ripe mango, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (adjust for spice tolerance)
  • 2 cups water or vegetable stock
  • 1 tbsp ghee or coconut oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the ghee or coconut oil using the sauté function, then toast the mustard seeds until they pop.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger and cook until softened. Stir in turmeric, coriander, and chili powder.
  3. Add the lentils, tomatoes, and water or stock, then pressure cook on high for 10 minutes. Allow a natural release.
  4. After cooking, stir in the diced mango and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  5. Garnish with cilantro and serve with naan or steamed basmati rice.

4. Thai Green Curry with Jackfruit

Move over, chicken—young green jackfruit is the perfect canvas for soaking up the bold, delicious flavors of Thai green curry. This plant-based dish is as resourceful as it is flavorful, giving adventurous cooks a chance to work with a less conventional ingredient.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can young green jackfruit, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tbsp green curry paste
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable stock
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegan)
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (green beans, zucchini, bell peppers)
  • Fresh Thai basil and lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In sauté mode, heat the green curry paste until fragrant.
  2. Stir in the coconut milk, vegetable stock, lime leaves, fish sauce, and sugar.
  3. Add the jackfruit and vegetables to the pot, then cook on high pressure for 5 minutes. Perform a quick release.
  4. Garnish with Thai basil and lime wedges, and serve with jasmine rice.

5. Chocolate Mole Pulled Chicken Tacos

Bring the bold, slightly sweet, and spicy flavors of traditional mole sauce to your dinner table with this easy pulled chicken taco recipe. This Mexican-inspired dish takes full advantage of the Instant Pot’s pressure-cooking capabilities to infuse rich, layered flavors into the chicken.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp mole paste
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Corn tortillas, for serving
  • Fresh cilantro, queso fresco, and lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Use the sauté setting to lightly cook the onion and garlic.
  2. Stir in the mole paste, cocoa powder, peanut butter, honey, cinnamon, chili powder, and chicken stock until combined.
  3. Add the chicken to the pot, coating it well with the sauce.
  4. Pressure cook on high for 15 minutes, then shred the chicken using two forks.
  5. Serve in warm tortillas, garnished with cilantro, queso fresco, and lime wedges.

Final Tips for Adventurous Instant Pot Cooks

When venturing into bold and exotic flavors, the key is to embrace curiosity and creativity. Don’t hesitate to substitute ingredients based on your pantry or dietary needs. Play with spices, sauces, and textures to make these recipes your own. The Instant Pot is here to help you cut down preparation time, allowing you to focus on experimenting with new and exciting flavor combinations.

Ready to embark on your next culinary adventure? Fire up your Instant Pot, and let the journey begin!
