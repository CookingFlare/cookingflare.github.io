Rosemary Olive Focaccia Bread: A Herbaceous Delight


Focaccia – the outrageously delicious Italian flatbread – has long been a staple in my kitchen. Its soft, pillow-like texture and mouthwatering flavors make it a crowd-pleaser and an excellent meal accompaniment. This time, I’m sharing a unique variation: Rosemary Olive Focaccia Bread that adds a touch of herbaceous delight to each bite.

Let’s gather our ingredients for this aromatic experience:

For the Bread Dough:

  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt (use half for the dough, half for the topping)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the Topping:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons minced fresh rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons chopped black olives
  • Salt, to taste

Now that our ingredients are ready, let’s set sail on this tasty journey!

Activating the Yeast:

First things first, let’s give our yeast the perfect home. Combine the warm water and yeast in a bowl and let it sit for about 5 minutes. Once it becomes frothy, we know our yeast is activated and ready for action!

Creating the Dough:

In a large mixing bowl, combine the activated yeast, 1 ½ cups of flour, and the salt. Gradually add the olive oil and mix well to form a soft and pliable dough.

Kneading the Dough:

Once the dough has come together, transfer it to a lightly floured surface. Knead for about 5 minutes, until the dough is smooth and elastic.

First Rest:

Now it’s time for our dough to rest. Shape it into a ball and place it in a bowl coated with a thin layer of olive oil. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it sit in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, until it has doubled in size.

Shaping and Second Rest:

After the first rise, punch down the dough gently. Transfer it to a greased 9x13-inch baking pan, spreading it evenly to the edges. Cover with the damp cloth once again and let it rise for an additional 30-45 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

Topping the Dough:

While our bread dough continues to rise, let’s prepare our topping. Combine minced fresh rosemary, chopped black olives, and the remaining salt in a bowl.

Adding the Topping:

Once your dough has finished its second rise, remove the damp cloth and scatter the rosemary-olive mixture evenly over the dough. You can lightly press the toppings into the dough to ensure they adhere properly during baking.

Baking:

Finally, it’s time to bring our Rosemary Olive Focaccia Bread to life. Bake it in a preheated oven at 425°F (220°C) for 20-25 minutes, until golden brown. Remove from the oven and allow it to cool on a wire rack.

Slicing & Serving:

Once your bread has cooled, slice and serve it warm as an aromatic side dish, or enjoy it as a standalone meal. Rosemary Olive Focaccia Bread is best enjoyed on the same day it’s made, but you can store leftovers in an airtight container for up to two days.

Allow this Rosemary Olive Focaccia Bread to transport your taste buds to a Mediterranean paradise. Its rich, herbaceous flavors will have your friends and family begging for seconds! From my kitchen to yours, happy cooking!
