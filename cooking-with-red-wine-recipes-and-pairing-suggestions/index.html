Cooking with Red Wine: Recipes and Pairing Suggestions


Red wine is not only a delightful beverage to enjoy on its own, but it also adds depth and richness to a wide variety of dishes. From savory stews to decadent desserts, the addition of red wine can elevate your culinary creations to new heights. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious recipes and pairing suggestions that showcase the versatility of cooking with red wine.

Recipes

1. Red Wine Braised Short Ribs

Tender, succulent, and packed with flavor, red wine-braised short ribs are a classic dish that never fails to impress. To make this mouthwatering dish, you will need:

  • 4 pounds of beef short ribs
  • 1 bottle of red wine (such as Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot)
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Start by seasoning the short ribs with salt and pepper. In a large Dutch oven, heat some olive oil over medium-high heat and brown the short ribs on all sides. Remove the ribs from the pot and set them aside.

In the same pot, sauté the onions, garlic, carrots, and celery until they begin to soften. Add the red wine and bring it to a simmer, scraping the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. Return the short ribs to the pot and add the beef broth and rosemary sprigs.

Cover the pot and transfer it to a preheated oven at 325°F (163°C). Allow the ribs to braise for about 3 hours, or until they are tender and the meat easily falls off the bone. Serve the braised short ribs with the reduced cooking liquid and enjoy the rich flavors of the red wine-infused meat.

2. Coq au Vin

Coq au Vin is a traditional French dish that epitomizes the art of cooking with red wine. It is a hearty and comforting chicken stew that combines tender chicken, aromatic vegetables, and a robust red wine sauce. To make this classic dish, gather the following ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups of red wine (such as Burgundy or Pinot Noir)
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

In a large Dutch oven, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, brown the chicken pieces on all sides. Remove the chicken and set it aside.

In the same pot, sauté the onions, garlic, and mushrooms until they are golden brown. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and cook for a minute to remove the raw flour taste. Add the tomato paste, red wine, and chicken broth. Stir well to combine.

Return the chicken and bacon to the pot, cover, and simmer for about 1 hour, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the Coq au Vin with crusty bread or buttery mashed potatoes, and garnish with fresh parsley.

Pairing Suggestions

Now that you have some delicious red wine-infused recipes, let’s explore the art of pairing these dishes with the perfect red wine. Here are some suggestions to enhance your culinary experience:

  • Red Wine Braised Short Ribs: Pair this rich and hearty dish with a full-bodied red wine, such as Cabernet Sauvignon or Syrah. The bold flavors of the wine will complement the robust flavors of the braised meat.

  • Coq au Vin: This classic French dish calls for a medium-bodied red wine with earthy undertones. Consider pairing it with a Pinot Noir or a red Burgundy for a harmonious combination of flavors.

  • Chocolate Red Wine Cake: Indulge your sweet tooth with a decadent chocolate cake infused with red wine. Pair this luscious dessert with a glass of Port or a sweet red wine like Zinfandel for a delightful combination of flavors.

Remember, the key to successful food and wine pairings is to experiment and have fun. Trust your taste buds and let your palate guide you to the perfect combination. Whether you’re cooking with red wine or enjoying it alongside your meal, the possibilities are endless. Cheers to the joy of cooking and the pleasures of red wine!
