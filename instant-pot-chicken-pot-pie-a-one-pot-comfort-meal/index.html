Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie: A One-Pot Comfort Meal


There’s something undeniably comforting about a warm, hearty chicken pot pie. It’s a dish that has been passed down through generations, often symbolizing home-cooked love and care. The crispy, golden crust, combined with creamy, savory chicken and vegetables, is a satisfying meal that can be enjoyed at any time of year. However, when you don’t have the time or patience for traditional pie-making, an Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie is the perfect solution.

This recipe delivers all the flavors you crave, but with a fraction of the time and effort. Thanks to the Instant Pot, this one-pot wonder can be prepared quickly, making it ideal for busy weeknights or when you just want a comforting, no-fuss meal. This chicken pot pie recipe is the ultimate in convenience without sacrificing flavor or texture.

Why Make Chicken Pot Pie in an Instant Pot?

The Instant Pot is a game-changer in the kitchen. Its ability to cook meals in a fraction of the time, while still delivering the rich, tender results of slow cooking, makes it perfect for dishes like chicken pot pie. Traditional chicken pot pie requires baking the pie in the oven, which can take quite a bit of time and attention. By using an Instant Pot, we eliminate the long baking process and make this dish more accessible for everyday cooking.

Instant Pot cooking allows the chicken and vegetables to cook under pressure, locking in all their flavors while keeping them tender and juicy. The creamy sauce is made with simple pantry staples and thickens beautifully, creating a rich and satisfying filling. Plus, since this recipe is made in a single pot, cleanup is a breeze—meaning you can enjoy your meal without having to worry about washing multiple dishes.

Ingredients You’ll Need for Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie

Before you start, make sure you have all the ingredients for this savory dish. This recipe is designed to be simple yet flavorful, so you won’t need anything too complicated. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Filling:

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 pound of boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or thighs for more flavor)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 4 cups chicken broth (preferably low-sodium)
  • 1 cup half-and-half (or whole milk for a lighter option)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (for thickening)

For the Crust:

  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crusts (or homemade, if preferred)

Optional Garnishes:

  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for a burst of freshness)

How to Make Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, let’s dive into the cooking process. You’ll be amazed at how easy it is to make a comforting, delicious chicken pot pie with the help of your Instant Pot.

Step 1: Sauté the Chicken

Start by setting your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” mode. Once the pot is heated, add the olive oil. Place the chicken breasts (or thighs) into the pot and sear them for about 3-4 minutes on each side, until they’re browned. This step helps to develop a deeper flavor in the chicken, making the dish even more flavorful.

Once the chicken is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside. You’ll shred the chicken later, but for now, just let it rest while you prepare the other ingredients.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pot, add the chopped onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Stir the vegetables around and sauté them for about 3-4 minutes until they start to soften and become fragrant. This step builds the base flavor for your chicken pot pie filling.

Step 3: Add the Broth and Seasonings

Next, pour in the chicken broth and half-and-half, followed by the thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together, making sure to scrape up any bits of browned chicken or vegetables stuck to the bottom of the pot (this is where a lot of flavor is!).

Step 4: Pressure Cook the Chicken

Now it’s time to add the chicken back into the Instant Pot. Place the chicken breasts or thighs on top of the vegetable mixture. Secure the lid, making sure the pressure valve is set to “Sealing.” Select the “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” setting and cook on high for 10 minutes.

While the chicken is cooking, it’s a great time to prepare the pie crust. If you’re using store-bought crusts, unroll them and set them aside. If you’re making your own, now’s the time to get them ready for the top of your pie.

Step 5: Shred the Chicken

Once the cooking time is up, allow the Instant Pot to naturally release pressure for 5 minutes before manually releasing the remaining pressure. Carefully open the lid and remove the chicken from the pot. Shred the chicken using two forks, then return it to the pot with the vegetables and broth.

Step 6: Thicken the Filling

To thicken the filling, combine the cornstarch with a tablespoon of water in a small bowl to make a slurry. Stir the slurry into the chicken and vegetable mixture, then set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode again. Allow the mixture to simmer for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the filling has thickened to a creamy consistency.

Step 7: Prepare the Pie Crust

While the filling is thickening, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out your pie crust on a lightly floured surface and place it over a pie dish that is slightly larger than the Instant Pot insert. Trim any excess dough, and set the pie crust aside.

Step 8: Assemble the Pot Pie

Once the filling is thickened, remove the Instant Pot insert from the cooker and place the chicken and vegetable filling into the prepared pie dish. Carefully drape the pie crust over the top of the filling, sealing the edges to the dish. You can use a fork to press down the edges for a more decorative look or leave it as is for a rustic appearance.

Step 9: Bake the Pie

Place the pie dish in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and crispy. You may want to check the pie halfway through and cover the edges with aluminum foil if they are browning too quickly.

Once the crust is golden and the filling is bubbly, remove the pie from the oven. Allow it to cool for about 10 minutes before serving. This step helps to set the filling, making it easier to cut and serve.

Step 10: Garnish and Serve

Garnish the chicken pot pie with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of color and flavor. Serve it alongside a simple green salad or your favorite side dishes for the perfect comfort meal.

Tips for a Perfect Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie

  • Chicken Variety: While this recipe uses boneless, skinless chicken breasts, chicken thighs will provide a richer, more flavorful filling. You can also use rotisserie chicken for a quick shortcut if you prefer.
  • Vegetable Variations: Don’t hesitate to add other vegetables to the filling. Mushrooms, potatoes, or even parsnips can be wonderful additions. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly if you add more dense vegetables like potatoes.
  • Thicker Filling: If you prefer a thicker filling, you can add more cornstarch or use heavy cream instead of half-and-half.
  • Crust Options: You can make your own pie crust or use store-bought for convenience. If you want to make this recipe gluten-free, opt for a gluten-free pie crust.

Why You’ll Love This Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie

This Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie is the epitome of comfort food, packed with flavor, texture, and warmth. Not only is it quick to prepare, but it also tastes just as delicious as traditional homemade pot pie. The creamy filling, combined with tender chicken and perfectly cooked vegetables, is simply irresistible.

The best part? It’s a one-pot dish that minimizes cleanup, so you can focus on enjoying the meal without the hassle. Whether you’re serving it to your family, sharing with friends, or enjoying it on your own after a long day, this dish will quickly become a staple in your meal rotation.

Final Thoughts

Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie is a game-changing recipe for those who crave the classic flavors of chicken pot pie without the hours of preparation. It’s a comforting, easy, and satisfying meal that’s sure to please everyone at the table. The next time you’re in the mood for a cozy dinner, don’t hesitate to fire up your Instant Pot and enjoy this one-pot wonder. Happy cooking!
