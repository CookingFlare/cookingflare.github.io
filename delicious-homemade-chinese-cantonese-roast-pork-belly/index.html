Delicious Homemade Chinese Cantonese Roast Pork Belly


Cantonese cuisine is renowned for its flavorsome dishes, and among its many iconic offerings, Cantonese Roast Pork Belly, or “Siu Yuk,” stands out as a beloved favorite. The perfect balance of crispy, crackling skin, juicy meat, and aromatic seasonings makes this dish a staple in Chinese kitchens and restaurants worldwide. While it might seem like a dish best left to the professionals, making Cantonese Roast Pork Belly at home can be surprisingly easy with the right techniques and ingredients. In this post, we will walk you through the step-by-step process to create this delicious, golden-brown masterpiece in your own kitchen.

Whether you are a seasoned chef or a home cook looking to try something new, this recipe will guide you to achieve the perfectly roasted pork belly, just like you would get at your favorite Cantonese restaurant.

Understanding the Cantonese Roast Pork Belly

Before diving into the recipe, it’s essential to understand what makes Cantonese Roast Pork Belly so special. The key components of the dish are the pork belly and the crispy skin. The pork belly itself is typically marinated with a mixture of seasonings that enhance its natural flavor, and the skin is prepared to achieve that signature crispy texture when roasted.

The Cantonese style of roasting pork belly is unique in that the meat is usually air-dried before roasting. This helps to draw out excess moisture from the skin, making it more conducive to crisping up in the oven. The traditional method requires a lot of time and attention to detail, but with some preparation and patience, you can easily recreate this delicious dish at home.

Ingredients for Cantonese Roast Pork Belly

To get started, you’ll need a few key ingredients. Here’s what you will need for the perfect Cantonese Roast Pork Belly:

For the Pork Belly:

  • 2 pounds of pork belly (skin-on, with a good layer of fat)
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar (for cleaning the skin)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for the skin and seasoning)
  • 1 tablespoon five-spice powder
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons rice wine (Shaoxing wine is ideal)
  • 2 teaspoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (for dusting the skin)

For the Marinade:

  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Chinese rice wine
  • 1/2 teaspoon five-spice powder

For Air-Drying:

  • Salt, for sprinkling on the skin
  • A rack or hook to hang the pork belly during the drying process

Step-by-Step Instructions for Homemade Cantonese Roast Pork Belly

Step 1: Preparing the Pork Belly

Start by purchasing fresh pork belly with skin on. The skin is essential to creating the crispy texture that makes this dish so irresistible. You’ll want to trim the pork belly to an even thickness if needed.

Next, clean the skin with white vinegar. This is an important step, as it helps to remove any residual hair and grime from the skin. Rub the vinegar into the skin and let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing it off.

After cleaning, use a sharp knife to score the skin lightly, making sure to only cut through the skin and not the meat. The scoring allows the skin to expand and crisp up during the roasting process. Ensure the cuts are shallow, spaced about 1/4 inch apart, to prevent cutting into the flesh beneath.

Step 2: Marinate the Pork Belly

Now, it’s time to marinate the pork belly. In a small bowl, combine the five-spice powder, sugar, soy sauce, rice wine, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, and white pepper. Stir well until all the ingredients are thoroughly mixed into a smooth marinade.

Rub the marinade all over the meat side of the pork belly, ensuring the flavors permeate the meat. Be careful not to get any marinade on the skin side, as the skin needs to remain dry to achieve the crispy texture.

Cover the pork belly and refrigerate it for at least 2 to 3 hours, or ideally overnight. The longer the pork belly marinates, the more flavor it will absorb.

Step 3: Air-Dry the Skin

The key to achieving crispy skin is air-drying. After marinating the pork belly, use a paper towel to pat the skin side dry. Then, rub the skin with a generous amount of salt and dust it lightly with cornstarch. The cornstarch helps to absorb moisture and encourages the skin to crisp up.

Hang the pork belly in the refrigerator for 6 to 12 hours, or overnight if possible. You can hang it on a rack, or use hooks to hang it from the top of your refrigerator if you have the space. The goal is to allow the skin to dry out completely, which will give you that perfect, crackling texture when roasted.

Step 4: Roasting the Pork Belly

Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Place a wire rack on a baking sheet, and lay the marinated pork belly on the rack, skin-side up.

Roast the pork belly in the preheated oven for about 30 to 40 minutes, or until the skin begins to bubble and turn golden brown. At this point, lower the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue roasting for another 30 to 40 minutes to cook the meat through and render the fat.

For an extra crispy skin, you can switch the oven to the broiler setting for the final 5 to 10 minutes of roasting. Keep a close eye on it during this time to ensure the skin doesn’t burn.

Step 5: Resting and Serving the Roast Pork Belly

Once the pork belly is beautifully roasted and the skin is crispy, remove it from the oven. Let it rest for 10 to 15 minutes before slicing it into bite-sized pieces. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and tender meat inside.

To serve, arrange the roasted pork belly on a platter and garnish with sliced green onions or fresh herbs, if desired. You can enjoy this dish as is, or pair it with steamed rice, stir-fried vegetables, or even a warm noodle soup. It’s versatile and perfect for any meal.

Tips for the Perfect Cantonese Roast Pork Belly

  1. Skin Preparation: The key to crispy skin lies in the drying process. Don’t rush it! If you can, allow the pork belly to dry out in the fridge overnight for the best results.

  2. Scoring the Skin: When scoring the skin, use a very sharp knife to make shallow cuts. If you go too deep, the skin won’t crisp as well.

  3. Marinade: Be sure not to get any marinade on the skin. This will prevent the skin from drying out properly and interfere with the crisping process.

  4. Roasting Time: The roasting time may vary depending on the size of your pork belly, so keep a close eye on it. If the skin isn’t as crispy as you’d like, you can always broil it for an additional few minutes.

  5. Resting the Meat: Let the roast rest after removing it from the oven. This will ensure that the meat stays juicy and tender when sliced.

Pairing Your Cantonese Roast Pork Belly

Cantonese Roast Pork Belly pairs wonderfully with a variety of side dishes. Consider serving it with:

  • Steamed Rice: The simple yet aromatic rice acts as the perfect base to soak up the savory juices of the roast pork belly.
  • Stir-Fried Vegetables: A mix of seasonal vegetables stir-fried in a light soy sauce adds freshness to balance the richness of the pork.
  • Chinese Noodles: A bowl of noodles in broth with a few pieces of roast pork belly on top is another delicious way to enjoy this dish.

Final Thoughts

Making Cantonese Roast Pork Belly at home is not as difficult as it may seem. With some preparation, patience, and attention to detail, you can create a restaurant-quality dish that is sure to impress your family and friends. The combination of tender, juicy pork and crispy, crackling skin makes it an irresistible treat for any occasion.

With this recipe, you are sure to unlock the secret to making Cantonese Roast Pork Belly just like a professional. So gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and enjoy a delicious homemade meal that brings the flavors of Cantonese cuisine straight to your kitchen.

Happy cooking!
