Grilling Corn on the Cob: Buttery and Smoky Recipes


There’s something magical about the taste of grilled corn on the cob. The smoky char, the tender kernels bursting with flavor, and the heavenly buttery goodness all combine to create a mouthwatering dish that is perfect for summer cookouts. In this blog post, we will explore two delicious recipes that will take your grilled corn on the cob to the next level. So fire up your grill and get ready to indulge in some corny goodness!

Recipe 1: Classic Buttered Grilled Corn on the Cob

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter, softened
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Peel back the husks of the corn, but leave them attached at the base.
  3. Remove the silk from the corn by gently pulling it off.
  4. Spread the softened butter evenly over each ear of corn.
  5. Sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Pull the husks back up over the corn, covering it completely.
  7. Secure the husks with a piece of kitchen twine or a strip of aluminum foil.
  8. Place the corn on the grill and cook for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally.
  9. Remove the corn from the grill and let it cool for a few minutes.
  10. Carefully remove the husks and serve the corn hot with additional butter, if desired.

Recipe 2: Smoky and Spicy Grilled Corn on the Cob

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Peel back the husks of the corn, but leave them attached at the base.
  3. Remove the silk from the corn by gently pulling it off.
  4. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, smoked paprika, garlic powder, chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Brush the butter mixture evenly over each ear of corn, making sure to coat all sides.
  6. Pull the husks back up over the corn, covering it completely.
  7. Secure the husks with a piece of kitchen twine or a strip of aluminum foil.
  8. Place the corn on the grill and cook for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally.
  9. Remove the corn from the grill and let it cool for a few minutes.
  10. Carefully remove the husks and serve the corn hot with lime wedges on the side.

Tips for Grilling Corn on the Cob

  • Soaking the corn in water for about 15 minutes before grilling can help prevent it from drying out.
  • If you prefer a smokier flavor, you can add some wood chips to your grill.
  • Experiment with different seasonings and spices to create your own unique grilled corn recipes.
  • If you’re short on time, you can also grill the corn without the husks for a quicker cooking process.

Grilled corn on the cob is a versatile and delicious dish that can be enjoyed on its own or as a side to complement your favorite grilled meats. Whether you prefer the classic buttery version or the smoky and spicy twist, these recipes are sure to impress your family and friends at your next barbecue. So fire up the grill, grab some fresh corn, and let the flavors of summer shine through!
