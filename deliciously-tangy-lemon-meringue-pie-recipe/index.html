Deliciously Tangy Lemon Meringue Pie Recipe


There’s something about the combination of tangy lemon and fluffy meringue that creates a symphony of flavors in every bite. If you’re a fan of citrusy desserts, then this Deliciously Tangy Lemon Meringue Pie recipe is a must-try. The zesty lemon filling paired with the light and airy meringue topping is sure to tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving for more. So, let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups of graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted

For the filling:

  • 1 cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon zest
  • 1 can (14 ounces) of sweetened condensed milk
  • 4 large egg yolks

For the meringue:

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cream of tartar
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated with the butter.

  3. Press the crumb mixture onto the bottom and sides of a 9-inch pie dish, forming an even crust. Make sure to press firmly to ensure it holds together during baking.

  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, lemon zest, sweetened condensed milk, and egg yolks until well combined. The mixture should be smooth and creamy.

  5. Pour the lemon filling into the prepared crust, spreading it evenly.

  6. Bake the pie in the preheated oven for about 15 minutes, or until the filling is set and slightly firm to the touch. Remove from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack.

  7. While the pie is cooling, prepare the meringue topping. In a clean bowl, beat the egg whites and cream of tartar on medium-high speed until soft peaks form.

  8. Gradually add the sugar, a tablespoon at a time, while continuing to beat the egg whites. Beat until stiff peaks form and the meringue is glossy.

  9. Once the pie has cooled completely, spread the meringue over the lemon filling, making sure to cover the entire surface. You can create decorative peaks using a spatula or the back of a spoon.

  10. Return the pie to the oven and bake for an additional 10-12 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown.

  11. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool completely before serving. This will allow the meringue to set and the flavors to meld together.

  12. Once cooled, slice into wedges and serve this deliciously tangy lemon meringue pie to your eager guests or enjoy it all by yourself!

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic lemon meringue pie, you can add a tablespoon of freshly grated ginger to the filling mixture. This will give the pie a subtle warmth and depth of flavor.

  • If you prefer a more rustic look, you can skip the meringue topping and opt for a dollop of whipped cream instead. The creamy sweetness of the whipped cream will complement the tangy lemon filling beautifully.

  • To make the pie ahead of time, you can prepare the crust and the filling separately and assemble them just before serving. This way, the crust will remain crispy, and the filling will retain its vibrant flavor.

  • Don’t forget to zest your lemons before juicing them! The zest adds an extra burst of citrusy aroma and flavor to the pie.

So, there you have it - a recipe for a Deliciously Tangy Lemon Meringue Pie that will surely become a favorite in your dessert repertoire. The combination of the zesty lemon filling, buttery crust, and fluffy meringue topping creates a harmonious balance of flavors and textures. Whether you’re serving it at a dinner party or enjoying it as a special treat at home, this pie is bound to impress. Give it a try and prepare to be delighted!
