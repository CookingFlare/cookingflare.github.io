Green Curry: A Spicy and Flavorful Thai Delight


Green curry is a popular Thai dish known for its vibrant green color, bold flavors, and spicy kick. Made with a combination of aromatic herbs and spices, this curry is a true delight for your taste buds. In this blog post, we will explore the secrets behind creating a delicious and authentic green curry from scratch.

Ingredients

To make green curry paste, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4-6 green Thai chilies (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 2 stalks of lemongrass, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 shallot, roughly chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of galangal or ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of shrimp paste (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon of cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon of white peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 cup of fresh cilantro leaves and stems
  • 1 cup of fresh Thai basil leaves

For the curry:

  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 500g of chicken, beef, or tofu, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 can (400ml) of coconut milk
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian version)
  • 1 tablespoon of palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 cup of sliced bamboo shoots
  • 1 cup of Thai eggplants, quartered
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by making the green curry paste. In a mortar and pestle or a food processor, grind the green Thai chilies, lemongrass, garlic, shallot, galangal or ginger, shrimp paste (if using), coriander seeds, cumin seeds, white peppercorns, salt, and sugar into a smooth paste.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously, until fragrant.

  3. Add the chicken, beef, or tofu to the pan and cook until browned on all sides. If using meat, make sure it is cooked through before proceeding.

  4. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken or vegetable broth. Stir well to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Add the fish sauce (or soy sauce) and palm sugar (or brown sugar) to the curry. Stir until the sugar has dissolved.

  6. Add the bamboo shoots, Thai eggplants, and red bell pepper to the pan. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

  7. Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning if needed. If you prefer a spicier curry, you can add more green Thai chilies at this stage.

  8. Remove the pan from the heat and garnish with fresh Thai basil leaves.

  9. Serve the green curry hot with steamed jasmine rice or noodles.

Tips and Variations

  • If you can’t find fresh Thai basil, you can substitute it with regular basil. However, Thai basil has a unique flavor that adds authenticity to the dish.

  • Feel free to customize your green curry by adding other vegetables like snow peas, baby corn, or mushrooms.

  • For a vegetarian or vegan version, omit the shrimp paste and use soy sauce instead of fish sauce.

  • If you prefer a milder curry, you can reduce the amount of green Thai chilies or remove the seeds before grinding.

  • Leftover green curry can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will intensify over time, making it even more delicious.

Conclusion

Green curry is a wonderful Thai dish that combines bold flavors, fragrant herbs, and a spicy kick. By making your own green curry paste from scratch, you can tailor the flavors to your liking and create a truly authentic dish. Whether you prefer it with chicken, beef, or tofu, this spicy and flavorful Thai delight is sure to satisfy your cravings. So gather your ingredients, unleash your inner chef, and enjoy the aromatic journey of making and savoring a homemade green curry.
