Cozy Winter Baked Macaroni and Cheese


Warm up your winter with this comforting and delicious Cozy Winter Baked Macaroni and Cheese recipe. This classic dish is the perfect comfort food for those chilly nights, and it’s so easy to make. With its gooey cheese, creamy sauce, and crispy breadcrumb topping, this dish will surely become a family favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. Cook the macaroni according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.

  3. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk constantly for about 2 minutes to create a roux.

  4. Slowly pour in the milk while whisking continuously. Cook for another 5 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.

  5. Add the shredded cheddar, Gruyere, and Parmesan cheese to the sauce. Stir until the cheese has melted and the sauce is smooth.

  6. Season the sauce with salt, black pepper, and nutmeg. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.

  7. Add the cooked macaroni to the sauce and mix until well coated.

  8. Transfer the macaroni and cheese mixture to the greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  9. In a small bowl, combine the panko breadcrumbs with a drizzle of melted butter. Sprinkle the breadcrumb mixture over the macaroni and cheese.

  10. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly.

  11. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve the Cozy Winter Baked Macaroni and Cheese as a main course with a side of green salad or roasted vegetables.
  • For an extra indulgent touch, add crumbled bacon or chopped cooked ham to the macaroni and cheese before baking.
  • This dish pairs well with a glass of rich red wine or a crisp white wine.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a creamier macaroni and cheese, you can substitute some of the whole milk with heavy cream.
  • Experiment with different types of cheese to create your own unique flavor combinations. Smoked Gouda, Fontina, or Monterey Jack are great options.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Final Thoughts

Cozy Winter Baked Macaroni and Cheese is the ultimate comfort food that will warm your soul and satisfy your cravings. With its creamy, cheesy goodness and crispy breadcrumb topping, it’s a dish that will bring joy to your family and friends. Whether you’re snuggled up on the couch or hosting a winter gathering, this recipe is sure to be a hit. So grab your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to indulge in this delicious winter treat.
