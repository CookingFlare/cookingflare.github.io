How to Make Delicious Agedashi Tofu: Fried Tofu in Broth


Agedashi tofu is a beloved dish in Japanese cuisine, known for its delicate balance of flavors and textures. This dish showcases the versatility of tofu, transforming it into a crispy delight when fried and served in a savory broth. The art of making agedashi tofu involves a few simple steps, ensuring that even novice cooks can impress their family and friends with this restaurant-quality dish right at home. Let’s dive into the steps you need to follow to create your very own delicious agedashi tofu. Ingredients List To successfully prepare agedashi tofu, gather the following ingredients: For the Tofu:

1 block of firm tofu (about 14 oz.) Cornstarch or potato starch, for dusting Vegetable oil, for deep frying Optional: 1 tablespoon of salt for brining

For the Broth:

1 cup of dashi (Japanese soup stock) 2 tablespoons of soy sauce 1 tablespoon of mirin (sweet rice wine) 1 teaspoon of grated ginger (optional) 1 teaspoon of sugar (adjust based on preference)

For Garnishing:

Chopped green onions or scallions Grated daikon radish Shredded bonito flakes (katsuobushi) Nori strips

Step-by-Step Instructions Step 1: Preparing the Tofu Properly preparing your tofu is crucial to achieving the best texture for your agedashi tofu. Start by taking the block of firm tofu and draining it from its packaging. Gently press the tofu between paper towels or a clean kitchen cloth to remove excess moisture. This is important because moisture will inhibit the tofu from frying properly, preventing that desirable crispiness. If you have time, consider brining your tofu in a light saltwater solution for about 15-30 minutes to infuse flavor. To make the brine, dissolve a tablespoon of salt in a bowl of water and fully submerge the tofu. After brining, remove the tofu and press it again to eliminate any remaining moisture. Step 2: Cutting the Tofu Once the tofu is adequately dried, cut it into bite-sized cubes. Typically, 1-inch cubes work best, as they allow for even cooking and frying, ensuring that each piece has a crispy exterior and a soft, creamy interior. Step 3: Dusting with Starch For that crunchy outer layer, dredge each tofu piece in cornstarch or potato starch. This coating is what enables the fried tofu to develop a perfectly crispy texture. Once dusted, shake off any excess starch, so that only a light coating remains. Step 4: Frying the Tofu In a deep pot or frying pan, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. You will need enough oil to submerge at least half of the tofu cubes. The ideal frying temperature is around 350°F (175°C). Test if the oil is ready by carefully dropping a small piece of tofu or a pinch of cornstarch into it. If it sizzles immediately, the oil is hot enough. Carefully place the dusted tofu cubes into the hot oil in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. This will ensure that the temperature of the oil remains consistent, which is key to frying. Fry each batch for about 3-4 minutes, until the tofu is golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally for even cooking. Once fried, transfer the tofu onto a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Step 5: Preparing the Broth While the tofu is frying, it’s time to prepare the broth. In a saucepan, combine the dashi, soy sauce, mirin, grated ginger, and sugar. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Taste it, and adjust the seasoning according to your preference - you may like a sweeter broth or a saltier flavor. Allow the broth to simmer for 5-10 minutes so that the flavors fully meld together. Once ready, turn off the heat and set aside. Step 6: Assembling the Agedashi Tofu To serve your agedashi tofu, place a few pieces of crispy tofu into individual bowls. Carefully ladle the hot broth over the tofu, ensuring it is generously coated. This not only enhances the flavor but also helps to keep the tofu warm. Step 7: Adding Garnishes Now comes the fun part – garnishing your agedashi tofu! Sprinkle the chopped green onions or scallions over each serving. Then, top with a dollop of grated daikon radish for a refreshing crunch. If available, add a sprinkle of bonito flakes, which will dance delightfully atop the hot broth. For an extra touch, include some nori strips to add an umami-rich flavor to the dish. Step 8: Serving and Enjoying Agedashi tofu is best enjoyed immediately while the tofu is warm and the broth is piping hot. Serve it as an appetizer or as part of a larger Japanese-inspired meal. Pair it with steamed rice, pickled vegetables, or a light salad to create a well-rounded dining experience. This dish not only tantalizes the taste buds but also offers a comforting warm sensation that makes it perfect for any occasion. Tips for Achieving the Best Agedashi Tofu

Choosing the Right Tofu: Opt for firm or extra-firm tofu for frying. Silken tofu can crumble and won’t hold its shape during frying.

Temperature Control: Maintain the oil temperature while frying. If it drops too low, the tofu will absorb excess oil and become greasy.

Keep it Simple: While you can certainly add more garnishes and sides, the beauty of agedashi tofu is in its simplicity. Let the flavors of the tofu and broth shine!

Experiment with Broth Variations: Feel free to experiment with the broth ingredients. Adding mushrooms or different types of miso can add depth and complexity to the dish.

Gluten-Free Options: For a gluten-free version, use tamari instead of soy sauce, and ensure your dashi does not contain any gluten.

Conclusion Creating delicious agedashi tofu at home may seem intricate, but with a bit of practice, you'll quickly master the technique. This dish celebrates the subtle flavors and unique textures that make Japanese cuisine so beloved worldwide. Whether you’re enjoying it as an appetizer or making it the centerpiece of your meal, homemade agedashi tofu is sure to impress. So gather your ingredients, follow these steps, and relish the experience of cooking this fantastic Japanese dish. The end result will reward your efforts with a delightful, crispy tofu immersed in a rich broth, crowned with fresh garnishes. Enjoy your culinary journey into the heart of Japanese cooking!
