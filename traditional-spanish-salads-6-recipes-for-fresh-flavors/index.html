Traditional Spanish Salads: 6 Recipes for Fresh Flavors


Spain is a culinary haven where vibrant, fresh ingredients merge seamlessly with centuries-old traditions. Among the countless dishes that represent the heart and soul of Spanish cuisine, traditional Spanish salads stand out for their simplicity, color, and robust flavor profiles. These salads are not just side dishes; they’re refreshing mainstays that capture the essence of Spain’s varied regions, from the sun-soaked coasts to the lush inland countryside.

In this post, we’re diving into six classic Spanish salads that perfectly showcase fresh flavors and heritage cooking. Each recipe highlights local ingredients, artisanal techniques, and the perfect balance of textures and aromas. Whether you’re craving a light starter or a wholesome meal, these salads offer something authentically Spanish and irresistibly delicious.

1. Ensalada Mixta – The Quintessential Spanish Salad

Perhaps the most ubiquitous Spanish salad, Ensalada Mixta is as straightforward as it gets yet yields a fragrant, colorful plate. Its beauty lies in the simplicity and freshness of its ingredients — ripe tomatoes, crisp lettuce, onions, olives, and hard-boiled eggs — all drizzled with excellent olive oil and vinegar.

Ingredients:

  • Mixed lettuce leaves (romaine, iceberg, or butter lettuce)
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, sliced or chopped
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced (optional)
  • Black olives, preferably Manzanilla or Arbequina
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, quartered
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Sherry vinegar or red wine vinegar
  • Salt to taste

Preparation:

  1. Rinse and dry the lettuce, tearing it into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Arrange lettuce in a large salad bowl.
  3. Add tomato slices, cucumber, pepper, and onion on top.
  4. Nestle the olives and hard-boiled egg quarters artistically.
  5. Season with salt, then drizzle generously with olive oil and a splash of vinegar.
  6. Toss gently just before serving to keep the textures vibrant.

2. Escalivada – Roasted Vegetables with Smoky Depth

A classic from Catalonia, Escalivada delivers a smoky, earthy flavor from charred eggplants, red peppers, and onions. Served cold or at room temperature, it works beautifully as a starter or side, often accompanied by crusty bread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 3 large red bell peppers
  • 2 medium onions, peeled
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: Anchovy fillets or roasted pine nuts

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prick the eggplants and red peppers with a fork and place on a baking tray alongside whole onions.
  3. Roast for approximately 45 minutes until the vegetables are soft and skin is charred.
  4. Remove from oven, cover with foil or a bowl to let them sweat for 15 minutes — this helps loosen skins.
  5. Peel the eggplants and peppers, removing seeds as well.
  6. Cut the peeled eggplant and peppers into strips; slice onions thinly.
  7. Arrange the vegetables on a platter, drizzle with olive oil and season with salt.
  8. Optionally add anchovy fillets for a salty punch or sprinkle roasted pine nuts for texture.

3. Salmorejo – Cordoban Creamy Tomato Salad

Hailing from Andalusia’s Córdoba, Salmorejo includes the essence of tomatoes but transforms it into a cold, creamy salad that borders on a soup. Unlike gazpacho, Salmorejo is richer with a velvety texture thanks to abundant olive oil and bread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 200 g stale white bread (crust removed)
  • 150 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt to taste
  • Toppings: diced Spanish cured ham (Jamón Serrano) and chopped hard-boiled eggs

Preparation:

  1. Immerse bread in a little water just enough to soak it lightly.
  2. Peel tomatoes by blanching in boiling water, then purée in a blender with soaked bread.
  3. Add garlic, salt, and olive oil gradually while blending to create a smooth emulsion.
  4. Chill the mixture for at least 2 hours to develop the flavors.
  5. Serve cold in bowls, garnished generously with diced ham and egg.

4. Ensalada de Bacalao – Salt Cod Salad from the Coast

Salt cod, or Bacalao, is a staple ingredient in Spanish coastal dishes. This vibrant salad combines the flaky fish with roasted peppers, onions, and olives for a refreshing, protein-packed dish.

Ingredients:

  • 300 g salt cod, soaked overnight and drained
  • 3 red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 50 g black olives
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Vinegar or lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

  1. Boil soaked salt cod until tender (around 10 minutes), then drain and flake into flakes.
  2. Slice roasted peppers and onion.
  3. In a large salad bowl, combine cod flakes, peppers, onions, and olives.
  4. Sprinkle chopped parsley, season with pepper and just a pinch of salt.
  5. Dress everything with olive oil and vinegar or a squeeze of lemon juice.
  6. Mix gently and chill before serving.

5. Ensalada de Naranja y Bacalao – Orange and Cod Salad from Andalusia

This salad is a brilliant fusion of sweet and salty flavors, marrying juicy oranges with salt cod, red onions, and olives. It’s a popular summertime treat that highlights Andalusia’s abundant citrus groves.

Ingredients:

  • 3 oranges, peeled and sliced
  • 200 g salt cod, soaked and flaked
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • Black olives, sliced
  • Fresh mint or parsley leaves
  • Olive oil
  • Red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

  1. Soak salt cod overnight to remove excess salt, then cook and flake.
  2. Peel oranges carefully to remove all white pith and slice thin.
  3. On a platter, layer orange slices and cod flakes.
  4. Scatter thin onion rings and olives on top.
  5. Toss fresh herbs and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Drizzle olive oil and a splash of red wine vinegar over everything.
  7. Chill in fridge for an hour before serving.

6. Ensalada de Pimientos Asados con Atún – Roasted Pepper and Tuna Salad

This classic salad from regions like Murcia and Valencia pairs smoky roasted peppers with tender tuna, red onions, and white beans, making it hearty and nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 3 red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 1 can of high-quality tuna in olive oil, drained
  • 1 small red onion, sliced
  • 100 g cooked white beans (cannellini or butter beans)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Olive oil
  • Vinegar or lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

  1. Cut roasted peppers into strips.
  2. In a bowl, combine peppers, tuna, sliced onion, and white beans.
  3. Stir gently, season with salt and pepper.
  4. Add chopped parsley, olive oil, and vinegar or a squeeze of lemon juice.
  5. Chill for 30 minutes for flavors to marry before serving.

Final Thoughts on Spanish Salads

Traditional Spanish salads enchant with their bright, fresh ingredients, often relying on superb-quality olive oil and local produce. They invite you to savor the authentic, simple pleasures of Mediterranean living — a celebration of sunshine, sea breezes, and fertile lands.

Whether you opt for the rustic charm of Ensalada Mixta, the smoky depth of Escalivada, or the refreshing zing of orange and cod, these salads bring a taste of Spain into your kitchen. Don’t hesitate to experiment with herbs, different varieties of olives, or regional cheeses like Manchego to add personal flair.

These six recipes can easily fit into any meal plan, offering quick, nutritious options that don’t compromise on flavor or tradition. Serve with crusty bread and a glass of crisp Spanish white wine for a true taste of the Iberian Peninsula’s culinary heritage.

Tips for Preparing Spanish Salads at Home

  • Invest in high-quality extra virgin olive oil: It is the backbone of flavor in many recipes.
  • Use fresh, seasonal produce: Tomatoes are best when at their peak ripeness.
  • Balance acidity: Vinegar or lemon juice should complement, not overpower, the salad.
  • Let ingredients rest: Some salads taste better when chilled and allowed to blend flavors.
  • Experiment with regional variants: Spain’s diversity means each region has its own twist.

By exploring these salads, you’ll not only enjoy delicious food but also connect to Spain’s rich cultural and culinary legacy. Ready to bring a bit of Spain’s freshness into your dining room? ¡Buen provecho!
