Homemade Coffee Toffee Ice Cream: A Caffeinated Delight


There’s something irresistible about the combination of creamy ice cream and the rich, bold flavor of coffee. Add in the sweet crunch of toffee, and you’ve got a winning dessert that’s sure to please any coffee lover. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make homemade coffee toffee ice cream that’s so delicious, you’ll never want to go back to store-bought.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup instant coffee granules
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup toffee bits

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and granulated sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is hot but not boiling.

  2. Once the mixture is hot, remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the instant coffee granules until they are completely dissolved. This will infuse the cream with a rich coffee flavor.

  3. Add the vanilla extract to the mixture and stir to combine. The vanilla will enhance the depth of flavor in the ice cream.

  4. Transfer the mixture to a heatproof bowl and cover it with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap directly onto the surface of the mixture to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate the mixture until it is completely chilled, at least 4 hours or overnight.

  5. Once the coffee ice cream base is chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. As the ice cream begins to thicken, add the toffee bits and continue churning until the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  6. Transfer the churned ice cream to a freezer-safe container, smoothing the top with a spatula. Sprinkle additional toffee bits on top for an extra crunch.

  7. Cover the container with a lid and freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until it is firm.

  8. When you’re ready to serve the ice cream, let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften slightly before scooping.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra indulgent treat, drizzle the ice cream with a shot of espresso or a splash of coffee liqueur before serving.
  • Experiment with different types of toffee, such as chocolate-covered toffee, for a unique twist on this recipe.
  • If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can still make this recipe! Simply pour the chilled coffee ice cream base into a freezer-safe container and freeze it, stirring every hour until it reaches the desired consistency.

Conclusion

Homemade coffee toffee ice cream is a delightful way to enjoy the bold flavor of coffee in a cool, creamy treat. With just a few simple ingredients and a bit of patience, you can create a dessert that’s sure to impress. Whether you’re serving it at a summer gathering or simply treating yourself to a special indulgence, this caffeinated delight is bound to become a favorite in your recipe collection. So, grab your ingredients and get ready to savor every spoonful of this homemade delight.
