Flavorful Nut-Free Thai Curry Recipe


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine but have a nut allergy, you might think that enjoying a delicious Thai curry is off the menu. However, fear not! This nut-free Thai curry recipe is bursting with authentic flavors and is completely safe for those with nut allergies. It’s a perfect way to bring the exotic tastes of Thailand into your kitchen without worrying about any nut-based ingredients.

Ingredients

For the Curry Paste:

  • 2 shallots, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1 stalk of lemongrass, tough outer layer removed and roughly chopped
  • 3 red chilies, deseeded and roughly chopped
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • Small bunch of fresh coriander (cilantro), stalks and leaves
  • 2 tablespoons tomato puree
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

For the Curry:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 2 chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces (substitute with tofu for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 red pepper, deseeded and sliced
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian option)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh coriander (cilantro) leaves, to garnish
  • Cooked rice, to serve

Instructions

Step 1: Make the Curry Paste

  1. Place all the curry paste ingredients in a food processor and blend until smooth.
  2. If the paste is too thick, add a little water to loosen it.

Step 2: Prepare the Curry

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onion and cook until softened.
  3. Add the chicken (or tofu) and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the prepared curry paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add the sliced red pepper and cook for a further 2 minutes.
  6. Pour in the coconut milk and stock, then stir in the brown sugar and fish sauce (or soy sauce).
  7. Bring the curry to a gentle simmer and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened slightly.
  8. Squeeze in the lime juice and give it a final stir.

Step 3: Serve

  1. Spoon the curry over cooked rice and garnish with fresh coriander (cilantro) leaves.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegan or vegetarian version, simply replace the chicken with tofu or your favorite vegetables such as bell peppers, zucchini, and bamboo shoots.
  • If you prefer a spicier curry, add an extra chili or leave the seeds in.
  • You can make a large batch of the curry paste and freeze it in portions for future use.

Final Thoughts

This nut-free Thai curry is a wonderful way to experience the bold and vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine without the worry of nut allergies. The combination of aromatic herbs and spices, creamy coconut milk, and the perfect balance of sweet, salty, and sour flavors makes this dish a true delight for the taste buds. Whether you’re cooking for family or friends, this curry is sure to impress and satisfy. So, why not take a culinary journey to Thailand right in your own kitchen and savor the magic of this nut-free Thai curry? Enjoy!
