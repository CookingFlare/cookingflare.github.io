Deliciously Sweet Cherry Pie Recipe


If you’re looking for a classic dessert that’s bursting with sweet and tangy flavors, then this Deliciously Sweet Cherry Pie recipe is just what you need. Made with fresh cherries and a buttery, flaky crust, this pie is sure to impress your friends and family. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s get baking!

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh cherries, pitted and halved
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 2 pie crusts, store-bought or homemade
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon turbinado sugar (optional, for sprinkling)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place one pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the cherries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, almond extract, and lemon juice. Mix well until the cherries are coated in the sugar mixture.

  3. Pour the cherry filling into the prepared pie crust and dot the top with the small pieces of butter.

  4. Roll out the second pie crust and place it on top of the cherry filling. Trim any excess crust and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  5. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking. Alternatively, you can create a lattice pattern by cutting strips of dough and weaving them over the filling.

  6. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg wash, and if desired, sprinkle with turbinado sugar for a sweet, crunchy topping.

  7. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips, and bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  8. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This will ensure that the filling sets properly.

  9. Serve the Deliciously Sweet Cherry Pie on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for the perfect summertime treat.

Tips:

  • For the best flavor, use fresh cherries when they are in season. However, you can also use frozen cherries if fresh ones are not available.
  • If you prefer a thicker filling, increase the amount of cornstarch to 1/3 cup.
  • To prevent the bottom crust from becoming soggy, you can blind bake it before adding the filling. Simply line the pie crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake it in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 10-12 minutes, then remove the weights and bake for an additional 5 minutes before adding the cherry filling.
  • Leftover pie can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply cover it with plastic wrap or foil to keep it fresh.

So there you have it – a Deliciously Sweet Cherry Pie that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply want to treat yourself to something special, this recipe is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. So go ahead, grab those cherries, and get ready to bake up a storm!
