Italian Elegance: Osso Buco - Braised Veal Shanks Recipe


When it comes to Italian cuisine, few dishes embody the essence of elegance and richness quite like Osso Buco. This classic Milanese dish, which translates to “bone with a hole,” features tender, braised veal shanks cooked to perfection in a flavorful broth. The result is a dish that is both comforting and sophisticated, making it a perfect choice for a special occasion or a memorable family dinner.

The Ingredients

To create this timeless Italian dish, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 veal shanks, each about 2 inches thick
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • All-purpose flour, for dredging
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken or beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Gremolata (a garnish made with lemon zest, garlic, and parsley)

The Preparation

  1. Begin by tying kitchen twine around the circumference of each veal shank. This will help the meat hold its shape during the cooking process.

  2. Season the veal shanks generously with salt and pepper, then dredge them in flour, shaking off any excess.

  3. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, melt 2 tablespoons of butter with 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the veal shanks to the pot and brown them on all sides, working in batches if necessary. This step is crucial for developing a rich flavor in the dish.

  4. Once the veal shanks are browned, transfer them to a plate and set them aside. In the same pot, add the remaining butter and olive oil. Sauté the onion, carrot, celery, and garlic until they are soft and fragrant.

  5. Deglaze the pot with the white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Allow the wine to reduce by half, then add the crushed tomatoes, chicken or beef broth, bay leaf, and thyme. Return the veal shanks to the pot, ensuring they are partially submerged in the liquid.

  6. Cover the pot with a lid and reduce the heat to low. Allow the veal shanks to simmer gently for about 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone.

  7. While the veal shanks are cooking, prepare the gremolata by combining finely chopped parsley, minced garlic, and grated lemon zest.

  8. Once the veal shanks are ready, remove them from the pot and place them on a serving platter. Discard the bay leaf and skim off any excess fat from the cooking liquid. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.

  9. To serve, spoon the braising liquid over the veal shanks and top them with a generous sprinkle of gremolata. This bright and zesty garnish adds a burst of flavor and freshness to the rich, savory dish.

The Presentation

Osso Buco is traditionally served with a side of creamy risotto, polenta, or mashed potatoes. The creamy texture of these accompaniments pairs beautifully with the tender, succulent veal shanks and the robust braising liquid. To complement the richness of the dish, a simple green salad dressed with a light vinaigrette can provide a refreshing contrast.

For a complete Italian dining experience, consider pairing Osso Buco with a full-bodied red wine such as Barolo or Chianti. The bold flavors of the wine will harmonize with the dish, enhancing the overall dining experience.

In Conclusion

With its rich flavors and tender, melt-in-your-mouth texture, Osso Buco is a true masterpiece of Italian cuisine. The careful preparation and slow cooking process yield a dish that is as indulgent as it is comforting, making it a standout choice for a special meal. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of Italian elegance, Osso Buco is sure to impress and delight all who have the pleasure of savoring it.

Buon appetito!
