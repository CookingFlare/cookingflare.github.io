Easy Homemade Churro Ice Cream Recipe


Churros and ice cream are beyond delicious on their own, but have you ever considered combining these two mouthwatering treats into a single, indulgent dessert? The warm, crispy churro flavors mixed with cold, creamy ice cream create an unforgettable experience that’s surprisingly simple to make at home. This easy homemade churro ice cream recipe will guide you through crafting a luscious dessert that’s perfect for impressing family and friends or just treating yourself after a long day.

Why Churro Ice Cream?

Churro ice cream merges the best of both worlds—the crunchy, cinnamon-sugar-coated dough of churros with silky, sweet ice cream. This combination offers a contrast of temperatures and textures that heightens your palate’s enjoyment, making every bite a fun surprise. Whether you savor it on a summer afternoon or as a cozy winter indulgence, the churro ice cream adds a twist to classic vanilla or cinnamon ice creams you can’t resist.

Ingredients Overview

There are two key components to focus on: the churro bits and the ice cream base. The beauty of this recipe is that both can be made from scratch with common ingredients found in any pantry and freezer.

For the Churro Bits:

  • All-purpose flour
  • Water
  • Unsalted butter
  • Granulated sugar
  • Ground cinnamon
  • Salt
  • Egg

For the Ice Cream Base:

  • Heavy cream
  • Whole milk
  • Granulated sugar
  • Egg yolks
  • Vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon

These simple ingredients combine to produce a dessert bursting with rich cinnamon and vanilla flavors complementing the fried churro crunch.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making the Churro Ice Cream

1. Preparing the Churro Nuggets

Instead of making traditional long churros, this recipe opts for bite-sized crispy churro bits, ideal for mixing into the ice cream without compromising its scoopable texture.

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, bring 1 cup of water, 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar, and a pinch of salt to a boil.
  2. Once boiling, reduce heat to low, remove the pan from heat, and immediately stir in 1 cup of all-purpose flour vigorously until the mixture forms a smooth dough that pulls away from the sides.
  3. Transfer the dough to a mixing bowl and let it cool for a few minutes.
  4. Beat in one large egg until fully combined and the dough becomes glossy.
  5. Using a piping bag fitted with a star tip or a zip-top bag with a corner cut off, pipe small 1-inch dollops of the dough onto parchment-lined baking sheets.
  6. Heat oil in a deep fryer or a heavy-bottomed pot to 350°F (175°C).
  7. Carefully drop a few dough bits into the hot oil. Fry in batches to avoid overcrowding.
  8. Fry the churro bits until golden and crisp, about 2-3 minutes.
  9. Drain on paper towels, then toss immediately in a mixture of ½ cup granulated sugar and 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon to coat evenly.

Set these aside while you prepare the ice cream base.

2. Making the Cinnamon Vanilla Ice Cream Base

This simple custard-style ice cream base carries the warm, aromatic notes of cinnamon that make churro ice cream unforgettable.

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine 2 cups of heavy cream, 1 cup of whole milk, and 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon. Warm over medium heat until tiny bubbles appear around the edges — do not boil.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together ¾ cup granulated sugar and 5 large egg yolks until the mixture is pale and thick.
  3. Gradually pour the warmed milk mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent curdling.
  4. Return the combined mixture to the saucepan. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon or spatula, until the custard thickens enough to coat the back of the spoon (about 170-175°F or 77-80°C).
  5. Remove from heat, stir in 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and strain the custard through a fine sieve to remove any cooked egg bits.
  6. Chill the custard overnight in the refrigerator to develop flavor and allow it to thicken.

3. Churning the Ice Cream and Combining Components

Once your chilled ice cream base and warm churro bits are ready, it’s time to bring them together.

Instructions:

  1. Pour the ice cream custard into an ice cream maker and churn following manufacturer instructions, usually about 20-25 minutes until it achieves a soft-serve consistency.
  2. Just before the ice cream finishes churning, fold in the cinnamon-sugar churro bits so they’re evenly distributed but not completely broken down.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a freezer-safe container, cover with parchment paper or plastic wrap directly on the surface to prevent ice crystals, and freeze for at least 4 hours until firm.

Serving Suggestions

Scoop your homemade churro ice cream into bowls or cones and garnish with a light dusting of cinnamon sugar for extra flair. For an extravagant dessert, drizzle caramel or chocolate sauce on top, or add a dollop of whipped cream.

For parties, serve alongside fresh berries or a warm chocolate dipping sauce to create a mini fiesta of flavors. This dessert pairs wonderfully with a robust coffee or rich hot chocolate.

Pro Tips for Perfect Churro Ice Cream

  • Churro Size: Smaller churro bits ensure the ice cream retains a creamy texture without becoming chewy or gritty.
  • Oil Temperature: Use a thermometer to maintain consistent oil heat; too hot and churros burn, too cool and they absorb excess oil.
  • Custard Texture: Patience is key when cooking the custard—stir and monitor temperature to avoid curdling and achieve a silky smooth base.
  • Chilling Time: Overnight chilling of the custard base improves flavor depth and texture, making the ice cream richer.
  • Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container to maintain freshness and prevent freezer burn.

Variations to Try Next

Feeling adventurous? Customize this recipe with these exciting twists:

  • Chocolate Churro Ice Cream: Substitute part of the cream with melted dark chocolate before churning.
  • Caramel Swirl: Drizzle caramel sauce during the final freeze for gooey ribbons throughout.
  • Spiced Addition: Add a pinch of nutmeg or cayenne pepper to the ice cream base for extra warmth and complexity.
  • Alcohol Infusion: Mix in a splash of cinnamon whiskey or rum for an adult-friendly version.

Final Thoughts

This easy homemade churro ice cream brings the familiar joy of a classic Mexican street dessert to your freezer, with the luxurious creaminess of premium ice cream. The harmonious blend of cinnamon sugar-coated crunchy churro bits with smooth vanilla-cinnamon ice cream will make this recipe a staple in your kitchen. Perfect for summer celebrations, festive gatherings, or anytime indulgence, it’s a treat you won’t want to miss.

Try it out, experiment with flavors, and most importantly, savor every heavenly bite. Happy cooking and eating!
