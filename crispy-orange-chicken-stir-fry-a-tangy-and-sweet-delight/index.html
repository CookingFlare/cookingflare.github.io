Crispy Orange Chicken Stir Fry: A Tangy and Sweet Delight


Are you ready to take your taste buds on an exciting culinary adventure? Look no further than this irresistible Crispy Orange Chicken Stir Fry recipe! With a perfect balance of tangy and sweet flavors, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is simple to follow and guarantees a mouthwatering result. Let’s dive into the delightful world of Crispy Orange Chicken Stir Fry!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Green onions, sliced, for garnish
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, coat the chicken pieces with 1/2 cup of cornstarch until evenly coated.

  2. Dip the cornstarch-coated chicken into the beaten eggs, ensuring each piece is well covered.

  3. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the coated chicken pieces and cook until golden and crispy. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  4. In the same skillet, combine the orange juice, soy sauce, honey, garlic, ginger, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

  5. In a small bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water to create a slurry. Add the slurry to the orange sauce in the skillet, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens.

  6. Return the crispy chicken to the skillet, tossing it in the thickened orange sauce until evenly coated.

  7. Serve the Crispy Orange Chicken Stir Fry over a bed of cooked white rice, and garnish with sliced green onions.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the orange sauce.
  • To make this dish gluten-free, use tamari instead of soy sauce and ensure all other ingredients are gluten-free.
  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables such as bell peppers, snap peas, or broccoli.
