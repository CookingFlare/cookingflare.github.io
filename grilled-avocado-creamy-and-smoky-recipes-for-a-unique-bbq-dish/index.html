Grilled Avocado: Creamy and Smoky Recipes for a Unique BBQ Dish


When it comes to BBQ dishes, most people think of meaty options like ribs, burgers, or chicken. However, if you’re looking to add a unique twist to your next BBQ gathering, why not try grilling avocados? Grilled avocados offer a creamy and smoky flavor that pairs perfectly with a variety of fillings and toppings. In this blog post, we’ll explore different recipes that showcase the deliciousness of grilled avocados. So fire up your grill and get ready to tantalize your taste buds!

Grilled Avocado with Tomato Salsa

This recipe is a simple yet flavorful way to enjoy grilled avocados. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno, seeded and minced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the avocados in half and remove the pits. Brush the cut sides with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Place the avocados cut side down on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes, until grill marks appear.
  4. While the avocados are grilling, prepare the tomato salsa by combining the diced tomato, red onion, jalapeno, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Once the avocados are grilled, remove them from the grill and fill the center with the tomato salsa.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy the creamy and smoky goodness of grilled avocados with a burst of fresh flavors from the tomato salsa.

Grilled Avocado Stuffed with Quinoa Salad

If you’re looking for a heartier option, try stuffing your grilled avocados with a delicious quinoa salad. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup cucumber, diced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley or cilantro)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the avocados in half and remove the pits. Brush the cut sides with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Place the avocados cut side down on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes, until grill marks appear.
  4. While the avocados are grilling, prepare the quinoa salad by combining the cooked quinoa, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, fresh herbs, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Once the avocados are grilled, remove them from the grill and fill the center with the quinoa salad.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy the creamy and smoky avocados paired with the refreshing flavors of the quinoa salad.

Grilled Avocado with Bacon and Cheese

For those who enjoy a more indulgent BBQ dish, this recipe combines the richness of grilled avocados with the irresistible combination of bacon and cheese. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 4 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (such as cheddar or Monterey Jack)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the avocados in half and remove the pits. Brush the cut sides with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Place the avocados cut side down on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes, until grill marks appear.
  4. While the avocados are grilling, prepare the bacon and cheese topping by combining the crumbled bacon, shredded cheese, chopped green onions, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Once the avocados are grilled, remove them from the grill and fill the center with the bacon and cheese topping.
  6. Place the filled avocados back on the grill, cut side up, and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy the creamy and smoky avocados with the crispy bacon and gooey cheese.

Grilled avocados are a unique and delicious addition to any BBQ menu. Whether you prefer a fresh and zesty salsa, a hearty quinoa salad, or a decadent combination of bacon and cheese, these recipes will surely impress your guests. So next time you fire up the grill, don’t forget to include some avocados for a creamy and smoky twist. Happy grilling!
