Irresistible Cranberry Walnut Bread: A Festive Delight


Who can resist the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through the kitchen? And when that bread is infused with the flavors of tart cranberries and crunchy walnuts, it becomes an irresistible treat that is perfect for any festive occasion. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making a delectable Cranberry Walnut Bread that will leave your taste buds dancing with joy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries, chopped
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, chopped
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x5-inch loaf pan and set it aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well until all the dry ingredients are thoroughly combined.

  3. Add the chopped cranberries and walnuts to the dry mixture. Toss them gently to ensure they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, buttermilk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense bread.

  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and spread it evenly. Tap the pan gently on the countertop to remove any air bubbles.

  6. Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the bread comes out clean.

  7. Once the bread is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

  8. Slice the Cranberry Walnut Bread and serve it as is or with a slather of butter for an extra indulgent treat. This bread is perfect for breakfast, afternoon tea, or as a delightful dessert.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more intense cranberry flavor, you can replace the fresh cranberries with dried cranberries. Soak the dried cranberries in warm water for a few minutes before adding them to the batter.

  • If you prefer a sweeter bread, you can increase the amount of sugar to 1 1/4 cups.

  • Feel free to add a sprinkle of cinnamon or a pinch of nutmeg to the batter for a warm and cozy twist.

  • This recipe can also be used to make Cranberry Walnut Muffins. Simply divide the batter evenly among muffin cups and adjust the baking time to around 20-25 minutes.

  • Leftover bread can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Final Thoughts

Cranberry Walnut Bread is a delightful combination of tart cranberries, crunchy walnuts, and moist bread. The festive flavors make it a perfect treat for the holiday season, but it can be enjoyed all year round. Whether you serve it for breakfast, as an afternoon snack, or as a dessert, this bread is sure to impress your family and friends. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to indulge in the irresistible goodness of Cranberry Walnut Bread!
