Seafood Stuffed Shells Recipe with Shrimp and Ricotta Cheese

When it comes to seafood and pasta, there are countless combinations that can result in a culinary masterpiece. One such recipe that stands out is the Seafood Stuffed Shells Recipe with Shrimp and Ricotta Cheese. This dish is a perfect blend of flavors, textures, and presentation, making it a great option for special occasions or even a weekend dinner. Ingredients 12 jumbo pasta shells 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined 1/2 cup ricotta cheese 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 teaspoon paprika 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/2 cup breadcrumbs 2 tablespoons olive oil 1/2 cup marinara sauce 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil 1/4 cup grated mozzarella cheese 1 egg, beaten Instructions Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the pasta shells according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and cook until pink and just cooked through, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the breadcrumbs and cook until lightly toasted, stirring frequently. In a large bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, parsley, salt, black pepper, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Stir until smooth. Add the cooked shrimp, breadcrumbs, and marinara sauce to the bowl with the ricotta cheese mixture. Stir until well combined. Stuff each pasta shell with the seafood and ricotta cheese mixture, placing them in a baking dish as you go. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and top each shell with the mozzarella cheese. Return to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove the dish from the oven and sprinkle with basil. Serve hot and enjoy! Tips and Variations To add an extra layer of flavor, sprinkle some dried oregano or thyme on top of the shells before baking. If you prefer a spicier dish, add some red pepper flakes to the ricotta cheese mixture. For a vegetarian option, substitute the shrimp with cooked and chopped mushrooms or zucchini. To make ahead, prepare the seafood and ricotta cheese mixture up to a day in advance and store it in the refrigerator. Assemble the shells just before baking. Conclusion The Seafood Stuffed Shells Recipe with Shrimp and Ricotta Cheese is a culinary masterpiece that is sure to impress your family and friends. With its perfect blend of flavors and textures, this dish is a great option for special occasions or even a weekend dinner. By following these simple steps and tips, you can create a dish that is both delicious and visually appealing. So go ahead, get creative, and enjoy the fruits of your labor! ...
