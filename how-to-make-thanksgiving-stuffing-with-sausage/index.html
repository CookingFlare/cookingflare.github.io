How to Make Thanksgiving Stuffing with Sausage


Thanksgiving is a time to celebrate family, gratitude, and delicious food. And one dish that often steals the spotlight at the dinner table is stuffing. There’s something magical about the comforting blend of bread, herbs, and savory flavors that pulls everything on your plate together. But if you’re looking to elevate your stuffing game this year, adding sausage to the mix can bring a whole new depth of flavor to this festive classic. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a holiday host looking for a new recipe, this guide will take you step by step through the process of making Thanksgiving stuffing with sausage that’s rich, flavorful, and irresistibly satisfying.

Why Sausage Stuffing?

Sausage stuffing adds a robust flavor to the dish, complementing the traditional bouquet of herbs and aromatics associated with Thanksgiving. The richness of the sausage combines harmoniously with bread cubes, vegetables, and seasonings, creating a stuffing that’s hearty and indulgent. Plus, this variation can be made inside the turkey or baked separately as a stand-alone casserole dish, giving you options based on your cooking preferences and holiday traditions.

Let’s dive into the ingredients and steps so you can create a memorable Thanksgiving stuffing with sausage that your guests will rave about for years to come.

Ingredients Needed

Before you begin, gather all the ingredients for a smooth cooking experience. This sausage stuffing recipe serves approximately 8–10 people—a perfect size for sharing during the holiday feast.

Bread Base

The foundation of any stuffing is the bread, and for this recipe, you’ll need about 10–12 cups of cubed bread. You can use anything from sourdough, Italian bread, or classic white bread. For best results, use slightly stale bread, as it absorbs flavors better without getting overly soggy. If your bread is fresh, you can dry it out by toasting the cubes lightly in the oven.

Main Flavor Components

  • 1 pound of sausage (Italian sausage or your preferred variety)
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 3 stalks of celery, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced

Herbs & Seasonings

These are the key elements that bring classic Thanksgiving flavors to your stuffing.

  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon parsley, chopped (plus extra for garnish)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg (optional, but adds warmth and depth)

Liquid Ingredients

Liquid keeps your stuffing moist and flavorful:

  • 2 ½ cups chicken broth (or turkey broth for a Thanksgiving twist)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs, beaten (for binding)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Bread

Begin by drying out your bread. If it’s already stale, you’re good to go; if not, preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C). Spread the bread cubes evenly on a baking sheet and toast them for about 20 minutes, stirring halfway through. You’re aiming for dry, crisp bread cubes—not browned or overly toasted.

Once the bread cubes are ready, set them aside in a large mixing bowl.

Step 2: Cook the Sausage

Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sausage and cook, breaking it up into small crumbles using a wooden spoon or spatula. Continue cooking until the sausage is browned and cooked through, about 8–10 minutes. Once done, transfer the sausage to a plate, leaving behind the rendered fat in the skillet.

Step 3: Sauté Vegetables & Aromatics

In the same skillet, add the chopped onions, garlic, and celery to the sausage fat. If there isn’t enough fat left behind, add a tablespoon of butter to the skillet. Sauté the vegetables over medium heat until softened and fragrant, approximately 6–8 minutes. Stir in the fresh sage, thyme, rosemary, and parsley about halfway through to let the herbs bloom and release their aroma.

Once the vegetables are ready, remove them from heat and set them aside.

Step 4: Combine Ingredients

In the large mixing bowl with the bread cubes, add the sautéed vegetables, cooked sausage, and seasonings (salt, pepper, and nutmeg). Stir everything together gently to distribute the flavors evenly.

Step 5: Add Liquids

Next, drizzle the melted butter over the stuffing mixture. Whisk your eggs into the chicken broth and pour this liquid over the bread mixture in small increments, gently stirring as you go. You want the bread cubes to be moistened but not drenched. Add just enough liquid to make the stuffing slightly moist—use your judgment based on the texture.

Step 6: Bake the Stuffing

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased casserole dish (or directly into the turkey’s cavity if you prefer a traditional stuffing). Cover the dish with aluminum foil.

  • For stuffing baked in a casserole dish: Bake for 25 minutes covered, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes to let the top crisp up.
  • For stuffing cooked inside a turkey: Ensure the stuffing reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) when cooked, which may take longer depending on the size of your turkey.

Tips for Success

  • Make It Ahead: Thanksgiving can get hectic, so prep your stuffing the day before. Combine the dry and wet ingredients, then store the mixture in the fridge. Bake it right before serving.
  • Gluten-Free Option: Use gluten-free bread to create a stuffing that’s suitable for guests with dietary restrictions.
  • Mix and Match Sausage: Experiment with different sausages to create unique flavor profiles. Spicy Italian sausage adds a kick; breakfast sausage brings subtle sweetness.
  • Get Creative with Add-Ins: Apples, cranberries, mushrooms, or pecans can add texture and seasonal flair to your stuffing.
  • Crispy vs. Moist: For crispier stuffing, bake in a shallow dish. For a softer texture, opt for a deeper casserole dish or cook inside the turkey.

Final Thoughts

Thanksgiving stuffing with sausage is a celebration of hearty flavors and comforting textures. The savory richness of sausage pairs beautifully with classic herbs and vegetables, creating a dish that’s both familiar and elevated. Whether you serve it as a stand-alone side or as the perfect companion to roast turkey, this stuffing is guaranteed to impress your holiday guests.

The beauty of this recipe is its versatility. You can tweak it to suit your family’s favorite flavors, incorporate seasonal additions, or adapt it to dietary needs. Once it’s ready and gracing your Thanksgiving table, expect smiles, compliments, and requests for seconds. Prepare to make this dish a new tradition in your holiday repertoire.

Happy cooking, and may your Thanksgiving feast be filled with love, laughter, and unforgettable flavors!
