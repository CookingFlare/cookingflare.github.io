Nut-Free Energy: 30 Vibrant Nut-Free Recipes to Fuel Your Culinary Exploration

Are you looking for new and exciting ways to bring life to your meals without the inclusion of nuts? We’ve compiled a list of 30 vibrant nut-free recipes that will bring energy and excitement to your plate while simultaneously providing you with nutritious goodness. So, put on your aprons and let’s dive right into exploring these delicious recipes! 1. Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers Ingredients: 6 large bell peppers 1 cup quinoa, rinsed 2 cups vegetable broth 1 medium zucchini, diced 1 medium yellow squash, diced 1 cup corn kernels 1/2 cup black beans, rinsed and drained 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1/4 cup chopped green onions 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder Salt and pepper, to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and arrange the bell peppers, hollowed out but still attached at the stem, in a baking dish. In a medium saucepan, combine quinoa and broth, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes, or until liquid is absorbed. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add zucchini, squash, corn, and cook for 5 minutes or until veggies are tender. Add quinoa mixture, black beans, cilantro, green onions, cumin, garlic powder, and mix well. Season with salt and pepper. Stuff the quinoa mixture into the hollowed-out peppers. Bake for 25-30 minutes, until peppers are tender. Serve immediately. 2. Avocado & Black Bean Tacos Ingredients: 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained 1 ripe avocado, mashed 1 tablespoon lime juice 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder Salt and pepper, to taste 8 corn tortillas 1/2 cup shredded lettuce 1/2 cup diced tomatoes 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro Instructions: In a medium bowl, mash avocado with lime juice, garlic powder, and season with salt and pepper. Heat tortillas on stovetop or in microwave, according to package instructions. Evenly divide black beans onto each tortilla, then top with avocado mixture, lettuce, tomatoes, and cilantro. Roll up tacos, and enjoy your fresh and nut-free meal! 3. Sweet Potato & Black Bean Burger Patties Ingredients: 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and diced 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained 1/4 cup rolled oats 1/4 cup hummus 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika Salt and pepper, to taste Instructions: Cook sweet potatoes until tender in boiling water, about 12-15 minutes. Drain and mash well. In a large bowl, combine sweet potatoes, black beans, oats, hummus, cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and season with salt and pepper. Mix well to combine and form into 4 patties. Cook patties in a non-stick skillet over medium heat, for 4-5 minutes on each side, until crispy and golden. Serve between buns or on top of greens, and enjoy! 4. Zucchini Noodles with Avocado Cream Sauce Ingredients: 4 medium zucchini, spiralized 1 ripe avocado 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves 2 cloves garlic Salt and pepper, to taste Instructions: In a blender or food processor, combine avocado, almond milk, basil leaves, and garlic. Blend until smooth, adding a little more almond milk if needed. Season with salt and pepper. Toss zucchini noodles with avocado cream sauce until well coated. Serve immediately, with shaved Parmesan cheese on top, if desired. 5. Chickpea & Apricot Stuffed Sweet Potatoes Ingredients: 4 medium sweet potatoes 2 cans chickpeas, rinsed and drained 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/2 cup chopped dried apricots 1/4 cup chopped green onions 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 teaspoon ground cumin Salt and pepper, to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Poke sweet potatoes a few times with a fork and bake for 45-50 minutes, until tender and cooked through. While sweet potatoes are baking, heat olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add chickpeas, garlic, and cumin. Cook for 5-7 minutes, until chickpeas are crispy and garlic is fragrant. In a separate bowl, combine cooked chickpeas, parsley, apricots, green onions, and season with salt and pepper. Once sweet potatoes are done baking, cut them in half and scoop out most of the flesh, leaving a small layer to hold the shape. Fill sweet potato shells with chickpea mixture and serve immediately. 6. Quinoa & Roasted Vegetable Salad Ingredients: 1 cup quinoa, rinsed 2 cups vegetable broth 1 large zucchini, diced 1 large red bell pepper, diced 1 large yellow bell pepper, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 3 tablespoons olive oil 1 teaspoon smoked paprika Salt and pepper, to taste 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley Juice of 1 lemon Instructions: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a medium saucepan, combine quinoa and broth, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes, or until liquid is absorbed. In a separate bowl, toss zucchini, red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, and garlic with olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread vegetables in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, until veggies are tender and slightly charred. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, roasted vegetables, parsley, and lemon juice. Toss well to combine and serve immediately. Creamy Nut-Free Avocado Pasta Recipe

Add the fresh basil leaves, minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to the food processor or blender. Step 2: Blend Until Smooth Blend the ingredients until the mixture is smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed to ensure everything is well combined. Step 3: Cook the Pasta Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Once the pasta is cooked, reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta water and then drain the pasta. Step 4: Combine the Pasta and Sauce In a large mixing bowl, toss the cooked pasta with the creamy avocado sauce, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water as needed to thin out the sauce and help it coat the pasta evenly. Step 5: Serve and Enjoy Divide the creamy nut-free avocado pasta among serving plates. Garnish with additional basil leaves, a drizzle of olive oil, and freshly cracked black pepper, if desired. Serve immediately and enjoy this delicious and nutritious meal! Why Choose This Recipe?
