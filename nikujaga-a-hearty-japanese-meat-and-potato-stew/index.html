Nikujaga: A Hearty Japanese Meat and Potato Stew


If you’re looking for a comforting and hearty dish that’s packed with flavor, look no further than Nikujaga. This traditional Japanese stew combines tender chunks of meat, sweet and savory flavors, and melt-in-your-mouth potatoes to create a dish that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine or simply looking to expand your culinary horizons, Nikujaga is a must-try recipe that is sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

To prepare Nikujaga, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound of thinly sliced beef (such as thinly sliced ribeye or chuck)
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup of dashi broth (or substitute with beef or vegetable broth)
  • 3 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of frozen green peas (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Start by slicing the beef into thin strips and preparing the vegetables as per the ingredient list.

  2. Cook the Beef: In a large pot or deep skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the sliced beef and cook until it’s browned on all sides. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Add the Vegetables: Once the beef is browned, add the onions and carrots to the pot. Sauté them for a few minutes until they begin to soften.

  4. Add the Potatoes and Broth: Now, add the potatoes to the pot, followed by the dashi broth, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Stir the ingredients gently to combine.

  5. Simmer: Bring the stew to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the stew cook for about 20-25 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

  6. Adjust the Seasoning: Once the potatoes are tender, taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. If you’re using green peas, add them to the pot at this stage and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the stew is ready, ladle it into individual bowls and serve hot. You can enjoy Nikujaga on its own or with a side of steamed rice for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, consider adding other vegetables such as mushrooms or green beans.
  • If you prefer a sweeter flavor, you can adjust the amount of sugar or mirin to suit your taste.
  • Leftover Nikujaga can be refrigerated and reheated the next day, allowing the flavors to meld even further.

Conclusion

Nikujaga is a delightful representation of Japanese home cooking, offering warmth and comfort in every bite. Its simple yet flavorful combination of meat and potatoes makes it a versatile dish suitable for both casual weeknight dinners and special occasions. Whether you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine or simply looking for a new and exciting recipe to try, Nikujaga is a delightful addition to any home cook’s repertoire. So, why not give this hearty Japanese stew a try and savor the delicious flavors of Nikujaga?
