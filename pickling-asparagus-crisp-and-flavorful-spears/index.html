Pickling Asparagus: Crisp and Flavorful Spears


If you’re a fan of pickled vegetables, you’re in for a treat with this pickled asparagus recipe. Asparagus, known for its unique and delicate flavor, takes on a whole new dimension when pickled. The result is a crisp and flavorful snack or accompaniment that is sure to impress. Whether you’re a pickling pro or a beginner looking to try something new, this recipe is a must-try. Let’s dive into the details of how to create these delightful pickled asparagus spears.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of fresh asparagus spears
  • 2 cups white vinegar
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 4 cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 2 teaspoons dill seeds
  • 2 teaspoons mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 2 bay leaves

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Asparagus: Start by rinsing the asparagus spears under cold water and trimming the tough ends. If the asparagus is thick, consider peeling the lower half of the spears to ensure a more tender result.

  2. Sterilize the Jars: Wash your canning jars and lids in hot, soapy water, and sterilize them by boiling in a large pot of water for 10 minutes. Then, remove them and allow them to air dry.

  3. Prepare the Brine: In a large saucepan, combine the white vinegar, water, and salt. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring to dissolve the salt.

  4. Add Flavorings: Once the brine is boiling, add the garlic, dill seeds, mustard seeds, red pepper flakes, and bay leaves. Let the mixture simmer for a few minutes to infuse the flavors.

  5. Pack the Jars: Carefully pack the prepared asparagus spears into the sterilized jars, standing them upright. It’s okay to trim them to fit the height of the jar if necessary.

  6. Pour the Brine: Using a funnel, carefully pour the hot brine over the asparagus spears, covering them completely and leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace at the top of the jar.

  7. Seal the Jars: Wipe the jar rims with a clean, damp cloth to remove any brine residue. Place the lids on the jars and screw on the bands until they are fingertip tight.

  8. Process the Jars: Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes to ensure proper sealing and preservation.

  9. Cool and Store: Once processed, carefully remove the jars from the water bath and place them on a towel-lined countertop to cool. After the jars have cooled, check the seals, and store them in a cool, dark place for at least 2 weeks to allow the flavors to develop.

Serving Suggestions

Pickled asparagus makes a delightful addition to a charcuterie board, providing a tangy and crunchy contrast to cured meats and cheeses. It can also be chopped and added to salads for a burst of flavor, or simply enjoyed as a standalone snack. The options are endless, so get creative with how you incorporate these flavorful spears into your meals.

Conclusion

Pickling asparagus is a wonderful way to preserve this seasonal vegetable and enjoy its unique flavor year-round. With the right balance of tanginess and crunch, pickled asparagus is a versatile and delicious addition to any culinary repertoire. Give this recipe a try and elevate your pickling game with these crisp and flavorful spears. Happy pickling!
