How to Make Homemade Doughnuts: Sweet and Simple


Doughnuts are a beloved treat enjoyed by many, and making them at home can be a delightful experience. The aroma of freshly fried doughnuts wafting through your kitchen is enough to make anyone’s mouth water. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of creating delicious homemade doughnuts that are sweet, simple, and sure to impress your family and friends.

The Joy of Homemade Doughnuts

There’s something special about homemade doughnuts. They are customizable, allowing you to experiment with flavors, fillings, and toppings. Whether you prefer classic glazed doughnuts, chocolate-covered varieties, or filled doughnuts bursting with jam or cream, the possibilities are endless. Plus, making doughnuts at home can be a fun activity to share with loved ones.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make your own doughnuts, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Doughnut Dough:

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • ½ cup warm milk (about 110°F or 43°C)
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 ½ to 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Oil for frying (vegetable or canola oil works best)

For the Glaze:

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • ¼ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Optional Toppings:

  • Sprinkles
  • Chopped nuts
  • Shredded coconut
  • Chocolate chips

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This step is crucial as it ensures that your doughnuts will rise properly.

Step 2: Prepare the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. Once the yeast mixture is frothy, add it to the bowl. Mix well to combine.

Gradually add 3 ½ cups of flour and salt to the wet ingredients. Stir until a dough begins to form. If the dough is too sticky, add more flour, a little at a time, until it is manageable but still soft.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead it for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. This process develops the gluten, which gives the doughnuts their structure.

Step 4: Let the Dough Rise

Place the kneaded dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size. This is the perfect time to clean up your kitchen and prepare your frying station.

Step 5: Shape the Doughnuts

Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Roll it out on a floured surface to about ½ inch thick. Use a doughnut cutter or two round cookie cutters (one larger and one smaller) to cut out the doughnuts. Place the cut doughnuts on a floured baking sheet and cover them with a towel. Let them rise for another 30-45 minutes.

Step 6: Heat the Oil

In a deep pot or a deep fryer, heat the oil to 350°F (175°C). Use a thermometer to ensure the oil is at the right temperature. If the oil is too hot, the doughnuts will burn on the outside and remain raw on the inside. If it’s not hot enough, the doughnuts will absorb too much oil and become greasy.

Step 7: Fry the Doughnuts

Carefully place a few doughnuts in the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pot. Fry them for about 1-2 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown. Use a slotted spoon to remove the doughnuts and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

Step 8: Prepare the Glaze

While the doughnuts are cooling, prepare the glaze. In a medium bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. If the glaze is too thick, add a little more milk until you reach your desired consistency.

Step 9: Glaze the Doughnuts

Once the doughnuts have cooled slightly, dip the tops into the glaze, allowing any excess to drip off. Place the glazed doughnuts on a wire rack to set. If you want to add toppings, sprinkle them on while the glaze is still wet.

Step 10: Enjoy!

Your homemade doughnuts are now ready to be enjoyed! Serve them fresh for breakfast, as a snack, or for dessert.
