Bringing Fresh Herbs to the Grill: Recipes for Summertime BBQs


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and enjoy delicious meals with family and friends. And what better way to elevate your BBQ experience than by incorporating fresh herbs into your grilled dishes? Not only do herbs add incredible flavors, but they also bring a touch of freshness and vibrancy to your meals. So, let’s dive into some mouthwatering recipes that will make your summertime BBQs truly unforgettable!

Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken breast, cut into chunks
  • 2 lemons, juiced and zested
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the lemon juice, lemon zest, minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken chunks to the bowl and toss until well coated. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken onto the soaked skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for about 8-10 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through and has a nice char.
  6. Serve the grilled lemon herb chicken skewers with a side of fresh salad or grilled vegetables for a complete and delectable meal.

Grilled Herb-Crusted Salmon

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the chopped dill, parsley, chives, minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the herb mixture onto both sides of the salmon fillets, ensuring they are fully coated.
  4. Place the salmon fillets on the grill and cook for about 4-6 minutes per side, or until the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Remove the grilled herb-crusted salmon from the grill and squeeze fresh lemon juice over the top.
  6. Serve the salmon with your favorite side dishes, such as roasted potatoes or grilled asparagus, for a delightful and healthy BBQ feast.

Grilled Herb and Garlic Shrimp Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped basil, parsley, cilantro, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the shrimp to the bowl and toss until well coated. Allow the shrimp to marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated shrimp onto the soaked skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until the shrimp turns pink and opaque.
  6. Serve the grilled herb and garlic shrimp skewers as an appetizer or as part of a seafood feast. Pair them with a zesty lemon aioli or a tangy cocktail sauce for an extra burst of flavor.

Grilled Herb and Garlic Corn on the Cob

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, minced garlic, chopped parsley, thyme leaves, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the herb and garlic mixture onto the corn on the cob, ensuring they are fully coated.
  4. Place the corn on the grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the corn is tender and slightly charred.
  5. Remove the grilled herb and garlic corn on the cob from the grill and serve hot.
  6. Enjoy the flavors of summer with this simple yet incredibly delicious side dish.

Conclusion

By bringing fresh herbs to the grill, you can take your summertime BBQs to a whole new level. These recipes are just a starting point to inspire your culinary creativity. Feel free to experiment with different herbs and flavors to suit your taste preferences. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and embark on a flavorful journey this summer. Happy grilling!
