Homemade Chicken and Waffles Recipe


Chicken and waffles is a beloved dish that pairs the savory, crispy goodness of fried chicken with the sweet, fluffy delight of waffles. Whether you’re making it for a special brunch, a holiday gathering, or just a weekend treat, this homemade chicken and waffles recipe will bring comfort, flavor, and a bit of indulgence to your table.

The History of Chicken and Waffles

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a brief look at the history of this iconic dish. Chicken and waffles is thought to have roots in both African American and Southern cuisine, though its exact origins are still debated. Some trace the dish back to the 1600s when Dutch settlers in Pennsylvania first began pairing waffles with meat. Others suggest the combination became popular in Harlem in the 1930s, where it was served at the famous Wells Supper Club by the renowned chef, Melvin “Stovepipe” Williams. Regardless of its origins, chicken and waffles has since become a staple in both Southern and soul food menus, beloved for its contrast of sweet and savory flavors.

Why This Recipe Works

This homemade chicken and waffles recipe works because it achieves the perfect balance of flavors and textures. The crispy, golden-brown chicken, seasoned just right, pairs beautifully with the fluffy, slightly sweet waffles. The addition of a drizzle of syrup or hot sauce enhances the flavor profile and adds that perfect touch of sweetness or heat.

One of the key factors that sets this recipe apart is the use of buttermilk for marinating the chicken, ensuring that each piece is tender, juicy, and full of flavor. The waffles, made from scratch with simple ingredients, have a rich flavor that complements the chicken perfectly. Plus, you can easily customize the seasoning to match your preferences, making this dish suitable for anyone’s palate.

Ingredients for the Chicken

  • 4 large chicken breasts or thighs (bone-in or boneless, depending on preference)
  • 2 cups buttermilk (for marinating)
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for extra heat)
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk (for dredging)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Ingredients for the Waffles

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional, for added flavor)
  • Non-stick spray (for the waffle iron)

For Serving

  • Maple syrup
  • Hot sauce (optional, for those who like a little heat)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Chicken and Waffles

1. Prepare the Chicken

To start, you’ll need to marinate the chicken. The buttermilk not only tenderizes the meat but also adds a tangy flavor that makes the fried chicken extra delicious. Here’s how to do it:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the buttermilk, salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using).
  2. Add the chicken breasts or thighs to the buttermilk mixture, making sure the chicken is fully submerged. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or up to overnight for maximum flavor and tenderness.

2. Prepare the Waffle Batter

While the chicken is marinating, it’s time to prepare the waffle batter. This recipe produces waffles that are light and fluffy with just the right amount of sweetness.

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, sugar, and cinnamon (if using).
  2. In another bowl, whisk the eggs, milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this could lead to dense waffles. The batter should be slightly lumpy.

3. Fry the Chicken

Once the chicken has marinated, it’s time to fry it. Follow these steps for crispy, golden-brown fried chicken:

  1. In a shallow bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, salt, pepper, paprika, and garlic powder. This seasoned flour will coat the chicken and give it a crispy exterior.
  2. In a separate bowl, pour the buttermilk (for dredging).
  3. Heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil in a deep skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C). You can test the oil’s readiness by dropping a small amount of batter into the oil; if it sizzles, the oil is ready.
  4. While the oil heats, dip each piece of marinated chicken into the buttermilk, then coat it evenly with the seasoned flour mixture. Shake off any excess flour.
  5. Fry the chicken in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 8-10 minutes per side, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C) and the exterior is golden and crispy. Remove the chicken from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

4. Cook the Waffles

While the chicken is frying, you can begin cooking the waffles. Using a waffle iron will give you the perfect texture—crispy on the outside, soft on the inside.

  1. Preheat your waffle iron and lightly grease it with non-stick spray.
  2. Pour the waffle batter into the waffle iron, using a ladle to ensure an even spread. Close the lid and cook the waffles according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Typically, this will take about 3-5 minutes, depending on your waffle iron.
  3. Once the waffles are golden brown and crispy, remove them from the iron and keep them warm by placing them on a baking sheet in the oven set to 200°F (93°C). This will keep the waffles warm while you finish frying the chicken.

5. Assemble the Dish

Now it’s time to assemble the chicken and waffles:

  1. Place one or two waffles on each plate.
  2. Top with a crispy piece of fried chicken.
  3. Drizzle with warm maple syrup for a touch of sweetness.
  4. For those who enjoy a bit of heat, add a few dashes of hot sauce to the chicken or drizzle it on the waffles.

6. Serve and Enjoy

Serve your homemade chicken and waffles immediately while the chicken is still crispy and the waffles are warm. This dish is perfect for breakfast, brunch, or dinner, and it’s sure to impress anyone who tastes it. The combination of textures and flavors will have everyone coming back for more!

Tips and Tricks for Perfect Chicken and Waffles

  • Marinate the chicken overnight: For the juiciest, most flavorful chicken, let it marinate overnight in the buttermilk mixture. This will ensure the chicken is tender and full of flavor.
  • Use a thermometer: To make sure your chicken is cooked perfectly, use a meat thermometer. Chicken should be cooked to an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C) to be safe.
  • Customize the waffles: If you prefer a richer waffle, you can add a tablespoon of sour cream to the batter for extra fluffiness. You can also add some chocolate chips or berries to the batter for a sweet twist.
  • Make it a meal prep: You can make the chicken and waffles in advance! Cook the chicken and waffles ahead of time, then store them in an airtight container in the fridge. Reheat in the oven for a few minutes to keep the chicken crispy.

Conclusion

This homemade chicken and waffles recipe brings together the best of both worlds—crispy, juicy fried chicken and fluffy, sweet waffles. Whether you’re serving it for a weekend brunch or a special occasion, this dish will undoubtedly be a crowd-pleaser. With just a few simple ingredients and some attention to detail, you can make this soul-food classic right in your own kitchen. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and enjoy this indulgent meal that will leave you craving more.
