The Best Christmas Pie Recipes: From Pumpkin to Pecan


As the holiday season approaches, the hustle and bustle of preparations begins. One of the most cherished traditions during Christmas is gathering around the table to enjoy delicious food, and what better way to celebrate than with a selection of festive pies? From the classic pumpkin to the rich pecan, Christmas pies bring warmth and joy to our holiday celebrations. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best Christmas pie recipes that you can bake for your family and friends. Get ready to impress everyone with your baking skills and spread the holiday cheer!

1. Classic Pumpkin Pie

No holiday feast is complete without a classic pumpkin pie. This velvety dessert, made with fresh pumpkin puree and aromatic spices, is a staple of Christmas celebrations. Here’s a simple yet delightful recipe to bring this iconic dessert to your table.

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups fresh pumpkin puree (or canned)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out your pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish, trimming any excess.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, heavy cream, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt until smooth.

  3. Pour the pumpkin filling into the prepared crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.

  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for an additional 40-50 minutes or until the filling is set and a knife inserted in the center comes out clean.

  5. Allow the pie to cool completely before serving. Top with whipped cream for an extra festive touch.

Tips

  • For a deeper flavor, consider roasting your own pumpkin before pureeing it.
  • Make sure to let the pie cool completely to achieve the perfect slice.

2. Decadent Pecan Pie

Pecan pie is another beloved holiday dessert that combines the rich flavor of pecans with a gooey, sweet filling. It’s a perfect addition to your Christmas dessert table.

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups pecan halves

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare your pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish.

  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well mixed.

  3. Stir in the pecan halves, ensuring they are evenly distributed.

  4. Pour the pecan mixture into the prepared crust, spreading the pecans out evenly.

  5. Bake for 60-70 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown. Cover the edges of the crust with foil if they begin to brown too quickly.

  6. Let the pie cool completely before slicing. Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful contrast of flavors.

Tips

  • For an extra twist, try adding a splash of bourbon to the filling for a richer flavor.
  • Ensure the pie is cooled properly for the perfect slice.

3. Apple Pie with Cinnamon Crust

Apple pie is a timeless classic that never goes out of style. The combination of sweet apples and a buttery cinnamon crust makes it a must-have during the holiday season.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 6 cups peeled and sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well)
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

For the Cinnamon Crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

Instructions

  1. Make the Cinnamon Crust: In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and cinnamon. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, shape into discs, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one dough disc to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Place the crust in the dish and set aside.

  3. In a mixing bowl, toss the sliced apples with sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and flour. Pour the apple filling into the prepared crust.

  4. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Cut slits for ventilation and crimp the edges to seal.

  5. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for an additional 35-45 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the crust is golden brown.

  6. Allow the pie to cool before serving. Pair it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a comforting dessert.

Tips

  • Try using a combination of different apple varieties for added flavor and texture.
  • Brush the top crust with an egg wash before baking for a beautiful golden finish.

4. Chocolate Cream Pie

For those who prefer a chocolatey indulgence, a chocolate cream pie is a fantastic choice. Rich, creamy, and utterly decadent, it’s sure to please chocolate lovers at your Christmas gathering.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 oz bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • Whipped cream for topping

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, cocoa powder, and cornstarch. Whisk constantly until the mixture thickens and begins to bubble.

  2. Remove from heat and stir in the chopped chocolate and vanilla extract until smooth.

  3. Pour the chocolate filling into the pre-baked pie crust, smoothing the top with a spatula. Allow it to cool for a few minutes before refrigerating for at least 3 hours, or until set.

  4. Once set, top with whipped cream before serving.

Tips

  • Garnish with chocolate shavings or cocoa powder for an elegant presentation.
  • For added flavor, consider adding a splash of coffee or espresso to the chocolate filling.

5. Cranberry Orange Pie

If you’re looking for a pie that adds a pop of color and a tangy flavor to your holiday spread, look no further than cranberry orange pie. This refreshing dessert balances tart cranberries with the sweetness of oranges perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 2 cups fresh or frozen cranberries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon orange zest
  • 1 cup fresh orange juice
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place your pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish.

  2. In a saucepan, combine cranberries, sugar, cornstarch, orange zest, and orange juice. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring until the cranberries start to burst and the mixture thickens.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and vanilla extract. Slowly add the egg mixture to the cranberry mixture, stirring well.

  4. Pour the cranberry filling into the pie crust and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is set.

  5. Allow the pie to cool before serving. This pie can also be served chilled for a refreshing treat.

Tips

  • Garnish with fresh cranberries or orange slices for a festive touch.
  • For a unique twist, try adding a splash of Grand Marnier to the filling.

Conclusion

The holiday season is the perfect time to showcase your baking skills and create delicious memories with loved ones. From the classic pumpkin pie to the indulgent chocolate cream pie, these Christmas pie recipes will surely impress your guests. Each recipe carries its own unique charm and flavor, allowing you to cater to a variety of tastes and preferences.

Gather your ingredients, invite friends and family to help, and enjoy the process of creating these delightful desserts. Whether it’s the warm spices of pumpkin pie or the tangy notes of cranberry orange pie, these desserts will be the highlight of your festive celebrations. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and let the holiday baking begin!
