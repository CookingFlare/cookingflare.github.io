Easy Garlic Knots: Perfect for Pasta Night


If you’re a pasta lover like me, then you know that no pasta night is complete without a side of warm and buttery garlic knots. These Easy Garlic Knots are not only incredibly delicious, but they are also super easy to make. So, whether you’re cooking for your family, hosting a dinner party, or just craving some homemade goodness, these garlic knots are the perfect addition to any pasta dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, combine the warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit for 5 minutes, or until the mixture becomes frothy.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture. Mix until the dough comes together.

  3. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  5. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  6. Punch down the risen dough and transfer it back onto the floured surface. Roll it out into a rectangle, about ½ inch thick.

  7. Using a sharp knife or a pizza cutter, cut the dough into strips, about 1 ½ inches wide.

  8. Take each strip and tie it into a knot, tucking the ends underneath. Place the knots onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving some space between each knot.

  9. In a small bowl, mix together the melted butter, minced garlic, and chopped parsley. Brush the garlic butter mixture onto each knot, making sure to coat them evenly.

  10. Drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the knots for added flavor.

  11. Bake the garlic knots in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until they turn golden brown.

  12. Once baked, remove the garlic knots from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These Easy Garlic Knots are incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in various ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to get you started:

  • Serve them as a side dish with your favorite pasta dishes, such as spaghetti, fettuccine alfredo, or lasagna.
  • Pair them with a bowl of homemade tomato soup for a comforting and satisfying meal.
  • Use them as a base for mini sandwiches or sliders, filled with your favorite ingredients.
  • Enjoy them as a snack on their own, dipped in marinara sauce or your favorite dip.

Conclusion

There you have it, a simple and foolproof recipe for Easy Garlic Knots that are perfect for pasta night or any occasion. These knots are soft, buttery, and bursting with the flavors of garlic and parsley. So, the next time you’re craving some delicious homemade bread, give this recipe a try. Your family and friends will be impressed, and you’ll have a new go-to side dish for all your pasta nights. Enjoy!
