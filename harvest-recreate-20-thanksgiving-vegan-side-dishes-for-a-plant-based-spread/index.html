Harvest & Recreate: 20 Thanksgiving Vegan Side Dishes for a Plant-Based Spread


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with friends and family, reflect on the abundance of the harvest, and give thanks for the blessings in our lives. For those who have adopted a plant-based diet, there’s no need to miss out on the traditional flavors of Thanksgiving. Here are 20 delicious vegan side dishes that will satisfy your palate and bring joy to your dinner table.

  1. Roasted Garlic and Rosemary Mashed Potatoes

To begin, try this simple yet mouthwatering Roasted Garlic and Rosemary Mashed Potatoes recipe. Begin by preheating your oven to 400°F and slice the tops off a head of garlic, drizzle with olive oil and season with salt, then roast for approximately 30-40 minutes or until tender.

Once done, mash the garlic with some rosemary, vegan butter, and plant-based milk to create the creamiest mashed potatoes you’ll ever taste.

  1. Cranberry and Pecan Stuffed Acorn Squash

Next up, a delightful Cranberry and Pecan Stuffed Acorn Squash dish. Cut two acorn squashes in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds. In a separate bowl, combine cooked quinoa, cranberries, pecans, herbs, and spices, then fill the squash halves with this savory mixture.

Pop them into a preheated oven at 375°F for around 30-35 minutes or until the squash is tender.

  1. Sautéed Green Beans with Shallots and Almonds

For a light and refreshing side, try this Sautéed Green Beans with Shallots and Almonds dish. Begin by blanching green beans for two minutes, then sauté them with sliced shallots, garlic, and seasoned with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes in vegan butter.

Finally, sprinkle with toasted almonds for added crunch.

  1. Maple Glazed Carrots with Thyme

Warm and full of flavor, Maple Glazed Carrots with Thyme are a fantastic addition to your table. Toss thinly sliced carrots with fresh thyme, vegan butter, and a drizzle of maple syrup, and roast in a preheated oven at 400°F for 25-30 minutes, until tender and caramelized.

  1. Creamy Vegan Scalloped Potatoes

Transport yourself to a French countryside bistro with this Creamy Vegan Scalloped Potatoes dish. Start by thinly slicing potatoes and layering them in a baking dish with thinly sliced onions and garlic.

Pour over a mixture of coconut milk, vegetable broth, flour, and seasonings, then bake at 375°F for 50 minutes to an hour or until potatoes are tender.

  1. Savory Sweet Potato Casserole

The Savory Sweet Potato Casserole is a savory spin on a classic, perfect for those who crave a bit of garlic and herbs. Begin by roasting sweet potatoes at 400°F until tender, then mash together with vegan butter, garlic, thyme, and a touch of nutmeg in a baking dish.

Top with breadcrumbs and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, until golden.

  1. Vegan Southern-Style Collard Greens

Constructed on Southern roots, the Vegan Southern-Style Collard Greens can be both hearty and delicate. Cook sliced collard greens with chopped onions, garlic, and vegetable broth until wilted and tender.

Season with smoked paprika, vegan chicken bouillon, and a splash of apple cider vinegar for a taste of home.

  1. Garlic and Parsnip Mash

While many are familiar with the mash of potatoes, the Garlic and Parsnip Mash offers an unique sweetness and delicate texture. Steam or boil parsnips and garlic until tender and mash together with vegan butter, nutritional yeast, and plant-based milk.

  1. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze

Bold and tangy, Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze deliver a flavorful kick. Toss halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, and a sprinkle of maple syrup, then roast at 400°F for 25-30 minutes.

Sprinkle with pinches of red pepper flakes for a hint of heat.

  1. Caramelized Onion and Kale Gratin

Melt in your mouth, this Caramelized Onion and Kale Gratin is savory yet sweet. Caramelize onions in vegan butter with garlic and thyme, then add kale, vegetable broth, and seasonings.

Pour into a baking dish, top with breadcrumbs, and bake at 350°F for 30 minutes.

  1. Vegan Stuffing with Mixed Mushrooms and Sage

Bring new life to the classic stuffing with this Vegan Stuffing with Mixed Mushrooms and sage recipe. Combine diced bread, sautéed mixed mushrooms, onions, garlic, and sage, then pour vegetable broth and bake for 30 minutes at 350°F.

  1. Jewish-Style Vegan Latkes

Celebrate Jewish heritage with the traditional Jewish-Style Vegan Latkes, a crispy potato pancake. Grate potatoes, onion, green onions, and garlic, then squeeze out excess moisture and mix in cornstarch, flour, and seasonings.

Fry patties in vegan oil until golden, and serve with vegan sour cream or applesauce.

  1. Cauliflower and Chickpea Curry

Bright and colorful, the Cauliflower and Chickpea Curry is a perfect complement to any festive spread. Blend coconut milk, tomatoes, onion, garlic, and spices to create a flavorful curry sauce, then simmer in with cauliflower, chickpeas, and other vegetables until tender.

  1. Linguine with Almond Pesto

For a nod to Italian cuisine, try this Linguine with Almond Pesto dish. Pulse almonds, basil leaves, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil together in a food processor to create a smooth, luscious pesto.

Serve over cooked, hot linguine and sprinkle with pine nuts.

  1. Couscous Stuffed Butternut Squash

Bring the warmth of North Africa to your dinner table with the Couscous Stuffed Butternut Squash. Cut a butternut squash in half, scoop out the seeds, and fill with cooked couscous mixed with herbs, spices, and dried fruit.

Bake the stuffed squash at 375°F for 45-50 minutes, until the squash is tender.

  1. Vegan Shepherds Pie

A beloved British classic, the Vegan Shepherds Pie layers mashed potatoes over a hearty filling of mushrooms, peas, and onions. Boil and mash potatoes, then brown mushrooms with onion, garlic, and vegan Worcestershire sauce in a separate pan.

Layer the filling in a baking dish, top with mashed potatoes, and bake for 30 minutes at 375°F.

  1. Quinoa Stuffed Peppers

Quinoa Stuffed Peppers offer a vibrant mix of flavors and textures. Cook quinoa and mix with corn, black beans, diced tomatoes, and spring onions, then stuff into roasted bell peppers.

Top with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro before serving.

  1. Vegan Mac and Cheese

Embrace childhood nostalgia with this beloved classic, Vegan Mac and Cheese. Blend together cashews, vegetable broth, nutritional yeast, and seasonings to make a creamy cheese sauce.

Pour over cooked macaroni and bake at 375°F for 20-25 minutes, until bubbly.

  1. Roasted Delicata Squash Rings

Minimalist and elegant, the Roasted Delicata Squash Rings offer a sophisticated and scrumptious dish. Slice delicata squash into rings, brush with olive oil, and fill with a mixture of pecans, cranberries, and thyme.

Bake at 400°F for 25-30 minutes, until the squash is tender.

  1. Vegan Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms

Delicious and unexpected, Vegan Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms are perfect for sharing. Scoop out the stems of portobello mushrooms and fill with cooked quinoa mixed with spinach, garlic, sun-dried tomatoes, and vegan feta.

Bake at 350°F for 20-25 minutes and finish with fresh basil and a drizzle of balsamic glaze.

These 20 vegan Thanksgiving side dishes offer a wealth of diverse flavors and textures, leaving something for everyone to enjoy. Embrace the bounty of the harvest, contemplate the love and care put into each dish, and share the warmth of connection with family and friends. Happy Thanksgiving!
