How to Make the Perfect Meatball Sub: A Comfort Food Favorite


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the classic meatball sub. This hearty sandwich, filled with savory meatballs, rich marinara sauce, and gooey cheese, is a staple in many households. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or a satisfying meal to impress your friends, mastering the art of the meatball sub is essential. In this guide, we’ll walk you through every step of the process, from making the perfect meatballs to assembling your sub for maximum flavor and enjoyment.

The History of the Meatball Sub

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a moment to appreciate the history of the meatball sub. Originating in Italian-American cuisine, this sandwich has roots that trace back to the early 20th century. Italian immigrants brought their culinary traditions to the United States, and the meatball sub quickly became a beloved dish. Over the years, it has evolved, with various regions adding their unique twists. Today, the meatball sub is a favorite in delis, pizzerias, and homes across the country.

Ingredients for the Perfect Meatball Sub

To create a mouthwatering meatball sub, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Meatballs:

  • 1 pound ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork for added flavor)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (preferably Italian-style)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley (or 1 tablespoon dried parsley)
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a bit of heat)

For the Marinara Sauce:

  • 2 cups marinara sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Assembling the Sub:

  • 4 hoagie rolls (or sub rolls)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish, optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving, optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Meatballs

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). This will ensure that your meatballs cook evenly and develop a nice crust.

  2. Mix the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped parsley, egg, minced garlic, salt, black pepper, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes. Use your hands to mix the ingredients until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to tough meatballs.

  3. Form the Meatballs: Using your hands, shape the mixture into meatballs about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, ensuring they are spaced apart to allow for even cooking.

  4. Bake the Meatballs: Once your oven is preheated, bake the meatballs for 20-25 minutes, or until they are cooked through and browned on the outside. You can check for doneness by using a meat thermometer; the internal temperature should reach 160°F (70°C).

Step 2: Prepare the Marinara Sauce

  1. Heat the Sauce: While the meatballs are baking, heat the marinara sauce in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the olive oil and garlic powder, stirring to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the sauce to simmer for about 10 minutes, letting the flavors meld together.

Step 3: Assemble the Meatball Subs

  1. Prepare the Rolls: Once the meatballs are done, take your hoagie rolls and slice them open lengthwise, being careful not to cut all the way through. You want to create a pocket for the meatballs and sauce.

  2. Add the Meatballs: Place 3-4 meatballs in each roll, depending on the size of your rolls and your appetite.

  3. Top with Sauce: Spoon a generous amount of marinara sauce over the meatballs, ensuring they are well-coated.

  4. Add Cheese: Sprinkle a handful of shredded mozzarella cheese on top of the sauce. If you’re a cheese lover, feel free to add more!

  5. Melt the Cheese: Place the assembled subs on a baking sheet and pop them under the broiler for 2-3 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Keep a close eye on them to prevent burning.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy
