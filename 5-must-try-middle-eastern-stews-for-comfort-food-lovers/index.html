5 Must-Try Middle Eastern Stews for Comfort Food Lovers


When it comes to comfort food, few cuisines can rival the rich, aromatic, and hearty stews of the Middle East. These dishes are not only packed with flavor but also steeped in tradition, often passed down through generations. Whether you’re looking for a cozy meal to warm you up on a chilly evening or a dish that brings a taste of the exotic to your dinner table, Middle Eastern stews are the perfect choice. Here are five must-try recipes that will satisfy your cravings and introduce you to the vibrant flavors of this region.

1. Moroccan Tagine

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs lamb or chicken, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1 cup chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked couscous or rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Sear the Meat: In a large tagine or heavy pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the lamb or chicken and sear until browned on all sides. Remove the meat and set aside.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent.

  3. Add Spices: Stir in the cumin, coriander, cinnamon, ginger, and turmeric. Cook for another minute until fragrant.

  4. Combine Ingredients: Return the meat to the pot. Add the diced tomatoes, broth, apricots, and chickpeas. Season with salt and pepper.

  5. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender.

  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve over couscous or rice.

Why You’ll Love It:

Moroccan tagine is a perfect blend of sweet and savory, with tender meat and a medley of spices that create a warm, inviting dish. The addition of dried fruits adds a unique sweetness that balances the spices beautifully.

2. Lebanese Mujadara

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup lentils, rinsed
  • 1/2 cup rice (preferably basmati)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Yogurt for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook Lentils: In a pot, combine lentils with 3 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 15 minutes until tender. Drain and set aside.

  2. Fry Onions: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until they are caramelized and golden brown, about 15-20 minutes. Remove half for garnishing later.

  3. Combine Ingredients: In the same skillet, add the cooked lentils, rice, cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir well and add 2 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce heat to low. Cook for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed.

  4. Serve: Fluff the mujadarra with a fork, top with the reserved caramelized onions, and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve with yogurt on the side if desired.

Why You’ll Love It:

Mujadara is a comforting vegetarian dish that highlights the earthy flavors of lentils and rice, complemented by the sweetness of caramelized onions. It’s simple yet satisfying, making it a staple in many Middle Eastern households.
