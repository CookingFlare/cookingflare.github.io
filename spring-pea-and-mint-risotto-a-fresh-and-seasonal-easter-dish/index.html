Spring Pea and Mint Risotto: A Fresh and Seasonal Easter Dish


Risotto is a classic Italian dish that never fails to impress. With its creamy texture and rich flavors, it’s no wonder why it’s a favorite among food enthusiasts. This Spring Pea and Mint Risotto recipe takes the traditional risotto up a notch by adding fresh peas and mint, creating a dish that perfectly captures the essence of spring. Whether you’re looking for a delicious Easter dish or simply want to savor the flavors of the season, this risotto recipe is sure to delight your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable broth in a saucepan over low heat and keep it warm.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic and sauté until the onion becomes translucent.

  3. Add the Arborio rice to the skillet and stir it constantly for about 2 minutes, until the rice grains are well coated with the oil and start to turn translucent around the edges.

  4. Pour in the white wine and stir until it is fully absorbed by the rice.

  5. Begin adding the warm vegetable broth to the skillet, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Allow each ladle of broth to be absorbed before adding the next. This process should take about 20 minutes.

  6. At the 15-minute mark, add the peas to the skillet and continue adding the broth as before.

  7. Taste the risotto to check if the rice is cooked al dente. It should be creamy and tender, with a slight bite.

  8. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and chopped mint leaves. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  9. Let the risotto sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  10. Serve the Spring Pea and Mint Risotto hot, garnished with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese and a few fresh mint leaves.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a squeeze of lemon juice to the risotto just before serving.

  • If you prefer a more vibrant green color, you can blanch the peas before adding them to the risotto. Simply boil them for 2-3 minutes, then plunge them into ice water to halt the cooking process.

  • To make this dish vegan-friendly, use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth and omit the Parmesan cheese. You can substitute it with nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor.

  • If you want to add some protein to the risotto, you can stir in cooked shrimp, grilled chicken, or sautéed mushrooms.

  • Leftover risotto can be transformed into delightful arancini (Italian rice balls) by shaping the cold risotto into balls, coating them in breadcrumbs, and deep-frying them until golden and crispy.

Enjoy this Spring Pea and Mint Risotto as a main course or as a side dish to complement your Easter feast. Its vibrant colors and fresh flavors are sure to impress your family and friends. Happy cooking and happy Easter!
