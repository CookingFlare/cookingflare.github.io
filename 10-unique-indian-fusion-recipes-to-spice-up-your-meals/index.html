10 Unique Indian Fusion Recipes to Spice Up Your Meals


As the world becomes increasingly gastronomically interwoven, the boundaries of culinary traditions are continually being pushed. The fusion of flavors, techniques, and ingredients from diverse cultures has resulted in a plethora of intriguing dishes that tantalize our taste buds. Speaking of fusion cuisine, Indian cooking is particularly well-suited for the blending of flavors and spices from around the world. In this blog post, we'll explore 10 unique Indian fusion recipes that will add a punch of excitement to your meals.

  1. Salade Niçoise Inspired Vindaloo Inspired by the French classic Salade Niçoise, this recipe combines the spicy and tangy flavors of Indian vindaloo with the freshness of a French salad. To create this heavenly dish, start with marinated chicken cooked in a mixture of Indian spices and yogurt. Then, toss juicy tomatoes, crispy bacon, and soft boiled eggs with the chicken mixture and top with crumbled feta cheese and chopped fresh cilantro. Marinade Chicken:

1 lb boneless chicken breast or thighs 2 tbsp yogurt 2 tbsp freshly ground cumin 1 tsp cayenne pepper 1 tsp garam masala Salt, to taste

Garlic and Herb Sauce:

3 cloves minced garlic 1 tbsp olive oil 2 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro 1 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice Salt and pepper, to taste

Salad:

2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved 4-6 pieces of crispy bacon, crumbled 2 large eggs, boiled and sliced 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese Salt and pepper, to taste

  1. Chicken Tikka Quesadilla For this Indian fusion twist on the classic Tex-Mex dish, marinate chicken in a mixture of yogurt, ginger, and cumin before grilling it. Then, stuff the chicken between a tortilla, sprinkle with cilantro and mozzarella cheese, and cook until crispy and golden. Marinade Chicken:

1 lb boneless chicken breast or thighs 1/2 cup plain yogurt 1/4 cup ghee or oil 1 tsp ground cumin 1 tsp ground coriander 1 tsp garam masala 1 tsp cayenne pepper

Assembly:

8-10 corn tortillas 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese 1 cup chopped cilantro 1/4 cup crumbled blue cheese (optional) Salsa, sour cream, or yogurt for serving

  1. Saffron Risotto with Coconut Chutney and Lamb Chops In this creamy and aromatic dish, Indian saffron eleverbrates with Italian risotto while the crunch of lamb chops adds a satisfying textural element. Start by toasting basmati rice in a mixture of saffron, cardamom, and cumin, then cook the risotto until it reaches a creamy consistency. Meanwhile, marinate lamb chops in a mixture of lime juice, coriander, and cumin before grilling them to perfection. Risotto:

1 cup Arborio rice 4 cups vegetable or chicken stock 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese 2 tbsp saffron threads, soaked in 2 tbsp hot water 1 tsp ground cumin 1 tsp ground cardamom 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon Salt and pepper, to taste

Chutney:

1 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1/2 cup green chilies 1/4 cup grated coconut 2 tbsp lemon juice Salt and pepper, to taste

Lamb Chops:

8-10 lamb chops 1/4 cup lime juice 2 tbsp olive oil 2 tbsp garam masala 1 tsp ground cumin 1 tsp ground coriander Salt and pepper, to taste

  1. Indian Butter Chicken Tacos In this surprising fusion of Indian and Mexican flavors, marinated chicken is cooked in a mixture of Indian spices and yogurt before being wrapped in a warm taco shell. Top with cilantro, radish, and a dollop of raita for an authentic Indian touch. Marinade Chicken:

1 lb boneless chicken breast or thighs 1 cup plain yogurt 2 tbsp ghee or oil 2 tsp garam masala 1 tsp ground cumin 1 tsp ground coriander 1 tsp cayenne pepper Salt, to taste

Taco Filling:

8-10 corn tortillas 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese 1 cup chopped cilantro 1 cup thinly sliced radish 1/4 cup raita (see below)

Raita:

1 cup plain yogurt 1/2 cup diced cucumber 1/4 cup diced cilantro 1 tsp chopped fresh mint Salt, to taste

  1. Thai Green Curry Naan This unique fusion dish replaces traditional naan bread with crispy Thai-style crepe wrappers. Marinate chicken in a mixture of coconut milk, Thai red curry paste, and fish sauce, then serve it wrapped in a crispy crepe with chopped cilantro, cucumber, and carrot. For an authentic touch, use a combination of red and green chilies for added depth of flavor. Marinade Chicken:

1 lb boneless chicken breast or thighs 1 cup coconut milk 2 tbsp Thai red curry paste 2 tbsp fish sauce 2 tbsp lime juice 1 tsp palm sugar Salt, to taste

Crepe Wrappers:

2 cups all-purpose flour 2 eggs 1 cup coconut milk Salt, to taste

Filling:

1 cup chopped cilantro 1 cup thinly sliced cucumber 1 cup shredded carrot 1 cup shredded red cabbage 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint

  1. Indian Spring Rolls with Chickpea and Pesto Filling For an innovative fusion twist on the classic spring roll, fill store-bought wrappers with a mixture of chickpeas, pesto sauce, and Indian spices. Deep-fry until crispy and serve with a side of cilantro-lime sauce. Chickpea Filling:

1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed 1/4 cup pesto sauce 1 tsp cumin 1 tsp coriander 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper Salt, to taste

Assembly:

1 package spring roll wrappers Vegetable oil for frying Cilantro-lime sauce for serving

**Cilantro-lime sauce:

1 cup chopped cilantro 2 tbsp lime juice 1/4 cup mayonnaise Salt and pepper, to taste

  1. Baingan Bhab Hari (Eggplant with Yogurt and Herbs) This classic Indian dish gets a Mediterranean twist with the addition of fresh herbs and creamy yogurt. Grill or bake eggplant until tender, then top with a dollop of yogurt and sprinkle with chopped fresh herbs and sumac. Grilled Eggplant:

2 large eggplants 2 tbsp olive oil Salt and pepper, to taste

Yogurt and Herb Sauce:

1 cup plain yogurt 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill 2 tbsp lemon juice 1 tsp sumac Salt and pepper, to taste

  1. Kashmiri Pulao with Lamb Meatballs In this dish, the warm, aromatic spices of Kashmiri cuisine meet the rich flavors of lamb meatballs. Marinate lamb in a mixture of cumin, coriander, and cardamom before mixing it with cooked onions and forming into balls. Serve the meatballs over a flavorful rice pilaf. Meatballs:

1 lb lamb 1 cup breadcrumbs 1 egg 1 onion, finely chopped 1 tsp ground cumin 1 tsp ground coriander 1 tsp ground cardamom 1/2 tsp garam masala Salt and pepper, to taste

Pilaf:

1 cup basmati rice 2 cups water 2 tbsp ghee or oil 1 tsp ground cumin 1 tsp ground coriander 1 tsp ground cardamom Salt and pepper, to taste

  1. Japanese Teriyaki Chicken Tikka Masala In this intriguing fusion dish, Japanese teriyaki sauce and Indian tikka masala come together in a harmonious blend of sweet and spicy flavors. Marinate chicken in a mixture of soy sauce, ginger, and garlic before grilling it and serving with a side of basmati rice and naan bread. Marinade Chicken:

1 lb boneless chicken breast or thighs 1/2 cup soy sauce 1/4 cup teriyaki sauce 2 tbsp grated ginger 2 cloves minced garlic 1 tsp garam masala 1 tsp cayenne pepper

Teriyaki Sauce:

1 cup soy sauce 1/4 cup brown sugar 2 tbsp rice vinegar 1 tsp grated ginger 1 tsp garlic, minced

  1. Chickpea and Mushroom Balti with Lemon Aioli In this demonstration of Asian-Indian fusion cooking, crispy chickpeas and sautéed mushrooms are served in a flavorful Balti curry sauce. To complete the meal, pair it with a tangy lemon aioli for dipping. Balti Sauce:

1 can chickpeas 2 cups sliced mushrooms 2 tbsp butter or oil 1 onion, finely chopped 1 tsp cumin 1 tsp coriander 1 tsp cayenne pepper 1 tsp garam masala Salt and pepper, to taste

Lemon Aioli:

1 cup mayonnaise 2 tbsp lemon juice 1 tsp chopped fresh parsley Salt and pepper, to taste

These intricate and innovative fusion dishes showcase the versatility of Indian cuisine and its ability to blend with flavors from around the world. In our next blog post, we'll delve into the bold twist and experimentations that await your taste buds.
