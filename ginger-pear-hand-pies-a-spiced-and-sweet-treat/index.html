Ginger Pear Hand Pies: A Spiced and Sweet Treat


If you’re a fan of warm, comforting flavors, then you’re in for a treat with these delightful ginger pear hand pies. This recipe combines the sweetness of ripe pears with the warm, spicy notes of ginger, creating a flavor combination that is both familiar and exciting. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply want to treat yourself to a delicious dessert, these hand pies are sure to delight your taste buds.

Ingredients

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 4 ripe pears, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

For the Egg Wash:

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pie Crust

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add the chilled, cubed butter to the bowl and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, and mix the dough until it comes together.
  4. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the diced pears, granulated sugar, grated ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the pears are tender, about 10-12 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a tablespoon of water to create a slurry, then add it to the pear mixture. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the filling thickens. Remove from heat and let it cool.

Step 3: Assemble the Hand Pies

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Roll out one of the chilled pie crust disks on a lightly floured surface to about 1/8 inch thickness.
  3. Use a round cutter to cut out circles from the dough, re-rolling the scraps as needed.
  4. Place a spoonful of the cooled pear filling in the center of each dough circle.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and water to make the egg wash.
  6. Brush the edges of the dough circles with the egg wash, then fold the dough over the filling to create half-moon shapes.
  7. Press the edges together with a fork to seal the pies, then place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  8. Brush the tops of the hand pies with the remaining egg wash.

Step 4: Bake and Enjoy

  1. Bake the hand pies for 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown and flaky.
  2. Allow the hand pies to cool slightly before serving. These ginger pear hand pies are delicious on their own, but you can also serve them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra special treat.

Conclusion

These ginger pear hand pies are a wonderful way to enjoy the flavors of the season in a convenient, handheld form. The combination of sweet, juicy pears and warm, aromatic ginger makes for a truly delightful treat that is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or new to the world of pastry, these hand pies are sure to impress with their delicious flavor and flaky, golden crust. So why not roll up your sleeves and give this recipe a try? Your taste buds will thank you!
