Preserving the Bounty: Recipes for Canning Stone Fruits


Canning is a fantastic way to preserve the bounty of summer stone fruits. With their juicy flesh, vibrant colors, and sweet flavors, stone fruits like peaches, plums, and cherries are the perfect candidates for canning. Whether you have a surplus of fruits from your own garden or you’ve picked them up from the local farmers’ market, canning allows you to enjoy these delicious fruits all year round. In this blog post, we will explore three mouthwatering recipes for canning stone fruits that will surely delight your taste buds.

1. Peach Jam

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of peeled, pitted, and chopped peaches
  • 4 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the peaches, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture comes to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the jam thickens.
  4. Stir in the vanilla extract and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and let the jam cool slightly.
  6. Transfer the jam into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace.
  7. Seal the jars tightly and process in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  8. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

2. Plum Chutney

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups of pitted and chopped plums
  • 1 cup of chopped red onion
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup of raisins
  • 1 tablespoon of grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine all the ingredients and bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 45 minutes, or until the chutney thickens.
  3. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. Remove from heat and let the chutney cool slightly.
  5. Transfer the chutney into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace.
  6. Seal the jars tightly and process in a boiling water bath for 15 minutes.
  7. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

3. Cherry Preserves

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of pitted cherries
  • 3 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of almond extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the cherries, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture comes to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the preserves thicken.
  4. Stir in the almond extract and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and let the preserves cool slightly.
  6. Transfer the preserves into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace.
  7. Seal the jars tightly and process in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  8. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Conclusion

Preserving stone fruits through canning is a wonderful way to enjoy their flavors long after the summer season has ended. These recipes for peach jam, plum chutney, and cherry preserves offer a delightful variety of options to savor the taste of stone fruits throughout the year. With their vibrant colors and sweet aromas, these canned delights will surely add a burst of flavor to your breakfast toast, afternoon snacks, or even as a topping for desserts. So, grab those fresh stone fruits, gather your canning supplies, and embark on a journey of preserving the bounty of summer for your future enjoyment. Happy canning!
