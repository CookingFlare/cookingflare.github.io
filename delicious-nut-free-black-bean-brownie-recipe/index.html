Delicious Nut-Free Black Bean Brownie Recipe


Who doesn’t love a rich, fudgy brownie? Imagine sinking your teeth into a decadent treat that not only satisfies your sweet tooth but also packs a nutritional punch. That’s right – today, we’re diving into the world of nut-free black bean brownies. These brownies are not only delicious but also gluten-free and perfect for anyone looking to incorporate more fiber and protein into their diet.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to whip up these scrumptious black bean brownies:

  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil or vegetable oil
  • 3/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • Optional: 1/2 cup chocolate chips (dark or semi-sweet)

Instructions

1. Prep Your Oven and Pan

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9 inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper for easy removal later.

2. Blend the Black Beans

In a food processor or blender, combine the rinsed and drained black beans, eggs, melted coconut oil, cocoa powder, salt, vanilla extract, sugar, and baking powder. Blend until smooth and well combined. You want a silky batter without any lumps of beans.

3. Fold in Chocolate Chips (Optional)

For an extra chocolatey kick, fold in the chocolate chips into the batter. This step is optional but highly recommended for all the chocolate lovers out there.

4. Bake the Brownies

Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan, spreading it out evenly with a spatula. Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the edges start to pull away from the sides of the pan and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs.

5. Cool and Serve

Once baked, remove the brownies from the oven and allow them to cool in the pan for about 15 minutes. This cooling period helps them set and makes them easier to slice. After 15 minutes, carefully lift the brownies out of the pan using the parchment paper (if using) and transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing into squares.

6. Enjoy Your Nut-Free Black Bean Brownies!

Now comes the best part – enjoying your freshly baked black bean brownies! These brownies are moist, chocolatey, and have a delightful fudgy texture thanks to the black beans. They’re perfect for satisfying your chocolate cravings while sneaking in some extra fiber and protein from the beans.

Tips for Success

  • Rinse the Beans: Make sure to rinse the black beans thoroughly before using them to remove any excess starch, which can affect the texture of your brownies.
  • Storage: Store any leftover brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or refrigerate for longer freshness.
  • Customization: Feel free to customize your brownies by adding nuts, dried fruits, or even a swirl of peanut butter on top before baking (if allergies aren’t a concern).

Why Choose Nut-Free Black Bean Brownies?

Nut-free black bean brownies are a fantastic option for those with nut allergies or dietary restrictions. They offer a healthier alternative to traditional brownies without sacrificing flavor or texture. The black beans not only add moisture but also boost the fiber and protein content, making these brownies a guilt-free indulgence.

Whether you’re baking for a special occasion, a family treat, or simply to satisfy a chocolate craving, these nut-free black bean brownies are sure to be a hit. They’re easy to make, require minimal ingredients, and can be enjoyed by everyone, regardless of dietary preferences.

So, what are you waiting for? Gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and treat yourself to a batch of these irresistible nut-free black bean brownies. Your taste buds – and your body – will thank you for it!
