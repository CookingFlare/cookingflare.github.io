Wrap It Up: 5 Healthy and Flavorful Wrap Recipes for Diabetics


Are you looking for delicious and nutritious meal options that are suitable for diabetics? Look no further! In this blog post, we will share five mouthwatering wrap recipes that are not only healthy but also packed with flavor. These recipes are specifically designed to meet the dietary needs of individuals with diabetes, making them a perfect choice for anyone looking to maintain stable blood sugar levels while enjoying a satisfying meal.

1. Greek Chicken Wrap

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole wheat tortilla
  • 4 ounces grilled chicken breast, sliced
  • 1/4 cup diced cucumber
  • 1/4 cup diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine Greek yogurt, lemon juice, dried oregano, salt, and pepper, and mix well to create the dressing.
  2. Lay the whole wheat tortilla on a flat surface and spread the Greek yogurt dressing evenly over it.
  3. Place the sliced grilled chicken breast, diced cucumber, diced tomatoes, and crumbled feta cheese on top of the dressing.
  4. Roll the tortilla tightly, tucking in the sides as you go.
  5. Slice the wrap in half and secure with toothpicks if necessary.
  6. Serve and enjoy!

2. Veggie and Hummus Wrap

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole grain tortilla
  • 2 tablespoons hummus
  • 1/4 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/4 cup sliced bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup sliced cucumbers
  • 1/4 cup baby spinach leaves
  • 1/4 avocado, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Spread the hummus evenly over the whole grain tortilla.
  2. Layer the shredded carrots, sliced bell peppers, sliced cucumbers, baby spinach leaves, and avocado on top of the hummus.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Roll the tortilla tightly, securing the contents inside.
  5. Slice the wrap in half and serve.

3. Turkey and Cranberry Wrap

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole wheat tortilla
  • 3 ounces low-sodium turkey breast slices
  • 2 tablespoons cranberry sauce (sugar-free)
  • 1/4 cup baby spinach leaves
  • 1/4 cup sliced red onions
  • 1/4 cup sliced Granny Smith apples

Instructions:

  1. Lay the whole wheat tortilla on a flat surface.
  2. Spread the cranberry sauce evenly over the tortilla.
  3. Place the low-sodium turkey breast slices, baby spinach leaves, sliced red onions, and sliced Granny Smith apples on top of the cranberry sauce.
  4. Roll the tortilla tightly, ensuring the filling is secure.
  5. Slice the wrap in half and serve.

4. Tuna Salad Wrap

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole grain tortilla
  • 3 ounces canned tuna (in water), drained
  • 2 tablespoons plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup diced celery
  • 1/4 cup diced red bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup diced red onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lettuce leaves for added crunch

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the canned tuna, plain Greek yogurt, lemon juice, diced celery, diced red bell peppers, diced red onions, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create the tuna salad.
  2. Lay the whole grain tortilla on a flat surface.
  3. Place a few lettuce leaves on the tortilla.
  4. Spoon the tuna salad onto the lettuce leaves, spreading it evenly.
  5. Roll the tortilla tightly, tucking in the sides as you go.
  6. Slice the wrap in half and serve.

5. Mexican Black Bean Wrap

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole wheat tortilla
  • 1/2 cup cooked black beans
  • 1/4 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced red onions
  • 1/4 cup diced bell peppers (any color)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/4 avocado, sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the cooked black beans, diced tomatoes, diced red onions, diced bell peppers, chopped fresh cilantro, lime juice, ground cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine the flavors.
  2. Lay the whole wheat tortilla on a flat surface.
  3. Spread the black bean mixture evenly over the tortilla.
  4. If desired, add sliced avocado on top of the black bean mixture.
  5. Roll the tortilla tightly, securing the contents inside.
  6. Slice the wrap in half and serve.

These five wrap recipes are not only diabetic-friendly but also versatile enough to suit different taste preferences. Feel free to get creative and customize the ingredients to your liking.
