Salmorejo: Creamy and Refreshing Tomato Soup from Andalusia


If you’re a fan of tomato soup, get ready to be blown away by the flavors of Salmorejo! Hailing from the beautiful region of Andalusia in southern Spain, Salmorejo is a traditional dish that combines the simplicity of tomatoes with the richness of olive oil and the tang of vinegar. The result? A creamy, refreshing, and absolutely delicious soup that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

To make Salmorejo, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 kg ripe tomatoes
  • 200 g stale bread, preferably a rustic loaf
  • 100 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon sherry vinegar
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the tomatoes. Wash them thoroughly and remove the stems. Cut them into quarters and place them in a blender or food processor.

  2. Take the stale bread and remove the crust. Tear the bread into small pieces and add them to the blender with the tomatoes.

  3. Peel the garlic clove and add it to the blender as well. Garlic adds a subtle kick to the soup, so adjust the quantity according to your taste preferences.

  4. Drizzle the olive oil over the ingredients in the blender. Extra virgin olive oil is key to achieving the rich and velvety texture of Salmorejo.

  5. Add the sherry vinegar to the blender. This vinegar not only enhances the flavor of the soup but also helps to preserve the vibrant red color of the tomatoes.

  6. Season the mixture with salt to taste. Remember, a little goes a long way, so start with a small amount and adjust as needed.

  7. Blend all the ingredients together until you achieve a smooth and creamy consistency. If the mixture appears too thick, you can add a splash of cold water to thin it out.

  8. Once blended, transfer the soup to a container and refrigerate for at least two hours. Chilling the Salmorejo allows the flavors to meld together and enhances the refreshing taste.

  9. When you’re ready to serve, garnish the Salmorejo with a drizzle of olive oil and some diced cucumbers or croutons if desired. The extra touch of freshness adds a delightful crunch to the creamy soup.

Enjoy the Flavors of Andalusia

Salmorejo is not just a soup; it’s a culinary experience that transports you to the sun-drenched streets of Andalusia. With every spoonful, you’ll taste the vibrant red tomatoes, the smoothness of the olive oil, and the subtle tang of vinegar. It’s a dish that celebrates simplicity and captures the essence of Mediterranean cuisine.

Whether you’re looking for a refreshing starter for a summer meal or a comforting bowl of goodness during the colder months, Salmorejo is a versatile recipe that will never disappoint. So, grab your blender and let the flavors of Andalusia take center stage in your kitchen. You won’t regret it!

Now that you have the recipe for Salmorejo, it’s time to gather the ingredients and embark on a culinary adventure. Prepare this creamy and refreshing tomato soup from Andalusia, and get ready to wow your taste buds. Bon appétit!
