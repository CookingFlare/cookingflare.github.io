Ultimate Comfort Food: Creamy Baked Mac and Cheese


There’s something about mac and cheese that just hits the spot. It’s the ultimate comfort food, with its creamy, cheesy goodness and crispy topping. Whether you’re making it for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion, baked mac and cheese is always a crowd-pleaser. In this recipe, we’ll take the classic mac and cheese to the next level with a rich and creamy twist that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz (450g) elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup (60g) all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups (950ml) whole milk
  • 2 cups (200g) sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup (100g) mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup (100g) Gruyère cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup (50g) panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons (30g) unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. Cook the elbow macaroni according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.

  3. In a large saucepan, melt 1/2 cup of butter over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour and cook for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly to make a roux.

  4. Gradually pour in the milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Cook the mixture until it thickens, which should take about 5 minutes.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and add the cheddar, mozzarella, and Gruyère cheese, stirring until melted and smooth. Season the cheese sauce with paprika, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.

  6. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce, stirring until well combined.

  7. Transfer the mac and cheese mixture to the greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  8. In a small bowl, combine the panko breadcrumbs with 2 tablespoons of melted butter. Sprinkle the breadcrumb mixture over the mac and cheese.

  9. Bake the mac and cheese in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

  10. Let the baked mac and cheese cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra flavor boost, try adding crumbled bacon or diced ham to the mac and cheese before baking.
  • If you’re a fan of spicy food, consider mixing in a bit of diced jalapeños for a kick.
  • Experiment with different cheese combinations to create your own signature mac and cheese blend.
  • To make this dish vegetarian-friendly, simply omit the bacon and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.

The Perfect Comfort Food

Creamy baked mac and cheese is the epitome of comfort food. Its rich, cheesy flavor and crispy breadcrumb topping make it a timeless favorite for people of all ages. Whether you’re serving it at a family gathering, a potluck, or just a cozy night in, this ultimate comfort food is sure to warm hearts and satisfy appetites. So, take your mac and cheese game to the next level with this irresistible recipe and enjoy the creamy, cheesy goodness that everyone loves.
