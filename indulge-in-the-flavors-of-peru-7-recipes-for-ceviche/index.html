Indulge in the Flavors of Peru: 7 Recipes for Ceviche


Peruvian cuisine is a melodic blend of indigenous, Spanish, African, and Asian influences, resulting in a vibrant and aromatic culinary scene. Among itssignature dishes, ceviche stands out as a refreshing and flavorful symbol of Peruvian gastronomy. This dish has gained worldwide recognition for its simplicity, yet complexity in flavors. In this culinary journey, we'll explore 7 recipes that showcase the art of preparing this beloved seafood salad. Ceviche 1370: A Traditional Favorite Ceviche 1370 is a beloved Peruvian recipe originating from the coastal city of Lima. This dish is characterized by its bold flavors, textures, and presentation. The ingredients used are:

1 pound of fresh white fish (usually halibut or snapper), cut into small pieces 1/2 cup fresh lime juice 1/4 cup chopped red onion 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1 minced garlic clove 1 diced aji amarillo pepper (Peruvian yellow pepper) Salt and pepper to taste 2 diced tomatoes 1 tablespoon olive oil 4-6 corn kernels 6-8 pieces of crispy plantain (optional)

Combine the fish, lime juice, onion, cilantro, garlic, and aji amarillo pepper in a large bowl. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Just before serving, stir in the diced tomatoes and olive oil. Garnish with crispy plantain and corn kernels. Ceviche 1370 with Seafood Medley For a more elaborate version of Ceviche 1370, add a mix of fresh seafood to the recipe. The ingredients needed are:

1 pound of fresh white fish (usually halibut or snapper), cut into small pieces 1/2 cup fresh lime juice 1/4 cup chopped red onion 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1 minced garlic clove 1 diced aji amarillo pepper (Peruvian yellow pepper) Salt and pepper to taste 1/2 cup cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined 1/2 cup sliced octopus 2 diced tomatoes 1 tablespoon olive oil 4-6 corn kernels 6-8 pieces of crispy plantain (optional)

Combine the fish, lime juice, onion, cilantro, garlic, and aji amarillo pepper in a large bowl. Add the cooked shrimp and sliced octopus. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Just before serving, stir in the diced tomatoes and olive oil. Garnish with crispy plantain and corn kernels. 1 pound of fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined 1/2 cup fresh lime juice 1/4 cup chopped red onion 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1 minced garlic clove 1 diced aji amarillo pepper (Peruvian yellow pepper) Salt and pepper to taste 2 diced mangoes 1 diced avocado 2 diced tomatoes 1 tablespoon olive oil 4-6 corn kernels 6-8 pieces of crispy plantain (optional)

Cozido Ceviche with a Mojito Twist For a refreshing twist on the traditional ceviche, combine the dish with the flavors of a mojito cocktail. The ingredients used are:

1 pound of fresh white fish (usually halibut or snapper), cut into small pieces 1/2 cup fresh lime juice 1/4 cup chopped red onion 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1 minced garlic clove 1 diced aji amarillo pepper (Peruvian yellow pepper) Salt and pepper to taste 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint 2 diced tomatoes 1 tablespoon olive oil 4-6 corn kernels 6-8 pieces of crispy plantain (optional) 2 mojito ice cubes (see below for recipe)

Combine the fish, lime juice, onion, cilantro, garlic, and aji amarillo pepper in a large bowl. Add the chopped mint and diced tomatoes. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Just before serving, stir in the olive oil and mojito ice cubes. Garnish with crispy plantain and corn kernels. Mojito Ice Cubes Recipe

1 cup water 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint 1 tablespoon brown sugar 1 tablespoon lime juice 1/4 cup chopped fresh lime zest 4-6 cubed ice

Combine the water, chopped mint, brown sugar, lime juice, and lime zest in a blender. Blend until smooth. Pour the mixture into ice cube trays. Freeze for at least 2 hours. Break the frozen cubes into small pieces and use in the Cozido Ceviche with a Mojito Twist. Ceviche is a versatile dish that invites experimentation and creativity. By combining traditional Peruvian ingredients with international flavors, we can create unique and exciting variations that showcase the diversity of this beloved seafood salad.
