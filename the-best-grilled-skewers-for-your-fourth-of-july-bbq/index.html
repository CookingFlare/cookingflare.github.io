The Best Grilled Skewers for Your Fourth of July BBQ


The Fourth of July is one of the most anticipated holidays of the year. It’s a time for fireworks, family gatherings, and, of course, an epic BBQ! Whether you’re hosting an all-American cookout in your backyard or enjoying a laid-back picnic in the park, the grill takes center stage. If you’re planning your menu, grilled skewers are a fantastic way to bring both variety and excitement to your BBQ spread.

Grilled skewers offer a unique and delicious twist on traditional BBQ fare. They’re easy to prepare, customizable, and packed with flavor. Whether you’re craving something meaty, vegetarian, or a little bit of both, there’s a skewer recipe that will suit everyone at your celebration. So, let’s dive into the best grilled skewers for your Fourth of July BBQ!

1. Classic BBQ Chicken Skewers

You can’t go wrong with the classic BBQ chicken skewers. Tender, juicy chicken breasts or thighs marinated in a smoky, sweet BBQ sauce and grilled to perfection will be the star of your BBQ table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or thighs)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (your favorite brand or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Cut the chicken into 1 to 1.5-inch cubes for even cooking. If you’re using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes to prevent burning on the grill.

  2. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, mix the BBQ sauce, olive oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken cubes and toss to coat. Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes, but for more flavor, marinate it for a few hours or overnight.

  3. Skewer the Chicken: Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the skewers, making sure not to overcrowd them.

  4. Grill the Skewers: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken skewers for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

  5. Serve: Remove the skewers from the grill and allow them to rest for a few minutes before serving. Serve with extra BBQ sauce on the side for dipping.

These BBQ chicken skewers are sure to be a crowd favorite. They’re simple to make and packed with smoky, sweet, and tangy flavors. Serve them with a side of grilled corn on the cob and a refreshing summer salad to complete your Fourth of July BBQ feast.

2. Grilled Steak Skewers with Chimichurri Sauce

For those who love a hearty, meaty skewer, grilled steak skewers are an excellent choice. The key to perfect steak skewers is marinating the meat in a flavorful marinade and pairing it with a zesty chimichurri sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs sirloin steak or flank steak
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

For the Chimichurri Sauce:

  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Steak: Cut the steak into 1-inch cubes. In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, soy sauce, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Add the steak cubes and toss to coat. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, but for the best flavor, let it marinate for a few hours or overnight.

  2. Make the Chimichurri Sauce: In a small bowl, combine the parsley, garlic, red wine vinegar, olive oil, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Mix well and set aside.

  3. Skewer the Steak: Thread the marinated steak cubes onto the skewers.

  4. Grill the Skewers: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the steak skewers for about 4-6 minutes per side, depending on your desired level of doneness. For medium-rare, aim for an internal temperature of 130°F (54°C).

  5. Serve: Once grilled to perfection, remove the skewers from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. Drizzle the chimichurri sauce over the skewers before serving.

These grilled steak skewers with chimichurri sauce are an elevated BBQ option that will impress your guests. The chimichurri adds a tangy and herbaceous kick that perfectly complements the rich, juicy steak.

3. Grilled Shrimp and Veggie Skewers

If you’re looking for a lighter option, shrimp skewers are a great way to add some seafood flair to your BBQ. Pairing shrimp with colorful vegetables makes for a balanced and visually appealing dish that’s full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Shrimp and Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine the shrimp, bell pepper, zucchini, and onion. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice. Add garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper, then toss to coat everything evenly.

  2. Skewer the Shrimp and Veggies: Thread the shrimp and vegetables onto the skewers, alternating between shrimp and veggies. If you’re using wooden skewers, remember to soak them in water for 30 minutes to prevent burning.

  3. Grill the Skewers: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the shrimp and veggie skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the shrimp turns pink and opaque, and the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.

  4. Serve: Once done, remove the skewers from the grill and serve immediately with extra lemon wedges on the side.

These shrimp and veggie skewers are light, fresh, and packed with flavor. They’re perfect for guests who prefer seafood or want something a little healthier at the BBQ.

4. Grilled Tofu Skewers with Peanut Sauce

For your vegetarian and vegan guests, tofu skewers are a delicious and satisfying option. Grilled tofu absorbs all the marinades and becomes wonderfully crispy on the outside while remaining soft and tender inside. Pairing it with a rich and creamy peanut sauce adds an extra layer of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cut into cubes
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

For the Peanut Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Tofu: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, olive oil, rice vinegar, sesame oil, maple syrup, garlic, salt, and pepper. Place the tofu cubes in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Let the tofu marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to a few hours for a deeper flavor.

  2. Make the Peanut Sauce: In a separate bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, maple syrup, warm water, and sriracha (if using). Adjust the water to get the desired consistency for dipping.

  3. Skewer the Tofu: Thread the marinated tofu onto the skewers, making sure the pieces are evenly spaced.

  4. Grill the Skewers: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Grill the tofu skewers for 3-4 minutes per side, or until they’re golden and slightly crispy on the outside.

  5. Serve: Drizzle the peanut sauce over the grilled tofu skewers or serve it on the side for dipping.

Grilled tofu skewers with peanut sauce are a flavorful and satisfying vegetarian BBQ option. The smoky, grilled tofu paired with the rich and creamy peanut sauce will have everyone coming back for more.

5. Grilled Veggie Skewers

If you’re looking for a fully plant-based option, grilled veggie skewers are the way to go. Packed with a colorful variety of vegetables, these skewers are not only delicious but also visually stunning. They can easily be customized to suit your favorite veggies and can be paired with a variety of marinades and dips.

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 8 oz mushrooms, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Veggies: Cut the vegetables into chunks or slices of similar sizes to ensure even cooking. If you’re using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes before grilling.

  2. Marinate the Veggies: In a large bowl, toss the veggies with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.

  3. Skewer the Veggies: Thread the veggies onto the skewers, alternating between different vegetables for a beautiful presentation.

  4. Grill the Skewers: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the veggie skewers for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.

  5. Serve: Serve the grilled veggie skewers as a side dish or as a main for your vegetarian or vegan guests.

Grilled veggie skewers are a healthy and delicious addition to your Fourth of July BBQ. The smoky flavors from the grill, combined with the tangy balsamic marinade, make these skewers a perfect option for a summer cookout.

Conclusion

Grilled skewers are an excellent way to add variety and excitement to your Fourth of July BBQ. Whether you’re serving up classic BBQ chicken, savory steak with chimichurri, fresh shrimp and veggies, crispy tofu, or vibrant veggie skewers, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and make your BBQ unforgettable.

Skewers are incredibly versatile, allowing you to customize them with your favorite ingredients and marinades. The best part? They’re easy to prepare and cook quickly, making them ideal for feeding a crowd. Pair them with refreshing sides like grilled corn on the cob, a tangy potato salad, or a light cucumber salad, and you’ve got a festive and flavorful Fourth of July spread.

So, fire up your grill, get creative with your skewer combinations, and enjoy the holiday with family, friends, and delicious food!
