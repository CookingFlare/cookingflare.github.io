Greek Style Baked Feta: A Simple and Delicious Appetizer


When it comes to easy yet impressive appetizers, Greek style baked feta is a standout choice. This dish combines the rich, creamy texture of feta cheese with aromatic herbs, vibrant vegetables, and a drizzle of olive oil, creating a mouthwatering starter that will transport your taste buds straight to the sunny shores of Greece. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, preparing for a festive gathering, or simply indulging in a cozy night at home, this baked feta recipe is a crowd-pleaser that requires minimal effort and maximum flavor.

Ingredients

For the Baked Feta:

  • 200g (7 oz) feta cheese (block)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional for a bit of heat)
  • **Freshly cracked black pepper to taste

For the Vegetables:

  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced (any color)
  • 1 small red onion, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Garnish:

  • Fresh basil or parsley, chopped
  • Crusty bread or pita chips, for serving

Equipment Needed

  • Baking dish or oven-safe skillet
  • Mixing bowl
  • Oven

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat Your Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). This temperature allows the feta to bake beautifully, becoming creamy while the vegetables roast to perfection.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables

In a mixing bowl, combine the halved cherry tomatoes, sliced bell pepper, sliced red onion, and minced garlic. Drizzle with 1 tablespoon of olive oil and 1 tablespoon of balsamic vinegar. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Toss everything together until the vegetables are well-coated.

Step 3: Arrange the Ingredients

In your baking dish or oven-safe skillet, create a nest for the feta cheese at the center. Place the feta block in the middle and surround it with the seasoned vegetables. Drizzle the remaining olive oil over the feta and sprinkle the dried oregano, thyme, crushed red pepper flakes, and freshly cracked black pepper on top.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30 minutes or until the feta is soft and slightly golden. The vegetables should be tender and fragrant, adding a delightful contrast to the creamy feta.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once out of the oven, let the dish cool for a couple of minutes. Garnish with freshly chopped basil or parsley for a pop of color and flavor. Serve warm with crusty bread or pita chips, allowing your guests to scoop up the melted feta and roasted veggies.

The Perfect Pairing

Greek style baked feta is delicious on its own, but it pairs wonderfully with a range of accompaniments. Consider serving it alongside:

  • Olive tapenade for a burst of briny flavor.
  • Hummus or tzatziki for a creamy contrast.
  • A light Greek salad featuring cucumbers, olives, and red onions.

Tips for Success

  • Choose High-Quality Feta: The flavor of your baked feta will depend heavily on the quality of the cheese. Opt for a good quality feta that is creamy and tangy.
  • Experiment with Vegetables: While cherry tomatoes and bell peppers are traditional, feel free to get creative. Zucchini, artichokes, or even olives can be excellent additions.
  • Adjust the Heat: If you enjoy a bit of spice, add more crushed red pepper flakes or serve with a spicy harissa sauce on the side.
  • Make it Ahead: You can prepare the vegetable mixture and arrange it around the feta in advance. Just cover and refrigerate until you’re ready to bake, making it a perfect make-ahead appetizer.

Nutritional Benefits

Not only is Greek style baked feta a delightful appetizer, but it also offers a range of nutritional benefits:

  • Feta Cheese: A good source of protein and calcium, feta is lower in fat than many other cheeses, making it a healthier alternative.
  • Olive Oil: Rich in heart-healthy monounsaturated fats, olive oil is known for its antioxidant properties.
  • Vegetables: The cherry tomatoes and bell peppers provide vitamins A and C, antioxidants, and fiber, making this dish a nutritious choice.

Conclusion

Greek style baked feta is a simple yet delicious appetizer that beautifully showcases the flavors of Mediterranean cuisine. Its ease of preparation and delightful taste make it a must-try for both novice cooks and seasoned chefs. Whether you’re enjoying it at a festive gathering or as a cozy evening treat, this baked feta dish will surely impress your guests and leave them asking for the recipe. So gather your ingredients, preheat the oven, and prepare to indulge in this mouthwatering appetizer that brings a taste of Greece to your table. Enjoy!
