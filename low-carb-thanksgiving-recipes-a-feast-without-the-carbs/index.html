Low Carb Thanksgiving Recipes: A Feast Without the Carbs


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering around the table with loved ones, expressing gratitude, and indulging in a delicious feast. But for those following a low-carb lifestyle, navigating this holiday can feel like a culinary challenge. Traditional staples like buttery mashed potatoes, sweet potato casseroles, and pumpkin pies are often brimming with carbs, but that doesn’t mean you have to miss out on the festivities. With a little creative thinking, you can create a low-carb Thanksgiving menu that’s just as flavorful and satisfying as the classic recipes.

In this blog post, we’ll share some standout dishes that deliver all the comforting flavors of Thanksgiving — minus the carb overload. From appetizers to sides, main courses, and desserts, there’s something here for everyone. These recipes are so delicious, your guests might not even notice the difference!

Appetizers to Get the Feast Started

Stuffed Mushrooms with Creamy Spinach and Cheese

Kick off your Thanksgiving celebration with these mouthwatering stuffed mushrooms. The bite-sized fun of this appetizer makes it perfect for mingling before dinner while being entirely low carb. Here’s how to make them:

Ingredients:

  • 20 large button mushrooms
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup cream cheese, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Clean the mushrooms and remove the stems, saving the caps for stuffing.
  3. Heat butter in a skillet over medium heat, sauté the spinach and garlic until wilted, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. In a bowl, combine the sautéed spinach, cream cheese, and mozzarella cheese.
  5. Spoon the cheese mixture into each mushroom cap and arrange them on a baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the stuffing is bubbling and golden brown.
  7. Serve warm and watch them disappear.

Irresistible Low-Carb Sides

Cauliflower Mashed “Potatoes”

One of the toughest sides to give up is mashed potatoes, but this creamy cauliflower alternative will satisfy any craving. With buttery richness and the same velvety texture, your guests might not be able to tell the difference.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, chopped into florets
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼ cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Steam the cauliflower florets in a large pot until tender, approximately 12–15 minutes.
  2. Transfer the cauliflower to a blender or food processor.
  3. Add the butter, garlic, heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, and seasonings.
  4. Blend until smooth and creamy. You can adjust the cream to achieve your desired texture.
  5. Serve hot with freshly chopped parsley sprinkled on top.

Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Pecans and Bacon

These caramelized Brussels sprouts are elevated by smoky bacon and crunchy pecans, creating a low-carb side dish that feels indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • ½ cup pecans, roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Spread the Brussels sprouts and chopped bacon on a baking sheet.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with pecans, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roast for 25–30 minutes, shaking the pan midway, until the Brussels sprouts are tender and the bacon is crispy.
  5. Serve warm and enjoy the perfect balance of savory and nutty flavors.

A Show-Stopping Main Course

No Thanksgiving table is complete without a centerpiece dish. While turkey is often the highlight, there are ways to enjoy it without sugary glazes or carb-laden stuffing.

Herb Butter Roasted Turkey

This roasted turkey is coated in a flavorful herb butter that keeps it juicy and aromatic without the need for extra carbs.

Ingredients:

  • 10–12 pound turkey, thawed and cleaned
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix the softened butter with the chopped herbs, lemon zest, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Gently loosen the skin on the turkey breast and thighs by sliding your fingers underneath, then rub the herb butter under the skin and over the exterior of the turkey.
  4. Place the turkey on a roasting pan with a rack and roast for 3–4 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) in the thickest part of the breast.
  5. Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving for perfectly juicy slices.

Low-Carb Desserts to Sweeten the Celebration

Many Thanksgiving desserts are laden with sugar, but there are plenty of ways to enjoy a sweet ending without breaking your low-carb goals.

Sugar-Free Pumpkin Pie

This pumpkin pie is everything you love about the classic dessert but without the carb-heavy crust or added sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups almond flour
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree (unsweetened)
  • ½ cup erythritol or your preferred sugar substitute
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix almond flour, coconut oil, and cinnamon to create the crust. Press the mixture into a pie pan.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, erythritol, eggs, heavy cream, vanilla extract, and pumpkin pie spice until smooth.
  4. Pour the filling into the prepared crust.
  5. Bake the pie for 45–50 minutes, or until set in the center.
  6. Allow the pie to cool completely, then refrigerate for 2 hours before serving.

Final Thoughts

Celebrating Thanksgiving on a low-carb diet doesn’t mean sacrificing the traditions or flavors that make the holiday special. With recipes like cauliflower mashed “potatoes,” roasted Brussels sprouts with pecans and bacon, and sugar-free pumpkin pie, your table will overflow with dishes that are as healthy as they are comforting.

This Thanksgiving, give thanks not just for family and friends, but also for the abundance of creative ways to enjoy your favorite foods in alignment with your dietary goals. Whether you’re hosting a dinner or contributing one dish to a potluck, these recipes are sure to be a hit. They’ll leave everyone satisfied, grateful, and none the wiser about their low-carb adjustments.
