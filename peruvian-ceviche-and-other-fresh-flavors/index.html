Peruvian Ceviche and Other Fresh Flavors


Ceviche, a traditional dish from Peru, is a refreshing and flavorful seafood delicacy that has gained popularity worldwide. Made with fresh raw fish or seafood, marinated in citrus juices, and mixed with various ingredients, Peruvian ceviche is a true explosion of flavors. In this blog post, we will explore the art of making Peruvian ceviche and other fresh flavors that will tantalize your taste buds.

The Art of Peruvian Ceviche

Peruvian ceviche is all about freshness and balance. The key ingredients are the fish or seafood, lime juice, onions, and aji amarillo, a Peruvian yellow chili pepper. The fish is typically cut into bite-sized pieces and marinated in lime juice, which effectively “cooks” the fish, giving it a tender and succulent texture.

To make the marinade, combine the lime juice, finely chopped onions, aji amarillo paste, salt, and pepper in a bowl. The aji amarillo adds a mild heat and vibrant color to the ceviche. Let the fish marinate in the lime juice mixture for about 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Once the fish is “cooked” by the lime juice, it’s time to add the finishing touches. Garnish the ceviche with chopped cilantro, diced tomatoes, and avocado slices. The cilantro adds a fresh and herbaceous note, while the tomatoes and avocado provide a creamy and refreshing contrast to the tangy ceviche.

Serve the Peruvian ceviche chilled and enjoy it with crunchy corn kernels, known as cancha, and sweet potato slices. The combination of textures and flavors in this dish is truly sensational.

Exploring Other Fresh Flavors

Peru is known for its diverse culinary traditions, and there are plenty of other fresh flavors to explore beyond ceviche. Here are a few dishes that you should try:

1. Lomo Saltado

Lomo saltado is a popular Peruvian stir-fry dish that combines tender strips of beef, onions, tomatoes, and french fries. The beef is marinated in soy sauce and vinegar, giving it a savory and tangy flavor. This dish showcases the fusion of Peruvian and Chinese culinary influences.

2. Tiradito

Tiradito is often referred to as the “sibling” of ceviche. It is made with thinly sliced raw fish, similar to sashimi, and marinated in a spicy citrus sauce. The sauce is made with aji amarillo, lime juice, and garlic, creating a zesty and fiery flavor profile.

3. Anticuchos

Anticuchos are skewered and grilled beef heart or chicken marinated in a spicy sauce. This popular street food is packed with smoky and bold flavors. The marinade typically includes aji panca, a Peruvian red chili pepper paste, cumin, garlic, and vinegar.

4. Causa Limeña

Causa Limeña is a traditional Peruvian dish made with layers of mashed yellow potatoes and a filling of tuna, chicken, or avocado. The layers are typically seasoned with lime juice, aji amarillo, and olive oil, creating a vibrant and tangy flavor.

5. Papa a la Huancaina

Papa a la Huancaina is a classic Peruvian appetizer made with boiled yellow potatoes covered in a creamy and spicy cheese sauce. The sauce is made with aji amarillo, queso fresco, evaporated milk, and saltine crackers. It is typically served over lettuce leaves and garnished with black olives and hard-boiled eggs.

Final Thoughts

Peruvian cuisine is a treasure trove of fresh flavors and culinary delights. From the tangy and refreshing ceviche to the bold and smoky anticuchos, there is something for everyone to enjoy. So, grab your apron, explore the vibrant flavors of Peru, and embark on a culinary journey like no other.
