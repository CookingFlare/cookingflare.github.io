Buttermilk Fried Chicken: Crispy, Juicy, and Oh-So-Comforting


There’s something undeniably satisfying about biting into a perfectly cooked piece of fried chicken. The crispy, golden exterior gives way to tender, juicy meat that is pure comfort food bliss. And when it comes to achieving that irresistible combination of flavors and textures, buttermilk fried chicken reigns supreme. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to create the ultimate buttermilk fried chicken that will have your taste buds dancing with joy.

Ingredients

To make this mouthwatering buttermilk fried chicken, you’ll need:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces (or your preferred chicken parts)
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, pour the buttermilk over the chicken pieces, making sure they are fully submerged. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight. This step is crucial as it helps tenderize the chicken and infuse it with flavor.

  2. In a shallow dish, mix together the flour, salt, black pepper, paprika, and garlic powder. This seasoned flour will give the chicken its signature crispy coating.

  3. Remove the chicken from the buttermilk, allowing any excess liquid to drip off. Dredge each piece in the seasoned flour, making sure to coat it evenly on all sides. Place the coated chicken on a baking sheet and let it rest for 15 minutes. This resting period allows the coating to adhere to the chicken, resulting in a crunchier crust.

  4. Meanwhile, heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or deep fryer to a temperature of 350°F (175°C). The amount of oil should be enough to fully submerge the chicken pieces.

  5. Carefully place the chicken in the hot oil, taking care not to overcrowd the skillet or fryer. Fry the chicken in batches, if necessary. Cook for about 15-20 minutes, flipping the pieces occasionally, until they are golden brown and cooked through. To ensure the chicken is fully cooked, use a meat thermometer to check that the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, transfer it to a wire rack or paper towels to drain any excess oil. Allow the chicken to rest for a few minutes before serving to ensure maximum juiciness.

  7. Serve your buttermilk fried chicken hot and enjoy it with your favorite sides. Whether it’s creamy mashed potatoes, tangy coleslaw, or fluffy biscuits, this chicken is sure to be the star of the meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra flavorful twist, add spices like cayenne pepper, onion powder, or dried herbs to the seasoned flour.
  • If you’re short on time, you can marinate the chicken in the buttermilk for at least 30 minutes before coating it in the seasoned flour. While overnight marination yields the best results, a shorter marination time will still enhance the flavor and tenderness of the chicken.
  • Feel free to experiment with different cuts of chicken or boneless chicken tenders. Adjust the cooking time accordingly to ensure they are cooked through.
  • To make your fried chicken even crispier, you can double-dip the chicken pieces in the seasoned flour. After the initial coating, dip the chicken back into the buttermilk and then into the flour again before frying.
  • Leftover fried chicken can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat it in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10-15 minutes to maintain its crispy texture.

Now that you have the secret to creating crispy, juicy, and oh-so-comforting buttermilk fried chicken, it’s time to roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure. Get ready to enjoy a mouthwatering meal that will leave you craving seconds!
