Christmas Breakfast Recipes for a Special Morning


Christmas morning is one of the most cherished times of the year, filled with joy, love, and the excitement of the holiday season. While the presents under the tree steal the spotlight, the breakfast table should not be overlooked. A special Christmas breakfast brings family and friends together to celebrate the start of the day in a delicious and memorable way. Whether you prefer sweet or savory dishes, traditional or contemporary recipes, there’s something for everyone. Below, we’ve gathered a collection of delightful Christmas breakfast recipes that will make your morning extra special.

1. Cinnamon Roll Casserole

If you love the rich, warm flavors of cinnamon rolls but don’t want to spend all morning assembling them, this cinnamon roll casserole is the perfect solution. It combines all the best elements of cinnamon rolls into one easy-to-make, mouth-watering casserole that’s perfect for Christmas morning.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of refrigerated cinnamon rolls (with icing)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1/4 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup of chopped pecans (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  3. Open the cinnamon roll cans and cut each roll into quarters.
  4. Layer the cinnamon rolls in the prepared baking dish.
  5. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, heavy cream, vanilla extract, cinnamon, salt, and brown sugar until smooth.
  6. Pour the mixture over the cinnamon rolls, making sure they are evenly coated.
  7. If using, sprinkle chopped pecans on top for an added crunch.
  8. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the casserole is golden brown and set.
  9. Drizzle the included icing over the casserole while it’s still warm.
  10. Serve with a hot cup of coffee or hot chocolate and enjoy!

Why It’s Perfect for Christmas Morning:

This casserole is a time-saver and still gives you that homemade cinnamon roll taste. The rich, gooey texture and warm spices are perfect for a cozy holiday breakfast.

2. Christmas Morning Pancakes

Pancakes are a breakfast staple, and adding a festive twist makes them even more special. These Christmas Morning Pancakes are light, fluffy, and studded with cranberries and white chocolate chips. The combination of tart and sweet is a match made in heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup white chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; it’s okay if the batter is a little lumpy.
  4. Gently fold in the cranberries and white chocolate chips.
  5. Heat a griddle or large skillet over medium heat and grease with a little butter or cooking spray.
  6. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the griddle for each pancake, cooking for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown.
  7. Serve with maple syrup, fresh berries, and a dusting of powdered sugar.

Why It’s Perfect for Christmas Morning:

The bright red cranberries and white chocolate chips give these pancakes a festive look and flavor. They’re sure to be a hit with both kids and adults, making them a perfect choice for your Christmas breakfast spread.

3. Bacon-Wrapped Breakfast Sausage

For those who enjoy a savory breakfast, bacon-wrapped breakfast sausage is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. The combination of smoky bacon and juicy sausage is irresistibly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 12 breakfast sausage links
  • 12 slices of bacon
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Wrap each sausage link with a slice of bacon and secure with a toothpick.
  3. Place the bacon-wrapped sausages on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the bacon is crispy and the sausage is cooked through.
  5. Optionally, drizzle with maple syrup and sprinkle with black pepper before serving.
  6. Serve alongside scrambled eggs and toast for a hearty breakfast.

Why It’s Perfect for Christmas Morning:

This savory dish is simple yet indulgent, and the maple syrup adds a touch of sweetness that pairs wonderfully with the saltiness of the bacon and sausage. It’s the perfect balance of flavors to start your day.

4. French Toast with Warm Apple Cinnamon Topping

French toast is a holiday classic that never goes out of style. For Christmas, elevate this dish with a warm, spiced apple topping that brings all the flavors of the season into one bite.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of thick-cut bread (brioche or challah works best)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, whisk together the eggs, milk, cinnamon, vanilla extract, and salt.
  2. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat and melt 1 tablespoon of butter.
  3. Dip each slice of bread into the egg mixture, making sure both sides are coated.
  4. Cook the bread in the skillet for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. In a separate pan, melt the remaining tablespoon of butter over medium heat.
  6. Add the sliced apples, brown sugar, cinnamon, and lemon juice. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the apples are tender and caramelized.
  7. Serve the French toast topped with the warm apple mixture and a dusting of powdered sugar.

Why It’s Perfect for Christmas Morning:

The warm, spiced apples create a festive topping for the French toast, while the cinnamon and vanilla in the batter add a cozy flavor. This dish is sweet, comforting, and perfect for a holiday breakfast.

5. Veggie and Cheese Breakfast Casserole

For a lighter, savory option, this veggie and cheese breakfast casserole is an excellent choice. Packed with fresh vegetables, eggs, and cheese, it’s a satisfying dish that can be prepped the night before, so you can enjoy a stress-free morning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (optional for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, spinach, and mushrooms. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until softened.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Stir in the sautéed vegetables and shredded cheese.
  5. Pour the mixture into a greased 9x9-inch baking dish.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the top is golden.
  7. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve warm.

Why It’s Perfect for Christmas Morning:

This casserole is nutritious and filling, with a perfect balance of vegetables and cheese. It’s an easy way to get everyone’s veggies in while still enjoying a flavorful dish that feels festive.

6. Cranberry Orange Muffins

These cranberry orange muffins are a wonderful addition to your Christmas breakfast table. The tartness of the cranberries and the zesty orange flavor create a refreshing combination that balances out the richness of other holiday treats.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2/3 cup buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh cranberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the melted butter, buttermilk, eggs, vanilla extract, and orange zest.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. Gently fold in the cranberries.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Allow the muffins to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Why It’s Perfect for Christmas Morning:

These muffins are light, moist, and full of festive flavors. The cranberry and orange combo is a seasonal favorite that pairs beautifully with a warm drink.

Conclusion

A Christmas breakfast doesn’t have to be complicated to be extraordinary. From sweet options like cinnamon roll casserole and French toast to savory dishes like bacon-wrapped sausages and veggie casseroles, there’s something to please everyone. Whether you’re serving a crowd or enjoying a quiet breakfast with your family, these recipes will help you create a morning filled with warmth, joy, and deliciousness. So, gather your loved ones, make a pot of coffee, and enjoy these Christmas breakfast dishes that will make your holiday morning truly special!
