Provolone and Prosciutto Flatbread: A Taste of Italy


If you’re a fan of Italian cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this flavorful and easy-to-make Provolone and Prosciutto Flatbread recipe. This dish perfectly combines the rich, creamy flavor of provolone cheese with the savory, delicate taste of prosciutto, all on top of a crispy flatbread crust. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight meal or an impressive appetizer for your next gathering, this recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings for a taste of Italy.

Ingredients

For the Flatbread:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the Toppings:

  • 8 ounces provolone cheese, thinly sliced or shredded
  • 4 ounces prosciutto, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Balsamic glaze for drizzling (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Flatbread Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Gradually add the warm water and olive oil to the dry ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Step 2: Preheat the Oven

  1. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C) and place a pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven to heat up.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

  1. After the dough has rested, divide it into two equal portions.
  2. On a floured surface, roll out each portion of dough into a thin oval or rectangular shape, about 1/4 inch thick.

Step 4: Assemble the Flatbreads

  1. Place the rolled-out dough on a piece of parchment paper.
  2. Brush the surface of each flatbread with olive oil and sprinkle minced garlic evenly over the top.
  3. Arrange the provolone cheese slices or shredded provolone evenly over the flatbreads.
  4. Tear the prosciutto slices into smaller pieces and distribute them over the provolone.

Step 5: Bake the Flatbreads

  1. Carefully transfer the parchment paper with the assembled flatbreads onto the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet.
  2. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the flatbreads are golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and melted.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

  1. Remove the flatbreads from the oven and sprinkle freshly grated Parmesan cheese over the top.
  2. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and drizzle with balsamic glaze, if desired.
  3. Slice the flatbreads into wedges and serve immediately.

Enjoy the Flavors of Italy

With its irresistible combination of provolone and prosciutto, this flatbread is a delightful homage to the flavors of Italy. The creamy, slightly tangy provolone pairs beautifully with the salty, savory prosciutto, creating a harmonious blend of textures and tastes. Whether you enjoy it as an appetizer, a light lunch, or a flavorful snack, this Provolone and Prosciutto Flatbread is a versatile and crowd-pleasing dish that will transport your taste buds to the heart of Italy.

So, the next time you’re craving a taste of Italy, whip up this simple yet elegant flatbread and savor the delicious flavors that make Italian cuisine so beloved around the world. Buon appetito!
