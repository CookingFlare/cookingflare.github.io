The Best Easter Cakes: Easy Recipes to Make at Home


Easter is a time of joy, renewal, and celebration, and what better way to mark the occasion than with a delicious homemade cake? Whether you’re hosting a family gathering or just want to indulge in a sweet treat to celebrate spring, Easter cakes are a fabulous way to bring festive cheer to your table. From classic carrot and lemon flavors to more inventive options, there’s a cake recipe that will delight every palate—and all are surprisingly easy to make from scratch.

In this post, we’ll explore five standout Easter cake recipes that anyone can bake at home. Each recipe includes simple ingredients, straightforward steps, and tips for achieving perfect results. These cakes not only taste fantastic but also look beautiful, making your holiday feast all the more memorable.

1. Classic Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cake is a perennial favorite for Easter, combining natural sweetness with a moist texture and rich cream cheese frosting. It’s a crowd-pleaser that brings nostalgia and freshness together perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 cups finely grated carrots
  • ½ cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, combine sugar and oil. Beat in eggs, one at a time, then stir in vanilla.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mix, stirring until just combined.
  5. Fold in grated carrots and walnuts if using.
  6. Divide the batter evenly between the two pans and bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow cakes to cool completely before frosting.
  8. For the frosting, beat cream cheese and butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla, beating until fluffy.
  9. Frost the cooled layers, stacking to create a classic layered carrot cake.

Tips:

  • For extra moisture, soak the grated carrots in pineapple juice for 10 minutes.
  • Toast walnuts before adding to enhance their flavor.
  • Decorate with chopped nuts or edible flowers for a springtime touch.

2. Zesty Lemon Poppy Seed Cake

Light, bright, and refreshing, lemon poppy seed cake is perfect for Easter brunch or a lighter dessert option. The poppy seeds add a lovely crunch, while the lemon glaze gives a tangy finish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest (from about 2 lemons)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 3 tablespoons poppy seeds

Lemon Glaze:

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 3-4 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 10-inch bundt pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in eggs one at a time, then add lemon zest and vanilla.
  5. Alternately add flour mixture and buttermilk to the batter, starting and ending with flour mixture.
  6. Stir in poppy seeds.
  7. Pour batter into bundt pan and bake for 55-65 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Let the cake cool in the pan for 15 minutes, then invert onto a rack to cool completely.
  9. Mix powdered sugar with lemon juice to make glaze and drizzle generously over the cooled cake.

Tips:

  • Use fresh lemons for the zest and juice to get the best flavor.
  • Buttermilk can be substituted with milk mixed with 1 tablespoon of vinegar or lemon juice.
  • For a more intense lemon flavor, add a teaspoon of lemon extract to the batter.

3. Coconut Vanilla Easter Cake

This cake brings the tropical sweetness of coconut paired with smooth vanilla, creating a moist and fragrant dessert that’s both simple and elegant.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ¾ cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup shredded sweetened coconut

Coconut Cream Frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3-4 cups powdered sugar
  • ¼ cup coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup shredded coconut for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cream butter and sugar until fluffy.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla.
  5. Alternately add the dry ingredients and coconut milk, mixing until combined.
  6. Fold in shredded coconut.
  7. Pour batter evenly into prepared pans and bake 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  8. Cool completely before frosting.
  9. For frosting, beat butter until creamy. Gradually add powdered sugar, then coconut milk and vanilla until fluffy.
  10. Spread frosting between layers and over the cake. Sprinkle shredded coconut on top.

Tips:

  • Toast shredded coconut lightly for a nuttier flavor on top.
  • Substitute coconut milk with regular milk for a less intense coconut taste.
  • Chill the cake before serving for the frosting to set nicely.

4. Rich Chocolate Hazelnut Cake

If you want an indulgent cake centerpiece for Easter, this chocolate hazelnut cake delivers deep flavors with a velvety texture. It’s a great alternative to traditional Easter sweets with a hint of sophistication.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup hot water
  • ½ cup chopped hazelnuts

Hazelnut Ganache Frosting:

  • 8 oz dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup ground hazelnuts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round pans.
  2. Whisk together flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another large bowl, combine milk, oil, granulated sugar, brown sugar, eggs, and vanilla.
  4. Add dry ingredients into wet, mix until smooth.
  5. Stir in hot water slowly; batter will be thin.
  6. Fold hazelnuts gently into batter.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool cakes completely.
  9. For ganache, heat cream until simmering and pour over chopped chocolate. Stir until smooth.
  10. Let ganache cool slightly, then mix in ground hazelnuts.
  11. Frost the cake evenly with ganache.

Tips:

  • Toast hazelnuts beforehand for enhanced flavor.
  • Serve with fresh berries or whipped cream for added texture.
  • Ganache can be kept thicker by cooling longer or adding more chocolate.

5. Strawberry Almond Easter Cake

Sweet strawberries and nutty almonds come together in this fresh and colorful cake that’s perfect for spring celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ¾ cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup sliced fresh strawberries
  • ½ cup sliced almonds

Strawberry Buttercream Frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • ½ cup pureed fresh strawberries (strained to remove seeds)
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch cake pans.
  2. Whisk flour, baking powder, and salt together.
  3. Cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, then beat in almond extract.
  5. Add flour mixture alternately with milk, starting and ending with flour.
  6. Gently fold in sliced strawberries.
  7. Divide batter evenly and sprinkle sliced almonds on top before baking.
  8. Bake for 30-35 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.
  9. Cool cakes completely.
  10. For frosting, beat butter until creamy, then gradually add powdered sugar, strawberry puree, and almond extract until smooth.
  11. Frost cake layers and decorate with fresh strawberries and almonds.

Tips:

  • Use ripe, firm strawberries for the best flavor.
  • Add a dollop of strawberry jam between layers for extra fruitiness.
  • For a moist cake, brush layers with a bit of almond syrup before frosting.

Final Thoughts: Baking Joy into Easter

Easter is made sweeter with the simple pleasure of homemade cake. These recipes range from classic to creative and suit both novice bakers and seasoned pros. Each cake embodies the vibrant spirit of spring and the festive mood of the holiday, guaranteed to bring smiles and delight at any gathering.

When baking your Easter cake at home, consider the preferences of your guests, the ambiance you want to create, and your personal cravings. Whether it’s the warming spices of carrot cake, the citrus punch of lemon poppy seed, or the indulgence of chocolate hazelnut, these recipes will help you create a centerpiece worthy of the holiday.

Enjoy the process—baking is an act of love and celebration. Then sit back with a slice of your creation and savor the sweet taste of Easter made at home.
