Homemade Oreo Ice Cream: A Cookie Lover's Dream


If you’re a fan of cookies and cream, then homemade Oreo ice cream is going to be your new favorite frozen treat. This indulgent dessert combines the classic flavors of Oreo cookies with rich, creamy ice cream for a delightful and satisfying experience. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering, celebrating a special occasion, or simply treating yourself to a sweet delight, this homemade Oreo ice cream recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 20 Oreo cookies, crushed

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Oreo Cookies: Begin by placing the Oreo cookies in a zip-top bag and crushing them into small pieces using a rolling pin or the back of a spoon. You want to achieve a mix of fine crumbs and small cookie chunks for texture.

  2. Whip the Cream: In a large mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This will give your ice cream a light and airy texture. Be careful not to over-whip the cream, as it can turn into butter if beaten for too long.

  3. Add Sweetened Condensed Milk and Vanilla: Gently fold in the sweetened condensed milk and vanilla extract into the whipped cream. The sweetened condensed milk provides the necessary sweetness and creaminess, while the vanilla extract enhances the overall flavor profile.

  4. Incorporate the Crushed Oreo Cookies: Once the cream, sweetened condensed milk, and vanilla are combined, gently fold in the crushed Oreo cookies. Ensure that the cookies are evenly distributed throughout the mixture for a consistent cookie flavor in every bite.

  5. Transfer to a Container: Pour the ice cream mixture into a freezer-safe container. Smooth the top with a spatula to create an even surface.

  6. Freeze Until Firm: Cover the container with a lid or plastic wrap and place it in the freezer. Allow the ice cream to freeze for at least 6 hours, or preferably overnight, to ensure a firm and scoopable consistency.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the ice cream is fully frozen, scoop it into bowls or cones and garnish with additional Oreo cookie crumbs if desired. This homemade Oreo ice cream is best enjoyed fresh, but can be stored in the freezer for up to two weeks.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent twist, consider adding a swirl of chocolate fudge or caramel sauce to the ice cream mixture before freezing.
  • Experiment with different Oreo cookie flavors, such as mint, peanut butter, or red velvet, to create unique variations of homemade Oreo ice cream.
  • To achieve a cookies and cream milkshake, simply blend the frozen Oreo ice cream with a splash of milk until smooth and creamy.

Conclusion

Homemade Oreo ice cream is a delightful and easy-to-make dessert that will surely impress your family and friends. With its rich and creamy texture, combined with the irresistible crunch of Oreo cookies, this frozen treat is a true delight for cookie lovers. Whether you’re enjoying it on a hot summer day or as a sweet ending to a special meal, this homemade Oreo ice cream is a must-try for anyone with a love for cookies and cream.

Give this recipe a try and indulge in the ultimate cookie lover’s dream – homemade Oreo ice cream!
