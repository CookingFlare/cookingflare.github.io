Fluffy and Moist Pineapple Upside-Down Cake Recipe


If you’re looking for a delightful dessert that’s both visually stunning and incredibly delicious, then you’ve come to the right place. This fluffy and moist pineapple upside-down cake is a timeless classic that never fails to impress. With its caramelized pineapple topping and tender cake crumb, this dessert is a true crowd-pleaser. So, let’s dive into the recipe and learn how to create this mouthwatering treat!

Ingredients:

For the Topping:

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 can (20 ounces) pineapple slices in juice, drained
  • Maraschino cherries (optional)

For the Cake:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice (reserved from the canned pineapple)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Topping:

    • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
    • Melt the butter and pour it into a 9-inch round cake pan, ensuring that the bottom is evenly coated.
    • Sprinkle the brown sugar over the melted butter, spreading it evenly.
    • Arrange the pineapple slices on top of the brown sugar. You can place a maraschino cherry in the center of each pineapple slice if desired.

  2. Prepare the Cake Batter:

    • In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
    • In a separate large bowl, whisk the eggs and granulated sugar until smooth and creamy.
    • Gradually add the vegetable oil, vanilla extract, and pineapple juice to the egg mixture, whisking until well combined.
    • Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter.

  3. Assemble and Bake:

    • Carefully pour the cake batter over the pineapple slices in the cake pan, spreading it into an even layer.

  4. Bake the Cake:

    • Place the cake pan in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean.

  5. Cool and Invert:

    • Once the cake is done, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for 5-10 minutes.
    • Place a serving plate over the cake pan, then carefully invert the cake onto the plate. Let the cake pan remain on top of the cake for a few minutes to allow the topping to release.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Gently lift off the cake pan, revealing the beautifully caramelized pineapple topping. Allow the cake to cool completely before slicing and serving.

Tips and Variations:

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding a sprinkle of ground cinnamon or a splash of rum to the topping.
  • If you prefer a denser cake, you can use a pound cake or butter cake recipe as the base for the pineapple upside-down cake.
  • Experiment with different fruit variations such as peaches, mangoes, or even mixed berries to create unique twists on the classic recipe.

Conclusion:

This fluffy and moist pineapple upside-down cake is a true delight for both the eyes and the taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a special occasion or simply craving a sweet treat, this recipe is sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to bake up a slice of tropical paradise in the form of a delectable pineapple upside-down cake. Happy baking!
