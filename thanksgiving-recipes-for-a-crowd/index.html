Thanksgiving Recipes for a Crowd


Thanksgiving is a time for coming together with family and friends to celebrate and feast on delicious food. If you’re hosting a crowd this Thanksgiving and are looking for recipes that will satisfy everyone’s taste buds, you’ve come to the right place. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or just starting out in the kitchen, these crowd-pleasing Thanksgiving recipes are sure to impress your guests.

Roast Turkey with Herb Butter

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (about 20 pounds)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F.
  2. In a bowl, mix the softened butter with the chopped herbs, salt, and pepper.
  3. Carefully loosen the skin of the turkey and spread the herb butter under the skin and all over the turkey.
  4. Roast the turkey in the oven for about 4 to 5 hours, basting occasionally, until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  5. Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving.

Classic Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 5 pounds potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the diced potatoes in a large pot of salted water until tender.
  2. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.
  3. Heat the cream and butter in a saucepan until the butter is melted.
  4. Mash the potatoes while gradually adding the cream mixture until smooth.
  5. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

Cranberry Pecan Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of bread, cubed and toasted
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 cup dried cranberries
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 stalks of celery, diced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, sauté the onion and celery in butter until softened.
  2. Combine the toasted bread cubes, pecans, cranberries, sage, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
  3. Pour the sautéed vegetables over the bread mixture and toss to combine.
  4. Gradually add the chicken broth until the stuffing is moist but not soggy.
  5. Transfer the stuffing to a baking dish and bake at 350°F for 30-40 minutes.

Pumpkin Pie with Whipped Cream

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust
  • 1 can (15 ounces) pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon cloves
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F.
  2. In a bowl, mix the pumpkin puree, sugar, spices, eggs, and cream until smooth.
  3. Pour the mixture into the pie crust and bake for 15 minutes.
  4. Reduce the oven temperature to 350°F and continue baking for 40-50 minutes, or until the filling is set.
  5. Let the pie cool before serving with whipped cream.

These Thanksgiving recipes are perfect for feeding a crowd and will surely be a hit at your holiday gathering. From the succulent roast turkey to the creamy mashed potatoes and flavorful stuffing, your guests will be raving about the meal for days to come. Don’t forget to end the feast on a sweet note with a slice of homemade pumpkin pie topped with luscious whipped cream. Wishing you a wonderful Thanksgiving filled with great food and even better company!
