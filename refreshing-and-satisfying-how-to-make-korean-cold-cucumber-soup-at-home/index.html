Refreshing and Satisfying: How to Make Korean Cold Cucumber Soup at Home


Are you looking for a refreshing and satisfying dish to beat the summer heat? Look no further than Korean Cold Cucumber Soup, also known as Oi Naengguk. This chilled soup is not only delicious but also incredibly easy to make at home. With its crisp cucumbers, tangy vinegar, and savory flavors, it’s the perfect dish to cool you down on a hot day.

Ingredients

To make this mouthwatering Korean Cold Cucumber Soup, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 large cucumbers
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Ice cubes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making this refreshing Korean Cold Cucumber Soup:

  1. Peel the cucumbers and slice them into thin rounds. If you prefer a chunkier texture, you can cut them into small cubes instead. Place the cucumber slices into a large mixing bowl.

  2. Add the minced garlic, sliced green onions, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, Korean red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper to the bowl with the cucumbers.

  3. Mix all the ingredients together, ensuring that the cucumbers are well coated with the seasonings. Let the mixture sit for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  4. Pour the cold water into the bowl and give everything a good stir. If you like your soup extra cold, you can add a few ice cubes to the mixture.

  5. Taste the soup and adjust the seasonings according to your preference. If you want it spicier, add more Korean red pepper flakes. If you prefer it tangier, add more rice vinegar. Don’t be afraid to experiment and make it your own!

  6. Once you’re satisfied with the taste, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to develop and the soup to chill.

  7. When you’re ready to serve, give the soup a final stir and ladle it into individual bowls. Garnish with some extra sliced green onions for a pop of color and freshness.

  8. Enjoy your homemade Korean Cold Cucumber Soup! Serve it as an appetizer or a light lunch on a hot summer day. The crispness of the cucumbers, combined with the tangy and savory flavors, will leave you feeling refreshed and satisfied.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the traditional recipe, you can add some shredded carrots, radishes, or even thinly sliced apple for added crunch and sweetness.
  • If you prefer a creamier texture, you can blend a portion of the soup and mix it back into the chunky base.
  • Customize the spice level by adjusting the amount of Korean red pepper flakes. If you’re not a fan of spice, you can omit it altogether.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to create your own unique flavor profile. Fresh mint or cilantro can add a refreshing twist, while a dash of ginger can add a subtle kick.

Conclusion

Korean Cold Cucumber Soup is a delightful dish that embodies the flavors of summer. With its cooling properties and satisfying taste, it’s the perfect addition to your repertoire of refreshing recipes. Whether you’re entertaining guests or simply looking for a light and healthy meal, this soup will not disappoint. So grab your cucumbers, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy this Korean culinary delight in the comfort of your own home.
