Hearty Winter Scones for a Delicious Brunch


Winter is here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to cozy up with a warm and comforting brunch. There’s nothing quite like a fresh-baked scone, straight from the oven, to brighten up a chilly winter morning. And today, we’re going to share with you a recipe for Hearty Winter Scones that will become a staple in your kitchen.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans (optional)

Instructions

Now that we have our ingredients, let’s move on to the instructions.

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone mat.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cardamom.
  3. Add the cold butter to the dry ingredients and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the butter into the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and eggs until well combined.
  5. Pour the cream and egg mixture into the dry ingredients and stir until a shaggy dough forms.
  6. Fold in the chopped cranberries and pecans (if using).
  7. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together in a cohesive mass.
  8. Pat the dough into a circle that’s about 1 inch (2.5 cm) thick.
  9. Use a biscuit cutter or the rim of a glass to cut out scones. You should be able to get about 8-10 scones.
  10. Place the scones on the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of space between each scone.
  11. Brush the tops of the scones with a little extra heavy cream and sprinkle with granulated sugar.
  12. Bake the scones for 20-25 minutes, or until they’re golden brown.
  13. Remove the scones from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack for 5-10 minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations

As with any scone recipe, there are a few tips and variations to keep in mind:

  • Make sure to use cold ingredients, including the butter and cream. This will help the scones to rise and give them a tender texture.
  • Don’t overmix the dough. This can cause the scones to become tough and dense.
  • If you want to add a little extra flavor to your scones, try substituting some of the heavy cream with buttermilk or sour cream.
  • You can also add other mix-ins to the dough, such as dried cranberries, chopped apples, or even chocolate chips.
  • To make ahead, you can prepare the dough up to the point where you shape the scones, then refrigerate it overnight and bake the scones in the morning.

Conclusion

And there you have it - a recipe for Hearty Winter Scones that’s sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. With their tender texture, sweet flavor, and crunchy topping, these scones are the perfect way to start your day. So why not give them a try and see what you think? We hope you enjoy them as much as we do. Happy baking!
