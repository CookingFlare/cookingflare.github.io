How to Grill Sweet Potatoes: Tips for a Healthy Side Dish


Sweet potatoes are a versatile and nutrient-packed addition to any meal, and grilling them is one of the tastiest ways to bring out their natural sweetness with a hint of smoky char. Whether you’re preparing a backyard barbecue, cooking for a weeknight dinner, or hosting a holiday gathering, grilled sweet potatoes make a healthy and flavorful side dish that pairs well with nearly anything.

This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about grilling sweet potatoes, including preparation, seasoning ideas, cooking tips, and creative variations. By the end, you’ll have all the tools you need to make grilled sweet potatoes a go-to dish in your cooking repertoire.

Why Grilled Sweet Potatoes Are a Must-Try

Sweet potatoes are a nutritional powerhouse, rich in vitamins A and C, potassium, fiber, and antioxidants, making them a healthy choice for any diet. Grilling sweet potatoes enhances their natural flavors by caramelizing their sugars, resulting in a lightly crisp, smoky exterior that complements their tender, creamy interior.

Grilled sweet potatoes also offer versatility. From sweet to savory seasonings, the possibilities are nearly endless. They’re naturally gluten-free, vegetarian, and can easily be made vegan, making them an inclusive option for diverse dietary preferences.

Choosing the Right Sweet Potatoes

Before you start grilling, you’ll need to pick the right sweet potatoes for the best flavor and texture. Look for medium-sized sweet potatoes with smooth, tight skin and an even shape to ensure consistent cooking. Avoid sweet potatoes with bruises, soft spots, or blemishes, as these can indicate spoilage or uneven cooking results.

In terms of variety, classic orange-fleshed sweet potatoes work beautifully for grilling, as they strike the perfect balance between sweetness and creaminess. Purple-fleshed or white sweet potatoes are also great options if you’re looking to try something unique.

Preparing Sweet Potatoes for the Grill

Washing and Peeling

Start by scrubbing the sweet potatoes thoroughly under running water. Sweet potatoes grow in the ground, so they often have dirt on their skin. While peeling is optional, leaving the skin on can add texture and flavor, as well as extra nutrients. If you prefer a softer texture, feel free to peel them.

Cutting for Consistent Cooking

Proper slicing is crucial to ensure even cooking on the grill. Sweet potatoes can be cut into slices, wedges, rounds, or even cubes, depending on your preference and intended presentation. If you’re short on time, thinner slices or smaller wedges will cook faster. Aim for pieces that are about ½ to ¾ inch thick for even cooking without the risk of burning.

Pre-Cooking to Save Time

Sweet potatoes are dense and take longer to cook than many vegetables. While grilling them raw is an option, it can result in long cook times and uneven doneness. For more consistent results, consider pre-cooking them.

Par-Boiling

Boiling the sweet potatoes briefly before grilling softens their interior, reducing the overall grill time. Place the cut sweet potatoes in a pot of boiling water and cook for 5-7 minutes until fork-tender but not falling apart. Drain and pat them dry before seasoning.

Steaming or Microwaving

Alternatively, you can steam the sweet potatoes or microwave them for a few minutes to achieve a similar effect. This method works well to preserve their vibrant flavors and nutrients.

Seasoning Sweet Potatoes for the Grill

Grilled sweet potatoes taste amazing on their own, but seasonings can take them to the next level. Whether you prefer savory spices, a touch of sweetness, or bold marinades, there are endless ways to create a flavor profile that matches your meal.

Basic Seasoning

A drizzle of olive oil, along with a sprinkle of sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, is often all you need to let the natural sweetness shine through. The oil helps prevent sticking to the grill grates while enhancing their caramelized exterior.

Savory Combinations

For a savory twist, try adding smoked paprika, garlic powder, cumin, or chili powder for a mildly spicy kick. Fresh or dried herbs like rosemary, thyme, or oregano can also elevate the flavor while complementing the smoky taste of the grill.

Sweet Options

If you want to play up the sweetness, toss the sweet potatoes with a light coating of melted butter, cinnamon, and a hint of brown sugar or maple syrup. This combination delivers a delightful dessert-like side dish that pairs especially well with grilled pork or chicken.

Creative Marinades

Take your sweet potatoes to another level by marinating them in bold flavors. A mixture of balsamic vinegar, honey, olive oil, and Dijon mustard can add a tangy yet sweet twist. For a tropical vibe, try coconut milk, lime juice, and a dash of curry powder.

Grilling Sweet Potatoes: Step-by-Step Instructions

Grilling sweet potatoes isn’t difficult, but having a clear plan will make the process seamless. Follow these steps to ensure your sweet potatoes come out perfectly grilled every time.

Step 1: Preheat the Grill

Heat your grill to medium-high heat, about 375°F to 400°F, for direct grilling. For charcoal grills, wait until the coals are covered with white ash before placing sweet potatoes on the grates.

Step 2: Oil the Grill Grates

Prevent sticking by lightly brushing or spraying the grates with oil before adding the sweet potatoes. Use an oil with a high smoke point, like avocado oil or vegetable oil, to avoid excessive smoking.

Step 3: Add Sweet Potatoes to the Grill

Place the sweet potato slices, wedges, or rounds directly on the grill grates in a single layer. Avoid overcrowding the grill to ensure even cooking. Close the lid to maintain a consistent temperature.

Step 4: Cook and Flip

Grill for about 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of your cuts. You’ll know it’s time to flip when the undersides develop a golden-brown crust and grill marks. Use tongs or a spatula to carefully turn the sweet potatoes.

Step 5: Check for Doneness

Pierce a piece with a fork to ensure the interior is tender before removing from the grill. If needed, move the sweet potatoes to indirect heat, cover, and continue cooking for a few extra minutes.

Serving Ideas and Pairings

Grilled sweet potatoes can be served hot off the grill or slightly cooled. Their natural sweetness and smoky profile make them an ideal side dish for a variety of main dishes, from grilled chicken and steak to plant-based burgers and tofu skewers.

Here are a few serving suggestions:

  • Herb Yogurt Sauce: Drizzle or serve with a tangy yogurt sauce mixed with lemon juice and fresh herbs like dill or parsley.
  • Avocado and Lime: Top with mashed avocado, a squeeze of lime, and a sprinkle of red chili flakes for a refreshing, creamy pairing.
  • Tahini Drizzle: Complement their sweet flavor with nutty tahini, sesame seeds, and fresh cilantro.
  • Loaded Sweet Potatoes: Sprinkle with crumbled feta or goat cheese, chopped green onions, and a dash of hot sauce for a hearty side dish.

Grilled sweet potatoes also make excellent leftovers! Add them to grain bowls, salads, or tacos for a quick and wholesome meal.

Tips for Grilling Success

  • Don’t Skimp on Oil: Brushing the sweet potatoes lightly with oil ensures they won’t stick and helps achieve a crispy exterior.
  • Avoid High Heat: Too much heat can burn the surface before the inside cooks, so stick to moderate heat for even grilling.
  • Experiment with Shapes: Varying the cut of your sweet potatoes can change their texture and how they cook, so don’t hesitate to try something new.
  • Use Foil or a Basket: If you’re worried about pieces falling through the grates, you can cook the sweet potatoes in a grill basket or wrap them in foil packets.

Grilled sweet potatoes are a simple yet delicious way to add a healthy and flavorful touch to your meals. With the right preparation, seasonings, and cooking techniques, you can transform this humble root vegetable into a standout dish that your family and friends will love. Dust off your grill tools, grab some fresh sweet potatoes, and get ready to impress your taste buds!
