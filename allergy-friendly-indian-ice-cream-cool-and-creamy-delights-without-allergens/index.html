Allergy-Friendly Indian Ice Cream: Cool and Creamy Delights without Allergens


Are you a fan of Indian cuisine but struggle with allergies? Do you find it challenging to enjoy the sweet treats that Indian desserts offer? Worry no more! In this blog post, I will share with you some allergy-friendly Indian ice cream recipes that are cool, creamy, and free from common allergens. Get ready to indulge in delightful flavors without the worry!

Introduction

Indian cuisine is known for its rich and diverse flavors, and desserts are no exception. However, many traditional Indian sweets and ice creams contain ingredients like dairy, nuts, and gluten, which can be problematic for those with allergies. But fear not! With a few simple substitutions and creative twists, we can create allergy-friendly versions of these delectable treats.

Recipe 1: Mango Lassi Ice Cream

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup dairy-free yogurt
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Pinch of saffron strands (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a blender, combine the diced mangoes, dairy-free yogurt, coconut milk, maple syrup, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using).
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. Once the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency, transfer it to a lidded container and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.
  5. Serve the mango lassi ice cream in bowls or cones and enjoy the refreshing flavors of mango and cardamom.

Recipe 2: Rose Pistachio Ice Cream

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dairy-free milk (such as almond or oat milk)
  • 1/2 cup canned coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon rose water
  • 1/2 cup shelled pistachios, roughly chopped

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, heat the dairy-free milk, canned coconut milk, maple syrup, and rose water over medium heat until it reaches a gentle simmer.
  2. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the mixture cool to room temperature.
  3. Once cooled, pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. In the last few minutes of churning, add the chopped pistachios and continue to churn until well combined.
  5. Transfer the rose pistachio ice cream to a lidded container and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.
  6. Serve this fragrant and nutty ice cream as a refreshing dessert or as a delightful accompaniment to Indian sweets.

Recipe 3: Coconut Cardamom Kulfi

Ingredients

  • 2 cups canned coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup desiccated coconut
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, combine the canned coconut milk, dairy-free condensed milk, cardamom powder, and desiccated coconut.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly until it thickens slightly. This should take about 10 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the mixture cool to room temperature.
  4. Once cooled, pour the mixture into popsicle molds or small paper cups.
  5. Insert popsicle sticks into the molds or cups and freeze for at least 6 hours or until firm.
  6. If desired, sprinkle chopped pistachios over the kulfi before freezing for an added crunch.
  7. Enjoy this creamy and aromatic coconut cardamom kulfi as a frozen treat on a hot summer day.

Conclusion

These allergy-friendly Indian ice cream recipes prove that you don’t have to miss out on the delightful flavors of Indian cuisine due to allergies. By making simple substitutions and using alternative ingredients, you can create cool and creamy treats that everyone can enjoy. So go ahead, indulge in these allergy-friendly delights and savor the taste of India without any worries. Happy cooking and happy eating!
