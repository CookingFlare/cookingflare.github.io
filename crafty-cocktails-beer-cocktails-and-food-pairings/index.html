Crafty Cocktails: Beer Cocktails and Food Pairings


Beer cocktails are a unique and creative way to enjoy your favorite brew combined with delicious spirits and mixers. Just as wine has long been paired with specific foods to enhance flavors and create memorable dining experiences, beer can also serve as the perfect accompaniment to your favorite dishes. In this post, we’ll explore some exciting beer cocktail recipes and which food pairings bring out the best in each brew.

  1. The Shandy

Originating in Denmark, the Shandy is a popular beer cocktail that combines lager with apple juice or lemonade. This refreshing, low-alcohol option is perfect for casual meals or warm-weather gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 part lager
  • 1 part apple juice or lemonade
  • Optional: splash of vodka

How to make

Mix equal parts lager and apple juice or lemonade in a tall glass filled with ice. For an added kick, add a splash of vodka. Stir gently and enjoy.

Food pairing

The Shandy’s tangy flavors complement savory dishes like grilled bratwurst, fish tacos, and cheesy quesadillas.

  1. The Red Dog

Dating back to the 19th century, the Red Dog is a combination of beer and red cinnamon candy.

Ingredients

  • Canned chili
  • Beer
  • Red cinnamon candies

How to make

Pour a can of chili directly into a beer and drop in a handful of red cinnamon candies. Stir gently and let the flavors meld.

Food pairing

The spicy and sweet notes of the Red Dog pair perfectly with comfort food dishes like slow-cooked ribs and loaded potato skins.

  1. The Black Velvet

This classic beer cocktail combines stout with Champagne or champagne substitute, creating a silky smooth and contrasts in flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 part stout
  • 1 part Champagne or champagne substitute

How to make

Pour a part of stout into a glass and gently top it off with Champagne or a champagne substitute. Stir gently and serve.

Food pairing

The rich, velvety taste of The Black Velvet complements decadent desserts like triple chocolate cake and warm brownies.

  1. The Boilermaker

One of the simplest beer cocktails, The Boilermaker consists of a shot of liquor added to a glass of beer.

Ingredients

  • Shot of liquor (whiskey, bourbon, or brandy recommended)
  • Beer

How to make

Fill a glass with your favorite beer and drop in a shot glass containing your preferred liquor. Enjoy responsibly.

Food pairing

The Boilermaker’s versatility allows it to pair well with a wide range of dishes, from barbecued meats to spicy Thai cuisine.

  1. The Dunkel Scotch Ale Cocktail

This sophisticated beer cocktail marries scotch ale with Fig & Honey Liqueur, creating a rich and complex flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 1 part scotch ale
  • 1 part Fig & Honey Liqueur
  • Dash of bitters

How to make

In a glass filled with ice, combine scotch ale and Fig & Honey Liqueur. Add a dash of bitters and stir gently.

Food pairing

The sophisticated and sweet flavors of the Dunkel Scotch Ale Cocktail perfectly complement savory meals like roasted lamb or hearty casseroles.

As you experiment with beer cocktails and food pairings, don’t be afraid to get creative and customize your brews to suit your unique tastes. Mix and match your favorite ingredients to discover new combinations and find your signature crafty cocktail. Cheers!
