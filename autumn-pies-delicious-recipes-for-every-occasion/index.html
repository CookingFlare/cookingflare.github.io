Autumn Pies: Delicious Recipes for Every Occasion


Are you ready to embrace the cozy flavors of fall? Look no further than autumn pies! These delicious treats are perfect for any occasion, whether you’re hosting a family gathering or simply craving a warm and comforting dessert. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of autumn pie recipes that are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth. So grab your apron and let’s get baking!

1. Classic Apple Pie

Let’s start with a timeless favorite – the classic apple pie. This pie is a staple during the autumn season, and for good reason. The combination of tart apples, warm spices, and flaky crust is simply irresistible. Begin by peeling and slicing 6 to 8 Granny Smith apples. In a large bowl, mix the apples with 1/2 cup of granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of cinnamon, and a pinch of nutmeg. Pour the mixture into a prepared pie crust and cover it with another layer of crust. Bake at 375°F for about 45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the apples are tender. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for the ultimate autumn dessert.

2. Pumpkin Pecan Pie

Combine two fall favorites – pumpkin and pecan – for a pie that will impress your guests. Start by preparing a traditional pumpkin pie filling. In a mixing bowl, whisk together 1 can of pumpkin puree, 1 cup of evaporated milk, 3/4 cup of granulated sugar, 2 eggs, 1 teaspoon of cinnamon, and 1/2 teaspoon each of ginger, nutmeg, and salt. Pour the mixture into a pie crust and set aside. In a separate bowl, combine 1 cup of pecan halves, 1/2 cup of brown sugar, 2 tablespoons of melted butter, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract. Sprinkle the pecan mixture over the pumpkin filling. Bake at 350°F for 40-45 minutes, or until the center is set. Let it cool before serving.

3. Maple Bourbon Sweet Potato Pie

For a pie with a unique twist, try this maple bourbon sweet potato pie. It brings together the flavors of sweet potatoes, maple syrup, and a hint of bourbon for a truly indulgent dessert. Start by roasting 2 large sweet potatoes until they are soft and can easily be mashed. In a bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes with 3/4 cup of maple syrup, 1/2 cup of brown sugar, 1/4 cup of melted butter, 2 eggs, 1/4 cup of bourbon, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon, and a pinch of nutmeg. Pour the mixture into a prepared pie crust and bake at 350°F for 50-55 minutes, or until the filling is set. Allow the pie to cool completely before serving.

4. Cranberry Pear Galette

If you’re looking for a rustic and elegant autumn pie, try a cranberry pear galette. The combination of tart cranberries and sweet pears creates a delightful balance of flavors. Start by rolling out a pie crust into a circular shape. In a bowl, toss together 2 cups of sliced pears, 1 cup of fresh cranberries, 1/2 cup of granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons of cornstarch, 1 teaspoon of lemon zest, and a pinch of salt. Transfer the fruit mixture onto the pie crust, leaving a border around the edges. Fold the edges of the crust over the fruit, creating a rustic galette shape. Bake at 375°F for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the fruit is tender. Serve warm with a dollop of whipped cream.

5. Salted Caramel Chocolate Pecan Pie

For a decadent and indulgent autumn pie, look no further than this salted caramel chocolate pecan pie. It’s a mouthwatering combination of rich chocolate, gooey caramel, and crunchy pecans. Begin by preparing a traditional pecan pie filling. In a bowl, whisk together 3 eggs, 1 cup of corn syrup, 3/4 cup of granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons of melted butter, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Stir in 1 cup of chopped pecans and 1/2 cup of chocolate chips. Pour the mixture into a prepared pie crust. Drizzle about 1/4 cup of caramel sauce over the top, and sprinkle with a pinch of sea salt. Bake at 350°F for 50-55 minutes, or until the filling is set. Let it cool before slicing.

Conclusion

Autumn pies are a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of the season. Whether you prefer a classic apple pie, a unique twist on pumpkin, or a combination of flavors like maple bourbon sweet potato or salted caramel chocolate pecan, there’s a pie for every occasion. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and let these delicious recipes fill your home with the comforting aromas of fall. Happy baking!
