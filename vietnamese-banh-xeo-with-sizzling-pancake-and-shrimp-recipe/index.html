Vietnamese Banh Xeo with Sizzling Pancake and Shrimp Recipe


If you’re looking to add a burst of flavor to your cooking repertoire, Vietnamese cuisine is a treasure trove of delightful dishes. Among these, Banh Xeo, a sizzling pancake filled with succulent shrimp and fresh herbs, stands out as a beloved classic. This vibrant and crispy pancake is a symphony of textures and flavors, making it a perfect choice for a special meal or a casual get-together. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating this delightful dish that will surely impress your friends and family.

Ingredients:

For the Pancake Batter:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper

For the Filling:

  • 1 lb medium-sized shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 1 cup fresh herbs (such as cilantro, mint, and Thai basil)
  • Cooking oil

Dipping Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1-2 Thai chilies, finely chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a large mixing bowl, combine the rice flour, coconut milk, water, turmeric powder, salt, and ground pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until you have a smooth, thin batter. Let the batter rest for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  2. Cook the Filling: Heat a tablespoon of cooking oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn translucent. Then, add the shrimp to the skillet and cook until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the cooked shrimp and onions from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Make the Pancakes: Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add a drizzle of cooking oil. Once the skillet is hot, pour a ladleful of the batter into the pan, swirling it around to create a thin, even layer. Arrange a small handful of bean sprouts on one half of the pancake, then add a few cooked shrimp and onions on top. Cover the skillet and let the pancake cook for 2-3 minutes until the edges turn crispy and golden brown.

  4. Fold and Serve: Once the edges are crispy, fold the pancake in half to cover the filling. Gently slide the pancake onto a serving plate. Repeat the process with the remaining batter and filling.

  5. Prepare the Dipping Sauce: In a small bowl, combine the fish sauce, sugar, lime juice, minced garlic, and chopped Thai chilies. Stir until the sugar is dissolved, and the ingredients are well combined.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Cut the Banh Xeo into wedges and serve with a generous helping of fresh herbs and the zesty dipping sauce on the side. Encourage your guests to assemble their own savory parcels, wrapping the pancake with herbs and dipping it into the tangy sauce.

Banh Xeo is a delightful dish that brings together the best of Vietnamese flavors – the crispy pancake, the succulent shrimp, and the aromatic herbs, all harmonizing with the zesty dipping sauce. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or a beginner, this recipe is sure to elevate your culinary skills and impress your loved ones. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a flavorful journey with this Vietnamese Banh Xeo recipe!
