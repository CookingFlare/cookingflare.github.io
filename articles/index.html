Spicy Keto Buffalo Chicken Cauliflower Casserole Recipe

Are you looking for a mouth-watering, spicy, and satisfying casserole recipe that’s perfect for a keto diet? Look no further! Today, we’re going to share a unique and delicious Spicy Keto Buffalo Chicken Cauliflower Casserole recipe that’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Ingredients 1 head of cauliflower, broken into florets 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces 1/2 cup buffalo wing sauce 1/4 cup ranch dressing 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder 1/2 teaspoon onion powder 1/2 teaspoon paprika Salt and pepper, to taste 2 tablespoons olive oil Instructions Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, toss the cauliflower florets with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, salt, and pepper until they’re evenly coated. Spread them out in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until they’re tender and lightly browned. While the cauliflower is roasting, cook the chicken in a large skillet over medium-high heat, adding the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside. In the same skillet, add the buffalo wing sauce and bring it to a simmer. Let it cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked chicken, buffalo wing sauce, and ranch dressing. Stir until the chicken is well coated with the sauce. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, create a layer of the roasted cauliflower. Top it with a layer of the chicken mixture, followed by a sprinkle of shredded cheddar cheese and chopped cilantro. Repeat the layering process one more time, finishing with a layer of cheese on top. Sprinkle the paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder over the top of the cheese. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the top is lightly browned. Tips and Variations To make this recipe more spicy, add more buffalo wing sauce or use hot sauce like Frank’s RedHot. If you prefer a milder flavor, use less buffalo wing sauce or substitute it with a milder sauce like teriyaki. To make this recipe more substantial, add some chopped bacon or diced onions to the chicken mixture.
