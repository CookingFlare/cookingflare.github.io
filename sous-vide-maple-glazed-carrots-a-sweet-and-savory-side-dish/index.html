Sous Vide Maple Glazed Carrots: A Sweet and Savory Side Dish


Carrots are a versatile vegetable that can be prepared in various ways, but one method that truly elevates their flavor and texture is sous vide cooking. Sous vide, French for “under vacuum,” involves cooking food in a temperature-controlled water bath, resulting in perfectly cooked and tender carrots every time. In this recipe, we will explore how to make sous vide maple glazed carrots, a delightful side dish that combines the sweetness of maple syrup with the earthiness of carrots. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of baby carrots
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons of pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 185°F (85°C).

  2. In a large resealable bag, combine the baby carrots, butter, maple syrup, salt, black pepper, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Seal the bag, ensuring all the air is removed.

  3. Place the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 1 hour. The long cooking time will allow the flavors to infuse into the carrots while maintaining their natural sweetness and texture.

  4. After 1 hour, carefully remove the bag from the water bath and open it, taking care to avoid any hot steam. Transfer the carrots to a serving dish, reserving the cooking liquid.

  5. In a small saucepan, bring the reserved cooking liquid to a simmer over medium heat. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the liquid has reduced and thickened slightly.

  6. Drizzle the reduced cooking liquid over the glazed carrots, ensuring they are evenly coated.

  7. Serve the sous vide maple glazed carrots as a side dish alongside your favorite protein or as a flavorful addition to a vegetarian meal.

Tips and Variations:

  • For added depth of flavor, you can experiment with different spices such as smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, or even a pinch of cloves.

  • If you prefer a sweeter glaze, you can increase the amount of maple syrup in the recipe. Adjust according to your taste preferences.

  • Feel free to customize the recipe by adding other vegetables to the sous vide bag. For example, parsnips or sweet potatoes would complement the flavors of the carrots beautifully.

  • If you don’t have a sous vide machine, you can still achieve delicious results by cooking the carrots in a covered pot with a small amount of water over low heat until tender. Then, proceed with the glazing step as described in the recipe.

Sous vide maple glazed carrots are a delightful side dish that offers a harmonious balance of sweet and savory flavors. The sous vide cooking method ensures that the carrots are perfectly cooked, tender, and infused with the delicious maple glaze. With a minimal amount of effort, you can create a stunning and flavorful side dish that will impress your family and friends. So, give this recipe a try and elevate your carrot game to a whole new level! Enjoy!
