Christmas Recipes for the Grill: Festive Outdoor Cooking


When you think of Christmas cooking, the image that typically comes to mind is a roaring oven cooking a turkey, side dishes bubbling away on stovetops, and a kitchen filled with warmth and the aroma of spices. But what if this year, you took your holiday celebrations outside? Grilling brings an exciting twist to traditional Christmas recipes, giving them a smoky, flame-kissed flavor that’s hard to beat. Whether you’re hosting a holiday gathering or enjoying a quiet family dinner, Christmas grilling promises to infuse your festivities with creativity and deliciousness. Let’s explore some festive recipes for the grill that will surely elevate your holiday cooking game.

Grilled Christmas Turkey: A Smoky Holiday Centerpiece

The turkey is the star of almost every Christmas dinner, but instead of roasting it in the oven, try grilling it for a flavorful new take. By cooking it on the grill, you’ll achieve juicy meat and irresistibly crispy skin, infused with subtle smokiness.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey (12-14 pounds)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Applewood or hickory wood chips (optional for extra smoky flavor)

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Preheat the grill to medium heat (around 325°F). If using wood chips, soak them in water for about 30 minutes to enhance the smokiness.
  2. Season the Turkey: In a bowl, mix the softened butter, garlic, rosemary, thyme, sage, lemon zest, salt, and pepper. Gently loosen the turkey’s skin and rub the butter mixture generously beneath the skin and over the exterior.
  3. Indirect Heat Setup: Arrange the grill for indirect cooking by placing the coals or burners on one side while leaving the other side empty.
  4. Grilling: Place a drip pan under the turkey to catch juices. Transfer the turkey to the grill on the cooler side and close the lid. Cook for about 10-12 minutes per pound, basting occasionally with the drippings.
  5. Monitor: Use a meat thermometer to check when the internal temperature of the thickest part of the turkey reaches 165°F.
  6. Rest and Serve: Allow the turkey to rest for 20 minutes before slicing. Serve as your centerpiece alongside your favorite holiday sides.

Grilled Honey-Glazed Ham: A Sweet and Smoky Delight

Ham is a traditional favorite for Christmas dinner, but grilling it adds a layer of complexity that you simply can’t achieve indoors. When paired with a honey glaze, it becomes a tantalizing combination of sweet, salty, and smoky.

Ingredients

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (7-9 pounds)
  • ¾ cup honey
  • ¼ cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ cup brown sugar

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-low heat (about 300°F). For a charcoal grill, arrange your coals for indirect cooking.
  2. Mix the Glaze: Combine honey, Dijon mustard, vinegar, cloves, and brown sugar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves, then remove from heat.
  3. Cook the Ham: Place the ham on the cooler side of the grill. Brush generously with the glaze, covering all sides.
  4. Grill: Cook the ham for 2-3 hours, basting every 30 minutes with additional glaze. Rotate periodically to ensure even cooking.
  5. Finish: When the glaze caramelizes and the ham reaches an internal temperature of 140°F, remove it from the grill and let it rest for 15 minutes. Slice thinly and serve.

Grilled Festive Vegetables: A Vegan-Friendly Side

Vegetables on the grill are the perfect accompaniment for your holiday feast. The char from the flames enhances their natural sweetness while imparting a unique depth of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 2 yellow squash, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Create the Marinade: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Prep the Vegetables: Toss the vegetables in the marinade, ensuring they’re evenly coated.
  3. Grill: Preheat your grill to medium heat. Place the vegetables directly on the grates or use a vegetable grilling basket. Grill for 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until they’re caramelized and slightly charred.
  4. Serve: Arrange the grilled vegetables on a platter and sprinkle with fresh parsley for a festive touch.

Grilled Christmas Dessert: Tangy Pineapple Rings

End your Christmas grilling feast on a high note with a delectable dessert. Grilled pineapple rings are easy to make, bursting with caramelized sweetness, and perfect either on their own or served with ice cream.

Ingredients

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • Vanilla ice cream (optional)

Instructions

  1. Create the Glaze: In a small bowl, mix honey, lime juice, and cinnamon.
  2. Prep the Pineapple: Brush the glaze onto the pineapple rings.
  3. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Place the pineapple rings on the grates and cook for 2-4 minutes per side until grill marks appear and the glaze caramelizes.
  4. Serve: Pair the grilled pineapple rings with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a tropical Christmas twist.

Pro Tips for Successful Christmas Grilling

  1. Choose Seasonal Marinades: Incorporate festive flavors like cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cranberries, or citrus into your marinades and glazes to enhance each dish with holiday flair.
  2. Use a Meat Thermometer: Grilling large cuts of meat like turkey or ham requires precision. A meat thermometer ensures your proteins are cooked perfectly without drying out.
  3. Prep Ahead: Many marinades and dishes can be prepared ahead of time so you can enjoy more time with family while the grill works its magic.
  4. Keep Safety in Mind: Ensure your grill’s cooking surface is clean and free of flare-ups to maintain ideal temperatures and even cooking.

Bring the Festivities Outside

Grilling for Christmas isn’t just about the food—it’s about creating an experience. The warmth of the grill, the aroma of smoke, and the joy of being outside blend beautifully with the festive spirit of the season. Bring chairs and blankets, set up cozy lighting, and add some holiday tunes to create the perfect atmosphere for outdoor Christmas cooking.

So, as you plan your holiday menu, remember that your recipes don’t have to be confined to the kitchen. Let the grill take center stage this Christmas and give your loved ones a holiday feast they’ll never forget.

Merry Christmas and happy grilling!
