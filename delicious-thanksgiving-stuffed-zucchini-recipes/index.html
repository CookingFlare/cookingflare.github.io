Delicious Thanksgiving Stuffed Zucchini Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones and indulging in delicious, comforting dishes. While turkey may be the star of the show, it’s always fun to experiment with unique and flavorful side dishes. This Thanksgiving, why not try something different and incorporate zucchini into your menu? Stuffed zucchini is a versatile and mouthwatering dish that will impress your guests and add a delightful twist to your holiday feast. In this blog post, we’ll explore three delectable stuffed zucchini recipes that are perfect for Thanksgiving.

1. Classic Sausage and Mushroom Stuffed Zucchini

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis
  • 1 pound Italian sausage, casings removed
  • 1 cup diced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the zucchinis in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds to create a hollow cavity.
  3. In a skillet, cook the Italian sausage over medium heat until browned. Add the diced mushrooms, onion, and garlic, and sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the breadcrumbs and Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Spoon the sausage and mushroom mixture into the hollowed-out zucchinis.
  6. Place the stuffed zucchinis on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the zucchinis are tender and the filling is golden brown.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot.

2. Quinoa and Vegetable Stuffed Zucchini Boats

Ingredients:

  • 4 large zucchinis
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup diced bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the zucchinis in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds to create a hollow cavity.
  3. In a bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, diced bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, olive oil, and dried oregano. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Fill the hollowed-out zucchinis with the quinoa and vegetable mixture.
  5. Place the stuffed zucchinis on a baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the zucchinis are tender and the filling is heated through.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil and serve warm.

3. Mediterranean Stuffed Zucchini with Lamb and Mint

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis
  • 1 pound ground lamb
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the zucchinis in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds to create a hollow cavity.
  3. In a skillet, cook the ground lamb over medium heat until browned. Add the diced red onion, fresh mint, pine nuts, cumin, cinnamon, and cooked rice. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Spoon the lamb and rice mixture into the hollowed-out zucchinis.
  5. Place the stuffed zucchinis on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the zucchinis are tender and the filling is aromatic.
  6. Serve with lemon wedges for a burst of citrus flavor.

These mouthwatering stuffed zucchini recipes are sure to elevate your Thanksgiving feast and impress your guests with their unique flavors and presentation. Whether you opt for the classic combination of sausage and mushrooms, the light and nutritious quinoa and vegetable filling, or the exotic Mediterranean flavors of lamb and mint, these recipes are guaranteed to delight your taste buds and add a touch of elegance to your holiday table. So, this Thanksgiving, think outside the traditional menu and treat your loved ones to a delightful culinary adventure with these delicious stuffed zucchini creations!
