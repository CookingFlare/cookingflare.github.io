Delicious Thanksgiving Biscuits with Herb Butter


Thanksgiving is a time for family, friends, and, of course, incredible food. While the turkey often steals the spotlight, the side dishes play a crucial role in making the meal truly memorable. Among the many sides that grace the Thanksgiving table, biscuits are often a favorite. These warm, buttery, soft, and flaky treats pair perfectly with turkey and gravy, and they are especially delightful when served with a rich herb-infused butter.

In this post, we’ll explore how to make mouthwatering Thanksgiving biscuits with herb butter. Whether you are a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is easy to follow, and the results will wow your guests.

The Perfect Thanksgiving Biscuits: Light, Fluffy, and Flavorful

To make truly exceptional biscuits, you need to master a few key techniques. The secret to achieving a soft, flaky texture lies in a combination of the right ingredients, careful handling of the dough, and proper baking techniques. Here’s a breakdown of how to create the perfect Thanksgiving biscuits.

Key Ingredients for the Biscuits

The ingredients for Thanksgiving biscuits are simple and accessible, but the magic comes from how they come together. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • All-purpose flour: The base of the biscuit dough. A high-quality flour will make a significant difference in the texture of your biscuits.
  • Baking powder: This will help the biscuits rise and become light and fluffy.
  • Salt: A pinch of salt enhances the overall flavor of the biscuits.
  • Unsalted butter: Cold, cubed butter is crucial for creating the delicate, flaky layers in the biscuits.
  • Whole milk: The liquid that brings everything together into a dough. You can also use buttermilk for an added tang.
  • Honey: A little sweetness goes a long way, adding a subtle depth to the biscuits.

The technique for combining these ingredients is what makes the difference. Using cold butter and not overworking the dough will yield biscuits that are light, tender, and perfectly flaky.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Thanksgiving Biscuits

  1. Prepare Your Ingredients: Start by preheating your oven to 450°F (230°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set it aside. Cube the unsalted butter into small pieces and place it in the refrigerator to keep it cold. Measure out the flour, baking powder, salt, and honey.

  2. Combine the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. The key here is to mix these ingredients thoroughly to ensure the baking powder is evenly distributed throughout the flour. This will help the biscuits rise evenly as they bake.

  3. Cut in the Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter into the dry ingredients. Use a pastry cutter, two forks, or your hands to work the butter into the flour. The goal is to break the butter into small pieces, about the size of peas, without fully incorporating it into the flour. The butter should remain in small, visible pieces as this is what creates the flaky layers in the biscuits.

  4. Add the Wet Ingredients: Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the milk (or buttermilk) and honey. Stir gently with a spoon until the dough just comes together. Avoid overmixing, as this can lead to tough biscuits.

  5. Shape the Biscuits: Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and gently pat it into a rectangle or circle that’s about 1-inch thick. Fold the dough over onto itself a few times, then flatten it again. This folding technique helps to create more layers in the biscuits.

    Use a round biscuit cutter (or even a drinking glass) to cut out the biscuits. Press down firmly but do not twist the cutter, as this can seal the edges and prevent the biscuits from rising properly. Re-roll the dough scraps and continue cutting biscuits until you have used up all the dough.

  6. Bake the Biscuits: Place the biscuits onto your prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the biscuits are golden brown on top. Keep an eye on them during the last few minutes of baking to ensure they don’t over-brown.

  7. Cool and Serve: Remove the biscuits from the oven and let them cool slightly on a wire rack. Serve them warm, slathered with herb butter, and enjoy the comforting, flaky texture.

The Herb Butter: A Flavorful Twist

While the biscuits themselves are delicious, the herb butter takes them to another level. Herb butter is simple to make and adds an aromatic, savory element that complements the warm, fluffy biscuits beautifully. You can use fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, parsley, or sage for a burst of flavor.

Ingredients for Herb Butter

  • Unsalted butter: Use room-temperature butter so that it’s easy to mix with the herbs and other ingredients.
  • Fresh herbs: Fresh rosemary, thyme, sage, or parsley work wonderfully. You can use a combination of these herbs to create a balanced, fragrant butter.
  • Garlic: A small amount of minced garlic adds a savory depth of flavor to the butter.
  • Lemon zest: A touch of lemon zest brightens the butter and gives it a fresh, zesty flavor that pairs perfectly with the biscuits.
  • Salt and pepper: These seasonings bring out the best in the butter and herbs.

Making the Herb Butter

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a small bowl, combine the room-temperature butter with the minced garlic, fresh herbs, lemon zest, salt, and pepper. Use a fork or a spoon to mix the ingredients together until they are evenly combined. The herbs should be finely chopped so that they integrate well with the butter.

  2. Chill the Butter: Once the herb butter is mixed, transfer it to a piece of parchment paper or plastic wrap. Shape it into a log and twist the ends to seal. Place the herb butter in the refrigerator to chill for at least 30 minutes. This will help the flavors meld together and make the butter easier to spread.

  3. Serve with the Biscuits: When you’re ready to serve the biscuits, slice the chilled herb butter into rounds and place a piece on top of each warm biscuit. The butter will melt beautifully, infusing the biscuit with rich, herby goodness.

Tips for Success

  • Use cold butter: Cold butter is key to creating flaky biscuits. It’s the pockets of cold butter that turn into steam in the oven, which causes the biscuits to rise and become light and airy.
  • Don’t overwork the dough: Overworking the dough can result in tough biscuits. Mix the ingredients just until they come together, and then handle the dough as gently as possible.
  • Bake immediately: Once the dough is mixed and cut, get the biscuits into the oven quickly to prevent the butter from melting before baking, which could result in less-flaky biscuits.
  • Experiment with herbs: While rosemary, thyme, and parsley are traditional choices, don’t be afraid to experiment with other herbs like tarragon, oregano, or chives. Each herb brings a unique flavor to the table.

Perfect for Thanksgiving and Beyond

These Thanksgiving biscuits with herb butter are an ideal side dish for your Thanksgiving feast. Their buttery, flaky texture pairs wonderfully with turkey, stuffing, and mashed potatoes, while the herb butter adds a savory touch that makes each bite extra special. Whether you’re hosting a large family gathering or enjoying a quiet meal at home, these biscuits are sure to impress.

While they are a fantastic addition to Thanksgiving dinner, these biscuits can also be enjoyed at any time of year. Serve them with a hearty soup in the fall, a cozy breakfast spread, or even as a side to a Sunday roast.

Final Thoughts

Baking biscuits from scratch may seem intimidating at first, but once you understand the fundamentals of the dough and the proper techniques, it becomes an enjoyable and rewarding process. The addition of herb butter takes these biscuits from simple to sensational, making them a standout feature at your Thanksgiving table.

Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting to explore baking, this recipe will guide you through the steps to create a delicious, comforting treat that everyone will love. Give these Thanksgiving biscuits with herb butter a try, and get ready for a taste of warmth, comfort, and holiday cheer.

Enjoy your biscuits, and happy baking!
