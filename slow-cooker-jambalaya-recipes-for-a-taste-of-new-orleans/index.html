Slow Cooker Jambalaya Recipes for a Taste of New Orleans


If you’re a fan of spicy, flavorful dishes, then you’re in for a treat! Jambalaya is a classic Louisiana dish that combines elements of French, Spanish, and African cuisines. It’s a one-pot wonder that bursts with bold flavors and a tantalizing aroma. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delicious slow cooker jambalaya recipes that will transport you straight to the heart of New Orleans.

1. Classic Chicken and Sausage Jambalaya

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the chicken, sausage, onion, bell pepper, celery, garlic, diced tomatoes (with their juice), chicken broth, Cajun seasoning, thyme, oregano, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  2. Cover and cook on low heat for 6-8 hours or on high heat for 3-4 hours, until the chicken is cooked through and the flavors have melded together.
  3. Serve the jambalaya over cooked rice, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy the explosion of flavors!

2. Seafood Jambalaya

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the shrimp, sausage, onion, bell pepper, celery, garlic, diced tomatoes (with their juice), chicken broth, Cajun seasoning, thyme, oregano, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Cover and cook on low heat for 6-8 hours or on high heat for 3-4 hours, until the shrimp is cooked through.
  3. Serve the flavorful seafood jambalaya over cooked rice and savor the taste of the sea!

3. Vegetarian Jambalaya

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 ounces) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the kidney beans, black beans, onion, bell pepper, celery, garlic, diced tomatoes (with their juice), vegetable broth, Cajun seasoning, thyme, oregano, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Cover and cook on low heat for 6-8 hours or on high heat for 3-4 hours, until the flavors have melded together.
  3. Serve the hearty vegetarian jambalaya over cooked rice and relish the blend of vibrant flavors.

Conclusion

Slow cooker jambalaya recipes are a fantastic way to bring the vibrant and soulful flavors of New Orleans into your own kitchen. Whether you prefer the classic combination of chicken and sausage, the tantalizing taste of seafood, or the hearty goodness of a vegetarian option, jambalaya has something for everyone. So grab your slow cooker, assemble the ingredients, and let the magic unfold as you savor the taste of New Orleans right at home. Enjoy the culinary journey!
