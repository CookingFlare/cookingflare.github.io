How to Make Classic Whoopie Pies at Home


Whoopie pies are a nostalgic treat that bring together the best of both worlds: cake and cookies, in a soft, pillowy sandwich with a creamy filling. Whether you’ve enjoyed these delightful desserts at a bakery or as a homemade treat passed down through generations, there’s no denying their charm. Making them at home can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially when you get to enjoy them fresh out of the oven.

In this guide, we’ll take you step-by-step through the process of making classic whoopie pies at home. From creating the perfect soft and chewy cake-like cookies to whipping up a smooth, velvety filling, you’ll be able to craft these delicious treats in your very own kitchen.

What are Whoopie Pies?

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a quick look at what whoopie pies are. A whoopie pie consists of two round, soft cookies filled with a sweet, creamy filling. They are sometimes called “Gobs” in some regions, especially in Pennsylvania and other parts of the Northeastern United States, where they are believed to have originated.

Whoopie pies are often associated with American childhood memories, with their cake-like cookies and rich filling offering a satisfying balance of sweetness and texture. The classic version features chocolate cookies and a marshmallow-like filling, but there are many variations, including pumpkin, red velvet, and peanut butter whoopie pies.

The beauty of whoopie pies lies in their versatility and the ease with which they can be made at home, allowing you to experiment with different flavors for both the cookies and the filling. Let’s get started with the classic chocolate whoopie pie recipe that will have you creating this dessert like a pro.

Ingredients

For the Whoopie Pie Cookies:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup buttermilk

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup marshmallow fluff (or marshmallow creme)
  • Pinch of salt

Optional for Decorating:

  • Mini chocolate chips, sprinkles, or colorful candies

Equipment Needed

  • Two baking sheets
  • Parchment paper or silicone baking mats
  • Electric mixer (hand or stand mixer)
  • Large mixing bowls
  • Spoon or ice cream scoop
  • Spatula
  • Cooling rack

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preheat the Oven and Prepare Baking Sheets

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats to ensure that the whoopie pie cookies don’t stick during baking. The parchment paper also helps the cookies bake evenly and gives them a smooth texture.

2. Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. This step is important because it ensures that all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed throughout the dough. The combination of baking soda and baking powder helps the cookies rise to perfection and achieve that soft, cake-like texture.

3. Cream the Butter and Sugar

In a separate large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter and brown sugar together using an electric mixer. Beat for about 3 to 4 minutes, until the mixture is light and fluffy. This process is crucial for incorporating air into the dough, making the cookies soft and chewy. If you don’t have an electric mixer, you can use a hand whisk, but it will take a bit more effort.

4. Add the Egg and Vanilla

Once the butter and sugar mixture is light and fluffy, add in the egg and vanilla extract. Continue mixing until everything is well combined. The egg helps bind the ingredients together, while the vanilla adds a nice depth of flavor to the cookies.

5. Combine the Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredient mixture to the wet ingredients, alternating with the buttermilk. Start with a little dry mix, then a bit of buttermilk, and continue alternating until everything is combined. Mix just until the batter is smooth, being careful not to overmix. Overmixing can lead to dense cookies.

6. Scoop and Shape the Cookies

Using a spoon or an ice cream scoop, drop rounded spoonfuls of batter onto the prepared baking sheets. Make sure to space each scoop about 2 inches apart, as the cookies will spread slightly as they bake. For uniform whoopie pies, try to make all the cookie portions roughly the same size.

If you want your cookies to have a smooth, even shape, gently use the back of the spoon or scoop to flatten them slightly. This step is optional, as some people prefer their whoopie pie cookies to have a more rustic, rounded shape.

7. Bake the Cookies

Bake the whoopie pie cookies in the preheated oven for 8 to 10 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cookie comes out clean. The cookies should be soft to the touch but firm enough to hold their shape.

Once baked, remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheets for about 5 minutes. Then, transfer the cookies to a cooling rack to cool completely before filling them.

8. Prepare the Filling

While the cookies are cooling, it’s time to make the creamy marshmallow filling. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter until smooth and creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time, mixing well after each addition. Once the powdered sugar is fully incorporated, add the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt.

Next, add the marshmallow fluff (or marshmallow creme) and beat the mixture until smooth and fluffy. The marshmallow fluff gives the filling a light, airy texture, while the butter and powdered sugar provide richness and sweetness. If the filling seems too thick, you can add a tiny bit of milk to reach your desired consistency.

9. Assemble the Whoopie Pies

Once the cookies have completely cooled, it’s time to assemble the whoopie pies. Turn half of the cookies face up and spoon a generous dollop of the marshmallow filling onto the center of each one. Use the back of the spoon or a spatula to spread the filling evenly.

Top each filled cookie with the remaining half of the cookies, pressing down gently to spread the filling to the edges. Be careful not to press too hard, as you want to maintain that pillowy, soft texture.

10. Optional Decoration

If you want to add a little extra flair to your whoopie pies, you can roll the edges in mini chocolate chips, sprinkles, or colorful candies. This step is entirely optional but can add a fun visual element to your treats, especially if you’re serving them for a party or special occasion.

11. Serve and Enjoy!

Now that your whoopie pies are assembled, it’s time to enjoy them! These treats are perfect for sharing with friends and family or simply indulging in as a sweet snack. The soft, cake-like cookies paired with the fluffy marshmallow filling are sure to satisfy any sweet tooth.

Whoopie pies are best enjoyed fresh, but if you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. If you want to keep them longer, you can refrigerate them for up to a week or freeze them for longer storage. Just make sure to bring them to room temperature before serving for the best taste and texture.

Tips for Perfect Whoopie Pies

  • Use Room Temperature Ingredients: For the best results, ensure your butter, egg, and other ingredients are at room temperature. This helps the ingredients blend more easily and creates a smoother batter.
  • Don’t Overbake the Cookies: Whoopie pie cookies should be soft and tender. Be careful not to overbake them, as they can become dry. Check for doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center of a cookie.
  • Customize the Filling: Feel free to get creative with the filling! You can add flavored extracts like mint, almond, or coconut, or even mix in some chocolate chips for added texture.
  • Make Mini Whoopie Pies: For a fun twist, make mini versions of the whoopie pies by using a smaller scoop for the cookies. They make great bite-sized treats for parties.

Conclusion

Making whoopie pies at home is a rewarding experience that brings together simple ingredients and a bit of creativity to create an irresistible dessert. Whether you’re preparing them for a special occasion or just a fun weekend baking project, these classic whoopie pies are sure to delight anyone who tastes them. The combination of soft chocolate cookies and creamy marshmallow filling will remind you of the charm of this timeless treat.

With this easy-to-follow recipe and helpful tips, you’ll be making whoopie pies like a pro in no time. Happy baking!
