Vegetarian Spanish Recipes: 10 Dishes Full of Flavor


Spanish cuisine is rich in history, culture, and flavor, and it’s not just limited to meat-centric dishes. In fact, Spanish cuisine offers an array of vegetarian dishes that are just as flavorful, colorful, and diverse. Whether you’re a dedicated vegetarian or simply looking to explore plant-based options, these dishes will give you a taste of Spain’s vibrant food scene without relying on meat.

In this post, we’ll explore 10 mouth-watering vegetarian Spanish recipes that are full of bold flavors. From hearty stews to savory tapas, there’s a vegetarian dish for everyone to enjoy. Let’s dive in and discover the delicious world of vegetarian Spanish food.

1. Vegetarian Paella

Paella is arguably the most iconic dish in Spanish cuisine. Originating from Valencia, this famous rice dish is typically made with seafood or meat, but it can easily be adapted into a vegetarian version. Vegetarian paella brings together a variety of fresh vegetables, including bell peppers, artichokes, tomatoes, peas, and green beans. The dish is cooked with saffron-infused rice, creating a rich, aromatic base.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of Arborio or short-grain rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup artichoke hearts, chopped
  • 1 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14 oz)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large paella pan or wide skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until soft, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the bell peppers, zucchini, and artichokes. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in the rice, saffron, smoked paprika, and diced tomatoes. Cook for 2 minutes to allow the rice to absorb the flavors.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and cook without stirring for 20-25 minutes, until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  5. In the last 10 minutes of cooking, add the peas and green beans. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve with lemon wedges and enjoy!

2. Patatas Bravas

Patatas Bravas is a quintessential Spanish tapa that’s beloved for its crispy potatoes and spicy tomato sauce. This dish is perfect as an appetizer or a snack. While the sauce is typically made with a combination of tomatoes, garlic, and a spicy kick, you can customize it to suit your preferred level of heat.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14 oz)
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the potato cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper, then spread them out on a baking sheet. Roast for 25-30 minutes, turning halfway through, until golden and crispy.
  3. While the potatoes are roasting, prepare the sauce. Heat olive oil in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, until the sauce thickens.
  5. Once the potatoes are done, drizzle the sauce over the top and serve immediately. Enjoy with a cold glass of Spanish wine!

3. Spanish Tortilla

The Spanish tortilla, also known as tortilla española, is a classic dish made with eggs, potatoes, and onions. It’s a simple yet satisfying dish that can be enjoyed at any time of day—whether for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. While the traditional tortilla uses eggs, this version can easily be adapted for a vegan diet by substituting eggs with a chickpea flour batter.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 6 eggs (or 1 cup chickpea flour mixed with 1 cup water for a vegan version)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the potatoes and onions, season with salt and pepper, and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 10-15 minutes until the potatoes are tender.
  2. If using eggs, whisk them in a bowl and season with a pinch of salt and pepper. If using chickpea flour, combine the chickpea flour with water to create a batter.
  3. Pour the eggs or chickpea batter over the potatoes and onions, making sure everything is evenly coated. Reduce the heat to low and cook for about 10 minutes until the edges start to set.
  4. Carefully flip the tortilla using a plate or large lid and cook for another 5-10 minutes until fully set.
  5. Let it cool slightly before slicing into wedges. Serve warm or at room temperature.

4. Gazpacho

Gazpacho is a refreshing, chilled soup that’s perfect for hot summer days. This Andalusian specialty is made with fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, onions, and garlic, all blended together into a smooth, savory concoction. Gazpacho is light, flavorful, and packed with nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 cups cold water
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, onion, and garlic in a blender or food processor.
  2. Add olive oil, red wine vinegar, and cold water. Blend until smooth.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, then refrigerate the soup for at least an hour to chill.
  4. Serve the gazpacho cold, garnished with a drizzle of olive oil and a few fresh herbs if desired. It’s perfect for a hot day or as a starter to a larger meal.

5. Pisto Manchego

Pisto Manchego is Spain’s answer to ratatouille. This dish is a flavorful vegetable stew made with tomatoes, zucchini, bell peppers, onions, and eggplant. It’s typically served with a fried egg on top, but for a vegan version, simply skip the egg and enjoy it as a hearty vegetable medley.

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 eggplant, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, zucchini, and eggplant. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic and tomatoes and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down and the vegetables are tender.
  3. Season with salt and pepper and simmer for another 5 minutes to meld the flavors together.
  4. Serve the pisto hot, garnished with fresh parsley. For a non-vegan option, top with a fried egg.

6. Chickpea and Spinach Stew (Espinacas con Garbanzos)

Espinacas con Garbanzos is a traditional Spanish stew from Seville that combines chickpeas with spinach in a savory broth. This dish is simple yet packed with flavor, often flavored with garlic, cumin, and a touch of paprika for added depth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 4 cups spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Stir in the cumin and smoked paprika, cooking for another minute.
  3. Add the spinach and cook until wilted.
  4. Add the chickpeas and vegetable broth, bringing the stew to a simmer. Cook for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve hot with crusty bread.

7. Tortas de Aceite (Spanish Olive Oil Cakes)

Tortas de Aceite are a delightful Spanish snack made from olive oil, flour, sugar, and sesame seeds. These thin, crispy cakes are a perfect treat with a cup of tea or coffee.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon anise seeds (optional)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, olive oil, sugar, sesame seeds, anise seeds (if using), and a pinch of salt.
  3. Mix until a dough forms, then roll out the dough to about ¼ inch thick.
  4. Cut the dough into rounds and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 10-12 minutes, until golden and crisp. Let cool before serving.

8. Escalivada

Escalivada is a simple yet flavorful Catalan dish made with roasted vegetables. The vegetables—typically eggplant, peppers, and onions—are charred, peeled, and served in a dressing of olive oil, garlic, and vinegar.

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggplants
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 onion
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roast the eggplants, bell peppers, and onion on a baking sheet for about 30-40 minutes until the skin is charred.
  3. Let the vegetables cool, then peel off the skins and slice them into strips.
  4. Mix the vegetables with olive oil, garlic, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  5. Serve at room temperature or slightly chilled.

9. Berenjenas con Miel (Eggplant with Honey)

Berenjenas con Miel is a unique Spanish dish from Andalusia that combines fried eggplant with a drizzle of honey. The sweetness of the honey complements the savory fried eggplant, making for an irresistible dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggplants, sliced into rounds
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Olive oil, for frying
  • 3 tablespoons honey

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour and salt in a shallow dish. Dredge the eggplant slices in the flour mixture, coating them evenly.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the eggplant slices in batches until golden and crispy.
  3. Remove the eggplant slices from the pan and drain them on paper towels.
  4. Drizzle with honey before serving. Enjoy!

10. Sopa de Ajo (Garlic Soup)

Sopa de Ajo is a comforting and flavorful garlic soup from Castile. With its rich garlic broth, this soup is perfect for a cozy dinner. It can be made vegetarian by using vegetable broth instead of meat stock.

Ingredients:

  • 10 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 slices of stale bread
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 3 minutes.
  2. Stir in the smoked paprika and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add the stale bread and cook for 10-15 minutes until the bread softens and the flavors meld together.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Spain’s vegetarian offerings are varied and bursting with flavor. These 10 dishes prove that you don’t need meat to create hearty, delicious meals. Whether you’re preparing a casual tapas spread, a comforting stew, or a fresh salad, these vegetarian Spanish recipes will transport your taste buds straight to Spain. So gather your ingredients, get cooking, and enjoy the rich, flavorful world of Spanish vegetarian cuisine!
