Quick Mexican Rice Bowls for Lunch on the Go


When it comes to quick and easy lunch options, few things beat a bowl of flavorful Mexican rice. Cooked with onions, bell peppers, and tomatoes, this simple dish is a staple of Mexican cuisine. But why settle for just a bowl of rice when you can make it into a filling meal that's perfect for lunch on the go? Ingredients

1 cup uncooked white or brown rice 2 medium onions, diced 2 medium bell peppers, diced 2 medium tomatoes, diced 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon chili powder Salt and pepper to taste 1 cup shredded cheese (Monterey Jack or Cheddar work well) 1 cup cooked chicken (optional) Salsa, diced avocado, and sour cream for topping (optional)

Instructions

Cook the rice: In a medium saucepan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced onions and cook for 2-3 minutes, until translucent. Add the diced bell peppers and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until tender. Add the diced tomatoes and cook for 1-2 minutes. Add the rice: Stir in the cooked rice and cook for 2-3 minutes, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Add the cumin and chili powder, and stir to combine. Season to taste: Add salt and pepper to taste, and adjust the seasoning as needed. Optional protein: If using cooked chicken, stir it in at this point. Assemble the bowls: Divide the rice mixture among four bowls. Top each bowl with shredded cheese, diced avocado, and a dollop of sour cream (if using). Add salsa and finishing touches: Serve the bowls with a side of warm tortilla chips and your favorite salsa. For added convenience, portion the toppings onto the side, so you can customize each bowl to your liking.

Make-Ahead Tips For a quicker lunch, cook the rice mixture ahead of time and store it in the fridge for up to a day. Simply reheat the mixture in the microwave or on the stovetop before assembling the bowls. Tips for Customizing Mexican rice bowls are a versatile canvas for your favorite toppings. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

Add some heat: For an extra spicy kick, sprinkle diced jalapenos or serrano peppers over the rice mixture. Go tropical: Mix in some diced mango or pineapple for a sweet and tangy twist. Get your greens: Add a handful of chopped fresh cilantro or scallions to add flavor and freshness.

Variations to Try

Breakfast bowls: Swap in scrambled eggs and diced cooked sausage for a breakfast-inspired Mexican rice bowl. Carnitas bowls: Add shredded carnitas (slow-cooked pork) to the rice mixture for a hearty and satisfying meal. Vegan bowls: Replace the cheese with vegan shredded cheese or diced avocado, and skip the optional chicken for a plant-based meal.

By following this simple recipe and experimenting with different toppings and variations, you can create a delicious and convenient Mexican rice bowl that's perfect for grabbing on the go. So next time you're in a lunchtime rush, try making these Quick Mexican Rice Bowls for a flavorful and satisfying meal that's sure to become a favorite.
