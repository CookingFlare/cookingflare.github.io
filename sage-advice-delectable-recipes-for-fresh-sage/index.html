Sage Advice: Delectable Recipes for Fresh Sage


Sage is a versatile herb that adds a delightful earthy flavor to dishes and pairs well with a variety of ingredients. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out in the kitchen, fresh sage can elevate your culinary creations to new heights. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delectable recipes that showcase the unique flavor of fresh sage. From appetizers to main courses and even desserts, there’s a sage-infused dish for every occasion.

Savory Sage and Butter Roasted Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 10 fresh sage leaves
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Rinse the chicken and pat it dry with paper towels.
  3. Gently lift the skin of the chicken and slide the fresh sage leaves underneath.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the softened butter with minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  5. Rub the butter mixture all over the chicken, including underneath the skin.
  6. Cut the lemon in half and place it inside the cavity of the chicken.
  7. Place the chicken in a roasting pan and roast for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Baste the chicken with the pan juices every 30 minutes.
  8. Once cooked, let the chicken rest for 10 minutes before carving.

This savory roasted chicken with a hint of fresh sage will surely impress your dinner guests and leave them craving for more.

Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 8 fresh sage leaves, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and diced butternut squash, and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the arborio rice to the pan and toast for 2 minutes.
  5. Pour in the white wine and cook until it has been absorbed by the rice.
  6. Begin adding the broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly and allowing each addition to be absorbed before adding more.
  7. When the rice is almost tender, stir in the chopped sage leaves and grated Parmesan cheese.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This creamy butternut squash and sage risotto makes for a comforting and flavorful meal that’s perfect for a cozy night in.

Sage Infused Apple Crisp

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups sliced and peeled apples
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 10 fresh sage leaves
  • 1 cup old-fashioned oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, lemon juice, ground cinnamon, and ground nutmeg.
  3. Place half of the apple mixture in a greased baking dish and layer with fresh sage leaves.
  4. Cover with the remaining apple mixture.
  5. In another bowl, mix together the oats, flour, brown sugar, and melted butter until crumbly.
  6. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the apples and sage leaves.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the apples are tender.

This sage-infused apple crisp is a delightful twist on a classic dessert, with the aromatic herb adding a subtle, savory note to the sweet and tangy apples.

Conclusion

Fresh sage is a culinary gem that can transform ordinary dishes into extraordinary culinary creations. Its earthy, peppery flavor and aromatic fragrance make it a delightful addition to a wide array of recipes. Whether you’re incorporating it into savory dishes like roasted chicken and risotto, or experimenting with it in sweet treats like apple crisp, fresh sage is sure to impress your taste buds and leave a lasting impression on your guests. So, next time you’re in the kitchen, don’t hesitate to reach for this wonderful herb and let its enchanting flavor take center stage in your cooking endeavors.
