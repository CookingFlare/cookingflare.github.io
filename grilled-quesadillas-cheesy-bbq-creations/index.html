Grilled Quesadillas: Cheesy BBQ Creations


There’s something irresistible about the combination of cheese, barbecue, and a crispy grilled tortilla. Grilled quesadillas are a delightful fusion of flavors and textures that make for a perfect snack, appetizer, or even a main course. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of creating the ultimate grilled quesadillas with a cheesy BBQ twist that will leave your taste buds singing.

Ingredients

For the Quesadillas:

  • Flour tortillas
  • Shredded cheddar cheese
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Cooked and shredded chicken or pulled pork
  • Diced onions
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Chopped cilantro
  • BBQ sauce

For the BBQ Sauce:

  • Ketchup
  • Brown sugar
  • Apple cider vinegar
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Garlic powder
  • Onion powder
  • Smoked paprika
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the BBQ Sauce

  1. In a saucepan, combine ketchup, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Simmer the mixture over low heat, stirring occasionally, for about 15-20 minutes until it thickens to a BBQ sauce consistency.

Step 2: Assemble the Quesadillas

  1. Lay out a flour tortilla and sprinkle a generous amount of cheddar and mozzarella cheese on one half of the tortilla.
  2. Add the cooked and shredded chicken or pulled pork on top of the cheese.
  3. Sprinkle diced onions, tomatoes, and chopped cilantro over the meat.
  4. Drizzle a spoonful of the homemade BBQ sauce over the filling.

Step 3: Grill the Quesadillas

  1. Fold the tortilla over the filling to create a half-moon shape.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and place the assembled quesadilla in the pan.
  3. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the tortilla turns golden brown and the cheese melts to perfection.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Remove the grilled quesadilla from the pan and let it cool for a minute.
  2. Cut the quesadilla into wedges and serve with extra BBQ sauce for dipping.

Tips and Tricks

  • Experiment with different cheese blends such as pepper jack, Monterey jack, or even a smoky gouda for a unique flavor profile.
  • For a vegetarian option, swap the meat with sautéed bell peppers, mushrooms, and spinach.
  • Don’t limit yourself to chicken or pork; leftover BBQ brisket or even grilled shrimp can take your quesadillas to the next level.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought BBQ sauce, but nothing beats the depth of flavor in a homemade sauce.

Final Thoughts

Grilled quesadillas with a cheesy BBQ twist are a crowd-pleaser that never fails to impress. The gooey cheese, savory meat, and tangy BBQ sauce create a harmonious blend of flavors that will have everyone coming back for more. Whether you’re hosting a summer cookout or simply craving a comforting snack, these cheesy BBQ creations are sure to be a hit. So fire up the grill or heat up the skillet, and get ready to indulge in the irresistible allure of grilled quesadillas.
