Homemade Chicken Pot Pie Soup: All the Flavors, Less Effort


If you love the comforting flavors of chicken pot pie but want a simpler way to enjoy them, this Homemade Chicken Pot Pie Soup recipe is the answer to your culinary dreams. It’s a soul-warming, hearty soup that captures all the essence of a traditional chicken pot pie in a much easier and quicker format. With a rich and creamy base, tender pieces of chicken, and a medley of vegetables, this soup is a delightful way to savor the classic flavors of the beloved pot pie without the hassle of making a crust.

Ingredients

For the Soup:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded or diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Topping:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup milk

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Soup:

    • In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are softened.
    • Stir in the dried thyme, rosemary, and all-purpose flour. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
    • Gradually pour in the chicken broth, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Add the milk and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.
    • Add the cooked chicken and frozen peas to the pot. Let the soup simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  2. Make the Topping:

    • In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt for the topping. Cut in the cold, cubed butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Pour in the milk and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

  3. Assemble and Serve:

    • Ladle the hot soup into individual oven-safe bowls or ramekins. Drop spoonfuls of the topping mixture on the surface of the soup.
    • Place the bowls on a baking sheet and transfer to a preheated oven. Bake at 400°F for 20-25 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the soup is bubbling.
    • Carefully remove from the oven and let the soup cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For a time-saving option, you can use rotisserie chicken for this recipe.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetable mix based on your preferences. You can add diced potatoes, parsnips, or even mushrooms.
  • To make the soup ahead of time, prepare the base and store it in the refrigerator. When ready to serve, make the topping and bake as directed.

Final Thoughts

This Homemade Chicken Pot Pie Soup is a delightful twist on the classic pot pie, offering all the cozy flavors in a convenient and comforting soup form. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner or looking to impress guests with a unique and delicious dish, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, grab a ladle, savor the aroma, and enjoy a heartwarming bowl of homemade comfort.
