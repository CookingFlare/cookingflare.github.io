Instant Pot Ramen: A Quick and Delicious Japanese Dish


Ramen has long been a favorite comfort food around the globe, combining slurp-worthy noodles, savory broth, and flavorful toppings in one dish. This Japanese culinary icon is as versatile as it is satisfying, with endless ways to customize it to suit your palate. While traditional ramen may require hours of simmering to achieve that signature depth of flavor from its broth, the Instant Pot has revolutionized the process, allowing anyone to create a delicious homemade version in a fraction of the time.

By using an Instant Pot, you can enjoy authentic ramen without the labor-intensive effort typically required. This method simplifies the process while preserving the irresistible complexity of flavors that make ramen so special. In this post, you’ll learn how to prepare Instant Pot ramen that’s both quick and undeniably delicious, bringing the bold tastes of Japan into your kitchen.

Why Make Ramen in an Instant Pot?

The Instant Pot is a game-changer for home cooks who crave efficiency without compromising on flavor. When it comes to ramen, pressure cooking allows you to extract all the rich taste from your ingredients quickly. Traditional broth-making often involves hours of simmering bones and vegetables to achieve the perfect balance; with the Instant Pot, you can achieve similar results in just 30 minutes to an hour.

Additionally, the Instant Pot keeps the cooking mess to a minimum. Instead of juggling multiple pots and pans for your broth, noodles, and toppings, you can streamline the process with this multifunctional appliance. Whether you’re a ramen enthusiast or just dipping your toes into Japanese cuisine, this recipe will make your Instant Pot a kitchen hero.

The Secrets to Perfect Instant Pot Ramen

Ramen is about balance. Each component plays a critical role in creating the perfect bowl of noodles: the broth serves as the soul of the dish, the noodles as its body, and the toppings as its personality. This recipe focuses on building a rich, umami-packed broth, perfectly cooked noodles, and complementary toppings to make your ramen bowl complete.

Here’s how you’ll break it down:

  1. The Broth: A flavorful, aromatic liquid made with ingredients like soup bones, ginger, garlic, and soy sauce.
  2. Noodles: Authentic ramen noodles are a must. You’ll learn how to ensure they remain springy and firm, even when cooked in the Instant Pot.
  3. Toppings: From soft-boiled eggs and sliced scallions to sautéed mushrooms or tender slices of pork, these additions elevate your bowl and make it unforgettable.

Now, let’s dive into the recipe!

Instant Pot Ramen Recipe: A Step-by-Step Guide

This Instant Pot ramen recipe is tailored for a medium-sized batch, great for sharing or storing leftovers. It serves about 4 generous portions.

Ingredients

Broth:

  • 4 cups chicken broth or pork broth (homemade or store-bought)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 pound pork bones (optionally substitute with chicken wings or drumsticks for a lighter broth)
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sake (optional for extra depth)
  • 1 tablespoon miso paste
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons white sugar
  • 2 green onions (use the whole stalk; save some for garnishing)

Noodles & Toppings:

  • 4 servings of fresh ramen noodles or dried ramen noodles (substitute with thin egg noodles if needed)
  • 4 soft-boiled eggs, halved
  • Thinly sliced pork belly or roasted chicken (about 1 cup total)
  • 1 cup sautéed shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup bean sprouts (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds
  • Thinly sliced scallions for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Broth

Start by creating the heart of your ramen—a bold and aromatic broth.

  1. Set your Instant Pot to the sauté mode. Add the sesame oil.
  2. Once heated, toss the ginger slices and smashed garlic into the pot. Sauté until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  3. Add the pork bones (or chicken wings), whole green onions, and miso paste. Cook for another 2 minutes to enhance their flavors.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth, water, soy sauce, sake, and sugar. Stir until the miso paste dissolves completely.
  5. Cancel the sauté mode and seal the Instant Pot lid. Set it to “Pressure Cook” on high for 40 minutes.

Step 2: Make Your Soft-Boiled Eggs and Prep Toppings

While the broth is cooking, prepare the toppings.

  1. Boil water in a small saucepan and gently lower the eggs into the boiling water. Cook for 7 minutes for a perfectly soft yolk.
  2. Remove the eggs and immediately place them in ice water to halt cooking. Peel and slice in half.
  3. Thinly slice your scallions and pork belly or roast chicken. Sauté mushrooms, and shred carrots if you prefer them cooked.

Step 3: Cook the Noodles

Fresh ramen noodles cook quickly, so leave this step for last to avoid soggy noodles.

  1. Bring a separate pot of water to a boil.
  2. Add the noodles and cook according to the package instructions. Typically, 1-2 minutes is enough for fresh noodles and 3-5 minutes for dried.
  3. Drain and rinse under cold water to prevent them from sticking.

Step 4: Assemble Your Ramen Bowl

Once the Instant Pot finishes its pressure cycle, allow it to naturally release for about 10 minutes before manually releasing the remaining pressure. Strain the broth through a fine sieve or cheesecloth into a clean pot to remove bones and solids.

Now, it’s time to assemble your ramen bowl:

  1. Start by adding a portion of cooked noodles to a deep bowl.
  2. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles generously.
  3. Arrange toppings over the noodles—add soft-boiled egg halves, pork belly or chicken slices, mushrooms, carrots, and bean sprouts.
  4. Sprinkle with scallions, toasted sesame seeds, and a drizzle of chili oil or extra sesame oil for added flair.

Tips for Customizing Your Instant Pot Ramen

Ramen is an incredibly adaptable dish. Here are a few customization tips to make it your own:

  • Vegetarian Version: Replace the chicken or pork broth with vegetable broth, omit the meat toppings, and load your ramen bowls with sautéed tofu, spinach, and extra mushrooms.
  • Spicy Kick: Mix in a dollop of chili paste or sriracha into the broth for a fiery flavor explosion.
  • Rich Creaminess: Add a splash of coconut milk or heavy cream to the broth before serving for a luscious texture.
  • Crunch Factor: Finish your bowl with crispy fried shallots or garlic chips for a satisfying crunch.

Storing and Reheating

Ramen is best enjoyed fresh, but the broth can be made ahead of time for convenience. Store the broth in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days or freeze it for up to 3 months. When reheating, bring it to a simmer on the stovetop or in the Instant Pot using the “sauté” function. Cook the noodles fresh when ready to serve, as reheating them can lead to an undesirable mushy texture.

Bringing Authentic Ramen to Your Table

Making homemade ramen in an Instant Pot is a celebration of flavor, speed, and ease. It’s proof that you can create a restaurant-quality dish in the comfort of your kitchen without a full day’s effort. Whether you’re impressing dinner guests or treating yourself to a cozy meal, this recipe is sure to satisfy your ramen cravings and transport you to the bustling alleys of Japan where this soul-warming dish is a way of life.

Grab your Instant Pot, gather your ingredients, and embark on this culinary journey—it’s time to create a bowl of ramen that’s both quick and unforgettable!
