10 Delicious Vegan and Vegetarian Pudding Recipes


Pudding is one of those timeless desserts that can be enjoyed by people of all ages. With a rich, creamy texture and the ability to be flavored in endless ways, it’s no surprise that this indulgent treat has such a loyal following. While traditional puddings are often made with dairy and eggs, there are many fantastic alternatives that cater to a vegan and vegetarian lifestyle without compromising on flavor or texture. In this post, we’ll explore 10 delightful vegan and vegetarian pudding recipes that are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth while keeping you feeling good about what you eat.

1. Classic Vegan Chocolate Pudding

There’s nothing quite like the smooth, rich taste of chocolate pudding. This vegan chocolate pudding is made without dairy and eggs but is just as decadent as the traditional version.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the coconut milk, cornstarch, cocoa powder, maple syrup, and sea salt until smooth.
  2. Place the saucepan over medium heat and continue to whisk constantly as the mixture thickens.
  3. Once the pudding has reached your desired consistency, remove it from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the pudding into individual serving dishes and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.
  5. Garnish with chocolate shavings or fresh berries for extra flair.

2. Creamy Vegan Rice Pudding

Rice pudding is a comforting dessert that is beloved across many cultures. This vegan rice pudding is creamy, warm, and perfect for a cozy night in.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar (or your preferred sweetener)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the rice, almond milk, and salt. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and cook for about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender and the pudding has thickened.
  3. Stir in the coconut sugar, vanilla extract, and cinnamon. Continue cooking for another 5-10 minutes, until fully incorporated.
  4. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving. This rice pudding can be enjoyed warm or chilled, topped with extra cinnamon or a handful of raisins.

3. Chia Seed Pudding with Coconut Milk

Chia seed pudding is an incredibly healthy dessert that’s also rich in omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and protein. This simple recipe uses coconut milk to create a deliciously creamy texture.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons chia seeds
  • 1 cup coconut milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the coconut milk, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  2. Stir in the chia seeds, making sure they’re evenly distributed in the liquid.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight. The chia seeds will absorb the liquid and create a thick, pudding-like consistency.
  4. Serve with fresh fruit, nuts, or granola for added texture.

4. Vegan Mango Pudding

Mango pudding is a popular dessert in many Asian countries, and this vegan version is just as sweet and satisfying as its non-vegan counterpart.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons agar-agar powder (or vegan gelatin)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon lime juice

Instructions:

  1. Blend the mangoes, coconut milk, and maple syrup in a blender until smooth.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine 1/4 cup of water and the agar-agar powder. Heat over medium heat until it dissolves.
  3. Add the mango mixture to the saucepan with the dissolved agar-agar. Stir well and bring to a boil, then simmer for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the lime juice and remove from heat.
  5. Pour the mixture into molds or individual serving dishes and let it cool for 1-2 hours to set.
  6. Once set, serve chilled and garnish with fresh mango slices.

5. Banana Pudding (Vegan Version)

Banana pudding is a beloved Southern treat, and this vegan version offers all the flavor without the dairy. It’s rich, creamy, and packed with banana goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 2 cups coconut milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the coconut milk, coconut sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Heat over medium heat, whisking constantly, until the mixture thickens.
  2. Stir in the mashed bananas and vanilla extract. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  3. Remove from heat and pour the pudding into serving dishes.
  4. Let the pudding cool to room temperature before refrigerating for at least 2 hours.
  5. Serve with vanilla wafers or a sprinkle of cinnamon for extra flavor.

6. Avocado Chocolate Pudding

Avocados may not be the first ingredient that comes to mind when you think of pudding, but they create a wonderfully smooth and creamy texture, making them perfect for a healthy chocolate pudding.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Scoop the flesh from the avocados and place it in a food processor or blender.
  2. Add the cocoa powder, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and sea salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Taste the pudding and adjust the sweetness as needed by adding more maple syrup.
  4. Chill the pudding in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour before serving.
  5. Serve topped with whipped coconut cream or berries for a refreshing contrast.

7. Coconut Tapioca Pudding

Tapioca pudding is a delightful dessert that’s chewy and satisfying. The coconut milk adds a tropical flair, making this pudding even more delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup tapioca pearls
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the tapioca pearls and coconut milk. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and cook for 15-20 minutes, until the tapioca pearls become translucent and the pudding thickens.
  3. Stir in the maple syrup, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt.
  4. Remove from heat and let the pudding cool before transferring to serving dishes.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving. This pudding can be enjoyed warm or cold.

8. Vegan Lemon Pudding

This tangy and sweet vegan lemon pudding is perfect for those who enjoy citrusy desserts. It’s light, refreshing, and has a vibrant flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice (about 3 lemons)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the lemon juice, coconut milk, maple syrup, cornstarch, and lemon zest.
  2. Heat over medium heat, whisking constantly, until the mixture thickens and becomes smooth.
  3. Once thickened, remove from heat and pour the pudding into individual serving dishes.
  4. Let the pudding cool to room temperature before refrigerating for at least 2 hours.
  5. Serve chilled, garnished with extra lemon zest or mint leaves.

9. Chia and Matcha Pudding

For those who enjoy the unique flavor of matcha, this chia and matcha pudding offers a combination of earthy and creamy flavors that will keep you coming back for more.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons chia seeds
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon matcha powder
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the almond milk, matcha powder, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  2. Stir in the chia seeds and make sure they’re evenly distributed throughout the mixture.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  4. Once the pudding has thickened, give it a good stir and serve with your favorite toppings like fresh fruit or coconut flakes.

10. Vegan Pumpkin Pudding

Pumpkin pudding is a seasonal favorite, perfect for fall or any time you’re craving a comforting dessert with warm spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 1/2 cups coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ginger
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the pumpkin puree, coconut milk, maple syrup, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt.
  2. Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the pudding thickens and becomes smooth.
  3. Remove from heat and pour into serving dishes.
  4. Let the pudding cool slightly before refrigerating for at least 2 hours.
  5. Serve with whipped coconut cream or a sprinkle of cinnamon on top.

Whether you’re craving something rich and chocolatey, fresh and fruity, or comforting and creamy, these vegan and vegetarian pudding recipes offer a wide variety of options to satisfy your sweet cravings. All of these puddings are simple to make, customizable, and perfect for any occasion. Enjoy exploring these plant-based alternatives that prove you don’t need dairy or eggs to create indulgent, satisfying desserts!
