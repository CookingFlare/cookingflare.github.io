Vegan Lemon Bars Recipe with a Shortbread Crust and a Tangy Filling


If you’re craving a zesty and indulgent dessert that’s completely plant-based, then you’re in for a treat. This vegan lemon bars recipe combines the buttery goodness of a shortbread crust with a tangy, refreshing lemon filling. It’s the perfect balance of sweet and sour, and it’s sure to impress vegans and non-vegans alike.

Shortbread Crust Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter, chilled and cubed
  • A pinch of salt

Tangy Lemon Filling Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup lemon juice (freshly squeezed for the best flavor)
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • A pinch of turmeric (for color)

Instructions

Making the Shortbread Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.
  2. In a food processor, combine the all-purpose flour, powdered sugar, chilled vegan butter, and a pinch of salt. Pulse the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs and starts to come together.
  3. Press the crust mixture firmly and evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Use the back of a spoon or a flat-bottomed glass to ensure a smooth and compact crust.
  4. Bake the crust for 20-25 minutes, or until it’s lightly golden. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool while you prepare the lemon filling.

Creating the Tangy Lemon Filling

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and cornstarch until well combined.
  2. Add the lemon juice, lemon zest, full-fat coconut milk, all-purpose flour, and a pinch of turmeric to the bowl. Whisk everything together until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour the lemon filling over the cooled shortbread crust, spreading it out into an even layer.
  4. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are lightly golden.

Cooling and Serving

  1. Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and allow the lemon bars to cool at room temperature for about 30 minutes.
  2. Transfer the pan to the refrigerator and chill the bars for at least 2-3 hours, or until they are completely set.
  3. When ready to serve, use the parchment paper overhang to lift the bars out of the pan. Place them on a cutting board and slice into squares using a sharp knife.
  4. Dust the tops of the lemon bars with powdered sugar for a beautiful finishing touch.

Tips and Variations

  • For a gluten-free version, you can use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend in both the crust and the filling.
  • Experiment with different citrus fruits such as limes or oranges to create unique variations of this recipe.
  • If you prefer a more intense yellow color for the filling, you can add a small amount of natural food coloring.

Final Thoughts

These vegan lemon bars are a delightful fusion of rich, buttery shortbread and a zingy, citrusy filling. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself, this recipe is a must-try for anyone who appreciates the bright flavors of lemon. Serve these bars at your next brunch, potluck, or afternoon tea, and watch them disappear in no time. Enjoy every bite of these sweet and tangy vegan lemon bars!
