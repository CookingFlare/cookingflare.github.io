Nut-Free Feasts: 30 Delectable Nut-Free Recipes for a Bounty of Tasty Eats


Are you searching for the perfect dishes to serve at your next gathering that cater to your nut-free guests? Perhaps you’ve discovered a nut allergy recently and are in need of inspiration to keep your meals exciting and satisfying without nuts. Worry not, for this guide is sure to provide you with a collection of ingenious nut-free recipes that are sure to ignite your senses and delight your taste buds!

  1. Berry Burst Chocolate Pudding Indulge in a luscious dessert that offers a delightful twist on the classic chocolate pudding. Made with cocoa powder, silken tofu, maple syrup, and your choice of fresh or frozen berries.

Ingredients:

  • 1 14 oz package of silken tofu
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen berries (strawberries, raspberries, or blueberries)

Instructions:

  1. Blend all the ingredients, including the berries, until you have a smooth mixture.

  2. Divide the mixture into serving glasses or cups.

  3. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.

  4. Creamy Veggie Pasta Comfort food at its finest, this pasta dish has all your classic favorites while skipping the nuts. Made with hearty vegetables, melted cheese, and a cashew-based sauce for a creamy finish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound penne pasta
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small zucchini, diced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup raw cashews
  • 2 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to package instructions.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil, and sauté zucchini, carrot, bell pepper, onion, and garlic until soft.

  3. In a blender, combine vegetable broth, cashews, miso paste, and nutmeg. Blend until smooth, then pour over the sautéed vegetables.

  4. Add cooked pasta and mix well, cooking until sauce thickens slightly.

  5. Stir in shredded Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Savory Lemon-Herb Roasted Chicken This simple yet delicious roasted chicken is infused with a mouthwatering blend of lemon and herbs to create a savory feast without the need for nuts.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (3-4 pounds)
  • 2 lemons, zested and juiced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.

  2. In a small bowl, combine lemon zest, lemon juice, garlic, rosemary, thyme, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

  3. Rub the mixture all over the chicken, making sure to get under the skin.

  4. Place the chicken in a roasting pan and brush any remaining mixture on top.

  5. Roast the chicken for approximately 1 hour or until internal temperature reaches 165°F.

  6. Fluffy Scallion Pancakes Bring an Asian-inspired twist to your table with these delicious and tender scallion pancakes. This recipe features a dipping sauce made from silken tofu and tamari, for a satisfying taste without nuts.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup silken tofu
  • 1/4 cup tamari or soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for cooking)

For the dipping sauce:

  • 1/2 cup silken tofu
  • 1/4 cup tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and water together until the dough forms a ball.
  2. Knead dough on a floured surface until smooth, approximately 5-7 minutes.
  3. Cover dough with a towel and let rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Roll out the dough into a thin rectangle, approximately 1/8-inch thickness.
  5. Sprinkle chopped scallions on half of the dough, then fold the other half over.
  6. Roll the dough tightly into a log, then slice into 1-inch thick pieces.
  7. Roll each piece into a small ball, then flatten slightly with the palm of your hand.
  8. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  9. Cook pancakes in small batches, until golden brown on each side.
  10. To make the dipping sauce, blend silken tofu, tamari, and sesame oil in a blender until smooth.
  11. Serve pancakes warm with the silken tofu-tamari dipping sauce.

Next time you’re getting ready to prepare a meal or host a gathering, remember that you can easily create delightful and satisfying nut-free dishes. Not only will these recipes be enjoyed by your nut-allergic friends and family, but they will also tempt everyone else with their flavor and charm. Happy cooking and enjoy your Nut-Free Feasts!
