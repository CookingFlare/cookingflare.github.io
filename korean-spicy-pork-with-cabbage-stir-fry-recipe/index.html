Korean Spicy Pork with Cabbage Stir-Fry Recipe


There’s something undeniably comforting about a sizzling, wholesome stir-fry served straight from the pan. Korean Spicy Pork with Cabbage Stir-Fry is a vibrant, flavorful dish that embodies the essence of Korean cuisine. A delightful medley of bold, spicy flavors paired with tender pork and crunchy cabbage, this recipe is perfect for those who crave a well-balanced dish that’s easy to prepare yet rich in taste.

While this recipe is deeply rooted in traditional Korean cooking practices, it also allows for customization, making it a versatile favorite for cooks of all levels. Whether you’re an experienced home chef or just beginning your culinary adventure, this dish is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Let’s dive straight into how you can make this irresistible meal for yourself!

Ingredients You’ll Need

For this recipe, you’ll want to assemble your ingredients first. Finding quality ingredients is essential in crafting authentic flavors. Most items should be readily available in your local grocery store or an Asian supermarket. Here’s what you’ll need:

Main Ingredients

  • 500g (about 1 lb) of thinly sliced pork belly or pork shoulder
  • 2 cups of green cabbage, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 2-3 scallions, chopped into 1-inch sections
  • 1 small zucchini, julienned (optional)

Marinade Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons of Korean gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) for an extra kick
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar (adjust based on your sweetness preference)
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 4 cloves of garlic, finely minced
  • 1 teaspoon of grated ginger
  • ½ teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of toasted sesame seeds (optional)

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

1. Prepare the Pork and Vegetables

Begin by slicing your pork into thin pieces to ensure even cooking. Thin cuts absorb marinade flavors better and cook faster, resulting in tender and juicy meat. Next, rinse your cabbage and all other vegetables under cold water, pat them dry with a paper towel, and slice them accordingly. Bite-sized, uniform pieces make stir-frying and eating the dish much easier.

2. Make the Marinade

In a mixing bowl, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sesame oil, honey (or sugar), rice vinegar, minced garlic, grated ginger, black pepper, and sesame seeds. Mix thoroughly until you achieve a smooth, cohesive marinade. Taste-test to ensure it’s balanced – adjust sweetness, spiciness, or saltiness to suit your preference.

3. Marinate the Pork

Add the sliced pork into the marinade. Use your hands (or a spoon) to coat each piece evenly. The marinade is the heart of this dish, infusing deep, fiery flavors into the pork. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the pork marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour. If you have more time, refrigerate overnight for optimal flavor absorption.

Cooking the Stir-Fry

4. Heat the Pan

Once your pork has marinated, it’s time to cook. Heat a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of neutral oil (such as vegetable oil) to the pan. Ensure the pan is hot enough to give the pork a nice sear when added.

5. Cook the Pork

Once the pan is heated, transfer the marinated pork into the skillet. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. The sugars in the marinade may caramelize as you cook, which adds a beautiful depth of flavor to the pork. Cook for 4-5 minutes until the pork begins to brown and sizzle.

6. Add the Vegetables

Push the pork to one side of the pan and add the sliced onions and carrots to the empty space. Stir and toss the vegetables for about 2 minutes or until they begin to soften slightly. Next, add the cabbage and zucchini. Stir everything together, ensuring the rich marinade coats the vegetables evenly. Cook for an additional 3-4 minutes or until the cabbage becomes tender but maintains a slight crunch.

7. Finish with Scallions

Fold in the scallions during the final minute of cooking. These add a pop of color, freshness, and mild onion-like flavor that brings the dish together beautifully. If desired, sprinkle additional sesame seeds on top for garnish.

Serving Suggestions

Korean Spicy Pork with Cabbage Stir-Fry is best enjoyed piping hot. Serve it alongside steaming bowls of jasmine rice or short-grain sticky rice for a hearty, satisfying meal. The rice acts as the perfect companion to balance out the bold, spicy flavors of the dish. For an authentic Korean touch, add a side of kimchi or pickled radish to your plate.

If you’re hosting a gathering, this stir-fry pairs wonderfully with other Korean side dishes like japchae, pajeon (Korean pancakes), or simple banchan (various small side dishes). Be sure to serve it family-style so everyone can dig in and enjoy the communal aspect of Korean meals.

Tips for Success

Adjust the Heat Level

If you’re sensitive to spice, reduce the amount of gochugaru or substitute gochujang with a milder chili paste. Conversely, if you’re a spice enthusiast, kick things up by adding sliced fresh chili peppers while cooking or serve with a drizzle of hot chili oil.

Quality Matters

For a truly authentic flavor, use ingredients imported from Korea, such as Korean soy sauce, gochujang, and gochugaru. They’re readily available in international food stores or online.

Don’t Overload the Pan

Ensure the wok or skillet isn’t overcrowded while cooking the pork and vegetables. If it’s too packed, the food will steam rather than stir-fry, resulting in a different texture. Cook in batches if necessary.

Batch Cooking

This dish can be scaled up easily for larger portions. It’s also great for meal prep – pack leftovers in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to three days. The flavors deepen after sitting, making reheated leftovers just as delicious.

Why You’ll Love This Dish

Korean Spicy Pork with Cabbage Stir-Fry stands out not just for its flavor but for how accessible it is to make. With a handful of staple ingredients and straightforward cooking techniques, you can recreate the taste of authentic Korean food right at home. Its blend of sweet, savory, spicy, and smoky undertones hits every note, delivering a dish that’s satisfying in every bite.

Moreover, the combination of meat and vegetables ensures you’re getting a balanced meal that’s high in protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. It’s as nourishing as it is delicious. Plus, it’s customizable, so you can tweak it to suit your taste preferences or to use up what’s available in your fridge.

Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or experimenting with new flavors, this Korean Spicy Pork with Cabbage Stir-Fry recipe is guaranteed to impress. So, tie your apron, gather your ingredients, and give this irresistible dish a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
