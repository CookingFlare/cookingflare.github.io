In Season: 5 Healthy Recipes with Fresh Spring Produce


Spring is the perfect time to enjoy the bounty of fresh produce that nature has to offer. From vibrant greens to juicy berries, the spring season brings an array of delicious and nutritious ingredients to our tables. In this blog post, we will explore five healthy recipes that showcase the best of the spring harvest. These recipes are not only packed with flavor but also provide a boost of vitamins and minerals to support your overall well-being.

1. Asparagus and Mushroom Frittata

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 25 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 8 asparagus spears, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 6 eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium-sized skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the asparagus, mushrooms, and onion to the skillet, and sauté for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the sautéed vegetables in the skillet, making sure they are evenly distributed.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the frittata is set and golden brown.
  7. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

2. Strawberry Spinach Salad

Preparation time: 10 minutes Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons sliced almonds
  • Balsamic vinaigrette dressing (to taste)

Instructions:

  1. In a large salad bowl, combine the spinach, strawberries, feta cheese, and sliced almonds.
  2. Drizzle the balsamic vinaigrette dressing over the salad, tossing gently to coat the ingredients evenly.
  3. Serve immediately and enjoy the refreshing combination of flavors.

3. Lemon Garlic Roasted Chicken with Spring Vegetables

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 1 hour 15 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 4 small red potatoes, quartered
  • 2 cups baby carrots
  • 1 cup snap peas, trimmed
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme sprigs (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large baking dish, toss the chicken thighs, potatoes, carrots, snap peas, minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt, and pepper together until well coated.
  3. Arrange the chicken and vegetables in a single layer in the baking dish.
  4. Roast in the preheated oven for 1 hour or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender, stirring occasionally.
  5. Remove from the oven and garnish with fresh thyme sprigs before serving.

4. Spring Vegetable Risotto

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 30 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup fresh peas
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, heat the vegetable broth over medium heat until simmering.
  2. In a separate large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the arborio rice to the pot and stir for a minute until it is well coated with the butter and onion mixture.
  4. Gradually add the simmering vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly until the liquid is absorbed before adding more.
  5. After about 20 minutes, when the rice is almost tender, add the asparagus and peas to the pot and continue stirring.
  6. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender and the rice is cooked to your desired consistency.
  7. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

5. Rhubarb Berry Crisp

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 45 minutes Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups rhubarb, chopped
  • 2 cups mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)
  • 1 cup oats
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Vanilla ice cream (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rhubarb, mixed berries, oats, flour, brown sugar, melted butter, and cinnamon. Mix well until the fruit is evenly coated.
  3. Transfer the fruit mixture to a greased baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes until the fruit is tender and the crisp topping is golden brown.
  5. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.
  6. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, if desired.

Enjoy these delicious and healthy recipes that celebrate the vibrant flavors of spring. Experiment with different combinations of fresh produce to create your own unique dishes. Embrace the season and savor the taste of nature’s bounty!
