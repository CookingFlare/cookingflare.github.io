Delicious Dairy-Free Chocolate Chip Cookies: A Perfect Allergy-Friendly Treat

Who doesn’t love a warm, gooey chocolate chip cookie fresh out of the oven? It’s a classic treat that brings joy to people of all ages. But what if you have a dairy allergy or follow a dairy-free diet? Don’t worry, because I’ve got you covered. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to make delicious dairy-free chocolate chip cookies that are just as tasty as their dairy-filled counterparts. Ingredients 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon baking soda 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted 1/2 cup brown sugar 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/4 cup almond milk (or any other dairy-free milk alternative) 1 cup dairy-free chocolate chips Instructions Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside. In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted coconut oil, brown sugar, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract. Stir until well combined. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing well after each addition. The dough should come together and be slightly sticky. Stir in the almond milk, followed by the dairy-free chocolate chips. Mix until the chocolate chips are evenly distributed throughout the dough. Using a cookie scoop or a tablespoon, drop rounded balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. The centers may still be soft, but they will firm up as the cookies cool. Remove from the oven and let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. Then, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. Tips and Variations For an extra burst of flavor, try adding a teaspoon of instant coffee granules to the dough. It will enhance the chocolate flavor and give your cookies a subtle mocha twist. If you’re a fan of nuts, feel free to add a handful of chopped walnuts or pecans to the dough. They will add a delightful crunch to each bite. To make these cookies gluten-free, simply substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend of your choice. Just make sure it’s suitable for baking cookies. Don’t overbake the cookies. They may seem slightly undercooked when you take them out of the oven, but they will continue to cook and firm up as they cool. Conclusion These dairy-free chocolate chip cookies are a true delight, perfect for anyone with a dairy allergy or those who simply prefer to avoid dairy in their diet. They have all the taste and texture you crave in a classic chocolate chip cookie, without any of the dairy. So go ahead, indulge in this allergy-friendly treat and enjoy every bite.
