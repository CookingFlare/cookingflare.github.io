Authentic Cantonese Shrimp Balls Recipe


Cantonese cuisine is celebrated for its delicate flavors, fresh ingredients, and a harmonious balance of textures. Among the many iconic dishes of Cantonese cooking, shrimp balls, or “蝦球” (pronounced “ha qiu”), stand out as a favorite appetizer or main course. These tender, juicy shrimp balls are often enjoyed at family gatherings, dim sum restaurants, or as a festive dish during special occasions. If you’ve ever tasted them, you’ll know just how irresistible these little morsels of seafood can be.

In this recipe, we will guide you through making authentic Cantonese shrimp balls from scratch, right in the comfort of your kitchen. By following this step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to recreate these delicious, springy, and flavorful shrimp balls that will have your taste buds dancing. Let’s dive into the world of Cantonese shrimp balls!

Ingredients for Authentic Cantonese Shrimp Balls

Before you start, it’s important to gather all the necessary ingredients to ensure the authenticity of your Cantonese shrimp balls. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Shrimp Balls:

  • 300 grams (10.5 oz) fresh shrimp (preferably large and raw)
  • 50 grams (1.75 oz) ground pork (optional but adds richness)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 egg white (this helps with binding and texture)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon rice wine (Shaoxing wine or dry white wine can also be used)
  • 1 small piece of ginger (finely minced)
  • 2 tablespoons water (for adjusting the consistency)

For the Coating:

  • 50 grams (1.75 oz) corn flour or potato starch (for that crisp, light coating)
  • Water (for binding the shrimp balls)

For Frying:

  • Vegetable oil (enough to deep fry the shrimp balls)

Optional Garnish:

  • Chopped cilantro or green onions for garnish (adds a fresh and vibrant look)
  • Sweet chili sauce or soy sauce with garlic (for dipping)

Preparing the Shrimp

Fresh shrimp is key to achieving the perfect texture for these shrimp balls. When selecting shrimp, look for those that are firm, slightly sweet in smell, and have a smooth, translucent appearance.

  1. Peel and Devein the Shrimp: Begin by peeling the shrimp, removing the shells, and deveining them. Ensure that all veins are removed, as they can add a gritty texture.
  2. Chop the Shrimp: Roughly chop the shrimp into smaller pieces. While you can use a food processor for this step, chopping by hand gives you more control over the texture. The goal is to break the shrimp into small pieces without turning them into a paste. A finely minced texture will give your shrimp balls the perfect bite.
  3. Salt the Shrimp: Place the chopped shrimp into a mixing bowl, add a pinch of salt, and mix well. Let them sit for about 5 minutes. This helps extract excess moisture, ensuring your shrimp balls won’t be too watery.

Making the Shrimp Ball Mixture

To make the shrimp balls light and bouncy, we will be combining several ingredients that enhance both flavor and texture.

  1. Add Ground Pork (Optional): If you want your shrimp balls to have a richer flavor, you can add a small amount of ground pork. The pork adds a tender and slightly fatty texture that complements the shrimp. Mix the pork with the chopped shrimp in a large bowl.
  2. Incorporate the Binding Ingredients: Next, add the egg white, cornstarch, oyster sauce, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and white pepper to the mixture. The egg white serves as a binder to hold everything together, while the cornstarch gives the shrimp balls their signature springy texture.
  3. Add Minced Ginger: Ginger is a staple in Cantonese cooking and adds a fragrant aroma and slight spiciness to the shrimp balls. Mix the ginger thoroughly into the shrimp mixture.
  4. Add Rice Wine: For a touch of depth and a slight sweetness, add the rice wine. This ingredient is essential in authentic Cantonese cooking and balances out the rich shrimp and pork flavors.
  5. Adjust Consistency: Gradually add water, a tablespoon at a time, to the mixture. You’re looking for a smooth, slightly sticky, and thick paste that can hold its shape when formed into balls. Don’t worry if it seems a little wet – this will help create that bouncy texture when cooked.

Once the mixture is well combined, use your hands or a spoon to shape the shrimp mixture into balls. The size of each ball can vary, but typically they are about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. Wetting your hands with water before shaping helps prevent the mixture from sticking to your hands.

Coating the Shrimp Balls

The delicate, crispy coating is one of the defining features of Cantonese shrimp balls. This light exterior ensures a perfect contrast between the crispy outside and tender, juicy shrimp inside.

  1. Prepare the Coating: In a shallow dish, add the corn flour or potato starch. This will provide the light and crispy texture you need.
  2. Coat the Shrimp Balls: Lightly roll each shrimp ball in the corn flour, ensuring that it is evenly coated. You don’t want to coat them too thickly, as this can affect the crispiness.

Frying the Shrimp Balls

Now it’s time for the fun part: frying the shrimp balls to golden perfection.

  1. Heat the Oil: In a deep pan or wok, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. To test if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of the shrimp mixture into the oil. If it sizzles immediately, the oil is ready.
  2. Fry the Shrimp Balls: Gently lower the coated shrimp balls into the hot oil using a slotted spoon. Fry them in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until the shrimp balls are golden brown and crispy on the outside, about 4-5 minutes.
  3. Drain the Shrimp Balls: Once fried, remove the shrimp balls from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

Serving Suggestions

Cantonese shrimp balls are best enjoyed while they are still hot and crispy. You can serve them as a standalone dish, paired with a dipping sauce such as sweet chili sauce, soy sauce with minced garlic, or a tangy vinegar-based sauce.

For a complete meal, these shrimp balls can be served alongside steamed rice or added to a vegetable stir-fry. Their delicate flavor makes them an ideal accompaniment to a variety of Cantonese dishes, from stir-fries to soups.

Garnish and Plating

To give the dish a vibrant touch, you can garnish the shrimp balls with freshly chopped cilantro or green onions. The bright green color will add contrast and freshness to the dish. If you’re serving the shrimp balls as an appetizer, arrange them on a decorative platter with a small bowl of dipping sauce in the center.

Tips for the Perfect Shrimp Balls

  • Use Fresh Shrimp: Fresh, high-quality shrimp will elevate the taste of your shrimp balls. Frozen shrimp can also work, but fresh shrimp provide the best texture.
  • Adjust Texture with Cornstarch: If your mixture feels too wet, add a little more cornstarch. If it’s too dry, a splash more water will do the trick.
  • Don’t Overmix: Overmixing the shrimp mixture can make the balls dense and tough. Mix just enough to combine all the ingredients evenly.
  • Test Frying Temperature: If the oil is too hot, the shrimp balls will burn on the outside without cooking through. If the oil is too cool, the shrimp balls will absorb too much oil and become greasy. Aim for a consistent temperature.

Variations on the Classic Recipe

While this recipe is for traditional Cantonese shrimp balls, there are many variations that you can try depending on your preferences:

  1. Spicy Shrimp Balls: Add some chopped chili or chili paste to the mixture for a spicy kick.
  2. Vegetarian Version: For a vegetarian version, substitute the shrimp with finely chopped mushrooms or tofu, keeping the same seasoning and binding ingredients.
  3. Baked Shrimp Balls: For a healthier alternative, you can bake the shrimp balls instead of frying them. Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

Conclusion

Authentic Cantonese shrimp balls are a perfect example of the region’s culinary artistry. With their delicate flavor, tender texture, and crispy exterior, they are sure to impress your family and friends. Whether you enjoy them as an appetizer, main dish, or part of a larger feast, they are a delightful treat that embodies the essence of Cantonese cuisine.

By following the steps outlined in this recipe, you can create your very own batch of Cantonese shrimp balls that taste just like those served in the finest dim sum restaurants. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to enjoy the deliciousness of homemade shrimp balls. Happy cooking!
