Seafood Ceviche Recipe with Shrimp, Mango, and Avocado


Ceviche is a refreshing and vibrant dish that originates from Latin America. It’s the perfect summer recipe, combining the fresh flavors of seafood with the sweetness of mango and the creaminess of avocado. In this recipe, we’ll be using shrimp as the main seafood ingredient, along with ripe mango and creamy avocado. The combination of these ingredients creates a delightful explosion of flavors in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 ripe mangoes, diced
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced
  • 1/2 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 4 limes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Tortilla chips, for serving

Instructions

  1. Start by cooking the shrimp. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and add the shrimp. Cook for about 2-3 minutes or until they turn pink and opaque. Avoid overcooking to ensure the shrimp remains tender. Once cooked, drain the shrimp and transfer them to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process. Drain again and set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the diced mangoes, avocados, red onion, jalapeno pepper, and fresh cilantro. Gently toss the ingredients together to ensure they are evenly distributed.

  3. Cut the cooked shrimp into bite-sized pieces and add them to the bowl with the mango and avocado mixture.

  4. Squeeze the juice of 4 limes over the mixture, ensuring that all the ingredients are coated in the tangy citrus juice.

  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remember to start with a small amount and adjust according to your preference.

  6. Gently toss the ceviche mixture, ensuring that all the flavors are well combined. Be careful not to mash the avocado or mango.

  7. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the shrimp to absorb the citrusy tang.

  8. When ready to serve, give the ceviche a final toss to redistribute the flavors. Spoon the ceviche into individual serving bowls or a large platter.

  9. Serve the seafood ceviche with tortilla chips on the side. The crispiness of the chips pairs perfectly with the creamy avocado and tangy shrimp.

Tips and Variations

  • For added freshness, you can add diced cucumber or diced tomatoes to the ceviche.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, you can leave the seeds in the jalapeno pepper or add a few dashes of hot sauce to the ceviche.
  • Feel free to adjust the amount of lime juice and salt according to your taste preferences.
  • Ceviche is best enjoyed immediately after preparing, but it can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. However, the texture of the shrimp may change slightly over time.

Enjoy this seafood ceviche recipe with shrimp, mango, and avocado as a refreshing appetizer or light lunch. The combination of flavors and textures will transport you to a tropical paradise with every bite. So grab a bag of tortilla chips and get ready to indulge in this delicious and healthy dish!
