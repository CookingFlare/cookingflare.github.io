Simple Vegan Banana Bread: A Sweet and Moist Recipe


Banana bread is one of the most beloved comfort foods, known for its sweet aroma, soft texture, and delightful flavor. While traditional banana bread recipes typically include eggs and dairy, this vegan version offers the same moist, flavorful loaf without the use of animal products. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or someone looking for a healthier, plant-based treat, this simple vegan banana bread recipe will satisfy your cravings for something sweet and indulgent.

In this blog post, we’ll walk you through an easy-to-follow recipe that guarantees a perfectly moist, flavorful banana bread every time. The best part? It’s made with common ingredients, and it’s both beginner-friendly and perfect for any occasion – from a cozy breakfast to an afternoon snack or even a sweet gift for a loved one.

Why Choose Vegan Banana Bread?

Vegan banana bread isn’t just about replacing eggs and dairy; it’s about enhancing the flavors and textures while keeping it light and plant-based. Here are a few reasons why you should try this version of banana bread:

  • Healthier Ingredients: Many traditional banana bread recipes call for refined sugar and butter. In contrast, this vegan banana bread uses whole, plant-based ingredients like maple syrup and coconut oil, making it a healthier option without compromising taste.
  • Suitable for Dietary Preferences: Whether you’re vegan, lactose intolerant, or simply seeking more plant-based options, this banana bread meets various dietary needs while still delivering the same delicious result.
  • Moist and Flavorful: The key to perfect banana bread is moisture, and the natural sugars and oils in bananas and coconut oil help achieve just that. You’ll never need to worry about your loaf being dry or crumbly.
  • Simple and Convenient: You don’t need any special tools or hard-to-find ingredients. This recipe is straightforward, making it the perfect choice for both beginners and experienced bakers.

Let’s dive into how to make the best vegan banana bread that will have your house smelling like a bakery and your taste buds dancing in delight.

Ingredients for Vegan Banana Bread

To make this simple yet delicious vegan banana bread, you will need the following ingredients:

Wet Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe bananas: Overripe bananas work best as they bring out the natural sweetness and moisture in the bread.
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup: Maple syrup adds sweetness and a touch of flavor, but feel free to substitute with agave or another liquid sweetener.
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil: Coconut oil enhances the texture and provides healthy fats. If you prefer, you can substitute with vegetable oil or any neutral-flavored oil.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract: For a hint of warm, aromatic flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice: This helps activate the baking soda and balances the sweetness of the bread.

Dry Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour: Regular all-purpose flour works best for creating a light, soft texture. If you prefer a healthier option, you can substitute with whole wheat flour.
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda: This is the leavening agent that helps the banana bread rise and become light and fluffy.
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt: A pinch of salt enhances the flavors and balances the sweetness of the bananas and syrup.
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon: Cinnamon adds warmth and depth to the flavor of the banana bread.

Optional Add-ins:

  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans: Adding nuts gives the banana bread a pleasant crunch and a nutty flavor.
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free chocolate chips: For a decadent twist, sprinkle in some dairy-free chocolate chips for a sweet surprise in every bite.

These simple ingredients come together to create a loaf that’s both soft and flavorful, with just the right amount of sweetness. The moist texture, along with the warm flavors of banana and cinnamon, makes each bite comforting and satisfying.

Instructions for Making Vegan Banana Bread

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s get into the step-by-step process of making this easy vegan banana bread.

1. Preheat the Oven and Prepare the Pan

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures the oven is at the perfect temperature when it’s time to bake the bread. Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan with a light coating of coconut oil, or line it with parchment paper for easy removal.

2. Mash the Bananas

Peel the ripe bananas and place them in a large mixing bowl. Use a fork or potato masher to mash the bananas until they’re smooth with just a few small chunks remaining. The bananas should be quite soft, which is key to getting a moist banana bread.

3. Mix Wet Ingredients

Once the bananas are mashed, add the maple syrup, melted coconut oil, vanilla extract, and lemon juice to the bowl. Mix everything together until it’s fully combined and smooth. The lemon juice helps activate the baking soda, giving the bread a nice rise.

4. Combine Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, salt, and ground cinnamon. Mixing the dry ingredients separately ensures that the leavening agents (like the baking soda) are evenly distributed throughout the flour, which helps the bread rise evenly.

5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet banana mixture. Stir gently to combine, being careful not to overmix. Overmixing can result in dense banana bread, so stir until the flour is just incorporated. The batter should be thick and slightly lumpy.

6. Add Optional Add-ins

If you’re adding nuts or chocolate chips, gently fold them into the batter at this point. You can use any variety of nuts or chocolate chips that you like, or leave them out altogether for a more classic banana bread.

7. Pour the Batter into the Pan

Transfer the batter into the prepared loaf pan, spreading it out evenly. Use a spatula to smooth the top of the batter. If you want a little extra sweetness, you can sprinkle some cinnamon sugar on top of the batter before baking.

8. Bake the Banana Bread

Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 55-65 minutes. Check the bread around the 50-minute mark by inserting a toothpick into the center. If it comes out clean or with just a few crumbs, the bread is done. If it’s still wet, let it bake for a few more minutes.

9. Cool and Enjoy

Once the banana bread is done baking, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, transfer the loaf to a wire rack to cool completely. This cooling process is essential for the bread to set properly and reach its optimal texture.

Once cooled, slice the banana bread and enjoy it as a breakfast treat, snack, or dessert. You can store any leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days or freeze slices for up to 3 months.

Tips for the Perfect Vegan Banana Bread

  • Use Overripe Bananas: The riper the bananas, the sweeter and more flavorful the banana bread will be. Look for bananas that are brown and spotty on the outside, as they are packed with natural sugars.
  • Don’t Overmix: When combining the dry and wet ingredients, mix until just combined. Overmixing can make the bread dense and less tender.
  • Customize Your Add-ins: Feel free to get creative with your add-ins! Chopped dates, dried fruit, or even shredded coconut can add unique textures and flavors to the banana bread.
  • Check for Doneness Early: Since oven temperatures can vary, check the banana bread after 50 minutes to ensure it’s not overbaking. Every oven is different, so adjusting the baking time by a few minutes might be necessary.

How to Serve Vegan Banana Bread

This vegan banana bread is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in many different ways. Here are some ideas for serving it:

  • Classic Style: Enjoy a slice on its own as a sweet snack or breakfast.
  • With Nut Butter: Spread almond butter, peanut butter, or cashew butter on a warm slice for added richness and protein.
  • With Fresh Fruit: Top your banana bread with sliced strawberries, blueberries, or other fresh fruits for a refreshing twist.
  • As a Dessert: Pair the banana bread with a scoop of dairy-free ice cream for a simple yet decadent dessert.

Final Thoughts

Making vegan banana bread is not only an easy and enjoyable baking project, but it’s also a perfect way to make use of ripe bananas. This simple recipe yields a moist, flavorful loaf that’s sweetened naturally with bananas and maple syrup. Whether you’re baking for yourself or sharing with family and friends, this vegan banana bread will be a hit at any gathering.

With its comforting texture and delicious aroma, it’s no wonder why banana bread is a favorite among so many. Try this recipe the next time you have a bunch of overripe bananas and want to make something delightful and plant-based. Happy baking!
