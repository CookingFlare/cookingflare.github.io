Pasta Recipes for the Slow Cooker: Set It and Forget It Meals


For many of us, pasta holds a special place at the heart of home cooking. From comforting bowls of spaghetti to creamy, indulgent casseroles, pasta dishes are versatile, budget-friendly, and beloved. But as our lives get busier, finding the time to prepare these culinary classics can become a challenge. Enter the slow cooker—a kitchen hero that allows you to effortlessly create delicious pasta dishes with minimal hands-on time. These recipes are ideal for days when you crave hearty, flavorful meals without the need to stand over the stove.

Slow cooker pasta recipes aren’t just about convenience; they’re also a way to develop robust flavors as the ingredients meld together over hours. Whether you’re seeking a creamy alfredo, a tangy marinara masterpiece, or a comforting baked ziti, your slow cooker is up to the task. Let’s dive into some creative and satisfying pasta recipes that you can “set and forget,” ensuring dinner is ready when you are.

1. Slow Cooker Tuscan Chicken Alfredo

This creamy, rich alfredo with a Tuscan twist is easily one of the tastiest meals you can prepare in a slow cooker. The tenderness of the chicken pairs beautifully with the velvety sauce and hearty pasta.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 12 oz fettuccine or penne pasta
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken breasts, chicken broth, minced garlic, Italian seasoning, sun-dried tomatoes, salt, and pepper into the slow cooker.
  2. Cook on low for 6-7 hours or high for 3-4 hours until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  3. Remove the chicken, shred it with two forks, and return it to the slow cooker.
  4. Add the heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, and dry pasta. Stir well to ensure the pasta is submerged in the liquid.
  5. Cover and cook on high for an additional 25-30 minutes, until the pasta is cooked al dente. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  6. Just before serving, stir in the fresh spinach until wilted. Serve hot, garnished with extra Parmesan or fresh parsley if desired.

2. Slow Cooker Bolognese Sauce with Pasta

A classic Bolognese sauce gets an upgrade with the slow cooker. The long simmer time melds the flavors of tomatoes, meat, and aromatic vegetables into a deep, savory sauce perfect for tossing with your favorite pasta.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 28 oz canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 lb spaghetti or tagliatelle
  • Freshly grated Parmesan for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, sauté the ground beef (or sausage) over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat and transfer the meat to the slow cooker.
  2. Add chopped onion, carrot, celery, garlic, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, beef broth, basil, oregano, and bay leaf to the slow cooker. Stir well to combine.
  3. Cook on low for 8-10 hours or high for 4-5 hours. The longer you cook it, the more the flavors will deepen.
  4. About 15 minutes before serving, cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente.
  5. Discard the bay leaf from the sauce, then toss it with the cooked pasta. Serve topped with freshly grated Parmesan and a sprinkle of fresh herbs.

3. Slow Cooker Vegetarian Lasagna

Lasagna may seem like an oven-only dish, but the slow cooker proves otherwise. With layers of rich marinara sauce, creamy ricotta, and tender pasta sheets, this vegetarian lasagna is wholesome and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 9 lasagna noodles (no-boil is best)
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 cups diced vegetables (zucchini, mushrooms, spinach, or bell peppers)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix ricotta cheese, egg, Parmesan, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  2. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of the slow cooker.
  3. Layer 3 lasagna noodles on top of the sauce, breaking them if necessary to fit.
  4. Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture on top of the noodles, followed by 1/3 of the diced vegetables and 1/3 of the marinara sauce. Sprinkle with mozzarella.
  5. Repeat the layers twice more, ending with a final layer of sauce and mozzarella.
  6. Cover and cook on low for 4-5 hours or until the noodles are tender and the lasagna is heated through.
  7. Let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

4. Creamy Slow Cooker Tomato Basil Pasta

Rich tomato flavor and the fragrant warmth of fresh basil come together in this comforting pasta dish. It’s incredibly easy to throw together with ingredients you likely already have on hand.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb dry pasta (fusilli, rotini, or penne work well)
  • 3 cups diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, torn
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine diced tomatoes, broth, garlic, onion powder, sugar, and red pepper flakes in the slow cooker. Stir well.
  2. Add dry pasta, ensuring all pieces are coated in liquid.
  3. Cover and cook on high for 2-3 hours, checking periodically to give it a gentle stir.
  4. When the pasta is tender, stir in heavy cream and Parmesan cheese. Let it cook for an additional 10 minutes until creamy.
  5. Mix in fresh basil just before serving. Serve topped with extra Parmesan and a drizzle of olive oil.

5. Slow Cooker Cheesy Sausage and Spinach Pasta

Looking for indulgence in a bowl? This sausage and spinach pasta dish is packed with gooey cheese, savory sausage, and a hint of fresh greens.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Italian sausage, crumbled
  • 3 cups baby spinach
  • 12 oz short pasta (rigatoni or ziti)
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the sausage in a skillet until browned, then drain excess grease and transfer to the slow cooker.
  2. Add chicken broth, tomato paste, dry pasta, and seasoning to the slow cooker. Stir until combined.
  3. Cover and cook on high for 2-3 hours, ensuring the pasta is cooked to your liking.
  4. Once pasta is tender, stir in heavy cream, mozzarella, and cheddar cheese. Let cook another 10 minutes until the cheese is melted and creamy.
  5. Fold in fresh spinach just before serving, allowing it to wilt slightly.

Tips for Perfect Slow Cooker Pasta Dishes

  1. Timing is Key: Add your pasta towards the end of the cooking time to avoid overcooking.
  2. Liquid Ratios Matter: Ensure there’s enough liquid in the slow cooker to prevent the pasta from drying out. Broth, cream, or tomato sauce are great options.
  3. Pre-Cook Proteins if Needed: For ground meat or sausages, browning them before adding to the slow cooker enhances the dish’s flavor.

By combining the timeless appeal of pasta with the ease of slow cooking, these meals can simplify your weeknights while delivering incredible flavor. From creamy alfredos to classic Bolognese, your slow cooker transforms dinnertime into a no-fuss experience. These recipes aren’t just about dinner—they’re about creating memorable moments around the table. So grab your ingredients, set your slow cooker, and let it work its magic.
