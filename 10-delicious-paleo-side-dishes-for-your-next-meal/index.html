10 Delicious Paleo Side Dishes for Your Next Meal


When it comes to preparing a meal on the Paleo diet, the focus is on whole, unprocessed foods that are rich in nutrients. While the main protein might steal the show, side dishes are just as important in creating a well-rounded, satisfying meal. If you’re looking for some inspiration for Paleo-friendly sides that complement your meals, look no further. Here are 10 mouth-watering and nutritious Paleo side dishes that will elevate your next dining experience.

1. Cauliflower Rice with Garlic and Herbs

Cauliflower rice is a versatile and nutritious alternative to traditional rice, making it a staple in many Paleo kitchens. It’s low-carb, rich in fiber, and packed with essential vitamins.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A squeeze of lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Break the cauliflower into florets and pulse them in a food processor until they resemble rice grains.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the cauliflower rice to the skillet and stir well. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally until tender.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  5. Remove from heat, garnish with fresh parsley, and serve.

This cauliflower rice pairs perfectly with any meat or seafood and is a fantastic way to get more veggies into your meal.

2. Roasted Sweet Potatoes with Cinnamon and Thyme

Sweet potatoes are naturally sweet, rich in antioxidants, and provide a healthy dose of fiber. This dish brings out their natural flavor with a bit of cinnamon and thyme.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the diced sweet potatoes with olive oil, cinnamon, thyme, salt, and pepper until well-coated.
  3. Spread the sweet potatoes on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the sweet potatoes are tender and caramelized.
  5. Serve warm, garnished with a few extra thyme sprigs for added flavor.

These roasted sweet potatoes are an ideal accompaniment to grilled meats or a hearty salad.

3. Zucchini Noodles with Avocado Pesto

Zucchini noodles, or “zoodles,” are a great Paleo-friendly pasta substitute. Combined with a creamy avocado pesto, this side dish is both refreshing and rich in healthy fats.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis, spiralized
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (or almonds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Use a spiralizer to turn the zucchinis into noodles. Set them aside.
  2. In a blender or food processor, combine the avocado, basil, pine nuts, olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  3. Toss the zucchini noodles with the avocado pesto until well coated.
  4. Serve immediately, garnished with extra basil or pine nuts for crunch.

This side dish is perfect for a light, refreshing addition to any meal, especially when paired with grilled chicken or fish.

4. Baked Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

Brussels sprouts are rich in vitamins, minerals, and fiber, making them an excellent addition to your Paleo meals. Adding crispy bacon to the mix creates a delicious contrast of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 3 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Heat a skillet over medium heat and cook the bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pan.
  3. Toss the halved Brussels sprouts in the bacon fat and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Spread the Brussels sprouts on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  5. Roast for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the Brussels sprouts are golden and crispy.
  6. Once done, toss the roasted Brussels sprouts with the crispy bacon bits and serve.

This savory dish is perfect for any fall or winter meal and pairs beautifully with roast meats or seafood.

5. Carrot and Beet Salad with Orange Vinaigrette

This vibrant salad is both refreshing and nourishing, with a beautiful balance of flavors from the earthy beets, sweet carrots, and citrusy dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large carrots, peeled and grated
  • 2 medium beets, peeled and grated
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the grated carrots and beets.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, orange juice, apple cider vinegar, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Pour the dressing over the grated vegetables and toss to coat.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

This vibrant salad is a fantastic side dish for any meal, offering a burst of color and nutrients.

6. Spaghetti Squash with Olive Oil and Garlic

Spaghetti squash is another excellent Paleo-friendly pasta alternative. With its stringy texture, it mimics traditional pasta, making it perfect for those looking to reduce their carb intake.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Slice the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and remove the seeds.
  3. Drizzle olive oil over the cut sides and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Place the squash cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast for 40-45 minutes, or until the flesh is tender and easily shredded with a fork.
  5. Once cooked, use a fork to scrape out the spaghetti-like strands of the squash.
  6. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the garlic for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  7. Add the spaghetti squash strands to the skillet and toss to coat.
  8. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

This dish works well as a side for grilled meats, adding a light yet satisfying element to your meal.

7. Garlic Herb Roasted Mushrooms

Mushrooms are low in calories and packed with essential nutrients like B vitamins and antioxidants. This simple roasted mushroom dish is bursting with savory flavors from garlic and herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound mushrooms, cleaned and halved
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss the mushrooms with olive oil, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the mushrooms in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the mushrooms are golden and tender.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with extra thyme if desired.

These mushrooms make a perfect side dish for roasted meats or as an addition to a veggie platter.

8. Cucumber and Avocado Salad

This light and refreshing salad is perfect for warm-weather meals. The creamy avocado pairs perfectly with the crisp cucumber, making it a wonderful complement to any main dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large cucumbers, sliced
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced cucumbers and diced avocado.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, then toss gently to combine.
  4. Garnish with fresh dill and serve immediately.

This Paleo-friendly salad is light, crisp, and perfect as a side to any grilled protein or seafood.

9. Sautéed Kale with Lemon and Garlic

Kale is one of the most nutrient-dense vegetables you can eat, and when sautéed with garlic and lemon, it transforms into a flavorful, hearty side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of kale, stems removed and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped kale to the skillet and toss to coat with the garlic and olive oil.
  4. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the kale is tender but still vibrant green.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  6. Serve immediately.

This simple sautéed kale adds a boost of nutrients and flavor to any meal.

10. Roasted Asparagus with Lemon Zest

Asparagus is a versatile vegetable that is high in fiber, vitamins A, C, and K. Roasting it with lemon zest adds a fresh, bright flavor that pairs wonderfully with any main dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss the asparagus with olive oil, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the asparagus on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Roast for 12-15 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender and slightly crispy at the tips.
  5. Serve warm.

This roasted asparagus is simple, flavorful, and perfect for accompanying any protein or salad.

Final Thoughts

With these 10 delicious and nutritious Paleo side dishes, you have a variety of options to complement your meals. Whether you’re looking for something light and refreshing like a cucumber salad or something hearty like roasted sweet potatoes, these dishes will not only enhance your meal but also provide essential nutrients that align with the principles of the Paleo diet. Enjoy!
