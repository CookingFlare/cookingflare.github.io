Smoky Black Bean Soup: A Southwestern Sensation


In the heart of the Southwest, where the desert sun beats down and the skies are a deep shade of blue, lies a culinary gem that’s been simmering for centuries. Smoky black bean soup, a staple of the region’s cuisine, is a flavorful and nutritious delight that warms the soul and satisfies the taste buds. In this recipe, we’ll explore the magical combination of smoky flavors, tender beans, and a hint of spice that makes this soup a true Southwestern sensation.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried black beans, soaked overnight and drained
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions

In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Next, add the chopped red bell pepper and cook until tender, about 5 minutes. Add the soaked and drained black beans, diced tomatoes, cumin, smoked paprika, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Stir well to combine.

Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 30 minutes or until the beans are tender.

The Magic of Smoked Paprika

Smoked paprika is the secret ingredient that gives this soup its distinctive smoky flavor. This sweet and smoky spice is made from bell peppers that have been smoked over oak wood, imparting a rich, savory flavor to the dish. If you can’t find smoked paprika, you can substitute with a combination of regular paprika and a pinch of liquid smoke.

The Finishing Touches

Once the soup has finished simmering, use an immersion blender to puree the mixture to your desired consistency. If you prefer a thicker soup, simmer it for an additional 10-15 minutes or add a slurry of cornstarch and water. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Serve the smoky black bean soup hot, garnished with a sprinkle of chopped cilantro and a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt, if desired. This soup is perfect for a chilly evening or a quick lunch on-the-go.

Variations and Substitutions

Feel free to customize this recipe to your taste by adding your favorite toppings or ingredients. Some ideas include:

  • A sprinkle of shredded cheese, such as cheddar or Monterey Jack
  • A dollop of salsa or hot sauce
  • A handful of chopped fresh cilantro or scallions
  • A side of crusty bread or tortilla chips
  • A can of diced green chilies or roasted poblanos for added heat

Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this smoky black bean soup recipe is sure to become a staple in your repertoire. With its rich flavors and comforting warmth, it’s the perfect dish to share with friends and family on a cold winter’s night. So go ahead, gather ‘round the table, and let the magic of the Southwest transport you to a world of flavor and delight.
