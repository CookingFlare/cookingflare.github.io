Lemon Meringue Cake: A Tangy and Sweet Delight


If you’re looking for a show-stopping dessert that perfectly balances tangy and sweet flavors, then a Lemon Meringue Cake might just be the perfect choice. This delightful cake combines the zesty brightness of lemons with the light and fluffy sweetness of meringue to create a truly unforgettable treat. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply want to indulge in a bit of culinary creativity, this Lemon Meringue Cake is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice

For the Lemon Curd:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest

For the Meringue:

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Cake

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the butter and beat on low speed until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, lemon zest, and lemon juice.
  5. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, beating until well combined.
  6. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans and smooth the tops.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.

Step 2: Make the Lemon Curd

  1. In a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water, whisk together the sugar, butter, eggs, egg yolks, lemon juice, and lemon zest.
  2. Cook the mixture, whisking constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
  3. Remove the bowl from the heat and let the lemon curd cool to room temperature.

Step 3: Prepare the Meringue

  1. In a clean, dry mixing bowl, beat the egg whites on high speed until foamy.
  2. Add the cream of tartar and continue beating until soft peaks form.
  3. Gradually add the sugar, a spoonful at a time, and continue beating until stiff, glossy peaks form.
  4. Beat in the vanilla extract.

Step 4: Assemble the Cake

  1. Place one of the cake layers on a serving plate.
  2. Spread the lemon curd evenly over the top of the cake layer.
  3. Top with the second cake layer.
  4. Frost the top and sides of the cake with the meringue, creating decorative peaks with the back of a spoon.
  5. If desired, lightly brown the meringue with a kitchen torch.

Serving and Storing

This Lemon Meringue Cake is best enjoyed fresh, but any leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Serve slices of the cake with a dollop of whipped cream and a sprinkle of fresh lemon zest for an extra burst of flavor.

Conclusion

The Lemon Meringue Cake is a delightful combination of citrusy zest and sweet meringue that is sure to impress your friends and family. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe is worth the effort for the stunning results it produces. Try it out for your next special occasion, and enjoy the tangy and sweet delight of this show-stopping dessert!
