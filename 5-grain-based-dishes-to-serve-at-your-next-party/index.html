5 Grain-Based Dishes to Serve at Your Next Party


When it comes to hosting a successful party, the food you serve plays a pivotal role in creating an unforgettable experience. While appetizers and finger foods are often the go-to for casual gatherings, adding some grain-based dishes to your menu can elevate the spread and provide variety for your guests. Grains are not only versatile but also offer an array of textures and flavors that can satisfy various dietary preferences. Whether you’re catering to a crowd with vegetarian, vegan, or gluten-free needs, grains can easily be incorporated into your party menu.

In this post, we’ll explore five grain-based dishes that will make your next event stand out. From savory salads to hearty mains, these recipes can be prepared in advance, ensuring you spend more time enjoying your party and less time in the kitchen.

1. Quinoa Salad with Roasted Vegetables and Feta

Quinoa is a fantastic choice for a party dish because it’s a complete protein and works well as a base for various flavor combinations. This colorful quinoa salad with roasted vegetables and feta is a vibrant, satisfying dish that works well as a side or a main for vegetarian guests. It’s also a great option for those looking to eat lighter but still feel full.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 red onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the chopped red bell pepper, zucchini, and onion on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly browned.
  2. Meanwhile, rinse the quinoa under cold water. In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable broth or water to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for about 15 minutes, or until the quinoa is fluffy and the liquid has been absorbed.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted vegetables, crumbled feta cheese, and fresh parsley.
  4. Drizzle with lemon juice and olive oil before serving. This dish can be served warm or at room temperature.

This quinoa salad is packed with flavors from the roasted vegetables and the creamy feta. It’s a refreshing option that will leave your guests satisfied without feeling weighed down.

2. Rice Pilaf with Almonds and Dried Fruit

Rice pilaf is a dish that’s both easy to make and highly adaptable. With its fragrant spices, the addition of almonds and dried fruit gives it a sweet and savory profile that’s perfect for a party setting. This dish works well as a side for meat-based dishes or can be enjoyed on its own for those seeking a lighter, vegetarian option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • 1/4 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/4 cup golden raisins
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Stir in the cumin, cinnamon, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Cook for another minute until the spices are fragrant.
  3. Add the rice to the saucepan, stirring to coat it in the spices. Pour in the broth and bring it to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed.
  5. Fluff the rice with a fork and stir in the almonds, apricots, and raisins.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This rice pilaf is a perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors, with the dried fruit adding a pleasant chewiness and the almonds providing a satisfying crunch. It’s a delightful dish that complements a variety of main courses.

3. Barley Risotto with Mushrooms and Parmesan

Barley is a whole grain that’s often overlooked but deserves more attention. When cooked to perfection, barley becomes tender and creamy, making it an excellent substitute for traditional risotto rice. This barley risotto with mushrooms and Parmesan is rich, savory, and comforting – an ideal addition to your party spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pearl barley
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced (cremini or button mushrooms work well)
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the broth to a simmer and keep it warm over low heat.
  2. In another large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook for another 5-6 minutes, until they release their moisture and become tender.
  4. Stir in the pearl barley and cook for 1-2 minutes, allowing it to lightly toast.
  5. Pour in the white wine (if using), and stir until the wine has mostly absorbed.
  6. Begin adding the warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Wait until most of the liquid is absorbed before adding more broth. Continue until the barley is cooked through and creamy, about 30-40 minutes.
  7. Once the barley is tender, stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This hearty barley risotto offers a satisfying texture and depth of flavor, with the mushrooms adding an earthy richness and the Parmesan providing a sharp, creamy finish. It’s a perfect dish for those looking for something more substantial.

4. Farro and Roasted Beet Salad with Goat Cheese

Farro is a nutty, chewy grain that pairs beautifully with roasted vegetables. This farro and roasted beet salad is a strikingly beautiful dish, with its vibrant colors and bold flavors. The earthy beets, tangy goat cheese, and fresh herbs make it a perfect side dish or a light main for your party.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup farro
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 medium beets, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the cubed beets on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender.
  2. While the beets are roasting, cook the farro. In a medium saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Add the farro, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes, until the farro is tender but still chewy.
  3. Once the farro is cooked, drain any excess water and let it cool slightly.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked farro, roasted beets, crumbled goat cheese, and fresh mint.
  5. Drizzle with lemon juice and olive oil before serving.

The combination of farro’s nutty texture, the earthy sweetness of the roasted beets, and the creamy tang of goat cheese makes this salad both satisfying and refreshing. It’s a dish that will surely impress your guests with both its flavors and its appearance.

5. Couscous with Grilled Vegetables and Pesto

Couscous is one of the quickest grains to prepare, making it an ideal option for last-minute party preparations. This dish combines fluffy couscous with smoky grilled vegetables and a flavorful pesto dressing. It’s a perfect dish for outdoor parties or gatherings where you want to offer a refreshing, Mediterranean-inspired option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1 cup boiling water or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small eggplant, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup pesto (store-bought or homemade)
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a heatproof bowl, combine the couscous and boiling water (or broth). Cover and let it steam for about 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  2. While the couscous is steaming, grill the vegetables. Heat a grill pan or outdoor grill and cook the zucchini, red bell pepper, and eggplant until they are tender and slightly charred, about 4-5 minutes per side.
  3. Once the vegetables are grilled, chop them into bite-sized pieces.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the couscous with the grilled vegetables and toss with pesto.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.

This couscous dish is light yet packed with flavor, with the grilled vegetables adding a smoky depth and the pesto providing a herby finish. It’s perfect for a party where you want something quick, easy, and full of Mediterranean flair.

Conclusion

Grain-based dishes are a fantastic way to diversify your party menu and introduce new flavors and textures. Whether you’re serving a fresh quinoa salad, a comforting barley risotto, or a light couscous dish, these grain-based recipes will leave your guests feeling satisfied and impressed. The beauty of grains is that they can be dressed up in so many ways, making them suitable for any occasion, from casual get-togethers to more formal dinner parties. So, the next time you host an event, be sure to include one (or more!) of these delicious dishes to make your spread truly memorable.
