Creative and Easy Thanksgiving Leftover Sandwich Ideas


Thanksgiving is a time of indulgence and abundance, and while the main event may be over, the leftovers are just beginning. Instead of simply reheating your Thanksgiving feast, why not get creative and turn those leftovers into delicious and satisfying sandwiches? These easy recipes will take your leftovers to the next level, ensuring that not a single bite is wasted.

1. The Turkey Club

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 0 minutes | Total Time: 10 minutes | Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of your favorite bread
  • Leftover roasted turkey, thinly sliced
  • Bacon, cooked and crispy
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Spread a generous amount of mayonnaise on each slice of bread.
  2. Layer the turkey slices on one slice of bread, followed by the bacon, lettuce leaves, and tomato slices.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and top with the remaining slice of bread.
  4. Cut the sandwich in half diagonally and enjoy!

2. The Stuffing Melt

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes | Total Time: 15 minutes | Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • Leftover stuffing
  • Leftover turkey or ham, sliced
  • Swiss cheese slices
  • Cranberry sauce
  • Butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat and melt a tablespoon of butter.
  2. Take a scoop of stuffing and form it into a patty shape. Place it in the skillet and cook until it is golden brown and crispy on both sides.
  3. Remove the stuffing patty from the skillet and set it aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add more butter if needed and place a slice of bread. Layer the Swiss cheese, turkey or ham, cranberry sauce, and the stuffing patty on top.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and top with another slice of bread.
  6. Cook the sandwich on both sides until the cheese is melted and the bread is toasted.
  7. Remove from heat, cut in half, and serve!

3. The Sweet Potato Slider

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 25 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • Leftover sweet potato casserole
  • Leftover turkey or ham, shredded
  • Slider buns
  • Cranberry sauce
  • Marshmallow fluff

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Take a scoop of sweet potato casserole and form it into a patty shape. Place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Bake the sweet potato patties for about 15 minutes, or until they are heated through and slightly crispy on the outside.
  4. While the sweet potato patties are baking, split the slider buns in half.
  5. Spread a layer of cranberry sauce on the bottom half of each slider bun.
  6. Top with the shredded turkey or ham, and a dollop of marshmallow fluff.
  7. Place a sweet potato patty on top and cover with the other half of the slider bun.
  8. Serve warm and enjoy this sweet and savory delight!

4. The Cranberry Brie Panini

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes | Total Time: 15 minutes | Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • Leftover cranberry sauce
  • Brie cheese, sliced
  • Leftover turkey or ham, sliced
  • Sourdough bread slices
  • Butter

Instructions:

  1. Heat a panini press or a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Butter one side of each bread slice.
  3. On the non-buttered side of one bread slice, spread a layer of cranberry sauce.
  4. Layer the brie cheese slices and the turkey or ham on top.
  5. Top with another bread slice, buttered side up.
  6. Place the sandwich in the panini press or skillet and cook until the bread is golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  7. Remove from heat, cut in half, and savor the gooey and tangy goodness!

5. The Gravy Soaker

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes | Total Time: 15 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • Leftover turkey or ham
  • Leftover mashed potatoes
  • Leftover gravy
  • Dinner rolls

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Slice the dinner rolls in half and place them on a baking sheet.
  3. Warm the rolls in the oven for about 5 minutes, or until they are slightly toasted.
  4. Heat the leftover turkey or ham, mashed potatoes, and gravy separately.
  5. To assemble, place a slice of turkey or ham on the bottom half of each dinner roll.
  6. Top with a spoonful of mashed potatoes and drizzle with gravy.
  7. Cover with the other half of the dinner roll and press gently to hold everything together.
  8. Serve warm and let the gravy soak into the bread for a mouthwatering experience!

Conclusion

Leftover sandwiches are a delicious and convenient way to enjoy the flavors of Thanksgiving long after the feast is over. With these creative and easy recipes, you can transform your leftovers into mouthwatering sandwiches that will leave you craving for more. Don’t let those leftovers go to waste – get creative and indulge in these Thanksgiving leftover sandwich ideas! Happy sandwich making!
