Vegetarian Thai Green Curry with Tofu


Thai green curry, with its aromatic blend of fresh herbs, creamy coconut milk, and vibrant green chilies, is one of the most beloved dishes in Thai cuisine. By making a vegetarian version with tofu, you can enjoy all the authentic flavors while keeping the meal plant-based, nutritious, and satisfying. This recipe combines fresh vegetables, soft tofu, and a rich, fragrant curry sauce that’s both comforting and exciting to the palate.

Introduction: Why Vegetarians Will Love This Thai Green Curry

There’s something magical about the way Thai green curry balances spicy, sweet, and savory notes. Many think it’s difficult to replicate such a complex flavor profile at home, but this recipe proves otherwise. It’s perfect for weekday dinners or impressing guests because it’s easy to make and packed with wholesome ingredients.

Using tofu instead of meat adds protein without overpowering the curry’s delicate flavors. Plus, tofu conveniently absorbs the curry sauce, making each bite intensely flavorful. Vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, and snap peas contribute texture and freshness, rounding out the dish beautifully.

Ingredients Breakdown

Let’s take a moment to look at the components that come together to create this delicious meal:

Fresh Herbs and Aromatics

  • Green chilies: Provide the distinctive heat and vibrant color.
  • Galangal or ginger: Adds a peppery, citrusy note.
  • Lemongrass: Imparts a fresh, lemony aroma.
  • Kaffir lime leaves: Essential for authentic flavor.
  • Garlic and shallots: Form the savory base for the curry paste.

Vegetables

  • Bell peppers (red or yellow): Offer sweetness and crunch.
  • Zucchini: Softens in curry while adding mild flavor.
  • Snap peas or green beans: Bring a crisp texture.
  • Baby corn and Thai eggplants (optional): For authentic texture variety.

Protein

  • Firm tofu: Sliced or cubed, it soaks up the curry sauce while providing protein.

Coconut Milk

  • Brings richness and balances the spiciness with creamy sweetness.

Other Seasonings

  • Soy sauce or tamari: Enhances umami for vegetarian diets.
  • Palm sugar or brown sugar: Adds subtle sweetness.
  • Lime juice: Brightens the curry with acidity.
  • Basil (Thai basil if available): Adds freshness and fragrance.

How to Make Thai Green Curry Paste From Scratch

While store-bought curry paste can save time, making your own green curry paste elevates the dish tremendously. Here’s the general method:

Ingredients for Green Curry Paste

  • 6 to 8 green chilies (deseeded for less heat)
  • 3 shallots, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1-inch piece fresh galangal or ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 1 stalk lemongrass (lower part only), finely sliced
  • Zest of 1 lime or a few kaffir lime leaves, finely shredded
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • A handful of fresh cilantro stems
  • 1 teaspoon shrimp paste (optional, omit for vegetarian diet)

Instructions

  1. Toast the coriander and cumin seeds lightly until fragrant, then grind them into powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  2. Combine all ingredients in a mortar and pestle or food processor, grinding or blending until a smooth, aromatic paste forms.
  3. Adjust chilies according to your spice preference.

This fresh paste can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week or frozen in portions.

Step-by-Step: Cooking the Vegetarian Thai Green Curry

Step 1: Preparing the Tofu

  • Press the tofu gently between paper towels or a clean cloth to remove excess moisture.
  • Cut into cubes about 1-inch in size.
  • Optionally, pan-fry the tofu cubes lightly in a little oil until golden on all sides for extra texture.

Step 2: Cooking the Curry Paste

  • Heat 1-2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
  • Add the green curry paste and sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant, stirring continuously to prevent burning.

Step 3: Adding Coconut Milk

  • Pour in about 400 ml (1 can) of coconut milk, stirring to combine thoroughly with the curry paste.
  • Allow it to simmer gently until the coconut milk starts to thicken and the curry paste aroma fully infuses the sauce (about 5 minutes).

Step 4: Adding Vegetables

  • Add the chopped vegetables (bell peppers, zucchini, snap peas, baby corn, and Thai eggplants if using).
  • Cook for 5-7 minutes, until vegetables are tender but maintain some crunch.

Step 5: Adding Tofu and Seasoning

  • Gently fold in the tofu cubes.
  • Season with 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari (to keep it vegetarian).
  • Add 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar to balance the flavors.
  • Tear a few kaffir lime leaves or add lime zest for extra aroma.

Step 6: Final Flavors

  • Simmer for another 2-3 minutes to let the tofu absorb the flavors.
  • Finish with a generous squeeze of fresh lime juice.
  • Toss in a handful of fresh Thai basil leaves and stir gently.

Serving Suggestions

Vegetarian Thai green curry tastes divine served over fragrant jasmine rice or sticky rice, which soak up the rich sauce beautifully. For a low-carb alternative, serve it with cauliflower rice or alongside a crisp green salad.

For a full Thai-inspired meal, pair the curry with fresh spring rolls or a simple cucumber salad with a tangy dressing. A cooling coconut or mango sticky rice dessert complements the heat and spice perfectly.

Tips for the Best Vegetarian Thai Green Curry

  • Customize your vegetables: Seasonal veggies like eggplants, carrots, or mushrooms work well.
  • Adjust spice level: Add more or fewer green chilies depending on your heat tolerance.
  • Make it creamy: Use full-fat coconut milk for the fullest flavor and creaminess.
  • Prep ahead: Curry paste can be made in bulk and refrigerated or frozen, saving time on busy days.
  • Tofu texture: For a chewier texture, try baking the tofu before adding to the curry.

Nutritional Highlights

This vegetarian Thai green curry is not only flavorful but also nutritionally balanced:

  • Protein-packed tofu provides essential amino acids.
  • Coconut milk adds healthy fats and creamy texture.
  • Fresh vegetables contribute vitamins, minerals, and fiber.
  • Herbs and spices offer antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.

Together, these ingredients produce a meal that satisfies hunger and supports overall wellness.

Variations to Explore

  • Add plant-based meat alternatives like soy curls or seitan for a different texture.
  • Make it gluten-free by ensuring soy sauce is tamari and checking other ingredients.
  • Increase the green herbs by adding extra cilantro or Thai basil on top before serving.
  • Try other proteins such as tempeh or chickpeas if you want variety.

Whether you’re a seasoned vegetarian or a curious cook, this recipe invites you to explore the harmony of fresh herbs, spicy heat, and creamy coconut milk.

Try it tonight, and savor the delightful balance of flavors with every bite.
