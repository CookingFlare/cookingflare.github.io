Delicious Scandinavian Dishes: 6 Recipes for a Cozy Meal


Scandinavian cuisine offers a rich blend of flavors that combine simplicity and hearty ingredients, perfect for the colder months when you crave comfort food that fills you up and warms your soul. From the Nordic countries of Sweden, Denmark, Norway, and Finland, these dishes are staples that have been passed down for generations. In this blog post, we will explore six delicious Scandinavian dishes that will make your meals cozy and satisfying, whether you’re cooking for yourself or hosting friends and family.

1. Swedish Meatballs (Köttbullar)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the gravy:

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Make the meatballs: In a small bowl, soak the breadcrumbs in milk and set aside for about 5 minutes. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, soaked breadcrumbs, chopped onion, egg, salt, and pepper. Mix everything together until it forms a smooth mixture.

  2. Form the meatballs: Roll the mixture into small meatballs, about 1 inch in diameter, using your hands. Set the meatballs aside on a plate.

  3. Cook the meatballs: Heat butter and olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the meatballs and cook them in batches, turning them to ensure they cook evenly on all sides. Once golden brown and cooked through, remove the meatballs from the pan and set them aside.

  4. Make the gravy: In the same skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for about 1 minute. Gradually add the beef broth while stirring, making sure there are no lumps. Stir in the soy sauce, salt, and pepper, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for about 5 minutes until it thickens.

  5. Finish the dish: Return the meatballs to the skillet with the gravy. Stir in the heavy cream and let everything simmer for another 5 minutes until the meatballs are fully coated and heated through.

  6. Serve: Serve the Swedish meatballs with mashed potatoes, lingonberry sauce, and pickled cucumbers for a true Scandinavian experience.

2. Norwegian Salmon with Dill and Mustard Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the salmon: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the salmon fillets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Drizzle olive oil over the fillets and season them with salt and pepper.

  2. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together Dijon mustard, honey, white wine vinegar, and heavy cream until smooth. Stir in the fresh dill and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Cook the salmon: Bake the salmon in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and easily flakes with a fork.

  4. Serve: Drizzle the mustard and dill sauce over the cooked salmon fillets and garnish with extra dill if desired. Serve with boiled potatoes or roasted vegetables for a complete Scandinavian meal.

3. Danish Open-Faced Sandwiches (Smørrebrød)

Ingredients:

  • 8 slices rye bread
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 8 ounces pickled herring (or other seafood, if preferred)
  • 1/2 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • Fresh dill sprigs
  • 1/2 cup cucumber slices
  • 1/4 cup beetroot, cooked and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon mustard (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the base: Spread a thin layer of butter on each slice of rye bread. This will keep the bread from getting soggy from the toppings.

  2. Assemble the toppings: Layer the pickled herring on the buttered bread. You can add a few slices of red onion, cucumber, and beetroot for a colorful, tangy contrast.

  3. Finish with garnishes: Top the open-faced sandwiches with slices of hard-boiled egg, fresh dill, and a dollop of mustard if you like an extra kick of flavor.

  4. Serve: Smørrebrød is best served immediately as a lunch or light dinner. You can create different variations by swapping out the pickled herring for other ingredients like roast beef, salmon, or even cheese.

4. Finnish Carrot and Potato Casserole (Porkkanalaatikko)

Ingredients:

  • 4 large carrots, peeled and grated
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and grated
  • 1/2 cup rice (uncooked)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish with butter.

  2. Prepare the ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the grated carrots, grated potatoes, rice, and melted butter. Stir in the heavy cream, vegetable broth, ground nutmeg, sugar, salt, and pepper.

  3. Bake the casserole: Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish and smooth the top with a spatula. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for about 45 minutes.

  4. Finish the dish: After 45 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes until the top is golden brown and crispy.

  5. Serve: Serve this comforting carrot and potato casserole as a side dish with roasted meats or enjoy it on its own for a lighter meal.

5. Swedish Cinnamon Buns (Kanelbullar)

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup sugar (for filling)
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • Pearl sugar for topping

Instructions:

  1. Make the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. In a separate bowl, mix the warm milk, melted butter, and egg. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until you form a dough. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes, until smooth and elastic.

  2. Let the dough rise: Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl and cover it with a clean towel. Let it rise for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.

  3. Prepare the filling: In a small bowl, combine the sugar and cinnamon to make the filling.

  4. Shape the buns: Once the dough has risen, roll it out on a floured surface into a rectangle about 1/4 inch thick. Spread the cinnamon-sugar mixture evenly over the dough. Roll up the dough tightly into a log and cut it into 1-inch slices.

  5. Bake the buns: Place the sliced buns on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush them with an egg wash and sprinkle with pearl sugar. Let them rise for another 20 minutes before baking them at 375°F (190°C) for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown.

  6. Serve: Serve warm with a cup of coffee or tea for a traditional Scandinavian fika (coffee break).

6. Norwegian Risengrynsgrøt (Rice Porridge)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup short-grain rice
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (for serving)
  • 2 tablespoons butter (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: In a large saucepan, combine the rice, whole milk, sugar, salt, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent the milk from burning.

  2. Simmer: Reduce the heat and let the mixture simmer, stirring occasionally, for about 40 minutes until the rice is tender and the porridge has thickened.

  3. Finish the dish: Once the porridge has thickened, remove the cinnamon stick and stir in the butter if you prefer a richer flavor.

  4. Serve: Serve the rice porridge hot, topped with ground cinnamon and a dollop of butter if desired. It’s a perfect breakfast or a comforting snack on a cold day.

Scandinavian cuisine is all about hearty, flavorful meals that are perfect for cozy gatherings. These six recipes—ranging from savory meatballs to sweet cinnamon buns and comforting rice porridge—are excellent choices for warming up on a chilly day. Whether you’re new to Nordic flavors or a seasoned fan, these dishes will bring a taste of the north to your kitchen. Happy cooking!
