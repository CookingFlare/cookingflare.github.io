Paleo-Friendly Soups: 7 Recipes to Keep You Cozy


When it comes to comfort food, soups are an undeniable champion. Not only do they warm your soul on chilly days, but they also serve as nutrient-packed meals that are both satisfying and nourishing. For those following a Paleo lifestyle, soups can be a versatile option—featuring wholesome, clean ingredients that align perfectly with the diet’s principles. Whether you’re looking for a hearty main course or a light appetizer, these seven Paleo-friendly soup recipes will keep you cozy while aligning with your health goals.

1. Creamy Butternut Squash Soup (Without the Cream)

Indulge in the rich, velvety flavors of this creamy butternut squash soup made entirely without dairy. Perfectly Paleo, this soup not only bursts with taste but is also packed with vitamins A and C.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth, free of preservatives
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and sauté until translucent.
  2. Stir in the garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add the butternut squash, chicken broth, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the squash is tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Stir in coconut milk and season with salt and pepper before serving.

This soup’s hint of sweetness combined with the warmth of cinnamon will make it your go-to comfort food during colder months.

2. Savory Paleo Chicken Zoodle Soup

If you’re craving a classic chicken noodle soup, this Paleo-friendly twist swaps out traditional noodles with zucchini spirals, delivering all the nostalgia without compromising your diet.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, spiraled into “zoodles”
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • ½ medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme or ½ tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp ghee or avocado oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat ghee or avocado oil in a large pot and sauté the onion, carrots, and celery until softened.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and add the chicken breasts. Simmer until the chicken is cooked through, approximately 20 minutes.
  4. Remove the chicken, shred it with a fork, and return it to the pot.
  5. Stir in the zucchini zoodles and simmer for 2-3 minutes until the zoodles are tender. Add thyme, salt, and pepper to taste.

This soup combines tender chicken, fresh vegetables, and light zucchini noodles for a healthy, flavorful meal perfect for every season.

3. Hearty Beef and Vegetable Stew

This beef and vegetable stew takes heartiness to a whole new level. It’s a protein-packed Paleo soup ideal for satisfying hunger on the most blustery of days.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 parsnips, sliced
  • 2 cups sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 4 cups beef bone broth
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat avocado oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the beef on all sides, then set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add the carrots, parsnips, and sweet potatoes, stirring in the tomato paste.
  4. Pour in the beef bone broth and return the beef to the pot. Add rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  5. Lower the heat, cover, and let simmer for 1-2 hours until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked.

This satisfying stew is all about rich, robust flavors, making it an absolute crowd-pleaser for family dinners.

4. Creamy Cauliflower and Leek Soup

For a Paleo soup that’s both creamy and nutrient-dense, this cauliflower and leek soup is a top contender. This dish leverages the natural creaminess of cauliflower and coconut milk to create a deceptively indulgent dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower, chopped
  • 2 leeks, white and light green parts only, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable stock
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot and sauté the leeks until softened.
  2. Add the garlic and cauliflower, cooking for 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in the vegetable stock, bring to a boil, and then simmer until the cauliflower is tender.
  4. Puree the soup with an immersion blender or in batches using a standard blender. Stir in the coconut milk, season with salt and pepper, and serve warm.

This light yet creamy soup embodies Paleo-friendly comfort food without sacrificing nutrition.

5. Spicy Thai-Inspired Coconut Soup

If you’re in the mood for soup with a kick, this spicy Thai-inspired coconut soup offers a Paleo twist on traditional Tom Kha Gai.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp or chicken
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp red curry paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat the coconut milk and red curry paste in a large pot, whisking until combined.
  2. Add chicken broth, garlic, and ginger, and bring to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the chicken or shrimp, bell pepper, and mushrooms. Cook until the protein is done.
  4. Add fish sauce and lime juice, adjusting seasoning to taste. Garnish with cilantro before serving.

This Paleo soup packs bold flavors and nutrient-rich ingredients, delivering a spicy, aromatic experience.

6. Rustic Tomato and Basil Soup

This Paleo rendition of tomato soup skips the cream but delivers on all the classic flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (28 oz) whole, peeled tomatoes with no added sugar
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp dried basil or handful of fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add the tomatoes, breaking them apart with a wooden spoon. Pour in the broth and bring to a simmer.
  3. Reduce heat and cook for 20 minutes. Puree until smooth with an immersion blender.
  4. Stir in coconut milk, basil, and seasoning before serving.

Enjoy a bowl of this rustic soup alongside your favorite Paleo sides.

7. Ginger Carrot Soup with Turmeric

This vibrant, immune-boosting soup combines the flavors of ginger, carrots, and turmeric to create a powerful bowl full of benefits.

Ingredients:

  • 5 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated
  • 3 cups vegetable stock
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot and sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until aromatic.
  2. Add the carrots and turmeric, stirring to coat.
  3. Pour in the vegetable stock and simmer until the carrots are tender.
  4. Blend until smooth, stir in coconut milk, and season with salt and pepper before serving.

This bright orange soup is as flavorful as it is packed with health-boosting properties.

With these seven Paleo-friendly soups, staying cozy and satisfied throughout the colder days has never been easier. From creamy classics to adventurous global flavors, these recipes celebrate nutritious, clean eating while keeping your meals exciting and full of variety. Get ready to stock up on fresh produce, nourishing broths, and Paleo essentials to whip up these soups for your next mealtime adventure!
