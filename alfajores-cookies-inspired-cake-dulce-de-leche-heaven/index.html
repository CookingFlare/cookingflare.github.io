Alfajores Cookies Inspired Cake: Dulce de Leche Heaven


Have you ever thought, “What if I could have my alfajores and eat them as cake too?” Well, wonder no more, my sweet-toothed foodie friends! Introducing the Alfajores Cookies Inspired Cake – a dreamy fusion of the beloved South American sandwich cookies and a soft, moist vanilla cake, drenched in a sumptuous dulce de leche sauce. This heavenly delicacy is not only an astonishing sight to behold but also a treat that’ll make your taste buds beg for more. Sink your teeth into this buttery masterpiece, and prepare yourself for a truly unforgettable culinary experience.

Preparing the Cake

To start whipping up this confectionery marvel, we first need to prepare the cake batter. For the flour base, we’ll go with a classic cake structure – incorporate the usual suspects, like all-purpose flour and baking powder. But what sets this cake apart are the delightful extras. Albumin powder, a secret ingredient in countless bakeries, guarantees an exceptionally light and airy texture. Got a sweet tooth that won’t be appeased? Add a generous dash of vanilla extract to the mix. The scent alone will have your senses tingling with anticipation.

Next, we’ll cream together unsalted butter and granulated sugar until they reach a whipped dreaminess. Don’t rush this step; it’s crucial for the perfect cake consistency. Then, gradually add the flour mixture while alternating with a homemade milk mixture (whole milk combined with a drizzle of pure vanilla extract). Fold in humble eggs one at a time, and voilà! Your dreamy, pale-yellow cake batter is ready for the oven.

The Biscuit Crumble

The alfajores-inspired cake wouldn’t be complete without incorporating the iconic cookie component. To achieve this, mix together all-purpose flour, arbitrarily sweetened condensed milk, ovomaltine (the secret ingredient for a nostalgic twist), softened unsalted butter, and a dash of baking powder until a soft and crumbly dough forms.

Take a leap from the classic alfajores by adding a crunchy dimension to the crumble. Create an additional batch of crumble using crushed amaretti cookies (for an Italian twist) and chopped peanuts for added texture. Once your base crumble is ready, gently fold in the amaretti cookie and peanut crumble to the dough.

Integrating the Flavors

To prepare for assembling the masterpiece, start by preheating your oven to 350°F (approximately 180°C) and generously grease two 8-inch round cake pans. Evenly distribute the crumble mixture between the two cake pans to form the base of your cake.

Pour the vanilla cake batter on top of the crumble layer, making sure each pan receives an equal portion. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until you can insert a toothpick into the cake and have it come out clean. Allow the cake to cool completely before removing it from the pan.

Dulce de Leche Drizzle

While your cake is cooling, it’s time to shine with the dulce de leche experience. A luscious homemade sauce cut with a bit of whole milk will create the perfect balance. Pantry indispensable, a candy thermometer, and an unhurried stirring technique are crucial for this step. Once the sugar syrup reaches 240°F (115°C), gently fold in the warm milk, incorporating it until it cools. For the best results, don’t skip the cooling process; it’s what smooths down any texture inconsistencies. After it reaches room temperature, thin out your caramel sauce by mixing it with heavy cream to achieve the desired consistency, it should’ve the consistency of a thick fudge sauce.

Frosting the Alfajores Cake

The finishing touch of the cake is a dulce de leche frosting. A combination of softened butter, powdered sugar, and, of course, generous dollops of dulce de leche will give you the perfect frosting consistency. Spread a layer of frosting to create a heavenly blanket of sweetness over the cooled cake.

Does your heart yearn for an alfajores experience conveniently wrapped in cake form? Bring back the memories of your sweet childhood, and prepare this Alfajores Cookies Inspired Cake. Dulce de leche goodness sandwiched between an irresistibly moist and tender vanilla cake with a biscuit crumble? Now, that’s what we call a heavenly dilemma!
