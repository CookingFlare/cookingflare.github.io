Flaky and Delicious Peach Hand Pies


Summer is here, and with it comes the sweet and juicy flavor of peaches. There’s nothing quite like biting into a warm, flaky pastry filled with the sweetness of fresh peaches. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make peach hand pies that are sure to become a new summer favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 2 cups sliced peaches
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Confectioners’ sugar (for dusting)

Instructions

To make the dough, combine the flour and salt in a large bowl. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Cut out circles of dough using a cookie cutter or the rim of a glass. You should be able to get about 12 circles.

In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Mix until the peaches are evenly coated with the dry ingredients.

Place a tablespoon of the peach mixture onto the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough over the filling, forming a triangle or a square shape, and press the edges together to seal. Use a fork to crimp the edges.

Place the hand pies on the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 1 inch of space between each pie.

Brush the tops of the pies with the beaten egg for a golden glaze.

Bake the pies for 25-30 minutes, or until they are golden brown.

Allow the pies to cool for a few minutes on the baking sheet before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Dust the pies with confectioners’ sugar before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • Use fresh, ripe peaches for the best flavor.
  • If you want a more intense peach flavor, you can add a splash of peach liqueur or peach jam to the filling.
  • You can also add a sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg on top of the pies before baking for extra flavor.
  • To make the pies more portable, you can wrap them individually in plastic wrap or aluminum foil and serve them at room temperature.
  • You can also freeze the pies for up to 2 months and bake them straight from the freezer. Just add a few extra minutes to the baking time.

Conclusion

Peach hand pies are a delicious and flaky treat that’s perfect for summer. With their sweet and juicy filling and buttery crust, they’re sure to become a new favorite. Whether you’re serving them at a summer barbecue or just want a sweet treat to enjoy on your own, these pies are sure to please. So go ahead, give them a try, and enjoy the sweet taste of summer!
