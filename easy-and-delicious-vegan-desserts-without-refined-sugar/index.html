Easy and Delicious Vegan Desserts Without Refined Sugar


If you’re looking for easy, healthy, and delicious vegan desserts without refined sugar, you’re in the right place! The world of plant-based treats is vast and diverse, offering a variety of options that are not only good for your health but also satisfying to your sweet tooth. Whether you’re vegan, health-conscious, or just looking for a way to cut back on refined sugar, there are so many ways to create decadent desserts that are both nourishing and indulgent. In this post, we’ll explore some of the most scrumptious, refined sugar-free vegan dessert recipes that anyone can enjoy—without compromising on taste!

Why Choose Vegan Desserts Without Refined Sugar?

Refined sugar can wreak havoc on your health when consumed in excess, contributing to weight gain, energy crashes, and even chronic diseases like diabetes. Choosing desserts without refined sugar means you can enjoy your favorite sweet treats without any of the negative side effects of highly processed sugars. Vegan desserts, which are naturally dairy- and egg-free, often use plant-based alternatives for sweetening, giving you healthier options.

By opting for healthier sweeteners like maple syrup, coconut sugar, dates, or bananas, you can satisfy your cravings without resorting to processed sugar. Not only do these alternatives provide natural sweetness, but they also offer additional nutrients, like fiber and antioxidants, to help nourish your body while indulging in something delicious.

So, let’s dive into some easy and delectable vegan dessert ideas that are sure to leave you and your guests in awe!

1. Raw Vegan Chocolate Brownies

These raw vegan chocolate brownies are rich, fudgy, and incredibly easy to make. They’re the perfect treat to satisfy your chocolate cravings while still being free of refined sugars. Packed with wholesome ingredients like raw cacao, medjool dates, and walnuts, these brownies provide a healthy dose of antioxidants, fiber, and healthy fats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup medjool dates (pitted)
  • 1 cup walnuts
  • 1/4 cup raw cacao powder
  • 1/4 cup almond flour (or any other flour of your choice)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • A pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, pulse the dates and walnuts until they form a sticky dough.
  2. Add in the raw cacao powder, almond flour, vanilla extract, and a pinch of sea salt. Blend until all the ingredients are well incorporated.
  3. Press the dough mixture into a lined baking dish or tray and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until firm.
  4. Cut into small squares and enjoy a rich, chocolatey treat without refined sugar!

These brownies are the ultimate guilt-free dessert, offering a combination of natural sweetness from the dates and a deep chocolate flavor from the raw cacao. Best of all, no baking is required!

2. Coconut Bliss Balls

Coconut bliss balls are a super simple and versatile vegan dessert option that requires minimal effort and no refined sugar. Made with shredded coconut, nuts, and sweetened with maple syrup, these little energy bites are packed with healthy fats and plant-based protein, making them a great snack or dessert option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup shredded unsweetened coconut
  • 1/2 cup almonds (or any nuts of your choice)
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • A pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, blend the almonds and shredded coconut until finely chopped.
  2. Add in the maple syrup, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt, and blend until the mixture starts to come together.
  3. Roll the mixture into small balls and place them on a lined tray.
  4. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to set.
  5. Enjoy these delightful coconut bliss balls as a quick and nourishing snack or dessert.

These bliss balls are so customizable. Feel free to experiment with different nuts, dried fruits, or even a sprinkle of cinnamon to suit your tastes!

3. Baked Apple Cinnamon Crumble

Warm, comforting, and bursting with natural sweetness, a baked apple cinnamon crumble is a perfect vegan dessert without refined sugar. The apples provide just the right amount of sweetness, while the oats and almond flour create a crunchy, satisfying crumble topping. It’s an ideal dessert for cooler weather, offering a cozy, homey feel.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium apples (peeled, cored, and sliced)
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil (melted)
  • A pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Place the sliced apples in a baking dish and drizzle with maple syrup and cinnamon. Toss the apples to coat evenly.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the rolled oats, almond flour, melted coconut oil, and a pinch of sea salt. Mix until crumbly.
  4. Sprinkle the crumble topping over the apples, ensuring an even layer.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown.
  6. Serve warm, topped with a dollop of dairy-free yogurt or a drizzle of almond butter for extra indulgence.

This baked apple cinnamon crumble is the epitome of comfort food, offering a satisfying, sweet dessert made from wholesome ingredients.

4. Vegan Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Avocados are a fantastic base for creamy desserts, and this vegan chocolate avocado mousse is no exception. The avocados lend a silky-smooth texture to the mousse, while the cocoa and maple syrup provide a rich and indulgent flavor. This dessert is a wonderful way to enjoy chocolate without any refined sugar, all while getting the benefits of healthy fats and nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/4 cup raw cacao powder
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • A pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Scoop the flesh from the avocados and place it in a blender or food processor.
  2. Add the raw cacao powder, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed.
  4. Taste the mousse and adjust sweetness if desired by adding more maple syrup.
  5. Transfer the mousse to serving bowls and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.

This mousse is decadently rich, smooth, and creamy—without the need for any refined sugar! It’s perfect for a simple, elegant dessert after dinner or for a special occasion.

5. Banana Nice Cream

For those hot summer days when you’re craving something sweet and cool, banana nice cream is a refreshing, healthy, and delicious option. With just a few simple ingredients, you can make this dairy-free ice cream that’s naturally sweetened with ripe bananas and other wholesome add-ins.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe bananas (peeled and frozen)
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional toppings: chopped nuts, shredded coconut, cacao nibs

Instructions:

  1. Place the frozen bananas into a high-speed blender or food processor.
  2. Add the almond milk and vanilla extract.
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy, stopping to scrape down the sides as needed.
  4. Spoon the nice cream into bowls and add any desired toppings, such as chopped nuts, shredded coconut, or cacao nibs.
  5. Serve immediately for a soft-serve consistency or freeze for a firmer texture.

Banana nice cream is one of the easiest vegan desserts to make, and it’s endlessly customizable. Add in some frozen berries, nut butters, or even a touch of cinnamon for added flavor.

6. Date-Sweetened Vegan Cheesecake

Yes, you can make a vegan cheesecake without refined sugar! This date-sweetened vegan cheesecake uses a combination of cashews and coconut milk for the creamy filling, and a date and nut crust for a crunchy base. The dates provide all the sweetness, eliminating the need for any processed sugars.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 cup almonds (or walnuts)
  • 1/2 cup medjool dates (pitted)
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil (melted)

For the filling:

  • 2 cups soaked cashews (soak for at least 4 hours or overnight)
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • A pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the almonds, dates, and melted coconut oil. Pulse until the mixture sticks together.
  2. Press the mixture into the base of a springform pan or pie dish, forming an even layer.
  3. In a high-speed blender, combine the soaked cashews, coconut milk, maple syrup, vanilla extract, lemon juice, and a pinch of sea salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  4. Pour the filling onto the crust and smooth out the top.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or until the cheesecake is firm and set.
  6. Slice and serve, garnished with fresh berries or a drizzle of maple syrup.

This date-sweetened vegan cheesecake is rich, creamy, and indulgent, making it the perfect dessert for a special occasion or when you’re craving something decadent yet healthy.

Final Thoughts

There’s no need to compromise on flavor when it comes to vegan desserts without refined sugar. From decadent chocolate brownies to refreshing banana nice cream, there are countless ways to satisfy your sweet tooth while nourishing your body with whole, plant-based ingredients. Whether you’re vegan, reducing your sugar intake, or simply exploring new ways to enjoy desserts, these recipes prove that you can enjoy delicious, indulgent treats without refined sugar.

So, next time you’re in the mood for something sweet, why not try one of these simple and delicious recipes? Your taste buds—and your body—will thank you!
