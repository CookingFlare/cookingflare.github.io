Perfect Pairings: Drinks That Elevate Tacos and Burritos


Tacos and burritos are undeniably two of the most beloved dishes in the culinary world. Their versatility, vibrant flavors, and ability to nail comfort food status make them crowd favorites. But the magic of these dishes doesn’t stop with the fillings, spices, and tortillas. The right drink pairing can take your taco or burrito experience to extraordinary heights, enhancing flavors, balancing heat, and complementing every layer of seasoning and texture.

Whether you’re hosting taco night, enjoying burritos for lunch, or crafting your own Mexican-inspired feast, the right beverage can transform your meal into a gourmet experience. From classic margaritas to unexpected tea pairings, this guide explores the best drinks to pair with tacos and burritos to help you create unforgettable moments at your table.

Margarita Madness: A Timeless Classic

No taco or burrito pairing discussion would be complete without the mention of margaritas. This quintessential Mexican cocktail strikes the perfect balance between sweet, citrusy, and tangy flavors that complement the spices commonly found in Mexican cuisine.

Best Tacos & Burritos to Pair:

Margaritas shine alongside tacos and burritos that feature bold, smoky seasonings. Think carne asada tacos or chipotle chicken burritos. The lime-forward profile of the margarita enhances the charred, earthy notes while cutting through any richness in the dish.

Variations to Consider:

  • Classic Lime Margarita: Perfect with spicy carnitas tacos or shrimp burritos.
  • Blood Orange Margarita: Ideal for al pastor tacos, as the orange notes highlight the pineapple sweetness.
  • Spicy Jalapeño Margarita: A great match for vegetarian tacos or burritos packed with roasted vegetables and avocado.

Find Comfort in Mexican Beers

If margaritas aren’t your style, Mexican beers make a fantastic pairing for tacos and burritos. The crisp, refreshing qualities of these beers complement Mexico’s bold and complicated flavors. They’re a natural choice for casual gatherings and low-effort meals.

Best Mexican Beers to Try:

  • Corona Extra: Its light body and slight sweetness balance spicy and rich fillings, such as barbacoa or chorizo.
  • Modelo Especial: With a fuller flavor, Modelo Especial pairs well with hearty burritos stuffed with chicken tinga or beef.
  • Dos Equis Lager: This classic is crisp and refreshing, perfect for street-style tacos or burritos topped with fresh pico de gallo.

For those who enjoy darker beers, consider Negra Modelo, which complements mole sauce-covered tacos or burritos featuring smoky, deep flavors.

Dive Into Mexican Sodas

Fancy something sans alcohol? Mexican sodas bring a vibrant harmony to taco and burrito dishes. These sodas, often made with real cane sugar and tropical fruit flavors, add an extra layer of sweetness and tang.

Best Mexican Sodas to Pair:

  • Jarritos Lime: Great for tacos loaded with fish or shrimp, lime soda mirrors the citrusy undertones often present in seafood-focused dishes.
  • Jarritos Mango: A soft drink with tropical flair that pairs wonderfully with spicy salsa or burritos with habanero accents.
  • Jarritos Tamarind: Its slightly tart flavor blends beautifully with pork tacos, offering a delightful contrast to smoky chipotle or rich adobo-style seasoning.

Pro tip: Serve these sodas chilled and with plenty of crushed ice for maximum refreshment.

The Rise of Craft Cocktails

Craft cocktails are gaining popularity for their sophisticated ability to pair with tacos and burritos—enhancing intricate flavors in a way that traditional drinks may not. These cocktails offer a creative opportunity to infuse regional Mexican flavors into your beverage selection.

Cocktails Worth Trying:

  • Paloma: Made with tequila and grapefruit soda, the Paloma is light and refreshing. It pairs wonderfully with tacos featuring grilled or citrus-marinated proteins.
  • Mezcal Mule: Replace vodka with mezcal in a Moscow Mule for an earthy, smoky variation that feels right at home next to adobo pork burritos.
  • Michelada: A spiced beer cocktail that includes lime juice and tomato juice—Micheladas serve as a savory pairing for spicy or fried tacos.

These cocktails are ideal for entertaining guests and experimenting with your mixing skills.

Wine Pairings You Didn’t Expect

Wine may not be the first thing that comes to mind for tacos and burritos, but it’s a pairing worth exploring. The acidity, fruit-forwardness, and balance of a good wine can amplify spice levels, cut through richness, or complement smoky flavors in the dish.

Taco & Burrito-Friendly Wines:

  • Albariño: This crisp, aromatic white wine pairs wonderfully with fish tacos or grilled shrimp burritos, bringing out citrus and herbaceous notes.
  • Tempranillo: A Spanish red wine with dusty tannins and notes of red fruit is perfect for beef tacos or burritos with mole.
  • Rosé: Its refreshing profile and slight sweetness make it versatile for vegetarian tacos or burritos stuffed with beans, corn, and roasted vegetables.

Smooth Tequila Pairings

Tequila isn’t just for shots—it can be sipped and savored as a pairing for flavors in tacos and burritos. The agave spirit has earthy, herbal, and sometimes caramel undertones that perfectly align with Mexican ingredients.

Tequila Styles and Pairing Suggestions:

  • Blanco Tequila: With bright, light flavors, blanco tequila works well with ceviche tacos or burritos filled with fresh greens.
  • Reposado Tequila: Slightly aged, reposado tequila complements bold flavors like steak burritos or tacos with mole sauce.
  • Añejo Tequila: Rich and complex, añejo tequila is a beautiful pairing for smoky or spicy tacos, such as chipotle beef or spicy carnitas.

Serve the tequila neat or on the rocks for a sipping experience that brings the entire meal together.

Non-Alcoholic Delights: Agua Fresca

Easy to make and endlessly customizable, agua fresca is a non-alcoholic Mexican drink that pairs beautifully with all things tacos and burritos. Its light, fruity flavor cleanses the palate and refreshes, especially during a spicy feast.

Popular Flavors and Pairings:

  • Hibiscus (Jamaica): Perfect for fish tacos or burritos, as its subtle floral tartness is refreshing.
  • Cucumber Lime: A cooling option for spicy chicken burritos or al pastor tacos.
  • Watermelon Mint: This agua fresca shines when paired with vegetarian tacos or burritos.

Consider making your own and experimenting with seasonal fruits and herbs for maximum flavor.

Unexpected Pairings: Tea and More

Want to truly expand your horizons? Tea can add depth, contrast, and refreshment to a taco and burrito spread. Opt for teas with complementary profiles to the fillings and spices of your dishes.

Tea Pairing Ideas:

  • Iced Green Tea with Lime: Complements fresh flavors in seafood or veggie tacos without overpowering the dish.
  • Hibiscus Iced Tea: Its tartness pairs beautifully with beef burritos or grilled pork tacos.
  • Chai Tea: Works exceptionally well with tacos featuring mole sauce, thanks to similar warm spices.

A Flavorful Finish

Tacos and burritos are culinary works of art, and the beverages you pair with them can transform your meal into a sensory masterpiece. Whether you love traditional margaritas, crave the refreshing zip of Mexican sodas, or prefer experimenting with unexpected pairings like wine or tea, there’s always an option to elevate your dining experience.

By exploring the colorful world of drink pairings, you can turn every taco Tuesday and burrito binge into a celebration of flavor, balance, and bliss—all while discovering new ways to enjoy your favorite Mexican dishes.
