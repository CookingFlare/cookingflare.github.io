Easy No-Knead Bread: Perfect for Busy Days


Are you tired of your usual sandwich bread or tired of buying factory-baked bread from the store? Here’s a simple, no-knead bread recipe for you to whip up in no time. This rustic artisan-style bread has a wonderfully chewy interior and a crispy, golden crust. The best part? No fancy equipment or extensive kneading required!

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar (if using honey, reduce the quantity to 1 tablespoon)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 cup of warm water
  • Optional: extra flour for dusting

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Mix well to incorporate all the ingredients.
  2. Gradually add the warm water to the mixture while stirring with a wooden spoon. The dough should be sticky and messy. Do not worry if it seems too wet; it will firm up as it rests.
  3. Once all the water has been incorporated, cover your bowl with a damp towel, a plastic wrap, or a lid to prevent any unraveling (dough may stick to materials like aluminum foil or gloss paper).
  4. Allow the dough to rest for 2 to 3 hours, or until it has doubled in size. Remember that it will keep on fermenting after that, so it is to your advantage to leave it as short as possible.
  5. Once the dough has risen, you have a few options. You can bake it in a preheated cast iron pot or Dutch oven, which will create the perfect environment for a crispy crust. Alternatively, you can place it directly onto a preheated baking sheet or pizza stone. Another fun option is to cook it on a charcoal or wood-fired grill. Just remember to invert your pot or pan to transform it into a makeshift oven!
  6. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C) and prepare for baking. Remove the dough from its resting place, dust off any excess flour, and shape it into a round or oblong loaf if desired. Placing the dough on a piece of parchment paper can help simplify the transfer to your pot, pan, or grill.
  7. Transfer the dough into your preheated vessel, onto your baking sheet or stone, or directly onto the grill. Be certain to cover with the lid or replace the pot’s lid to create the ideal environment for baking. Bake for 30 minutes with the lid on, then remove the lid and bake for another 10 to 30 minutes (depending on your oven) until the crust is golden brown and the internal temperature registers at 190°F (88°C).
  8. Remove the bread from the oven, and allow it to cool in the pan for about 10 to 15 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack. This step is crucial; as bread cools, its structure solidifies, making it easier to handle without breaking.
  9. Once entirely cooled, store your homemade no-knead bread in an airtight container or plastic bag for up to five days. You can also freeze it for up to three months by wrapping it tightly in plastic and removing as much air as possible before placing it in the freezer.

Tips

  • Use filtered or spring water, as tap water with added chlorine can hinder the fermentation process.
  • You can add various flavors to your no-knead bread, such as herbs, seeds, or grated cheese.
  • There’s no need to fear baking with steam. A pan filled with water placed beneath your dough in the oven can provide essential moisture for the baking process.
  • If you don’t have a Dutch oven or cast iron pot, you can submerge your dough into a cold water bath to create a steamy environment. This process, known as “YG method” after Jeffrey Hamm and Geoffroy’s upside-down steam method, works just as well!

Now, impress your friends and family members with artisan-style, freshly baked bread from the comfort of your own kitchen. Using this easy no-knead bread recipe, you’ll enjoy a hearty and satisfying dinner with minimal effort! Even on the busiest of days, homemade bread is within reach. Happy baking!
