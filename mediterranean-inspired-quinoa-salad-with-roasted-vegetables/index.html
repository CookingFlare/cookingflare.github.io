Mediterranean-inspired Quinoa Salad with Roasted Vegetables


Welcome to a culinary journey through the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean! Today, we’re diving into a delicious and nutritious recipe that brings together the wholesome goodness of quinoa with a medley of roasted vegetables. This Mediterranean-inspired quinoa salad is perfect for a healthy lunch, a side dish, or even a light dinner. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the Salad:

  • 1 cup of quinoa
  • 2 cups of water or vegetable broth
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 eggplant, chopped
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped

For the Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Quinoa

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water using a fine-mesh sieve. This helps to remove the natural coating called saponin, which can make the quinoa taste bitter.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed quinoa and water (or vegetable broth for added flavor). Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and simmer for about 15 minutes, or until all the liquid is absorbed and the quinoa is tender.
  4. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 5 minutes. Fluff the cooked quinoa with a fork and set aside to cool.

Step 2: Roasting the Vegetables

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. On a large baking sheet, spread out the diced red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, chopped zucchini, chopped eggplant, and sliced red onion.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Toss the vegetables to ensure they are evenly coated with oil and seasoning.
  4. Roast in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized, stirring halfway through the cooking time.
  5. Remove from the oven and allow the roasted vegetables to cool slightly.

Step 3: Assembling the Salad

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted vegetables, cherry tomatoes, Kalamata olives, crumbled feta cheese, chopped parsley, and chopped basil.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the extra virgin olive oil, red wine vinegar, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper to create the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the quinoa and vegetable mixture, and gently toss to combine all the ingredients thoroughly.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary, adding more salt, pepper, or lemon juice as needed.

Step 4: Serving Suggestions

This Mediterranean-inspired quinoa salad can be served warm or chilled, making it a versatile dish for any occasion. Here are a few serving suggestions:

  • As a main course: Serve the salad on a bed of fresh greens such as arugula or spinach for a complete meal.
  • As a side dish: Pair it with grilled chicken, fish, or tofu for a balanced and satisfying meal.
  • For meal prep: Divide the salad into individual containers for easy grab-and-go lunches throughout the week.

Additional Tips

  • Customize your salad: Feel free to add or substitute other vegetables based on your preference or what you have on hand. Roasted sweet potatoes, asparagus, or artichoke hearts are great options.
  • Vegan option: Omit the feta cheese or replace it with a dairy-free alternative to make this salad vegan-friendly.
  • Nutty twist: Add a handful of toasted pine nuts or slivered almonds for extra crunch and flavor.
  • Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will continue to meld together, making the salad even more delicious over time.

Nutritional Benefits

This Mediterranean-inspired quinoa salad is not only bursting with flavor but also packed with nutritional benefits:

  • Quinoa is a complete protein, providing all nine essential amino acids, making it a fantastic choice for vegetarians and vegans.
  • Roasted vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, promoting overall health and well-being.
  • Olive oil is a healthy fat that supports heart health and has anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Feta cheese adds calcium and protein, while herbs like parsley and basil contribute to the salad’s freshness and nutrient profile.

By incorporating this recipe into your meal rotation, you’re treating yourself to a wholesome and delightful dish that celebrates the best of Mediterranean cuisine.

Conclusion

There you have it—a vibrant, flavorful, and nutritious Mediterranean-inspired quinoa salad with roasted vegetables. This dish is a wonderful way to enjoy the diverse and healthful ingredients that the Mediterranean diet has to offer. Whether you’re looking for a quick lunch, a side dish for dinner, or a meal prep option, this salad is sure to satisfy your taste buds and nourish your body.

Feel free to share your experience with this recipe or any variations you’ve tried. Happy cooking!
