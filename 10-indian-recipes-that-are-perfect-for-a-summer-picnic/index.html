10 Indian Recipes That Are Perfect for a Summer Picnic


Summer is here, and with it, the perfect opportunity to spend quality time outdoors with family and friends. A summer picnic is the ideal way to make the most of the warm weather, and what better way to make it memorable than with a variety of delectable and refreshing Indian dishes? Indian cuisine is famous for its rich and diverse flavors, making it an excellent choice for outdoor gatherings. In this blog post, we will explore 10 Indian recipes perfect for a summer picnic, making it an unforgettable experience for your guests.

  1. Cucumber and Mint Raita As the mercury soars during the summer months, cooling down with a refreshing side dish is a must. Cucumber and mint raita is a delicious and healthy option that is easy to carry and serve. This Indian delight combines the soothing taste of cucumber, the freshness of mint, and the creaminess of yogurt. Ingredients:

1 large cucumber, diced 1 cup plain yogurt 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder Salt, to taste

Instructions: In a large bowl, mix together yogurt, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Then, add diced cucumber and chopped mint leaves. Refreshing and Delicious Indian Recipes to Enjoy on a Summer Picnic Summer is here, and what better way to enjoy the warm weather with family and friends than with a fun-filled picnic? With a variety of delicious and refreshing Indian dishes, you can create an unforgettable experience for your guests. In this blog post, we'll explore 10 delectable Indian recipes perfect for a summer picnic.

  1. Cucumber and Mint Raita Stay cool during the summer months with this palate-pleasing side dish, Cucumber and Mint Raita. This creamy and soothing dish is rich in nutrients and pairs well with a variety of Indian recipes. Ingredients:

1 large cucumber, diced 1 cup plain yogurt 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder Salt, to taste

Instructions:

Mix yogurt, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt in a bowl. Add diced cucumber and chopped mint leaves. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator before serving.

  1. Papdi Chaat Experience the authentic taste of India with Papdi Chaat, a refreshing and crunchy snack perfect for a summer picnic. Ingredients:

1 cup boiled potato skins 1 cup chickpeas, boiled and salted 1 cup yogurt 2 tablespoons lemon juice 1 tablespoon coriander-mint chutney Chopped cilantro

Instructions:

Mix mashed chickpeas with cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix the mixture with yogurt, lemon juice, and strain to adjust the consistency. Combine the mixture with boiled potato skins, coriander-mint chutney, and garnish with chopped cilantro.

  1. Steamy Rice and Lentil Porridge A nutritious and light kitchadi made from rice and lentils is a perfect dish for hot summer days. This recipe is inspired by Indian traditions of Ayurveda. Ingredients:

1 cup basmati rice 1 cup split red lentils 2 cups water Salt to taste 1 tablespoon cumin seeds

Instructions:

Rinse rice and lentils thoroughly. Boil the rice, lentils, and water. Add cumin seeds towards the end of cooking.

  1. Chana Tikki A delicious finger food for your summer picnic is Chana Tikki, a simple and comforting chickpea patty. Ingredients:

1 cup chickpeas (cooked) 1 small onion (chopped) 1 small piece of ginger (grated) 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder 1/4 inch ginger Breadcrumbs Salt to taste

Instructions:

Mix the cooked chickpeas with onion, grated ginger, red chili powder, cumin powder, salt, and breadcrumbs. Shape the mixture into patties. Cook the patties in a pan with oil.

  1. Mumbai Pav Munch on crispy and fluffy pav, a staple recipe from Mumbai. Perfect to enjoy at your summer picnic. Ingredients:

1 cup hot dogs, sliced 2 tablespoons butter 6 pav bread slices 1 small onion 1-2 green chilies Salt to taste

Instructions:

Butter the pav bread slices. Grill the bread until toasted. Serve with hot dogs, onion, and green chilies.

  1. Rajma Chawal Indulge in the classic North Indian dish, Rajma Chawal, a flavorsome and cooling dish perfect for a summer menu. Ingredients:

1 cup cooked kidney beans 1 cup basmati rice 2 cups water Salt to taste 1 tablespoon cumin seeds

Instructions:

Boil the kidney beans and rice. Add cumin seeds towards the end of cooking. Mix in salt before serving.

  1. Humme Chhapte Ka Aloo Stir-fry some potatoes to add some crunchy texture to your summer picnic menu. Ingredients:

2 medium-sized potatoes, chopped 2 tablespoons butter or oil Salt to taste

Instructions:

Fry the chopped potatoes until crispy. Season with salt before serving.

8.Gajar Ka Halwa Enjoy a warm and comforting dessert at your summer picnic with Gajar Ka Halwa, a traditional Indian recipe. Ingredients:

2 large carrots, grated 2 tablespoons ghee or oil 1 cup milk 1/4 cup chopped almonds and pistachios Cardamom powder, to taste

Instructions:

Grate the carrots. Mix with milk, ghee, and cardamom powder. Cook until the mixture thickens. Garnish with chopped nuts.

  1. Meen Machchhali Quench your thirst with a refreshing Meen Machchalli, a classic fish curry. Ingredients:

1 cup fish (cut into small pieces) 2 tablespoons ginger paste 1 cup vinegar 2 tablespoons curry paste 1 tablespoon lemon juice Salt to taste

Instructions:

Boil the fish pieces. Mix in ginger paste, curry paste, vinegar, and lemon juice. Season with salt before serving.

  1. Coconut Rasogullas Round off your summer picnic menu with a sweet treat - Coconut Rasogullas. Ingredients:

1 can coconut milk 1 can milk 1/4 cup cornstarch 1/4 cup sugar Cardamom powder

Instructions:

Mix coconut milk, milk, and cornstarch. Boil the mixture and stir in sugar and cardamom powder. Shape into small balls and serve.

Tips for a Perfect Summer Picnic

Stay hydrated: Bring plenty of water and cold beverages to keep your guests hydrated throughout the picnic.

Use the right ingredients: Chose fresh and seasonal fruits and vegetables to create the perfect dishes for your summer picnic.

Don't overcooking: Be cautious not to overcook your dishes so they stay flavorful and tender.

Provide plenty of shade: Create a shaded area for your picnic to prevent your guests from getting overheated.

Prepare for a fun and memorable summer picnic experience with our mouthwatering Indian recipes and essential tips. Sit back, relax, and enjoy the love and laughter of your family and friends as you sip on refreshing drinks and relish these delightful dishes.
