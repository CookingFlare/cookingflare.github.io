How to Make Soft and Chewy Pretzel Rolls


If you’ve ever walked into a bakery and been greeted by the smell of freshly baked pretzels, you know how irresistible that scent can be. The soft, chewy texture, the glossy golden brown crust, and the subtle saltiness all come together to create a snack or bread that’s simply too delicious to pass up. But what if you could bring that experience into your own kitchen? Making pretzel rolls at home is surprisingly easy, and the result is a batch of pillowy, chewy rolls that are perfect for everything from burgers to breakfast sandwiches. In this post, we’ll walk you through each step to make soft and chewy pretzel rolls from scratch.

Ingredients for Soft and Chewy Pretzel Rolls

Before you dive into the dough-making process, let’s go over the ingredients you’ll need to make these pretzel rolls. The beauty of this recipe is that most of the ingredients are basic pantry staples that you likely already have on hand. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • All-purpose flour – The main ingredient in any bread recipe, flour provides structure and texture. You’ll need about 4 cups for this recipe.
  • Active dry yeast – This helps the dough rise, giving it a light, airy texture. Be sure your yeast is fresh for the best results.
  • Warm water – Yeast thrives in warm water, which activates the yeast and starts the fermentation process. You’ll need about 1 1/2 cups.
  • Brown sugar – A small amount of sugar feeds the yeast and adds a slight sweetness to balance the salty crust of the pretzel rolls.
  • Salt – Salt is essential to bring out the flavors in the dough, giving the pretzels their signature savory taste.
  • Butter – This adds richness and tenderness to the dough. Use unsalted butter for more control over the flavor.
  • Baking soda – This is what creates that iconic pretzel crust. The baking soda bath gives pretzel rolls their characteristic color, texture, and flavor.
  • Coarse salt – The finishing touch! This is sprinkled on top of the pretzel rolls before baking to give them that salty, crunchy topping.

With your ingredients gathered, let’s get started.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Soft and Chewy Pretzel Rolls

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Start by preparing the yeast. In a large bowl, combine the warm water (about 110°F or 45°C) with the brown sugar. Stir gently to dissolve the sugar, then sprinkle the active dry yeast over the water. Give the mixture a quick stir and let it sit for about 5 minutes. During this time, the yeast will activate, and the mixture will start to bubble and foam. If you don’t see any bubbling, your yeast might be expired or the water might have been too hot or too cold. Try again with fresh yeast and water at the correct temperature.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

Once your yeast mixture is bubbly, it’s time to add the flour and salt. Gradually stir in the flour, one cup at a time, until a dough starts to form. After all the flour is incorporated, sprinkle in the salt and add the melted butter. Stir until the dough begins to come together and pull away from the sides of the bowl. If the dough feels too sticky, add a little more flour, one tablespoon at a time.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Now that the dough is coming together, transfer it to a floured surface and begin kneading. Knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can also use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment for this step, which will make the process faster and easier. The dough should be soft, slightly tacky, and not too sticky. If it’s too sticky, add a bit more flour as needed.

Step 4: Let the Dough Rise

After kneading, shape the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly greased bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Set the dough in a warm, draft-free area to rise for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size. This step is important, as it allows the dough to develop its flavor and texture.

Step 5: Shape the Pretzel Rolls

Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any trapped air bubbles. Transfer the dough to a clean surface and divide it into 12 equal portions. Roll each portion into a smooth ball, then gently stretch the dough into a round shape, creating the signature roll shape. To achieve that perfect pretzel roll shape, fold the edges of the dough under the ball to create a smooth, taut surface.

Step 6: Prepare the Baking Soda Bath

While the dough is resting, it’s time to prepare the baking soda bath. This step is what gives pretzel rolls their signature deep golden color and chewy texture. In a large pot, bring 8 cups of water to a boil. Once it’s boiling, carefully add the baking soda. The water will bubble up quickly, so be sure to stir the mixture gently. Reduce the heat to a simmer.

Step 7: Boil the Pretzel Rolls

Carefully drop the dough balls, one at a time, into the simmering baking soda water. Let each roll cook for about 30 seconds, then use a slotted spoon to remove it and transfer it to a parchment-lined baking sheet. Be sure not to overcrowd the pot, as this can cause the rolls to stick together. Once all the rolls have been boiled, they are ready for the next step.

Step 8: Score and Salt the Rolls

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). While the oven is heating, use a sharp knife or a razor blade to score a small “X” on the top of each roll. This will allow the rolls to expand evenly as they bake. After scoring, sprinkle a generous amount of coarse salt on top of each roll. The salt will stick to the dough and add that signature pretzel flavor.

Step 9: Bake the Pretzel Rolls

Place the baking sheet with the rolls in the preheated oven and bake for about 12-15 minutes, or until the rolls are golden brown and have a shiny, crisp crust. Keep an eye on them towards the end to prevent burning. Once done, remove the rolls from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Step 10: Enjoy!

Your soft and chewy pretzel rolls are now ready to be enjoyed! Whether you use them for sandwiches, burgers, or just as a tasty snack, these rolls are sure to impress. They’re best served fresh, but you can also store them in an airtight container for a few days or freeze them for longer storage.

Tips for Perfect Pretzel Rolls

Making pretzel rolls can seem intimidating at first, but with a few helpful tips, you’ll be able to create perfect rolls every time.

  • Use the right yeast – Active dry yeast is essential for this recipe. Make sure it’s fresh and stored properly to ensure good results.
  • Don’t skip the baking soda bath – This is what gives pretzel rolls their distinct chewy texture and deep golden color. Make sure your water is boiling before adding the baking soda, and simmer the rolls for 30 seconds to ensure the best results.
  • Shape your rolls tightly – When forming the rolls, make sure to shape them tightly and smoothly to help them maintain their round form during baking.
  • Experiment with toppings – While coarse salt is traditional, you can also experiment with toppings like sesame seeds, poppy seeds, or even a sprinkle of garlic powder for a unique twist.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

While making pretzel rolls is relatively simple, there are a few common mistakes that can lead to less-than-perfect results. Here are some pitfalls to avoid:

  • Using too much flour – While it’s important to add enough flour to prevent the dough from sticking, too much flour can lead to dry rolls. Use just enough to keep the dough manageable.
  • Not giving the dough enough time to rise – Be patient and let the dough rise for the full time. If you don’t let it rise enough, your rolls may end up dense instead of soft and chewy.
  • Skipping the baking soda bath – This step is crucial for achieving the characteristic pretzel texture and flavor. Skipping it will result in rolls that don’t have the same chewy texture and golden crust.
  • Overbaking the rolls – Pretzel rolls bake quickly, and the golden brown color can turn to burnt if you’re not careful. Keep an eye on them towards the end of the baking time.

Final Thoughts

Making soft and chewy pretzel rolls at home is a fun and rewarding experience that yields delicious results. Whether you’re looking to create the perfect sandwich roll or enjoy a fresh, homemade snack, these pretzel rolls are a great option. With just a few simple ingredients and some patience, you can enjoy the taste and texture of authentic pretzel rolls in the comfort of your own kitchen. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and treat yourself to a batch of soft, chewy, and perfectly salted pretzel rolls. Happy baking!
