Weeknight Winner: 30-Minute Beef and Broccoli Recipe


When the clock is ticking and hunger pangs are setting in, having a go-to recipe that can be whipped up in just 30 minutes is a game-changer. One such dish that never fails to impress is the classic Beef and Broccoli stir-fry. This recipe isn’t just quick; it’s a delightful combination of tender beef, vibrant broccoli, and a savory sauce that hits all the right notes. Perfect for busy weeknights, this dish promises a satisfying meal without the hassle.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s gather all the ingredients you’ll need. This dish is straightforward, and most of the ingredients are likely already in your pantry.

For the Beef Marinade:

  • 1 pound flank steak (or sirloin, thinly sliced against the grain)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 2 cups broccoli florets (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for cooking)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger (grated or minced)
  • 1 red bell pepper (sliced, optional for extra color)
  • 2 green onions (sliced, for garnish)

For the Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (to balance flavors)
  • 1/2 cup beef broth (or water)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (mixed with 2 tablespoons water)

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Beef

Begin by marinating the beef to enhance its flavor and tenderness. In a medium bowl, combine the thinly sliced flank steak with soy sauce, cornstarch, and vegetable oil. Mix well until the beef is evenly coated. Let it marinate for about 15 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients. This not only infuses the beef with flavor but also helps create a nice sear when cooked.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

In another bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, sugar, beef broth, and cornstarch mixture. This sauce is the heart of the dish, bringing together all the flavors and ensuring that each bite is packed with deliciousness. Set this mixture aside.

Step 3: Cook the Broccoli

Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat and add a tablespoon of vegetable oil. Once the oil is shimmering, toss in the broccoli florets. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until they turn vibrant green and are crisp-tender. If using frozen broccoli, cook until heated through. Remove the broccoli from the skillet and set aside.

Step 4: Cook the Beef

In the same skillet, add a bit more vegetable oil if needed. Add the marinated beef in a single layer, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Allow it to sear for about 2-3 minutes without stirring. This helps develop a beautiful crust. Afterward, stir-fry the beef for an additional 2-3 minutes until it’s browned and cooked through. Add the minced garlic and ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.

Step 5: Combine Everything

Return the cooked broccoli to the skillet with the beef. Pour the prepared sauce over the beef and broccoli, stirring to coat everything evenly. Allow the mixture to simmer for 2-3 minutes, letting the sauce thicken and the flavors meld together. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a splash of water or beef broth to reach your desired consistency.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once everything is well combined and heated through, remove the skillet from the heat. Serve your beef and broccoli hot over a bed of fluffy white rice or steamed jasmine rice. Garnish with sliced green onions for a fresh touch. This dish is sure to become a family favorite, providing a satisfying and complete meal in just half an hour.

Tips for the Perfect Beef and Broccoli

  • Beef Selection: Flank steak is a great choice for this recipe because of its flavor and tenderness. You can also use sirloin or ribeye if you prefer. Just make sure to slice against the grain for maximum tenderness.

  • Veggie Variations: Feel free to customize your stir-fry by adding other vegetables such as carrots, snap peas, or bell peppers for added color and nutrition. The more colorful your dish, the more appetizing it will look!

  • Meal Prep: This dish is perfect for meal prep! You can marinate the beef in advance and keep it in the fridge until you’re ready to cook. The flavors will deepen, making the dish even more delicious.

  • Leftovers: If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat in a skillet over medium heat, adding a splash of water or broth to keep it moist.

Nutritional Information

This 30-minute Beef and Broccoli recipe is not only quick and easy but also packed with nutrients. Here’s a quick look at what you’re getting in a standard serving:

  • Calories: Approximately 350
  • Protein: 30g
  • Fat: 15g
  • Carbohydrates: 25g
  • Fiber: 3g

This dish is a healthy option that provides a good balance of protein, healthy fats, and carbohydrates.

Conclusion

In just 30 minutes, you can create a mouthwatering Beef and Broccoli dish that’s both satisfying and nutritious. This recipe is perfect for busy weeknights when you want to spend more time enjoying dinner with family and less time in the kitchen. With its simple preparation and delicious flavors, it’s sure to become a staple in your weeknight dinner rotation. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and treat yourself to a homemade meal that rivals your favorite takeout. Enjoy your culinary adventure!
