Sous Vide Chocolate Mousse: A Sinfully Delicious Dessert


Who doesn’t love a rich and velvety chocolate mousse? It’s one of those desserts that never fails to impress, and the sous vide cooking method takes it to a whole new level of perfection. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a sinfully delicious chocolate mousse using the sous vide technique. Get ready to indulge your taste buds!

Ingredients

  • 200g dark chocolate, chopped
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Equipment

  • Sous vide cooker
  • Sous vide container
  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Rubber spatula
  • Electric mixer

Instructions

  1. Preheat your water bath to 176°F (80°C) using your sous vide cooker. Make sure to use a large enough container to hold the mousse mixture.

  2. In a heatproof bowl, melt the chopped dark chocolate over a pan of simmering water, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the granulated sugar until pale and creamy. This will take about 2-3 minutes.

  4. Gradually pour the melted chocolate into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to combine. Set aside.

  5. In another bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form. Add in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt, and continue whipping until stiff peaks form.

  6. Gently fold the whipped cream into the chocolate mixture using a rubber spatula. Be careful not to overmix, as you want to maintain the light and airy texture of the mousse.

  7. In a clean bowl, whisk the egg whites until stiff peaks form. This will take about 2-3 minutes using an electric mixer.

  8. Carefully fold the egg whites into the chocolate mixture, again being cautious not to overmix.

  9. Transfer the mousse mixture into individual serving dishes or ramekins. Seal each dish with plastic wrap, ensuring there are no air bubbles.

  10. Immerse the sealed dishes into the preheated water bath, making sure they are fully submerged. Cook for 1 hour.

  11. After 1 hour, remove the dishes from the water bath and let them cool to room temperature. Then refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until set.

  12. Once the mousse has set, remove from the refrigerator and serve chilled. You can garnish with a sprinkle of cocoa powder or a dollop of whipped cream, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more intense chocolate flavor, you can use a higher percentage of cocoa in the dark chocolate.
  • If you prefer a sweeter mousse, you can add an additional tablespoon or two of granulated sugar to the egg yolks.
  • Experiment with different flavor variations by adding a shot of espresso, a splash of liqueur, or a pinch of cinnamon to the chocolate mixture.
  • This recipe can easily be doubled or halved to suit your needs.

Conclusion

Sous vide chocolate mousse is a dessert that will wow your guests and leave them craving for more. The precise temperature control of the sous vide method ensures a smooth and creamy texture that is simply irresistible. With just a few simple steps, you can create a decadent treat that will elevate your dessert game to new heights. So go ahead, give this sinfully delicious sous vide chocolate mousse a try, and indulge in a little taste of heaven.
