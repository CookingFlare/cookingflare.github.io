10 Paleo Salad Recipes You Can Make in Under 10 Minutes


Salads are a quick and easy way to stay healthy, but when you’re following a Paleo lifestyle, it’s crucial to choose ingredients that align with the diet’s principles. Paleo-friendly salads are typically packed with fresh vegetables, lean meats, and healthy fats, making them an excellent choice for a nourishing meal or snack. In this post, we’re sharing 10 simple and delicious Paleo salad recipes that you can prepare in under 10 minutes. These recipes are perfect for busy weekdays, meal prep, or when you need a light, refreshing meal on the go.

1. Avocado & Chicken Salad

A hearty and satisfying salad that’s full of healthy fats and lean protein. This simple dish combines juicy chicken breast with creamy avocado for a rich, flavorful bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 grilled chicken breast (sliced)
  • 1 ripe avocado (diced)
  • 1 cup mixed greens (arugula, spinach, or kale)
  • ½ cucumber (sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Slice the grilled chicken breast into thin strips.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, avocado, and cucumber.
  3. Add the chicken slices on top.
  4. Drizzle olive oil and fresh lemon juice over the salad.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then toss everything together and serve immediately.

2. Tuna & Cucumber Salad

Packed with protein and omega-3 fatty acids, this tuna and cucumber salad is quick to prepare and offers a refreshing crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of wild-caught tuna (drained)
  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • ½ red onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Fresh dill (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the drained tuna, cucumber slices, and red onion.
  2. Add olive oil and apple cider vinegar for a tangy dressing.
  3. Season with fresh dill, salt, and pepper to taste.
  4. Mix everything together and serve chilled.

3. Spinach, Bacon & Egg Salad

This hearty salad is perfect for those who want a protein-packed meal in a hurry. The combination of crispy bacon, boiled eggs, and spinach is satisfying and nutrient-dense.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boiled eggs (chopped)
  • 2 slices of bacon (cooked and crumbled)
  • 2 cups spinach leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Boil the eggs and chop them into small pieces.
  2. Cook the bacon until crispy and crumble it into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Toss the spinach with olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
  4. Add the chopped eggs and crumbled bacon to the salad.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, then toss and serve immediately.

4. Greek Salad with Chicken

This Mediterranean-inspired salad is a great option for those who love vibrant, flavorful dishes. It features fresh veggies and lean chicken, with a tangy dressing to tie everything together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 grilled chicken breast (sliced)
  • 1 cucumber (diced)
  • 1 tomato (diced)
  • ½ red onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Slice the grilled chicken breast and set it aside.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cucumber, tomato, and red onion.
  3. Add the chicken slices to the salad.
  4. Drizzle olive oil and red wine vinegar over the vegetables.
  5. Sprinkle with oregano, salt, and pepper, and toss well to combine.

5. Zucchini Noodle Salad with Avocado

If you’re craving something light and low-carb, this zucchini noodle salad is a great option. The creamy avocado dressing complements the crisp zucchini noodles for a refreshing salad.

Ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis (spiralized into noodles)
  • 1 avocado (peeled and pitted)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)

Directions:

  1. Spiralize the zucchinis into noodles and set them aside.
  2. In a blender or food processor, combine the avocado, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Blend until smooth to create a creamy dressing.
  4. Toss the zucchini noodles with the avocado dressing.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve immediately.

6. Shrimp & Avocado Salad

This shrimp and avocado salad is a light yet satisfying meal, offering protein, healthy fats, and a burst of freshness from the veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 avocado (diced)
  • 1 cup mixed greens
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Toss the cooked shrimp with mixed greens in a large bowl.
  2. Add the diced avocado to the salad.
  3. Drizzle olive oil and lime juice over the salad.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and gently toss everything together.

7. Cobb Salad

A classic salad that’s easy to prepare and packed with protein. The combination of chicken, bacon, avocado, and hard-boiled eggs makes it a filling and nutritious option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 grilled chicken breast (sliced)
  • 2 slices bacon (cooked and crumbled)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (chopped)
  • 1 avocado (diced)
  • 2 cups mixed greens
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Slice the grilled chicken breast into thin strips.
  2. Crumble the bacon and chop the hard-boiled eggs.
  3. Toss the mixed greens with olive oil and apple cider vinegar.
  4. Add the chicken, bacon, eggs, and avocado to the salad.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then toss and serve.

8. Roasted Veggie Salad

This roasted veggie salad is great for those who want a hearty yet Paleo-friendly meal. The roasted vegetables add depth of flavor, and they pair wonderfully with fresh greens.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes (cubed)
  • 1 cup roasted Brussels sprouts (halved)
  • 2 cups mixed greens
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Roast the sweet potatoes and Brussels sprouts until tender and caramelized.
  2. Toss the roasted vegetables with the mixed greens in a large bowl.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve warm or chilled.

9. Kale & Salmon Salad

For a nutrient-packed salad, try this kale and salmon combo. The kale provides fiber and antioxidants, while the salmon adds heart-healthy omega-3s.

Ingredients:

  • 1 grilled salmon fillet (flaked)
  • 2 cups kale (massaged with olive oil)
  • ½ avocado (sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Grill the salmon and flake it into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Massage the kale with olive oil to soften it.
  3. Toss the kale with the flaked salmon and sliced avocado.
  4. Drizzle with lemon juice and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Toss everything together and serve immediately.

10. Caprese Salad with Avocado

This twist on the classic Caprese salad adds avocado for a creamy texture. With fresh tomatoes, basil, and a drizzle of olive oil, it’s a simple and satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tomatoes (sliced)
  • 1 avocado (sliced)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Balsamic vinegar (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Arrange the tomato slices and avocado slices on a plate.
  2. Top with fresh basil leaves.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar if desired.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve immediately.

Tips for Making Quick Paleo Salads

  • Use pre-cooked proteins: To save time, use leftover grilled chicken, salmon, or hard-boiled eggs. You can also use canned tuna or shrimp for even quicker options.
  • Make your own dressing: Avoid store-bought dressings that may contain hidden sugars and preservatives. Simply combine olive oil, lemon or lime juice, and your favorite herbs for a fresh and Paleo-friendly dressing.
  • Pre-chop your veggies: If you’re short on time during the week, prep your veggies in advance. Store them in airtight containers so they’re ready to toss together for a salad.
  • Experiment with herbs and spices: Fresh herbs like basil, cilantro, and parsley can elevate the flavor of any salad. Add a pinch of sea salt, black pepper, or red pepper flakes for extra zest.

Final Thoughts

Paleo salads are a fantastic option when you’re looking for a quick, nutritious meal. With a variety of ingredients like fresh veggies, lean proteins, and healthy fats, these salads not only support your health goals but also satisfy your cravings. By using these 10 simple recipes, you can create flavorful and filling meals in less than 10 minutes, helping you stay on track with your Paleo lifestyle.
