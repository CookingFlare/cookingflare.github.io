Korean Sweet and Savory Soy Sauce Chicken (Dakjjim) Recipe


There’s something undeniably comforting about Korean cuisine—it balances savory, sweet, spicy, and umami flavors in a way that feels both indulgent and wholesome. One dish that perfectly exemplifies this harmony is Dakjjim, also known as Korean soy sauce braised chicken. This delicious recipe is a well-loved classic perfect for dinner parties, family meals, or even special occasions.

Dakjjim boasts tender, succulent chicken simmered to perfection in a rich, glossy sauce that blends soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and a hint of sweetness. The addition of root vegetables such as carrots and potatoes lends texture, while the integration of sesame oil and green onions adds layers of nutty aroma and freshness. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking, expanding your repertoire, or simply looking for an unforgettable chicken dish, this recipe is bound to impress.

Ingredients for Korean Sweet and Savory Soy Sauce Chicken

This Dakjjim recipe calls for easily accessible ingredients, and substitutions can often be made depending on availability or dietary needs. Here’s a detailed breakdown for the dish:

For the Chicken and Braising:

  • 2 lbs Chicken (bone-in, skin-on preferred for maximum flavor and texture: drumsticks, thighs, or even wings)
  • 3 medium-sized Potatoes, peeled and cut into large chunks
  • 2 large Carrots, peeled and sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 medium Onion, quartered
  • 2 cups of Water or Chicken Stock

For the Sauce:

  • 1/3 cup Soy Sauce (use a Korean soy sauce for the most authentic flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons Dark Soy Sauce (optional for deeper color)
  • 1/3 cup Brown Sugar or Honey
  • 5 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon Ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons Sesame Oil
  • 2 tablespoons Rice Wine or Mirin
  • 1/2 teaspoon Black Pepper
  • 1 tablespoon Corn Syrup or Maple Syrup (for added gloss, optional)
  • 1 tablespoon Chile Flakes or Korean Gochugaru (optional for mild heat)
  • 1 teaspoon Toasted Sesame Seeds, for garnish
  • 2 stalks Green Onion, finely chopped for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Dakjjim

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Before you begin, wash and prep all your ingredients. Peel and chop the vegetables into manageable chunks—they’ll need to withstand extended cooking without falling apart. Rinse the chicken under cold water and pat it dry with a paper towel. If using larger cuts, you can score the meat with shallow cuts to ensure the flavors penetrate deeply.

Step 2: Marinate the Chicken

In a large mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, dark soy sauce, brown sugar or honey, garlic, grated ginger, sesame oil, rice wine (or mirin), black pepper, and any optional chile flakes or gochugaru. Stir the marinade well and add the chicken, turning to coat each piece evenly. Let it sit for 20-30 minutes at room temperature to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 3: Sauté the Chicken

Heat a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat with a drizzle of oil. Once the pan is hot, place the marinated chicken in a single layer and sear each side for 2-3 minutes until slightly browned. This step locks in juices and ensures a rich flavor profile. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

Step 4: Braise the Vegetables

In the same skillet, add the onions, carrots, and potatoes. Sauté them briefly, until lightly golden. Reintroduce the chicken to the pan, arranging it on top of the vegetables.

Step 5: Add the Liquid Base

Pour in the water or chicken stock, ensuring the chicken and vegetables are partially submerged in the liquid. Bring the mixture to a boil, then immediately reduce the heat to a low simmer.

Step 6: Simmer and Reduce

Cover the skillet with a lid, leaving a small gap to allow steam to escape. Let the Dakjjim simmer gently for 35-40 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. As the sauce reduces, it’ll thicken and coat the chicken and vegetables in a glossy, flavorful glaze.

Step 7: Check for Doneness

After simmering, the chicken should be tender, and the vegetables fork-soft yet intact. Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning if needed—add more soy sauce for saltiness or an extra drizzle of honey for sweetness.

Step 8: Garnish and Serve

Remove the skillet from heat and sprinkle toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions over the dish. Serve warm, paired with freshly steamed white rice for a complete meal.

Tips and Tricks for the Perfect Dakjjim

  1. Choose the Right Chicken Cuts: While you can use boneless chicken breast, bone-in cuts such as drumsticks or thighs deliver more flavor and stay juicier during the braising process.

  2. Customize the Vegetables: In addition to potatoes and carrots, you can incorporate mushrooms, radish, zucchini, or bell peppers depending on your preferences.

  3. Cook Low and Slow: The key to Dakjjim’s signature tenderness and robust flavor lies in extended simmering. Resist the temptation to rush through this step.

  4. Refrigerate for Stronger Flavor: If time allows, prepare Dakjjim ahead of time and let it rest overnight before reheating—it’ll taste even better as the flavors meld together.

  5. Adjust Heat Levels: If you prefer a spicier dish, increase the amount of gochugaru or add slices of fresh chili. For a milder flavor, omit the chile flakes altogether.

Serving Suggestions

Dakjjim is splendid on its own, but pairing it with complementary side dishes will elevate your dining experience. Here are a few ideas:

  • Steamed White Rice: The sweet and savory sauce pairs perfectly with plain rice, creating a satisfying balance.
  • Korean Side Dishes (Banchan): Serve with kimchi, seasoned spinach, soy-marinated eggs, or stir-fried zucchini for an authentic spread.
  • Crisp Salad: Add a plate of refreshing cucumber or sesame oil-dressed greens to offset the rich flavors of Dakjjim.

Why You’ll Love Dakjjim (Korean Braised Soy Sauce Chicken)

Dakjjim showcases everything there is to adore about Korean food. The dish feels celebratory yet accessible, hearty yet surprisingly healthy. The interplay of soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and sesame oil creates a sauce that’s deep and complex, while the addition of sweeteners provides a captivating glaze.

Not only does Dakjjim deliver unforgettable taste, but it’s also easy to make, forgiving to tweak, and adaptable to whatever ingredients you have on hand. Whether enjoyed as your first foray into Korean food or a nostalgic favorite bringing back fond memories, this recipe is sure to secure a spot on your kitchen table.

Make this sweet and savory delight today, and experience the comfort and joy that Dakjjim brings to every bite!
