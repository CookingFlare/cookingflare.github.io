How to Make Grilled BBQ Chicken


Grilled BBQ chicken is a classic dish that has been a favorite for summer cookouts, family gatherings, and any time you’re craving something flavorful and satisfying. There’s something about the smoky flavor of chicken paired with the rich, tangy sweetness of barbecue sauce that makes this meal irresistible. Whether you’re grilling over a charcoal fire or using a gas grill, mastering the art of grilling BBQ chicken can elevate your cooking skills to a whole new level.

In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make grilled BBQ chicken from start to finish, sharing tips and tricks to get that perfectly cooked, juicy, and flavorful bird. From prepping your chicken to grilling it to perfection, here’s how you can make a mouthwatering BBQ chicken that everyone will love.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before you fire up your grill, let’s gather all the ingredients you’ll need for your BBQ chicken recipe. Here’s a list of the basic ingredients:

  • Chicken Pieces (drumsticks, thighs, breasts, or wings)
  • Olive oil (for coating the chicken)
  • Salt (to season)
  • Pepper (to season)
  • Paprika (adds smoky flavor)
  • Garlic powder (for an extra layer of flavor)
  • Onion powder (to enhance taste)
  • Cayenne pepper (optional, for a bit of heat)
  • BBQ Sauce (your favorite brand or homemade)

Optional Marinade Ingredients:

  • Apple cider vinegar (adds tang)
  • Brown sugar (for sweetness)
  • Mustard (a base for the marinade)
  • Worcestershire sauce (adds depth of flavor)
  • Honey (natural sweetness)

The great thing about BBQ chicken is its versatility. You can adjust the seasonings and BBQ sauce to suit your personal preferences. Now, let’s talk about how to prepare everything and get that grill ready!

Preparing the Chicken

When it comes to making BBQ chicken, starting with the right preparation is key. The first step is to decide which parts of the chicken you’ll be grilling. Drumsticks and thighs are popular choices because they’re juicy and full of flavor, but you can also use breasts or wings. Keep in mind that cooking times may vary depending on the size and cut of the chicken, so it’s important to adjust accordingly.

1. Seasoning the Chicken

The first step is seasoning your chicken. This can be done in two ways: dry seasoning or marinating.

Dry Seasoning:

  • Coat your chicken pieces with a light drizzle of olive oil to ensure the spices stick.
  • Sprinkle salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper (if you want a spicy kick) generously over the chicken. Rub the seasoning into the chicken with your hands to ensure even coverage.

Marinating:

If you want to take your BBQ chicken to the next level, marinating it before grilling is a great idea. This adds even more flavor and helps the chicken stay juicy. To make a simple marinade:

  • In a bowl, combine 1/4 cup of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar, 1 tablespoon of mustard, 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce, and 2 tablespoons of honey or brown sugar.
  • Whisk together to form a smooth marinade.
  • Place the chicken in a resealable plastic bag or shallow dish and pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring it’s well-coated.
  • Marinate the chicken for at least 30 minutes, though overnight is best for deeper flavor.

Once your chicken is seasoned (or marinated), let it sit at room temperature for about 15–20 minutes before grilling. This helps it cook more evenly.

2. Preparing the Grill

Whether you’re using a charcoal grill or a gas grill, proper grill preparation is essential to ensure even cooking and that smoky flavor.

Charcoal Grill:

  1. Light the Charcoal: Arrange your charcoal in a pyramid shape and light it. Allow the coals to burn for about 20–30 minutes until they’re covered with a layer of gray ash. This means they’re at the right temperature for grilling.

  2. Set Up the Grill: Once your coals are ready, divide them into two zones. One zone should have direct heat for searing the chicken, while the other should have indirect heat for cooking the chicken through slowly.

  3. Oil the Grill Grates: Before placing the chicken on the grill, use a paper towel dipped in oil to lightly oil the grill grates. This will prevent the chicken from sticking.

Gas Grill:

  1. Preheat the Grill: Turn on your gas grill and set it to medium-high heat. Allow it to preheat for about 10–15 minutes. You can adjust the heat for different zones on a gas grill by using the knobs.

  2. Clean and Oil the Grates: Use a grill brush to clean the grates, and then oil them lightly to prevent sticking.

Now that the grill is ready, let’s move on to the grilling process.

Grilling the Chicken

Grilling BBQ chicken is all about balancing heat and time to ensure the chicken is cooked thoroughly without burning the exterior. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to do it:

1. Place the Chicken on the Grill

For both charcoal and gas grills, place the chicken on the direct heat side of the grill for the initial sear. Searing the chicken will help lock in the juices and give it that beautiful golden-brown crust.

  • Grill the Chicken: Place the chicken skin-side down (if using skin-on pieces) on the grill. Grill for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until the skin is crispy and browned. Don’t move the chicken around too much – let it sear.

2. Move to Indirect Heat

After searing the chicken, move it to the indirect heat side of the grill. This allows the chicken to cook through without burning the outside.

  • Cover the Grill: Close the grill lid to create an oven-like environment, which helps the chicken cook evenly and retain moisture.
  • Cook the Chicken: Let the chicken cook for about 20–30 minutes, turning occasionally to ensure it cooks evenly. The internal temperature of the chicken should reach 165°F (75°C) when checked with a meat thermometer.

3. Brush with BBQ Sauce

During the last 10 minutes of grilling, it’s time to start applying the BBQ sauce. Brush a generous layer of your favorite BBQ sauce over the chicken, then close the grill lid to let the sauce caramelize. Be careful not to add the sauce too early, as the sugar in the BBQ sauce can burn if exposed to direct heat for too long.

  • Flip the Chicken: After applying the first layer of sauce, flip the chicken to the other side and repeat the process. Continue grilling for an additional 5–10 minutes, allowing the sauce to set.

4. Check for Doneness

The most accurate way to check if your chicken is cooked is by using a meat thermometer. Insert the thermometer into the thickest part of the chicken (without touching the bone) to ensure it has reached the safe internal temperature of 165°F (75°C).

Tips for Perfect Grilled BBQ Chicken

  • Avoid Overcooking: Overcooking chicken can lead to dry meat. Always check the internal temperature to ensure it’s cooked to perfection.
  • Use a Two-Zone Fire: This allows you to sear the chicken and then cook it slowly, resulting in a crisp exterior and juicy interior.
  • Let the Chicken Rest: After grilling, let the chicken rest for 5 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, keeping the chicken moist.
  • Experiment with Sauces: BBQ chicken is versatile, so feel free to experiment with different BBQ sauce flavors. Sweet, tangy, spicy – find what works for you!

Serving Your Grilled BBQ Chicken

Once your grilled BBQ chicken is ready, it’s time to serve it! Pair it with classic sides like coleslaw, potato salad, corn on the cob, or grilled vegetables for a complete meal. A chilled bottle of iced tea or a cold beer pairs wonderfully with BBQ chicken as well.

Creative Serving Ideas:

  • BBQ Chicken Sandwiches: Shred the grilled chicken and serve it on a soft bun with extra BBQ sauce, pickles, and slaw.
  • BBQ Chicken Salad: Chop the chicken into pieces and add it to a fresh salad with greens, tomatoes, cucumbers, and your favorite dressing.
  • BBQ Chicken Tacos: Shred the chicken and serve it in soft tortillas with salsa, guacamole, and sour cream for a fun twist.

Conclusion

Grilled BBQ chicken is a meal that never goes out of style. With the right preparation, seasoning, and grilling technique, you can create a flavorful dish that is sure to impress. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or just enjoying a casual dinner at home, this grilled BBQ chicken recipe will always be a hit. With a few simple steps, you’ll be grilling up delicious, juicy chicken every time. So fire up your grill and enjoy the mouthwatering flavors of BBQ chicken!
