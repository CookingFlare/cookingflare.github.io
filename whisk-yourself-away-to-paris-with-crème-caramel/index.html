Whisk Yourself Away to Paris with Crème Caramel


Are you ready to embark on a culinary journey to Paris without leaving the comfort of your kitchen? Crème Caramel, also known as flan or caramel custard, is a classic French dessert that will transport your taste buds to the enchanting streets of Paris. With its silky smooth texture and luscious caramel sauce, this timeless dessert is sure to impress your friends and family. Let’s dive into the art of creating this indulgent treat and bring a touch of Parisian elegance to your table.

Ingredients:

For the Caramel:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water

For the Custard:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Caramel

  1. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the granulated sugar and water over medium heat.
  2. Stir the mixture gently until the sugar dissolves, then stop stirring and allow it to boil.
  3. Let the mixture boil undisturbed until it turns a deep amber color, swirling the pan occasionally to ensure even caramelization.
  4. Once the caramel reaches the desired color, immediately pour it into the bottom of your ramekins or mold, swirling to coat the bottom evenly. Be careful as the caramel will be extremely hot.

Step 2: Prepare the Custard

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and granulated sugar until well combined.
  3. In a separate saucepan, heat the whole milk until it just begins to simmer.
  4. Gradually pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
  5. Stir in the vanilla extract, then strain the custard mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to ensure a smooth texture.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Carefully pour the custard mixture over the set caramel in the ramekins or mold.
  2. Place the filled ramekins in a deep baking dish and create a water bath by adding hot water to the dish until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  3. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and carefully transfer it to the preheated oven.
  4. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the custard is set around the edges but slightly wobbly in the center.
  5. Once baked, remove the ramekins from the water bath and allow them to cool to room temperature.
  6. Refrigerate the crème caramels for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and the custard to fully set.

Serving:

To serve, run a knife around the edge of each ramekin to loosen the custard. Place a dessert plate on top of the ramekin and quickly invert it to release the crème caramel onto the plate, allowing the luscious caramel sauce to cascade over the silky custard. Garnish with a sprig of mint or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra touch of elegance.

Bon Appétit!

Indulge in the luxurious simplicity of Crème Caramel and savor each spoonful of this divine dessert. Whether you’re hosting a sophisticated dinner party or simply treating yourself to a moment of indulgence, this classic French delicacy is sure to elevate any occasion. So, whisk yourself away to Paris with every delectable bite of homemade crème caramel and let the sweet flavors transport you to the heart of the City of Light.
