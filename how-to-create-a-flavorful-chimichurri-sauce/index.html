How to Create a Flavorful Chimichurri Sauce


When it comes to versatile condiments that add a punch of flavor to just about any dish, chimichurri sauce reigns supreme. This vibrant, herbaceous, and garlic-forward sauce hails from Argentina and Uruguay, where it is traditionally used as a savory accompaniment to grilled meats, particularly steak. However, its uses extend far beyond this traditional pairing. Chimichurri’s bright, zesty, and herbaceous profile can elevate seafood, vegetables, roasted potatoes, and even as a marinade.

In this guide, we’ll unlock the secrets to crafting a truly flavorful chimichurri sauce. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just discovering this Latin American treasure, this step-by-step approach will help you whip up a sauce that bursts with freshness and aroma.

Understanding Chimichurri Sauce

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a moment to understand what sets chimichurri apart. At its core, this uncooked sauce is a masterful blend of chopped fresh herbs, tangy vinegar, pungent garlic, and a touch of spice. Unlike smooth, emulsified condiments such as pesto, chimichurri has a chunky or rustic texture that feels refreshing on the palate.

There are two primary variations—green chimichurri (chimichurri verde) and red chimichurri (chimichurri rojo). While green chimichurri is fresh and herbaceous, the red version includes additional ingredients such as smoked paprika or dried chilies, giving it a slightly different flavor profile. For today’s recipe, we’ll focus on the classic chimichurri verde, but we’ll provide tips for customizing it to suit your preferences.

Essential Ingredients for Chimichurri Sauce

The best part about making chimichurri is its simplicity. It requires just a handful of ingredients that are likely already in your pantry or local grocery store. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Fresh herbs: Traditionally, chimichurri is made with fresh parsley (flat-leaf parsley for a more authentic flavor). You can also use cilantro or a mix of parsley and cilantro for a more unique twist.
  • Garlic: This is a non-negotiable. Garlic is what gives chimichurri its pungent bite.
  • Olive oil: Use high-quality extra-virgin olive oil for richness and depth.
  • White or red wine vinegar: This provides a zesty acidity that balances out the richness from the oil.
  • Red pepper flakes: These add a subtle kick of spice to the sauce.
  • Oregano: Dried oregano is the herb of choice here—it plays beautifully alongside the fresh herbal notes.
  • Salt and pepper: Essential for seasoning and enhancing the other flavors.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Chimichurri Sauce

  1. Gather Fresh Ingredients Start by gathering the freshest parsley you can find. Flat-leaf parsley is ideal because it offers a milder, sweeter flavor compared to curly parsley. If you’re using cilantro as well, ensure it’s fresh and bright green for maximum flavor.

  2. Chop the Herbs Finely Chimichurri is all about texture, so resist the urge to use a food processor. While convenient, it can easily turn the herbs into a paste. Instead, finely chop the parsley (and cilantro, if using) by hand with a sharp knife. You don’t want a powdery consistency—opt for a fine yet textured chop.

  3. Mince the Garlic Once your herbs are chopped, finely mince 3-4 cloves of garlic. For an even more pronounced garlic kick, you can mash the garlic into a paste using a little pinch of coarse salt.

  4. Blend the Dry Ingredients In a bowl, combine the chopped parsley, minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of dried oregano, ½ teaspoon of red pepper flakes (or more if you like heat), and a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  5. Incorporate the Wet Ingredients Add ⅓ cup of red or white wine vinegar to the herb mixture. Then slowly drizzle in ½ cup of high-quality olive oil while gently stirring. This ensures all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  6. Let It Rest and Marinate Chimichurri develops its best flavor after resting for at least 30 minutes. The garlic and herbs infuse the oil and vinegar, creating a bold and cohesive sauce. Cover the bowl and let it sit at room temperature while you prepare your main dish.

  7. Taste and Adjust Before serving, taste the chimichurri and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add a pinch more salt for balance, a drizzle of vinegar for tang, or extra red pepper flakes for heat.

Tips for Customizing Your Chimichurri Sauce

While the classic recipe is a crowd-pleaser, don’t hesitate to tweak it to suit your tastes:

  • Make it spicier: Add minced fresh chili peppers or increase the quantity of red pepper flakes.
  • Citrus twist: Instead of (or alongside) vinegar, add a splash of fresh lemon or lime juice for a citrusy zing.
  • More vibrant green: Toss in fresh green onions or chives along with your parsley for brightness.
  • Smoky variation: Add a teaspoon of smoked paprika or use roasted garlic for a deeper, smoky flavor.

How to Use Chimichurri Sauce

Chimichurri is incredibly versatile. Once you’ve mastered the basic recipe, you’ll find countless ways to use it:

  • As a condiment: Pair it with grilled steak, chicken, pork, lamb, or seafood. It’s particularly popular with churrasco, a type of grilled beef.
  • Marinade: Use it as a marinade for proteins like flank steak or shrimp. Let your meat soak in the herbaceous mixture for a few hours to absorb all the flavors.
  • Vegetables: Drizzle it over roasted, grilled, or steamed vegetables. It’s especially delicious with roasted sweet potatoes, grilled zucchini, or charred corn.
  • Bread spread: Serve it as a dipping sauce for fresh bread or use it as a spread for sandwiches and wraps.
  • Eggs: Brighten up your breakfast by spooning chimichurri over scrambled eggs, fried eggs, or omelets.

Storage Tips for Chimichurri Sauce

Chimichurri keeps well in the refrigerator for up to a week, making it perfect for meal prep. Store it in an airtight container, and ensure the surface is covered with a thin layer of olive oil to prevent oxidation. Before serving, let it come to room temperature and give it a quick stir. You can also freeze chimichurri in small portions (using an ice cube tray) for longer storage.

Final Thoughts

Crafting a flavorful chimichurri sauce is an exercise in simplicity and freshness. With just a few basic ingredients and minimal prep, you can create a versatile condiment that elevates any dish it touches. Whether you’re out on the grill or preparing a weeknight dinner, chimichurri adds a vibrant, herbaceous flair that never fails to impress.

With these tips in hand, you’re now ready to take your culinary creations to the next level. From signature grilling sessions to experimenting with plant-based dishes, chimichurri sauce is your secret weapon. So grab your fresh herbs, fire up your knives, and get ready to fall in love with this bold, delicious, and endlessly adaptable sauce. Your taste buds will thank you!
