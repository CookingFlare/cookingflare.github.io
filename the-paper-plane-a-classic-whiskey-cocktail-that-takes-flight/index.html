The Paper Plane: A Classic Whiskey Cocktail That Takes Flight


If you’re a fan of whiskey and enjoy a well-crafted cocktail, then the Paper Plane may just become your new favorite drink. This classic cocktail is a delightful blend of bourbon, Aperol, Amaro Nonino, and lemon juice, creating a balanced and flavorful libation that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a gathering with friends or simply unwinding after a long day, the Paper Plane is sure to elevate your mixology game. So, let’s dive into the details of this delightful cocktail and learn how to make it at home.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 ounce bourbon
  • 3/4 ounce Aperol
  • 3/4 ounce Amaro Nonino
  • 3/4 ounce fresh lemon juice
  • Ice cubes
  • Lemon twist for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by filling a cocktail shaker with ice cubes.
  2. Measure and pour the bourbon, Aperol, Amaro Nonino, and fresh lemon juice into the shaker.
  3. Secure the lid on the shaker and shake the ingredients vigorously for about 15 seconds. This will ensure that the flavors are well combined and the drink is perfectly chilled.
  4. Strain the mixture into a chilled coupe glass, using a fine-mesh strainer to remove any ice chips.
  5. Garnish your Paper Plane cocktail with a twist of lemon. This not only adds a touch of elegance but also enhances the aroma of the drink.

The Flavor Profile

The Paper Plane cocktail boasts a unique flavor profile that’s both bold and refreshing. The bourbon provides a rich and warm base, while the Aperol adds a subtle bitterness and a hint of orange. The Amaro Nonino brings depth with its complex herbal notes, and the fresh lemon juice provides a bright and zesty finish. When these elements come together, they create a harmonious blend that’s sure to tantalize your taste buds.

Tips for Success

  • Use a good quality bourbon for the best flavor. The bourbon serves as the backbone of the cocktail, so it’s worth investing in a bottle that you enjoy sipping on its own.
  • Fresh lemon juice is key to achieving the bright, citrusy notes that make the Paper Plane so delightful. Avoid using bottled lemon juice for this cocktail.
  • Shake the ingredients with gusto! The vigorous shaking not only chills the drink but also helps to aerate it, creating a velvety texture.

The Origins of the Paper Plane

The Paper Plane cocktail is a relatively modern creation, with its origins traced back to Sam Ross, a renowned bartender who crafted this recipe in 2007. This cocktail quickly gained popularity for its intriguing combination of ingredients and its well-balanced flavor profile. The name “Paper Plane” is said to be a nod to the song “Paper Planes” by M.I.A., which was playing in the background as Sam Ross experimented with the cocktail.

Serving Suggestions

The Paper Plane is best enjoyed as an aperitif, thanks to its refreshing and palate-cleansing qualities. Serve it before a meal to stimulate the appetite and set the stage for a delightful dining experience. This cocktail also pairs beautifully with a variety of cuisines, making it a versatile choice for entertaining.

In Conclusion

The Paper Plane is a modern classic that has earned its place in the world of mixology. Its well-balanced flavor, intriguing history, and simple yet elegant presentation make it a standout cocktail for both seasoned cocktail enthusiasts and those new to the world of mixology. With just a few quality ingredients and a little bit of shaking, you can transport yourself to a world of flavor and sophistication with the Paper Plane cocktail. Cheers to your next cocktail adventure!
