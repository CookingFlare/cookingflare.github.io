Sous Vide Tuna Nicoise Salad Recipe


Looking to recreate a classic dish with a modern twist? This Sous Vide Tuna Nicoise Salad recipe provides a remarkable fusion of flavors, textures, and cooking techniques that will make your next meal unforgettable. With sous vide cooking as the star technique, this salad ensures perfectly tender, flavorful tuna that pairs beautifully with the traditional components of a Nicoise salad. Let’s dive into the step-by-step guide to create this vibrant, protein-packed dish!

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Before we jump into the recipe, let’s take a moment to talk about sous vide. French for “under vacuum,” sous vide is a precise method of cooking where food is vacuum-sealed in a bag and heated in a water bath to an exact temperature. The result? Perfectly cooked food every time. For tuna, sous vide preserves tenderness and ensures even cooking, making it an excellent choice for this salad recipe.

Why Tuna Nicoise Salad is a Timeless Favorite

The Nicoise salad originated in Nice, France, as a vibrant and fresh dish inspired by Mediterranean flavors. Traditionally, it combines ingredients like boiled potatoes, green beans, tomatoes, olives, and hard-boiled eggs, with either canned or fresh seared tuna. By incorporating sous vide tuna, we’re elevating this classic dish to new heights without compromising its rich tradition.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Tuna Nicoise Salad

For this recipe, you’ll need fresh and high-quality ingredients to achieve the best possible flavor and presentation. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Salad:

  • Fresh tuna steaks (about 1-inch thick) – 2 steaks
  • Green beans – 1 cup, trimmed
  • Baby potatoes – 1 lb, halved
  • Cherry tomatoes – 1 cup, halved
  • Kalamata or Nicoise olives – ½ cup
  • Hard-boiled eggs – 4, peeled and halved
  • Mixed greens (optional) – 2 cups
  • Capers – 2 tbsp (optional for garnish)

For the Dressing:

  • Extra virgin olive oil – ⅓ cup
  • Fresh lemon juice – 2 tbsp
  • Dijon mustard – 1 tbsp
  • Minced garlic – 1 clove
  • Anchovy fillets (optional) – 2, finely chopped
  • Fresh thyme or parsley – 1 tsp, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper – to taste

Steps to Create Sous Vide Tuna Nicoise Salad

1. Preparing the Sous Vide Water Bath

Start by filling a sous vide container or large pot with water. Attach your sous vide immersion circulator and set the temperature to 125°F (52°C) for medium-rare tuna or adjust to your desired doneness. Preheat the water bath while you prepare the tuna.

2. Season and Seal the Tuna

Pat the tuna steaks dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and pepper. Place each steak in a vacuum-seal bag or Ziploc-style freezer-safe bag, adding a drizzle of olive oil and a few sprigs of fresh thyme. If you’re using Ziploc bags, use the water displacement method to remove air before sealing.

3. Cook the Tuna Sous Vide

Once the water bath is ready, submerge the sealed tuna into the water. Cook for about 45 minutes to 1 hour. This slow and precise method will leave your tuna perfectly tender and flavorful.

4. Prepare the Salad Components

While the tuna is cooking, work on the remaining ingredients for the salad:

  • Boil the potatoes: In a pot of salted water, boil halved baby potatoes for 10-12 minutes until tender. Drain and set aside.
  • Blanch the green beans: In the same pot (or a new one), boil water and blanch the green beans for 3 minutes. Immediately transfer the beans to an ice bath to maintain their bright green color and crisp texture.
  • Hard-boil the eggs: In a separate pot, boil eggs for 8-10 minutes. Peel and slice them in half once cooled.
  • Slice and prep: Halve cherry tomatoes and drain the olives if necessary.

5. Make the Dressing

In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, minced garlic, and anchovies (if using). Add fresh thyme or parsley, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the acidity or thickness, based on your preference, by altering the ratio of lemon juice to olive oil.

6. Sear the Tuna (Optional)

To add a touch of golden crust for visual appeal and texture, heat a skillet over high heat with a splash of oil. Quickly sear the sous vide tuna steaks for about 30-45 seconds on each side.

7. Assemble the Salad

In a large serving platter or bowl, layer bedrock components for your Nicoise salad:

  • Arrange the mixed greens as a base (optional).
  • Create sections with boiled potatoes, blanched green beans, cherry tomatoes, olives, and hard-boiled eggs.
  • Place the sous vide tuna steaks in the center or slice them into portions and arrange them artfully atop the salad.
  • Drizzle the dressing generously over all components and garnish with capers or extra herbs, if desired.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide and Salad Assembly

  • Temperature is everything: Monitor your sous vide closely to achieve perfectly cooked tuna. For those who prefer their tuna more cooked, opt for 130°F (54°C) – just above medium rare.
  • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients: With such a simple dish, ingredient quality is key to achieving bold flavors and satisfying textures.
  • Keep the salad cold: To contrast the warm tuna and maintain the freshness of the veggies, refrigerate boiled potatoes and green beans before assembly.
  • Customize as you like: Swap out baby potatoes with sweet potatoes or try heirloom cherry tomatoes for added flavor variation. Adjust the dressing thickness by incorporating Greek yogurt for creaminess, if desired.

Why Sous Vide Tuna Nicoise Salad Stands Out

This dish is a testament to the idea that classic recipes can be redefined with modern kitchen techniques. The sous vide method ensures consistency in cooking, eliminating worries about over- or undercooking the delicate tuna. Combined with the bright and bold flavors of the Nicoise salad, this recipe showcases a perfect balance of indulgence and healthfulness.

Serving Suggestions and Pairings

Pair this Sous Vide Tuna Nicoise Salad with a glass of chilled Sauvignon Blanc or a crisp rosé to complement the briny notes of the olives and capers. For a complete meal, serve alongside a crusty artisan baguette or roasted asparagus.

Final Thoughts

Whether for a light weekday lunch or an elegant weekend dinner, this Sous Vide Tuna Nicoise Salad will impress everyone who tastes it. By embracing sous vide cooking, you can elevate simple ingredients into culinary masterpieces that celebrate the artistry of food.

Dive into this refreshing salad today and savor the harmonious balance of Mediterranean flavors with the perfect sous vide tuna centerpiece!
