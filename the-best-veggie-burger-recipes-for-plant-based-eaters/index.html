The Best Veggie Burger Recipes for Plant-Based Eaters


Looking for delicious and satisfying veggie burger recipes that will satisfy your cravings? Look no further! In this blog post, we will be sharing some of the best veggie burger recipes for plant-based eaters. These recipes are not only packed with flavor but also provide a great source of plant-based protein. Whether you’re a vegan, vegetarian, or simply looking to reduce your meat consumption, these burgers are sure to impress!

Classic Black Bean Burger

Let’s start with a classic black bean burger recipe that never fails to please. This recipe combines the hearty texture of black beans with a variety of spices and seasonings to create a tasty and satisfying burger.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup of cooked quinoa
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped bell pepper
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole wheat burger buns
  • Toppings of your choice (lettuce, tomato, avocado, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mash the black beans with a fork until they have a slightly chunky texture.
  2. Add the cooked quinoa, red onion, bell pepper, garlic, soy sauce, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper to the bowl. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.
  3. Shape the mixture into patties, about 1/2 inch thick.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and lightly coat it with oil or cooking spray.
  5. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes on each side, until they are nicely browned and heated through.
  6. Toast the burger buns, if desired, and assemble the burgers with your favorite toppings.

Mushroom and Lentil Burger

If you’re a fan of mushrooms, you’ll love this mushroom and lentil burger. The combination of earthy mushrooms and hearty lentils creates a burger that is both flavorful and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of cooked lentils
  • 1 cup of finely chopped mushrooms (button or cremini)
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon of soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole wheat burger buns
  • Toppings of your choice (spinach, caramelized onions, vegan cheese, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cooked lentils, mushrooms, onion, garlic, bread crumbs, parsley, soy sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Using a fork or your hands, mash the ingredients together until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into patties, about 1/2 inch thick.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and lightly coat it with oil or cooking spray.
  5. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown and heated through.
  6. Toast the burger buns, if desired, and assemble the burgers with your favorite toppings.

Sweet Potato and Chickpea Burger

For a burger with a slightly sweet and nutty flavor, try this sweet potato and chickpea burger. The combination of sweet potato and chickpeas creates a patty that is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of mashed sweet potato
  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of bread crumbs
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole wheat burger buns
  • Toppings of your choice (sliced red onion, tahini sauce, sprouts, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mashed sweet potato, chickpeas, red onion, cilantro, garlic, bread crumbs, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Mash the ingredients together until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into patties, about 1/2 inch thick.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and lightly coat it with oil or cooking spray.
  5. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes on each side, until they are nicely browned and heated through.
  6. Toast the burger buns, if desired, and assemble the burgers with your favorite toppings.

Conclusion

These veggie burger recipes are not only delicious but also versatile. Feel free to experiment with different spices, seasonings, and toppings to make them your own. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue or simply craving a satisfying meal, these burgers are sure to please both plant-based eaters and meat lovers alike. So, go ahead and give them a try – your taste buds will thank you!
