Tasty Thanksgiving Bread Bowl Recipes

The Thanksgiving holiday is a time for gathering with loved ones and indulging in delicious food. One way to elevate your Thanksgiving feast is by incorporating tasty bread bowl recipes that are sure to impress your guests. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or having an intimate dinner with family, these creative bread bowl recipes are perfect for adding a unique twist to your Thanksgiving menu. Savory Pumpkin Soup Bread Bowl Ingredients: 4 small pumpkins 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 onion, chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg 4 cups vegetable broth 1 cup heavy cream Salt and pepper to taste 4 small bread bowls Instructions: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut off the tops of the pumpkins and scoop out the seeds. Place the pumpkins on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened. Stir in the cinnamon and nutmeg, then add the vegetable broth. Bring to a simmer. Once the pumpkins are roasted, scoop out the flesh and add it to the pot. Simmer for 10 minutes. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper. Carve out the center of each bread bowl to create a hollow space. Ladle the pumpkin soup into the bread bowls and serve immediately. Cranberry Turkey Salad Bread Bowl Ingredients: 2 cups cooked turkey, chopped 1/2 cup mayonnaise 1/4 cup dried cranberries 1/4 cup chopped pecans 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley Salt and pepper to taste 4 small bread bowls Lettuce leaves (optional) Instructions: In a large bowl, combine the chopped turkey, mayonnaise, dried cranberries, chopped pecans, and fresh parsley. Mix well. Season the turkey salad with salt and pepper to taste. Carve out the center of each bread bowl to create a cavity for the salad. If desired, line the bread bowls with lettuce leaves. Spoon the cranberry turkey salad into the bread bowls, filling them to the top. Serve the bread bowls immediately, or cover and refrigerate until ready to serve. Apple Pecan Bread Pudding Bread Bowl Ingredients: 4 cups cubed bread 1 cup milk 2 eggs 1/2 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/2 cup chopped pecans 1/2 cup diced apples 4 small bread bowls Instructions: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, sugar, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Add the cubed bread, chopped pecans, and diced apples to the mixture. Toss until the bread is evenly coated. Allow the mixture to sit for 10-15 minutes, letting the bread absorb the liquid. Carve out the center of each bread bowl to create a well for the bread pudding. Spoon the bread pudding mixture into the bread bowls, distributing evenly. Place the filled bread bowls on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the bread pudding is set and golden. Serve the apple pecan bread pudding bread bowls warm, optionally topped with a dollop of whipped cream. These delightful bread bowl recipes are a fantastic way to bring a touch of creativity to your Thanksgiving celebration. Whether you opt for a savory pumpkin soup, a delightful cranberry turkey salad, or a warm apple pecan bread pudding, these recipes are sure to leave a lasting impression on your guests. Embrace the warmth and comfort of the holiday season with these delectable bread bowl creations that are as visually appealing as they are delicious. ...

Thanksgiving Recipes That Will Make You Look Like a Pro

Herb-Roasted Turkey Ingredients: 1 whole turkey (12-14 pounds) 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). In a small bowl, mix together the softened butter, rosemary, thyme, sage, salt, and pepper. Pat the turkey dry with paper towels and carefully loosen the skin from the breast without tearing it. Spread the herb butter mixture evenly under the skin and over the breast meat. Place the turkey on a roasting rack in a roasting pan, breast side up. Roast the turkey for about 3 to 3.5 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) in the thickest part of the thigh. Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving. Serve and enjoy! Cranberry Pecan Stuffing Ingredients: 1 loaf of day-old bread, cubed 1 cup chopped pecans 1 cup dried cranberries 1 onion, chopped 2 stalks of celery, chopped 2 cloves of garlic, minced 2 cups chicken broth 1/4 cup unsalted butter 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion, celery, and garlic. Cook until softened. Add the sage, thyme, pecans, and cranberries. Cook for another 2-3 minutes. In a large bowl, combine the bread cubes and the onion mixture. Season with salt and pepper. Slowly pour in the chicken broth, stirring until the bread is evenly moistened. Transfer the stuffing to a baking dish and cover with foil. Bake for 30 minutes, then uncover and bake for an additional 15 minutes until golden brown. Maple Glazed Sweet Potatoes Ingredients: 3 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed 1/4 cup maple syrup 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted 1 teaspoon cinnamon Salt to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a bowl, mix together the maple syrup, melted butter, cinnamon, and salt. Add the sweet potatoes to the bowl and toss until evenly coated. Spread the sweet potatoes in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast for 30-35 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and caramelized.
