Cooking with Nutritional Yeast: A Vegan Cheese Alternative


Are you a vegan looking for a delicious and nutritious cheese alternative? Look no further than nutritional yeast! This versatile ingredient is not only packed with essential nutrients, but it also adds a savory, cheesy flavor to your favorite dishes. In this blog post, we will explore the wonders of cooking with nutritional yeast and share some mouth-watering recipes that will satisfy even the most discerning cheese lover.

What is Nutritional Yeast?

Nutritional yeast is a deactivated yeast, often sold in the form of flakes or powder. It is made from Saccharomyces cerevisiae, the same species used in baking and brewing. However, unlike active yeast, nutritional yeast does not cause fermentation. Instead, it undergoes a heating and drying process that deactivates the yeast.

Health Benefits of Nutritional Yeast

Not only does nutritional yeast add a burst of flavor to your dishes, but it also boasts an impressive nutritional profile. It is a complete protein, meaning it contains all nine essential amino acids that our bodies need. Moreover, nutritional yeast is rich in B vitamins, including thiamine, riboflavin, niacin, and vitamin B12, making it an excellent choice for vegans who may have difficulty obtaining these nutrients from plant-based sources.

Cooking with Nutritional Yeast

Now that we know the benefits of nutritional yeast, let’s dive into some delectable recipes that showcase its versatility:

1. Cheesy Vegan Mac and Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked macaroni
  • 1 cup raw cashews
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the cashews, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, garlic powder, onion powder, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the sauce over medium heat until it thickens slightly.
  3. Pour the sauce over the cooked macaroni and stir until well coated.
  4. Serve hot and enjoy the cheesy goodness!

2. Vegan Parmesan Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw cashews
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the cashews, nutritional yeast, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt. Pulse until the mixture resembles grated cheese.
  2. Store the vegan parmesan cheese in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.
  3. Sprinkle it on top of pasta, salads, or roasted vegetables for an extra savory kick.

3. Vegan Nacho Cheese Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peeled and diced potatoes
  • 1/4 cup peeled and diced carrots
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring water to a boil and add the diced potatoes and carrots. Cook until tender, then drain.
  2. In a blender, combine the cooked vegetables, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Transfer the sauce back to the saucepan and heat over medium heat until warmed through.
  4. Drizzle the nacho cheese sauce over tortilla chips or use it as a dip for your favorite snacks.

Conclusion

Nutritional yeast is a vegan’s secret weapon when it comes to adding a cheesy flavor to dishes. Not only does it satisfy your taste buds, but it also provides essential nutrients that contribute to a healthy lifestyle. Whether you’re making mac and cheese, sprinkling vegan parmesan on your pasta, or enjoying a bowl of nacho cheese sauce, nutritional yeast is sure to elevate your vegan cheese game. So go ahead, get creative in the kitchen, and enjoy the wonders of cooking with nutritional yeast!
