Preserving the Harvest: Canning and Pickling Farm to Table Recipes


As the summer comes to a close and the bountiful harvest season arrives, it’s time to make the most of the delicious produce that nature has provided. Preserving the harvest through canning and pickling is a fantastic way to extend the lifespan of fresh fruits and vegetables, ensuring that their vibrant flavors can be enjoyed throughout the year. In this blog post, we will explore some farm-to-table recipes that will take your preserving game to the next level.

Canning Recipes

Classic Tomato Sauce

Preparation Time: 20 minutes Canning Time: 45 minutes Yield: 4 pints

Ingredients:

  • 10 pounds of ripe tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Start by blanching the tomatoes in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then transfer them to an ice bath to stop the cooking process.
  2. Peel and core the tomatoes, then coarsely chop them.
  3. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat and sauté the onions and garlic until translucent.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, salt, sugar, oregano, basil, and black pepper to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Meanwhile, sterilize your canning jars and lids by boiling them in a large pot of water for 10 minutes.
  6. Ladle the hot tomato sauce into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, then place the lids on top and screw on the bands until fingertip tight.
  7. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 45 minutes. Remove the jars from the water and let them cool completely before storing.

Spicy Bread and Butter Pickles

Preparation Time: 30 minutes Canning Time: 10 minutes Yield: 6 pints

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds of pickling cucumbers, sliced
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup of pickling salt
  • 2 cups of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 1/2 cups of white sugar
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of celery seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon of crushed red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced cucumbers, onions, and pickling salt. Mix well and let it sit for 2 hours.
  2. Drain the cucumber mixture and rinse it thoroughly under cold water to remove excess salt.
  3. In a large pot, combine the apple cider vinegar, white sugar, brown sugar, mustard seeds, turmeric, celery seeds, and crushed red pepper flakes. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the sugar has dissolved.
  4. Add the cucumber mixture to the pot and return it to a boil. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Meanwhile, sterilize your canning jars and lids by boiling them in a large pot of water for 10 minutes.
  6. Using a slotted spoon, pack the hot pickles into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace. Ladle the hot pickling liquid over the pickles, again leaving 1/2 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, then place the lids on top and screw on the bands until fingertip tight.
  7. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. Remove the jars from the water and let them cool completely before storing.

Pickling Recipes

Dilly Beans

Preparation Time: 20 minutes Pickling Time: 10 minutes Yield: 4 pints

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of green beans, trimmed
  • 4 cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 4 sprigs of fresh dill
  • 2 teaspoons of red pepper flakes
  • 2 cups of apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of pickling salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the apple cider vinegar, water, and pickling salt to a boil, stirring until the salt has dissolved.
  2. Meanwhile, sterilize your canning jars and lids by boiling them in a large pot of water for 10 minutes.
  3. Pack the green beans, garlic cloves, fresh dill, and red pepper flakes into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace.
  4. Ladle the hot pickling liquid into the jars, covering the beans and leaving 1/2 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, then place the lids on top and screw on the bands until fingertip tight.
  5. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. Remove the jars from the water and let them cool completely before storing.

Pickled Jalapeños

Preparation Time: 15 minutes Pickling Time: 10 minutes Yield: 4 pints

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of jalapeño peppers, sliced
  • 2 cups of white vinegar
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of pickling salt
  • 4 cloves of garlic, peeled

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the white vinegar, water, and pickling salt to a boil, stirring until the salt has dissolved.
  2. Meanwhile, sterilize your canning jars and lids by boiling them in a large pot of water for 10 minutes.
  3. Pack the sliced jalapeños and garlic cloves into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace.
  4. Ladle the hot pickling liquid into the jars, covering the jalapeños and leaving 1/2 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, then place the lids on top and screw on the bands until fingertip tight.
  5. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. Remove the jars from the water and let them cool completely before storing.

Conclusion

Preserving the harvest through canning and pickling is not only a practical way to make the most of the bountiful harvest season, but it also allows you to enjoy the vibrant flavors of fresh produce all year round. Whether you choose to make classic tomato sauce, tangy bread and butter pickles, crunchy dilly beans, or spicy pickled jalapeños, these farm-to-table recipes will surely impress your taste buds. So grab your canning jars, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a journey of preserving the harvest. Happy canning and pickling!
