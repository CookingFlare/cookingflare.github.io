Salted Pretzel Caramel Ice Cream: A Sweet and Salty Treat


If you’re a fan of the sweet and salty flavor combination, then you’re in for a treat with this homemade Salted Pretzel Caramel Ice Cream recipe. This indulgent frozen dessert perfectly balances the rich sweetness of caramel with the satisfying crunch of salted pretzels, creating a flavor sensation that will have your taste buds singing. Whether you’re hosting a summer soiree or simply craving a delightful homemade treat, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup salted pretzels, coarsely chopped
  • 1 cup caramel sauce

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ice Cream Base:

    • In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, and salt over medium heat. Stir the mixture occasionally until it reaches a gentle simmer. Remove the saucepan from the heat.

  2. Whisk the Egg Yolks:

    • In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks until they are slightly thickened. Slowly pour about 1/2 cup of the warm cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to temper the eggs.

  3. Combine the Mixtures:

    • Pour the egg and cream mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream mixture. Return the saucepan to the stovetop and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon.

  4. Chill the Mixture:

    • Pour the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Stir in the vanilla extract. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, ensuring that the wrap touches the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  5. Churn the Ice Cream:

    • Once the custard is thoroughly chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. During the last few minutes of churning, add the chopped salted pretzels to the ice cream.

  6. Layer with Caramel Sauce:

    • Transfer the churned ice cream to a freezer-safe container, drizzling layers of caramel sauce as you fill the container. Use a knife to gently swirl the caramel through the ice cream, creating a marbled effect.

  7. Freeze and Serve:

    • Cover the container and freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until firm. When ready to serve, scoop the Salted Pretzel Caramel Ice Cream into bowls and garnish with additional caramel sauce and pretzel pieces.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For an extra indulgent touch, consider adding a sprinkle of flaky sea salt over the ice cream just before serving. The salt will enhance the sweet and salty contrast, elevating the flavor experience.
  • Experiment with different types of caramel sauce, such as salted caramel or bourbon-infused caramel, to customize the flavor profile of the ice cream.

Conclusion:

There’s something truly irresistible about the combination of sweet, creamy caramel and crunchy, salty pretzels. This Salted Pretzel Caramel Ice Cream recipe brings together these contrasting elements in a delightful frozen dessert that is both luxurious and comforting. Whether enjoyed on a hot summer day or as a special treat any time of the year, this homemade ice cream is sure to become a new favorite in your dessert repertoire. Treat yourself and your loved ones to the delightful flavors of this sweet and salty masterpiece!
