Paleo Chicken and Vegetable Kabobs: A Healthy and Delicious Recipe for Summer


Summer is here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to fire up the grill and enjoy some delicious and healthy outdoor cooking. One of the most popular and easy-to-make recipes for summer is the Paleo Chicken and Vegetable Kabobs. This recipe is not only a crowd-pleaser, but it’s also packed with nutrients and flavor. In this post, we’ll take a closer look at how to make this dish and why it’s a great option for a warm weather meal.

Why Choose Paleo?

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s talk about why Paleo is such a popular diet. The Paleo diet is based on the idea of eating foods that were available to our ancestors during the Paleolithic era. This means focusing on whole, unprocessed foods like meats, fish, fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds. The diet eliminates grains, dairy products, and processed foods, which are believed to be responsible for many modern health problems.

The Benefits of Grilling

Grilling is a great way to cook vegetables and meats during the summer months. It’s a low-fat cooking method that helps retain the natural flavors and textures of the ingredients. Grilling also allows for even cooking and can help prevent the formation of carcinogens that can occur when foods are cooked at high temperatures.

The Recipe

Here’s what you’ll need for this recipe:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 onion, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 zucchini, cut into 1-inch slices
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 10 bamboo skewers, soaked in water for at least 30 minutes

Here’s how to make the kabobs:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, and dried oregano. Add the chicken, bell peppers, onion, garlic, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes. Toss to combine, making sure all the ingredients are well coated with the marinade.
  3. Thread the ingredients onto the bamboo skewers, leaving a little space between each piece.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Place the kabobs on the grill and cook for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to keep in mind:

  • Use any combination of vegetables you like or have on hand. Some other options include mushrooms, asparagus, and bell peppers.
  • Add some heat to your kabobs by including sliced jalapeños or red pepper flakes.
  • For a Mediterranean twist, add some Kalamata olives and artichoke hearts to the marinade.
  • If you don’t have bamboo skewers, you can use metal skewers or even toothpicks.
  • Consider making a big batch of kabobs and freezing them for a quick and easy meal later.

Conclusion

Paleo Chicken and Vegetable Kabobs are a delicious and healthy way to enjoy the flavors of summer. With its focus on whole, unprocessed foods and low-fat cooking method, this recipe is a great option for anyone looking to eat healthier. Try it out and enjoy the perfect summer meal!
