Mini Chef Special: DIY Personal Pizza Pockets


Are you craving a delicious, homemade pizza but don’t want the hassle of making a traditional pie? Look no further than our Mini Chef Special: DIY Personal Pizza Pockets! These delightful pockets of cheesy, saucy goodness are easy to make and perfect for a quick lunch or dinner. Plus, they’re a hit with both kids and adults alike!

Ingredients

  • 1 package of refrigerated pizza dough
  • 1 cup of marinara sauce
  • 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup of your favorite pizza toppings (pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Optional: additional seasonings like oregano, garlic powder, or red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Roll out the refrigerated pizza dough onto a lightly floured surface. Use a rolling pin to create a rectangle shape, about 1/4 inch thick.

  3. Using a sharp knife or pizza cutter, cut the dough into smaller rectangles, approximately 4x6 inches in size. You should end up with about 6-8 rectangles, depending on the size of your dough.

  4. Spoon a tablespoon of marinara sauce onto one half of each dough rectangle, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese on top of the sauce.

  5. Now it’s time to get creative! Add your favorite pizza toppings on top of the cheese. Whether you prefer classic pepperoni or a veggie medley, the choice is yours. Don’t be afraid to mix and match flavors!

  6. Fold the empty half of the dough over the toppings, creating a pocket. Use your fingers or a fork to seal the edges, ensuring that no filling escapes while baking.

  7. Carefully transfer the pizza pockets onto the prepared baking sheet. Brush the tops with the beaten egg wash to give them a beautiful golden finish.

  8. If desired, sprinkle additional seasonings like oregano, garlic powder, or red pepper flakes on top of the pockets for added flavor.

  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the pockets are golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  10. Remove from the oven and let the pizza pockets cool for a few minutes before serving. Be careful, as the filling will be hot!

  11. Serve your Mini Chef Special: DIY Personal Pizza Pockets with a side of marinara sauce for dipping, and enjoy the delicious combination of flavors and textures.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different pizza toppings to customize your pizza pockets. Try adding cooked bacon, sliced olives, or even pineapple for a unique twist!

  • If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought pizza dough instead of making it from scratch. Just make sure to follow the package instructions for baking.

  • Don’t limit yourself to just marinara sauce! Try using barbecue sauce, pesto, or even alfredo sauce as a base for your pizza pockets.

  • If you have leftovers, these pizza pockets can be refrigerated and reheated for a quick and easy lunch the next day. Simply pop them in the microwave or toaster oven until heated through.

So, what are you waiting for? Get your mini chefs in the kitchen and start creating your own Personal Pizza Pockets. It’s a fun and delicious way to satisfy your pizza cravings, and the best part is, you can customize them to your heart’s content. Enjoy!
