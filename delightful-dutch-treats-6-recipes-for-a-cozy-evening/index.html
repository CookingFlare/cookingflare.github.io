Delightful Dutch Treats: 6 Recipes for a Cozy Evening


As the chilly winds of autumn begin to swirl and the days grow shorter, there’s nothing quite like curling up with a warm drink and delightful treats inspired by the cozy traditions of the Netherlands. Dutch cuisine offers a variety of sweet and savory dishes that embody comfort and warmth. In this blog post, we’ll explore six mouthwatering Dutch recipes that are perfect for a cozy evening at home. Each recipe is rich in flavor and deeply rooted in Dutch culinary heritage, making them ideal for sharing with family and friends.

1. Stroopwafels: Caramel-Syrup Waffles

Ingredients:

  • For the Waffle:

    • 250g all-purpose flour
    • 125g unsalted butter, softened
    • 100g brown sugar
    • 1 packet (7g) active dry yeast
    • 1 egg
    • ½ cup milk
    • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the Syrup:

    • 200g brown sugar
    • 100g unsalted butter
    • 200g dark syrup (or treacle)
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, mix the warm milk with the yeast and let it sit for about 5 minutes until frothy. Add the butter, sugar, egg, cinnamon, and salt. Gradually stir in the flour until a dough forms. Knead for about 5 minutes until smooth. Cover and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour.

  2. Make the Syrup: In a saucepan, combine the brown sugar, butter, dark syrup, and vanilla. Heat until everything is melted and well combined. Allow it to cool slightly.

  3. Form Waffles: Preheat your waffle iron. Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a golf ball). Flatten each ball and place in the waffle iron. Cook until golden brown.

  4. Assemble: Once the waffles are baked, slice them in half horizontally while still warm. Spread a generous layer of syrup inside and press the halves together. Enjoy with a warm cup of coffee or tea!

2. Bitterballen: Dutch Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 300g beef stew meat, finely minced
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • 500ml beef broth
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Breadcrumbs for coating
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling: In a saucepan, melt the butter and sauté the onions until translucent. Add the minced beef and cook until browned. Stir in the flour and cook for another minute. Gradually add the beef broth, stirring constantly until thickened.

  2. Season: Remove from heat and mix in Worcestershire sauce, mustard, salt, and pepper. Pour the mixture into a dish and let it cool completely in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or overnight.

  3. Form Meatballs: Once chilled, shape the mixture into small balls. Roll each ball in breadcrumbs.

  4. Fry: Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry the bitterballen in batches until golden brown and crispy. Serve hot with mustard for dipping.

3. Poffertjes: Fluffy Mini Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 250g all-purpose flour
  • 250ml milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • A pinch of salt
  • Butter for greasing the pan
  • Icing sugar for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Batter: In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt. In another bowl, whisk together milk and eggs. Gradually add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, combining until smooth. Stir in the yeast and let the batter sit for about 30 minutes.

  2. Cook Poffertjes: Heat a poffertjes pan or a non-stick skillet over medium heat and lightly grease with butter. Pour the batter into the wells of the poffertjes pan, filling each about halfway. Cook for 1-2 minutes until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for another minute.

  3. Serve: Dust with icing sugar and serve warm with butter or maple syrup.

4. Haring: Dutch Herring

Ingredients:

  • Fresh herring fillets (about 4)
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 pickles, diced
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Herring: Clean and gut the herring fillets if not already prepared. Rinse under cold water.

  2. Assemble: Place the herring on a serving platter. Top with sliced onions and diced pickles. Garnish with fresh parsley.

  3. Serve: Serve chilled and allow guests to enjoy with cocktail picks.

5. Oliebollen: Dutch Doughnuts

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 10g active dry yeast
  • 250ml warm milk
  • 50g sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 100g currants or raisins
  • A pinch of salt
  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Dough: In a bowl, dissolve yeast in warm milk. Add sugar, eggs, and salt, mixing well. Gradually add flour, kneading until smooth. Incorporate the currants or raisins. Cover and let rise for about 1 hour.

  2. Fry Oliebollen: Heat oil in a deep-fryer. Use a spoon to drop balls of dough into the hot oil, frying in batches until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.

  3. Serve: Dust generously with powdered sugar and serve warm.

6. Dutch Apple Pie (Appeltaart)

Ingredients:

  • For the Pastry:

    • 300g all-purpose flour
    • 200g unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
    • 150g sugar
    • 1 egg
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the Filling:

    • 1.5 kg apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
    • 100g sugar (or to taste)
    • 1 tsp cinnamon
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • 50g raisins (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Pastry: In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, and salt. Cut in the butter until crumbly. Stir in the egg and form into a dough. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare Filling: In a large bowl, toss apples with sugar, cinnamon, lemon juice, and raisins (if using).

  3. Assemble Pie: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Roll out 2/3 of the pastry and line a pie dish. Fill with the apple mixture. Roll out the remaining pastry for the top, cutting slits for steam.

  4. Bake: Bake for 50 minutes or until the apples are tender and the crust is golden. Allow to cool slightly before serving, ideally with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

Conclusion

These six delightful Dutch recipes are perfect for transforming a chilly evening into a warm and cozy experience. Whether you’re indulging in sweet stroopwafels, crispy bitterballen, or a slice of rich appeltaart, each dish brings a taste of the Netherlands right to your home. Gather your loved ones, brew some fragrant coffee or tea, and enjoy the comforting flavors that these traditional treats offer. Perfect for sharing, these recipes will surely become a staple in your cozy evening repertoire!
