Quick and Delicious Indian Street Food Recipes


Indian street food is a reflection of the country's rich culinary diversity, with each region offering a unique fusion of flavors and ingredients. The vibrant colors, enticing aromas, and delicious tastes of Indian street food have captured the hearts of people from all over the world. From spicy chaat items to sumptuous kebabs, the variety of Indian street food is extraordinary. In this blog, we'll explore some quick and delicious Indian street food recipes that you can easily make at home. These recipes will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of India, where food is a way of life. Whether you're a food enthusiast or a beginner in the kitchen, these simple and mouth-watering recipes will surely entice you to give them a try. Chaat Items - Sweet, Savory, and Tangy Indian chaat items are a staple of street food, and their popularity cuts across age groups and regions. These popular items have interesting combinations of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors that tantalize the taste buds. Sev Puri Recipe

Sev puri is a popular North Indian chaat dish made with crispy puris topped with a flavorful mixture of boiled potatoes, chickpeas, onions, and a drizzle of tamarind sauce. Ingredients

4-5 puris 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled, peeled, and diced 1 cup chickpeas, boiled 1/4 cup oil 1 small onion, finely chopped 2 tsp carom seeds 1 tsp cumin seeds 1 tsp chaat masala Salt to taste Tamarind sauce for serving

Instructions

To make sev puri, heat oil in a pan over medium heat and add cumin seeds and carom seeds. Let them sizzle. Add the boiling potatoes and chickpeas to the pan and stir for about 5 minutes. Add the chopped onion and mix well. Season with chaat masala and salt to taste. Warm the puris by heating them in a pan for a few minutes. Then, break them into pieces and fill with the potato-chickpea mixture. Garnish with tamarind sauce and sev (fried gram flour noodles) for added crunch.

Ragda Patties Recipe

Ragda patties is a popular chaat item that consists of crispy potato patties served in a spicy curry made with boiled and mashed potatoes, peas, and spices. Ingredients

2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed 1 cup gram flour 1 cup semolina 1/2 cup rice flour Vegetable oil for frying For the ragda: 2 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and diced 1 cup peas 2 tbsp coriroller, fennel seeds, and cumin seeds Salt to taste Chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

To make the patties, combine the mashed potatoes, gram flour, semolina, and rice flour. Mix well. Add a little water to the mixture to make a thick batter. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat and fry the potato mixture in small portions to make the patties. To make the ragda, add the boiled potatoes, peas, and spices to a pan. Simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the potatoes are slightly soft. Serve the crispy patties in the spicy ragda sauce and garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

Kebabs - Varied and Vibrant Kebabs are a staple in Indian street food, offering a fascinating mix of flavors, colors, and textures. These popular dishes are made with marinated meat (chicken, lamb, or vegetables) grilled on skewers and served hot. Chicken Tikka Kebab Recipe

Chicken tikka kebabs are marinated pieces of chicken breast cooked in the oven and served in a creamy tomato-curry sauce. Ingredients

500 gms boneless chicken breast 1 cup plain yogurt 1 tsp cumin powder 1 tsp coriander powder 1 tsp cayenne pepper 1 tsp garam masala powder Salt to taste 2 tbsp lemon juice 2 tbsp vegetable oil 2 medium-sized onions, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 medium-sized tomatoes, diced 1 tsp cumin seeds Chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

To make the marinade, combine the yogurt, cumin powder, coriander powder, cayenne pepper, garam masala powder, salt, lemon juice, cumin seeds, minced garlic, and chopped onion in a bowl. Add the chicken breast to the marinade and mix well. Refrigerate the mixture for about 30 minutes to an hour. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the marinated chicken on a baking sheet lined with baking paper and bake for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally. While the chicken is baking, heat oil in a pan over medium heat and sauté the chopped onions until they're translucent. Add the diced tomatoes to the pan and sauté for about 5 minutes. Add the baked chicken to the pan and stir well. Serve in a sauce of tomato-curry sauce garnished with chopped coriander leaves.

Noodles and Rice - A Delight for Comfort Food Indian noodles and rice are simple and comforting dishes that adapt to the moods and preferences of the people in India. These staple dishes are often made add elegant twists to make them suitable for special occasions. Schezwan Noodle Recipe

Schezwan noodle is a popular Indo-Chinese dish consisting of stir-fried noodles with the bold flavors of soy sauce and schezwan sauce. Ingredients

1 batch egg noodles 2 medium-sized onions, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup mixed vegetables 2 tbsp soy sauce 1 tsp schezwan sauce Salt to taste Greens for garnish

Instructions

Cook the egg noodles according to the packet instructions. Drain and set aside. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat and add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onions are lightly browned. Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and sauté for about 5-7 minutes. Add the cooked noodles, soy sauce, and schezwan sauce to the pan. Stir well and mix everything. Season with salt to taste and garnish with greens.

These Indian street food dishes make a reflection of the country's rich culinary culture and are perfect to enjoy on its streets or in comfort of one's home. By trying these recipes, you can bring the essence of India's streets to your kitchen. Explore this blog to find more Indian street food recipes to satisfy your taste buds.
