Christmas Recipes for a Beautiful Holiday Buffet


When the holiday season rolls around, there’s something magical about gathering with family and friends around a table brimming with festive fare. A holiday buffet offers the perfect opportunity to serve an array of dishes that cater to every taste—savory, sweet, light, and indulgent. Whether you’re hosting a small cozy gathering or a grand celebration, these Christmas recipes will help you create a stunning spread that delights and impresses.

Setting the Scene for the Perfect Holiday Buffet

Before diving into the recipes themselves, consider the overall design of your buffet. Presentation matters just as much as taste, especially when you want to capture that warm holiday spirit. Mix rustic elements like wooden boards and burlap runners with elegant touches such as crystal bowls and polished silverware.

Create sections on your buffet table—starters, mains, sides, and desserts—for smooth flow and ease of access. Incorporating fresh evergreens, candles, and colorful serving dishes will imbue the table with festive cheer.

Now, let’s explore recipes to fill your buffet table with mouthwatering delights.

Starters and Appetizers

1. Cranberry Brie Puff Pastry Bites

These bite-sized appetizers balance creamy, tart, and flaky textures in one easy-to-eat morsel—ideal for holiday mingling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1/2 cup cranberry sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 8 oz Brie cheese, cut into small cubes
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out puff pastry and cut into 16 squares.
  3. Place a cube of Brie in the center of each square.
  4. Top with about a teaspoon of cranberry sauce.
  5. Fold the corners toward the center and pinch to seal lightly.
  6. Brush the tops with egg wash and bake for 12-15 minutes until golden.
  7. Garnish with a sprinkle of fresh thyme before serving.

2. Roasted Red Pepper and Goat Cheese Crostini

This starter combines the sweetness of roasted peppers with tangy goat cheese and crispy bread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 1 jar roasted red peppers, drained and chopped
  • 6 oz goat cheese, softened
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Brush baguette slices lightly with olive oil; bake for 5-7 minutes until crisp.
  3. In a bowl, mix goat cheese with basil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spread a generous layer of goat cheese on each toast.
  5. Top with chopped roasted red peppers.
  6. Serve immediately or set out for guests to enjoy.

Main Course Selections

3. Herb-Crusted Prime Rib Roast

A centerpiece prime rib instantly elevates your Christmas buffet, bringing impressive flavor and tenderness to the table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 (5-6 lb) prime rib roast, boneless
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix garlic, rosemary, thyme, mustard, salt, pepper, and olive oil.
  3. Rub the herb mixture evenly over the entire roast.
  4. Place roast on a rack in a roasting pan.
  5. Roast for 15 minutes at 450°F, then reduce temperature to 325°F (165°C).
  6. Continue roasting for approximately 15 minutes per pound for medium-rare.
  7. Let rest for 20 minutes before carving.

4. Maple-Glazed Ham with Cloves

Sweet, savory, and aromatic, a maple-glazed ham is a holiday buffet classic that pleases the palate and the eye.

Ingredients:

  • 8-10 lb fully cooked ham
  • 1 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • Whole cloves

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Score the ham in a diamond pattern and stud with cloves at each intersection.
  3. Place ham in a roasting pan.
  4. Mix maple syrup and mustard; brush generously over ham.
  5. Bake ham for about 2 hours, basting every 30 minutes with glaze.
  6. Let rest before slicing.

Delicious Side Dishes

5. Garlic-Parmesan Roasted Brussels Sprouts

These Brussels sprouts transform from humble veggies into irresistible, crispy bites with a touch of garlic and cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and black pepper

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  5. Sprinkle with Parmesan and roast another 5 minutes until golden.

6. Creamy Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Topping

Sweet potatoes are a must-have—this dish combines silky sweet potatoes and crunchy pecan goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/2 cup mini marshmallows (optional)

Method:

  1. Boil sweet potatoes until tender; drain and mash.
  2. Stir in brown sugar, butter, cream, vanilla, and cinnamon.
  3. Transfer mixture to a greased baking dish.
  4. Mix pecans with a tablespoon of brown sugar; sprinkle over sweet potatoes.
  5. Optionally, add mini marshmallows on top.
  6. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25-30 minutes until bubbly and topping is toasted.

Fresh and Festive Salads

7. Winter Citrus and Pomegranate Salad

Bright colors and crisp textures make this salad a refreshing contrast to richer buffet items.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 2 oranges, peeled and segmented
  • 1 grapefruit, peeled and segmented
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper

Method:

  1. Combine greens, citrus segments, pomegranate, almonds, and feta in a large bowl.
  2. Whisk olive oil, honey, vinegar, salt, and pepper for dressing.
  3. Drizzle dressing over salad and toss gently before serving.

Dessert Delights

8. Classic Yule Log (Bûche de Noël)

This charming chocolate cake shaped like a log is a beautifully traditional holiday dessert.

Ingredients:

  • Chocolate sponge cake (recipe follows)
  • Chocolate buttercream frosting
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Chocolate Sponge Cake:

  • 4 eggs
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Buttercream Frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 2-3 tbsp milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Beat eggs and sugar until thick and pale.
  3. Sift flour, cocoa, and salt; fold gently into egg mixture.
  4. Spread batter evenly on a lined jelly roll pan.
  5. Bake 10-12 minutes.
  6. While still warm, roll cake in a kitchen towel dusted with powdered sugar; let cool.
  7. Mix buttercream ingredients until smooth.
  8. Unroll cake gently; spread buttercream evenly; re-roll.
  9. Frost exterior with remaining buttercream.
  10. Create bark texture with a fork.
  11. Dust lightly with powdered sugar before serving.

9. Spiced Gingerbread Cookies

Nothing says holiday like aromatic gingerbread. These cookies double as edible decorations or treats.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Method:

  1. Whisk together dry ingredients.
  2. Beat butter and sugar until fluffy.
  3. Add egg, molasses, and vanilla.
  4. Gradually add dry ingredients until combined.
  5. Chill dough for at least 2 hours.
  6. Roll out dough and cut into shapes.
  7. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 8-10 minutes.
  8. Decorate with icing once cooled.

Tips for a Stress-Free Holiday Buffet

  • Plan Ahead: Many dishes like the ham, some desserts, and even the appetizers can be prepped the day before.
  • Variety is Key: Include an assortment of flavors and textures—creamy, crunchy, sweet, savory—to create an exciting buffet experience.
  • Label Your Dishes: Consider small name cards next to dishes, especially if you have dietary considerations or unique ingredients.
  • Keep Food Warm: Use chafing dishes or warming trays if needed to maintain ideal serving temperatures.
  • Beverage Pairings: Don’t forget to offer drinks that match your menu, from mulled wine and eggnog to sparkling water or cider.

Creating a beautiful Christmas buffet is all about combining traditional favorites with fresh ideas and stunning presentation. The recipes above offer a diverse but harmonious selection, sure to leave your guests feeling merry and satisfied. So set your table with care, fire up the oven, and let your holiday feast become an unforgettable celebration of food and togetherness. Cheers to a delicious and joyful Christmas!
