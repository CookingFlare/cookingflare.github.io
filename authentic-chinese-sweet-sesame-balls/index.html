Authentic Chinese Sweet Sesame Balls


Few dishes evoke the comforting nostalgia of traditional Chinese treats quite like sweet sesame balls, known as “Jian Dui” in Mandarin. These golden, crispy spheres hold a delicate balance between texture and taste, offering a mouthful of crunchy sesame-studded exterior and soft, chewy interior. Highly coveted during celebrations like Lunar New Year, sweet sesame balls have earned a place not only in homes but also at bustling dim sum restaurants. This authentic recipe will guide you step-by-step to achieving true perfection in recreating this timeless Chinese dessert.

A Deep Dive into Jian Dui

Sweet sesame balls have not only been a part of Chinese culinary tradition for centuries but also serve as symbolisms of prosperity and happiness. Their round shape mirrors the idea of completeness and unity, making them a cornerstone during family holidays and joyous occasions. Made primarily from glutinous rice flour and stuffed with sweet fillings, these deep-fried treats embody culinary ingenuity, blending simple ingredients to create a dish that’s packed with flavor and texture.

Crafting the ideal sweet sesame balls at home requires an understanding of the key components: the flour, the filling, and the frying process. By paying close attention to these aspects, you’ll be able to achieve the authentic taste and texture that rivals those from the best pastry shops.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • ¾ cup water (warm, but not boiling)
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar

For the Filling:

  • ½ cup red bean paste or sesame paste (homemade or store-bought)
    • Optional: substitute with lotus seed paste or peanut paste for varied flavors

For Coating:

  • 1 cup white sesame seeds

For Frying:

  • Enough neutral oil (vegetable or canola oil) to cover the sesame balls while frying

Step-by-Step Instructions to Perfect Sesame Balls

1. Preparing the Dough

Start by sifting the glutinous rice flour into a large mixing bowl. This step prevents clumps and ensures the flour is evenly aerated, making for smoother dough. Mix in the granulated sugar, which adds just enough sweetness to complement the rich filling.

Gradually pour the warm water into the bowl while stirring, forming a shaggy dough. Once the water is fully incorporated, knead the mixture by hand until it comes together in a smooth, pliable ball. The dough should be elastic but not sticky, soft enough to mold yet firm enough to hold its shape.

Pro tip: If the dough feels sticky, add a small amount of rice flour; if it feels dry, incorporate a teaspoon of warm water at a time until it’s perfected.

2. Making the Filling

The filling can make or break the overall taste experience of sweet sesame balls. Red bean paste is a classic choice, but sesame paste brings a nuttier, more earthy profile that many people adore. Shape the paste into small, teaspoon-sized balls and set them aside. If you want a mix of textures, you can even incorporate chopped nuts or seeds into the paste.

3. Assembling the Sesame Balls

Pinch off a small piece of dough and roll it into a ball, roughly 1–1.5 inches in diameter. Flatten the ball into a disc in your palm, ensuring uniform thickness. Place the filling in the center of the disc and carefully fold the edges toward the center, sealing the filling completely.

Gently roll the stuffed dough between your palms to create a smooth, round ball. Repeat the process until all the dough and filling are used. You should end up with about 12–14 sesame balls, depending on their size.

4. Coating with Sesame Seeds

Pour the sesame seeds onto a large, shallow plate. Lightly dampen each ball with water—this helps the seeds stick effectively. Roll each ball in the sesame seeds, pressing gently to ensure a thick, even coating. A properly coated sesame ball should look vibrant and slightly textured.

5. Frying the Sweet Sesame Balls

Heat oil in a deep pot or skillet to medium heat (around 325°F or 160°C). It’s crucial to maintain the oil temperature throughout frying; if it’s too hot, the sesame balls will cook unevenly, leaving the center raw. If it’s too low, they will absorb excess oil and turn greasy.

Carefully lower the sesame balls into the oil, frying just a few at a time to avoid overcrowding. Stir gently and continuously with a slotted spoon or chopsticks to promote even cooking. After about 6–8 minutes, the balls should puff up and develop a rich, golden hue.

Remove the sesame balls with a slotted spoon and drain them on a wire rack or paper towels to remove excess oil.

Tips for Best Results

Achieving the Perfect Texture

The hallmark of a great Jian Dui is its texture: light, hollow, and crisp on the outside with just the right chewiness inside. To achieve this, make sure to knead the dough well and seal the filling tightly to prevent leaks during frying. Take care not to over-fry, as this can turn the balls dense and overly hard.

Oil Temperature Matters

Using a food thermometer is highly recommended to keep the temperature consistent. If you don’t own one, test the oil by dropping in a small piece of dough; it should sizzle but not turn brown immediately.

Variations to Explore

Experiment with alternative fillings to keep this recipe exciting! Sweet yam paste, matcha-flavored bean paste, or chocolate spread can offer innovative twists without straying too far from tradition.

Tips for Storing Sweet Sesame Balls

While sweet sesame balls are best enjoyed fresh and crunchy, you can store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for a day or two. To reheat, pop them into the oven at 350°F (175°C) for 5–7 minutes until warmed through and slightly crisp.

If you’d like to make them ahead of time, freeze the uncooked sesame balls after they’ve been coated with sesame seeds. When ready to fry, allow them to thaw slightly before frying as instructed.

Why Homemade is Better

There’s something deeply satisfying about making authentic sweet sesame balls from scratch. Unlike store-bought versions, homemade Jian Dui gives you complete control over quality, ingredients, and flavor. You can adjust the sweetness of the dough, tweak the filling proportions, or even introduce healthier alternatives like whole-grain glutinous flour. Plus, the process itself connects you to the rich culinary traditions of China, ensuring each bite tastes not only delicious but meaningful.

Closing Thoughts

Sweet sesame balls are not just a dessert—they’re a journey into the heart of Chinese culture and cuisine. Making them at home allows you to experience the intricate layers of flavor and texture that make these treats so beloved. Whether you’re serving them during a holiday, preparing them as a unique addition to your dim sum spread, or simply indulging, sweet sesame balls promise joy in every bite.

Now that you’ve mastered this recipe, you’ll be able to impress your family, friends, and guests with your culinary prowess, always bringing excitement and prosperity to any occasion.
