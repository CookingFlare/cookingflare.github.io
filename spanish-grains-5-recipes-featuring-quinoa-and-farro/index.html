Spanish Grains: 5 Recipes Featuring Quinoa and Farro


When you think of Spanish cuisine, images of saffron-infused paella, crispy tapas, and savory chorizo often come to mind. However, two grains—quinoa and farro—are increasingly making their way into the Spanish culinary landscape, adding a nutritious twist to traditional dishes. These ancient grains, celebrated for their nutty flavors and hearty textures, blend effortlessly with vibrant Spanish spices and ingredients. Today, we’ll explore five inspiring recipes that showcase quinoa and farro as starring ingredients in Spanish-inspired dishes. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or a curious foodie, these recipes will bring a fresh spin to your kitchen and elevate your grain game.

Why Include Quinoa and Farro in Spanish Cooking?

Quinoa, originally from the Andean region of South America, and farro, an ancient wheat grain with roots in the Mediterranean, have become staples in health-conscious kitchens worldwide. Their benefits go beyond taste—they pack protein, fiber, and essential minerals. Spanish cooking, known for its bold flavors and simple ingredient combinations, adapts seamlessly to these grains.

Quinoa offers a light, fluffy texture with a subtle nuttiness that complements vibrant salads and stews. Farro, on the other hand, boasts a chewy bite and robust nutty flavor well-suited for rustic dishes and hearty meals. Together, they prove that Spanish cuisine can evolve without losing its soul.

1. Spanish Quinoa Salad with Roasted Peppers and Manchego

This salad embodies everything people love about Spanish flavors—smoky, sweet, and savory—with quinoa as the base grain.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 roasted red peppers, sliced (jarred or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup Manchego cheese, cubed
  • 1/4 cup green olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon sherry vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the quinoa: Bring the water to a boil in a medium saucepan. Add quinoa, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes, or until water is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and let cool.
  2. Prepare the dressing: In a small bowl, whisk olive oil, sherry vinegar, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Assemble the salad: In a large bowl, combine quinoa, roasted peppers, Manchego cheese, olives, and red onion.
  4. Dress and toss: Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat evenly.
  5. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle fresh parsley on top before serving. This salad works well chilled or at room temperature, perfect for summer lunches or tapas.

2. Farro and Chorizo Spanish Stew

Hearty and comforting, this farro and chorizo stew is packed with robust flavors and a satisfying chew from the farro.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup farro, rinsed
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 200g Spanish chorizo, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the farro: In a pot, bring chicken broth to a boil and add farro. Reduce heat and simmer for 25–30 minutes, until tender but chewy. Drain any excess liquid and set aside.
  2. Cook chorizo: In a deep skillet or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chorizo slices and cook until browned and fragrant. Remove and set aside.
  3. Sauté aromatics: In the same pan, add onion, garlic, and bell pepper. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add spices and tomatoes: Stir in smoked paprika, cumin, and red pepper flakes. Add crushed tomatoes and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Combine all: Return chorizo to the pan, then fold in cooked farro. Simmer gently to blend flavors, seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve hot: Garnish with fresh herbs and serve with crusty bread for a full meal.

3. Spanish Farro Paella with Saffron and Seafood

Paella needs no introduction, but this farro-based version offers a nutritious and slightly nuttier take on the classic, substituting traditional rice with farro.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups farro, soaked for at least 1 hour
  • 4 cups seafood or vegetable broth
  • A pinch of saffron threads
  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup mussels, cleaned
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, grated or finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare saffron broth: Heat broth and sprinkle in saffron threads, letting it steep.
  2. Cook aromatics: In a wide paella pan or large skillet, heat olive oil. Sauté onion, garlic, and bell pepper until softened.
  3. Add tomatoes and spices: Stir in tomatoes and smoked paprika, cooking until the mixture thickens slightly.
  4. Add farro: Drain soaked farro and add it to the pan, stirring to coat grains.
  5. Pour saffron broth: Add saffron-infused broth to the pan, bringing to a simmer. Reduce heat and cook uncovered for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  6. Add seafood and peas: Nestle shrimp and mussels into the farro mixture. Scatter peas on top and cook until seafood is done and mussels open, about 5-7 minutes. Discard any unopened mussels.
  7. Season and serve: Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve warm, with lemon wedges to squeeze over the top.

4. Quinoa-Stuffed Piquillo Peppers with Chorizo and Manchego

Piquillo peppers, sweet and mildly smoky, are a staple in Spanish cuisine. Stuffing them with protein-rich quinoa, spicy chorizo, and creamy Manchego creates a perfect appetizer or light meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked and cooled
  • 8-10 piquillo peppers, drained
  • 150g chorizo, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Manchego cheese, shredded or crumbled
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook chorizo: Heat olive oil in a pan and cook chorizo until browned. Add garlic towards the end and sauté briefly.
  2. Mix filling: In a bowl, combine cooked quinoa, chorizo, Manchego, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stuff peppers: Gently fill each piquillo pepper with the quinoa mixture.
  4. Bake: Place stuffed peppers in a baking dish and bake at 375°F (190°C) for about 15 minutes, until heated through and cheese slightly melted.
  5. Garnish: Sprinkle with fresh parsley and serve warm as a tapas-style dish.

5. Spanish Farro and Quinoa Breakfast Bowl with Chorizo and Eggs

Start your day with a protein-packed breakfast that carries all the smoky, satisfying flavors of Spain. Combining both grains adds texture and depth for a wholesome morning boost.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup farro, cooked
  • 1/2 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 100g chorizo, sliced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 small avocado, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Smoked paprika for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook chorizo: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add chorizo slices and cook until crisp.
  2. Prepare eggs: In the same pan, crack eggs and cook sunny side up or your preferred style.
  3. Heat grains: Warm farro and quinoa together in a bowl or pan.
  4. Assemble bowl: Layer grains at the bottom, top with chorizo, eggs, cherry tomatoes, and avocado slices.
  5. Season and garnish: Sprinkle salt, pepper, and smoked paprika over the eggs, and add fresh herbs if desired.
  6. Serve: Perfect for a filling breakfast or brunch that keeps you energized.

Final Thoughts

The marriage of Spanish flavors with nutrient-rich grains like quinoa and farro offers exciting opportunities for creative and healthy cooking. These recipes illustrate how traditional Spanish ingredients—smoked paprika, chorizo, Manchego cheese—can be reimagined alongside versatile grains to create dishes that delight the palate and nourish the body.

Next time you want to bring a taste of Spain to your table with a healthful twist, give these recipes a try. They combine the rustic charm of Spanish cuisine with modern superfoods, guaranteeing meals that are as satisfying as they are vibrant.

Happy cooking!
