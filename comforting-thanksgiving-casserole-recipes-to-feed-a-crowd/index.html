Comforting Thanksgiving Casserole Recipes to Feed a Crowd


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones and enjoying a delicious feast together. And what better way to feed a crowd than with comforting and hearty casseroles? These dishes are not only easy to make, but they also provide a warm and satisfying meal that will leave everyone feeling content and grateful. So, let’s dive into some mouthwatering Thanksgiving casserole recipes that are sure to please even the pickiest eaters.

1. Classic Turkey and Stuffing Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 2 cups prepared stuffing
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • 1 cup diced celery
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked turkey, stuffing, frozen peas, diced carrots, diced celery, chicken broth, shredded cheddar cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, melted butter, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Transfer the mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly.
  4. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  5. Let the casserole cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy the warm and comforting flavors of Thanksgiving in every bite!

2. Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Streusel Topping

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mashed sweet potatoes
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, brown sugar, melted butter, milk, vanilla extract, and beaten eggs. Mix well until smooth.
  3. Transfer the sweet potato mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the chopped pecans, all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, and melted butter. Mix well to form a crumbly streusel topping.
  5. Sprinkle the streusel topping over the sweet potato mixture, covering it evenly.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes until the top is golden and the casserole is heated through.
  7. Allow the casserole to cool for a few minutes before serving. The creamy sweet potato base combined with the crunchy pecan streusel will surely be a hit at your Thanksgiving table.

3. Green Bean Casserole with Crispy Onion Topping

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (14.5 ounces each) French-style green beans, drained
  • 1 can (10.5 ounces) condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup crispy fried onions, plus more for topping
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the drained green beans, condensed cream of mushroom soup, milk, shredded cheddar cheese, crispy fried onions, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Transfer the green bean mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly.
  4. Sprinkle additional crispy fried onions on top for extra crunch and flavor.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes until the casserole is heated through and the edges are bubbly.
  6. Allow the casserole to cool for a few minutes before serving. The combination of tender green beans, creamy mushroom soup, and crispy onion topping will make this a Thanksgiving favorite.

Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or simply want to enjoy a comforting meal during the holiday season, these Thanksgiving casserole recipes are perfect for feeding a crowd. With their delicious flavors and easy preparation, you can take the stress out of cooking and focus on spending quality time with your loved ones. So, grab your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to indulge in these hearty and satisfying dishes. Happy Thanksgiving!
