Thanksgiving Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon


Thanksgiving dinner is a time-honored tradition, and while the star of the meal may be the turkey, the sides often steal the show. One dish that consistently turns heads and wins hearts is roasted Brussels sprouts with bacon. This smoky, savory, and slightly caramelized dish is the perfect complement to the rich flavors of a Thanksgiving spread. Whether you’re hosting a traditional feast or experimenting with contemporary twists, this recipe brings bold flavor with minimal effort.

The Magic of Brussels Sprouts

Often underappreciated in the vegetable world, Brussels sprouts have grown in popularity thanks to their versatility and ability to transform into tender, golden nuggets of flavor when roasted. Their natural earthiness pairs beautifully with crispy bacon and a blend of spices for a dish that’s both simple and indulgent.

Not only are they delicious, but Brussels sprouts are also a nutritional powerhouse. Packed with fiber, vitamins C and K, and antioxidants, this cruciferous vegetable is a standout choice on the Thanksgiving table for health-conscious guests looking to enjoy the feast without the guilt.

Why Bacon Makes a Difference

Adding bacon to Brussels sprouts isn’t just about indulging in crispy, meaty goodness—it’s about balance. Bacon introduces a layer of smoky depth and much-needed fat, which enhances the caramelization of the sprouts as they roast. The rendered bacon grease coats the vegetables in flavor while ensuring they achieve that perfect golden-brown exterior with tender centers. The salty, savory richness complements the mild bitterness of the sprouts, creating a harmonious dish that’s impossible to resist.

Essential Ingredients for Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

To create holiday-worthy roasted Brussels sprouts with bacon, you’ll need a handful of ingredients, many of which you may already have on hand. Here’s what you’ll need:

- Fresh Brussels Sprouts:

Choose sprouts that are firm, vibrant, and uniformly green. Aim for smaller sprouts, as larger ones may take longer to roast evenly. If available, consider purchasing them still attached to the stalk for maximum freshness.

- Thick-Cut Bacon:

Thick-cut bacon delivers the best flavor and texture for this recipe. You can experiment with maple-cured, applewood-smoked, or hickory-smoked varieties depending on the flavor profile you’re aiming for.

- Olive Oil:

Although bacon grease provides much of the richness, a touch of olive oil helps evenly coat the sprouts and facilitates additional caramelization.

- Garlic:

Fresh garlic adds pungent depth that elevates the dish. Thinly sliced or minced cloves roast beautifully alongside the sprouts.

- Balsamic Vinegar:

Balsamic vinegar introduces an acidic sweetness that balances the smoky bacon and roasted sprouts. Opt for a high-quality vinegar for the best results.

- Salt and Black Pepper:

Simple seasonings like sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper allow the natural flavors of the ingredients to shine.

- Optional Garnishes:

For added flair, consider garnishing the dish with grated Parmesan cheese, chopped fresh parsley, or a handful of dried cranberries for a tart, festive touch.

Step-by-Step Recipe for Thanksgiving Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

Roasting Brussels sprouts with bacon is a fantastic way to save time in the kitchen while delivering big on flavor. Follow these detailed steps to create a crowd-pleasing side dish that will have everyone reaching for seconds.

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 25 minutes

Serves: 6-8

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). A high temperature is key to achieving crispy, caramelized Brussels sprouts that brown evenly.

Step 2: Prep the Ingredients

Begin by washing the Brussels sprouts thoroughly under cold water to remove any dirt or debris. Trim off the tough ends and remove any discolored outer leaves. Cut each sprout in half lengthwise; this will expose a flat surface ideal for caramelization.

Next, slice the thick-cut bacon into small pieces, about half an inch wide. Set aside.

Step 3: Combine in a Mixing Bowl

In a large mixing bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with the sliced bacon, minced garlic, and a drizzle of olive oil. Add a generous pinch of salt and several grinds of freshly cracked black pepper. Stir or toss until everything is well coated.

Step 4: Arrange on a Baking Sheet

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup. Spread the sprouts and bacon pieces evenly across the sheet in a single layer, ensuring they aren’t overcrowded. This helps the vegetables roast properly instead of steaming.

Step 5: Roast to Perfection

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and roast for 20 minutes. At the halfway mark, carefully remove the sheet and toss the sprouts using a spatula to ensure even cooking. Return them to the oven for an additional 5 to 10 minutes or until they’re golden brown, with crispy edges and caramelized surfaces.

Step 6: Add Balsamic Vinegar

Once the roasting is complete, drizzle the hot sprouts and bacon with about two tablespoons of balsamic vinegar directly on the baking sheet. The heat will help the vinegar coat the dish without softening the crispy textures. Toss again, and transfer to a serving bowl.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

For an extra festive touch, garnish the roasted Brussels sprouts with Parmesan, parsley, or dried cranberries. Serve warm alongside your other Thanksgiving dishes.

Tips for Elevating This Dish

To make your roasted Brussels sprouts with bacon even more memorable, consider these variations and enhancements:

1. Add Nuts for Crunch:

Sprinkle chopped toasted pecans or almonds over the dish just before serving to add texture and a nutty flavor.

2. Incorporate Maple Syrup:

Drizzle a small amount of maple syrup onto the sprouts before roasting for a hint of sweetness. The syrup will caramelize and complement the bacon’s smokiness.

3. Infuse with Cheese:

Melt grated Gruyère or cheddar over the roasted sprouts for a creamy, decadent element.

4. Use Pancetta Instead of Bacon:

For a fancier variation, swap out bacon for pancetta. Its lighter, slightly sweet flavor works beautifully in this recipe.

5. Make It Vegan:

Replace bacon with smoked paprika and olive oil for a smoky, bacon-like flavor. You can also toss in tempeh bacon or coconut bacon to mimic the meaty texture.

Pairing Suggestions for Thanksgiving

This recipe pairs beautifully with other Thanksgiving staples. Serve it alongside creamy mashed potatoes, homemade cranberry sauce, or stuffing to round out your table. And, of course, it’s the perfect companion to turkey, whether roasted, fried, or smoked. The smoky bacon and earthy Brussels sprouts also balance out sweeter dishes, such as candied yams or honey-glazed carrots, making for a well-rounded feast.

Conclusion

Thanksgiving roasted Brussels sprouts with bacon is a dish that effortlessly combines simplicity and sophistication. With its vibrant flavors, crispy textures, and mouthwatering aroma, this side is bound to impress your guests and become a beloved addition to your holiday table. Whether you’re a longtime fan of Brussels sprouts or introducing them to skeptical taste buds, one bite of this savory delight will win over everyone. Make this recipe the highlight of your Thanksgiving dinner, and enjoy the magic of bringing people together over great food.
