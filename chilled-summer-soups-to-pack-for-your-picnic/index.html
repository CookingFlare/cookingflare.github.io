Chilled Summer Soups to Pack for Your Picnic


As the warm summer days approach, there’s no better way to cool off and enjoy a refreshing meal than with a chilled soup. Perfect for outdoor picnics, these soups provide a light, nutritious option that’s easy to pack, easy to serve, and incredibly satisfying. Whether you’re headed to the beach, a park, or a backyard gathering, these chilled summer soups will become your go-to picnic companions.

In this blog post, we will explore some of the best chilled soups to bring along on your next picnic. From vibrant gazpachos to creamy chilled avocado soups, these recipes will not only impress your guests but also keep everyone cool and refreshed under the summer sun.

Why Choose Chilled Soups for Your Picnic?

Chilled soups are the ideal picnic dish for a variety of reasons:

  1. Cool and Refreshing: On hot days, chilled soups provide a cooling effect, making them a perfect choice for outdoor meals. Their refreshing nature helps you stay hydrated and energized.

  2. Easy to Make and Pack: Many chilled soups can be made in advance and stored in mason jars or portable containers, making them simple to transport.

  3. Healthy and Light: These soups are often packed with fresh vegetables, fruits, and herbs, making them a nutritious choice. They are also light on the stomach, so you won’t feel weighed down during your picnic activities.

  4. Versatile and Flavorful: The world of chilled soups is incredibly diverse. Whether you enjoy vibrant, tangy flavors or creamy, soothing textures, there’s a chilled soup for everyone.

Classic Spanish Gazpacho

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups tomato juice
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, onion, and garlic. Pulse until smooth.
  2. Add the tomato juice, olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper. Blend again until fully combined.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  4. Chill the soup in the refrigerator for at least two hours before serving.
  5. Serve in bowls or mason jars, and garnish with fresh basil leaves before enjoying.

This Spanish gazpacho is a quintessential summer soup, offering a perfect balance of refreshing flavors and textures. The combination of tomatoes, cucumbers, and bell peppers provides a vibrant and light base, while the garlic and vinegar give it an extra layer of complexity.

Creamy Avocado and Cucumber Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup cold vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil, for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the avocados, cucumber, Greek yogurt, vegetable broth, lime juice, and fresh dill. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Chill the soup in the refrigerator for at least one hour.
  4. Before serving, drizzle with a little olive oil and garnish with additional fresh dill.

This creamy avocado and cucumber soup is light yet rich, with a smooth texture and a refreshing flavor profile. The creaminess of the avocado pairs wonderfully with the coolness of the cucumber, making it a great choice for a summer picnic.

Chilled Sweet Corn Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh corn kernels (about 4 ears of corn)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté for about 5 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the corn kernels and vegetable broth, and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool slightly. Once cooled, blend the soup in batches until smooth.
  4. Stir in the coconut milk, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  5. Chill the soup in the refrigerator for at least two hours.
  6. Before serving, garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

Sweet corn brings a natural sweetness to this chilled soup, complemented by the rich coconut milk and a touch of lime. It’s the ideal summer soup for those who enjoy creamy yet light dishes with a hint of sweetness.

Chilled Tomato and Watermelon Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cups watermelon, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 small red onion, chopped
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the tomatoes, watermelon, cucumber, and red onion. Blend until smooth.
  2. Add the balsamic vinegar, fresh mint, salt, and pepper, and blend again until fully incorporated.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  4. Chill the soup for at least one hour before serving.

This chilled soup brings together two summer favorites: juicy tomatoes and sweet watermelon. The watermelon adds a refreshing touch to the soup, while the mint and balsamic vinegar provide a subtle tang and complexity.

Cantaloupe and Basil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cantaloupe, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the cantaloupe, basil leaves, Greek yogurt, honey, and lime juice. Blend until smooth.
  2. Taste the soup and add salt as needed.
  3. Chill the soup in the refrigerator for at least one hour before serving.

The sweet and slightly floral flavor of cantaloupe is elevated by the fresh basil and tangy yogurt in this chilled soup. It’s a delightful and unexpected option that’s sure to be a hit at any summer picnic.

How to Pack Your Chilled Soups for a Picnic

When preparing for a picnic, it’s important to pack your chilled soups properly to ensure they stay fresh and cool. Here are some tips for packing your chilled soups:

1. Use Insulated Containers:

Invest in high-quality, insulated food containers that will keep your soups cool for hours. Mason jars, thermos containers, or portable coolers work well for transporting soups without risking spills or warm temperatures.

2. Portion Control:

If you’re serving a group, consider pre-portioning your soups into individual containers. This not only makes serving easier but also reduces the chances of contamination or mess.

3. Keep Soups Cold:

Pack your soups with ice packs or freeze the containers the night before. This will help maintain the soup’s temperature and keep it refreshing throughout your picnic.

4. Bring Extra Garnishes:

Pack any garnishes separately, such as fresh herbs or croutons, to keep them fresh. Add them just before serving for the best presentation and flavor.

5. Label Your Containers:

If you’re bringing more than one type of soup, label your containers to avoid any confusion. This can be as simple as using masking tape and a marker.

Final Thoughts

Chilled soups are the perfect picnic companion for hot summer days, offering a refreshing and light meal that’s easy to prepare and transport. From the classic Spanish gazpacho to the creamy avocado and cucumber soup, there’s a chilled soup for every taste and preference. These soups not only keep you cool but also add vibrant, refreshing flavors to your picnic spread.

So the next time you’re planning a summer outing, skip the usual heavy sandwiches and opt for a chilled soup instead. Your friends and family will appreciate the light and healthy option, and you’ll enjoy the easy preparation and mess-free serving. Let these chilled summer soups become your go-to picnic dishes for every sunny day!
