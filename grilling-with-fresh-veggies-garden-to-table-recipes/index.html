Grilling with Fresh Veggies: Garden-to-Table Recipes


Grilling is not just for meat lovers anymore. With an abundance of fresh vegetables available during the summer months, it’s time to take your grilling game to a whole new level. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious garden-to-table recipes that will make your taste buds dance and your friends and family ask for seconds.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Corn on the cob is a classic summer treat, and grilling it adds a smoky flavor that takes it to a whole new level. Start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the husks and silk from the corn, and brush them lightly with olive oil. Place the corn directly on the grill grates and cook for about 10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are slightly charred.

While the corn is grilling, prepare the herb butter. In a small bowl, mix together softened butter, minced garlic, chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, thyme, and basil), salt, and pepper. Once the corn is done, remove it from the grill and generously spread the herb butter over each cob. Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of flavors in every bite.

Grilled Vegetable Skewers

Skewers are a fun and versatile way to grill a variety of fresh vegetables. Start by soaking wooden skewers in water for about 30 minutes to prevent them from burning. Meanwhile, prepare your favorite vegetables for grilling. Some great options include bell peppers, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, red onions, and mushrooms. Cut the vegetables into bite-sized pieces and thread them onto the soaked skewers.

Preheat your grill to medium heat. Brush the vegetable skewers with olive oil and season them with salt, pepper, and any other desired herbs or spices. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred. Serve the grilled vegetable skewers as a side dish or as a colorful addition to your favorite salad.

Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burger

Who says burgers have to be made with meat? Grilled portobello mushrooms are a delicious and satisfying alternative for vegetarians and meat lovers alike. Start by removing the stems from the portobello mushrooms and brushing them with olive oil. Season them with salt, pepper, and any desired herbs or spices. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat and place the mushrooms directly on the grill grates, cap-side down.

Grill the mushrooms for about 4-5 minutes on each side until they are tender and have grill marks. While the mushrooms are grilling, prepare your burger toppings. Toast some burger buns, and spread them with your favorite condiments, such as mayonnaise, mustard, or barbecue sauce. Add lettuce, tomato slices, pickles, and any other toppings you desire.

Once the mushrooms are done, place them on the toasted buns and top them with your favorite toppings. Serve the grilled portobello mushroom burgers with a side of sweet potato fries or a fresh garden salad for a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Grilled Veggie Pizza

Take your pizza game to the grill with this delicious and creative recipe. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Roll out your favorite pizza dough into individual-sized crusts or one large crust. Brush the crusts lightly with olive oil and place them directly on the grill grates. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until they are lightly charred and crispy.

Remove the crusts from the grill and start assembling your pizzas. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce or pesto on each crust, and top them with your favorite grilled vegetables, such as bell peppers, eggplant, zucchini, and red onions. Add some fresh mozzarella cheese and sprinkle with salt, pepper, and dried herbs, such as oregano or basil.

Place the pizzas back on the grill and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove the pizzas from the grill and let them cool slightly before slicing and serving. This grilled veggie pizza is a perfect way to showcase the flavors of summer and impress your guests at your next backyard gathering.

Conclusion

Grilling fresh veggies brings out their natural flavors and adds a delicious smoky touch to your meals. From corn on the cob to vegetable skewers, grilled portobello mushroom burgers to veggie pizzas, the possibilities are endless. Experiment with different vegetables, herbs, and spices to create your own garden-to-table masterpieces. So fire up your grill, embrace the summer vibes, and enjoy the vibrant and healthy goodness of grilled veggies. Happy grilling!
