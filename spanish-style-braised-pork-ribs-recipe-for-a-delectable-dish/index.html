Spanish Style Braised Pork Ribs: Recipe for a Delectable Dish


If you’re a fan of Spanish cuisine and enjoy the rich, robust flavors of slow-cooked meats, then you’re in for a treat with this Spanish Style Braised Pork Ribs recipe. This dish is a celebration of the bold and aromatic flavors that are characteristic of Spanish cooking, and it’s sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection.

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds of pork ribs
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 cup of dry red wine
  • 2 cups of beef or chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat and Sear: Preheat your oven to 350°F. In a large Dutch oven or oven-safe pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Season the pork ribs with salt and pepper, then sear them in the hot oil until they are browned on all sides. This step is crucial for developing deep flavors in the dish.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: Once the ribs are nicely browned, remove them from the pot and set them aside. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and sliced red bell pepper. Sauté them until they are softened and aromatic.

  3. Add Spices: Sprinkle the smoked paprika, ground cumin, dried oregano, and cayenne pepper over the sautéed vegetables. Stir well to coat the vegetables and toast the spices, releasing their flavors.

  4. Deglaze and Simmer: Pour in the dry red wine to deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Let the wine simmer for a few minutes until it reduces slightly. Then, return the seared pork ribs to the pot, along with any juices that may have accumulated.

  5. Braise in Broth: Pour in the beef or chicken broth, ensuring that the ribs are mostly submerged in the liquid. Bring the pot to a gentle simmer, then cover it with a lid and transfer it to the preheated oven. Let the ribs braise in the oven for 2 to 2 1/2 hours, or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once the pork ribs are beautifully tender, remove the pot from the oven. Carefully transfer the ribs to a serving platter. Spoon the flavorful cooking liquid and vegetables over the ribs, and garnish with fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

Tips for Serving:

  • This Spanish Style Braised Pork Ribs dish pairs wonderfully with creamy mashed potatoes, crusty bread, or a simple green salad.
  • The leftover cooking liquid can be strained and reduced to make a delicious sauce to drizzle over the ribs before serving.

Final Thoughts:

The beauty of this Spanish Style Braised Pork Ribs recipe lies in its simplicity and depth of flavor. The slow braising process allows the meat to become incredibly tender and infused with the aromatic spices and vegetables. Whether you’re hosting a special dinner or simply craving a comforting, hearty meal, this dish is a perfect choice. So, gather your ingredients, channel the spirit of Spanish cuisine, and indulge in the delightful flavors of this delectable dish.
