Christmas Cookies and Bars That Are Easy to Make


The holiday season brings out the best in everyone’s baking skills—or at least the desire to get cozy in the kitchen with flour on your hands and the smell of spices wafting through the air. When it comes to festive treats, Christmas cookies and bars hold a special place on every holiday table. They’re perfect for gift-giving, sharing with friends and family, and satisfying sweet cravings after a long day of holiday hustle.

If you’re looking for delicious recipes that don’t require hours of preparation or fiddly decorating, you’ve come to the right place. These Christmas cookies and bars are easy to make, bursting with festive flavor, and guaranteed to impress both kids and grown-ups alike.

Classic Snickerdoodle Cookies

Snickerdoodles are iconic around the holidays with their cinnamon-sugar coating giving a cozy warmth to each bite. What makes these cookies ideal is the simplicity—only a handful of pantry staples needed and a quick dough come-together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp sugar (for rolling)
  • 3 tsp ground cinnamon (for rolling)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, cream together the butter and 1 ½ cups sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in eggs one at a time.
  3. Whisk together flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add to the butter mixture until combined.
  4. Mix the 3 tbsp sugar and cinnamon in a separate small bowl.
  5. Roll dough into 1-inch balls, then roll each ball in the cinnamon-sugar mixture.
  6. Place on baking sheets and bake for 8–10 minutes until edges are set but centers are soft.
  7. Let cool on sheet for 2 minutes before transferring to wire racks.

These cookies will have cracking tops and a soft, chewy middle—a texture that’s impossible to resist.

Peppermint Chocolate Chip Bars

For those who love the combination of mint and chocolate during the holidays, these bars bring both in an easy, slice-and-serve format perfect for parties or gift boxes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 ½ cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup crushed peppermint candies

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease or line a 9×13-inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix melted butter and brown sugar until smooth. Beat in eggs and vanilla extract.
  3. Combine flour, baking soda, and salt in another bowl, then fold into the wet mixture.
  4. Stir in chocolate chips gently, reserving a handful for topping.
  5. Spread batter evenly in the pan, sprinkle crushed peppermint candies and reserved chocolate chips on top.
  6. Bake 25–28 minutes, or until the top is set and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out mostly clean.
  7. Cool completely in the pan before slicing into squares.

The crushed peppermint adds crunch and holiday cheer while the chocolate chips melt into luscious pockets of sweetness.

Soft Gingerbread Cookies With Simple Icing

Gingerbread cookies are a Christmas staple, but sometimes rolling and cutting out shapes can be daunting. This recipe simplifies the process while retaining that nostalgic ginger-spice charm.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Simple Icing:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1-2 tbsp milk
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, beat butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Add egg and molasses, mixing well.
  2. Sift together flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. Gradually blend dry ingredients into butter mixture.
  3. Cover dough and chill for at least 1 hour.
  4. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll dough into 1-inch balls and place on parchment-lined baking sheets, 2 inches apart. Flatten balls slightly.
  5. Bake 8–10 minutes until edges are firm. Cool completely.
  6. For icing, mix powdered sugar, vanilla, and milk until smooth and just pipeable. Decorate cooled cookies as desired.

The dough produces soft, chewy gingerbread with just enough spice to fill your kitchen with holiday magic.

Easy No-Bake Peppermint Cheesecake Bars

Sometimes an easy Christmas treat doesn’t have to be baked at all. These creamy peppermint cheesecake bars are rich, refreshing, and ready in under 30 minutes plus chilling time.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups crushed chocolate graham crackers
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup crushed peppermint candies

Instructions:

  1. Combine crushed graham crackers and melted butter. Press firmly into the bottom of a 9×13 inch pan to form crust. Refrigerate while preparing filling.
  2. Beat cream cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Whip heavy cream to stiff peaks, then fold gently into cream cheese mixture.
  4. Fold in 3/4 cup crushed peppermints (reserve the rest). Spread evenly over crust, then sprinkle with remaining peppermint candies.
  5. Chill at least 3 hours or overnight until set. Cut into bars.

These bars present a perfect blend of creamy texture and festive mint flavor with a chocolaty crunch base that everyone will ask for seconds of.

Lemon Cranberry Sugar Cookie Bars

This cookie bar variation is a fruity twist on traditional Christmas cookies with bright citrus notes and tart cranberries adding balance and color.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup dried cranberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C), grease or line a 9x13-inch pan with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter and sugar together until light and fluffy. Beat in eggs one at a time, then vanilla and lemon zest.
  3. Whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt, then incorporate into butter mixture.
  4. Fold in dried cranberries. Spread batter evenly in prepared pan.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden and a toothpick comes out clean. Cool before slicing.

Cranberries provide a pop of holiday red and tartness, perfectly offset by bright lemon and sweet cookie dough.

Final Tips for Stress-Free Holiday Baking

To keep things enjoyable and not overwhelming while baking these festive treats, here are a few pointers:

  • Prep ingredients and equipment ahead: Measure out flour, spices, and other ingredients before you start mixing. This streamlines the process and avoids last-minute scrambles.
  • Use parchment paper or silicone mats: They make clean-up easier and prevent sticky cookie disasters.
  • Cool cookies completely before decorating or storing: This preserves texture and prevents decorations or icing from melting or sliding.
  • Gift in airtight containers or tins: This keeps treats fresh longer and makes transportation simple.
  • Enjoy the process: Playing holiday music, sipping a mug of hot cocoa, or baking with loved ones turns these recipes into treasured memories, not just chores.

The beauty of Christmas cookies and bars lies in their variety and ease. Whether you want a classic cinnamon-spiced cookie, a decadent chocolate-mint bar, or a no-bake cheesecake delight, there’s something here to suit every taste and skill level. So grab your apron, preheat the oven, and let the delicious smell of holiday baking take over your home. Happy baking and even happier tasting!
