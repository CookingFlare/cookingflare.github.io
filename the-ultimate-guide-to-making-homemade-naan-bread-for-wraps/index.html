The Ultimate Guide to Making Homemade Naan Bread for Wraps


If you’re a fan of Indian cuisine, you’ve probably enjoyed the delicious, pillowy texture of naan bread alongside your favorite curry. But have you ever considered using naan as a wrap for your favorite fillings? Homemade naan bread is the perfect vehicle for creating flavorful and unique wraps that will elevate your lunch or dinner to a whole new level. In this ultimate guide, we’ll take you through the process of making homemade naan bread from scratch and show you how to turn it into the perfect wrap for your favorite fillings.

Ingredients

For the Naan Bread:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

For the Filling:

  • Grilled chicken, paneer, or vegetables
  • Lettuce
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced onions
  • Greek yogurt or tzatziki sauce
  • Fresh herbs (mint, cilantro, or parsley)

Instructions

Making the Naan Bread:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and active dry yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. In another bowl, mix the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the frothy yeast mixture, plain yogurt, and melted butter.

  3. Mix the ingredients together until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into 8 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball.

  6. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-high heat.

  7. Roll out each ball of dough into an oval or teardrop shape, about 1/4 inch thick.

  8. Place the rolled-out dough onto the hot skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until it puffs up and develops golden brown spots.

  9. If desired, brush the cooked naan with melted butter and sprinkle with minced garlic and chopped cilantro.

Assembling the Naan Wraps:

  1. Once the naan bread is ready, it’s time to assemble the wraps. Place a generous portion of your desired filling in the center of each naan.

  2. Add lettuce, sliced tomatoes, and onions on top of the filling.

  3. Drizzle with Greek yogurt or tzatziki sauce and sprinkle with fresh herbs.

  4. Gently fold the sides of the naan over the filling to create a wrap.

  5. Serve your homemade naan wraps with your favorite chutney or dipping sauce.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a smoky flavor, you can cook the naan bread directly on a hot grill instead of a skillet.
  • Experiment with different fillings such as tandoori chicken, grilled vegetables, or even falafel for a fusion twist.
  • To keep your naan bread warm and soft, wrap them in a clean kitchen towel as they come off the skillet.

Conclusion

Making homemade naan bread for wraps is not only a fun and rewarding experience, but it also allows you to customize your wraps with your favorite flavors and ingredients. Whether you’re serving them for a casual lunch or a special dinner, these naan wraps are sure to impress your family and friends. So, roll up your sleeves, get ready to knead some dough, and enjoy the delicious results of your homemade naan bread wraps!
