Rice and Grain Breads: Homemade Bread Recipes Featuring Nutritious Grains


Bread is a staple in many households, but have you ever considered adding the goodness of rice and other nutritious grains to your homemade bread? Not only do these grains add a delightful texture and flavor, but they also amp up the nutritional value of your loaves. In this blog post, we’ll explore some exciting recipes for rice and grain breads that you can easily make at home. Let’s dive right in!

1. Brown Rice Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of brown rice flour
  • 1 cup of oat flour
  • 1 cup of buckwheat flour
  • 2 tablespoons of ground flaxseeds
  • 1 tablespoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 ½ cups of almond milk
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the brown rice flour, oat flour, buckwheat flour, ground flaxseeds, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the almond milk, olive oil, and maple syrup.
  4. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the greased loaf pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the bread to cool before slicing and serving.

2. Quinoa and Chia Seed Bread

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of quinoa flour
  • 1 cup of almond flour
  • ½ cup of chia seeds
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • ¼ cup of melted coconut oil
  • 3 tablespoons of honey
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup of unsweetened almond milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the quinoa flour, almond flour, chia seeds, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, honey, eggs, and almond milk.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until well incorporated.
  5. Pour the batter into the lined loaf pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for approximately 50-55 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Remove from the oven and let the bread cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

3. Millet and Sunflower Seed Bread

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of millet flour
  • 1 cup of tapioca flour
  • ½ cup of sunflower seeds
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 3 tablespoons of melted coconut oil
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 1 cup of warm water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the millet flour, tapioca flour, sunflower seeds, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, honey, and warm water.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until well combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the greased loaf pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for approximately 50-55 minutes or until the bread is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Remove from the oven and allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Conclusion

Experimenting with different grains in your homemade bread not only adds variety to your baking repertoire but also boosts the nutritional value of your loaves. These rice and grain bread recipes are a delicious way to incorporate nutritious grains into your daily diet. So, why not roll up your sleeves and give them a try? Your taste buds and your health will thank you!
