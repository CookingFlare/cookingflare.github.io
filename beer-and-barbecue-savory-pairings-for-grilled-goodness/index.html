Beer and Barbecue: Savory Pairings for Grilled Goodness


When it comes to the perfect summer gathering, few combinations can rival the harmony of beer and barbecue. The rich, smoky flavors of grilled meats and the crisp, refreshing taste of a cold beer are a match made in heaven. In this post, we’ll explore some of the most iconic pairings between beer and barbecue, and provide some tips for taking your grill game to the next level.

The Classic Combination: Lager and Ribs

When it comes to pairing beer with barbecue, few combinations are more iconic than lager and ribs. The crisp, clean taste of a lager cuts through the richness of the ribs, while the beer’s subtle hop flavor complements the smoky, savory flavors of the meat. For a classic take on this pairing, try pairing a cold pint of Pilsner Urquell with some slow-cooked pork ribs slathered in a tangy BBQ sauce.

A Hopsy Twist: IPA and Tri-Tip

For a bolder take on the classic pairing, try pairing an IPA with some grilled tri-tip. The hoppy, bitter flavors of the IPA cut through the richness of the meat, while the beer’s citrusy notes complement the charred, smoky flavors of the grill. For a particularly bold take on this pairing, try pairing a hoppy West Coast-style IPA with some tri-tip seasoned with a spicy chili powder blend.

A Fruitful Combination: Wheat Beer and Chicken

For a lighter take on the classic pairing, try pairing a wheat beer with some grilled chicken. The crisp, refreshing taste of the wheat beer pairs perfectly with the delicate flavors of the chicken, while the beer’s citrusy notes complement the smoky, savory flavors of the grill. For a particularly refreshing take on this pairing, try pairing a cold pint of Hoegaarden with some grilled chicken breasts seasoned with a zesty lemon-herb marinade.

A Smoky Showstopper: Stout and Brisket

For a truly decadent take on the classic pairing, try pairing a stout with some slow-cooked brisket. The rich, roasted flavors of the stout complement the bold, beefy flavors of the brisket, while the beer’s smoky notes enhance the charred, savory flavors of the grill. For a particularly indulgent take on this pairing, try pairing a cold pint of Guinness with some slow-cooked brisket slathered in a rich, creamy BBQ sauce.

Tips for Taking Your Grill Game to the Next Level

While the perfect pairing between beer and barbecue is a matter of personal taste, there are a few tips you can follow to take your grill game to the next level:

  • Experiment with different beer styles: While lagers and IPAs are classic pairings for barbecue, don’t be afraid to experiment with other beer styles, such as stouts, porters, and wheat beers.
  • Pay attention to the flavor profiles: When pairing beer with barbecue, pay attention to the flavor profiles of both the beer and the meat. Look for complementary flavors, such as the smoky notes in a stout pairing with a charred, savory brisket.
  • Don’t be afraid to get creative: While classic pairings are always a good bet, don’t be afraid to get creative and try new combinations. You might be surprised at how well a citrusy IPA pairs with some grilled shrimp or a hoppy pale ale complements the bold flavors of a grilled pork chop.

In conclusion, the perfect pairing between beer and barbecue is a matter of personal taste, but with a few simple tips and some creative experimentation, you can take your grill game to the next level and enjoy the perfect summer gathering. So grab a cold beer, fire up the grill, and get ready to savor the savory flavors of the great outdoors!
