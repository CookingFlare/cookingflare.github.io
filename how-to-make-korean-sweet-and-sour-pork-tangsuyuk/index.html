How to Make Korean Sweet and Sour Pork (Tangsuyuk)


Korean cuisine offers a wide variety of flavorful dishes, from spicy stews to savory pancakes, but one dish that stands out for its combination of flavors is Tangsuyuk, or Korean Sweet and Sour Pork. This delicious dish features tender, crispy pork coated in a vibrant, tangy sauce that’s both sweet and sour, making it a favorite among Korean food lovers worldwide.

Tangsuyuk is typically enjoyed in Korean homes and restaurants, often served as part of a larger meal or during festive occasions. It’s a perfect balance of crispy meat and refreshing sauce, and when made right, it offers a satisfying bite every time. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe will guide you step by step in making authentic Korean Sweet and Sour Pork right in your kitchen.

Ingredients You Will Need for Korean Sweet and Sour Pork (Tangsuyuk)

Before diving into the preparation, let’s first look at the ingredients you’ll need to make the perfect Tangsuyuk:

For the Pork

  • 500 grams of pork tenderloin (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1/4 cup of potato starch (or cornstarch)
  • 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon of soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • Oil for frying (vegetable or canola oil works best)

For the Sweet and Sour Sauce

  • 1/4 cup of white vinegar (or apple cider vinegar for a milder taste)
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/4 cup of ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 1 tablespoon of potato starch (for thickening)
  • 1/4 cup of pineapple chunks (optional but recommended for an extra fruity twist)
  • 1/4 cup of bell pepper (red or green, julienned)
  • 1/4 cup of onion (sliced thinly)
  • 1/4 cup of carrot (sliced thinly)
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)

Preparing the Pork for Tangsuyuk

  1. Cut and Marinate the Pork: Start by cutting the pork tenderloin into small, bite-sized pieces. Each piece should be about 2-3 cm in size, perfect for dipping and frying. Once cut, place the pork in a mixing bowl. Add the soy sauce, garlic powder, black pepper, and salt. Mix the pork well, ensuring all the pieces are coated with the seasoning. Let the pork marinate for about 10-15 minutes. This step helps to infuse the meat with flavor, ensuring a tasty and tender bite.

  2. Batter the Pork: In a separate bowl, whisk the egg. In another shallow dish, combine the potato starch and all-purpose flour. Dip each piece of marinated pork into the egg wash, followed by coating it thoroughly in the flour and starch mixture. The batter should be thick enough to create a crispy exterior when fried. Repeat this process until all the pieces of pork are coated.

  3. Fry the Pork: Heat up a large frying pan or deep fryer with oil to about 170°C (340°F). The oil should be enough to submerge the pork pieces. Fry the pork in batches to avoid overcrowding, which could cause the pieces to stick together. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-5 minutes per batch, depending on the size of the pork pieces. Once done, remove the pork from the oil and set it aside on a paper towel to drain any excess oil.

Making the Sweet and Sour Sauce

While the pork is frying, you can begin preparing the sweet and sour sauce. The key to a perfect tangy sauce is balancing the sweetness and acidity, so pay attention to the proportions.

  1. Mix the Sauce Ingredients: In a small saucepan, combine the white vinegar, sugar, ketchup, soy sauce, and water. Stir these ingredients together over medium heat until the sugar has completely dissolved and the sauce is well combined. This is your base for the sweet and sour sauce.

  2. Thicken the Sauce: In a separate small bowl, mix the potato starch with a tablespoon of water to create a slurry. Slowly add this slurry to the sauce mixture while stirring constantly. This will help the sauce thicken to a nice, glossy consistency. Let it simmer for a few more minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Add the Vegetables and Fruit: Once the sauce has thickened, add the julienned bell pepper, onion, carrot, and pineapple chunks (if using). These vegetables not only add a pop of color but also contribute texture and flavor to the sauce. Let the sauce simmer for another 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.

  4. Finish the Sauce: To finish off the sauce, add the sesame oil for a deep, aromatic flavor. Stir everything together, and your sweet and sour sauce is now ready to be poured over the fried pork.

Assembling the Tangsuyuk

Now that both the fried pork and sweet and sour sauce are prepared, it’s time to bring them together for the final touch.

  1. Toss the Pork in the Sauce: Place the crispy fried pork pieces into a large mixing bowl or serving platter. Pour the sweet and sour sauce, along with the vegetables and pineapple, over the pork. Gently toss the pork in the sauce until every piece is evenly coated. Be careful not to break the crispy batter, but ensure that the sauce clings to each piece of pork.

  2. Garnish and Serve: To add an extra touch of flavor and presentation, sprinkle some sesame seeds over the top of the Tangsuyuk. The nutty sesame seeds will complement the tangy sauce perfectly. Serve the dish immediately, while the pork is still crispy, with a side of steamed rice or your favorite Korean side dishes (banchan).

Tips for Perfect Korean Sweet and Sour Pork (Tangsuyuk)

  • Crispiness: The key to a great Tangsuyuk is the crispy pork. Make sure your batter is thick enough to create a crunchy exterior, and avoid overcrowding the pan when frying to ensure each piece cooks evenly.

  • Adjust the Sauce: The sweet and sour sauce can be adjusted according to your taste. If you like it more tangy, increase the amount of vinegar. For a sweeter version, add more sugar. Play around with the balance to suit your preferences.

  • Vegetable Variations: While bell peppers, onions, and carrots are traditional, feel free to add other vegetables like zucchini, mushrooms, or even baby corn to the sauce for added texture and flavor.

  • Serving Suggestions: Tangsuyuk is often served with steamed rice or Korean fried rice (bokkeumbap). You can also serve it alongside kimchi and other banchan to create a well-rounded Korean meal.

Why You’ll Love Korean Sweet and Sour Pork (Tangsuyuk)

Tangsuyuk is a beloved dish for many reasons. It offers a perfect balance of flavors, combining the sweetness of the sugar, the acidity of the vinegar, and the savory richness of the fried pork. The crispy pork provides a delightful contrast to the smooth, tangy sauce, and the addition of colorful vegetables makes the dish both delicious and visually appealing.

Whether you’re making it for a special occasion, a dinner party, or just a family meal, Tangsuyuk is sure to be a hit. Its delightful crunch, tender pork, and flavorful sauce make it a standout dish that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

Conclusion

Making Korean Sweet and Sour Pork (Tangsuyuk) at home might seem challenging at first, but with the right ingredients and a little patience, you can create an impressive and delicious dish that rivals any Korean restaurant. The combination of crispy pork and the tangy, sweet sauce will undoubtedly win over the hearts (and stomachs) of anyone who tries it. So, gather your ingredients, follow this step-by-step guide, and enjoy the delightful flavors of Korean cuisine in the comfort of your own home.
