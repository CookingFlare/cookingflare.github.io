Savory Beef and Cheese Empanadas: A Comfort Snack


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the warm, flaky goodness of empanadas. These delightful pastries, filled with a savory mixture of beef and cheese, are not just a treat for the taste buds; they’re a nostalgic nod to culinary traditions that span cultures and generations. Whether you’re looking for a hearty snack, a party appetizer, or a satisfying meal, savory beef and cheese empanadas are your perfect companion.

What Are Empanadas?

Empanadas are a type of pastry that originated in Spain and have since made their way into various cuisines across Latin America, the Philippines, and beyond. The name “empanada” comes from the Spanish verb “empanar,” meaning “to wrap” or “to coat with bread.” Traditionally, an empanada consists of a dough shell filled with a variety of ingredients, which can be sweet or savory. The dough is typically baked or fried until golden brown, creating a deliciously crisp outer layer that encases a flavorful filling.

The Comfort of Savory Beef and Cheese Empanadas

Savory beef and cheese empanadas embody the essence of comfort food. The combination of seasoned ground beef, melted cheese, and aromatic spices wrapped in a flaky crust is nothing short of heavenly. They can easily be prepared in large batches, making them ideal for gatherings, family dinners, or simply as a satisfying snack throughout the week.

Ingredients for Savory Beef and Cheese Empanadas

For the Dough

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup cold water

For the Filling

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a blend)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro or parsley (optional)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Savory Beef and Cheese Empanadas

Step 1: Preparing the Dough

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to blend until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Incorporate the Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, vinegar, and cold water. Gradually add this mixture to the flour mixture, stirring until a dough forms.

  3. Knead and Chill: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead gently until smooth. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This step helps the dough relax and makes it easier to roll out.

Step 2: Preparing the Filling

  1. Cook the Beef: In a large skillet over medium heat, add the ground beef. Cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon as it cooks.

  2. Add Aromatics: Once the beef is browned, drain any excess fat and add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is soft and translucent.

  3. Season the Mixture: Stir in the cumin, paprika, chili powder (if using), salt, and pepper. Cook for a few more minutes, allowing the flavors to combine. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.

  4. Mix in the Cheese: Once the beef mixture has cooled, stir in the shredded cheese and chopped herbs (if using). This filling is now ready to be enveloped in the dough.

Step 3: Assembling the Empanadas

  1. Roll Out the Dough: On a floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to about 1/8 inch thick. Use a round cutter or the rim of a glass to cut out circles, approximately 4-6 inches in diameter.

  2. Fill the Empanadas: Place a spoonful of the beef and cheese filling in the center of each dough circle. Be careful not to overfill, as this can cause the empanadas to burst during cooking.

  3. Seal the Edges: Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges together firmly to seal. For an extra touch, you can crimp the edges with a fork or pinch them with your fingers.

  4. Prepare for Baking: Place the assembled empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the tops with the beaten egg to give them a beautiful golden color when baked.

Step 4: Cooking the Empanadas

Baking: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the empanadas for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.

Frying: If you prefer a crispier texture, heat oil in a deep skillet or fryer to 350°F (175°C). Fry the empanadas in batches, cooking them for about 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy. Drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Serving Suggestions

Savory beef and cheese empanadas can be enjoyed on their own or paired with a variety of dipping sauces. Here are a few ideas:

  • Salsa: A fresh tomato salsa adds a refreshing contrast to the rich filling.
  • Guacamole: Creamy guacamole balances the savory flavors perfectly.
  • Crema: A drizzle of sour cream or Mexican crema adds a delightful tang.
  • Hot Sauce: For those who enjoy a kick, a splash of hot sauce can elevate the experience.

Storing and Reheating Empanadas

If you have leftovers (which is rare, given how delicious they are), empanadas can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, simply pop them in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10-15 minutes, or until warmed through. For frozen empanadas, bake directly from the freezer, increasing the baking time by a few minutes.

The Joy of Making Empanadas

Making savory beef and cheese empanadas at home is not just about the food; it’s a way to connect with family and friends. Gather your loved ones in the kitchen and turn the preparation into a fun activity. Each person can shape their own empanadas, allowing for creativity and personalization.

Conclusion

Savory beef and cheese empanadas are a comfort snack that transcends borders and brings joy to any table. Their flaky crust, delicious filling, and versatility make them a favorite for gatherings, casual lunches, or late-night cravings. With this recipe, you can easily whip up a batch of these delightful pastries right in your own kitchen. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in a comforting snack that is sure to warm your heart and satisfy your appetite. Happy cooking!
