Authentic Mexican Picadillo Tacos: A Versatile Meat Filling


Tacos are a beloved staple in Mexican cuisine, and one of the most versatile and delicious fillings you can use is picadillo. Originating from Mexico, picadillo is a flavorful combination of ground meat, vegetables, and spices that can be used in various dishes, but today we’re going to focus on how to make authentic Mexican picadillo tacos. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or hosting a taco night with friends, these tacos are sure to be a hit!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef (or your choice of ground meat)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup sliced green olives
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Toppings of your choice: salsa, guacamole, chopped cilantro, diced onions, etc.

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become soft and translucent.

  2. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up into small crumbles with a wooden spoon or spatula.

  3. Once the meat is browned, add the diced tomato, potato, and carrot. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.

  4. Stir in the raisins, sliced green olives, ground cumin, dried oregano, and ground cinnamon. These ingredients will add a unique flavor and aroma to the picadillo.

  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the picadillo simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the flavors have melded together. Make sure to stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  6. While the picadillo is simmering, warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet or directly over a gas flame until they become soft and pliable. Keep them warm in a clean kitchen towel or tortilla warmer.

  7. Once the picadillo is ready, season with salt and pepper according to your taste preferences. Remove from heat and allow it to cool slightly before assembling the tacos.

  8. To assemble the tacos, spoon a generous amount of picadillo onto each warm tortilla. Top with your favorite toppings such as salsa, guacamole, chopped cilantro, and diced onions.

  9. Serve the picadillo tacos immediately and enjoy the explosion of flavors with every bite!

Conclusion

Authentic Mexican picadillo tacos are a fantastic way to enjoy the bold and vibrant flavors of Mexican cuisine. The combination of the savory ground meat, aromatic spices, and the sweetness of raisins creates a truly delightful filling that pairs perfectly with the soft and warm corn tortillas. These tacos are not only delicious but also versatile, as you can customize them with your favorite toppings and accompaniments. So, the next time you’re craving a taste of Mexico, whip up a batch of these mouthwatering picadillo tacos and transport yourself to the streets of Mexico City. Buen provecho!
