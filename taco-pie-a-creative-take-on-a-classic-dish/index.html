Taco Pie: A Creative Take on a Classic Dish


Taco lovers, rejoice! Today, we’re going to take a delicious detour from the traditional taco and introduce you to a mouthwatering creation: Taco Pie. This creative twist on a classic dish combines all the flavors and textures you love in a taco, but in a convenient and visually appealing pie form. Get ready to wow your taste buds and impress your friends and family with this unique recipe!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup canned diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

For the toppings:

  • Shredded lettuce
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Sliced avocado
  • Sour cream
  • Salsa

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt for the crust. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, mixing until the dough comes together. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

  3. While the dough chills, prepare the filling. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat until cooked through. Add the diced onion, bell pepper, and minced garlic, and cook until the vegetables are tender. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add the diced tomatoes, black beans, and frozen corn, and simmer for 10 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the fresh cilantro.

  4. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Transfer the dough to the pie dish, pressing it gently into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges.

  5. Pour the prepared filling into the pie crust, spreading it evenly. Top with the shredded cheddar cheese.

  6. Bake the Taco Pie in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Remove the Taco Pie from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Slice and serve with your favorite taco toppings, such as shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, sliced avocado, sour cream, and salsa.

Tips and Variations

  • If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought pie crust instead of making your own.
  • Feel free to customize the filling to your liking. Add more vegetables like zucchini or mushrooms, or spice it up with some jalapeños for an extra kick.
  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the ground beef with plant-based meat or use a mixture of beans for protein.
  • Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Taco Pie is not only a creative twist on a classic dish, but it’s also a versatile and crowd-pleasing recipe. Whether you’re hosting a casual dinner party or simply craving a flavorful meal, this pie will undoubtedly satisfy your taco cravings in a whole new way. So, gather your ingredients, let your creativity flow, and get ready to indulge in a slice of Taco Pie perfection. Enjoy!
