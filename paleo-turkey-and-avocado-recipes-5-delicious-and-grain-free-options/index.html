Paleo Turkey and Avocado Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


Are you following a Paleo diet and looking for new and exciting recipes to try? Look no further! In this post, we’ll be sharing five delicious and grain-free turkey and avocado recipes that are perfect for a Paleo lifestyle. These recipes are not only tasty but also packed with nutrients, making them a great choice for anyone looking to eat healthier. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Avocado-Stuffed Turkey Burgers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1 ripe avocado, pitted and mashed
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lettuce leaves, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, mashed avocado, red onion, garlic, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  2. Divide the mixture into four equal portions and shape them into patties.
  3. Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium heat. Cook the turkey patties for about 5-6 minutes per side or until they reach an internal temperature of 165°F.
  4. Serve the turkey burgers on lettuce leaves and enjoy!

2. Turkey and Avocado Lettuce Wraps

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cooked turkey breast, shredded
  • 2 ripe avocados, pitted and diced
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Large lettuce leaves, for wrapping

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded turkey, diced avocados, tomatoes, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine all the flavors.
  2. Spoon the turkey and avocado mixture onto the lettuce leaves and wrap them up tightly.
  3. Serve the lettuce wraps as a light and refreshing Paleo-friendly meal.

3. Avocado-Topped Turkey Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, almond flour, parsley, red onion, garlic, egg, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake the meatballs for about 20-25 minutes or until they are cooked through and golden brown.
  5. Top each meatball with a slice of avocado and serve.

4. Turkey, Avocado, and Bacon Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cooked turkey breast, diced
  • 2 ripe avocados, pitted and diced
  • 6 slices cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the diced turkey, diced avocados, crumbled bacon, red onion, parsley, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to mix all the ingredients together.
  2. Serve the salad as a satisfying and flavorful Paleo meal.

5. Avocado and Turkey Lettuce Cups

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cooked turkey breast, sliced
  • 2 ripe avocados, pitted and sliced
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Large lettuce leaves, for cups

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced turkey, sliced avocados, tomatoes, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to combine all the flavors.
  2. Arrange the lettuce leaves on a platter and spoon the turkey and avocado mixture into each leaf, creating cups.
  3. Serve the lettuce cups as a light and satisfying meal.

These five delicious and grain-free Paleo turkey and avocado recipes are sure to satisfy your taste buds while keeping you on track with your healthy eating goals. Give them a try and let us know which one is your favorite! Happy cooking!
