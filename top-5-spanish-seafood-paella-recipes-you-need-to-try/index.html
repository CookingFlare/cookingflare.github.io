Top 5 Spanish Seafood Paella Recipes You Need to Try


When it comes to Spanish cuisine, few dishes embody its vibrant culture and rich flavors as much as paella. Originating from the sunny shores of Valencia, this iconic dish has evolved through the years, incorporating a variety of ingredients that reflect the region’s bountiful offerings. Among the many variations, seafood paella stands out for its fresh flavors and delectable combinations of the sea. In this blog post, we will explore five exceptional seafood paella recipes that you absolutely need to try. Each recipe brings a unique twist to this classic dish, ensuring there’s something for everyone to enjoy.

1. Classic Valencia Seafood Paella

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Bomba rice (or any short-grain rice)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 4 cups seafood stock (or fish stock)
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 1 pound squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large paella pan or a wide, shallow skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.

  2. Stir in the garlic, red bell pepper, and green bell pepper. Cook for an additional 5 minutes until the vegetables soften.

  3. Add the saffron threads and smoked paprika, stirring to combine. This will infuse the oil with rich flavors.

  4. Add the Bomba rice to the pan, stirring to coat the rice with the oil and vegetable mixture. Allow it to toast for 2-3 minutes.

  5. Pour in the seafood stock and bring to a gentle boil. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and add the shrimp, mussels, and squid. Arrange them evenly on top of the rice.

  7. Cover the pan with a lid or aluminum foil and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice has absorbed the liquid and is tender.

  8. Remove from heat and let the paella rest for 5 minutes. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and lemon wedges before serving.

This classic Valencia seafood paella is a crowd-pleaser and a true representation of traditional Spanish cooking.

2. Spicy Chorizo and Seafood Paella

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 chorizo sausage, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound clams, scrubbed
  • 1 pound squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large paella pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced chorizo and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes.

  2. Add the onion and cook until softened. Stir in the garlic, red pepper flakes, and smoked paprika, cooking for an additional minute.

  3. Add the Arborio rice and stir to coat in the oil and spices. Toast the rice for 2-3 minutes.

  4. Pour in the chicken stock and season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil.

  5. Reduce heat to low, add the shrimp, clams, and squid, arranging them evenly on top of the rice. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes.

  6. Once the rice is cooked and the seafood is tender, remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.

  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges for a zesty finish.

This spicy chorizo and seafood paella adds a delightful kick to the traditional recipe, perfect for those who enjoy bold flavors.

3. Mediterranean Herb-Infused Seafood Paella

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Calasparra rice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 4 cups fish stock
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound scallops
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil, for garnish
  • Lemon zest for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large paella pan over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until translucent.

  2. Stir in the garlic, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes, cooking until softened.

  3. Add the dried oregano and thyme, mixing well to combine.

  4. Add the Calasparra rice to the pan, stirring for a few minutes until the rice is slightly toasted.

  5. Pour in the fish stock and season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil.

  6. Lower the heat and add the shrimp and scallops on top of the rice. Cover and let simmer for 20-25 minutes.

  7. Once the seafood is cooked and the rice is tender, remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.

  8. Garnish with fresh basil and lemon zest for a refreshing finish.

This herb-infused seafood paella brings the flavors of the Mediterranean right to your table, making it a perfect dish for any occasion.

4. Coconut Curry Seafood Paella

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups jasmine rice
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 1 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large paella pan, heat coconut oil over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until softened.

  2. Stir in the garlic and curry powder, cooking for an additional minute until fragrant.

  3. Add the jasmine rice and stir to coat with the oil and spices, toasting for 2-3 minutes.

  4. Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth, seasoning with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil.

  5. Reduce heat to low and add the shrimp, mussels, and peas, arranging them evenly on top of the rice. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes.

  6. Once the rice is tender and the seafood is cooked, remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.

  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges for a tropical twist.

This coconut curry seafood paella offers a unique fusion of flavors, perfect for those looking to explore unconventional pairings in their cooking.

5. Lemon and Garlic Butter Seafood Paella

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice (like Bomba or Arborio)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound lobster, cooked and cut into chunks
  • 1 pound clams, scrubbed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large paella pan, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until translucent.

  2. Stir in the garlic, lemon zest, and lemon juice, cooking for an additional minute.

  3. Add the short-grain rice, stirring to coat with the butter and aromatics. Toast for 2-3 minutes.

  4. Pour in the seafood stock and season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil.

  5. Lower the heat and add the shrimp, lobster, and clams, arranging them evenly on top of the rice. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes.

  6. Once the rice is cooked and the seafood is tender, remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.

  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

This lemon and garlic butter seafood paella is a luxurious take on the classic, combining the richness of butter with the freshness of seafood.

Conclusion

Seafood paella is a dish that embodies the essence of Spanish cooking, showcasing the beautiful flavors of the sea and the vibrant ingredients that make each meal a celebration. Whether you prefer the classic Valencia style or are intrigued by a more modern twist, these five seafood paella recipes are sure to impress. Gather your ingredients, invite your friends and family, and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure that will transport you to the sun-kissed shores of Spain. Happy cooking!
