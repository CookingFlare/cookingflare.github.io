Simple Seafood Fra Diavolo Pasta Recipe


If you’re looking to spice up your weeknight dinner routine, this Simple Seafood Fra Diavolo Pasta recipe is the perfect dish to try. Combining the bold flavors of crushed red pepper and garlic with the sweetness of fresh seafood, this dish strikes the perfect balance between indulgent and easy to prepare. It’s an impressive meal that can go from stove to table in under an hour, making it ideal for dinner parties, date nights, or simply treating yourself to a lavish plate of pasta.

Fra Diavolo, which translates to “Brother Devil” in Italian, is named for its fiery flavor profile. This tomato-based seafood pasta dish is unapologetically bold with a kick of heat—but don’t let that intimidate you. The spice level can easily be tailored to suit your preference. Alongside the heat, you’ll also enjoy the bright acidity of tomatoes, the savory complexity of garlic, and the delicate sweetness of shrimp and scallops. Let’s dive into this satisfying recipe and bring a touch of coastal Italian flair to your dining table.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Although this dish boasts complex flavors, the ingredient list is surprisingly simple and easy to source. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pasta:

  • 12 oz spaghetti or linguine: Classic long noodles are a great choice for this recipe, but feel free to use your favorite pasta shape.
  • Salt: For cooking the pasta.

For the Seafood:

  • 1 lb large shrimp: Peeled and deveined.
  • 1/2 lb scallops: Pat them dry with a paper towel for better searing.
  • 2 tbsp olive oil: For sautéing the seafood.

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil: Another drizzle for building the sauce.
  • 4 garlic cloves: Thinly sliced for maximum flavor release.
  • 1 tsp crushed red pepper: Adjust according to your spice tolerance.
  • 1 cup dry white wine: Adds acidity and depth. Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc work well here.
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes: A good-quality canned tomato is essential for rich flavor.
  • 1/2 cup seafood stock: Enhances the overall seafood profile. You can substitute with chicken stock if needed.
  • 2 tsp dried oregano: Adds a touch of quintessential Italian herbiness.
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper: For seasoning the sauce.

Optional Garnishes:

  • Fresh parsley: Chopped for a pop of green and added freshness.
  • Grated Parmesan cheese: While not traditional, some like a touch of creaminess on their Fra Diavolo pasta.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Pasta

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until it’s al dente. Be sure to reserve about 1 cup of starchy pasta water before draining the noodles. This can be used later to adjust the consistency of the sauce. Set the cooked pasta aside while you prepare the seafood and sauce.

2. Sear the Seafood

Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is shimmering, add the shrimp and scallops in a single layer. Season them lightly with salt and pepper. Cook for about 1–2 minutes per side, just until they turn opaque and lightly golden. Avoid overcooking the seafood—it will finish cooking later when combined with the sauce. Remove the shrimp and scallops from the skillet and set them aside.

3. Build the Sauce

Using the same skillet, add another drizzle of olive oil and reduce the heat to medium. Toss in the sliced garlic and crushed red pepper, sautéing until fragrant, about 1 minute. Be careful not to brown the garlic, as this can make it taste bitter.

Pour in the white wine, scraping any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. Let the wine simmer for about 2–3 minutes to allow the alcohol to cook off. Next, add the crushed tomatoes and seafood stock, stirring to combine. Sprinkle in the dried oregano and season with salt and pepper, adjusting to taste.

Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for 15–20 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld beautifully while the sauce thickens slightly.

4. Combine the Seafood and Sauce

Return the seared shrimp and scallops to the skillet, stirring gently to coat them in the vibrant tomato sauce. Cook for an additional 2–3 minutes to ensure the seafood is fully cooked and infused with the spicy, savory flavors of the sauce.

5. Toss in the Pasta

Add the cooked pasta to the skillet, tossing thoroughly to coat the noodles with the sauce. If the mixture seems too thick, use some of the reserved pasta water to loosen it until you reach your desired consistency. Let the pasta cook with the sauce for about 1–2 minutes over medium heat to marry the flavors.

Serving Suggestions

Transfer the pasta and seafood mixture to a large serving dish or divide it into individual plates. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of color and freshness, and, if desired, a light sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese. Serve immediately alongside crusty Italian bread and a glass of dry white wine to complete the meal.

Pro Tips for Perfect Seafood Fra Diavolo

  • Adjust the Spice Level: If you’re sensitive to heat, start with just 1/2 tsp of crushed red pepper and add more gradually to taste. Conversely, if you love spice, you can add extra chili flakes or a touch of cayenne pepper.
  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh garlic, seafood, and herbs truly elevate this dish. When possible, opt for high-quality canned tomatoes for the best sauce flavor.
  • Prep Your Seafood: Pat the shrimp and scallops dry before cooking to ensure a nice sear. You can also remove the tails from the shrimp for easier eating or keep them on for presentation purposes.
  • Pair It Well: Serve this dish with a crisp green salad dressed simply with olive oil and lemon juice. A refreshing citrus note works wonders alongside the spicy tomato flavors.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Seafood Fra Diavolo isn’t just delicious; it’s a dish that feels luxurious yet attainable for home cooks of all skill levels. The combination of spicy, tangy, and savory flavors will make every bite a memorable experience. Plus, the recipe is highly versatile—swap in mussels, clams, or chunks of white fish for the shrimp and scallops to suit your preferences. This pasta is a testament to the fact that you don’t need complicated techniques or hard-to-find ingredients to create a restaurant-quality meal.

Bring the warmth and spice of Italy to your kitchen with this Simple Seafood Fra Diavolo Pasta recipe. Whether you’re cooking for family, impressing guests, or treating yourself to an elegant dinner, this dish is sure to become a regular in your meal rotation. Enjoy the process, savor the flavors, and celebrate the magic of bold, beautiful cooking!
