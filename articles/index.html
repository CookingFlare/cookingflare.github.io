Kids' Cooking Challenge: How to Make a Simple Stir-Fry

Cooking with kids can be a fun and educational experience. It allows them to explore their creativity, develop essential life skills, and encourages them to try new foods. In this cooking challenge, we will be making a simple and delicious stir-fry that kids can enjoy making and eating! Ingredients To make this simple stir-fry, you will need the following ingredients: 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 2 chicken breasts, sliced into thin strips 1 red bell pepper, sliced 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced 1 carrot, sliced 1 cup broccoli florets 1 cup snap peas 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon honey Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken breast strips to the skillet and cook until they are no longer pink in the center. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the sliced bell peppers, carrot, broccoli florets, and snap peas to the skillet. Cook for about 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce and honey until well combined. Pour the soy sauce mixture over the vegetables in the skillet and stir to coat evenly. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes to heat through. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat and let cool slightly before serving. Tips for Cooking with Kids Cooking with kids can sometimes be messy, but it’s all part of the fun!
