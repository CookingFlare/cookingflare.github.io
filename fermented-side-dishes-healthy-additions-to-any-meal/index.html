Fermented Side Dishes: Healthy Additions to Any Meal


Fermented foods have been a staple of many cultures for centuries, known for their tangy flavors and numerous health benefits. From kimchi to sauerkraut, these fermented side dishes not only add a burst of flavor to your meals but also provide a range of gut-friendly bacteria that can improve digestion and boost your immune system. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious and easy-to-make fermented side dishes that you can incorporate into your daily meals.

1. Tangy Kimchi Slaw

Ingredients:

  • 1 small head of cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sea salt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chili flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cabbage, carrots, and red bell pepper.
  2. Sprinkle sea salt over the vegetables and massage it in with your hands for about 5 minutes. This will help draw out the water from the vegetables.
  3. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, fish sauce (if using), and chili flakes (if using). Mix well to combine.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a clean, sterilized jar, pressing it down firmly to remove any air bubbles.
  5. Cover the jar with a clean cloth or paper towel and secure it with a rubber band.
  6. Allow the jar to sit at room temperature for 3 to 5 days, or until the desired level of tanginess is achieved.
  7. Once fermented, transfer the jar to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process. The kimchi slaw can be enjoyed for up to a month.

2. Zesty Fermented Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 small cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon dill seeds
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup filtered water

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cucumber slices and sea salt. Mix well and let it sit for about 30 minutes to draw out the moisture.
  2. Rinse the cucumbers under cold water and drain.
  3. In a clean jar, layer the cucumber slices, dill seeds, mustard seeds, and minced garlic.
  4. Pour the filtered water over the cucumbers, making sure they are fully submerged.
  5. Seal the jar with a lid and allow it to sit at room temperature for 2 to 3 days, or until the desired level of tanginess is achieved.
  6. Once fermented, transfer the jar to the refrigerator to prolong the shelf life. These zesty pickles can be enjoyed for up to 2 months.

3. Fermented Beet and Apple Slaw

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium beets, grated
  • 2 apples, grated
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the grated beets, grated apples, and sliced red onion.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the apple cider vinegar, honey, caraway seeds, and sea salt.
  3. Pour the dressing over the beet and apple mixture, tossing well to combine.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a clean, sterilized jar, pressing it down firmly to remove any air bubbles.
  5. Cover the jar with a clean cloth or paper towel and secure it with a rubber band.
  6. Allow the jar to sit at room temperature for 3 to 5 days, or until the desired level of tanginess is achieved.
  7. Once fermented, transfer the jar to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process. The beet and apple slaw can be enjoyed for up to 2 weeks.

Conclusion

Fermented side dishes are not only delicious but also offer a wide range of health benefits. Incorporating these tangy additions into your meals can improve digestion, boost your immune system, and add a burst of flavor to any dish. Whether you’re a fan of kimchi, pickles, or slaws, these fermented delicacies are a great way to enhance the nutritional value of your meals. So, why not give them a try and embark on a flavorful and healthy journey?
