Satisfying Quiche Provençale: A French Summer Sensation


There’s something undeniably charming about French cuisine, especially during the summer. The vibrant flavors, fresh ingredients, and delightful aromas come together to create a culinary experience like no other. One dish that perfectly captures the essence of a French summer is the Quiche Provençale. This savory, satisfying tart is a celebration of seasonal produce and traditional French cooking techniques. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of making Quiche Provençale from scratch, allowing you to bring a taste of Provence right into your own kitchen.

The Perfect Crust

Every great quiche starts with a perfect crust. For this Quiche Provençale, we’ll be making a classic shortcrust pastry. Begin by combining 250g of all-purpose flour, a pinch of salt, and 125g of cold, diced unsalted butter in a large mixing bowl. Use your fingertips to rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs. Next, add 1 egg yolk and 2-3 tablespoons of cold water, and gently knead the mixture until it comes together to form a smooth dough. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes.

After the dough has rested, roll it out on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Carefully press the dough into the pan, trimming any excess from the edges. Prick the base of the crust with a fork and blind bake it in a preheated oven at 180°C for 15 minutes, or until the crust is lightly golden. Remove it from the oven and set it aside to cool while you prepare the filling.

The Flavorful Filling

The filling of a Quiche Provençale is where the magic truly happens. This recipe calls for a delightful combination of fresh, summery ingredients that perfectly capture the essence of Provence. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together 4 eggs and 200ml of heavy cream until well combined. Season the mixture with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg for a subtle warmth.

Now, it’s time to add the star ingredients. Begin by sautéing 1 finely chopped onion and 2 minced garlic cloves in a pan with a drizzle of olive oil until they are soft and fragrant. Add 200g of diced tomatoes and cook until they have released their juices. Then, stir in 100g of pitted and sliced black olives, 100g of diced ham, and a handful of fresh basil leaves for an aromatic touch. Allow the mixture to cool slightly before spreading it evenly over the pre-baked crust.

Pour the egg and cream mixture over the filling, ensuring that it seeps into all the nooks and crannies. Top the quiche with a generous sprinkling of grated Gruyère cheese, adding a touch of indulgence to the dish.

Baking and Serving

Preheat your oven to 180°C. Carefully transfer the assembled quiche into the oven and bake it for 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown. Once baked, remove the quiche from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before slicing it into wedges.

The Quiche Provençale is best enjoyed warm, allowing the flavors to fully develop and the cheese to melt into luscious goodness. Serve it alongside a crisp, green salad dressed with a tangy vinaigrette for a complete and satisfying meal. This dish pairs beautifully with a glass of chilled rosé, adding a touch of elegance to your summer dining experience.

The Essence of Provence

In every bite of this Quiche Provençale, you’ll taste the vibrant flavors of the French countryside. The sweetness of the tomatoes, the brininess of the olives, and the richness of the cheese come together to create a truly satisfying dish that embodies the spirit of Provence. Whether you’re hosting a summer brunch or simply craving a taste of France, this quiche is sure to impress and delight.

So, embrace the essence of Provence in your own kitchen and let the flavors of a French summer transport you to a place of culinary bliss. Bon appétit!
