How to Make a Delicious Coconut Cream Pie


Coconut cream pie is a classic dessert that never fails to impress. Its silky custard filling, topped with whipped cream and toasted coconut, is a perfect combination of flavors and textures. Whether you’re preparing this pie for a holiday gathering, a birthday celebration, or just because you’re craving something sweet, this comprehensive recipe will guide you through every step to ensure your coconut cream pie turns out beautifully.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we dive into the process, let’s go over the ingredients required for this heavenly dessert. Having everything ready will make the preparation smooth and enjoyable.

For the Pie Crust:

  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • ½ cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 4–6 tablespoons ice-cold water

For the Coconut Custard Filling:

  • 2 ¾ cups whole milk
  • 1 cup sweetened shredded coconut
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup coconut milk (not cream)

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup cold heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup sweetened shredded coconut (toasted)

Instructions

Step 1: Make the Pie Crust

A flaky crust is the foundation of any great pie. Follow these steps to create a homemade pie crust that complements the creamy coconut custard filling:

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Incorporate the Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry ingredients. Using a pastry blender or your fingertips, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Add Cold Water: Gradually add 4–6 tablespoons of ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently with a fork after each addition. Stop when the dough comes together but isn’t sticky.
  4. Shape and Chill: Gather the dough into a ball, flatten it into a disc, and wrap it in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to let the butter chill completely.
  5. Roll Out the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Carefully transfer it to a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it into the edges. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges.
  6. Blind Bake: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and bake for an additional 8–10 minutes until golden brown. Allow the crust to cool fully before adding the filling.

Step 2: Prepare the Coconut Custard Filling

The filling is where all the magic happens. This creamy, coconut-infused custard is the heart of your pie, so take your time to get it just right.

  1. Heat the Milk and Coconut: In a medium saucepan, heat the milk and shredded coconut over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat and let the coconut steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the sugar, cornstarch, and salt until well combined.
  3. Temper the Eggs: In another bowl, whisk the egg yolks until smooth. Gradually add the warm milk mixture to the yolks while whisking constantly. This prevents the eggs from scrambling.
  4. Cook the Custard: Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened. This should take about 5–7 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract and coconut milk.
  5. Cool the Filling: Transfer the custard to a bowl and cover it with plastic wrap, pressing it against the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate until cool.

Step 3: Assemble the Pie

Once the custard has cooled, it’s time to bring all the elements together:

  1. Fill the Crust: Spoon the chilled coconut custard into the cooled pie crust, spreading it into an even layer.
  2. Chill the Pie: Refrigerate the assembled pie for at least 4 hours or overnight to ensure the filling sets properly.

Step 4: Make and Add the Whipped Cream Topping

A light, airy whipped cream topping balances the richness of the custard below.

  1. Whip the Cream: In a large mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream with an electric mixer until soft peaks form. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, then continue whipping until stiff peaks form.
  2. Spread or Pipe: Spread the whipped cream over the chilled pie using a spatula or pipe it for a decorative touch.

Step 5: Toast the Coconut

The final flourish is a sprinkle of toasted coconut. Here’s how to toast coconut perfectly:

  1. Toast in the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the shredded coconut on a baking sheet in an even layer.
  2. Bake: Toast the coconut for 5–7 minutes, stirring halfway, until golden brown. Keep a watchful eye to prevent burning.
  3. Garnish the Pie: Sprinkle the toasted coconut over the whipped cream topping for a lovely visual and flavorful finishing touch.

Tips for Success

  1. Use Fresh Ingredients: Ensure your shredded coconut is fresh for maximum flavor.
  2. Don’t Rush the Custard: Stir continuously while cooking to prevent lumps and achieve a smooth texture.
  3. Keep the Whipped Cream Cold: Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream to help it set up perfectly.
  4. Plan Ahead: Coconut cream pie requires cooling time for both the custard and the assembled pie, so make it the day before you plan to serve it.

Serving Coconut Cream Pie

When it’s finally time to serve your coconut cream pie, slice it carefully with a sharp knife to preserve its clean layers. Pair this elegant dessert with a cup of coffee or tea for the ultimate treat. Store any leftovers in the refrigerator, covered, for up to 3 days (though it’s unlikely this irresistible pie will last that long!).

A Sweet Conclusion

Making coconut cream pie from scratch may seem like a labor of love, but the effort is well worth it. The harmonious blend of buttery crust, creamy filling, and cloud-like whipped cream makes every bite an indulgent delight. Whether it’s your first time tackling this dessert or you’re a seasoned pro, this recipe will deliver a show-stopping pie that’s sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

Take your time, enjoy the process, and savor the rewards of this homemade masterpiece. Your family and friends will thank you—and likely ask for seconds!
