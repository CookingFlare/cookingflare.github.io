Fun Cookie Shapes to Try This Holiday Season


The holiday season is a time for joy, creativity, and, most importantly, delicious treats! What better way to celebrate than by baking fun and festive cookies? Not only do they make fantastic gifts, but they also bring warmth and delight to any gathering. This year, why not step out of the traditional cookie-cutting box and explore some exciting shapes that will wow your friends and family? In this blog post, we’ll dive into a variety of cookie shapes to try this holiday season, along with tips and unique recipes to make your baking experience as enjoyable as it is tasty.

The Magic of Cookie Shapes

Baking cookies is more than just a culinary task; it’s a form of art. The shape of your cookies can set the tone for the occasion, whether it’s cheerful snowmen, whimsical stars, or elegant snowflakes. Using different cookie shapes can elevate your cookie game and give you the chance to experiment with flavors, colors, and decorations. Here are some fun cookie shapes to inspire your holiday baking!

1. Classic Holiday Shapes

Snowflakes

Snowflakes are a quintessential symbol of winter and make for beautiful cookie shapes. To create snowflake cookies, use a snowflake cookie cutter. After baking, you can decorate them with royal icing and sprinkle edible glitter for a sparkling effect.

Recipe: Basic Sugar Cookie Dough for Snowflakes

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
    • 1 1/2 cups white sugar
    • 1 egg
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 3/4 teaspoon almond extract
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. In a bowl, mix flour, baking soda, and baking powder.
    3. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until smooth. Beat in the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract.
    4. Gradually blend the dry ingredients into the creamed mixture.
    5. Roll rounded teaspoonfuls of dough into balls, then place them on ungreased cookie sheets. Flatten each ball slightly and press a snowflake cutter into the dough.
    6. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes in the preheated oven until golden. Cool on wire racks before decorating.

Christmas Trees

Christmas tree cookies are a festive favorite and can be customized with colorful icing and sprinkles. Use a tree-shaped cookie cutter and get creative with different shades of green icing to mimic the look of a decorated tree.

Decorating Tip: Use mini chocolate chips as ornaments and a star-shaped candy for the top of the tree.

2. Whimsical Shapes

Gingerbread People

Gingerbread cookies are a classic holiday treat, but why not take it a step further with gingerbread people? Use a gingerbread cookie cutter and decorate them with icing, candies, and other fun edible decorations.

Recipe: Gingerbread Cookie Dough

  • Ingredients:

    • 3 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
    • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
    • 1/2 cup brown sugar
    • 1/2 cup molasses
    • 1 egg

  • Instructions:

    1. In a bowl, mix flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt.
    2. In a separate bowl, cream butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Add molasses and egg, mixing well.
    3. Gradually add dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until combined.
    4. Roll the dough into a ball, wrap in plastic, and chill for at least 2 hours.
    5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out the dough and cut out gingerbread people shapes.
    6. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes. Cool before decorating with icing and candy.

Reindeer Cookies

Get into the holiday spirit with adorable reindeer cookies! Use a round cookie cutter for the base and a smaller cutter for the antlers. Decorate with chocolate-covered pretzels for antlers and candy eyes for a fun touch.

3. Unique Shapes

Ornaments

Cookies shaped like ornaments are not only festive but also make excellent gifts for friends and family. Use a round cookie cutter and decorate with vibrant icing and edible glitter to resemble beautiful holiday ornaments.

Recipe: Almond Ornament Cookies

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1 cup unsalted butter
    • 1 cup powdered sugar
    • 1/2 cup almond flour
    • 1 teaspoon almond extract

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
    2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
    3. In another bowl, beat butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. Mix in almond flour and almond extract.
    4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture.
    5. Roll out the dough, cut into ornament shapes, and place on a baking sheet.
    6. Bake for 10-12 minutes until lightly golden. Cool before decorating.

Stars

Star-shaped cookies are versatile and can be used for any holiday occasion. Decorate them in various colors and stack them for an impressive display. Consider using a star-shaped cutter in various sizes to create a fun layered effect.

4. Themed Shapes

Nativity Scene Cookies

Bring the story of Christmas to life with cookies shaped like nativity scene characters. Use cookie cutters to create shapes like Mary, Joseph, baby Jesus, and the animals. Decorate with icing and edible paint for a delightful twist.

Candy Cane Cookies

Candy cane-shaped cookies are perfect for the holiday season. Use peppermint extract in the dough for an extra festive flavor.

Recipe: Candy Cane Cookies

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
    • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1/2 teaspoon peppermint extract
    • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • Red food coloring

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. In a bowl, cream together butter and powdered sugar until fluffy. Add vanilla and peppermint extracts.
    3. Gradually mix in flour and salt until combined.
    4. Divide the dough in half; color one half with red food coloring.
    5. Roll out small pieces of each color, twist together, and form into candy cane shapes.
    6. Bake for 9-11 minutes. Allow to cool before enjoying.

5. Interactive Shapes

Cookie Decorating Kits

Create a fun activity for the whole family by preparing cookie decorating kits. Bake various shapes like stars, hearts, and circles, then package them with different colored icing, sprinkles, and edible decorations. This allows everyone to unleash their creativity while making delicious treats!

Tips for Perfect Cookie Shapes

  1. Chill Your Dough: Chilling your cookie dough helps maintain the shape during baking. It prevents the cookies from spreading too much, ensuring your fun shapes turn out perfectly.

  2. Use Quality Cookie Cutters: Invest in high-quality cookie cutters in various shapes to achieve clean cuts. Metal cutters often provide sharper edges compared to plastic ones.

  3. Don’t Overbake: Keep a close eye on your cookies while they bake. The edges should be lightly golden, while the centers may appear slightly soft. They will continue to firm up as they cool.

  4. Experiment with Flavors: While traditional vanilla or chocolate cookies are always a hit, consider adding spices, extracts, or even flavored zests to your dough for a unique twist.

  5. Get Creative with Decoration: Use royal icing, fondant, or edible paints to bring your cookie shapes to life. Don’t be afraid to experiment with colors and patterns.

Conclusion

This holiday season, let your creativity shine through with fun cookie shapes that will delight everyone around you. From classic snowflakes and Christmas trees to whimsical reindeer and nativity scene cookies, there’s no shortage of inspiration. Remember that baking is not just about the end result, but also the joy of creating something special with loved ones. So gather your ingredients, invite your family into the kitchen, and start making memories one cookie at a time. Happy baking!
