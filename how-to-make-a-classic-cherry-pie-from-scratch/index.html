How to Make a Classic Cherry Pie from Scratch


There’s something undeniably comforting about a freshly baked cherry pie. With its golden, flaky crust and sweet, tangy filling, it’s a dessert that evokes memories of family gatherings, summer picnics, and cozy afternoons. Making a classic cherry pie from scratch is not as daunting as it may seem. By following this easy-to-follow guide, you’ll create a pie that is sure to impress friends and family alike.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To make a cherry pie from scratch, it’s important to start with high-quality ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pie Crust

  1. 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    Flour is the backbone of any good pie crust. It provides structure and a crisp, flaky texture.

  2. 1 cup unsalted butter (cold and cut into cubes)
    Butter gives the crust its rich flavor and flaky texture. Using cold butter is crucial for creating those signature flaky layers.

  3. 1/4 cup granulated sugar
    This will add a subtle sweetness to the crust, balancing out the tartness of the cherries.

  4. 1/2 teaspoon salt
    A pinch of salt helps enhance the overall flavor and brings out the sweetness of the pie.

  5. 1/4 to 1/2 cup cold water
    You’ll need this to bring the dough together. Add it gradually to avoid making the dough too wet.

For the Cherry Filling

  1. 4 cups fresh or frozen cherries (pitted)
    Fresh, in-season cherries are ideal, but frozen cherries can be used year-round if fresh ones aren’t available. Make sure to thaw them and drain any excess juice.

  2. 1 cup granulated sugar
    This will provide the sweetness needed to balance the tartness of the cherries.

  3. 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    Cornstarch will help thicken the filling, ensuring it doesn’t run out when you slice into the pie.

  4. 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    A little acidity enhances the flavor of the cherries and brightens the overall taste of the pie.

  5. 1/2 teaspoon almond extract (optional)
    Almond extract pairs beautifully with cherries and adds a subtle depth of flavor. This is optional but highly recommended for a more complex filling.

  6. 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
    Adding cinnamon brings warmth and a cozy spice note to the pie, which complements the cherries wonderfully.

For the Pie Topping (Optional)

  1. 1 egg (for egg wash)
    An egg wash gives the crust a golden, glossy finish that’s visually appealing and adds a slight sheen.

  2. 1 tablespoon milk or cream
    This is mixed with the egg to create the egg wash.

Preparing the Pie Crust

Step 1: Make the Dough

  1. Combine the dry ingredients
    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. This helps evenly distribute the ingredients before adding the butter.

  2. Cut in the butter
    Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized chunks of butter. This step is essential for a flaky texture, as it ensures the butter stays in small pieces throughout the dough.

  3. Add water
    Gradually add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently with a fork until the dough just comes together. You want the dough to be moist but not sticky. Be careful not to overwork it, as that can make the crust tough.

  4. Divide and chill
    Once the dough has come together, divide it in half. Shape each half into a disc, wrap them in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Chilling the dough is crucial because it helps the butter firm up and makes the dough easier to roll out later.

Step 2: Roll Out the Dough

  1. Prepare your work surface
    Lightly flour your countertop or work surface to prevent the dough from sticking. Take one disc of dough from the refrigerator and roll it out into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Try to keep the thickness even to ensure the pie crust bakes uniformly.

  2. Transfer to the pie dish
    Gently roll the dough around the rolling pin and lift it onto your pie dish. Press it gently into the bottom and sides, trimming any excess dough around the edges. Don’t worry if there are imperfections—this is a rustic, homemade pie!

  3. Chill the crust
    Once the dough is in the pie dish, place it back in the refrigerator to chill while you prepare the filling.

Preparing the Cherry Filling

Step 1: Mix the Filling Ingredients

In a large bowl, combine the pitted cherries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, almond extract (if using), and cinnamon (if using). Stir gently to combine and make sure the cherries are well-coated with the sugar and cornstarch mixture. The cornstarch will help thicken the filling while it bakes, preventing it from being too runny.

Step 2: Let the Filling Sit

Let the cherry filling sit for about 10 to 15 minutes. This will allow the juices to release from the cherries and mix with the sugar and cornstarch, creating a syrup-like consistency.

Assembling the Cherry Pie

  1. Fill the pie crust
    Once the dough has chilled, take it out of the refrigerator and spoon the prepared cherry filling into the pie shell. Spread it out evenly, making sure the cherries are distributed well.

  2. Roll out the top crust
    Take the second disc of dough from the fridge and roll it out into a circle, just like you did with the bottom crust. Once rolled, place it over the filled pie. Trim any excess dough from the edges, leaving about an inch of overhang.

  3. Crimp the edges
    Fold the top crust under the bottom crust along the edge of the pie dish. Pinch the edges together with your fingers or use a fork to crimp them, ensuring the top and bottom crusts are sealed well. This will prevent the filling from spilling out during baking.

  4. Create vents
    Using a sharp knife, cut several slits or small decorative shapes into the top crust to allow steam to escape while baking. This step is important to prevent the filling from bubbling over.

Baking the Cherry Pie

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). It’s crucial to have the oven fully preheated before baking to achieve a golden, flaky crust.

Step 2: Apply an Egg Wash

In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and milk (or cream) to create the egg wash. Using a pastry brush, lightly brush the top crust with the egg wash. This will give the pie a beautiful golden color once baked.

Step 3: Bake the Pie

Place the pie on the middle rack of the preheated oven and bake for 45 to 50 minutes. The crust should be golden brown, and the filling should be bubbling through the vents in the top crust. If the edges of the pie crust begin to brown too quickly, you can cover them with aluminum foil to prevent burning.

Step 4: Cool the Pie

Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for at least 2 hours. Cooling allows the filling to set, ensuring that each slice holds its shape. It’s tempting to cut into it right away, but patience will reward you with a perfectly sliced pie!

Serving Your Cherry Pie

Once the pie has cooled and the filling has set, slice it up and serve. Cherry pie is wonderful on its own, but it’s even better with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream. The balance of sweet and tart flavors, along with the flaky, buttery crust, makes each bite a delicious experience.

Tips for the Perfect Cherry Pie

  1. Use fresh cherries when in season
    Fresh cherries offer the best flavor, but if they’re out of season, frozen cherries work just as well. Be sure to drain any excess liquid from frozen cherries to prevent a soggy crust.

  2. Don’t skip the chilling step
    Chilling the dough before rolling it out and after placing it in the pie dish helps the butter firm up, resulting in a flakier crust.

  3. Adjust the sweetness to your liking
    Depending on the tartness of your cherries, you may want to adjust the amount of sugar in the filling. Taste your filling before assembling the pie and add more sugar if you prefer a sweeter pie.

  4. Use a combination of cornstarch and sugar for thickening
    If you don’t have cornstarch, you can use flour as an alternative. However, cornstarch tends to create a smoother, clearer filling, while flour may result in a slightly cloudy texture.

  5. Bake on a baking sheet
    To catch any potential drips from the pie, place your pie dish on a baking sheet while baking. This will make cleanup much easier in case any filling bubbles over.

Final Thoughts

Making a classic cherry pie from scratch is a rewarding and enjoyable experience. From the buttery, flaky crust to the perfectly sweetened cherry filling, every step is worth the effort. Whether you’re baking for a holiday, a special occasion, or just because you’re craving a slice of pie, this recipe is sure to become a family favorite.

Now that you’ve learned how to make this timeless dessert, why not try experimenting with other fruit fillings? Peach, blueberry, or mixed berry pies are also delicious and just as easy to make. Happy baking!
