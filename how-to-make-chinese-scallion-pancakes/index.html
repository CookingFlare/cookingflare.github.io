How to Make Chinese Scallion Pancakes


Chinese scallion pancakes, or “Cong You Bing,” are a savory, flaky delight that combines simplicity with bold flavors. These golden pan-fried wonders are crispy on the outside, aromatic, and packed with scallions on the inside, making them a popular comfort food in Chinese cuisine. While they may appear intricate, making scallion pancakes at home is entirely achievable—and incredibly satisfying. This recipe will guide you through a foolproof process to create your own delicious scallion pancakes from scratch.

What Are Chinese Scallion Pancakes?

Chinese scallion pancakes are unleavened flatbreads filled with minced scallions. Unlike a traditional Western pancake, they resemble a layered bread with a chewy texture and crispy edges. The magic lies in the unique folding technique, which creates flaky layers and a touch of complexity in every bite. A perfect balance between simplicity and depth, these savory pancakes are enjoyed as breakfast, appetizers, or even snacks throughout China and beyond.

Ingredients You’ll Need to Make Scallion Pancakes

To make authentic Chinese scallion pancakes, you’ll need just a few basic ingredients. These are pantry staples in many households, making this recipe convenient as well as delicious.

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups (250g) of all-purpose flour: The foundation of your scallion pancakes.
  • ¾ cup of hot water: Helps create a pliable dough and ensures the pancakes stay tender.
  • ½ teaspoon of salt: Enhances flavor and texture.

For the Filling:

  • ½ cup of finely chopped scallions: The star ingredient, lending freshness and aroma.
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil or vegetable oil: To brush over the dough and aid layering.
  • ½ teaspoon of salt: For seasoning the filling.

For Frying:

  • 3-4 tablespoons of vegetable or cooking oil: To help crisp up the pancakes into golden perfection.

Equipment You’ll Need

  • A large mixing bowl
  • A rolling pin
  • A skillet or frying pan
  • A pastry brush
  • A spatula
  • Kitchen towels or plastic wrap

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Make Chinese Scallion Pancakes

Here is the complete process for crafting these delectable pancakes, from preparing the dough to frying them to golden perfection.

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Start by preparing the foundation of your scallion pancakes—the dough.

  1. Mix the flour and salt in a large mixing bowl. Gradually add the hot water while stirring with chopsticks or a fork. The hot water hydrates the flour and partially cooks it, creating dough that’s pliable yet firm.

  2. Once the dough comes together, transfer it to a floured surface. Knead it for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels sticky, sprinkle a little more flour to help with kneading.

  3. Once the dough has a smooth texture, cover it with plastic wrap or a kitchen towel and let it rest for 30 minutes. Resting allows the gluten to relax, making the dough easier to roll out.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the dough rests, prepare the scallion filling.

  1. Wash the scallions thoroughly and pat them dry with a towel. Chop them finely into small bits.

  2. Mix the chopped scallions with a little salt and set them aside. The scallions will soften slightly when combined with salt, making them easier to incorporate into the dough.

Step 3: Roll Out and Fill the Dough

Once the dough is rested, it’s time to roll, fill, and shape your pancakes.

  1. Divide the rested dough into 4 equal portions for smaller pancakes or 2 portions for larger ones.

  2. Roll out one portion of the dough into a thin, circular shape on a lightly floured surface. Brush the surface with a thin layer of sesame oil or vegetable oil.

  3. Sprinkle a generous handful of chopped scallions evenly over the oiled dough. Add a pinch of salt for additional flavor.

  4. Roll and coil the dough: Roll the dough into a tight cylinder, almost like rolling up a carpet. Then coil the rolled dough into a swirl, resembling the shape of a spiral. This step creates flaky layers as the dough is folded onto itself.

  5. Gently flatten the spiral into a disc using your palms or a rolling pin until it’s about 5-6 inches in diameter. Repeat the process for the remaining dough portions.

Step 4: Fry the Pancakes

Once your scallion pancakes are shaped, it’s time to pan-fry them to achieve crispy, golden perfection.

  1. Heat your skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Add about 1 tablespoon of oil, spreading it evenly across the pan’s surface.

  2. Place one pancake into the skillet and allow it to fry for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden and crispy. Use a spatula to gently flip the pancake.

  3. Adjust the heat if necessary to prevent burning. If the pan dries out, add a little more oil for subsequent pancakes.

  4. Once cooked, transfer the pancake to a plate lined with paper towels to remove any excess oil.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Now comes the best part—serving your scallion pancakes!

  1. Slice the pancakes into wedges or squares for easy sharing. You can serve them plain or with dipping sauces such as soy sauce, chili oil, or black vinegar.

  2. Pair them with a hot cup of tea or serve them alongside a main dish. They’re perfect for breakfast, brunch, or as an afternoon snack.

Tips for Perfect Scallion Pancakes

Achieving restaurant-quality scallion pancakes at home might seem challenging at first, but these helpful tips will ensure success:

1. Use Hot Water for the Dough: The key to tender scallion pancakes lies in using hot water when mixing the dough. It helps develop pliability while ensuring the pancakes remain soft.

2. Don’t Skimp on Oil: Brushing sesame oil or vegetable oil over the dough during layering gives the pancakes their flaky, crisp texture. Be generous for the best results.

3. High-Quality Scallions Are Essential: Fresh and fragrant scallions are crucial. Avoid using dry or wilted ones, as they may not add the desired burst of flavor.

4. Adjust Heat During Frying: Since pancakes can burn quickly, maintain medium heat and adjust as needed. The goal is to cook them evenly to achieve crisp edges and a tender interior.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

While scallion pancakes are delicious as they are, you can personalize them with creative variations:

  • Add Flavored Oils: Brush chili oil, garlic oil, or mushroom oil over the dough for additional depth.
  • Include Extra Fillings: Thin slices of cooked bacon, diced shrimp, or shredded carrots can be added along with the scallions to create unique flavor profiles.
  • Dipping Sauce Pairings: Enhance the flavor by serving your pancakes with a soy-ginger dipping sauce or a spicy chili paste.

Why Make Scallion Pancakes at Home?

Making Chinese scallion pancakes at home is not only rewarding but also allows you to tailor the recipe to your preferences. Store-bought pancakes often lack the flaky layers and robust flavor of freshly prepared ones. By making them yourself, you’ll enjoy the authentic taste and texture that’s hard to replicate.

Conclusion

Chinese scallion pancakes are a beloved dish that celebrates simplicity and savory satisfaction. Whether you’re new to Chinese cooking or an enthusiast exploring classic recipes, scallion pancakes are perfect for honing your kitchen skills. With their crispy edges, chewy layers, and bursts of scallion flavor, they’re guaranteed to impress friends, family, or anyone lucky enough to taste them.

Follow this recipe step-by-step, and you’ll master the art of scallion pancake-making in no time. Once you’ve tried it, these addictive pancakes are bound to become a staple in your culinary repertoire!
