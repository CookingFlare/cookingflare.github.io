Indulge in the Creaminess of Authentic Indian Kadai Paneer


Kadai Paneer is a classic Indian dish that is loved by many for its rich and creamy texture. Made with succulent paneer (Indian cottage cheese) and a medley of aromatic spices, this dish is a must-try for all lovers of Indian cuisine. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced chef, this recipe will guide you through the process of creating a flavorful and indulgent Kadai Paneer that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams paneer, cut into cubes
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a kadai (wok) or a deep pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. This will take around 5-6 minutes.

  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell of ginger-garlic disappears.

  4. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the mixture. This will take around 8-10 minutes.

  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a minute to let the spices release their flavors.

  6. Add the bell peppers and cook for 3-4 minutes until they are slightly tender.

  7. Gently add the paneer cubes and mix them with the masala. Be careful not to break the paneer cubes while stirring.

  8. Sprinkle kasuri methi over the paneer and masala. Mix gently to combine.

  9. Cover the kadai with a lid and let the paneer cook in the masala for 5-6 minutes on low heat. This will allow the flavors to meld together.

  10. Remove the lid and give it a gentle stir. Check for seasoning and add salt as per your taste.

  11. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Tips

  • To make the dish spicier, you can increase the quantity of red chili powder or add a pinch of cayenne pepper.
  • If you want a more smoky flavor, you can roast the bell peppers before adding them to the dish.
  • For a creamier texture, you can add a tablespoon of fresh cream or cashew paste to the masala.
  • Kadai Paneer tastes best when served hot, so make sure to enjoy it immediately after preparing.

Final Thoughts

Indulging in the creaminess of authentic Indian Kadai Paneer is an experience that will transport you to the vibrant streets of India. The combination of tender paneer, aromatic spices, and colorful bell peppers creates a dish that is not only visually appealing but also a delight to your taste buds. Whether you’re a fan of Indian cuisine or looking to explore new flavors, this recipe is a must-try. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary journey that will leave you craving for more Kadai Paneer goodness.
