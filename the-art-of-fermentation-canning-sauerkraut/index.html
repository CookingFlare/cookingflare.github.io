The Art of Fermentation: Canning Sauerkraut


Sauerkraut is a traditional fermented food that has been enjoyed for centuries. Its tangy flavor and crunchy texture make it a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of dishes. If you’re a fan of sauerkraut and want to learn how to preserve it for longer periods, canning is the way to go. In this blog post, we will explore the art of fermentation and share a step-by-step guide on canning sauerkraut.

What is Fermentation?

Fermentation is a natural process that occurs when microorganisms, such as bacteria or yeast, convert sugars into acids or alcohol. This process not only preserves food but also enhances its flavor and nutritional value. Sauerkraut is made through a process called lacto-fermentation, where bacteria naturally present on cabbage convert its sugars into lactic acid.

Why Can Sauerkraut?

Canning sauerkraut is a great way to preserve it for longer periods, ensuring that you have a steady supply of this tasty condiment all year round. The canning process involves sealing the sauerkraut in jars and subjecting them to heat, which kills any remaining bacteria and prevents spoilage. Canned sauerkraut can last for up to a year when stored in a cool, dark place.

Canning Sauerkraut: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather the Ingredients

To can sauerkraut, you will need:

  • Fresh cabbage
  • Salt (preferably non-iodized)
  • Glass canning jars with lids
  • Canning pot or a large stockpot
  • Jar lifter
  • Kitchen scale
  • Cutting board
  • Knife
  • Mixing bowl

Step 2: Prepare the Cabbage

Start by removing the outer leaves of the cabbage and washing it thoroughly. Cut the cabbage into quarters and remove the core. Finely shred the cabbage using a knife or a mandoline slicer. Weigh the shredded cabbage to determine the amount of salt needed.

Step 3: Salt the Cabbage

In a mixing bowl, combine the shredded cabbage with the calculated amount of salt. Massage the salt into the cabbage, breaking down its cell walls and extracting the natural juices. This will create a brine that will aid in the fermentation process.

Step 4: Pack the Jars

Using clean hands or a sterilized utensil, tightly pack the salted cabbage into the canning jars, leaving about 1 inch of headspace at the top. Press down firmly to remove any air bubbles and ensure the cabbage is fully submerged in the brine.

Step 5: Seal the Jars

Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any brine or cabbage residue. Place the lids on the jars and screw them on tightly. Make sure the lids are in good condition and have no dents or rust.

Step 6: Process the Jars

Fill a canning pot or a large stockpot with enough water to cover the jars by at least 1 inch. Place the jars in the pot, making sure they are not touching each other. Bring the water to a boil and process the jars for 15 minutes.

Step 7: Cool and Store

Using a jar lifter, carefully remove the jars from the pot and place them on a towel-lined countertop. Let the jars cool completely before checking the seals. The lids should be slightly concave, indicating a proper seal. Store the jars in a cool, dark place for at least 2 weeks before opening to allow the flavors to develop.

Enjoying Your Canned Sauerkraut

Canned sauerkraut can be enjoyed in various ways. It makes a delicious topping for hot dogs and sausages, adds a tangy kick to sandwiches and salads, and can even be used as a flavorful ingredient in stews and casseroles. Experiment with different recipes and discover your favorite ways to incorporate this versatile condiment into your meals.

Conclusion

Canning sauerkraut is a rewarding way to preserve this classic fermented food. By following the simple steps outlined in this guide, you can enjoy homemade sauerkraut throughout the year. Remember to always use fresh ingredients and proper canning techniques to ensure the safety and quality of your canned sauerkraut. So, roll up your sleeves, grab some cabbage, and embark on your journey into the art of fermentation!
