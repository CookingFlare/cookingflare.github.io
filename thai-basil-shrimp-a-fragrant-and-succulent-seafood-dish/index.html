Thai Basil Shrimp: A Fragrant and Succulent Seafood Dish


If you’re a seafood lover like me, then you’re in for a treat with this mouthwatering recipe for Thai Basil Shrimp. Bursting with flavors and fragrances, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. The combination of succulent shrimp, aromatic Thai basil, and a medley of spices creates a harmonious balance that will leave your taste buds dancing with delight. So, let’s dive right in and discover how to make this irresistible seafood dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili pepper, thinly sliced (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic and sliced red chili pepper. Sauté for about a minute until the garlic becomes fragrant and slightly golden.

  2. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for about 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and opaque. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp, as they can become rubbery. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set them aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the sliced red and yellow bell peppers. Sauté for about 2-3 minutes until they start to soften.

  4. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet and add the fish sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar. Stir well to coat the shrimp and vegetables with the flavorful sauce. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until the shrimp are fully cooked and the sauce has thickened slightly.

  5. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the fresh Thai basil leaves. The residual heat will wilt the basil leaves just enough to release their aromatic flavors without overpowering them.

  6. Serve the Thai Basil Shrimp over steamed jasmine rice or with a side of noodles. Garnish with some extra Thai basil leaves and sliced red chili peppers for an extra pop of color and flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, you can add a few dashes of Thai chili paste or Sriracha sauce to the dish.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables according to your preference. You can add broccoli, snap peas, or mushrooms for added texture and flavor.
  • If you’re not a fan of shrimp, you can easily substitute it with chicken, beef, or even tofu for a vegetarian version.
  • Don’t skimp on the Thai basil, as it is the star ingredient that gives this dish its distinctive flavor. If you can’t find Thai basil, you can use regular basil as a substitute, although the taste will be slightly different.

Final Thoughts

Thai Basil Shrimp is a delightful seafood dish that embodies the essence of Thai cuisine. With its fragrant aroma, succulent shrimp, and vibrant blend of flavors, it’s no wonder this dish is a favorite among Thai food enthusiasts. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is easy to follow and guarantees a restaurant-quality meal in the comfort of your own home. So, gather your ingredients, unleash your inner chef, and enjoy the tantalizing flavors of Thai Basil Shrimp!
