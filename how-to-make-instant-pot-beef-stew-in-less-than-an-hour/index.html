How to Make Instant Pot Beef Stew in Less Than an Hour


Beef stew is one of those timeless, comfort foods that brings warmth and satisfaction on a cold day. With tender chunks of beef, hearty vegetables, and a savory broth, it’s a dish that fills you up and makes you feel at home. Traditionally, beef stew can take hours to cook, but thanks to the magic of the Instant Pot, you can make a rich and flavorful stew in less than an hour! This recipe provides a quick and easy solution for those busy nights when you want a delicious home-cooked meal without the wait.

Why Use an Instant Pot for Beef Stew?

Using an Instant Pot for beef stew is a game-changer. The pressure cooking method reduces the cooking time drastically while still delivering the deep flavors and tenderness that slow cooking provides. Normally, beef stew requires simmering for several hours to achieve the desired texture and flavor, but with the Instant Pot, you can achieve the same results in less than an hour. Here are a few reasons why the Instant Pot is perfect for making beef stew:

  • Faster Cooking: Pressure cooking locks in flavors and cooks ingredients much faster than traditional methods.
  • Tender Meat: The pressure helps break down the meat, making it tender without long cooking times.
  • Hands-Off Cooking: Once everything is in the Instant Pot, it does most of the work, leaving you with more time to relax or prepare other parts of your meal.
  • Convenient: You don’t need to constantly check or stir your stew—just set it and let the Instant Pot handle the rest.

Now, let’s dive into how to make a hearty and delicious Instant Pot beef stew in under an hour!

Ingredients for Instant Pot Beef Stew

For the best beef stew, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Stew:

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat (chuck roast or any other cut suitable for stewing), cut into 1–2 inch cubes
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3 large carrots, peeled and sliced into rounds
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup beef broth (low-sodium preferred)
  • 1 cup red wine (optional, but adds depth of flavor)
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes (with juice)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Optional for Thickening:

  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Instant Pot Beef Stew

Step 1: Sauté the Meat

Begin by setting your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function. Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the pot and wait for it to heat up. Once the oil is hot, add the beef stew meat in batches to avoid overcrowding. Sauté the meat for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it is browned on all sides. Browning the beef adds a rich flavor to the stew, so don’t skip this step.

Once the meat is browned, remove it from the Instant Pot and set it aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

Next, add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the Instant Pot. Sauté for 2-3 minutes, until the onions become translucent and fragrant. Stir occasionally to prevent burning. This step helps to bring out the flavors of the onion and garlic, infusing the broth with aromatic goodness.

Step 3: Deglaze the Pot

Now, it’s time to deglaze the pot to scrape up any delicious browned bits from the bottom of the Instant Pot. Pour in the red wine (if using) and stir to loosen the bits stuck to the pot. The wine will add depth and richness to the stew, but if you prefer not to use alcohol, you can replace the wine with an additional cup of beef broth.

Let the wine simmer for a minute, allowing it to reduce slightly and concentrate the flavors.

Step 4: Add the Remaining Ingredients

Once the pot is deglazed, add the beef stew meat back into the Instant Pot. Then, add the cubed potatoes, sliced carrots, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, dried thyme, and bay leaves. Pour in the beef broth and stir everything together, ensuring the ingredients are evenly distributed.

Season the stew with salt and pepper to taste. At this stage, you can adjust the seasoning based on your preferences.

Step 5: Pressure Cook

Secure the Instant Pot lid and make sure the vent is set to the sealing position. Set the Instant Pot to the “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” function on high for 35 minutes. The Instant Pot will take a few minutes to come to pressure, so don’t worry if it takes a bit longer to start cooking.

While the stew is cooking, you can take a moment to relax. The pressure cooking process will tenderize the beef and cook the vegetables to perfection.

Step 6: Natural Release

Once the cooking time is complete, let the Instant Pot naturally release pressure for 10 minutes. After 10 minutes, carefully switch the vent to the “venting” position to release any remaining steam. Open the lid, and you’ll be greeted with a fragrant, hearty stew that’s ready to serve.

Step 7: Thicken the Stew (Optional)

If you prefer a thicker stew, you can easily achieve that by making a slurry. In a small bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water. Stir the mixture until smooth. Turn the Instant Pot back to the “Sauté” setting, and add the cornstarch slurry to the stew. Stir well and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the stew thickens to your liking.

Step 8: Taste and Adjust Seasoning

Before serving, taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with additional salt, pepper, or herbs as needed. The flavors will have melded together beautifully, but this step ensures the stew has the perfect balance for your taste buds.

Serving Your Instant Pot Beef Stew

Once your Instant Pot beef stew is ready, it’s time to serve! Ladle the stew into bowls, making sure to get a good portion of the beef, potatoes, and carrots in each serving. Garnish with fresh herbs like parsley or thyme if desired.

This beef stew pairs wonderfully with crusty bread for dipping or a side of buttered noodles. The rich, flavorful broth is perfect for soaking up, making it a satisfying and filling meal.

Tips for Perfect Instant Pot Beef Stew

1. Choose the Right Cut of Meat

For the best stew, you’ll want a cut of beef that becomes tender after cooking for a while. Chuck roast is ideal because it breaks down well under pressure, resulting in tender pieces of beef. If you’re in a pinch, other cuts like round or brisket will work as well, but chuck is generally the best for stews.

2. Don’t Skip the Browning Step

Browning the beef before pressure cooking adds a layer of flavor to the stew. This Maillard reaction enhances the overall taste, giving your stew a deeper, richer flavor profile.

3. Use Fresh Herbs for the Best Flavor

While dried herbs are convenient, fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley can elevate the flavor of your stew. If using fresh herbs, add them at the end of cooking or use them as a garnish.

4. Adjust the Thickness to Your Liking

Some people prefer their beef stew thick, while others like a more brothy consistency. You can easily adjust the thickness by adding a cornstarch slurry or by simply allowing the stew to simmer for a few extra minutes without the lid.

5. Make It Your Own

This recipe is versatile, and you can add other vegetables or seasonings to suit your taste. Peas, parsnips, or celery are great additions. If you like a spicier kick, add a dash of hot sauce or cayenne pepper to the broth.

Storing and Reheating Beef Stew

If you have leftovers, you can store the stew in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3-4 days. The flavors will continue to develop, making it even better the next day! To reheat, simply place the stew in a pot over medium heat or use the “Sauté” function on your Instant Pot to warm it up. You may need to add a little extra broth if the stew thickens too much during storage.

Conclusion

Making beef stew in an Instant Pot is a game-changer for busy home cooks who crave the flavors of slow-cooked meals without the hours of wait time. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a hearty, tender, and flavorful beef stew in under an hour. Whether you’re preparing a cozy family dinner or meal prepping for the week, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

So, grab your Instant Pot, gather your ingredients, and enjoy a delicious beef stew in record time!
