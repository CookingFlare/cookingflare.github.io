Savory Chicken and Dumplings: A Cozy Comfort Food


When the chill of winter settles in or a rainy day calls for something warm and comforting, few dishes can compete with a hearty bowl of chicken and dumplings. This classic comfort food has been a staple in many households for generations, offering a delightful combination of tender chicken, rich broth, and fluffy dumplings. In this blog post, we will explore the origins of chicken and dumplings, the essential ingredients, and a step-by-step guide to creating this cozy dish in your own kitchen.

The Origins of Chicken and Dumplings

Chicken and dumplings have a rich history that dates back to the early 19th century. While the exact origins are debated, it is widely believed that this dish has roots in both European and American culinary traditions. The concept of combining meat with a starchy component, such as dumplings, can be traced back to various cultures, including German, French, and British cuisines.

In the United States, chicken and dumplings became particularly popular in the Southern states, where home cooks would prepare it as a way to feed large families on a budget. The dish is often associated with comfort and nostalgia, evoking memories of family gatherings and warm kitchens filled with the aroma of simmering broth.

Essential Ingredients for Chicken and Dumplings

To create a truly satisfying chicken and dumplings dish, you’ll need a few key ingredients. Here’s a breakdown of what you’ll need:

For the Chicken and Broth

  • Chicken: You can use a whole chicken, chicken thighs, or a combination of both. Bone-in, skin-on pieces are ideal for flavor.
  • Vegetables: Classic choices include onions, carrots, and celery. These aromatics add depth to the broth.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic enhances the flavor profile and adds a touch of warmth.
  • Herbs: Fresh or dried thyme, bay leaves, and parsley are commonly used to infuse the broth with herbal notes.
  • Chicken Broth: Homemade or store-bought, this is the base of your dish. If you’re using a whole chicken, you can make your own broth as the chicken cooks.

For the Dumplings

  • All-Purpose Flour: The main ingredient for dumplings, providing structure.
  • Baking Powder: This leavening agent helps the dumplings rise and become fluffy.
  • Salt: Enhances the flavor of the dumplings.
  • Milk: Adds moisture and richness to the dough.
  • Butter: Melted butter gives the dumplings a tender texture and a rich flavor.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Savory Chicken and Dumplings

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s dive into the cooking process. Follow these steps to create a delicious pot of chicken and dumplings that will warm your heart and soul.

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken and Broth

  1. Cook the Chicken: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces, skin-side down, and sear until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes. Flip and brown the other side. Remove the chicken and set it aside.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pot, add the chopped onions, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are softened. Add minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  3. Add the Broth: Return the chicken to the pot and pour in enough chicken broth to cover the chicken. Add thyme, bay leaves, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover. Let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

  4. Shred the Chicken: Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and let it cool slightly. Shred the meat into bite-sized pieces, discarding the skin and bones. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and stir to combine.

Step 2: Make the Dumpling Dough

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.

  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Pour in the milk and melted butter. Stir until just combined; be careful not to overmix. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.

Step 3: Cook the Dumplings

  1. Prepare for Dumpling Cooking: Bring the chicken and broth mixture back to a gentle simmer. If the broth is too thick, you can add a little more chicken broth or water to reach your desired consistency.

  2. Drop the Dumplings: Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop spoonfuls of the dumpling dough directly into the simmering broth. Make sure to leave space between each dumpling, as they will expand while cooking.
