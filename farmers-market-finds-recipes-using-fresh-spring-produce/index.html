Farmers Market Finds: Recipes Using Fresh Spring Produce


Spring is a season of renewal, not just for the earth but also for your kitchen. As temperatures warm and the first signs of vibrant green return to farmers markets, an abundance of fresh, seasonal produce becomes available. From crisp asparagus spears to sweet strawberries that taste almost like candy, the choices are as exciting as they are inspiring.

In this post, we’ll explore the delights of springtime produce, highlighting delicious recipes that make the most of these seasonal treasures. Along the way, we’ll share helpful tips for selecting the best at the market and storing your bounty to ensure nothing goes to waste.

What’s in Season During Spring?

Spring’s offerings are as diverse as they are colorful. At your local farmers market, you might find produce like:

  • Asparagus – One of the earliest vegetables to pop up, asparagus is tender, earthy, and perfect for roasting, grilling, or tossing into salads.
  • Peas – Whether it’s shelled peas, snap peas, or snow peas, these greens bring a natural sweetness to your meals.
  • Radishes – Crisp and peppery, radishes add a refreshing crunch to salads or stand out when roasted.
  • Strawberries – Bursting with sweetness and flavor, the first crop of strawberries in spring is undeniably satisfying.
  • Spring onions and garlic – Mild and tender, these early aromatics add both flavor and depth to dishes.
  • Rhubarb – Though often thought of as a fruit, this tart veg is a springtime favorite for desserts.
  • Herbs – Think mint, parsley, dill, and tarragon that sprout with vigor at this time of year.
  • Artichokes, fava beans, beets, and leafy greens – All add pops of texture and flavor to seasonal menus.

Recipe 1: Lemon Herb Asparagus and Pea Risotto

This creamy risotto is a celebration of spring flavors. The vibrant green of asparagus and peas not only makes for a visually stunning dish but also highlights two of the season’s best.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1/3 cup dry white wine
  • 3-4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 3/4 cup fresh peas (or thawed frozen peas)
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh tarragon, chopped
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until translucent.
  2. Stir in the Arborio rice, toasting gently for 2 minutes to coat in oil.
  3. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer away until absorbed.
  4. Begin adding the warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Let each addition absorb before pouring in more.
  5. About 10 minutes before the rice is fully cooked, stir in the asparagus and peas.
  6. Once the rice is creamy, remove from heat and fold in the lemon zest, fresh herbs, Parmesan (if using), and season to taste.
  7. Serve hot with an extra sprinkle of parsley on top.

Recipe 2: Spring Strawberry and Arugula Salad

This refreshing salad hits all the right notes—sweet, tangy, and slightly peppery. It’s perfect as a light lunch or an appetizer for a larger meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh arugula
  • 1 cup strawberries, sliced
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup pecans or walnuts, toasted
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Salt and freshly cracked pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the arugula, strawberries, feta cheese, and toasted nuts.
  2. In a small jar, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad. Toss until everything is evenly coated.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy the blend of seasonal flavors.

===

Recipe 3: Garlic-Butter Roasted Radishes

Roasting radishes transforms their sharp, peppery bite into something mellow and slightly sweet. This simple side dish pairs beautifully with grilled fish or roasted chicken.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch radishes, trimmed and cut in half
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the radishes with melted butter, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread them out on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Roast in the oven for 15-20 minutes, or until tender and golden around the edges.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm.

===

Recipe 4: Rhubarb Compote

This tart and tangy compote is endlessly versatile. Spoon it over yogurt, swirl it into oatmeal, or layer it into parfaits.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups rhubarb, chopped
  • 1/3 cup sugar (adjust based on sweetness preference)
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine rhubarb, sugar, orange juice, and zest.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the rhubarb breaks down and the mixture thickens (about 10-15 minutes).
  3. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Let the compote cool before storing in an airtight container in the fridge.

Tips for Making the Most of Spring Produce

  1. Shop Early: Farmers markets are best explored early in the day when produce is at its freshest and selection is plentiful.
  2. Bring Reusable Bags: Sturdy, reusable totes make carrying your haul a breeze.
  3. Ask Questions: Most farmers are more than happy to share tips on how to cook, store, or prepare their produce.
  4. Use Quickly: Spring vegetables tend to be delicate and are best enjoyed within a few days of harvest.
  5. Freeze Extras: If you overstock, blanch items like peas or asparagus and freeze them for later use.

===

Why Choose Local and Seasonal?

Spring produce from the farmers market isn’t just fresher—it’s also more nutrient-rich and flavorful. Buying local supports sustainable farming practices and reduces the food industry’s environmental footprint. Plus, there’s something inherently rewarding about creating meals that celebrate the season’s bounty.

===

Your Turn to Get Cooking

Spring is fleeting, but its produce offers a wealth of inspiration. Whether you’re crafting a delicate risotto, tossing a salad with fresh strawberries, or trying your hand at rhubarb desserts, remember that simple preparations often let the natural flavors shine brightest. Head to your local farmers market this weekend and see what treasures you can uncover—you might just find a new favorite dish waiting to be made.
