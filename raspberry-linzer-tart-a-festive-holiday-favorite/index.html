Raspberry Linzer Tart: A Festive Holiday Favorite


The holiday season is upon us, and with it comes the opportunity to indulge in sweet and savory treats that bring joy and warmth to our loved ones. One of the most iconic and beloved desserts of the season is the Linzer tart, a classic Austrian pastry that is both elegant and delicious. In this recipe, we’ll be giving the traditional Linzer tart a festive twist by incorporating fresh raspberries into the filling.

The History of the Linzer Tart

The Linzer tart has a rich history that dates back to the 17th century in Linz, Austria. The pastry was originally created as a way to use up leftover bread and nuts, and was typically filled with a mixture of ground nuts, sugar, and spices. Over time, the Linzer tart evolved to include a lattice-top crust and a variety of fillings, including fruit preserves and chocolate.

The Magic of Raspberry Linzer Tart

The addition of fresh raspberries to the Linzer tart is a game-changer. The tartness of the raspberries perfectly balances the sweetness of the pastry and the sugar, creating a flavor combination that is both sweet and savory. The raspberries also add a pop of color and texture to the tart, making it a stunning centerpiece for any holiday gathering.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice water
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup ground almonds
  • 1/2 cup fresh raspberries
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup confectioners’ sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, confectioners’ sugar, and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add the ice water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Trim the edges of the dough and press it into the corners of the pan.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together the granulated sugar, ground almonds, and cornstarch. Sprinkle the mixture evenly over the bottom of the tart crust.
  6. Arrange the fresh raspberries over the sugar mixture, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges of the tart.
  7. Fold the edges of the tart crust up over the raspberries, pressing gently to seal. Brush the crust with a little bit of water and sprinkle with confectioners’ sugar.
  8. Bake the tart for 40-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Allow the tart to cool completely on a wire rack.
  9. Serve the tart at room temperature, garnished with additional fresh raspberries if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the tart more festive, you can add a few sprigs of fresh rosemary or thyme to the filling along with the raspberries.
  • If you prefer a more intense raspberry flavor, you can use frozen raspberries instead of fresh.
  • To make the tart ahead of time, you can prepare the crust and filling separately and assemble the tart just before baking.
  • The Linzer tart is also delicious with other types of fruit, such as strawberries or blueberries. Simply substitute the raspberries with your preferred fruit and adjust the amount of sugar to taste.

Conclusion

The Raspberry Linzer Tart is a show-stopping dessert that is sure to impress your friends and family this holiday season. With its flaky crust, sweet and tart filling, and stunning presentation, it’s the perfect centerpiece for any holiday gathering. So why not give it a try? With its rich history and delicious flavors, the Raspberry Linzer Tart is sure to become a new holiday tradition.
