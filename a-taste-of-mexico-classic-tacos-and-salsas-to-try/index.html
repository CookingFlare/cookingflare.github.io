A Taste of Mexico: Classic Tacos and Salsas to Try


Mexican cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors and rich culinary heritage. One of the most beloved and iconic dishes from Mexico is the humble taco. With its versatile fillings and delicious accompanying salsas, tacos have become a favorite among food lovers worldwide. In this blog post, we will explore the world of classic Mexican tacos and salsas, providing you with authentic recipes that will transport your taste buds to the streets of Mexico.

Classic Tacos

Carnitas Tacos

Carnitas, which translates to “little meats,” are succulent, slow-cooked pork tacos that are a staple in Mexican cuisine. This traditional dish boasts tender, flavorful pork that is crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside. Here’s how you can make your own carnitas tacos:

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 orange, juiced
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat some oil over medium-high heat and brown the pork chunks on all sides.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the pan and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Pour in the orange and lime juice, along with the oregano and cumin. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the pork simmer for about 2 hours or until it becomes tender and easily shreds.
  5. Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the pan and shred it using two forks.
  6. Heat the corn tortillas in a dry pan until soft and pliable.
  7. Assemble your tacos by placing a generous amount of the shredded pork onto each tortilla. Top with your favorite garnishes, such as diced onions, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.

Baja Fish Tacos

Originating from the Baja California region of Mexico, Baja fish tacos are a delightful fusion of Mexican and American flavors. These tacos feature crispy battered fish, tangy cabbage slaw, and a zesty white sauce. Here’s how you can recreate this iconic dish:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound white fish fillets (such as cod or halibut)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Corn tortillas
  • Shredded cabbage
  • Sliced radishes
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges

White Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon lime zest
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, mix together the flour, paprika, garlic powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  2. Pat the fish fillets dry with a paper towel, then dredge them in the flour mixture, ensuring they are evenly coated.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or fryer to around 375°F (190°C).
  4. Carefully add the fish fillets to the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy, around 3-4 minutes per side. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, sour cream, lime juice, lime zest, cilantro, salt, and pepper to make the white sauce.
  6. Heat the corn tortillas in a dry pan until warm and pliable.
  7. To assemble the tacos, place a piece of fried fish on each tortilla, top with shredded cabbage, sliced radishes, fresh cilantro, and a drizzle of the white sauce. Serve with lime wedges on the side.

Classic Salsas

No taco is complete without a delicious salsa to accompany it. Here are two classic Mexican salsas that will elevate your taco experience:

Salsa Roja

Salsa Roja, or “red sauce,” is a versatile and flavorful tomato-based salsa that adds a kick to any dish. It strikes the perfect balance between tangy, spicy, and savory flavors. Here’s how you can make your own Salsa Roja:

Ingredients:

  • 4 large tomatoes
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2-3 jalapeño peppers (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your broiler or grill to high heat.
  2. Place the tomatoes, onion, garlic, and jalapeño peppers on a baking sheet or grill pan.
  3. Broil or grill the vegetables until they are charred and blistered, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  4. Remove the vegetables from the heat and let them cool slightly.
  5. Peel the skin off the tomatoes and remove the stems from the jalapeño peppers.
  6. In a blender or food processor, combine the charred vegetables, cilantro, lime juice, and salt. Blend until smooth.
  7. Taste and adjust the seasonings according to your preference.

Salsa Verde

Salsa Verde, or “green sauce,” is a tangy and refreshing salsa made with tomatillos, a staple ingredient in Mexican cooking. It pairs perfectly with grilled meats and adds a burst of freshness to any dish. Here’s how you can make your own Salsa Verde:

Ingredients:

  • 10 tomatillos, husks removed
  • 1 small onion, quartered
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2-3 jalapeño peppers (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your broiler or grill to high heat.
  2. Place the tomatillos, onion, garlic, and jalapeño peppers on a baking sheet or grill pan.
  3. Broil or grill the vegetables until they are charred and blistered, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  4. Remove the vegetables from the heat and let them cool slightly.
  5. In a blender or food processor, combine the charred vegetables, cilantro, lime juice, and salt. Blend until smooth.
  6. Taste and adjust the seasonings according to your preference.

Enjoy the Flavors of Mexico!

There you have it, a taste of Mexico with classic tacos and salsas that will transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico City or the sun-soaked beaches of Baja California. Whether you’re hosting a Mexican-themed dinner party or simply craving a delicious homemade meal, these recipes are sure to impress. So gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and embark on a culinary journey to discover the authentic flavors of Mexico. ¡Buen provecho!
