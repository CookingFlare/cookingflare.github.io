10 Vegan Recipes That Will Convince Even the Meat Lovers


Transitioning to a plant-based diet can seem like a challenge, especially for those who’ve always had meat as the main star of their meals. But what if we told you that going vegan doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor or the satisfaction of a hearty meal? With the right ingredients and a little creativity, vegan recipes can easily stand up to even the most meat-centric dishes. Whether you’re already on a plant-based journey or just looking for healthier, cruelty-free meal options, these 10 vegan recipes are sure to convince even the staunchest meat lovers.

1. Vegan BBQ Pulled Jackfruit Sandwiches

If you miss the tangy, smoky flavor of pulled pork, then jackfruit is your new best friend. This tropical fruit has a texture that mimics pulled meat and absorbs any flavor it’s cooked with. When combined with vegan barbecue sauce, you get a satisfying and savory dish that’s perfect for sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of young green jackfruit (in brine or water)
  • 1 cup of vegan BBQ sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegan buns
  • Coleslaw (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the jackfruit. Use your hands or two forks to shred it into pieces, removing any seeds or tough bits.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until soft and fragrant.
  3. Add the shredded jackfruit, smoked paprika, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together, ensuring the jackfruit is well-coated with spices.
  4. Pour in the BBQ sauce and mix well. Cover and simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the jackfruit absorbs all the flavors and becomes tender.
  5. Serve on vegan buns with a generous scoop of coleslaw for that perfect sandwich experience.

The texture of the jackfruit combined with the smoky BBQ sauce creates an incredibly satisfying sandwich that will have even meat lovers wondering how they ever lived without it.

2. Vegan Mushroom Stroganoff

For those who love creamy pasta dishes, a vegan mushroom stroganoff is a comforting alternative to the traditional beef version. The rich and creamy sauce made from cashews and plant-based milk pairs beautifully with the earthy mushrooms, creating a dish that feels indulgent but is entirely plant-based.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz pasta (any type you prefer)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lb mushrooms, sliced (button, cremini, or portobello work well)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • ½ cup raw cashews (soaked in water for at least 2 hours)
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until soft and translucent.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become golden brown, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. In a blender or food processor, combine the soaked cashews, vegetable broth, almond milk, nutritional yeast (if using), smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  5. Pour the cashew sauce into the pan with the mushrooms, stirring to combine. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  6. Toss the cooked pasta into the sauce and stir to coat evenly.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This rich and creamy vegan stroganoff is perfect for a cozy dinner and will satisfy your cravings for something indulgent, all while being completely plant-based.

3. Vegan Tempeh Tacos

Tacos are a crowd favorite, and these vegan tempeh tacos are no exception. Tempeh is a fermented soy product that has a firm texture and a nutty flavor, making it an excellent substitute for ground meat. With the right seasoning, you’ll have a taco filling that’s bursting with flavor and perfect for taco night.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of tempeh, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • ½ cup vegetable broth
  • Corn or flour tortillas
  • Toppings: lettuce, tomatoes, avocado, cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until soft.
  2. Crumble the tempeh into the skillet and cook, stirring occasionally, until it starts to brown and crisp up, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Add the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, soy sauce, and vegetable broth. Stir to combine, and let it simmer for another 5 minutes until the liquid has evaporated.
  4. Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave.
  5. Spoon the tempeh mixture into the tortillas and top with your favorite toppings like avocado, tomatoes, and cilantro.

These tempeh tacos are a tasty and filling option that’s perfect for meal prepping or a fun family dinner. The smoky, spiced tempeh filling will convince even the most devoted meat eaters that plant-based tacos can be just as satisfying as their meat-filled counterparts.

4. Vegan Lentil Shepherd’s Pie

A hearty and comforting meal, vegan lentil shepherd’s pie is a great choice for cozy dinners. The rich, savory filling made with lentils and vegetables is topped with creamy mashed potatoes, creating a dish that’s as satisfying as any traditional meat-based shepherd’s pie.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked green or brown lentils
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 cup peas
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 4 cups mashed potatoes (vegan butter and plant-based milk to taste)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and garlic and cook until the vegetables are soft.
  3. Stir in the cooked lentils, tomato paste, vegetable broth, and soy sauce. Cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the mixture to thicken and the flavors to meld together. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Transfer the lentil mixture to a baking dish and spread the mashed potatoes on top, smoothing them out with a spatula.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is slightly golden and crispy.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

This vegan shepherd’s pie is a hearty, comforting meal that will fill you up without any animal products. The lentils provide a meaty texture, while the mashed potatoes add a creamy finish.

5. Vegan Chickpea “Chicken” Salad

Sometimes, the simplest meals are the most satisfying. This vegan chickpea “chicken” salad is a quick, protein-packed dish that’s perfect for lunch or as a sandwich filling. Chickpeas make an excellent substitute for chicken, offering a hearty texture and the ability to soak up all the creamy flavors of the dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and mashed
  • 2 tablespoons vegan mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon chopped dill pickles
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh greens or bread for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mash the chickpeas with a fork or potato masher, leaving some chunks for texture.
  2. Add the vegan mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, chopped pickles, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir until well combined.
  3. Serve on fresh greens, as a sandwich, or with crackers.

This vegan chickpea salad is perfect for meal prepping, and it’s just as tasty as any traditional chicken salad. The creamy dressing combined with the chickpeas creates a satisfying and flavorful dish that’s sure to please everyone.

6. Vegan Sweet Potato and Black Bean Burritos

Burritos are an easy and customizable meal, and these vegan sweet potato and black bean burritos are filled with nutritious, flavorful ingredients that make for a satisfying meal. The sweetness of the roasted sweet potatoes pairs beautifully with the savory black beans and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cooked rice (optional)
  • 4 large tortillas
  • Toppings: avocado, salsa, cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the sweet potato cubes with olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread them out on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, until tender and golden.
  2. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, warm the black beans and rice in a saucepan over medium heat.
  3. Assemble the burritos by placing the roasted sweet potatoes, black beans, and rice (if using) in the center of each tortilla. Add any desired toppings like avocado, salsa, and cilantro.
  4. Roll up the tortillas, folding in the sides and then rolling them tightly.
  5. Serve with a squeeze of lime juice for extra flavor.

These vegan burritos are not only tasty but also packed with nutrients. They’re a great option for lunch, dinner, or meal prepping for the week ahead.

7. Vegan Cauliflower Buffalo Wings

Craving some crispy, spicy wings but want to keep it plant-based? These cauliflower buffalo wings are an excellent alternative to traditional chicken wings. They’re crispy, spicy, and satisfying, making them perfect for a snack, appetizer, or even a main dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup flour (use chickpea flour for a gluten-free option)
  • 1 cup plant-based milk
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 cup buffalo sauce
  • Olive oil spray

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, plant-based milk, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Dip each cauliflower floret into the batter, ensuring it’s fully coated, and place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Lightly spray the cauliflower with olive oil and bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through until golden and crispy.
  5. Toss the baked cauliflower in buffalo sauce and return it to the oven for another 5-10 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with vegan ranch or blue cheese dressing and celery sticks.

These cauliflower buffalo wings are a perfect game day snack or a fun appetizer for any gathering. They’re spicy, crispy, and absolutely addictive!

8. Vegan Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a popular dish known for its combination of savory, sweet, and sour flavors. With a few simple swaps, you can enjoy this Thai classic in a vegan version that’s just as delicious as the original.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 cup tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • ¼ cup tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • ¼ cup chopped peanuts
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the tofu and cook until golden and crispy on all sides.
  3. Add the garlic and cook for another minute before adding the bean sprouts and carrots. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until tender-crisp.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the tamari, maple syrup, lime juice, and rice vinegar.
  5. Add the cooked noodles to the skillet and pour the sauce over everything. Toss to coat and heat through.
  6. Serve with chopped peanuts and fresh cilantro.

This vegan pad Thai is a flavorful and satisfying dish that brings together all the essential flavors of Thai cuisine in a plant-based version.

9. Vegan Spaghetti Bolognese

Traditional spaghetti bolognese gets a vegan makeover with lentils as a hearty meat substitute. The lentils are simmered in a rich tomato sauce, creating a filling and flavorful dish that pairs perfectly with pasta.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, cooked
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in the cooked lentils, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Serve the sauce over the cooked spaghetti and garnish with fresh basil.

This vegan spaghetti bolognese is hearty, flavorful, and packed with protein from the lentils. It’s the perfect meal for a family dinner or a quick weeknight meal.

10. Vegan Pizza with Cashew Cheese

Who doesn’t love pizza? With this vegan pizza recipe, you can enjoy a cheesy, delicious pizza without any dairy or animal products. The cashew cheese is creamy, tangy, and the perfect complement to your favorite pizza toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup raw cashews, soaked for at least 2 hours
  • ½ cup water
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Toppings: tomatoes, bell peppers, mushrooms, spinach, olives

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C).
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. In a blender, combine the soaked cashews, water, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, garlic powder, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  4. Spread a thin layer of the cashew cheese over the pizza dough.
  5. Add your favorite toppings and bake the pizza for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the toppings are cooked.
  6. Slice and serve hot.

This vegan pizza with cashew cheese is so creamy and flavorful that it’s hard to believe it’s dairy-free! It’s a perfect way to enjoy pizza night while keeping things plant-based.

With these 10 delicious vegan recipes, you can create meals that are not only plant-based but also bursting with flavor and texture. Whether you’re a meat lover or a long-time vegan, these dishes are sure to please everyone at the table. So, give them a try and see how easy and tasty it is to enjoy a plant-based diet without compromising on taste!
